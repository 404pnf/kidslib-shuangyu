恭贺佳节 Compliments of the Season
作者：欧·亨利 by O. Henry
再也没有圣诞故事可写了。虚构的故事已经用尽，而仅次于它的报刊新闻，是被早早就结了婚的年轻聪明的记者捏造出来的，他们对生活的态度悲观得可爱。因此，为了节日娱乐，我们只好使用两个不靠谱的来源——事实和哲学。我们将用其中之一开始我们的故事——随你怎么称呼它。 There are no more Christmas stories to write.Fiction is exhausted; and newspaper items, the next best, are manufactured by clever young journalists who have married early and have an engagingly pessimistic view of life.Therefore, for seasonable diversion, we are reduced to very questionable sources—facts and philosophy.We will begin with—whichever you choose to call it.
孩子们像令人厌烦的小动物，我们得在各种手足无措的情况下应对他们。尤其是他们耍小孩子脾气，悲伤不已时，我们就会被弄得束手无策。当我们绞尽脑汁仍不能安慰他们时，就只能打他们一顿，让他们大哭一场，抽泣着入睡。接着，我们匍匐在苍茫大地上，向上帝发问：“为什么会这样？”我们如困兽般绝望地呼喊。除了老女仆、驼背人和牧羊犬之外，没人能理解孩子们。 Children are pestilential little animals with which we have to cope under a bewildering variety of conditions.Especially when childish sorrows overwhelm them are we put to our wits’ end.We exhaust our paltry store of consolation; and then beat them, sobbing, to sleep.Then we grovel in the dust of a million years, and ask God why.Thus we call out of the rat-trap.As for the children, no one understands them except old maids, hunchbacks, and shepherd dogs.
现在来看一个事例，即发生在十二月二十五日的布娃娃和流浪汉事件。 Now comes the facts in the case of the Rag-Doll, the Tatterdemalion, and the Twenty-fifth of December.
同月十号，百万富翁孩子的布娃娃丢了。百万富翁的宅第坐落于哈得孙，很多仆人把房子里里外外翻了个遍，还是没找到这个丢失的宝贝。这个小女孩五岁，是众多任性的小怪兽之一，他们经常把注意力放到粗俗廉价的玩具上而无视那些镶有钻石的汽车和敞篷小马车，这着实让他们有钱的父母感到闹心。 On the tenth of that month the Child of the Millionaire lost her rag-doll.There were many servants in the Millionaire’s palace on the Hudson, and these ransacked the house and grounds, but without finding the lost treasure.The child was a girl of five, and one of those perverse little beasts that often wound the sensibilities of wealthy parents by fixing their affections upon some vulgar, inexpensive toy instead of upon diamond-studded automobiles and pony phaetons.
小女孩因宝贝的丢失而着实伤心，这令百万富翁非常费解，他对布娃娃市场就像对马萨诸塞州煤气公司的股票一样并不看好；而孩子的妈妈，举止得体的女主人呢——她几乎是非常得体的，往下看你就知道了。 The Child grieved sorely and truly, a thing inexplicable to the Millionaire, to whom the rag-doll market was about as interesting as Bay State Gas; and to the Lady, the Child’s mother, who was all form—that is, nearly all, as you shall see.
孩子整天伤心欲绝地痛哭，眼睛凹陷下去了，打着八字脚连路都走不稳，人瘦得像纺锤似的，在许多别的方面就像科瑞·基尔弗蒂画的那样。百万富翁拍着他的保险箱自信地笑了笑。法国和德国玩具制造商的精选产品通过特快专递火速抵达，但这并没有抚慰雷切尔受伤的心。她依旧为她的布娃娃哭泣，好像支持对所有外国的蠢东西征收高额保护关税。接着，临床态度最好的医生们被邀请来了，还带来了计秒表。他们一个接着一个徒劳无益地聊着养胃补血、安神益脑的药物，或是建议去海上航行来换换环境，直到秒表时间提示他们的钱该到账时，才停止装腔作势。然后，出于成人的理智，他们建议尽快让布娃娃回到它悲痛的小妈妈身边。孩子拒绝治疗，咬着大拇指，哭喊着要她的贝特西。这个时候，圣诞老人发来电报说他很快就会到达，并吩咐我们要展示出真正的基督徒精神，将台球娱乐、汤鼎氏联合养老保险单以及步兵分队制暂且搁置一边，以迎接他的到来。到处都洋溢着圣诞节的气氛。银行停止贷款；当铺助理工人的数量增加了一倍；在街上人们的红色雪橇会时不时碰到你的小腿；你单脚站着等酒时，你面前吧台上的啤酒正冒着泡；商店的橱窗上挂着迎客的冬青枝花环；有裘皮大衣的人穿上了裘皮外套。你几乎不知道哪种球是最好的选择——桌球中的三球，球形高脚酒杯，樟脑球，或者雪球。心肝布娃娃丢得太不是时候了。 The Child cried inconsolably, and grew hollow-eyed, knock-kneed, spindling, and cory-kilverty in many other respects.The Millionaire smiled and tapped his coffers confidently.The pick of the output of the French and German toymakers was rushed by special delivery to the mansion; but Rachel refused to be comforted.She was weeping for her rag child, and was for a high protective tariff against all foreign foolishness.Then doctors with the finest bedside manners and stop-watches were called in.One by one they chattered futilely about pepto-manganate of iron and sea voyages and hypophosphites until their stop-watches showed that Bill Rendered was under the wire for show or place.Then, as men, they advised that the rag-doll be found as soon as possible and restored to its mourning parent.The Child sniffed at therapeutics, chewed a thumb, and wailed for her Betsy.And all this time cablegrams were coming from Santa Claus saying that he would soon be here and enjoining us to show a true Christian spirit and let up on the pool-rooms and tontine policies and platoon systems long enough to give him a welcome.Everywhere the spirit of Christmas was diffusing itself.The banks were refusing loans, the pawn-brokers had doubled their gang of helpers, people bumped your shins on the streets with red sleds, Thomas and Jeremiah bubbled before you on the bars while you waited on one foot, holly-wreaths of hospitality were hung in windows of the stores, they who had ’em were getting their furs.You hardly knew which was the best bet in balls—three, high, moth, or snow.It was no time at which to lose the rag-doll or your heart.
如果沃森医生的侦探朋友被邀请来调查这次的神秘失踪事件，他一定会注意到挂在百万富翁墙上的一幅“吸血鬼”图画仿制品。那会使人快速联想到“破布、骨头和头发”。孩子心目中仅次于布娃娃的苏格兰小猎犬“弗利普”蹦蹦跳跳地穿过大厅。这一卷头发！啊哈！X，这个未知数，代表的正是布娃娃。可骨头会在哪儿呢？好，狗找骨头的时候他们会发现什么呢？——问题解决了！检查弗利普的前爪是件很轻松且富有成效的事情。看，沃森！土——狗爪子缝里有干土。一定是这狗——但福尔摩斯不在这儿。因此问题转移了。但是周围的地形和建筑必需作为参考的因素。 If Doctor Watson’s investigating friend had been called in to solve this mysterious disappearance he might have observed on the Millionaire’s wall a copy of “The Vampire.”That would have quickly suggested, by induction, “A rag and a bone and a hank of hair.”“Flip,” a Scotch terrier, next to the rag-doll in the Child’s heart, frisked through the halls.The hank of hair!Aha! X, the unfound quantity, represented the rag-doll.But, the bone?Well, when dogs find bones they—Done!It were an easy and a fruitful task to examine Flip’s forefeet.Look, Watson!Earth—dried earth between the toes.Of course, the dog—but Sherlock was not there.Therefore it devolves.But topography and architecture must intervene.
百万富翁的豪宅占地面积很大。房子前面是修剪过的一块儿草坪，草坪的样子就像刮过脸的南爱尔兰男人在两天后又长出来的胡子茬。房子一边面向另一街道的是每一片叶子都修剪过的庭园、车库和马厩。那只苏格兰小狗从育儿室叼走布娃娃，把它拖到草坪的一角，挖了个洞埋起来，就像草草了事的殡葬工那样。疑案就这样解决了，不必给有吗啡瘾的奇才开酬金支票，也不必付给警官五英镑。疲倦的读者们，现在让我们来处理整个事件的核心吧——有关圣诞的故事核心。 The Millionaire’s palace occupied a lordly space.In front of it was a lawn close-mowed as a South Ireland man’s face two days after a shave.At one side of it, and fronting on another street was a pleasaunce trimmed to a leaf, and the garage and stables.The Scotch pup had ravished the rag-doll from the nursery, dragged it to a corner of the lawn, dug a hole, and buried it after the manner of careless undertakers.There you have the mystery solved, and no checks to write for the hypodermical wizard or fi’-pun notes to toss to the sergeant.Then let’s get down to the heart of the thing, tiresome readers—the Christmas heart of the thing.
富齐喝醉了——但不像你我那样一喝醉就闹事，走路摇摇晃晃或胡言乱语，他倒像走霉运的绅士那样得体、举止适当、不寻衅滋事。 Fuzzy was drunk—not riotously or helplessly or loquaciously, as you or I might get, but decently, appropriately, and inoffensively, as becomes a gentleman down on his luck.
富齐是个不幸的士兵。马路、干草堆、公园长椅、厨房门口、慈善机构规定的洗过澡才能分配床铺的侮辱人的规矩、能轻易挣得的数量少得可怜的钱和得来不光彩的大城市慷慨救济物——这些都是他生活历史的篇章。 Fuzzy was a soldier of misfortune.The road, the haystack, the park bench, the kitchen door, the bitter round of eleemosynary beds-with-shower-bath-attachment, the petty pickings and ignobly garnered largesse of great cities—these formed the chapters of his history.
富齐沿着百万富翁房子和庭园外侧的那条街道朝河边走去。他从篱笆一角那不合时宜的“墓穴”上看到了布娃娃贝特西露出的一条腿，像发现了小人国神秘谋杀案的线索一样。他拉出这个受虐待的婴儿，夹在腋下，哼着在安定家庭中成长的布娃娃不应听的流浪汉同胞所唱的马路歌曲继续赶路。幸亏贝特西没有耳朵。还好她除了两个看不到东西的黑圆圈外没有其它眼睛；因为富齐和苏格兰小猎犬的脸像兄弟般相似，没有一个布娃娃的心脏能经受得住两次成为如此可怕怪物的猎物所带来的刺激。 Fuzzy walked toward the river, down the street that bounded one side of the Millionaire’s house and grounds.He saw a leg of Betsy, the lost rag-doll, protruding, like the clue to a Lilliputian murder mystery, from its untimely grave in a corner of the fence.He dragged forth the maltreated infant, tucked it under his arm, and went on his way crooning a road song of his brethren that no doll that has been brought up to the sheltered life should hear.Well for Betsy that she had no ears.And well that she had no eyes save unseeing circles of black; for the faces of Fuzzy and the Scotch terrier were those of brothers, and the heart of no rag-doll could withstand twice to become the prey of such fearsome monsters.
或许你不知道，格罗根的酒馆就在河流附近，就在富齐所游荡的街道尽头附近。格罗根的酒馆里已经洋溢着圣诞的欢乐气氛。 Though you may not know it, Grogan’s saloon stands near the river and near the foot of the street down which Fuzzy traveled.In Grogan’s, Christmas cheer was already rampant.
富齐带着他的娃娃走进酒馆。他想，作为太阳神节的滑稽演员或许能从祝酒杯中捞几滴酒喝。 Fuzzy entered with his doll.He fancied that as a mummer at the feast of Saturn he might earn a few drops from the wassail cup.
他把贝特西放到吧台上，大声地与她逗趣攀谈，像取悦女朋友似的讲着夸张，谄媚且亲密的话语。周围的游手好闲之辈和酒鬼们觉得这挺滑稽，顿时哄堂大笑。酒吧招待赏给富齐一杯酒。噢，我们当中有很多人带布娃娃来酒吧。 He set Betsy on the bar and addressed her loudly and humorously, seasoning his speech with exaggerated compliments and endearments, as one entertaining his lady friend.The loafers and bibbers around caught the farce of it, and roared.The bartender gave Fuzzy a drink.Oh, many of us carry rag-dolls.
“给这位女士也来杯酒？”富齐厚着脸皮建议到，于是另一杯对艺术的捐献下了肚。 “One for the lady?” suggested Fuzzy impudently, and tucked another contribution to Art beneath his waistcoat.
富齐开始看到贝特西身上可利用的价值。他第一晚的表演就很成功。一个在全镇举行滑稽剧巡回演出的想法出现在他的脑海里。 He began to see possibilities in Betsy.His first-night had been a success.Visions of a vaudeville circuit about town dawned upon him.
火炉附近坐着一群人，其中有“鸽子”麦卡锡、黑赖利和“一只耳”迈克，他们在顽固的政治选区臭名昭著，把河的左岸搞得乌烟瘴气。他们把一张报纸互相传来传去。每个人用粗壮的食指指出的那条新闻是一则标题为“一百美金酬金”的广告。想要得到酬金的人，必须找回从百万富翁宅第遗失的，走失的或者说失窃的布娃娃。这个过于忠诚的孩子心中的悲痛似乎仍在肆意地蹂躏着她。小猎犬弗利普摇着可笑的胡须在她面前跳跃着，但这也没能转移她的注意力。虽然有一堆来自法国梅布尔和维奥莱特的会走路、会说话、会叫妈妈、会眨眼的布娃娃簇拥着她，但她仍为她的贝特西痛哭。这则广告是最后一招了。 In a group near the stove sat “Pigeon” McCarthy, Black Riley, and “One-ear” Mike, well and unfavorably known in the tough shoestring district that blackened the left bank of the river.They passed a newspaper back and forth among themselves.The item that each solid and blunt forefinger pointed out was an advertisement headed “One Hundred Dollars Reward.”To earn it one must return the rag-doll lost, strayed, or stolen from the Millionaire’s mansion.It seemed that grief still ravaged, unchecked, in the bosom of the too faithful Child.Flip, the terrier, capered and shook his absurd whisker before her, powerless to distract.She wailed for her Betsy in the faces of walking, talking, mama-ing, and eye-closing French Mabelles and Violettes.The advertisement was a last resort.
黑赖利从火炉后面绕到富齐身边。 Black Riley came from behind the stove and approached Fuzzy in his one-sided parabolic way.
这个圣诞节的滑稽演员激动得面颊发红，他把贝特西一把塞到胳膊下，准备离开去其它地方进行即兴演出。 The Christmas mummer, flushed with success, had tucked Betsy under his arm, and was about to depart to the filling of impromptu dates elsewhere.
黑赖利操着一口浓重的地方口音对他说：“嘿，老兄，你从哪儿弄到的这个娃娃？” “Say, ’Bo,” said Black Riley to him, “where did you cop out dat doll?”
“这个娃娃吗？”富齐问，并且用食指抚摸着贝特西以确定说的就是她。“为什么这么问？这个娃娃是俾路支斯坦皇帝赠予我的。我新港的乡下家里还有700个像这样的娃娃。这个娃娃——” “This doll?” asked Fuzzy, touching Betsy with his forefinger to be sure that she was the one referred to.“Why, this doll was presented to me by the Emperor of Beloochistan.I have seven hundred others in my country home in Newport.This doll—”
“别再逗乐了，”赖利说，“你是在小山岗的房子里偷的或捡的，不过这没关系。如果你想用那破烂儿换五十美分，就赶快拿钱走人。我哥哥正在家里的小孩儿或许想要拿它玩玩。嘿，怎么样？” “Cheese the funny business,” said Riley.“You swiped it or picked it up at de house on de hill where—but never mind dat.You want to take fifty cents for de rags, and take it quick.Me brother’s kid at home might be wantin’ to play wid it.Hey—what?”
他掏出一枚硬币。 He produced the coin.
富齐满脸酒气，粗野地咯咯笑着。去萨拉·伯恩哈特的经纪人办公室提议抽出一个晚上免除贝特西去塔奇镇文化宫和文学俱乐部的表演吧。你将会听到几乎和富齐一模一样的笑声。 Fuzzy laughed a gurgling, insolent, alcoholic laugh in his face.Go to the office of Sarah Bernhardt’s manager and propose to him that she be released from a night’s performance to entertain the Tackytown Lyceum and Literary Coterie.You will hear the duplicate of Fuzzy’s laugh.
黑赖利像摔跤运动员那样，用他那蓝莓色的眼睛迅速地打量着富齐。他的手痒痒，想要当一回罗马人，从这个对着天使即兴表演而不自知的滑稽演员手里掳掠来那个布制的萨宾姑娘。但他忍住了。富齐很胖，高大且强壮。三英寸厚的营养良好的脂肪夹在汗衫和裤子之间，靠脏兮兮的亚麻制品抵御冬天的寒风。他衣袖和膝盖上的无数小的圆形皱纹显示出他有着发达的肌肉和强健的骨胳。他的蓝色小眼睛沐浴在利他主义和头脑眩晕的湿润中，他友好地看着你，一副毫不害臊的模样。他留着连鬓胡须，醉醺醺的，又胖得出奇，看着有点害怕。因此，黑赖利没有轻举妄动。 Black Riley gauged Fuzzy quickly with his blueberry eye as a wrestler does.His hand was itching to play the Roman and wrest the rag Sabine from the extemporaneous merry-andrew who was entertaining an angel unaware.But he refrained.Fuzzy was fat and solid and big.Three inches of well-nourished corporeity, defended from the winter winds by dingy linen, intervened between his vest and trousers.Countless small, circular wrinkles running around his coat-sleeves and knees guaranteed the quality of his bone and muscle.His small, blue eyes, bathed in the moisture of altruism and wooziness, looked upon you kindly, yet without abashment.He was whiskerly, whiskyly, fleshily formidable.So, Black Riley temporized.
“难道你不要拿她换钱吗？”他问。 “Wot’ll you take for it, den?” he asked.
“钱，”富齐用沙哑的声音坚定地说，“买不走它。” “Money,” said Fuzzy, with husky firmness, “cannot buy her.”
他正陶醉于艺术家第一次甜蜜的成就感中。他把褪色的沾有泥点的蓝色布娃娃放在吧台上，假装和她谈话。随着赢得的喝彩声他的心起伏跳动，敬赠的免费酒使他喉咙发烫——难道几个臭钱能给他带来这样的成就感吗？你会发现富齐很有个性。 He was intoxicated with the artist’s first sweet cup of attainment.To set a faded-blue, earth-stained rag-doll on a bar, to hold mimic converse with it, and to find his heart leaping with the sense of plaudits earned and his throat scorching with free libations poured in his honor—could base coin buy him from such achievements?You will perceive that Fuzzy had the temperament.
富齐像一只受过训练的海狮那样迈步走出酒馆，寻找另一家要征服的酒馆。 Fuzzy walked out with the gait of a trained sea-lion in search of other cafés to conquer.
尽管天色还不算晚，但是灯光犹如深口平底锅里爆炸的玉米花一样开始在城市里闪烁。期盼已久的平安夜初现端倪。几百万人已经为平安夜的庆祝做好了准备。城镇将被映得一片通红。你们自己也已经听到了喇叭声，躲闪着雀跃的狂欢者。 Though the dusk of twilight was hardly yet apparent, lights were beginning to spangle the city like pop-corn bursting in a deep skillet.Christmas Eve, impatiently expected, was peeping over the brink of the hour.Millions had prepared for its celebration.Towns would be painted red.You, yourself, have heard the horns and dodged the capers of the Saturnalians.
“鸽子”麦卡锡，黑赖利和“一只耳”迈克在格罗根酒馆外进行了一场仓促的谈话。他们都是身材瘦削、脸色苍白的年轻人，虽不是公开的打手，但他们的打斗方式比土耳其人最可怕的打法还要危险。在对阵战中富齐一定能击败他们三个。但在毫无章法的撕斗中，他必败无疑。 “Pigeon” McCarthy, Black Riley, and “One-ear” Mike held a hasty converse outside Grogan’s.They were narrow-chested, pallid striplings, not fighters in the open, but more dangerous in their ways of warfare than the most terrible of Turks.Fuzzy, in a pitched battle, could have eaten the three of them.In a go-as-you-please encounter he was already doomed.
富齐带着贝特西正进科斯蒂根赌场时，他们追了上来。他们拦住他，把报纸甩到他眼皮底下。富齐识字——并且懂更多。 They overtook him just as he and Betsy were entering Costigan’s Casino.They deflected him, and shoved the newspaper under his nose.Fuzzy could read—and more.
“伙计们，”他说，“你们真够朋友。给我一个星期，我仔细考虑考虑。” “Boys,” said he, “you are certainly damn true friends.Give me a week to think it over.”
一位真正的艺术家内心陷入了挣扎。 The soul of a real artist is quenched with difficulty.
小伙子们谨慎地向他指出广告是无情的，而且今日的承诺明日也不见得能兑现。 The boys carefully pointed out to him that advertisements were soulless, and that the deficiencies of the day might not be supplied by the morrow.
“100元呢。”富齐若有所思地嘀咕着。 “A cool hundred,” said Fuzzy thoughtfully and mushily.
“小伙子们，”他说，“你们真够朋友。我去领赏金。表演这一行已不如以前了。” “Boys,” said he, “you are true friends.I’ll go up and claim the reward.The show business is not what it used to be.”
天完全黑了。三人簇拥着他来到百万富翁房子所在的小山脚下。富齐突然恶狠狠地转向他们。 Night was falling more surely.The three tagged at his sides to the foot of the rise on which stood the Millionaire’s house.There Fuzzy turned upon them acrimoniously.
“你们这群灰头土脸的猎兔狗子，”他吼道，“滚开。” “You are a pack of putty-faced beagle-hounds,” he roared.“Go away.”
他们走开了——只是稍微离远了些。 They went away—a little way.
“鸽子”麦卡锡的口袋里揣着一根长八英寸、直径一英寸的煤气管。中间和一端灌了铅。一半用焊锡包裹得紧紧的。作为一名传统的歹徒，黑赖利拿着一根硬头鞭。“一只耳”迈克则戴着一对祖传的铜指虎。 In “Pigeon” McCarthy’s pocket was a section of one-inch gas-pipe eight inches long.In one end of it and in the middle of it was a lead plug.One-half of it was packed tight with solder.Black Riley carried a slung-shot, being a conventional thug.“One-ear” Mike relied upon a pair of brass knucks—an heirloom in the family.
“何劳我们亲自出马，”黑赖利说，“在有人为我们效劳的时候？让他替我们去取。嘿——怎么样？” “Why fetch and carry,” said Black Riley, “when some one will do it for ye?Let him bring it out to us.Hey—what?”
“我们可以在他脚上绑块石头把他扔到河里去。”“鸽子”麦卡锡说。 “We can chuck him in the river,” said “Pigeon” McCarthy, “with a stone tied to his feet.”
“你们这些家伙真让我心烦，”“一只耳”迈克阴着张脸说，“你们怎么都没一点长进？在他身上浇些汽油，把他推到马路上点把火烧了不就得了？” “Youse guys make me tired,” said “One-ear” Mike sadly.“Ain’t progress ever appealed to none of yez?Sprinkle a little gasoline on ’im, and drop ’im on the Drive—well?”
富齐走进百万富翁家的大门，沿着曲折的小路朝有柔和亮光的房子入口走去。三个地精恶霸在门口徘徊着，两个守在门的两侧，一个在马路对面。他们胸有成竹地把弄着冰冷的金属和皮革。 Fuzzy entered the Millionaire’s gate and zigzagged toward the softly glowing entrance of the mansion.The three goblins came up to the gate and lingered—one on each side of it, one beyond the roadway.They fingered their cold metal and leather, confident.
富齐带着恍惚的神情，傻笑着按响门铃。出于遗传的本能，他伸手去摸右手套的扣子。但他没戴手套，于是尴尬地放下左手。 Fuzzy rang the door-bell, smiling foolishly and dreamily.An atavistic instinct prompted him to reach for the button of his right glove.But he wore no gloves; so his left hand dropped, embarrassed.
负责开门的势利门仆以前遇到的都是衣冠楚楚的客人，第一眼看到富齐时吃了一惊。但他第二眼瞥到了富齐的通行证，他一定会受欢迎的凭证——在他腋下摇摆的主人女儿丢失的布娃娃。 The particular menial whose duty it was to open doors to silks and laces shied at first sight of Fuzzy.But a second glance took in his passport, his card of admission, his surety of welcome—the lost rag-doll of the daughter of the house dangling under his arm.
富齐被领进大厅，厅里视野模糊，光线柔和看不见灯。这名佣人离开了，回来的时候身后跟着一个女佣和孩子。布娃娃回到了伤心的小主人身边。她把失而复得的宝贝搂在怀里；然后，带着小孩子过分的自私和天真，她跺着脚哭喊起来，表达她对这个把她从悲哀绝望的深渊中挽救出来的讨厌的大怪物的憎恨和恐惧。富齐赶快表现得谦恭顺从，脸上堆着憨笑，闲聊着一些赞美的话，试图讨好这个还没长大的孩子。孩子高声叫喊着被拖走了，怀里紧紧地抱着贝特西。 Fuzzy was admitted into a great hall, dim with the glow from unseen lights.The hireling went away and returned with a maid and the Child.The doll was restored to the mourning one.She clasped her lost darling to her breast; and then, with the inordinate selfishness and candor of childhood, stamped her foot and whined hatred and fear of the odious being who had rescued her from the depths of sorrow and despair.Fuzzy wriggled himself into an ingratiatory attitude and essayed the idiotic smile and blattering small talk that is supposed to charm the budding intellect of the young.The Child bawled, and was dragged away, hugging her Betsy close.
秘书来了，他面色苍白、举止优雅、走路小心翼翼，一副崇尚浮华和礼仪的样子。他数出十张十美元钞票塞到富齐手里；接着，他把眼光移向门口，朝看门人詹姆斯使了个眼色，暗示他把这个可恶的领赏人带出去，然后悄悄回秘书岗位工作了。 There came the Secretary, pale, poised, polished, gliding in pumps, and worshipping pomp and ceremony.He counted out into Fuzzy’s hand ten ten-dollar bills; then dropped his eye upon the door, transferred it to James, its custodian, indicated the obnoxious earner of the reward with the other, and allowed his pumps to waft him away to secretarial regions.
詹姆斯那威风凛凛的眼光笼罩着富齐，一直把他扫到前门口。 James gathered Fuzzy with his own commanding optic and swept him as far as the front door.
富齐的脏手一碰到钱，他的第一反应是拔腿就跑；但转念一想，他克制住了这种失礼的举动。钱是他的，已经给他了。这笔钱——啊，在他的心目中，这笔钱为他开创了一个多么美妙的极乐世界！他曾经跌落到了社会的底层；他饥寒交迫、无家可归、没有朋友、衣衫褴褛、流浪漂泊；现在他手中有了通往天堂的钥匙，这个玉露琼浆的天堂正是他所向往的。那个仙女娃娃用她塞了破布的手挥舞着魔棒；现在，无论他走到哪里，有闪亮的搁脚横档和盛有红色魔水的泛光玻璃容器的魔宫大门都会为他敞开。 When the money touched fuzzy’s dingy palm his first instinct was to take to his heels; but a second thought restrained him from that blunder of etiquette.It was his; it had been given him.It—and, oh, what an loria it opened to the gaze of his mind’s eye!He had tumbled to the foot of the ladder; he was hungry, homeless, friendless, ragged, cold, drifting; and he held in his hand the key to a paradise of the mud-honey that he craved.The fairy doll had waved a wand with her rag-stuffed hand; and now wherever he might go the enchanted palaces with shining foot-rests and magic red fluids in gleaming glassware would be open to him.
他跟随詹姆斯来到门口。 He followed James to the door.
在那个仆从为他打开进入门廊的宏伟的红木大门时，富齐停了下来。 He paused there as the flunky drew open the great mahogany portal for him to pass into the vestibule.
铸铁门对面黑暗的公路上，黑赖利和他的两个同伙悠闲地散着步，用手指拨弄着衣服下面藏着的可以将布娃娃酬金据为己有的必然致命的武器。 Beyond the wrought-iron gates in the dark highway Black Riley and his two pals casually strolled, fingering under their coats the inevitably fatal weapons that were to make the reward of the rag-doll theirs.
富齐在百万富翁的门口停了下来，思考了一下。鲜活的想法与回忆如同一棵枯树上的榭寄生小枝，开始装点起他混乱的头脑。要知道，他可是喝得烂醉如泥，开始脱离现实了。夹杂着深红色浆果的冬青枝编成的花环和彩饰使得大厅光彩夺目——他以前在哪儿见过这些东西？冬天的时候，他在某个地方见过像这样的光滑油亮的地板，闻过这样的花香，而且——这个屋子里的人在唱的一首歌他似乎也在哪儿听过。一些人边弹竖琴边唱歌。当然，现在是圣诞节——富齐想，自己一定喝得太多了以至于忽视了这一点。 Fuzzy stopped at the Millionaire’s door and bethought himself.Like little sprigs of mistletoe on a dead tree, certain living green thoughts and memories began to decorate his confused mind.He was quite drunk, mind you, and the present was beginning to fade.Those wreaths and festoons of holly with their scarlet berries making the great hall gay—where had he seen such things before?Somewhere he had known polished floors and odors of fresh flowers in winter, and—and some one was singing a song in the house that he thought he had heard before.Some one singing and playing a harp.Of course, it was Christmas—Fuzzy though he must have been pretty drunk to have overlooked that.
然后他又脱离了现实，一个来自虚构的、已经消失的、不可改变的过去的纯白的、转瞬即逝的、被遗忘的小幽灵回到他身边——那是一种“位高则任重”的精神。作为绅士，有些事情非做不可。 And then he went out of the present, and there came back to him out of some impossible, vanished, and irrevocable past a little, pure-white, transient, forgotten ghost—the spirit of noblesse oblige.Upon a gentleman certain things devolve.
詹姆斯打开前门。一束灯光洒到通往铁门的砾石路面上。黑赖利、麦卡锡和“一只耳”迈克看见了，不经意地将他们的险恶封锁线移近铁门。 James opened the outer door.A stream of light went down the graveled walk to the iron gate.Black Riley, McCarthy, and “One-ear” Mike saw, and carelessly drew their sinister cordon closer about the gate.
富齐用詹姆斯的主人从未做也不会做过的专横手势命令詹姆斯把门关上。对于绅士来说有些事情非做不可。尤其是在圣诞期间。 With a more imperious gesture than James’s master had ever used or could ever use, Fuzzy compelled the menial to close the door.Upon a gentleman certain things devolve.Especially at the Christmas season.
他对一脸迷惑的詹姆斯说：“绅士在圣诞前夕拜访时要向房子的女主人致以节日的问候。你明白吗？我在向房子的女主人送达问候之前不能离开。懂吗？” “It is cust—customary,” he said to James, the flustered, “when a gentleman calls on Christmas Eve to pass the compliments of the season with the lady of the house.You und’stand?I shall not move shtep till I pass compl’ments season with lady the house.Und’stand?”
他们两人起了争执。詹姆斯输了。富齐提高嗓音，整幢房子回荡着他不快的声音。我没说他是名绅士。他只不过是被幽灵附身的流浪汉罢了。 There was an argument.James lost.Fuzzy raised his voice and sent it through the house unpleasantly.I did not say he was a gentleman.He was simply a tramp being visited by a ghost.
纯银制的铃响了。詹姆斯回去看看有什么吩咐，把富齐留在大厅。詹姆斯在某个地方向一些人解释着。 A sterling silver bell rang.James went back to answer it, leaving Fuzzy in the hall.James explained somewhere to some one.
接着，他回来把富齐带到了客厅。 Then he came and conducted Fuzzy into the library.
过了一会儿，女主人进来了。她比富齐见过的任何画像里的女子都要美丽神圣。她笑着说一些有关布娃娃的事情。富齐听不懂；有关布娃娃的一切他都不记得了。 The Lady entered a moment later.She was more beautiful and holy than any picture that Fuzzy had seen.She smiled, and said something about a doll.Fuzzy didn’t understand that; he remembered nothing about a doll.
一个男仆用刻有家族纹章的纯银托盘端进来两小杯冒着泡的酒。女主人拿了一杯。另一杯端给了富齐。 A footman brought in two small glasses of sparkling wine on a stamped sterling-silver waiter.The Lady took one.The other was handed to Fuzzy.
富齐的手指触碰到细长杯柄的一刹那，他的头脑清醒了片刻。他打起精神；时间对我们大多数人都不留情面，但对富齐却很眷顾。 As his fingers closed on the slender glass stem his disabilities dropped from him for one brief moment.He straightened himself; and Time, so disobliging to most of us, turned backward to accommodate Fuzzy.
被遗忘的圣诞精灵从格罗根威士忌酒的雾气中升起，颜色要比多数肥胖的圣诞老人的假胡须还要白。在有护壁板的弗吉尼亚长大厅里，骑士们围在盛潘趣酒的银碗旁为古老的贵族祝酒，那里与百万富翁的宅邸有什么关系？而外面结冰的街道上的出租马车的达达马蹄声为什么无论如何该和西侧游廊庇护下打猎骑手的踩踏声有关系？这与富齐又有什么关系？ Forgotten Christmas ghosts whiter than the false beards of the most opulent Kris Kringle were rising in the fumes of Grogan’s whisky.What had the Millionaire’s mansion to do with a long, wainscoted Virginia hall, where the riders were grouped around a silver punch-bowl, drinking the ancient toast of the House?And why should the patter of the cab horses’ hoofs on the frozen street be in any wise related to the sound of the saddled hunters stamping under the shelter of the west veranda?And what had Fuzzy to do with any of it?
女主人透过杯子看着他，她屈尊俯就的微笑像假曙光似的褪去了。她的眼神变得严肃起来。她在破衣服和苏格兰猎狗毛似的络腮胡须下看到了一些无法理解的东西。但这没有关系。 The Lady, looking at him over her glass, let her condescending smile fade away like a false dawn.Her eyes turned serious.She saw something beneath the rags and Scotch terrier whiskers that she did not understand.But it did not matter.
富齐举起酒杯，茫然地笑着。 Fuzzy lifted his glass and smiled vacantly.
“打……打扰了，夫人，”他说，“但不能不向房子的女主人致以节日的问候就走。否则有违绅士的身份。” “P-pardon, lady,” he said, “but couldn’t leave without loriaes’ comp’ments sheason with lady th’ house.’Gainst princ’ples gen’leman do sho.”
接着他开始说古老的祝词，这是贵族的传统，每当这时，男人们戴着有褶皱的领饰和带扑粉的假发。 And then he began the ancient salutation that was a tradition in the House when men wore lace ruffles and powder.
“愿新年的福气——” “The blessings of another year—”
富齐忘词了。女主人提示道： Fuzzy’s memory failed him.The Lady prompted:
“——降临到这户人家。” “—Be upon this hearth.”
“——客人——”富齐结结巴巴地说道。 “—The guest—” stammered Fuzzy.
“——降临到家中的女主人——”女主人面带诱导性的微笑继续说。 “—And upon her who—” continued the Lady, with a leading smile.
“哦，得啦，”富齐有失礼仪地说道，“我记不起来了。纵情喝酒吧。” “Oh, cut it out,” said Fuzzy, ill-manneredly.“I can’t remember.Drink hearty.”
富齐带头。大家喝起了酒。女主人又得体地笑了笑。詹姆斯的目光笼罩着富齐并再次带他朝前门走去。竖琴声仍旧轻轻地在房间里回荡。 Fuzzy had shot his arrow.They drank.The Lady smiled again the smile of her caste.James enveloped and re-conducted him toward the front door.The harp music still softly drifted through the house.
房子外面，黑赖利对着冰冷的手哈气，紧贴着门。 Outside, Black Riley breathed on his cold hands and hugged the gate.
女主人沉思着自言自语道：“我想知道这个人是谁——不过来的客人太多了。不知道对于那些已经如此落魄的人来说，记忆究竟是诅咒还是祝福。” “I wonder,” said the Lady to herself, musing, “who—but there were so many who came.I wonder whether memory is a curse or a blessing to them after they have fallen so low.”
富齐和他的陪同者快到门前了。女主人喊道：“詹姆斯！” Fuzzy and his escort were nearly at the door.The Lady called: “James!”
詹姆斯大步退了回去，一副卑躬屈膝的样子，富齐摇摇晃晃地独自呆在那里，心中圣火的短暂闪耀的火花一下子就熄灭了。 James stalked back obsequiously, leaving Fuzzy waiting unsteadily, with his brief spark of the divine fire gone.
屋外，黑赖利跺着冰冷的双脚，手中的煤气管握得更紧了。 Outside, Black Riley stamped his cold feet and got a firmer grip on his section of gas-pipe.
“你把这位绅士带到楼下，”女主人说，“然后吩咐路易斯把那辆梅赛德斯开出来带这位先生去他想去的任何地方。” “You will conduct this gentleman,” said the Lady, “Downstairs.Then tell Louis to get out the Mercedes and take him to whatever place he wishes to go.”
