我的前参议员秘书生涯 My Late Senatorial Secretaryship
作者：马克·吐温 by Mark Twain
我现已不再任参议员私人秘书一职。我踏踏实实地在那个位子上坐了两个月，做事的热情极其高涨，可怎料我干的好事又都找上门来——也就是说，我的杰作又都寄了回来，原形毕露了。想来我还是辞职为妙。事情的始末是这样的。 I am not a private secretary to a senator any more now.I held the berth two months in security and in great cheerfulness of spirit, but my bread began to return from over the waters then—that is to say, my works came back and revealed themselves.I judged it best to resign.The way of it was this.
某天一大早，我的老板便差人来唤我，我刚刚暗中把几句机智的问答插入到他最后那篇重要的财政演讲稿中，于是立马赶去见他。他的表情使我略感不妙。他松着领带，头发蓬乱，面容里带着强抑怒火的信号。他紧紧攥住一叠信，我一下子明白过来，那是太平洋彼岸的可怕来信。他说：“我原以为你是可以信任的。”我答道：“我是的，先生。”他接着说：“我给过你一封信，是我在内华达州的一些选民的来信，他们要求在鲍德温牧场建一个邮局，我还吩咐你尽可能巧妙地答复他们，讲些有说服力的理由让他们明白那地方压根用不着设邮局。”我顿感轻松了一些。“哦，如果就是这件事，先生，我确实照做了。”“是啊，你照做了。那我就来念念你的回信，看看你丢不丢人： My employer sent for me one morning tolerably early, and, as soon as I had finished inserting some conundrums clandestinely into his last great speech upon finance, I entered the presence.There was something portentous in his appearance.His cravat was untied, his hair was in a state of disorder, and his countenance bore about it the signs of a suppressed storm.He held a package of letters in his tense grasp, and I knew that the dreaded Pacific mail was in.He said: “I thought you were worthy of confidence.”I said, “Yes, sir.”He said, “I gave you a letter from certain of my constituents in the State of Nevada, asking the establishment of a post-office at Baldwin’s Ranch, and told you to answer it, as ingeniously as you could, with arguments which should persuade them that there was no real necessity for an office at that place.”I felt easier.“Oh, if that is all, sir, I did do that.”“Yes, you did.I will read your answer for your own humiliation:
‘11月24日于华盛顿。 ‘WASHINGTON, Nov. 24.
‘史密斯先生，琼斯先生及其余诸位。 ‘Messrs. Smith, Jones, and others.
‘先生们：你们逗我玩呢，竟想在鲍德温牧场设邮局？那对你们一点儿好处都没有。就算信寄到那里，你们也不识字，你们知道的；再说了，那些装着钱的信要经过你们那里寄到别处，恐怕很难顺利过关，想必你们能立刻明白过来吧；那样对你我都麻烦。别，千万别自找麻烦地在你们牧场建什么邮局。我心系你们的切身利益，但我觉得建邮局不过是个华而不实的蠢主意。你们需要的其实是座像样的监狱，知道吗——一座像样的、牢固的监狱和一所免费的学校。这些才能给你们带来永久的福利。这些才能真正满足你们，带给你们真正的幸福。我会马上向国会提出该议案。 ‘GENTLEMEN: What the mischief do you suppose you want with a post-office at Baldwin’s Ranch?It would not do you any good.If any letters came there, you couldn’t read them, you know; and, besides, such letters as ought to pass through, with money in them, for other localities, would not be likely to get through, you must perceive at once; and that would make trouble for us all.No, don’t bother about a post-office in your camp.I have your best interests at heart, and feel that it would only be an ornamental folly.What you want is a nice jail, you know—a nice, substantial jail and a free school.These will be a lasting benefit to you.These will make you really contented and happy.I will move in the matter at once.
‘你们真诚的， ‘Very truly,
马克·吐温， Mark Twain,
‘以美国参议员詹姆斯·W. N—的名义。’ ‘For James W. N—, U. S. Senator.’
“你就是这样回的信。那些人扬言，如果我胆敢再踏入他们地盘一步，就把我勒死，我也毫不怀疑他们一定会这么干。”“不会吧，先生，我不知道这会害了您。我不过是想说服他们。”“啊。是啊，你的确说服了他们，我一点儿也不怀疑。瞧瞧，这儿还有个例子。我给了你一封请愿书，来自内华达州的几位绅士，他们央求我在国会通过一项法案，组建内华达州卫理圣公会教堂。我吩咐你答复他们说，这种法案由州立法机关创立才是更加正规化的渠道，并且要你竭力向他们证明新州的宗教势力之弱，组建教会是否必需还有待商榷。可你是怎么回信的呢？ “That is the way you answered that letter.Those people say they will hang me, if I ever enter that district again; and I am perfectly satisfied they will, too.”“Well, sir, I did not know I was doing any harm.I only wanted to convince them.”“Ah. Well, you did convince them, I make no manner of doubt.Now, here is another specimen.I gave you a petition from certain gentlemen of Nevada, praying that I would get a bill through Congress incorporating the Methodist Episcopal Church of the State of Nevada.I told you to say, in reply, that the creation of such a law came more properly within the province of the state legislature; and to endeavor to show them that, in the present feebleness of the religious element in that new commonwealth, the expediency of incorporating the church was questionable.What did you write?
“‘11月24日于华盛顿。 “‘WASHINGTON, Nov. 24.
“‘约翰·哈利法克斯牧师及其余诸位。 “‘Rev. John Halifax and others.
“‘先生们：看来你们得去州立法机关实践你们那天马行空的臆想了——国会对宗教的事一无所知。等等，还是先别急着去好了；因为你们打算做的这事儿对新州而言非当务之急——事实上，还十分荒唐可笑。你们那儿的信徒实力太弱，聪慧不足，德行不够，信仰不坚，样样都差，差远了。还是打消这个念头吧——反正你们做不来。创建这种团体，又不能发行股票——或者，就算发行了，也只会始终陷你们于无尽的烦恼之中。其他教派会恶言诋毁你们的股票，然后“抛售”、“卖空”，最后搞垮它。就像对付你们那里的银矿那样，他们会用同样的手段对付你们——想尽办法让全世界都相信那是宗“未经批准的计划”。你们不该蓄意干出这事儿，把一件神圣的宗教之事都搞得声名狼藉了。你们该为自己感到羞愧——这就是我对此事的看法。你们在请愿书的结尾这样写道：“我们将永远祈祷”。我认为你们最好这样做——你们是需要好好祷告祷告。 “‘GENTLEMEN: You will have to go to the state legislature about that speculation of yours—Congress don’t know anything about religion.But don’t you hurry to go there, either; because this thing you propose to do out in that new country isn’t expedient—in fact, it is ridiculous.Your religious people there are too feeble, in intellect, in morality, in piety, in everything, pretty much.You had better drop this—you can’t make it work.You can’t issue stock on an incorporation like that—or if you could, it would only keep you in trouble all the time.The other denominations would abuse it, and “bear” it, and “sell it short,” and break it down.They would do with it just as they would with one of your silver-mines out there—they would try to make all the world believe it was “wildcat.”You ought not to do anything that is calculated to bring a sacred thing into disrepute.You ought to be ashamed of yourselves—that is what I think about it.You close your petition with the words: “And we will ever pray.”I think you had better—you need to do it.
“‘你们真诚的， “‘Very truly,
“‘马克·吐温， “‘MARK TWAIN,
“‘以美国参议员詹姆斯·W. N—的名义。’ “‘For James W. N—, U. S. Senator.’
“这封锋芒毕露的信直接让我脱离了选区的宗教群众。可我的政治生涯好像还被断送得不够彻底。不知受什么邪念所驱，我竟把旧金山市参议会几位长老议员递来的申请书交给你，让你施展拳脚。就是那份恳请国会制定法律，把旧金山市海滨地区的航运税收权划归他们那个市。我告诫你提出这项议案是有风险的。我让你给那些市长老议员回一封不表态的信——一封含糊其词的信——信里应竭力避免涉及航运税的一切实质性的考虑和磋商。如果你还有点儿良心——还知道羞愧——那么听到这封你遵照我的意思写下的信时，就应该唤起你的羞耻感： “That luminous epistle finishes me with the religious element among my constituents.But that my political murder might be made sure, some evil instinct prompted me to hand you this memorial from the grave company of elders composing the board of aldermen of the city of San Francisco, to try your hand upon a memorial praying that the city’s right to the water-lots upon the city front might be established by law of Congress.I told you this was a dangerous matter to move in.I told you to write a non-committal letter to the aldermen—an ambiguous letter—a letter that should avoid, as far as possible, all real consideration and discussion of the water-lot question.If there is any feeling left in you—any shame—surely this letter you wrote, in obedience to that order, ought to evoke it, when its words fall upon your ears:
‘11月27日于华盛顿。 ‘WASHINGTON, Nov. 27.
‘尊敬的诸位市参议会议员。 ‘The Honorable Board of Aldermen, etc.
‘诸位阁下：乔治·华盛顿，我们敬爱的国父，已经亡故了。他那漫长而辉煌的职业生涯结束了， 唉！永远结束了。他在这片国土上深受爱戴，他的英年早逝使得举国上下陷入了黑暗、悲伤之中。他是1799年12月14日去世的。他安详地走下了记载他一生荣耀和伟大成就的舞台，真乃是古今屈于死神的人中最受敬爱、最令人嗟悼的英雄。这种关头，你们居然还在提什么航运税！他这是什么命运啊！ ‘GENTLEMEN: George Washington, the revered Father of his Country, is dead.His long and brilliant career is closed, alas! forever.He was greatly respected in this section of the country, and his untimely decease cast a gloom over the whole community.He died on the 14th day of December, 1799.He passed peacefully away from the scene of his honors and his great achievements, the most lamented hero and the best beloved that ever earth hath yielded unto Death.At such a time as this, you speak of water-lots! what a lot was his!
‘什么是名望！名望就是一次机遇。艾萨克·牛顿爵士发现一个苹果落到地上——这的确是个无足轻重的发现，比他早发现的人何止百万——怎奈他父母影响力大啊，于是他们把那点儿小事吹得神乎其神、妙不可言，结果，瞧！单纯的世人也开始吆喝起来，几乎是一眨眼的功夫，那人就名震八方了。谨记这番见解吧。 ‘What is fame!Fame is an accident.Sir Isaac Newton discovered an apple falling to the ground—a trivial discovery, truly, and one which a million men had made before him—but his parents were influential, and so they tortured that small circumstance into something wonderful, and, lo! the simple world took up the shout and, in almost the twinkling of an eye, that man was famous.Treasure these thoughts.
‘诗歌啊，美妙的诗歌，汝之惠泽，谁人能断！ ‘Poesy, sweet poesy, who shall estimate what the world owes to thee!
“玛丽有只小绵羊，羊毛白如雪——无论玛丽去哪儿，羊儿准跟在身旁。” “Mary had a little lamb, its fleece was white as snow—And everywhere that Mary went, the lamb was sure to go.”
“杰克、吉尔去爬山，为提一桶水下山，杰克跌倒磕破头，吉尔跟着滚下山。” “Jack and Gill went up the hillTo draw a pail of water;Jack fell down and broke his crown,And Gill came tumbling after.”
‘这两首诗措辞质朴，格调高雅，且无伤风败俗之嫌，被我视为珍品。它们适于各种文化程度、各种生活领域的读者——适于乡野俗夫，适于待育的幼儿，适于行会商人。尤其少不了这两首诗的正是市参议会的诸位。 ‘For simplicity, elegance of diction, and freedom from immoral tendencies, I regard those two poems in the light of gems.They are suited to all grades of intelligence, to every sphere of life—to the field, to the nursery, to the guild.Especially should no Board of Aldermen be without them.
‘德高望重、顽固不化的老先生们！再来信吧。再没有比这样友好的书信往来更催人进步的方法了。再来信吧——如果你们的申请书里特别提到了什么问题，用不着羞于解释。我们永远乐于倾听你们絮叨。 ‘Venerable fossils! write again.Nothing improves one so much as friendly correspondence.Write again—and if there is anything in this memorial of yours that refers to anything in particular, do not be backward about explaining it.We shall always be happy to hear you chirp.
‘你们真诚的， ‘Very truly,
‘马克·吐温， ‘MARK TWAIN,
‘以美国参议员詹姆斯·W. N—的名义。’ ‘For James W. N—, U. S. Senator.’
“真是恶劣透顶、毁人于一旦的信啊！你个疯子！” “That is an atrocious, a ruinous epistle!Distraction!”
“哎，先生，这信要是哪里写得不对，我万分抱歉——可是——可是依我看，我确实避开了航运税问题。” “Well, sir, I am really sorry if there is anything wrong about it—but—but it appears to me to dodge the water-lot question.”
“避开你个头！噢！——先不管这个了。反正灾祸是跑不掉了，干脆来个彻底。干脆来个彻底吧——让你这篇最后的杰作，这篇我要念的杰作，来做个收场吧。我算是给毁了。我把洪堡地区的来信交给你时就心有疑虑。信上要求对印第安峡谷到莎士比亚峡谷及其中间各站的邮递路线进行部分调整，将其中一部分改到摩门老路上去。但我提醒过你，这个问题很敏感，还告诫过你要灵活应付——信回得含糊些，好让他们摸不着头脑。你这致命的蠢脑袋竟驱使你作出如此糟糕透顶的回复。我看你要是还没完全丧失羞耻心，就该把耳朵捂起来： “Dodge the mischief!Oh!—but never mind.As long as destruction must come now, let it be complete.Let it be complete—let this last of your performances, which I am about to read, make a finality of it.I am a ruined man.I had my misgivings when I gave you the letter from Humboldt, asking that the post route from Indian Gulch to Shakespeare Gap and intermediate points be changed partly to the old Mormon trail.But I told you it was a delicate question, and warned you to deal with it deftly—to answer it dubiously, and leave them a little in the dark.And your fatal imbecility impelled you to make this disastrous reply.I should think you would stop your ears, if you are not dead to all shame:
‘11月30日于华盛顿。 ‘WASHINGTON, Nov. 30.
‘珀金斯先生、瓦格纳先生及其余诸位。 ‘Messes. Perkins, Wagner, et at.
‘先生们：印第安路线的问题很棘手，但若采取适当的灵活手腕和模棱两可的态度来处理，我坚信我们多少能想出些措施来，因为自去年冬天两位肖尼人酋长‘颓废报复’和‘食云者’在离开拉森草原的那条路线上被剥掉头皮，有些人就喜欢上了那些路线，可是另有些人由于种种原因更喜欢别的路线。摩门路线每天凌晨三点离开莫斯比,途经颌骨平地抵达布卢彻,而后到达壶把镇。路在镇的右侧,自然就经镇的右侧离开，而道森镇又在路的左侧，路线便沿镇的左侧行走，往前走就到了托马霍克镇，这样的路线比较省钱，沿途所有人都更容易上线，还兼顾了其他人的理想目的。因此，这样做达到了绝大多数人利益的最大化。所以，我才倍受鼓舞，认为问题是可以解决的。但是，如果你们希望进一步了解这个问题的更多信息，只要邮政部门提供给我相关信息，我将准备时常为你们效劳，且乐意之至。 ‘GENTLEMEN: It is a delicate question about this Indian trail, but, handled with proper deftness and dubiousness, I doubt not we shall succeed in some measure or otherwise, because the place where the route leaves the Lassen Meadows, over beyond where those two Shawnee chiefs, Dilapidated Vengeance and Biter-of-the-Clouds, were scalped last winter, this being the favourite direction to some, but others preferring something else in consequence of things, the Mormon trail leaving Mosby’s at three in the morning, and passing through Jaw bone Flat to Bluther, and then down by Jug-Handle, the road passing to the right of it, and naturally leaving it on the right, too, and Dawson’s on the left of the trial where it passes to the left of said Dawson’s and onward thence to Tomahawk, thus making the route cheaper, easier of access to all who can get at it, and compassing all the desirable objects so considered by others, and, therefore, conferring the most good upon the greatest number, and, consequently, I am encouraged to hope we shall.However, I shall be ready, and happy, to afford you still further information upon the subject, from time to time, as you may desire it and the Post-office Department be enabled to furnish it to me.
‘你们真诚的， ‘Very truly, etc.,
‘马克·吐温， ‘MARK TWAIN,
‘以参议员詹姆斯·W· N—的名义。’ ‘For James W. N—, U. S. Senator.’
“听听——你现在感觉如何？” “There—now what do you think of that?”
“唉，我不知道，先生。这——嗯，依我看——这已经够含糊的了。” “Well, I don’t know, sir.It—well, it appears to me—to be dubious enough.”
“含——你给我滚出去！我是被毁了。让他们伤透脑筋看了这么封不近人情的信，那些洪堡地区的蛮人绝不会饶了我。我失去了卫理公会的尊重，失去了市参议会议员的尊重——” “Du—leave the house!I am a ruined man.Those Humboldt savages never will forgive me for tangling their brains up with this inhuman letter.I have lost the respect of the Methodist Church, the board of aldermen—”
“好吧，这些我都无话可说，也许这两封回信我写得稍有偏差，但是鲍德温牧场那事我可有得说啊，长官！” “Well, I haven’t anything to say about that, because I may have missed it a little in their cases, but I was too many for the Baldwin’s Ranch people, General!”
“你给我滚！给我滚！永远永远别再回来！” “Leave the house!Leave it forever and forever, too.”
我想他这话是隐隐地暗示他不再需要我的帮助了，于是我辞了职。我再也不想做一名参议员的私人秘书了。你难以取悦那种人。他们什么都不懂。别人费尽心力，他们却并不赏识。 I regarded that as a sort of covert intimation that my service could be dispensed with, and so I resigned.I never will be a private secretary to a senator again.You can’t please that kind of people.They don’t know anything.They can’t appreciate a party’s efforts.
