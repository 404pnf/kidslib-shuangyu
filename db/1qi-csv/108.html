言语的力量 The Power of Words
作者：艾伦·坡 By Edgar Allan Poe
奥伊洛斯：阿加索斯，请原谅一个刚获得永恒的灵魂的软弱！ OINOS.Pardon, Agathos, the weakness of a spirit new-fledged with immortality!
阿加索斯：我的奥伊洛斯，你所说的话并不需要原谅。甚至在这里，知识也不是一种直觉的东西。要获取智慧，请随意向天使们讨教，他们可能会告诉你！ AGATHOS.You have spoken nothing, my Oinos, for which pardon is to be demanded.Not even here is knowledge a thing of intuition.For wisdom, ask of the angels freely, that it may be given!
奥伊洛斯：但是在这种存在中，我梦到我能在顷刻间知道一切，并因为无所不知而立刻感到很幸福。 OINOS.But in this existence, I dreamed that I should be at once cognizant of all things, and thus at once be happy in being cognizant of all.
阿加索斯：哦，幸福不在于知识，而在于获取知识！在不懈的求知中，我们永远受到祝福；但是无所不知则是魔鬼的诅咒。 AGATHOS.Ah, not in knowledge is happiness, but in the acquisition of knowledge!In for ever knowing, we are for ever blessed; but to know all were the curse of a fiend.
奥伊洛斯：但是难道上帝不是无所不知吗？ OINOS.But does not The Most High know all?
阿加索斯：（既然他是最幸福之人）那一定还有一件连他也不知道的事。 AGATHOS.That (since he is The Most Happy) must be still the one thing unknown even to Him.
奥伊洛斯：但是，我们的知识无时无刻不在增长，到最后，一切不都会为人知吗？ OINOS.But, since we grow hourly in knowledge, must not at last all things be known?
阿加索斯：请向下看那深不可测的远处——当我们的视线像这样缓缓地掠过星群——这样——像这样——请努力凝视那远处的繁星！即便是心灵的视野，不也在各个方向都被宇宙延绵不断的金墙所阻挡吗？——那些墙由无数闪闪发光的天体构成，似乎光是众多的数目就已经把它们联成一体了。 AGATHOS.Look down into the abysmal distances!—attempt to force the gaze down the multitudinous vistas of the stars, as we sweep slowly through them thus—and thus—and thus!Even the spiritual vision, is it not at all points arrested by the continuous golden walls of the universe?—the walls of the myriads of the shining bodies that mere number has appeared to blend into unity?
奥伊洛斯：我清晰地感觉到，物质的无穷并不是梦。 OINOS.I clearly perceive that the infinity of matter is no dream.
阿加索斯：天堂里没有梦——可是这里暗暗流传一种说法，无穷物质的唯一目的就是为灵魂提供无穷的源泉，从而减轻灵魂求知的渴望，这种渴望是不可抑制的——因为抑制这种渴望就等于毁灭灵魂自身。因此，我的奥伊洛斯，无所顾忌地向我提问吧，别害怕。来！我们将向左飞，离开昴星团喧嚣的和谐，从王座飞出，越过猎户星座，去那片繁星点缀的草地，那里有三色堇、紫罗兰，还有心安草，是三个一样的三色太阳的安歇之地。 AGATHOS.There are no dreams in Aidenn—but it is here whispered that, of this infinity of matter, the sole purpose is to afford infinite springs, at which the soul may allay the thirst to know, which is for ever unquenchable within it—since to quench it, would be to extinguish the soul’s self.Question me then, my Oinos, freely and without fear.Come! we will leave to the left the loud harmony of the Pleiades, and swoop outward from the throne into the starry meadows beyond Orion, where, for pansies and violets, and heart’s-ease, are the beds of the triplicate and triple-tinted suns.
奥伊洛斯：现在，阿加索斯，趁我们正在前进，教导我吧！用地球上那种熟悉的语调对我讲吧！对于“创造天地”（我们在人世间的时候习惯这样叫）的方式或方法，你刚才给了我暗示，但我没有理解这些暗示。你的意思是说，造物主不是上帝？ OINOS.And now, Agathos, as we proceed, instruct me!—speak to me in the earth’s familiar tones.I understand not what you hinted to me, just now, of the modes or of the method of what, during mortality, we were accustomed to call Creation.Do you mean to say that the Creator is not God?
阿加索斯：我的意思是说，上帝现在并不创造。 AGATHOS.I mean to say that the Deity does not create.
奥伊洛斯：请解释！ OINOS.Explain.
阿加索斯：他只是在开始的时候创造过。现在整个宇宙中不断涌现出各种创造物，这些表面上的创造物只能看作是上帝创造力的间接或不直接的产物，而不是直接的产物。 AGATHOS.In the beginning only, he created.The seeming creatures which are now, throughout the universe, so perpetually springing into being, can only be considered as the mediate or indirect, not as the direct or immediate results of the Divine creative power.
奥伊洛斯：我的阿加索斯，在人类当中，这种观点会被看作是极端的异教观点。 OINOS.Among men, my Agathos, this idea would be considered heretical in the extreme.
阿加索斯：我的奥伊洛斯，在天使中，这被视为绝对的真理。 AGATHOS.Among angels, my Oinos, it is seen to be simply true.
奥伊洛斯：对于你的话，我能理解到这种程度——我们所说的“大自然”或“自然规律”的某些作用，在一定条件下能够产生一些东西，这些东西具有创造物的所有外观。我清晰地记得，在地球最终毁灭前不久，有很多极其成功的实验，一些哲学家很没有说服力地将这些实验命名为微生物的创造。 OINOS.I can comprehend you thus far—that certain operations of what we term Nature, or the natural laws, will, under certain conditions, give rise to that which has all the appearance of creation.Shortly before the final overthrow of the earth, there were, I well remember, many very successful experiments in what some philosophers were weak enough to denominate the creation of animalculae.
阿加索斯：你所讲的情况实际上是第二创造的例子——也是自第一句话宣告第一条规律出现以来，唯一一种创造物的例子。 AGATHOS.The cases of which you speak were, in fact, instances of the secondary creation—and of the only species of creation which has ever been, since the first word spoke into existence the first law.
奥伊洛斯：阿加索斯，难道那些每时每刻都在从虚无的深渊冲入天际的星光闪闪的天体——难道这些星球，不是上帝直接创造的杰作？ OINOS.Are not the starry worlds that, from the abyss of nonentity, burst hourly forth into the heavens—are not these stars, Agathos, the immediate handiwork of the King?
阿加索斯：我的奥伊洛斯，让我努力地一步一步引导你到达我所指的概念。你深知，正如思想不会消亡，行动也有无穷无尽的后果。比如，我们在地球上居住时，动一动我们的手就会振动围绕在周围的空气。这种振动无限扩展，直到把冲力传给地球空气中的每一个粒子，从那以后直到永远，地球的空气都要受那只手的一次运动的影响。我们星球的数学家深知这个事实。事实上，他们利用经过精确计算的特殊冲力，使这种特殊效果作用于流体——这样就很容易测定一个已知大小的冲力具体会在多长时间内环绕地球，而且（永远）作用于周围大气层的每一个原子。颠倒实验顺序，他们发现，在特定的条件下，不难从一个已知的结果中测出初始冲力的值。现在，数学家们发现任何已知冲力的结果都是绝对无止境的，发现可以通过代数分析对这些结果中的一部分进行精确追踪，还发现逆向测定很方便；同时，这些人发现这种分析本身就带有一种无限进行下去的能力，发现这种分析的发展和应用没有可以设想得到的界限，除非受到其发展者或应用者才智的限制。可是这个时候，我们的数学家停了下来。 AGATHOS.Let me endeavor, my Oinos, to lead you, step by step, to the conception I intend.You are well aware that, as no thought can perish, so no act is without infinite result.We moved our hands, for example, when we were dwellers on the earth, and, in so doing, gave vibration to the atmosphere which engirdled it.This vibration was indefinitely extended, till it gave impulse to every particle of the earth’s air, which thenceforward, and for ever, was actuated by the one movement of the hand.This fact the mathematicians of our globe well knew.They made the special effects, indeed, wrought in the fluid by special impulses, the subject of exact calculation—so that it became easy to determine in what precise period an impulse of given extent would engirdle the orb, and impress (for ever) every atom of the atmosphere circumambient.Retrograding, they found no difficulty, from a given effect, under given conditions, in determining the value of the original impulse.Now the mathematicians who saw that the results of any given impulse were absolutely endless—and who saw that a portion of these results were accurately traceable through the agency of algebraic analysis—who saw, too, the facility of the retrogradation—these men saw, at the same time, that this species of analysis itself, had within itself a capacity for indefinite progress—that there were no bounds conceivable to its advancement and applicability, except within the intellect of him who advanced or applied it.But at this point our mathematicians paused.
奥伊洛斯：阿加索斯，为什么他们应该继续下去？ OINOS.And why, Agathos, should they have proceeded?
阿加索斯：因为还有一些重要的考虑。从他们所知道的东西能够推断，对于一个拥有无尽理解力的人来说——对他来说代数分析的完美已经展现出来——追踪作用于空气的每一次冲力以及穿越空气的以太应该不会有任何困难，他甚至能够追踪到任何无限遥远的时代中那最遥远的结果。的确可以证明，每一个作用于空气的这种冲力最终一定会作用于宇宙中的每一个事物；这位拥有无尽理解力的人——我们想象出来的人——也许会追踪这种冲力的遥远的波动，向上、向前追踪它们对一种物质所有粒子的影响，无止境地向上、向前追踪它们对旧形式的改造，或者换句话说，追踪它们对新形式的创造，直到他发现这些冲力最终没有发生作用，而是从上帝的宝座上被反射了回来。这个人不但能做这件事，而且在任何时代，只要给他一个已知的结果——比如将无数彗星中的一颗呈现在他面前，供他查看——他就能通过逆向分析不费吹灰之力地测定它是由什么初始冲力引起的。这种绝对完整、绝对完美的逆推能力——这种能把所有时代的所有结果都归结到各种起因的能力——当然是上帝一人独有的权力；但是在各个不同的程度上，在缺少绝对完美的情况下，这种能力本身被所有天使们的才智所用。 AGATHOS.Because there were some considerations of deep interest beyond.It was deducible from what they knew, that to a being of infinite understanding—one to whom the perfection of the algebraic analysis lay unfolded—there could be no difficulty in tracing every impulse given the air—and the ether through the air—to the remotest consequences at any even infinitely remote epoch of time.It is indeed demonstrable that every such impulse given the air, must, in the end, impress every individual thing that exists within the universe;—and the being of infinite understanding—the being whom we have imagined—might trace the remote undulations of the impulse—trace them upward and onward in their influences upon all particles of an matter—upward and onward for ever in their modifications of old forms—or, in other words, in their creation of new—until he found them reflected—unimpressive at last—back from the throne of the Godhead.And not only could such a being do this, but at any epoch, should a given result be afforded him—should one of these numberless comets, for example, be presented to his inspection—he could have no difficulty in determining, by the analytic retrogradation, to what original impulse it was due.This power of retrogradation in its absolute fulness and perfection—this faculty of referring at all epochs, all effects to all causes–is of course the prerogative of the Deity alone—but in every variety of degree, short of the absolute perfection, is the power itself exercised by the whole host of the Angelic intelligences.
奥伊洛斯：但是你仅仅谈到了对空气的冲力。 OINOS.But you speak merely of impulses upon the air.
阿加索斯：谈到空气，我指的仅仅是地球；可是这个总的陈述指的是对以太的冲力——以太四处弥漫，并且只有它遍布整个太空，因此它就是创造过程中的最大媒介。 AGATHOS.In speaking of the air, I referred only to the earth; but the general proposition has reference to impulses upon the ether—which, since it pervades, and alone pervades all space, is thus the great medium of creation.
奥伊洛斯：那么一切运动，不管是哪一种，都能创造吗？ OINOS.Then all motion, of whatever nature, creates?
阿加索斯：这是一定的，可是一位真正的哲学家早就教导我们，所有运动的源头是思想——而所有思想的源头则是—— AGATHOS.It must: but a true philosophy has long taught that the source of all motion is thought—and the source of all thought is—
奥伊洛斯：上帝。 OINOS.God.
阿加索斯：奥伊洛斯，我已经对你——就像对一个来自地球（刚刚毁灭的美好的地球）的孩子——讲了作用于地球大气层的冲力。 AGATHOS.I have spoken to you, Oinos, as to a child of the fair Earth which lately perished—of impulses upon the atmosphere of the Earth.
奥伊洛斯：你确实讲了。 OINOS.You did.
阿加索斯：难道在我讲述的时候，关于言语的自然力量的某些想法没有闪过你的脑海吗？每一个字不正是一道对空气的冲力吗？ AGATHOS.And while I thus spoke, did there not cross your mind some thought of the physical power of words?Is not every word an impulse on the air?
奥伊洛斯：但是，阿加索斯，为什么你要哭泣？为什么，哦，当我们在这个美好的星球上空盘旋时，你为什么垂下你的翅膀？这是我们在飞行途中所遇到的最绿意盎然但又最可怕的星球。它那些鲜艳的花朵看上去就像一个仙境般的梦——但是其凶猛的火山就像一颗骚动不安的心灵中的激情。 OINOS.But why, Agathos, do you weep—and why, oh why do your wings droop as we hover above this fair star—which is the greenest and yet most terrible of all we have encountered in our flight?Its brilliant flowers look like a fairy dream—but its fierce volcanoes like the passions of a turbulent heart.
阿加索斯：它们是这样的！——它们是这样的！这颗荒芜的星球——三个世纪前，我紧握双手，带着泪水，在我深爱的人脚边，用几句充满激情的话宣布它的诞生。它鲜艳的花朵是一切未圆之梦中最可爱的梦，它愤怒的火山是最骚动不安、最不敬神明的心灵中的激情。 AGATHOS.They are!—they are!This wild star—it is now three centuries since, with clasped hands, and with streaming eyes, at the feet of my beloved—I spoke it—with a few passionate sentences—into birth.Its brilliant flowers are the dearest of all unfulfilled dreams, and its raging volcanoes are the passions of the most turbulent and unhallowed of hearts.
