新婚的姊妹们 SISTERS OF THE GOLDEN CIRCLE
作者：欧·亨利 by O. Henry
观光车马上就要出发了。彬彬有礼的乘务员为兴高采烈的顶层乘客安排好座位。人行道上挤满了围观这些观光者的看客们，证明着那条自然法则：地球上的每一种动物都是另一种动物的猎物。 The Rubberneck Auto was about ready to start.The merry top-riders had been assigned to their seats by the gentlemanly conductor.The sidewalk was blockaded with sightseers who had gathered to stare at sightseers, justifying the natural law that every creature on earth is preyed upon by some other creature.
手持话筒的讲解员举起他折磨人的设备；这辆大汽车内部就像爱喝咖啡的人的心脏一样砰砰地震动起来。顶层乘客紧张地抓住座椅；一位来自印第安纳州瓦尔帕莱索的老妇人尖叫着要下车。但是，在车轮转动之前，透过心灵的听筒去听一段简短的开场白吧，它将为你指出人生观光旅途中的一件趣事。 The megaphone man raised his instrument of torture; the inside of the great automobile began to thump and throb like the heart of a coffee drinker.The top-riders nervously clung to the seats; the old lady from Valparaiso, Indiana, shrieked to be put ashore.But, before a wheel turns, listen to a brief preamble through the cardiaphone, which shall point out to you an object of interest on life’s sightseeing tour.
白人对生活在非洲荒原上的白人很快便能充分地理解和认识；母亲与婴儿之间的精神交汇在一瞬间即可得到确信；主人和狗可以即刻跨越动物与人类的细小鸿沟而进行沟通；一个人同爱人之间只消寥寥数语就能无比迅速而睿智地相互理解。但所有这些心有灵犀与思想共鸣的例子与接下来观光车上所要展示的事例相比，都只能算是迟钝的暗中摸索而已。你将会了解（倘若你之前还不清楚的话），在这世间的所有生物中，什么样的两个人能在面对面的时候最快地了解彼此的心灵。 Swift and comprehensive is the recognition of white man for white man in African wilds; instant and sure is the spiritual greeting between mother and babe; unhesitatingly do master and dog commune across the slight gulf between animal and man; immeasurably quick and sapient are the brief messages between one and one’s beloved.But all these instances set forth only slow and groping interchange of sympathy and thought beside one other instance which the Rubberneck coach shall disclose.You shall learn (if you have not learned already) what two beings of all earth’s living inhabitants most quickly look into each other’s hearts and souls when they meet face to face.
铜锣敲响，这辆闪耀整个纽约城的观光车庄严地开始了它那富有教育意义的旅程。 The gong whirred, and the Glaring-at-Gotham car moved majestically upon its instructive tour.
坐于最上面一层后排座位的是来自密苏里州科洛弗台尔的詹姆斯·威廉斯和他的新婚妻子。 On the highest, rear seat was James Williams, of Cloverdale, Missouri, and his Bride.
请把它加粗，排字工朋友——上句话的最后一个词——这个词代表领悟了爱与生活的一切言辞。花朵的芬芳、蜂蜜的香甜、初涌的泉水、云雀的婉转啼声、造物的鸡尾酒杯上盘旋的柠檬果皮——这便是这位新娘。妻子是神圣的，母亲是崇高的，夏日的少女是动人的——但新娘则是凡人成婚时上帝作为结婚礼物为新郎送来的保付支票。 Capitalise it, friend typo—that last word—word of words in the epiphany of life and love.The scent of the flowers, the booty of the bee, the primal drip of spring waters, the overture of the lark, the twist of lemon peel on the cocktail of creation—such is the bride.Holy is the wife; revered the mother; galliptious is the summer girl—but the bride is the certified check among the wedding presents that the gods send in when man is married to mortality.
观光车缓缓驶入金光大道。船长站在这艘大游艇的船桥上，对他的乘客大肆宣扬着这座大都市的胜景。张大着嘴、竖着耳朵的游人们，听着这些都会景观轰鸣着驶到他们眼前。他们带着兴奋不已的心情与土里土气的热望，既迷乱又激动，试图用眼睛来回应这场扩音器的仪式。在一片大教堂庄严的尖顶中，他们见到了范德比尔家族的宅邸；在大中央车站一带拥挤的建筑群中，他们惊奇地看到了拉塞尔·赛奇的陋居。他们被领去观看哈得孙河两岸的高地，却深信不疑地对着新挖掘的排水沟两边倒三角形的土堆目瞪口呆。很多人以为高架铁路就是百老汇剧场街，那些坐在车站门口身着制服的人正为人们检票。时至今日，许多身处边城的人们仍然相信，查克·康纳斯正诚心诚意地领导着改革，而且倘若不是一位姓帕克赫斯特的地方检察官在市政工作方面的高尚努力，臭名昭著的波特“主教”团伙就要把鲍厄里大街到哈莱姆河之间地区的法律与秩序全部破坏殆尽了。 The car glided up the Golden Way.On the bridge of the great cruiser the captain stood, trumpeting the sights of the big city to his passengers.Wide-mouthed and open-eared, they heard the sights of the metropolis thundered forth to their eyes.Confused, delirious with excitement and provincial longings, they tried to make ocular responses to the megaphonic ritual.In the solemn spires of spreading cathedrals they saw the home of the Vanderbilts; in the busy bulk of the Grand Central depot they viewed, wonderingly, the frugal cot of Russell Sage.Bidden to observe the highlands of the Hudson, they gaped, unsuspecting, at the upturned mountains of a new-laid sewer.To many the elevated railroad was the Rialto, on the stations of which uniformed men sat and made chop suey of your tickets.And to this day in the outlying districts many have it that Chuck Connors, with his hand on his heart, leads reform; and that but for the noble municipal efforts of one Parkhurst, a district attorney, the notorious “Bishop” Potter gang would have destroyed law and order from the Bowery to the Harlem River.
但我请你好好观察一下詹姆斯·威廉斯夫人——闺名是海蒂·查尔莫斯——曾经是科洛弗台尔的美人。如果新娘愿意，习俗上婚服应是浅蓝色的，而她正是选择了这一颜色。含苞的苔藓玫瑰心甘情愿地将自己的红润借予她的双颊——至于紫罗兰——多谢，不过她的双眸已经够美了。一条用处不大的白色司——哦不，司机正在驾驶这辆观光车呢——白色丝绸帽带——或者也许是丝毛或绢纱的吧——系在她的下巴下面，装成把帽子束缚在适当位置的样子。但你跟我都明白，那其实是帽针起的作用罢了。 But I beg you to observe Mrs. James Williams—Hattie Chalmers that was—once the belle of Cloverdale.Pale-blue is the bride’s, if she will; and this colour she had honoured.Willingly had the moss rosebud loaned to her cheeks of its pink—and as for the violet!—her eyes will do very well as they are, thank you.A useless strip of white chaf—oh, no, he was guiding the auto car—of white chiffon—or perhaps it was grenadine or tulle—was tied beneath her chin, pretending to hold her bonnet in place.But you know as well as I do that the hatpins did the work.
詹姆斯·威廉斯夫人的脸庞上，一部三卷的小型丛书记载了关于这个世界最美好的思想。卷一包括詹姆斯·威廉斯一贯是正确的这一信仰。卷二是一篇关于泛论世界的文章，声明它是一处绝妙的好地方。卷三表明了这样一个信念，那就是他们正坐在这辆观光车最高层的座位上，以非凡的速度行驶着。 And on Mrs. James Williams’s face was recorded a little library of the world’s best thoughts in three volumes.Volume No. 1 contained the belief that James Williams was about the right sort of thing.Volume No. 2 was an essay on the world, declaring it to be a very excellent place.Volume No. 3 disclosed the belief that in occupying the highest seat in a Rubberneck auto they were travelling the pace that passes all understanding.
詹姆斯·威廉斯，你大概已经猜到了，马上要二十四岁了。知道自己的猜测如此准确应该会令你感到欣慰吧。确切的说，他的年龄是二十三岁十一个月零二十九天。他体格健壮、充满活力，有着线条硬朗的下巴和善良的天性，对未来充满希望。他正在新婚旅行。 James Williams, you would have guessed, was about twenty-four.It will gratify you to know that your estimate was so accurate.He was exactly twenty-three years, eleven months and twenty-nine days old.He was well built, active, strong-jawed, good-natured and rising.He was on his wedding trip.
亲爱的仁慈的仙子，请别理会那些想要金钱，想要四十匹马力的旅行车，想要声名和脱发重生，想要轮船俱乐部所有权之类的请求吧。抛开那些，让时光倒一下带——哎，倒一下带，然后给我们重放一下哪怕只是一丁点儿的新婚旅行片段。就一小时，亲爱的仙子，这样我们就能记起那些青草白杨的模样，记起帽带如何在她的下巴下面束成蝴蝶结——尽管帽针才是起实际作用的。做不到？好极了，那就催一催那辆观光车赶紧把存油耗光吧。 Dear kind fairy, please cut out those orders for money and 40 H. P. touring cars and fame and a new growth of hair and the presidency of the boat club.Instead of any of them turn backward—oh, turn backward and give us just a teeny-weeny bit of our wedding trip over again.Just an hour, dear fairy, so we can remember how the grass and poplar trees looked, and the bow of those bonnet strings tied beneath her chin—even if it was the hatpins that did the work.Can’t do it?Very well; hurry up with that touring car and the oil stock, then.
詹姆斯·威廉斯夫人正前方坐着一个女孩，身着宽松的浅褐色上衣，头戴饰有葡萄和玫瑰的草帽。只有在梦中和女帽店铺里，哎呀！我们才能一下子把葡萄和玫瑰汇集在一起。当手持话筒的讲解员大声宣扬着他的学说：亿万富翁才是我们应当关注的群体，这个女孩深信不疑地用她蓝色的大眼睛注视着他。在一阵阵话筒冲击波的间隙，她通过咀嚼含有胃蛋白酶的口香糖这一方式，向爱比克泰德哲学寻求依靠。 Just in front of Mrs. James Williams sat a girl in a loose tan jacket and a straw hat adorned with grapes and roses.Only in dreams and milliners’ shops do we, alas! gather grapes and roses at one swipe.This girl gazed with large blue eyes, credulous, when the megaphone man roared his doctrine that millionaires were things about which we should be concerned.Between blasts she resorted to Epictetian philosophy in the form of pepsin chewing gum.
在这个女孩右边坐着一个大约二十四岁的年轻人。他体格健壮、充满活力，有着硬朗的下巴线条和善良的天性。不过倘若对他的描述看起来与詹姆斯·威廉斯的重复了，就把任何科洛弗台尔人特质的部分删去吧。这个男人属于充满艰险狡诈的街头巷尾。他目光锐利地环视四周，从他的位置居高临下地俯视身边的人群，仿佛他们踩在柏油马路上都令他不快。 At this girl’s right hand sat a young man about twenty-four.He was well-built, active, strong-jawed and good-natured.But if his description seems to follow that of James Williams, divest it of anything Cloverdalian.This man belonged to hard streets and sharp corners.He looked keenly about him, seeming to begrudge the asphalt under the feet of those upon whom he looked down from his perch.
当话筒冲着一座著名的旅馆大声叫嚷的时候，让我透过心灵的听筒小声地告诉你坐稳当点，因为这会儿事情就要发生了，而这座大城市将立刻把他们淹没，就像淹没那些飘落在百老汇空头们巢穴里的废弃电报纸条一般。 While the megaphone barks at a famous hostelry, let me whisper you through the low-tuned cardiaphone to sit tight; for now things are about to happen, and the great city will close over them again as over a scrap of ticker tape floating down from the den of a Broad street bear.
穿着浅褐色上衣的女孩转过去观察最后排的游客。其他乘客都已吸引过她的注意力了，只有她身后的座位仍像是蓝胡子的密室。 The girl in the tan jacket twisted around to view the pilgrims on the last seat.The other passengers she had absorbed; the seat behind her was her Bluebeard’s chamber.
她的目光与詹姆斯·威廉斯夫人的目光交汇了。在钟表滴答两声的时间内，她们便交换了彼此的生活经历、身世、希望与幻想。并且，请你注意，这一切都取决于眼神，换作两个男人的话，恐怕还没能决定是拔刀子还是借个火呢。 Her eyes met those of Mrs. James Williams.Between two ticks of a watch they exchanged their life’s experiences, histories, hopes and fancies.And all, mind you, with the eye, before two men could have decided whether to draw steel or borrow a match.
这位新娘低低地向前探下身去。她同这个女孩迅速地交谈着，她们舌头转动的速度之快就仿佛两条蛇的芯子——这个比方并没有继续引申下去的意思。两个微笑与一顿相互点头示意结束了这场谈话。 The bride leaned forward low.She and the girl spoke rapidly together, their tongues moving quickly like those of two serpents—a comparison that is not meant to go further.Two smiles and a dozen nods closed the conference.
此时，在这辆观光车前方宽阔而宁静的大街上，一个身着深色服装的男人举起手拦在那里。人行道上又有一个人匆匆来到他身边。 And now in the broad, quiet avenue in front of the Rubberneck car a man in dark clothes stood with uplifted hand.From the sidewalk another hurried to join him.
那个帽子装饰得硕果累累的女孩抓住她同伴的手臂，在他耳边耳语了几句。年轻人展示出他身手敏捷的能力。他低低地猫着腰，从车子一侧翻出去，有一瞬间还轻巧地攀在那里，随后便消失了。有六七个顶层的乘客默不作声地目睹了他这一不可思议的壮举，以为在这个光怪陆离的城市里这或许恰是合于习俗的下车方式，于是都谨慎地不让自己惊异的神情流露出来。这个半途离去的乘客避开一辆汉萨姆马车，随后像一片漂浮在溪流中的树叶一般从一辆装载家具的篷车和一辆花匠的运输货车间穿过。 The girl in the fruitful hat quickly seized her companion by the arm and whispered in his ear.That young man exhibited proof of ability to act promptly.Crouching low, he slid over the edge of the car, hung lightly for an instant, and then disappeared.Half a dozen of the top-riders observed his feat, wonderingly, but made no comment, deeming it prudent not to express surprise at what might be the conventional manner of alighting in this bewildering city.The truant passenger dodged a hansom and then floated past, like a leaf on a stream between a furniture van and a florist’s delivery wagon.
这个身穿浅褐色上衣的女孩又转过身去，望了望詹姆斯·威廉斯夫人的眼睛。随后，一个便衣警探亮了亮他大衣里面的警徽，观光车便停下了，而那个女孩一动不动地面向前方坐着。 The girl in the tan jacket turned again, and looked in the eyes of Mrs. James Williams.Then she faced about and sat still while the Rubberneck auto stopped at the flash of the badge under the coat of the plainclothes man.
“出什么篓子啦？”拿话筒的讲解员丢掉他那套职业性的纯正英语问道。 “What’s eatin’ you?” demanded the megaphonist, abandoning his professional discourse for pure English.
“把车先停一会儿，”警官命令道，“车上有个通缉犯——一个叫‘平基’麦奎尔的费城抢匪。他就在后排座位那儿。防着两侧，多诺万。” “Keep her at anchor for a minute,” ordered the officer.“There’s a man on board we want—a Philadelphia burglar called ‘Pinky’ McGuire.There he is on the back seat.Look out for the side, Donovan.”
多诺万走到后车轮那里，抬头望着詹姆斯·威廉斯。 Donovan went to the hind wheel and looked up at James Williams.
“下来吧，老兄。”他愉快地说，“你被捕了。准备回号子里去吧。尽管躲到观光车上倒不是个坏主意。我会记着这个的。” “Come down, old sport,” he said, pleasantly.“We’ve got you.Back to Sleepytown for yours.It ain’t a bad idea, hidin’ on a Rubberneck, though.I’ll remember that.”
话筒里传出乘务员轻柔的建议： Softly through the megaphone came the advice of the conductor:
“请您最好下车解释一下，先生。这辆车的行程必须得继续了。” “Better step off, sir, and explain.The car must proceed on its tour.”
詹姆斯·威廉斯是那种普通人。他带着不可避免的迟缓从乘客中挤过，走下汽车前面的阶梯。他的妻子跟在后面，但她首先转过视线去看那个逃逸的旅客从家具车后面溜走，躲在小公园边上一棵距此处不到五十英尺的大树后面。 James Williams belonged among the level heads.With necessary slowness he picked his way through the passengers down to the steps at the front of the car.His wife followed, but she first turned her eyes and saw the escaped tourist glide from behind the furniture van and slip behind a tree on the edge of the little park, not fifty feet away.
詹姆斯·威廉斯下到地面上，微笑地面对着他的拘捕者。他想着回到科洛弗台尔讲起自己曾被误认成一个抢匪会是一个绝妙的故事。观光车为遵从主顾们的意愿，迟迟未开。还有什么景象能比这更有趣呢？ Descended to the ground, James Williams faced his captors with a smile.He was thinking what a good story he would have to tell in Cloverdale about having been mistaken for a burglar.The Rubberneck coach lingered, out of respect for its patrons.What could be a more interesting sight than this?
“我的名字是詹姆斯·威廉斯，来自密苏里州的科洛弗台尔。”他和善地说道，以免他们感到过于窘迫，“我这里有些信件能够证明——” “My name is James Williams, of Cloverdale, Missouri,” he said kindly, so that they would not be too greatly mortified.“I have letters here that will show—”
“你得跟我们走一趟，请吧。”便衣警探宣布道，“‘平基’麦奎尔的相貌描述跟你完全对得上，就像在热肥皂水里洗过的法兰绒一样毫不走样。一个探员看见你在中央公园上了观光车，打电话来让我扣住你。到局子里再申辩吧。” “You’ll come with us, please,” announced the plainclothes man.“‘Pinky’ McGuire’s description fits you like flannel washed in hot suds.A detective saw you on the Rubberneck up at Central Park and ’phoned down to take you in.Do your explaining at the station-house.”
詹姆斯·威廉斯的妻子——他才两个星期的新娘——眼睛里闪出一种奇异的、温柔的光芒，脸上现出一丝红晕。她望着他的脸说道： James Williams’s wife—his bride of two weeks—looked him in the face with a strange, soft radiance in her eyes and a flush on her cheeks, looked him in the face and said:
“安静地跟他们去吧，‘平基’，也许会对你有好处呢。” “Go with ’em quietly, ‘Pinky,’ and maybe it’ll be in your favour.”
随后，当这辆闪耀纽约城的游览车咆哮着开动起来，她转过身去飞了一个吻——他的妻子飞了一个吻——冲着观光车上层座位上的某个人。 And then as the Glaring-at-Gotham car rolled away she turned and threw a kiss—his wife threw a kiss—at some one high up on the seats of the Rubberneck.
“你的女人给了你很好的建议，麦奎尔。”多诺万说，“现在，走吧。” “Your girl gives you good advice, McGuire,” said Donovan.“Come on, now.”
这下詹姆斯·威廉斯可给气疯了。他把帽子往后脑勺使劲一推。 And then madness descended upon and occupied James Williams.He pushed his hat far upon the back of his head.
“看起来我老婆以为我是个抢匪。”他不顾一切地说，“我从没听说她疯过；那就一定是我疯了。倘若我疯了，那我即便是在发疯的时候宰了你们这两个蠢货，他们也不能把我怎么样。” “My wife seems to think I am a burglar,” he said, recklessly.“I never heard of her being crazy; therefore I must be.And if I’m crazy, they can’t do anything to me for killing you two fools in my madness.”
于是他起劲地、奋力地拒抗逮捕；他们不得不吹哨唤来些警员，随后又叫来预备队驱散数千名兴奋的看客。 Whereupon he resisted arrest so cheerfully and industriously that cops had to be whistled for, and afterwards the reserves, to disperse a few thousand delighted spectators.
警局里值内勤的警员询问着他的名字。 At the station-house the desk sergeant asked for his name.
“麦蠢尔·平克，或者蛮子平基，我忘了是哪个了。”詹姆斯·威廉斯这样答道，“但你肯定我是个抢匪，别把这一条忘了。你大概还可以加上他们用了五个人才把品克制服这一条。我特别希望这条出现在记录里。” “McDoodle, the Pink, or Pinky the Brute, I forget which,” was James Williams’s answer.“But you can bet I’m a burglar; don’t leave that out.And you might add that it took five of ’em to pluck the Pink.I’d especially like to have that in the records.”
一个小时之后，詹姆斯·威廉斯夫人同麦迪逊大街的托马斯舅舅，带着证明主人公清白的证件，坐着令人肃然起敬的汽车来了——因为全世界都喜欢戏剧的第三幕有个汽车制造公司做背景。 In an hour came Mrs. James Williams, with Uncle Thomas, of Madison Avenue, in a respect-compelling motor car and proofs of the hero’s innocence—for all the world like the third act of a drama backed by an automobile mfg. co.
由于冒用了版权所有的抢匪的名号，詹姆斯·威廉斯被警方好一顿教训，而后便在警局权力所及的范围内被尽可能体面地开释了。之后,威廉斯夫人再次拘捕了他，一把把他拽到了警局的一角。詹姆斯·威廉斯只睁着一只眼睛看她。他总是说，有个人抓住了他灵活的右手，与此同时，多诺万把他另一只眼打得睁不开了。此前，他从未对她说过一句责备或埋怨的话。 After the police had sternly reprimanded James Williams for imitating a copyrighted burglar and given him as honourable a discharge as the department was capable of, Mrs. Williams rearrested him and swept him into an angle of the station-house.James Williams regarded her with one eye.He always said that Donovan closed the other while somebody was holding his good right hand.Never before had he given her a word of reproach or of reproof.
“你要是能解释一下，”他颇为强硬地开了头，“可为什么你——” “If you can explain,” he began rather stiffly, “why you—”
“亲爱的，”她打断他，“听我说。这对你不过是一个钟头痛苦的审问。我是为她这么做的——我是说那个在车上同我说过话的女孩。我那么幸福，吉姆——和你在一起我那么幸福，我不敢拒绝让其他人也享有这份幸福。吉姆，他们今天早晨才结婚的——他们俩；我想让他逃走。当他们跟你打作一团的时候，我看到他从藏身的树后穿过那个公园溜走了。就是这样，亲爱的——我不得不这么做。” “Dear,” she interrupted, “listen.It was an hour’s pain and trial to you.I did it for her—I mean the girl who spoke to me on the coach.I was so happy, Jim—so happy with you that I didn’t dare to refuse that happiness to another.Jim, they were married only this morning—those two; and I wanted him to get away.Jim, they were married only this morning—those two; and I wanted him to get away.That’s all of it, dear—I had to do it.”
就这样，一个笼罩在清晰的金色光环中的新婚的姐姐认出了另一个新婚的妹妹，一生中只有这极短暂的一次，施了魔法般的金光照耀着她们二人。只有大米和绸缎领结才能让男人意识到婚礼的存在。但新娘只消一眼就能够认出新娘来。她们以一种男人和孀妇无法领悟的语言在彼此间飞快地传递着慰藉与心意。 Thus does one sister of the plain gold band know another who stands in the enchanted light that shines but once and briefly for each one.By rice and satin bows does mere man become aware of weddings.But bride knoweth bride at the glance of an eye.And between them swiftly passes comfort and meaning in a language that man and widows wot not of.
