秃顶山峰——《客迈拉》的引子 Bald-Summit.—Introductory to @The Chimera@
尤斯塔斯·布赖特和他的同伴们沿着陡峭、树木繁茂的山坡往上爬。树上的叶子还没长满，但是那些小芽足以投下斑驳的阴影，在太阳的照耀下显得十分青翠。有些岩石长满了青苔，半露在旧的、暗棕色的落叶中；有些腐朽的树干横躺在很久以前倒下的地方；还有一些被冬季寒风吹下来的枯枝，被分散得到处都是。然而，尽管这些景象看起来十分古老，但树林整体的景致还是生机勃勃；因为，不论你怎样四处张望，总有一些清新、翠绿的东西映入眼帘，准备迎接夏天的到来。 Upward, along the steep and wooded hill-side, went Eustace Bright and his companions.The trees were not yet in full leaf, but had budded forth sufficiently to throw an airy shadow, while the sunshine filled them with green light.There were moss-grown rocks, half hidden among the old, brown, fallen leaves; there were rotten tree-trunks, lying at full length where they had long ago fallen; there were decayed boughs, that had been shaken down by the wintry gales, and were scattered everywhere about.But still, though these things looked so aged, the aspect of the wood was that of the newest life; for, whichever way you turned your eyes, something fresh and green was springing forth, so as to be ready for the summer.
终于，这伙年轻人到达了树林的上缘，这时他们发现已经快到山顶了。山顶并不是一个尖峰，也不是一个大而圆的山包，而是一片相当空旷的平地，像桌子一样平，不远处有一座房子和一个谷仓。那所房子里住着孤零零的一家人，常常在下雨时，或暴风雪刮下山的时候，云就会压得比这所荒凉而又孤单的房子还要低。 At last, the young people reached the upper verge of the wood, and found themselves almost at the summit of the hill.It was not a peak, nor a great round ball, but a pretty wide plain, or table-land, with a house and barn upon it, at some distance.That house was the home of a solitary family; and oftentimes the clouds, whence fell the rain, and whence the snow-storm drifted down into the valley, hung lower than this bleak and lonely dwelling-place.
山的最高处有一堆石头，石头中间插着一根长长的旗杆，它的顶端飘着一面小旗子。尤斯塔斯带着孩子们到那里，并命令他们环顾四周，看看一眼就可以领略到的广袤而美丽的世界。孩子们越看，眼睛张得越大。 On the highest point of the hill was a heap of stones, in the centre of which was stuck a long pole, with a little flag fluttering at the end of it.Eustace led the children thither, and bade them look around, and see how large a tract of our beautiful world they could take in at a glance.And their eyes grew wider as they looked.
南面的莫纽门特山仍然屹立在景色的中心，但似乎已经有点下沉和消退，所以现在它在一大群峰峦中一点也不起眼了。而莫纽门特山另一边的塔科尼克山看起来比以往更高大，更巍峨。美丽的湖泊，以及它所有的小湾和小支流都可以一览无余；不仅如此，还有两三个新湖泊正向太阳睁着蓝色的眼睛。远处分布着几个白色的村庄，每个村庄都能看到村内教堂的尖塔。还有许许多多的农舍和周围大量的林地、牧场、草地以及耕地，以至于孩子们已无足够的精力去欣赏它们。当然了，也有坦格尔伍德镇，迄今为止他们一直认为那是世界一个重要的城市。它现在只占据着非常小的一块地方，因此孩子们都往比它实际更远的地方看，在两侧仔细地找了半天，才发现了它的位置。 Monument Mountain, to the southward, was still in the centre of the scene, but seemed to have sunk and subsided, so that it was now but an undistinguished member of a large family of hills.Beyond it, the Taconic range looked higher and bulkier than before.Our pretty lake was seen, with all its little bays and inlets; and not that alone, but two or three new lakes were opening their blue eyes to the sun.Several white villages, each with its steeple, were scattered about in the distance.There were so many farm-houses, with their acres of woodland, pasture, mowing-fields, and tillage, that the children could hardly make room in their minds to receive all these different objects.There, too, was Tanglewood, which they had hitherto thought such an important apex of the world.It now occupied so small a space, that they gazed far beyond it, and on either side, and searched a good while with all their eyes, before discovering whereabout it stood.
天空中悬浮着羊毛般的白云，在大地上投下这一片那一片的阴影。但是，当阳光逐渐照在阴影出现的地方时，阴影就又跑到了其他地方。 White, fleecy clouds were hanging in the air, and threw the dark spots of their shadow here and there over the landscape.But, by and by, the sunshine was where the shadow had been, and the shadow was somewhere else.
向西望去，远处是连绵起伏的蓝色山脉，尤斯塔斯·布赖特告诉孩子们那是卡茨基尔山。他说，就在那些云雾缭绕的山峦中的一块地方，有几个老荷兰人在那里玩过一场持续很久的九柱戏游戏，还有一个叫里普·范温克尔的懒人在那里睡过觉，而且一觉竟睡了二十年。孩子们热切地恳求尤斯塔斯给他们讲讲这个神奇的故事。可是这个学生回答他们说，这个故事已经被别人讲过了，而且讲得十分完美，所以任何人都没有权利修改一个字，除非等到它像《戈耳戈的头》、《三个金苹果》以及其他传奇的神话故事一样历史悠久。 Far to the westward was a range of blue mountains, which Eustace Bright told the children were the Catskills.Among those misty hills, he said, was a spot where some old Dutchmen were playing an everlasting game of nine-pins, and where an idle fellow, whose name was Rip Van Winkle, had fallen asleep, and slept twenty years at a stretch.The children eagerly besought Eustace to tell them all about this wonderful affair.But the student replied that the story had been told once already, and better than it ever could be told again; and that nobody would have a right to alter a word of it, until it should have grown as old as “The Gorgon’s Head,” and “The Three Golden Apples,” and the rest of those miraculous legends.
“至少，”长春花说，“我们在这里休息、欣赏风景时，你可以给我们讲一个你自己的故事。” “At least,” said Periwinkle, “while we rest ourselves here, and are looking about us, you can tell us another of your own stories.”
“是啊，尤斯塔斯表哥，”报春花大声说道，“我建议你在这儿给我们讲个故事。找一个特别的或不同寻常的题目，看看你的想象力能不能跟得上。或许山上的空气能让你变得诗意呢一回呢。不管你的故事有多么奇怪，多么不可思议，既然我们到了山顶，置身云端，就什么都相信。” “Yes, Cousin Eustace,” cried Primrose, “I advise you to tell us a story here.Take some lofty subject or other, and see if your imagination will not come up to it.Perhaps the mountain air may make you poetical, for once.And no matter how strange and wonderful the story may be, now that we are up among the clouds, we can believe anything.”
“你相信吗，”尤斯塔斯问道，“从前有一匹马长着翅膀？” “Can you believe,” asked Eustace, “that there was once a winged horse?”
“相信！”调皮的报春花说，“但我觉得你恐怕永远都逮不住它吧。” “Yes,” said saucy Primrose; “but I am afraid you will never be able to catch him.”
“有你这句话，报春花，”这个学生反驳道，“我也许能逮住珀加索斯，而且还能骑到它背上，我还知道其他十二个人也能做到。不管怎样，这个故事是关于这匹马的，与世界各地相比，在一个山顶上讲它的故事最合适不过了。” “For that matter, Primrose,” rejoined the student, “I might possibly catch Pegasus, and get upon his back, too, as well as a dozen other fellows that I know of.At any rate, here is a story about him; and, of all places in the world, it ought certainly to be told upon a mountain-top.”
于是，尤斯塔斯坐在那堆石头上，孩子们则在石头的底部围坐了下来，他凝视着一片飘来的白云，开始讲起来。 So, sitting on the pile of stones, while the children clustered themselves at its base, Eustace fixed his eyes on a white cloud that was sailing by, and began as follows.

很久很久以前（我给你们讲的所有奇怪的事情都发生在没人能记得住的、很久以前的过去），在希腊那片神奇的土地上，有一股喷泉从一个山坡上涌了出来。也许，在过了许多个千年之后，它还是从同一个地方汩汩流出。总之，那里有一股欢快的喷泉，清新纯净的泉水汩汩流出，顺着山坡向下，在金色的落日晚霞中闪闪发光。此时，一个名叫柏勒洛丰的英俊青年走近泉边。他手里拿着一个马勒，上面镶满了光彩夺目的宝石，还配有金色的马嚼子。在泉边，他看到一个老人、一个中年人、一个小男孩，还有一个正用罐子打水的姑娘。他停了下来，请求姑娘给他喝点水提提神。 Once, in the old, old times (for all the strange things which I tell you about happened long before anybody can remember), a fountain gushed out of a hill-side, in the marvelous land of Greece.And, for aught I know, after so many thousand years, it is still gushing out of the very selfsame spot.At any rate, there was the pleasant fountain, welling freshly forth and sparkling adown the hill-side, in the golden sunset, when a handsome young man named Bellerophon drew near its margin.In his hand he held a bridle, studded with brilliant gems, and adorned with a golden bit.Seeing an old man, and another of middle age, and a little boy, near the fountain, and likewise a maiden, who was dipping up some of the water in a pitcher, he paused, and begged that he might refresh himself with a draught.
“这水真甜，”他喝完之后，一边涮了罐子，重新打满水，一边对少女说道。“请问您能告诉我这个泉是否有名字吗？” “This is very delicious water,” he said to the maiden as he rinsed and filled her pitcher, after drinking out of it.“Will you be kind enough to tell me whether the fountain has any name?”
“有，它叫碧莉妮泉，”姑娘说，然后她又补充道，“我的祖母告诉我这股清澈的泉水曾经是个漂亮的女人。自从她的儿子被女猎人狄安娜用箭射死后，她就整个人都化成了泪水。所以你喝的这清凉而又甘甜的泉水，实际上是那个可怜的母亲心中的悲伤！” “Yes; it is called the Fountain of Pirene,” answered the maiden; and then she added, “My grandmother has told me that this clear fountain was once a beautiful woman; and when her son was killed by the arrows of the huntress Diana, she melted all away into tears.And so the water, which you find so cool and sweet, is the sorrow of that poor mother’s heart!”
“我做梦也不会想到，”这位年轻的陌生人说道，“这么清澈的泉水，从阴凉处涌出流淌到阳光下，欢快地舞蹈，心中会有这么多的眼泪！那么，这就是碧莉妮吗？美丽的姑娘，谢谢你告诉我它的名字。我从很远的国家来，就是为了寻找这个地方。” “I should not have dreamed,” observed the young stranger, “that so clear a well-spring, with its gush and gurgle, and its cheery dance out of the shade into the sunlight, had so much as one tear-drop in its bosom!And this, then, is Pirene?I thank you, pretty maiden, for telling me its name.I have come from a far-away country to find this very spot.”
一个中年农夫（牵着他的牛来泉边饮牛）一直盯着年轻的柏勒洛丰和他手里漂亮的马勒。 A middle-aged country fellow (he had driven his cow to drink out of the spring) stared hard at young Bellerophon, and at the handsome bridle which he carried in his hand.
“朋友，如果你大老远来这儿只为找到碧莉妮泉的话，”他评论道，“那么你家乡的河道水位一定是在下降啊。不过，请问，你是不是丢了匹马啊？我看到你手里拿着马勒，而且有那两排闪亮的宝石装饰，它可真漂亮！如果马和这马勒一样出众，你丢了它真是太可惜了。” “The water-courses must be getting low, friend, in your part of the world,” remarked he, “if you come so far only to find the Fountain of Pirene.But, pray, have you lost a horse?I see you carry the bridle in your hand; and a very pretty one it is with that double row of bright stones upon it.If the horse was as fine as the bridle, you are much to be pitied for losing him.”
“我没丢过马，”柏勒洛丰微笑着说，“不过我正好在寻找一匹非常有名的马。智者告诉我，要是能找到它的话，一定是在这附近。你们知不知道那匹长着翅膀的马，珀加索斯，是否还像你们祖先时代那样，在碧莉妮泉水一带出没？” “I have lost no horse,” said Bellerophon, with a smile.“But I happen to be seeking a very famous one, which, as wise people have informed me, must be found hereabouts, if anywhere.Do you know whether the winged horse Pegasus still haunts the Fountain of Pirene, as he used to do in your forefathers’ days?”
可这时那个农夫笑了起来。 But then the country fellow laughed.
我年轻的朋友们，你们中也许有人听说过，珀加索斯是一匹雪白的骏马，背上长着一对银色而美丽的翅膀，它大部分时间都呆在赫利孔山上。它在空中飞翔时，就像天上的雄鹰一样直入云霄，狂野、敏捷且充满活力。世界上没有哪一匹马能比得上它。它没有伴侣，也从没有主人给它套马勒或骑它。许多年来，它过着孤单而又快乐的生活。 Some of you, my little friends, have probably heard that this Pegasus was a snow-white steed, with beautiful silvery wings, who spent most of his time on the summit of Mount Helicon.He was as wild, and as swift, and as buoyant, in his flight through the air, as any eagle that ever soared into the clouds.There was nothing else like him in the world.He had no mate; he never had been backed or bridled by a master; and, for many a long year, he led a solitary and a happy life.
啊，做一匹双翼飞马是一件多么幸福的事呀！它晚上在巍峨的高山上睡觉，白天大部分的时光在空中翱翔，珀加索斯看上去几乎不是一个地面上的动物。不论何时，当看到它在人们头顶上方的高空中，阳光洒在它银色的翅膀上，你就会觉得它属于天空。如果飞翔的高度低一些，你会觉得它在迷雾与水汽中失去了方向，正在寻找归家的路。如果你看到它纵身跃入灿烂而轻柔的云彩中并消失不见，一会儿又看到它从云的另一端冲出来，那情景真是十分漂亮。或者在阴沉的暴风雨天气里，空中乌云密布，有时双翼飞马会正好穿云而降，较高的区域会在它身后闪耀着欢乐的光芒。下一刻，珀加索斯和那欢快的光芒就会一同隐去，这是真的。但是如果有谁足够幸运，能看到这一神奇的景象，那么他就会在接下来的一整天都兴高采烈，而且比暴风雨持续的时间长得多。 Oh, how fine a thing it is to be a winged horse!Sleeping at night, as he did, on a lofty mountain-top, and passing the greater part of the day in the air, Pegasus seemed hardly to be a creature of the earth.Whenever he was seen, up very high above people’s heads, with the sunshine on his silvery wings, you would have thought that he belonged to the sky, and that, skimming a little too low, he had got astray among our mists and vapors, and was seeking his way back again.It was very pretty to behold him plunge into the fleecy bosom of a bright cloud, and be lost in it, for a moment or two, and then break forth from the other side.Or, in a sullen rain-storm, when there was a gray pavement of clouds over the whole sky, it would sometimes happen that the winged horse descended right through it, and the glad light of the upper region would gleam after him.In another instant, it is true, both Pegasus and the pleasant light would be gone away together.But any one that was fortunate enough to see this wondrous spectacle felt cheerful the whole day afterwards, and as much longer as the storm lasted.
夏天，在最美好的天气里，珀加索斯经常降落在厚实的大地上，收起它银色的羽翼，风一般地翻山越岭、嬉戏作乐。它被看到次数最多的地方就是在碧莉妮泉附近，饮着甘美的泉水，或是在泉边柔软的草地上来回打着滚。有时候，它也会吃几朵碰巧非常香甜的红花草的花（不过，珀加索斯对食物还是很讲究的）。 In the summer-time, and in the beautifullest of weather, Pegasus often alighted on the solid earth, and, closing his silvery wings, would gallop over hill and dale for pastime, as fleetly as the wind.Oftener than in any other place, he had been seen near the Fountain of Pirene, drinking the delicious water, or rolling himself upon the soft grass of the margin.Sometimes, too (but Pegasus was very dainty in his food), he would crop a few of the clover-blossoms that happened to be sweetest.
所以，人们的曾祖父辈们常有去碧莉妮泉的习惯（那时他们还年轻，对双翼飞马的存在深信不疑），他们都希望能看一眼美丽的珀加索斯。但是，近几年来，人们很少能看到它了。事实上，很多村民的住所离喷泉只有不到半个小时的路程，但他们从没见过珀加索斯，他们也根本不相信世界上有这样的生物存在。此时和柏勒洛丰说话的农夫恰好就是这样一个不相信飞马的人。 To the Fountain of Pirene, therefore, people’s great-grandfathers had been in the habit of going (as long as they were youthful, and retained their faith in winged horses), in hopes of getting a glimpse at the beautiful Pegasus.But, of late years, he had been very seldom seen.Indeed, there were many of the country folks, dwelling within half an hour’s walk of the fountain, who had never beheld Pegasus, and did not believe that there was any such creature in existence.The country fellow to whom Bellerophon was speaking chanced to be one of those incredulous persons.
这也是他为什么会笑的原因。 And that was the reason why he laughed.
“珀加索斯，真是！”他把扁平的鼻子努力扬起到最高点，大声地说，“珀加索斯，真是的！一匹长着翅膀的马，真是！哎呀，朋友，你神志清楚吗？马长翅膀干什么？你想想，有翅膀的马怎么能拉好犁呢？当然了，这也许会省点马掌钱。但是，有谁喜欢看到自己的马从马厩的窗户里飞出去呢？是啊，或者主人只想骑着马去磨房，而飞马却突然把他驮到了天上？不，不！我不相信会有珀加索斯飞马。世上绝不会有这种既是马又是鸟的荒唐东西！” “Pegasus, indeed!” cried he, turning up his nose as high as such a flat nose could be turned up,—“Pegasus, indeed!A winged horse, truly!Why, friend, are you in your senses?Of what use would wings be to a horse?Could he drag the plow so well, think you?To be sure, there might be a little saving in the expense of shoes; but then, how would a man like to see his horse flying out of the stable window?—yes, or whisking up him above the clouds, when he only wanted to ride to mill?No, no!I don’t believe in Pegasus.There never was such a ridiculous kind of a horse-fowl made!”
“我有理由有不同的想法。”柏勒洛丰平静地说。 “I have some reason to think otherwise,” said Bellerophon, quietly.
然后他转向一个头发花白的老人，老人正倚在一根拐杖上专心致志地听着，头向前倾，一只手放在耳朵上，因为近二十年来，他的耳朵越来越不好使了。 And then he turned to an old, gray man, who was leaning on a staff, and listening very attentively, with his head stretched forward, and one hand at his ear, because, for the last twenty years, he had been getting rather deaf.
“尊敬的先生，您有什么看法呢？”他问道，“我可以想象出，在您年轻时，您一定可以经常看见那长着翅膀的骏马！” “And what say you, venerable sir?” inquired he.“In your younger days, I should imagine, you must frequently have seen the winged steed!”
“唉，年轻的外地人，我的记性很差！”老人说，“如果我没记错的话，在我年轻时，我和大家一样都相信有这样一匹飞马。但现在，我几乎不知道该想些什么，更别说想起那匹飞马了。即便是我见过那东西，也是很久很久以前的事了。说老实话，我不确定自己真的曾经见过它。的确，在我还很年轻时，有一天，我记得在泉边看到过马蹄印子。可能是珀加索斯留下的，也可能其他的马踩的。” “Ah, young stranger, my memory is very poor!” said the aged man.“When I was a lad, if I remember rightly, I used to believe there was such a horse, and so did everybody else.But, nowadays, I hardly know what to think, and very seldom think about the winged horse at all.If I ever saw the creature, it was a long, long while ago; and, to tell you the truth, I doubt whether I ever did see him.One day, to be sure, when I was quite a youth, I remember seeing some hoof-tramps round about the brink of the fountain.Pegasus might have made those hoof-marks; and so might some other horse.”
“美丽的姑娘，你从没见过它吗？”柏勒洛丰问那个一直头顶着水罐听他们谈话的姑娘，“如果有谁能见着珀加索斯的话，你也一定能，因为你的眼睛如此明亮。” “And have you never seen him, my fair maiden?” asked Bellerophon of the girl, who stood with the pitcher on her head, while this talk went on.“You certainly could see Pegasus, if anybody can, for your eyes are very bright.”
“我觉得有一次我见着它了，”姑娘微笑着答道，脸还红了，“可能是珀加索斯，也可能是一只大白鸟，在很远很高的天空中飞着。还有一次，我带着水罐来泉边，听到一声马的嘶鸣。啊，那嘶鸣声如此欢快、如此优美！听到它的叫声，我激动得心跳不已。但是，那声音吓到我了，所以我没打水就跑回家了。” “Once I thought I saw him,” replied the maiden, with a smile and a blush.“It was either Pegasus, or a large white bird, a very great way up in the air.And one other time, as I was coming to the fountain with my pitcher, I heard a neigh.Oh, such a brisk and melodious neigh as that was!My very heart leaped with delight at the sound.But it startled me, nevertheless; so that I ran home without filling my pitcher.”
“那实在是可惜啊！”柏勒洛丰说道。 “That was truly a pity!” said Bellerophon.
然后他又转向那个我们在故事的开端就提到过的小男孩。他大张着红润的嘴巴正盯着柏勒洛丰，因为小孩子都喜欢盯着陌生人看。 And he turned to the child, whom I mentioned at the beginning of the story, and who was gazing at him, as children are apt to gaze at strangers, with his rosy mouth wide open.
“嗨，小朋友，”柏勒洛丰打趣地拽拽他的一缕卷发，大声说，“我觉得你经常能看见那匹双翼飞马。” “Well, my little fellow,” cried Bellerophon, playfully pulling one of his curls, “I suppose you have often seen the winged horse.”
“我见过的，”小孩非常迅速地回答，“我昨天还见它了呢，以前也见过好多次。” “That I have,” answered the child, very readily.“I saw him yesterday, and many times before.”
“你真是个了不起的小家伙！”柏勒洛丰边说边把孩子拉到他的身边，“来，都讲给我听听。” “You are a fine little man!” said Bellerophon, drawing the child closer to him.“Come, tell me all about it.”
“嗯，”小孩子回答道，“我经常来这里往泉水里放小船，还从水底收集漂亮的鹅卵石。有时候，在我低头看着水面的时候，我就能从天空在水中的倒影里看到双翼飞马的身影。我真希望它能落下来，把我驮在背上，带我到月亮上去！但是，如果我搅动一下想看看他的话，它就飞得远远的，无影无踪了。” “Why,” replied the child, “I often come here to sail little boats in the fountain, and to gather pretty pebbles out of its basin.And sometimes, when I look down into the water, I see the image of the winged horse, in the picture of the sky that is there.I wish he would come down, and take me on his back, and let me ride him up to the moon!But, if I so much as stir to look at him, he flies far away out of sight.”
柏勒洛丰完全相信这个说在水中看到过珀加索斯倒影的孩子，他也相信那个姑娘，她说她听到过飞马动听的嘶鸣声，他却不去理会那个只看重拉车的马的中年乡下人，还有那个忘掉自己年轻时代美好事物的老人。 And Bellerophon put his faith in the child, who had seen the image of Pegasus in the water, and in the maiden, who had heard him neigh so melodiously, rather than in the middle-aged clown, who believed only in cart-horses, or in the old man who had forgotten the beautiful things of his youth.
所以，后来他在碧莉妮泉边徘徊逗留了很多天。他时刻注意观察，一会儿向上看看天空，一会儿低头看看泉水，一心希望看到双翼飞马在水面的倒影或者它那神奇的真面目。他手握着马勒，时刻都准备着。马勒上面镶着闪闪发光的宝石和金色的嚼子。那些住在附近的乡下人，赶着牛来泉边饮牛，常常取笑可怜的柏勒洛丰，有的时候还会非常严厉地批评他。他们说，像他这样年轻力壮的小伙子应该做点像样的事情，而不是把时光浪费在追求无聊的事物上。他们提出如果他想要一匹马的话，可以卖给他一匹。当柏勒洛丰拒绝买的时候，他们又试图拿东西交换他漂亮的马勒。 Therefore, he haunted about the Fountain of Pirene for a great many days afterwards.He kept continually on the watch, looking upward at the sky, or else down into the water, hoping forever that he should see either the reflected image of the winged horse, or the marvelous reality.He held the bridle, with its bright gems and golden bit, always ready in his hand.The rustic people, who dwelt in the neighborhood, and drove their cattle to the fountain to drink, would often laugh at poor Bellerophon, and sometimes take him pretty severely to task.They told him that an able-bodied young man, like himself, ought to have better business than to be wasting his time in such an idle pursuit.They offered to sell him a horse, if he wanted one; and when Bellerophon declined the purchase, they tried to drive a bargain with him for his fine bridle.
甚至乡下的男孩子们也觉得他特别傻，拿他开很多玩笑。即使柏勒洛丰看到听到了，他们也仍旧非常粗鲁，一点也不在乎。比如，一个小淘气鬼会扮演珀加索斯，做飞翔的动作来开能想象得到的最搞怪的玩笑。这时，他其中的一个同学总会蹦蹦跳跳地追赶他，手里拿着一把灯心草，当做柏勒洛丰手里装饰性的马勒。可是那个从泉水里看到过珀加索斯倒影的乖巧小孩一直给这个年轻的外地人安慰，而这安慰远远超过了那些调皮孩子对他的折磨。那个可爱的小家伙，在他玩耍的时间里，常常陪坐在柏勒洛丰的身边，默默不语。他时而低头看看泉水，时而抬头看看天空。他的信念如此纯真，柏勒洛丰不由自主地深受鼓舞。 Even the country boys thought him so very foolish, that they used to have a great deal of sport about him, and were rude enough not to care a fig, although Bellerophon saw and heard it.One little urchin, for example, would play Pegasus, and cut the oddest imaginable capers, by way of flying; while one of his schoolfellows would scamper after him, holding forth a twist of bulrushes, which was intended to represent Bellerophon’s ornamental bridle.But the gentle child, who had seen the picture of Pegasus in the water, comforted the young stranger more than all the naughty boys could torment him.The dear little fellow, in his play-hours, often sat down beside him, and, without speaking a word, would look down into the fountain and up towards the sky, with so innocent a faith, that Bellerophon could not help feeling encouraged.
现在，你也许想知道为什么柏勒洛丰肩负捕捉双翼飞马的任务了吧。此刻正是个绝妙的机会，趁他在等待珀加索斯出现的这会儿，我们来讲讲这个问题。 Now you will, perhaps, wish to be told why it was that Bellerophon had undertaken to catch the winged horse.And we shall find no better opportunity to speak about this matter than while he is waiting for Pegasus to appear.
我要是讲柏勒洛丰以前全部的冒险经历，那这个故事可能就太长了。简单地说，在亚洲的某一个国家，出现了一个叫作客迈拉的凶猛怪兽，它所干的坏事多到从现在讲到太阳下山都讲不完。根据我所能获得的最翔实的描述，这个客迈拉，差不多是地球上出现的最丑陋、最恶毒、最古怪、最无法解释、最难打败和最难以让人逃脱的怪物。它的尾巴像蟒蛇，它的身体像什么，我倒不在乎；它有三颗分开的头，一个是狮子的头，一个是山羊的头，第三个是可怕巨蛇的头。它的三张嘴里都能喷出熊熊燃烧的火焰！它是一个地面上的怪物，我怀疑它可能没有翅膀。不过，不管它有没有翅膀，它跑起来既像山羊，又像狮子，扭动起来还像一条巨蛇。所以，它的速度相当于这三个动物的速度加在一起。 If I were to relate the whole of Bellerophon’s previous adventures, they might easily grow into a very long story.It will be quite enough to say, that, in a certain country of Asia, a terrible monster, called a Chimera, had made its appearance, and was doing more mischief than could be talked about between now and sunset.According to the best accounts which I have been able to obtain, this Chimera was nearly, if not quite, the ugliest and most poisonous creature, and the strangest and unaccountablest, and the hardest to fight with, and the most difficult to run away from, that ever came out of the earth’s inside.It had a tail like a boa-constrictor; its body was like I do not care what; and it had three separate heads, one of which was a lion’s, the second a goat’s, and the third an abominably great snake’s.And a hot blast of fire came flaming out of each of its three mouths!Being an earthly monster, I doubt whether it had any wings; but, wings or no, it ran like a goat and a lion, and wriggled along like a serpent, and thus contrived to make about as much speed as all the three together.
啊，这个恶毒的怪物所干的坏事真是太多，太多，太多了！它喷出来的火焰可以把森林点燃，可以把田地里的庄稼烧完，甚至可以把整个村庄的篱笆和房子都烧得干干净净。它把附近的整个村庄都变成了荒地，过去还经常把人和动物活活吞下去，再用它滚烫的、火炉般的胃烹煮他们。孩子们啊，愿上帝保佑我们，永远都不会遇到客迈拉！ Oh, the mischief, and mischief, and mischief that this naughty creature did!With its flaming breath, it could set a forest on fire, or burn up a field of grain, or, for that matter, a village, with all its fences and houses.It laid waste the whole country round about, and used to eat up people and animals alive, and cook them afterwards in the burning oven of its stomach.Mercy on us, little children, I hope neither you nor I will ever happen to meet a Chimera!
正当这个可恨的野兽（如果我们可以叫它野兽的话）做出以上这些恶劣的暴行时，碰巧柏勒洛丰来那个地方拜见当地的国王。国王叫伊俄巴忒斯，他统治的国家叫利西亚。柏勒洛丰是世界上最勇敢的年轻人之一，他唯一的愿望就是成就一番英勇而又有益的事业，可以让全人类敬佩他、喜欢他。那个时候，年轻人出人头地的唯一办法就是去打仗，不是和国家的敌人战斗，就是和邪恶的巨人或惹事的飞龙搏斗，如果实在找不到更危险的战斗，就与野兽打斗。国王伊俄巴忒斯了解到这个年轻客人的英勇，就建议他去和那个人人都望而却步的客迈拉搏斗，要是不尽早铲除它，利西亚很可能就会变成一片沙漠。柏勒洛丰毫不犹豫，立刻向国王承诺，不是他杀死这个可恶的客迈拉，就是在搏斗中牺牲自己。 While the hateful beast (if a beast we can anywise call it) was doing all these horrible things, it so chanced that Bellerophon came to that part of the world, on a visit to the king.The king’s name was Iobates, and Lycia was the country which he ruled over.Bellerophon was one of the bravest youths in the world, and desired nothing so much as to do some valiant and beneficent deed, such as would make all mankind admire and love him.In those days, the only way for a young man to distinguish himself was by fighting battles, either with the enemies of his country, or with wicked giants, or with troublesome dragons, or with wild beasts, when he could find nothing more dangerous to encounter.King Iobates, perceiving the courage of his youthful visitor, proposed to him to go and fight the Chimera, which everybody else was afraid of, and which, unless it should be soon killed, was likely to convert Lycia into a desert.Bellerophon hesitated not a moment, but assured the king that he would either slay this dreaded Chimera, or perish in the attempt.
但是，首先，这个怪物的速度快得惊人，他认为，如果徒步作战，他永远都不可能取胜。所以，最明智的办法就是要找到一匹全世界最出众的、跑得最快的马。而世界上有哪匹马能跑得比既有翅膀又有腿，在天空中比在陆地上还要活跃的非凡马匹珀加索斯一半快呢？当然了，许许多多的人都不相信有这种长着翅膀的马，都说关于它的故事是虚幻的胡说八道。虽然听起来有些神奇，但是柏勒洛丰还是相信珀加索斯是一匹真实存在的骏马，他希望自己能有足够的运气找到它，这样，一旦跨上马背，他就能在与客迈拉战斗的时候有更大的优势。 But, in the first place, as the monster was so prodigiously swift, he bethought himself that he should never win the victory by fighting on foot.The wisest thing he could do, therefore, was to get the very best and fleetest horse that could anywhere be found.And what other horse, in all the world, was half so fleet as the marvelous horse Pegasus, who had wings as well as legs, and was even more active in the air than on the earth?To be sure, a great many people denied that there was any such horse with wings, and said that the stories about him were all poetry and nonsense.But, wonderful as it appeared, Bellerophon believed that Pegasus was a real steed, and hoped that he himself might be fortunate enough to find him; and, once fairly mounted on his back, he would be able to fight the Chimera at better advantage.
这就是他手握着装饰得非常漂亮的马勒，从利西亚一路来到希腊的目的。那是个施过魔法的马勒。只要把金嚼子成功套进珀加索斯的嘴里，那飞马便会变得非常温顺，而且接受柏勒洛丰作为它的主人，飞向任何他想去的地方。 And this was the purpose with which he had traveled from Lycia to Greece, and had brought the beautifully ornamented bridle in his hand.It was an enchanted bridle.If he could only succeed in putting the golden bit into the mouth of Pegasus, the winged horse would be submissive, and would own Bellerophon for his master, and fly whithersoever he might choose to turn therein.
事实上，在等待珀加索斯的这段时间里，柏勒洛丰既疲倦又焦虑，他真希望珀加索斯能来碧莉妮泉边饮水。他还担心国王伊俄巴忒斯会以为他临阵逃跑了。他又想到那个怪物一定又干了不少坏事，而他自己非但没有与它搏斗，反而被迫坐在碧莉妮泉边无所事事，看着清澈的泉水从那闪闪发光的沙土里涌出来。想到这一切，他觉得十分痛苦。由于珀加索斯最近这些年来很少来这里，有人一辈子也很难见着它降落，所以柏勒洛丰担心等到双翼飞马出现的时候，他也可能已经老态龙钟了，胳膊早已没有力气，心中也再也没有勇气了。唉，当一个充满冒险精神的年轻人如此渴望成就他的事业，获取功名的丰收时，时间过得多么沉重啊！等待是多么艰难的一课！我们的生命短暂，但我们用了这么长时间才仅仅学会了这一点！ But, indeed, it was a weary and anxious time, while Bellerophon waited and waited for Pegasus, in hopes that he would come and drink at the Fountain of Pirene.He was afraid lest King Iobates should imagine that he had fled from the Chimera.It pained him, too, to think how much mischief the monster was doing, while he himself, instead of fighting with it, was compelled to sit idly poring over the bright waters of Pirene, as they gushed out of the sparkling sand.And as Pegasus came thither so seldom in these latter years, and scarcely alighted there more than once in a lifetime, Bellerophon feared that he might grow an old man, and have no strength left in his arms nor courage in his heart, before the winged horse would appear.Oh, how heavily passes the time, while an adventurous youth is yearning to do his part in life, and to gather in the harvest of his renown!How hard a lesson it is to wait!Our life is brief, and how much of it is spent in teaching us only this!
让柏勒洛丰感到欣慰的是那个乖巧的孩子越来越喜欢他了，老是陪着他，从来不觉得烦。每天清晨，那个孩子都会给他的心中注入一个新的希望，来取代昨天那个破灭了的希望。 Well was it for Bellerophon that the gentle child had grown so fond of him, and was never weary of keeping him company.Every morning the child gave him a new hope to put in his bosom, instead of yesterday’s withered one.
“亲爱的柏勒洛丰，”他总是充满希望地盯着他的脸，大声地说，“我觉得我们今天会看见珀加索斯！” “Dear Bellerophon,” he would cry, looking up hopefully into his face, “I think we shall see Pegasus to-day!”
总之，要不是这个小男孩毫不动摇的信念，柏勒洛丰早就放弃了所有希望，转身回到利西亚，在没有双翼飞马的帮助下使出浑身解数杀掉客迈拉。要是那样的话，可怜的柏勒洛丰至少也会被那个怪物呼出来的火焰烧伤，也有极大的可能被它杀死然后被吞掉。除非能骑上飞马，否则任何人都不应该尝试和出身在大地上的客迈拉进行搏斗。 And, at length, if it had not been for the little boy’s unwavering faith, Bellerophon would have given up all hope, and would have gone back to Lycia, and have done his best to slay the Chimera without the help of the winged horse.And in that case poor Bellerophon would at least have been terribly scorched by the creature’s breath, and would most probably have been killed and devoured.Nobody should ever try to fight an earth-born Chimera, unless he can first get upon the back of an aerial steed.
一天早晨，那个孩子和柏勒洛丰说话时比以往更加充满信心。 One morning the child spoke to Bellerophon even more hopefully than usual.
“亲爱的，亲爱的柏勒洛丰，”他大声地说道，“我说不出为什么，但我总觉得我们今天一定会见到珀加索斯！” “Dear, dear Bellerophon,” cried he, “I know not why it is, but I feel as if we should certainly see Pegasus to-day!”
整整一天，他一步都不愿离开柏勒洛丰，他们俩一起吃了一块干面包，喝了点泉水。下午，他们坐在一起，柏勒洛丰搂着小男孩，小男孩也亲热地把他的一只小手放在柏勒洛丰的手里。柏勒洛丰思绪万千，眼睛无神地盯着泉水上方的大树树干和缠绕在树枝上的葡萄藤。但是，那个乖巧的孩子却低头盯着泉水，他为柏勒洛丰感到难过。因为，今天的希望，像往常一样，又要破灭了。他无声地哭了，掉落的两三滴眼泪溶进了碧莉妮泉里，据说这泉水是碧莉妮为她被害的孩子而流下的眼泪。 And all that day he would not stir a step from Bellerophon’s side; so they ate a crust of bread together, and drank some of the water of the fountain.In the afternoon, there they sat, and Bellerophon had thrown his arm around the child, who likewise had put one of his little hands into Bellerophon’s.The latter was lost in his own thoughts, and was fixing his eyes vacantly on the trunks of the trees that overshadowed the fountain, and on the grapevines that clambered up among their branches.But the gentle child was gazing down into the water; he was grieved, for Bellerophon’s sake, that the hope of another day should be deceived, like so many before it; and two or three quiet tear-drops fell from his eyes, and mingled with what were said to be the many tears of Pirene, when she wept for her slain children.
可是，就在柏勒洛丰最想不到的时候，他感觉到男孩的小手使劲按了一下，还听到了他屏住呼吸的柔软低语： But, when he least thought of it, Bellerophon felt the pressure of the child’s little hand, and heard a soft, almost breathless, whisper.
“看那儿，亲爱的柏勒洛丰！水里有个倒影！” “See there, dear Bellerophon!There is an image in the water!”
年轻人低下头向波光粼粼的镜子似的水面望去，看到一个倒影，他以为是一只在高空飞翔的鸟的倒影，它有一双的雪白色或银白色的翅膀，在阳光下闪闪发光。 The young man looked down into the dimpling mirror of the fountain, and saw what he took to be the reflection of a bird which seemed to be flying at a great height in the air, with a gleam of sunshine on its snowy or silvery wings.
“真是一只了不起的鸟！”他说，“它看起来好大，尽管它肯定飞得比云还要高！” “What a splendid bird it must be!” said he.“And how very large it looks, though it must really be flying higher than the clouds!”
“它让我直哆嗦！”小孩轻声说，“我都不敢往天上看！它太漂亮了，我只敢在水中看它的倒影。亲爱的柏勒洛丰，难道你没看出来这不是鸟吗？它是双翼飞马珀加索斯啊！” “It makes me tremble!” whispered the child.“I am afraid to look up into the air!It is very beautiful, and yet I dare only look at its image in the water.Dear Bellerophon, do you not see that it is no bird?It is the winged horse Pegasus!”
柏勒洛丰的心剧烈地跳动起来！他仔细地抬头看着天空，却没看到什么长翅膀的生物，因为就在那一瞬间，它已经跃进了夏日里羊毛般的云层深处了。然而就在一瞬间，那家伙又出现了，从云朵里轻快地往下降，虽然离地面还很远。柏勒洛丰一把抱住小男孩就往后退缩，两人躲进了泉边茂密的灌木丛中。他倒不是害怕飞马会伤害他们，而是担心万一珀加索斯瞥见他们，会飞到很远的地方，降落到某个高不可攀的山顶上。原来它真的是那匹双翼飞马。他们等了它那么久后，它终于来碧莉妮泉饮水解渴了。 Bellerophon’s heart began to throb!He gazed keenly upward, but could not see the winged creature, whether bird or horse; because, just then, it had plunged into the fleecy depths of a summer cloud.It was but a moment, however, before the object reappeared, sinking lightly down out of the cloud, although still at a vast distance from the earth.Bellerophon caught the child in his arms, and shrank back with him, so that they were both hidden among the thick shrubbery which grew all around the fountain.Not that he was afraid of any harm, but he dreaded lest, if Pegasus caught a glimpse of them, he would fly far away, and alight in some inaccessible mountain-top.For it was really the winged horse.After they had expected him so long, he was coming to quench his thirst with the water of Pirene.
那空中的奇观越来越近，它在飞翔时画出巨大的圆弧，你可能见过鸽子降落，两者是一样的。珀加索斯绕着巨大的圈子从天而降，当它慢慢接近地面时，盘旋的圈子也越来越小了。他们越近距离看它，它看上去就越美丽，挥动的银色翅膀就越迷人。终于，它落地了，轻盈得都没有踏弯泉边的草，也没有在泉边的沙土上留下任何马蹄印，狂野的它俯下头，开始饮水。它吸了一口泉水，发出了长长的、欢快的嘶鸣，然后停顿下来，安静地品味了一会儿，又继续一口接一口地喝。因为，不管在地上还是天上，再也找不到比这碧莉妮泉更让珀加索斯喜欢喝的泉水了。它解渴之后，咬了几朵红花草的花蜜，细细地品味起来。但是它不打算饱餐一顿，因为在云朵下那巍峨的赫利孔山上，有比这普通的小草更适合它口味的牧草。 Nearer and nearer came the aerial wonder, flying in great circles, as you may have seen a dove when about to alight.Downward came Pegasus, in those wide, sweeping circles, which grew narrower, and narrower still, as he gradually approached the earth.The nigher the view of him, the more beautiful he was, and the more marvelous the sweep of his silvery wings.At last, with so light a pressure as hardly to bend the grass about the fountain, or imprint a hoof-tramp in the sand of its margin, he alighted, and, stooping his wild head, began to drink.He drew in the water, with long and pleasant sighs, and tranquil pauses of enjoyment; and then another draught, and another, and another.For, nowhere in the world, or up among the clouds, did Pegasus love any water as he loved this of Pirene.And when his thirst was slaked, he cropped a few of the honey-blossoms of the clover, delicately tasting them, but not caring to make a hearty meal, because the herbage, just beneath the clouds, on the lofty sides of Mount Helicon, suited his palate better than this ordinary grass.
在它心满意足地饮了泉水，又十分挑剔地屈尊吃了点食物后，由于无聊，想嬉戏一会儿，它开始前后跳跃起来，可以说是跳舞。世上没有比珀加索斯更贪玩的马了。它活蹦乱跳，我想起那情景来就高兴，它像朱顶雀那样轻轻地拍着它巨大的翅膀，马蹄一半着地，一半腾空，像在比赛似的小跑，我不知该说它是在飞翔还是在奔跑。当一个动物完全有能力飞的时候，有时它只为了娱乐才奔跑，珀加索斯就是这样的。尽管奔跑对于它来说有点小麻烦，因为它得让马蹄紧贴在地面上。此时，柏勒洛丰抓着小男孩的手，正在灌木丛中窥探。他心想，他从没见过如此美妙的情景，也从没见过有哪匹马的眼睛如珀加索斯的这样狂野而又充满生机。给它套上马勒，还骑在它背上，似乎单单想想这件事都是一种罪过。 After thus drinking to his heart’s content, and, in his dainty fashion, condescending to take a little food, the winged horse began to caper to and fro, and dance as it were, out of mere idleness and sport.There never was a more playful creature made than this very Pegasus.So there he frisked, in a way that it delights me to think about, fluttering his great wings as lightly as ever did a linnet, and running little races, half on earth and half in air, and which I know not whether to call a flight or a gallop.When a creature is perfectly able to fly, he sometimes chooses to run, just for the pastime of the thing; and so did Pegasus, although it cost him some little trouble to keep his hoofs so near the ground.Bellerophon, meanwhile, holding the child’s hand, peeped forth from the shrubbery, and thought that never was any sight so beautiful as this, nor ever a horse’s eyes so wild and spirited as those of Pegasus.It seemed a sin to think of bridling him and riding on his back.
有一两次，珀加索斯停下来，嗅嗅空气，竖竖耳朵，晃晃脑袋，四处看看，似乎有点怀疑有什么异样的情况。然而，它什么也没有看到，什么也没有听到，马上又开始玩耍起来。 Once or twice, Pegasus stopped, and snuffed the air, pricking up his ears, tossing his head, and turning it on all sides, as if he partly suspected some mischief or other.Seeing nothing, however, and hearing no sound, he soon began his antics again.
终于——不是因为疲倦了，只是因为悠闲到有点慵懒——珀加索斯收起了他的翅膀，躺在了柔软的绿色草皮上。但是它在空中生活得太久了，不习惯长时间保持安静。因此它很快又翻过身子，四条修长的腿朝空中翘着。它看起来真是美丽，这孤独的生物，上天从未给它创造伴侣，但是它根本不需要同伴，好几百年来，它也一直过着快乐的生活。它越是做着普通马的习惯动作，看上去就越超凡脱俗，神奇迷人。柏勒洛丰和男孩几乎屏住呼吸，一方面出于兴奋和敬畏，而更多的是因为他们害怕一点细微的动静或低语就会使它飞箭一般地冲上天，飞到蔚蓝色天空的最深处。 At length—not that he was weary, but only idle and luxurious—Pegasus folded his wings, and lay down on the soft green turf.But, being too full of aerial life to remain quiet for many moments together, he soon rolled over on his back, with his four slender legs in the air.It was beautiful to see him, this one solitary creature, whose mate had never been created, but who needed no companion, and, living a great many hundred years, was as happy as the centuries were long.The more he did such things as mortal horses are accustomed to do, the less earthly and the more wonderful he seemed.Bellerophon and the child almost held their breath, partly from a delightful awe, but still more because they dreaded lest the slightest stir or murmur should send him up, with the speed of an arrow-flight, into the farthest blue of the sky.
最终，当珀加索斯在地上打滚打够了，便翻过身子，和其他马匹一样，懒洋洋地伸出前腿，准备从地上站起来。而柏勒洛丰早就猜到它会这样做，猛地从灌木丛中冲了出来，跨到了它的背上。 Finally, when he had had enough of rolling over and over, Pegasus turned himself about, and, indolently, like any other horse, put out his fore legs, in order to rise from the ground; and Bellerophon, who had guessed that he would do so, darted suddenly from the thicket, and leaped astride of his back.
是的，他坐上去了，坐在了双翼飞马的背上！ Yes, there he sat, on the back of the winged horse!
可是对于珀加索斯，它平生第一次感觉到了一个凡人的重量，它蹦得很高！的确，蹦得非常高！柏勒洛丰还没来得及喘口气，他就发现自己已经身处500英尺的高空了，而且还在继续往上冲，那双翼飞马由于恐惧和气愤，不断地喷着鼻息，颤抖着。它还继续上升，上升，再上升，直到冲进了一团冰冷的裹着雾气的云朵里。而就是刚才，柏勒洛丰还凝望着这片云朵，幻想这里是个多么漂亮的地方。然而，珀加索斯又冲出了云中，像霹雳似的急剧下坠，仿佛要把自己和骑手同时使劲地撞向岩石。接着，它又翻滚腾跃无数次，没有一只鸟或是一匹马能像它那样狂野。 But what a bound did Pegasus make, when, for the first time, he felt the weight of a mortal man upon his loins!A bound, indeed!Before he had time to draw a breath, Bellerophon found himself five hundred feet aloft, and still shooting upward, while the winged horse snorted and trembled with terror and anger.Upward he went, up, up, up, until he plunged into the cold misty bosom of a cloud, at which, only a little while before, Bellerophon had been gazing, and fancying it a very pleasant spot.Then again, out of the heart of the cloud, Pegasus shot down like a thunderbolt, as if he meant to dash both himself and his rider headlong against a rock.Then he went through about a thousand of the wildest caprioles that had ever been performed either by a bird or a horse.
我给你们描述的连它一半的动作都不到。它快速地在空中掠过，时而向前冲，时而往边上撞，时而又向后退。它身体直立，前腿伸进一团雾里，后腿凌空。它把后腿向后乱踢，把头低俯到两个前腿中间，翅膀冲向上空。离地两英里的时候，它翻了个筋斗，使柏勒洛丰头朝下，脚朝上，而它也似乎正向下而非朝上地看着天空。它扭过头，看着柏勒洛丰的脸，两眼冒火，凶狠地想要咬他。它疯狂地扑扇翅膀，结果一根银色的羽毛落了下来，飘向地面，被那个男孩捡了去。为了纪念珀加索斯和柏勒洛丰，男孩把羽毛珍藏了一辈子。 I cannot tell you half that he did.He skimmed straight forward, and sideways, and backward.He reared himself erect, with his fore legs on a wreath of mist, and his hind legs on nothing at all.He flung out his heels behind, and put down his head between his legs, with his wings pointing right upward.At about two miles’ height above the earth, he turned a somerset, so that Bellerophon’s heels were where his head should have been, and he seemed to look down into the sky, instead of up.He twisted his head about, and, looking Bellerophon in the face, with fire flashing from his eyes, made a terrible attempt to bite him.He fluttered his pinions so wildly that one of the silver feathers was shaken out, and, floating earthward, was picked up by the child, who kept it as long as he lived, in memory of Pegasus and Bellerophon.
至于柏勒洛丰（也许你们已经判断出来了，他是最优秀的骑手），他一直在等待机会，最后终于把施过魔法的马勒上的金嚼子套进了飞马的嘴巴。他刚一套住飞马，珀加索斯立刻就变得非常温顺，仿佛它从小就是由柏勒洛丰喂大的。说句实话，看到这样一个狂野的动物突然变得这么驯服，几乎让人觉得悲哀。珀加索斯似乎也这样觉得。它回头看看柏勒洛丰，美丽的眼睛不再像刚才那样充满怒火，而是充满泪水。但当柏勒洛丰拍拍它的脑袋，对它说了几句命令、但却友好、安慰的话后，珀加索斯的眼睛闪过另一种神情。因为它内心很高兴，过了几百年孤独的生活，它终于找到一个同伴和主人了。 But the latter (who, as you may judge, was as good a horseman as ever galloped) had been watching his opportunity, and at last clapped the golden bit of the enchanted bridle between the winged steed’s jaws.No sooner was this done, than Pegasus became as manageable as if he had taken food, all his life, out of Bellerophon’s hand.To speak what I really feel, it was almost a sadness to see so wild a creature grow suddenly so tame.And Pegasus seemed to feel it so, likewise.He looked round to Bellerophon, with the tears in his beautiful eyes, instead of the fire that so recently flashed from them.But when Bellerophon patted his head, and spoke a few authoritative, yet kind and soothing words, another look came into the eyes of Pegasus; for he was glad at heart, after so many lonely centuries, to have found a companion and a master.
其实双翼飞马和所有其他狂野、孤独的动物是一样的。如果你能抓住它们，驯服它们，你就一定能赢得它们的喜爱。 Thus it always is with winged horses, and with all such wild and solitary creatures.If you can catch and overcome them, it is the surest way to win their love.
在珀加索斯竭尽全力想把柏勒洛丰从它背上甩下去的那会儿，它已经飞了很远的一段距离了。当柏勒利洛丰把金嚼子套进珀加索斯嘴里的时候，他们已经来到一座巍峨的大山附近。柏勒洛丰以前见过这座山，知道它的名字叫赫利孔山，双翼飞马就住在这山顶上。珀加索斯温顺地看看骑手的脸庞，似乎在请求他的允许。然后飞马飞往山顶，落下来之后耐心地等待柏勒洛丰下马。于是，年轻人从马背上跳下来，但仍然紧紧地抓着马勒。然而，当柏勒洛丰看到珀加索斯的眼睛，立即被它温柔的神情所打动，想到它以前一直过着自由自在的生活，如果它实在渴求自由，柏勒洛丰也不忍心束缚它。 While Pegasus had been doing his utmost to shake Bellerophon off his back, he had flown a very long distance; and they had come within sight of a lofty mountain by the time the bit was in his mouth.Bellerophon had seen this mountain before, and knew it to be Helicon, on the summit of which was the winged horse’s abode.Thither (after looking gently into his rider’s face, as if to ask leave) Pegasus now flew, and, alighting, waited patiently until Bellerophon should please to dismount.The young man, accordingly, leaped from his steed’s back, but still held him fast by the bridle.Meeting his eyes, however, he was so affected by the gentleness of his aspect, and by the thought of the free life which Pegasus had heretofore lived, that he could not bear to keep him a prisoner, if he really desired his liberty.
他一冲动便从珀加索斯头上大方地摘下了被施魔法的马勒，从它嘴里取下了金嚼子。 Obeying this generous impulse he slipped the enchanted bridle off the head of Pegasus, and took the bit from his mouth.
“去吧，珀加索斯！”他说，“要么就离开我，要么就喜欢我。” “Leave me, Pegasus!” said he.“Either leave me, or love me.”
就在那一瞬间，双翼飞马几乎就飞出了他的视线，从赫利孔山顶直入青天。太阳下山很久了，山顶上也已经是黄昏时分，暮霭笼罩着周围所有的地区。但是珀加索斯飞得太高了，它追上了消逝的白天，在上空沐浴着阳光。它越飞越高，看上去像一个明亮的小点，最终，消失在无尽的苍穹里。柏勒洛丰担心他永远也见不到珀加索斯了。但是，就在他为自己的愚蠢而痛惜不已时，那个明亮的小点又出现了，而且越来越近，直到远离了阳光。看啊，珀加索斯飞回来了！经过这场考验，他再也不用担心双翼飞马会逃跑了。飞马和柏勒洛丰成为了朋友，互相喜爱，互相信任。 In an instant, the winged horse shot almost out of sight, soaring straight upward from the summit of Mount Helicon.Being long after sunset, it was now twilight on the mountain-top, and dusky evening over all the country round about.But Pegasus flew so high that he overtook the departed day, and was bathed in the upper radiance of the sun.Ascending higher and higher, he looked like a bright speck, and, at last, could no longer be seen in the hollow waste of the sky.And Bellerophon was afraid that he should never behold him more.But, while he was lamenting his own folly, the bright speck reappeared, and drew nearer and nearer, until it descended lower than the sunshine; and, behold, Pegasus had come back!After this trial there was no more fear of the winged horse’s making his escape.He and Bellerophon were friends, and put loving faith in one another.
那天晚上，他们俩躺在一起睡觉，柏勒洛丰的胳膊搂着珀加索斯的脖子，这并不是出于谨慎，而是因为亲热。他们在天破晓时醒来，用各自的语言互道早安。 That night they lay down and slept together, with Bellerophon’s arm about the neck of Pegasus, not as a caution, but for kindness.And they awoke at peep of day, and bade one another good morning, each in his own language.
就这样，柏勒洛丰和那匹神奇的骏马呆了几天，越来越互相了解，越来越喜欢彼此。他们一直在天上旅行，有时飞得特别高，甚至觉得地球看上去比月亮大不了多少。他们去过很远的国家，那些国家的人民都非常吃惊，认为双翼飞马背上的英俊青年一定是天上下来的神仙。一天飞1000英里对于敏捷的珀加索斯来说简直易如反掌。柏勒洛丰很喜欢这样的生活，非常愿意永远过这样的日子，高高地飞在清净的大气中。因为不管下面的天气是死气沉沉还是阴雨连绵，上空的天气总是阳光灿烂。但他忘不了恐怖的客迈拉，他答应过国王伊俄巴忒斯要把它杀死。所以，最终当他对空中的马术烂熟于心，可以用最少的手势指挥珀加索斯，并教会了飞马听从他的声音时，他决定去尝试完成那充满冒险的壮举。 In this manner, Bellerophon and the wondrous steed spent several days, and grew better acquainted and fonder of each other all the time.They went on long aerial journeys, and sometimes ascended so high that the earth looked hardly bigger than the moon.They visited distant countries, and amazed the inhabitants, who thought that the beautiful young man, on the back of the winged horse, must have come down out of the sky.A thousand miles a day was no more than an easy space for the fleet Pegasus to pass over.Bellerophon was delighted with this kind of life, and would have liked nothing better than to live always in the same way, aloft in the clear atmosphere; for it was always sunny weather up there, however cheerless and rainy it might be in the lower region.But he could not forget the horrible Chimera, which he had promised King Iobates to slay.So, at last, when he had become well accustomed to feats of horsemanship in the air, and could manage Pegasus with the least motion of his hand, and had taught him to obey his voice, he determined to attempt the performance of this perilous adventure.
因此，第二天天刚破晓，他一睁开眼睛就轻轻地捏了下双翼飞马的耳朵，把它叫醒。珀加索斯立刻一跃而起，向上蹦了四分之一英里，又绕着山顶跑了一圈，以此来证明它已经非常清醒，准备好进行任何远行了。就在这次短暂的飞行中，它发出嘹亮、清脆、悦耳的嘶鸣，最后，它来到了柏勒洛丰的身边，像麻雀落在树枝上一样轻巧。 At daybreak, therefore, as soon as he unclosed his eyes, he gently pinched the winged horse’s ear, in order to arouse him.Pegasus immediately started from the ground, and pranced about a quarter of a mile aloft, and made a grand sweep around the mountain-top, by way of showing that he was wide awake, and ready for any kind of an excursion.During the whole of this little flight, he uttered a loud, brisk, and melodious neigh, and finally came down at Bellerophon’s side, as lightly as ever you saw a sparrow hop upon a twig.
“太棒了，亲爱的珀加索斯！太棒了，我的天行者！”柏勒洛丰一边喊，一边温柔地抚摸着飞马的脖子，“现在，我美丽而神速的朋友，我们得吃点东西了。今天，我们就要去和那个可怕的客迈拉搏斗了。” “Well done, dear Pegasus! well done, my sky-skimmer!” cried Bellerophon, fondly stroking the horse’s neck.“And now, my fleet and beautiful friend, we must break our fast.To-day we are to fight the terrible Chimera.”
他们一吃完早餐，就去灵泉喝了点清凉剔透的水。珀加索斯主动探出头，好让主人给它戴上马勒。接着，它表示已经等得不耐烦了，不断地欢腾，在空中不断地跳跃。与此同时，柏勒洛丰配上剑，脖子上套上盾牌，作好了战斗的准备。一切就绪之后，骑手跨上了飞马，（按照他的习惯，每次长途跋涉时）垂直往上飞五英里，以便看清楚应该向哪个方向前进。然后，他把珀加索斯的头转向东方，前往利西亚。在飞行途中，他们赶上了一只鹰，那鹰来不及避开他们，离他们特别近，柏勒洛丰甚至可以很容易地抓住它的腿。他们以这样的速度急速前进，大清早就看到了利西亚巍峨的高山以及草木丛生的深谷。如果人们对柏勒洛丰讲的是真话，那么那个恐怖的客迈拉就住在其中一个阴森的山谷里。 As soon as they had eaten their morning meal, and drank some sparkling water from a spring called Hippocrene, Pegasus held out his head, of his own accord, so that his master might put on the bridle.Then, with a great many playful leaps and airy caperings, he showed his impatience to be gone; while Bellerophon was girding on his sword, and hanging his shield about his neck, and preparing himself for battle.When everything was ready, the rider mounted, and (as was his custom, when going a long distance) ascended five miles perpendicularly, so as the better to see whither he was directing his course.He then turned the head of Pegasus towards the east, and set out for Lycia.In their flight they overtook an eagle, and came so nigh him, before he could get out of their way, that Bellerophon might easily have caught him by the leg.Hastening onward at this rate, it was still early in the forenoon when they beheld the lofty mountains of Lycia, with their deep and shaggy valleys.If Bellerophon had been told truly, it was in one of those dismal valleys that the hideous Chimera had taken up its abode.
由于他们马上要到目的地了，飞马便载着骑手慢慢地往下飞。为了隐蔽，他们藏在了几朵从山顶飘过的云朵里。他们徘徊在云朵的上面，从云端望去，柏勒洛丰可以清楚地分辨出利西亚山区的面貌，也可以立刻对所有阴暗的山谷一览无余。起初，他看不出有什么异样的地方。巍峨而陡峭的山上遍地荒凉，布满岩石。乡下比较平坦的地方，有一些房屋被烧毁后留下的废墟，在牛以前被喂养的牧场上四处躺着死牛。 Being now so near their journey’s end, the winged horse gradually descended with his rider; and they took advantage of some clouds that were floating over the mountain-tops, in order to conceal themselves.Hovering on the upper surface of a cloud, and peeping over its edge, Bellerophon had a pretty distinct view of the mountainous part of Lycia, and could look into all its shadowy vales at once.At first there appeared to be nothing remarkable.It was a wild, savage, and rocky tract of high and precipitous hills.In the more level part of the country, there were the ruins of houses that had been burnt, and, here and there, the carcasses of dead cattle, strewn about the pastures where they had been feeding.
“一定是客迈拉干的好事，”柏勒洛丰心想，“但这怪物能在哪里呢？” “The Chimera must have done this mischief,” thought Bellerophon.“But where can the monster be?”
我早就说过，第一眼看去，在陡峭的高山上，大大小小的山谷都没有发现什么特别。什么也没有。只是，其实有三股黑色的浓烟正往上冒，好像是从一个洞穴口出来的，阴沉地升入大气层。那三股烟还没有升到山顶就汇合成了一股。那个洞穴就在双翼飞马和骑手的正下方，大约有一千英尺远。那缓缓升入上空的滚滚浓烟，散发出一种恶臭的硫磺味，令人窒息，珀加索斯使劲喷鼻息，柏勒洛丰不停地打喷嚏。神马对于这种气味真是厌恶之极（它习惯了呼吸高空纯净的空气），它拍拍翅膀，蹦出半英里远，远离那令人作呕的气味。 As I have already said, there was nothing remarkable to be detected, at first sight, in any of the valleys and dells that lay among the precipitous heights of the mountains.Nothing at all; unless, indeed, it were three spires of black smoke, which issued from what seemed to be the mouth of a cavern, and clambered sullenly into the atmosphere.Before reaching the mountain-top, these three black smoke-wreaths mingled themselves into one.The cavern was almost directly beneath the winged horse and his rider, at the distance of about a thousand feet.The smoke, as it crept heavily upward, had an ugly, sulphurous, stifling scent, which caused Pegasus to snort and Bellerophon to sneeze.So disagreeable was it to the marvelous steed (who was accustomed to breathe only the purest air), that he waved his wings, and shot half a mile out of the range of this offensive vapor.
但当柏勒洛丰回头望的时候，看到了一个东西，他赶紧拽住马勒，让珀加索斯转了过来。他示意了一下，双翼飞马会意，慢慢地从空中降落了下来，直到马蹄离布满岩石的谷底不到一人高。前面就是只要扔一块石头就可以打到的洞穴口，那里一直冒出三股盘旋上升的浓烟。柏勒洛丰还看到了什么呢？ But, on looking behind him, Bellerophon saw something that induced him first to draw the bridle, and then to turn Pegasus about.He made a sign, which the winged horse understood, and sunk slowly through the air, until his hoofs were scarcely more than a man’s height above the rocky bottom of the valley.In front, as far off as you could throw a stone, was the cavern’s mouth, with the three smoke-wreaths oozing out of it.And what else did Bellerophon behold there?
洞里面似乎蜷缩着一堆奇怪而恐怖的动物。它们的身体挨得很近，柏勒洛丰无法一一辨认。但是，从它们的脑袋来看，一个是条大蛇，一个是头凶猛的狮子，第三个是头丑陋的山羊。狮子和山羊正在睡觉，蛇却很清醒，闪着怒火的眼睛一直在巡视着四周。然而——最神奇的是——那三股浓烟显然就是从那三个脑袋的鼻孔里喷出来的！这情景太离奇了，虽然柏勒洛丰一直在等待客迈拉的出现，但他还没立即反应过来，这就是那个恐怖的三头客迈拉。他已经找到了客迈拉的洞穴。那蛇、狮子还有山羊根本不是他想象中分开的三个动物，而是一个大怪物！ There seemed to be a heap of strange and terrible creatures curled up within the cavern.Their bodies lay so close together, that Bellerophon could not distinguish them apart; but, judging by their heads, one of these creatures was a huge snake, the second a fierce lion, and the third an ugly goat.The lion and the goat were asleep; the snake was broad awake, and kept staring around him with a great pair of fiery eyes.But—and this was the most wonderful part of the matter—the three spires of smoke evidently issued from the nostrils of these three heads!So strange was the spectacle, that, though Bellerophon had been all along expecting it, the truth did not immediately occur to him, that here was the terrible three-headed Chimera.He had found out the Chimera’s cavern.The snake, the lion, and the goat, as he supposed them to be, were not three separate creatures, but one monster!
这邪恶凶狠、令人憎恶的东西！尽管它三分之二的身体在睡觉，那令人厌恶的爪子里，还抓着一个吃剩下的不幸的小羊——也可能是（可我实在不愿这样想）一个可爱的小男孩——在那两颗头睡觉之前，被那三张嘴都咬过！ The wicked, hateful thing!Slumbering as two thirds of it were, it still held, in its abominable claws, the remnant of an unfortunate lamb,—or possibly (but I hate to think so) it was a dear little boy,—which its three mouths had been gnawing, before two of them fell asleep!
突然，柏勒洛丰如梦初醒，意识到这就是客迈拉。与此同时，珀加索斯似乎也明白了，发出一声嘶鸣，如同吹响了战斗的号角。听到声响，那三颗脑袋立马竖了起来，喷出了浓浓的火焰。没等柏勒洛丰来得及考虑下一步该怎么办，那个怪物已经冲出了洞穴，张开巨大的爪子，直扑过来，后面的蛇尾恶狠地扭动着。如果不是珀加索斯像鸟那样敏捷，它和它的骑手早就被客迈拉的迎头一扑掀翻在地了，那样的话，战斗还没开始就结束了。但是双翼飞马才不会这样容易被擒。一眨眼，它已经腾空而起，半悬空中，愤怒地直喷鼻息。它还哆嗦了一下，不是因为害怕，而是面对这个既恶毒又令人厌恶的三头怪物，感到极度厌恶。 All at once, Bellerophon started as from a dream, and knew it to be the Chimera.Pegasus seemed to know it, at the same instant, and sent forth a neigh, that sounded like the call of a trumpet to battle.At this sound the three heads reared themselves erect, and belched out great flashes of flame.Before Bellerophon had time to consider what to do next, the monster flung itself out of the cavern and sprung straight towards him, with its immense claws extended, and its snaky tail twisting itself venomously behind.If Pegasus had not been as nimble as a bird, both he and his rider would have been overthrown by the Chimera’s headlong rush, and thus the battle have been ended before it was well begun.But the winged horse was not to be caught so.In the twinkling of an eye he was up aloft, halfway to the clouds, snorting with anger.He shuddered, too, not with affright, but with utter disgust at the loathsomeness of this poisonous thing with three heads.
而客迈拉呢，尾巴着地，站了起来，它锋利的爪子在空中凶猛地乱抓，三颗脑袋对准珀加索斯和它的骑手直喷火焰。天哪，它大吼大叫，还嘶鸣不已！这时候，柏勒洛丰拿好盾牌，拔出宝剑。 The Chimera, on the other hand, raised itself up so as to stand absolutely on the tip-end of its tail, with its talons pawing fiercely in the air, and its three heads spluttering fire at Pegasus and his rider.My stars, how it roared, and hissed, and bellowed!Bellerophon, meanwhile, was fitting his shield on his arm, and drawing his sword.
“我亲爱的珀加索斯，此刻，”他在飞马耳边悄声说，“你一定要帮助我杀死这只令人无法忍受的怪物，要不然，你就丢下你的朋友柏勒洛丰，飞回到你那孤独的山顶吧。因为，不是客迈拉死，就是这个三头怪物咬下我这颗曾在你脖子上睡过的头！” “Now, my beloved Pegasus,” he whispered in the winged horse’s ear, “thou must help me to slay this insufferable monster; or else thou shalt fly back to thy solitary mountain-peak without thy friend Bellerophon.For either the Chimera dies, or its three mouths shall gnaw this head of mine, which has slumbered upon thy neck!”
珀加索斯哀鸣一声，转过头，用鼻子温柔地蹭了蹭骑手的脸颊。它这是用它特有的方式告诉柏勒洛丰，虽然它长着翅膀，是一匹长生不老的马，但是如果可以的话，它宁可死去，也决不会丢下柏勒洛丰的。 Pegasus whinnied, and, turning back his head, rubbed his nose tenderly against his rider’s cheek.It was his way of telling him that, though he had wings and was an immortal horse, yet he would perish, if it were possible for immortality to perish, rather than leave Bellerophon behind.
“谢谢你，珀加索斯，”柏勒洛丰回答道，“那么，我们向那个怪兽冲过去吧！” “I thank you, Pegasus,” answered Bellerophon.“Now, then, let us make a dash at the monster!”
话音刚落，他一拽马勒，珀加索斯就箭一般地斜冲了下去，扑向三头怪客迈拉，而客迈拉正一直使劲往上探它的身子。来到离怪物不到一臂之遥的地方，柏勒洛丰就挥剑砍向怪物，可是还没看清砍成功了没，骏马就带他向前飞了过去。珀加索斯又继续往前冲了一段距离，很快它调转方向，和客迈拉保持了和刚才一样的距离。珀加索斯这才发现，他几乎从怪物身上砍下了那颗山羊头，羊头跟身子只有一层皮连着，看上去已经奄奄一息了。 Uttering these words, he shook the bridle; and Pegasus darted down aslant, as swift as the flight of an arrow, right towards the Chimera’s three-fold head, which, all this time, was poking itself as high as it could into the air.As he came within arm’s-length, Bellerophon made a cut at the monster, but was carried onward by his steed, before he could see whether the blow had been successful.Pegasus continued his course, but soon wheeled round, at about the same distance from the Chimera as before.Bellerophon then perceived that he had cut the goat’s head of the monster almost off, so that it dangled downward by the skin, and seemed quite dead.
但是，为了补偿羊头的损失，那个蛇头和狮子头把死羊头的凶猛全部吸收了过来，喷着火焰，嘶嘶地叫着、狂吼着，比刚才更加凶猛。 But, to make amends, the snake’s head and the lion’s head had taken all the fierceness of the dead one into themselves, and spit flame, and hissed, and roared, with a vast deal more fury than before.
“没关系，我勇敢的珀加索斯！”柏勒洛丰大声说，“再来那么一次，我们就会让那嘶嘶声或怒吼声停止。” “Never mind, my brave Pegasus!” cried Bellerophon.“With another stroke like that, we will stop either its hissing or its roaring.”
他又拽了一下马勒。和刚才一样，双翼飞马箭一般地向客迈拉斜冲过去，就在俯冲交错的那瞬间，柏勒洛丰对准剩下的那两颗头使劲砍下去。但是这一次，他和珀加索斯都没有像第一次那么好脱身了。客迈拉的一个爪子在年轻人肩膀上深深地挖了一个口子，另一个爪子把骏马的左翼也略微抓伤了。柏勒洛丰呢，他给了怪兽致命的一剑，把那怪兽的狮子头砍了下来，狮子头耷拉下来，火焰几乎熄灭了，只冒着几股黑色的浓烟。但是，那个蛇头（唯一剩下的脑袋），比刚才要凶猛、恶毒两倍。它喷出的火焰有五百码那么远，嘶嘶声非常响亮，非常凶狠，震耳欲聋，连五十英里以外的国王伊俄巴忒斯都听到了，他哆嗦得连身下的宝座都一起颤抖起来。 And again he shook the bridle.Dashing aslantwise, as before, the winged horse made another arrow-flight towards the Chimera, and Bellerophon aimed another downright stroke at one of the two remaining heads, as he shot by.But this time, neither he nor Pegasus escaped so well as at first.With one of its claws, the Chimera had given the young man a deep scratch in his shoulder, and had slightly damaged the left wing of the flying steed with the other.On his part, Bellerophon had mortally wounded the lion’s head of the monster, insomuch that it now hung downward, with its fire almost extinguished, and sending out gasps of thick black smoke.The snake’s head, however (which was the only one now left), was twice as fierce and venomous as ever before.It belched forth shoots of fire five hundred yards long, and emitted hisses so loud, so harsh, and so ear-piercing, that King Iobates heard them, fifty miles off, and trembled till the throne shook under him.
“天哪！”可怜的国王心想，“客迈拉一定是来吃我了！” “Well-a-day!” thought the poor king; “the Chimera is certainly coming to devour me!”
此时，珀加索斯又停在半空中，愤怒地嘶鸣一声，他眼中纯净的、水晶般的火焰迸出了火星。和客迈拉那令人毛骨悚然的火焰相比真是大相径庭！神马的斗志全部被激发了起来，柏勒洛丰也一样。 Meanwhile Pegasus had again paused in the air, and neighed angrily, while sparkles of a pure crystal flame darted out of his eyes.How unlike the lurid fire of the Chimera!The aerial steed’s spirit was all aroused, and so was that of Bellerophon.
“我永生的骏马，你留血了吗？”年轻人不顾自己的伤痛，大声地问。他关心的是这匹非凡骏马的疼痛，因为这飞马是永远也不应该遭受痛苦的。“这个可恨的客迈拉，一定要让它最后一颗脑袋搬家，为它的恶毒赎罪！” “Dost thou bleed, my immortal horse?” cried the young man, caring less for his own hurt than for the anguish of this glorious creature, that ought never to have tasted pain.“The execrable Chimera shall pay for this mischief with his last head!”
接着，他一拽马勒，怒喝一声，指引着珀加索斯就向前冲。这次，他不再像前两次那样斜刺，还是直接从那怪兽可怕的正面冲杀了过去。这场攻击，速度惊人，令人眼花缭乱，一刹那，柏勒洛丰已经和敌人交战起来。 Then he shook the bridle, shouted loudly, and guided Pegasus, not aslantwise as before, but straight at the monster’s hideous front.So rapid was the onset, that it seemed but a dazzle and a flash before Bellerophon was at close gripes with his enemy.
这个时候，客迈拉已经失去了两颗头，它疼痛难忍，变得狂暴愤怒。它东蹦西蹿，一半在地上，一半在空中，简直无法说清它到底在哪里。它把它的蛇嘴张开得非常之大，令人厌恶，要我说，大到可以让张开翅膀的飞马和骑手一同飞进它的喉咙！看到他们冲了过来，它呼出一股巨大的火焰，把柏勒洛丰和他的骏马团团围住。烈火烧着了珀加索斯的翅膀，烧焦了年轻人一侧垂下的金色卷发，使他们从头到脚，都火烧火燎，难以忍受。 The Chimera, by this time, after losing its second head, had got into a red-hot passion of pain and rampant rage.It so flounced about, half on earth and partly in the air, that it was impossible to say which element it rested upon.It opened its snake-jaws to such an abominable width, that Pegasus might almost, I was going to say, have flown right down its throat, wings outspread, rider and all!At their approach it shot out a tremendous blast of its fiery breath, and enveloped Bellerophon and his steed in a perfect atmosphere of flame, singeing the wings of Pegasus, scorching off one whole side of the young man’s golden ringlets, and making them both far hotter than was comfortable, from head to foot.
但是同后面比起来，这真是微不足道。 But this was nothing to what followed.
当从天而降的飞马带着柏勒洛丰离客迈拉不足一百码的时候，客迈拉猛地一跃，它那巨大、笨重、有毒而且令人极度厌恶的身躯扑在了可怜的珀加索斯身上，它用尽力气缠住了飞马，还把蛇尾巴绕成了一个结！飞马一直往上飞，越飞越高，高过了山峰，高过了云层，几乎都快看不到坚实的地面了。但是，那个地面上的怪兽仍然紧缠不放，跟着一直生活在上空的飞马一起往上升。此时，柏勒洛丰转过头，发现他和客迈拉那张丑陋而狰狞的脸孔正对峙着，为了避免被烧死或被咬成两半，他举起了盾牌。越过盾牌的上边缘，他用犀利的眼神盯着怪物野蛮的双眼。 When the airy rush of the winged horse had brought him within the distance of a hundred yards, the Chimera gave a spring, and flung its huge, awkward, venomous, and utterly detestable carcass right upon poor Pegasus, clung round him with might and main, and tied up its snaky tail into a knot!Up flew the aerial steed, higher, higher, higher, above the mountain-peaks, above the clouds, and almost out of sight of the solid earth.But still the earth-born monster kept its hold, and was borne upward, along with the creature of light and air.Bellerophon, meanwhile, turning about, found himself face to face with the ugly grimness of the Chimera’s visage, and could only avoid being scorched to death, or bitten right in twain, by holding up his shield.Over the upper edge of the shield, he looked sternly into the savage eyes of the monster.
但是，客迈拉的疼痛使它发疯、抓狂，因此它没有像平时那样谨慎地保护自己。也许，跟客迈拉搏斗最好的方式，就是尽量去接近它。怪物正在使劲用它可怕的铁爪抓它的敌人，却把自己的胸膛暴露在外。意识到了这点，柏勒洛丰用尽全力挥剑刺进它凶残的心脏。打结的蛇尾巴立刻松开了。怪物松开了珀加索斯，从巨高的空中往下坠落。它胸中的火焰非但没有熄灭，反而比以前燃烧得更加猛烈，大火很快就开始吞噬那具尸体。于是，它就这样熊熊燃烧着，从天空中摔了下来，（当时地面上正好是黄昏）人们都误以为那是一颗流星或彗星。然后，第二天大清早，当一些村民去干活的时候，他们惊讶地看到好几英亩的土地上都撒满了黑色的灰烬。在一片土地的中央，有一堆白骨，比干草堆还要高。除此之外，那可怕的客迈拉再也没有留下别的痕迹！ But the Chimera was so mad and wild with pain, that it did not guard itself so well as might else have been the case.Perhaps, after all, the best way to fight a Chimera is by getting as close to it as you can.In its efforts to stick its horrible iron claws into its enemy, the creature left its own breast quite exposed; and perceiving this, Bellerophon thrust his sword up to the hilt into its cruel heart.Immediately the snaky tail untied its knot.The monster let go its hold of Pegasus, and fell from that vast height, downward; while the fire within its bosom, instead of being put out, burned fiercer than ever, and quickly began to consume the dead carcass.Thus it fell out of the sky, all a-flame, and (it being nightfall before it reached the earth) was mistaken for a shooting star or a comet.But, at early sunrise, some cottagers were going to their day’s labor, and saw, to their astonishment, that several acres of ground were strewn with black ashes.In the middle of a field, there was a heap of whitened bones, a great deal higher than a haystack.Nothing else was ever seen of the dreadful Chimera!
当柏勒洛丰取得胜利之后，他激动得热泪盈眶，俯身亲吻珀加索斯。 And when Bellerophon had won the victory, he bent forward and kissed Pegasus, while the tears stood in his eyes.
“回去吧！我亲爱的骏马！”他说，“回到碧莉妮泉去吧！” “Back now, my beloved steed!” said he.“Back to the Fountain of Pirene!”
珀加索斯从空中掠过，它飞得比以往任何时候都要迅速，很快就到达了碧莉妮泉。在那里，他看到拄着拐杖的老人、饮牛的村民和正在用罐子打水的漂亮姑娘。 Pegasus skimmed through the air, quicker than ever he did before, and reached the fountain in a very short time.And there he found the old man leaning on his staff, and the country fellow watering his cow, and the pretty maiden filling her pitcher.
“我现在想起来了，”老人说道，“我年轻的时候，看到过这匹双翼飞马。但它那时候可比现在漂亮十倍。” “I remember now,” quoth the old man, “I saw this winged horse once before, when I was quite a lad.But he was ten times handsomer in those days.”
“我有一匹拉车的马，顶得上三匹它这样的家伙！”那个村民说道，“如果这马是我的，我第一件事就把它的翅膀剪掉！” “I own a cart-horse, worth three of him!” said the country fellow.“If this pony were mine, the first thing I should do would be to clip his wings!”
但是那个楚楚可怜的姑娘什么也没说，因为她总是碰巧在不该害怕的时候害怕起来。所以她跑开了，水罐滚到地上摔破了。 But the poor maiden said nothing, for she had always the luck to be afraid at the wrong time.So she ran away, and let her pitcher tumble down, and broke it.
“那个乖孩子呢，”柏勒洛丰问道，“就是从前经常在这里陪着我、从不丧失信心、总是不知疲倦盯着泉水的那个？” “Where is the gentle child,” asked Bellerophon, “who used to keep me company, and never lost his faith, and never was weary of gazing into the fountain?”
“我在这呢，亲爱的柏勒洛丰！”小孩轻轻地回答道。 “Here am I, dear Bellerophon!” said the child, softly.
原来，这个小男孩每天都守在碧莉妮泉旁边，等待他的朋友归来。但是，当他看到柏勒洛丰驾着飞马，从云端下降，他就藏进了灌木丛。他是一个细腻且温柔的孩子，他害怕那老人和村民看到他泪流满面的样子。 For the little boy had spent day after day, on the margin of Pirene, waiting for his friend to come back; but when he perceived Bellerophon descending through the clouds, mounted on the winged horse, he had shrunk back into the shrubbery.He was a delicate and tender child, and dreaded lest the old man and the country fellow should see the tears gushing from his eyes.
“你打赢了，”他跑到柏勒洛丰膝旁，兴高采烈地说。柏勒洛丰那会儿仍然坐在珀加索斯的背上，“我知道你会赢的。” “Thou hast won the victory,” said he, joyfully, running to the knee of Bellerophon, who still sat on the back of Pegasus.“I knew thou wouldst.”
“是的，亲爱的孩子！”柏勒洛丰说着，从马背上跳了下来，“但是，如果没有你的信念帮助我，我永远也等不来珀加索斯，永远也不会飞到云朵上面，永远也不可能征服那个可怕的客迈拉。是你，我亲爱的小朋友，做到了这一切。现在，让我们给珀加索斯自由吧。” “Yes, dear child!” replied Bellerophon, alighting from the winged horse.“But if thy faith had not helped me, I should never have waited for Pegasus, and never have gone up above the clouds, and never have conquered the terrible Chimera.Thou, my beloved little friend, hast done it all.And now let us give Pegasus his liberty.”
于是，他从神奇的骏马头上取下了施过魔法的马勒。 So he slipped off the enchanted bridle from the head of the marvelous steed.
“自由了，你永远自由了，我的珀加索斯！”他大声地说，语气里有一丝伤感，“像你无拘无束地飞翔那样自由自在地生活吧！” “Be free, forevermore, my Pegasus!” cried he, with a shade of sadness in his tone.“Be as free as thou art fleet!”
可是，珀加索斯把脑袋依偎在柏勒洛丰的肩膀上，不愿意飞走。 But Pegasus rested his head on Bellerophon’s shoulder, and would not be persuaded to take flight.
“那好吧，”柏勒洛丰抚摸着神马说道，“你就跟着我吧，愿意跟多久就跟多久。我们一起去告诉国王伊俄巴忒斯，那个客迈拉已经被除掉了。” “Well then,” said Bellerophon, caressing the airy horse, “thou shalt be with me, as long as thou wilt; and we will go together, forthwith, and tell King Iobates that the Chimera is destroyed.”
柏勒洛丰拥抱了那个温柔的孩子，许诺以后一定回来看他，然后就离开了。但是，在后来的那些岁月里，那个孩子乘着飞马遨游天际，比柏勒洛丰还要飞得高远，做出了比他的朋友战胜客迈拉还要光荣的事迹。因为，他虽然文静、温柔，但他成为了一名伟大的诗人！ Then Bellerophon embraced the gentle child, and promised to come to him again, and departed.But, in after years, that child took higher flights upon the aerial steed than ever did Bellerophon, and achieved more honorable deeds than his friend’s victory over the Chimera.For, gentle and tender as he was, he grew to be a mighty poet!
秃顶山峰——故事讲完以后 Bald-Summit.—After the Story
尤斯塔斯·布赖特激情飞扬地讲述着柏勒洛丰的神话，仿佛他真的亲自骑过双翼飞马疾驰。讲完故事后，他感到很满足。因为他从听众热情洋溢的脸上看出，他们刚才都听得兴趣盎然。除了报春花之外，所有人的眼睛都大放光彩。她的眼睛里明显有眼泪，因为她从神话里有所感悟，而其他孩子都因太小还无法体会。虽然这是个儿童故事，可是这个学生却设法从中读出了激情、无限的希望和年轻人对理想的进取之心。 Eustace Bright told the legend of Bellerophon with as much fervor and animation as if he had really been taking a gallop on the winged horse.At the conclusion, he was gratified to discern, by the glowing countenances of his auditors, how greatly they had been interested.All their eyes were dancing in their heads, except those of Primrose.In her eyes there were positively tears; for she was conscious of something in the legend which the rest of them were not yet old enough to feel.Child’s story as it was, the student had contrived to breathe through it the ardor, the generous hope, and the imaginative enterprise of youth.
“现在，我原谅你了，报春花，”他说，“尽管你一直取笑我和我的故事。可是你的一滴泪就把嘲笑全弥补回来了。” “I forgive you, now, Primrose,” said he, “for all your ridicule of myself and my stories.One tear pays for a great deal of laughter.”
“哼，布赖特先生，”报春花擦了擦眼泪，又朝他调皮地笑了一下，回答道，“你把脑袋升到了云层上面，当然能提高你的思想了。我建议，以后除非像现在一样在山顶上，不然你就别讲故事了。” “Well, Mr. Bright,” answered Primrose, wiping her eyes, and giving him another of her mischievous smiles, “it certainly does elevate your ideas, to get your head above the clouds.I advise you never to tell another story, unless it be, as at present, from the top of a mountain.”
“或者骑在珀加索斯的背上。”尤斯塔斯笑着回答说，“你不觉得我非常成功地把飞马抓住了吗？” “Or from the back of Pegasus,” replied Eustace, laughing.“Don’t you think that I succeeded pretty well in catching that wonderful pony?”
“这真像你那些鲁莽的恶作剧！”报春花拍着手大声说，“我觉得我现在就看得到你骑在马背上，飞了两英里高，不过是脑袋朝下！幸好你只在稳重的戴维或老百岁背上展示你的马术，还没有机会真正地骑在更狂野的骏马身上。” “It was so like one of your madcap pranks!” cried Primrose, clapping her hands.“I think I see you now on his back, two miles high, and with your head downward!It is well that you have not really an opportunity of trying your horsemanship on any wilder steed than our sober Davy, or Old Hundred.”
“对我来说，我倒希望珀加索斯此时此刻就在这儿，”大学生说道，“我一定立刻就骑上去，在附近方圆几英里的地方，策马奔腾，对我的作家兄弟做文学访问。我可以去拜访住在塔科尼克山脚下的杜威博士。在远处的斯托克布里奇，住着詹姆斯先生，他写过的历史和传奇小说有山那么高，令他闻名于世。我想朗费罗现在没住在牛脖套，否则飞马见到他也会嘶鸣的。但在莱诺克斯，我应该可以找到我们最真实的小说家，因为她的素材都取自伯克郡的景物和生活。在皮茨菲尔德这边，坐着赫尔曼·梅尔维尔，他正在构思他的伟大著作《白鲸》，书房的窗户外面隐约可以看到巍峨的格雷洛克山。我的飞马再一蹦，就把我带到了霍姆斯的家门口。我最后才提他，是因为珀加索斯一定会把我掀翻在地，立刻就认这个诗人是它的主人。” “For my part, I wish I had Pegasus here, at this moment,” said the student.“I would mount him forthwith, and gallop about the country, within a circumference of a few miles, making literary calls on my brother-authors.Dr. Dewey would be within my reach, at the foot of Taconic.In Stockbridge, yonder, is Mr. James, conspicuous to all the world on his mountain-pile of history and romance.Longfellow, I believe, is not yet at the Ox-bow, else the winged horse would neigh at the sight of him.But, here in Lenox, I should find our most truthful novelist, who has made the scenery and life of Berkshire all her own.On the hither side of Pittsfield sits Herman Melville, shaping out the gigantic conception of his ‘White Whale,’ while the gigantic shape of Graylock looms upon him from his study-window.Another bound of my flying steed would bring me to the door of Holmes, whom I mention last, because Pegasus would certainly unseat me, the next minute, and claim the poet as his rider.”
“我们邻居不也是个作家吗？”报春花问道，“那个少言寡语的人，住在坦格尔伍德大街附近的一幢古老的红房子里，我们有时候能在树林里或湖边遇到他，还有两个孩子跟着他。我听说他写过一首诗，或是传奇小说，或是算术，要么是学校历史书，要么就是别的什么书。” “Have we not an author for our next neighbor?” asked Primrose.“That silent man, who lives in the old red house, near Tanglewood Avenue, and whom we sometimes meet, with two children at his side, in the woods or at the lake.I think I have heard of his having written a poem, or a romance, or an arithmetic, or a school-history, or some other kind of a book.”
“嘘，报春花，嘘！”尤斯塔斯把手指挡在嘴唇前，十分激动地悄声说，“千万别谈论那个人，在山顶上也不行！如果我们的闲言碎语传到他的耳朵里，碰巧他又不喜欢这样的话，他只要往火炉里扔一两张纸，那么你报春花、我，还有长春花、香蕨木、南瓜花、蓝眼睛、越橘、红花草、黄花九轮草、车前草、木克草、马利筋、蒲公英和毛莨——对，还有对我的神话评价不高的聪明的普林格尔先生和他可怜的太太——都会化成烟雾，忽地从烟囱里飞出去了！据我所知，住在红房子里的邻居对世界上其他人来说，毫不危险。但是，有东西悄悄地告诉我，他有可怕的力量，能控制我们，可以把我们都毁灭。” “Hush, Primrose, hush!” exclaimed Eustace, in a thrilling whisper, and putting his finger on his lip.“Not a word about that man, even on a hill-top!If our babble were to reach his ears, and happen not to please him, he has but to fling a quire or two of paper into the stove, and you, Primrose, and I, and Periwinkle, Sweet Fern, Squash-Blossom, Blue Eye, Huckleberry, Clover, Cowslip, Plantain, Milkweed, Dandelion, and Buttercup,—yes, and wise Mr. Pringle, with his unfavorable criticisms on my legends, and poor Mrs. Pringle, too,—would all turn to smoke, and go whisking up the funnel!Our neighbor in the red house is a harmless sort of person enough, for aught I know, as concerns the rest of the world; but something whispers to me that he has a terrible power over ourselves, extending to nothing short of annihilation.”
“那坦格尔伍德会不会也像我们一样化成灰？”长春花对于这个可能降临的灾难感到非常害怕，问道，“本和布鲁因会怎么样？” “And would Tanglewood turn to smoke, as well as we?” asked Periwinkle, quite appalled at the threatened destruction.“And what would become of Ben and Bruin?”
“坦格尔伍德没事儿，”大学生回答道，“会和现在一模一样，只不过，会住着另外一些人。本和布鲁因还会活着，吃着餐桌上剩下的骨头，过得很舒服，从来也不会想起它们和我们在一起度过的美好时光！” “Tanglewood would remain,” replied the student, “looking just as it does now, but occupied by an entirely different family.And Ben and Bruin would be still alive, and would make themselves very comfortable with the bones from the dinner-table, without ever thinking of the good times which they and we have had together!”
“你在胡说八道什么呀！”报春花嚷道。 “What nonsense you are talking!” exclaimed Primrose.
说笑着，这伙人已经开始下山了，钻进了树林的阴凉里。报春花采了一些山月桂，那些叶子还是去年长的，但仍然翠绿而新鲜，似乎冰雪和解冻都没有对它们造成伤害。她用月桂编了一个花环，摘掉那个学生的帽子，把花环戴在了他头上。 With idle chat of this kind, the party had already begun to descend the hill, and were now within the shadow of the woods.Primrose gathered some mountain-laurel, the leaf of which, though of last year’s growth, was still as verdant and elastic as if the frost and thaw had not alternately tried their force upon its texture.Of these twigs of laurel she twined a wreath, and took off the student’s cap, in order to place it on his brow.
“别人都不会因为你的故事给你戴花环的，”调皮的报春花说道，“所以接受我的吧。” “Nobody else is likely to crown you for your stories,” observed saucy Primrose, “so take this from me.”
“别太自以为是啊，”尤斯塔斯回答说，他富有光泽的卷发上顶着花环，看起来真像一个年轻的诗人，“别以为我的这些神奇的讨人喜欢的故事不会让我得到别的桂冠。我打算利用我所有的空闲时间，包括剩余的暑假，还有大学的夏季学期，把这些故事写下来送给报社。J. T. 菲尔茨先生（去年夏天在伯克郡，我认识了他，他是个诗人，也是个出版商）一眼就会看出我的作品有不同寻常之处。他会找人给故事配插图，我希望是比林斯来画，他还会找到最厉害的赞助，通过有名的蒂克纳出版公司把它们推向全世界。从现在起，大概五个月内，我无疑会成为一名当代了不起的作家！” “Do not be too sure,” answered Eustace, looking really like a youthful poet, with the laurel among his glossy curls, “that I shall not win other wreaths by these wonderful and admirable stories.I mean to spend all my leisure, during the rest of the vacation, and throughout the summer term at college, in writing them out for the press.Mr. J.T. Fields (with whom I became acquainted when he was in Berkshire, last summer, and who is a poet, as well as a publisher) will see their uncommon merit at a glance.He will get them illustrated, I hope, by Billings, and will bring them before the world under the very best of auspices, through the eminent house of TICKNOR & CO.In about five months from this moment, I make no doubt of being reckoned among the lights of the age!”
“可怜的孩子！”报春花低声地说，“等待他的将是多么大的失望啊！” “Poor boy!” said Primrose, half aside.“What a disappointment awaits him!”
他们又往下走了一段距离，布鲁因开始吠叫，惹得稳重的本也跟着汪汪地叫了起来。他们很快就发现那只好心的老狗，谨慎地看护着蒲公英、香蕨木、黄花九轮草和南瓜花。这几个小家伙，体力已经恢复得差不多了，采了些浆果，现在正赶着和伙伴会合呢。后来他们碰面了，这伙人穿过了卢瑟·巴特勒的果园，兴高采烈地向坦格尔伍德家走去。 Descending a little lower, Bruin began to bark, and was answered by the graver bow-wow of the respectable Ben.They soon saw the good old dog, keeping careful watch over Dandelion, Sweet Fern, Cowslip, and Squash-Blossom.These little people, quite recovered from their fatigue, had set about gathering checkerberries, and now came clambering to meet their playfellows.Thus reunited, the whole party went down through Luther Butler’s orchard, and made the best of their way home to Tanglewood.
