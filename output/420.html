替身 The Substitute
作者：莫泊桑 by Guy de Maupassant
“邦德赫瓦夫人？” “Madame Bonderoi?”
“是的，就是邦德赫瓦夫人。” “Yes, Madame Bonderoi.”
“不可能。” “Impossible.”
“我跟你说，就是她。” “I tell you it is.”
邦德赫瓦夫人，就是那个戴着花边帽子、对主虔诚且圣洁高贵的邦德赫瓦老夫人，她那小卷的假发像是绕着脑袋粘了一圈似的。 Madame Bonderoi, the old lady in a lace cap, the devout, the holy, the honorable Madame Bonderoi, whose little false curls looked as if they were glued round her head.
“就是那个女人。” “That is the very woman.”
“噢！ “Oh!
得了吧，你肯定是疯了。” Come, you must be mad.”
“我对你发誓，真的是邦德赫瓦夫人。” “I swear to you that it is Madame Bonderoi.”
“那请把这事讲清楚。” “Then please give me the details.”
“且听我娓娓道来。 “Here they are.
邦德赫瓦先生生前是一位律师。人们说她利用丈夫的办事员来为自己提供特殊服务。 During the life of Monsieur Bonderoi, the lawyer, people said that she utilized his clerks for her own particular service.
她只是众多备受尊敬的中产阶级女性中的一分子，这群人有着不可告人的恶习和死板的原则。 She is one of those respectable middle-class women, with secret vices, and inflexible principles, of whom there are so many.
她喜欢英俊的年轻小伙子，我倒想知道，还有什么比这更正常的呢？ She liked good-looking young fellows, and I should like to know what is more natural than that?
我们不都喜欢漂亮的女孩子吗？” Do not we all like pretty girls?”
“年老的邦德赫瓦先生一去世，他的遗孀就靠一份丰厚固定的收入过着平静祥和、无可指摘的日子。 “As soon as old Bonderoi was dead, his widow began to live the peaceful and irreproachable life of a woman with a fair, fixed income.
她勤勤恳恳地去教堂做礼拜，说邻居的坏话，却不会让邻居抓到任何可以用来指责她的把柄。随着年岁渐高，她变成了现在你所知道的那个瘦小干瘪、面容憔悴、到处惹是生非的女人。 She went to church assiduously, and spoke evil of her neighbors, but gave no handle to anyone for speaking ill of her, and when she grew old she became the little wizened, sour-faced, mischievous woman whom you know.
就这样，令你无法相信的这一传奇故事就发生在上星期五。 Well, this adventure, which you would scarcely believe, happened last Friday.
“我的朋友，你也认识的，让·德安马雷，是一个骑兵团里的上尉，驻扎在里维特街的兵营里。一天早上，他去军营时，他发现中队里的两个士兵刚刚发生了一场激烈的争吵。 “My friend, Jean d’Anglemare, is, as you know, a captain in a dragoon regiment, who is quartered in the barracks in the Rue de la Rivette, and when he got to his quarters the other morning, he found that two men of his squadron had had a terrible quarrel.
关乎军事荣誉的事是很严肃的，所以他们之间进行了一场决斗。 The rules about military honor are very severe, and so a duel took place between them.
决斗之后，他们和好如初。军官质问他们时，他们将决斗的前因后果和盘托出。 After the duel they became reconciled, and when their officer questioned them, they told him what their quarrel had been about.
他们是为邦德赫瓦夫人而战。” They had fought on Madame Bonderoi’s account.”
“噢！” “Oh!”
“是的，我亲爱的伙伴，是与邦德赫瓦夫人有关。” “Yes, my dear fellow, about Madame Bonderoi.”
“但我将让骑兵西巴尔来讲。” “But I will let Trooper Siballe speak.”
“事情是这样的，上尉。 “This is how it was, Captain.
大约在一年半前，一天晚上六七点钟，我在营房的院子里闲逛。一个女人走过来和我搭讪，她假借问路和我交谈，说：‘士兵，老实说，你想一个星期赚十法郎吗？’ About a year and a half ago, I was lounging about the barrack-yard, between six and seven o’clock in the evening, when a woman came up and spoke to me, and said, just as if she had been asking her way: ‘Soldier, would you like to earn ten francs a week, honestly?’
当然，我告诉她我确实想要。因此，她便说：‘明天早上十二点过来见我。 Of course, I told her that I decidedly should, and so she said: ‘Come and see me at twelve o’clock tomorrow morning.
我是邦德赫瓦夫人，我的地址是战壕街六号。’ I am Madame Bonderoi, and my address is No. 6, Rue de la Tranchée.’
‘您放心，我一定会到的，夫人。’ ‘You may rely upon my being there, Madame.’
而后，她看起来非常高兴地走开了，临走她还补充了一句：‘非常感谢您，士兵。’ And then she went away, looking very pleased, and she added: ‘I am very much obliged to you, soldier.’
‘是我非常感谢您，夫人。’我回答。 ‘I am obliged to you, Madame,’ I replied.
但我却一直为这件事所困扰，直到那个时间来临，也还是如此。 But I plagued my head about the matter, until the time came, all the same.
“十二点整时，我按响了门铃，她亲自开门让我进去。 “At twelve o’clock, exactly, I rang the bell, and she let me in herself.
她的头上缠满了丝带。 She had a lot of ribbons on her head.
“‘我们必须抓紧时间，’她说，‘我的佣人可能会进来。’ “‘We must make haste,’ she said; ‘as my servant might come in.’
“‘我很想抓紧时间，’我回答，‘但要我做什么呢？’ “‘I am quite willing to make haste,’ I replied, ‘but what am I to do?’
“但她只是笑了笑，回答道：‘像您这样精明的小伙子，难道还不明白？’ “But she only laughed, and replied: ‘Don’t you understand, you great knowing fellow?’
“我仍有些莫名其妙，上尉，这点我可以向您保证，但她过来坐在我旁边，说： “I was no nearer her meaning, I give you my word of honor, Captain, but she came and sat down by me, and said:
‘如果你向任何人提起这件事，我将会让你进监狱，所以你要发誓你永远不会将它说出去。’ ‘If you mention this to anyone, I will have you put in prison, so swear that you will never open your lips about it.’
“我如她所愿发了誓，尽管我实在琢磨不透她的意图。我的额头直冒汗，我从头盔里拿出手帕，她一把抓过去，擦拭我眉毛上的汗，然后她亲吻了我，轻声说：‘那么，你答应了？’ “I swore whatever she liked, though I did not at all understand what she meant, and my forehead was covered with perspiration, so I took my pocket-handkerchief out of my helmet, and she took it and wiped my brow with it; then she kissed me, and whispered: ‘Then you will?’
‘我会听从您的任何指示，夫人，’我回答，‘这就是我来的目的’。 ‘I will do anything you like, Madame,’ I replied, ‘as that is what I came for.’
“接下来，她用行动清楚地表明了她的意图。当我明白她的想法后，我把头盔放在椅子上，以此向她表明骑兵团的士兵是从来不退缩的，上尉。 “Then she made herself clearly understood by her actions, and when I saw what it was, I put my helmet onto a chair, and showed her that in the dragoons a man never retires, Captain.
“并不是我对此非常在意，因为显然她已过了风华正茂、如花似玉的年纪，但在这种情况下，过分挑剔也徒劳无益。毕竟十法郎也是不可多得的。随后我就和这个我想帮助的女人发生了关系，我对自己说：‘这里面有五法郎是给我的父亲的，管他的。’ “Not that I cared much about it, for she was certainly not in her prime, but it is no good being too particular in such a matter, as ten francs are scarce, and then I have relations whom I like to help, and I said to myself: ‘There will be five francs for my father, out of that.’
“我完成了分配的任务后，上尉，我就准备离开，尽管她要我多留一会儿，但是我对她说： “When I had done my allotted task, Captain, I got ready to go, though she wanted me to stop longer, but I said to her:
“‘一分钱，一分货，夫人。 “‘To everyone their due, Madame.
一小杯白兰地要花两个苏，两杯要花四个苏。’ A small glass of brandy costs two sous, and two glasses cost four.’
“她听懂了我的话，将一枚十法郎的金币放入我手中。 “She understood my meaning, and put a gold ten-franc piece into my hand.
我不喜欢那样的硬币，因为它太小了，如果你口袋的做工不够好，缝线绽开，很容易掉进靴子里，或者就完全找不到了，就是这样，当我看着硬币时，她也正看着我。 I do not like that coin, because it is so small that if your pockets are not very well made, and come at all unsewn, one is apt to find it in one’s boots, or not to find it at all, and so, while I was looking at it, she was looking at me.
由于她误解了我的表情，脸霎时红了，她说：‘这难道还不够吗？’ She got red in the face, as she had misunderstood my looks, and she said: ‘Is not that enough?’
“‘我不是那个意思，夫人，’我回答，‘但如果这对您来说无妨的话，我更想要两个五法郎的硬币。’ “‘I did not mean that, Madame,’ I replied; ‘but if it is all the same to you, I would rather have two five-franc pieces.’
她给我两个五法郎的硬币，我就离开了。 And she gave them to me, and I took my leave.
这种情况一直持续了一年半，上尉。 This has been going on for a year and a half, Captain.
我每星期二晚上去，也就是您让我放假出军营的时候；她也喜欢那个时段，因为那时她的佣人都已经上床睡觉了。但上周我身体不适，不得不上医务室。 I go every Tuesday evening, when you give me leave to go out of barracks; she prefers that, as her servant has gone to bed then, but last week I was not well, and I had to go into the infirmary.
星期二到了，我不能出去，为了我每星期能得到的十法郎，我心急如焚，我自言自语： When Tuesday came, I could not get out, and I was very vexed, because of the ten francs which I had been receiving every week, and I said to myself:
“‘如果有谁去了，我就完了；她肯定会找一个炮兵，想到这儿我气愤不已。 “‘If anybody goes there, I shall be done; and she will be sure to take an artilleryman, and that made me very angry.
所以我就拜托波梅尔去，他是我老乡，我把具体情况告诉他： So I sent for Paumelle, who comes from my part of the country, and I told him how matters stood:
“‘事成之后，你得五法郎，我得五法郎。’我说。 “‘There will be five francs for you, and five for me,’ I said.
他答应了，我给他做了充分的指示，他就动身了。 He agreed, and went, as I had given him full instructions.
他一敲门夫人就开门让他进去了。她没有看他的脸，因此没有察觉到那并不是原来那个人，因为上尉您是知道的，骑兵带上头盔后就很难分辨了。 She opened the door as soon as he knocked, and let him in, and as she did not look at his face, she did not perceive that it was not I, for, you know, Captain, one dragoon is very like another, with their helmets on.
“然而，她突然间发现不对劲，恼怒地问：‘你是谁？ “Suddenly, however, she noticed the change, and she asked, angrily: ‘Who are you?
你要干什么？ What do you want?
我不认识你。’ I do not know you.’
“而后，波梅尔就解释这件事，他告诉夫人我不舒服，就托他来代替我；她看着他，让他发誓保守这个秘密。就这样她接受了他，这是你可想而知的，因为波梅尔长得也并不难看。 “Then Paumelle explained matters; he told her that I was not well, and that I had sent him as my substitute; so she looked at him, made him also swear to keep the matter secret, and then she accepted him, as you may suppose, for Paumelle is not a bad-looking fellow, either.
可是，上尉，他回来时却不肯给我那五法郎。 But when he came back, Captain, he would not give me my five francs.
如果这些钱是给我自己用的，我无话可说，但它们是要给我父亲的，在这点上，我绝不能容忍这样胡来，我对他说： If they had been for myself, I should not have said a word, but they were for my father, and on that score, I would stand no nonsense, and I said to him:
“‘你做得实在太不光彩了，作为一个骑兵，你是在给你的军服抹黑。’ “‘You are not particular in what you do, for a dragoon; you are a discredit to your uniform.’
“上尉，他使出拳头，说像那样耗费精力的差事，应得双份报酬。 “He raised his fist, Captain, saying that fatigue duty like that was worth double.
当然，各人有各人的看法，但他不应该这样做。 Of course, everybody has his own ideas, and he ought not to have accepted it.
接下来的事，您都知道了。” You know the rest.”
“当他给我讲这个故事时，让·德安马雷笑得眼泪都要流出来了，但他也要我承诺保守这个秘密，就像他对这两名士兵承诺过的一样。 “Captain d’Anglemare laughed until he cried as he told me the story, but he also made me promise to keep the matter a secret, just as he had promised the two soldiers.
所以，千万要记住，不要出卖我，向我保证不把这件事说出去。” So, above all, do not betray me, but promise me to keep it to yourself.”
“噢！ “Oh!
你大可放心。 You may be quite easy about that.
但最后事情是如何解决的呢？” But how was it all arranged, in the end?”
“如何解决的？ “How?
说出来实在可笑…… It is a joke in a thousand!...
邦德赫瓦大妈要了这两位骑兵，为他们各自安排好特定时间。这样，每个人都满意了。” Mother Bonderoi keeps her two dragoons, and reserves his own particular day for each of them, and in that way everybody is satisfied.”
“噢！ “Oh!
那太妙了！ That is capital!
真是绝妙啊！” Really capital!”
“这样，他能给年老的父母送钱了，因此良心也得到满足。” “And he can send his old father and mother the money as usual, and thus morality is satisfied.”
