十月和六月 October and June
作者：欧·亨利 by O. Henry
上尉郁闷地注视着他那把挂在墙上的佩剑。 The Captain gazed gloomily at his sword that hung upon the wall.
旁边的衣橱里放着他穿得太久的、由于日晒雨淋而褪了色的军装。 In the closet near by was stored his faded uniform, stained and worn by weather and service.
那回荡着战斗号角的峥嵘岁月好像已经是很久很久以前的事了！ What a long, long time it seemed since those old days of war’s alarms!
他这个曾经在国家困难时期服役的老兵，如今退了役，却沦为一个女人的柔情目光和含笑唇下可怜兮兮的败将。 And now, veteran that he was of his country’s strenuous times, he had been reduced to abject surrender by a woman’s soft eyes and smiling lips.
他坐在寂静的房间里，手里拿着刚收到的她的信——让他变得郁闷的那封信。 As he sat in his quiet room he held in his hand the letter he had just received from her—the letter that had caused him to wear that look of gloom.
他又读了一遍那段致命的、使他希望破灭的话。 He re-read the fatal paragraph that had destroyed his hope.
承蒙厚爱，你提出要和我结婚，但我谢绝了你的好意，我觉得有必要向你坦白我的想法。 In declining the honour you have done me in asking me to be your wife, I feel that I ought to speak frankly.
我之所以拒绝你，是因为我们之间的年龄差异太大。 The reason I have for so doing is the great difference between our ages.
我非常、非常喜欢你，然而我确信我们的婚姻不会幸福。 I like you very, very much, but I am sure that our marriage would not be a happy one.
很抱歉，我不得不谈及这一点，但我相信，你是希望我真诚地告诉你真实原因的。 I am sorry to have to refer to this, but I believe that you will appreciate my honesty in giving you the true reason.
上尉叹了口气，用手撑着头。 The Captain sighed, and leaned his head upon his hand.
是的，他们的年龄确实相差很大。 Yes, there were many years between their ages.
但是他身强体壮，又拥有地位和财富。 But he was strong and rugged, he had position and wealth.
难道他的爱、他的温柔体贴、他能带给她的种种优越，还不能使她忘记年龄的问题吗？ Would not his love, his tender care, and the advantages he could bestow upon her make her forget the question of age?
此外，他几乎可以肯定她也很喜欢自己。 Besides, he was almost sure that she cared for him.
上尉是个雷厉风行的人。 The Captain was a man of prompt action.
在战场上，他的果断和干劲是有目共睹的。 In the field he had been distinguished for his decisiveness and energy.
他要亲自去见她并再次向她恳求。 He would see her and plead his cause again in person.
年龄！年龄怎么能成为挡在他和他所爱的人中间的问题呢？ Age!—what was it to come between him and the one he loved?
两小时以后，他收拾完毕，轻装上阵，准备投入他人生中一场最伟大的战役。 In two hours he stood ready, in light marching order, for his greatest battle.
他乘上火车，前往她所在的田纳西州南部的那个古老小镇。 He took the train for the old Southern town in Tennessee where she lived.
上尉进了大门，走在布满沙砾的路上时，西奥多拉·戴明正坐在古韵十足的老宅门廊的台阶上，欣赏夏日的暮色。 Theodora Deming was on the steps of the handsome, porticoed old mansion, enjoying the summer twilight, when the Captain entered the gate and came up the gravelled walk.
她冲他莞尔一笑，没有丝毫的尴尬。 She met him with a smile that was free from embarrassment.
上尉站在她下面的台阶上，他们的年龄差异并不十分明显。 As the Captain stood on the step below her, the difference in their ages did not appear so great.
他高大挺拔，眼睛清澈，皮肤黝黑。 He was tall and straight and clear-eyed and browned.
她风姿绰约，正值女人的大好年华。 She was in the bloom of lovely womanhood.
“没想到你会来，”西奥多拉说，“既然来了，就上台阶来坐吧。 “I wasn’t expecting you,” said Theodora; “but now that you’ve come you may sit on the step.
你没收到我的信吗？” Didn’t you get my letter?”
“收到了，”上尉说，“所以我来了。 “I did,” said the Captain; “and that’s why I came.
我说，西奥，再考虑一下你的答复，好吗？” I say, now, Theo, reconsider your answer, won’t you?”
西奥多拉冲他温柔地笑了笑。 Theodora smiled softly upon him.
他确实很成熟。 He carried his years well.
她确实喜欢他的强壮、健康的模样和男子气概——也许，如果—— She was really fond of his strength, his wholesome looks, his manliness—perhaps, if—
“不，不行，”她摇摇头，断然说道，“不能考虑。 “No, no,” she said, shaking her head, positively; “it’s out of the question.
我很喜欢你，但结婚却不行。 I like you a whole lot, but marrying won’t do.
我的年龄和你的年龄——别让我再说了——在信上我都告诉你了。” My age and yours are—but don’t make me say it again—I told you in my letter.”
上尉那古铜色的脸上泛起了微红。 The Captain flushed a little through the bronze on his face.
他沉默了一会儿，悲伤地望着暮色。 He was silent for a while, gazing sadly into the twilight.
他看到那一排树的后面有一片空地，当初穿蓝色制服的士兵向海岸行军时曾在那里露营。 Beyond a line of woods that he could see was a field where the boys in blue had once bivouacked on their march toward the sea.
现在想来，那仿佛是多久以前的事了啊！ How long ago it seemed now!
的确，命运和时间狠狠地捉弄了他。 Truly, Fate and Father Time had tricked him sorely.
他和幸福之间仅隔着那么几年！ Just a few years interposed between himself and happiness!
西奥多拉的手滑落下来，被他那有力、黝黑的手紧紧握住。 Theodora’s hand crept down and rested in the clasp of his firm, brown one.
她感到至少有种近似爱的感情。 She felt, at least, that sentiment that is akin to love.
“别太难过了，”她温柔地说， “Don’t take it so hard, please,” she said, gently.
“这对我们都好。 “It’s all for the best.
我自己冷静地思前想后过了。 I’ve reasoned it out very wisely all by myself.
总有一天，你会为没有和我结婚而高兴。 Some day you’ll be glad I didn’t marry you.
如果我们结婚的话，短期内可能会很幸福美满——但是你再想想！ It would be very nice and lovely for a while—but, just think!
短短几年之后，我们的爱好将有多大的差别！ In only a few short years what different tastes we would have!
我们俩中的一个人想要坐在壁炉边看书，晚上还要调养神经痛或风湿病，而另一个人则渴望参加舞会、看戏或出席晚宴。 One of us would want to sit by the fireside and read, and maybe nurse neuralgia or rheumatism of evenings, while the other would be crazy for balls and theatres and late suppers.
不，我亲爱的朋友。 No, my dear friend.
虽然我们的差异不像寒冬的一月和初夏的五月那么明显，但至少也像晚秋的十月与仲夏的六月。” While it isn’t exactly January and May, it’s a clear case of October and pretty early in June.”
“我会一直按照你的意愿做，西奥。 “I’d always do what you wanted me to do, Theo.
如果你让我——” If you wanted to—”
“不，你不会。 “No, you wouldn’t.
你现在觉得你能做到，但其实不可能。 You think now that you would, but you wouldn’t.
请别再求我了。” Please don’t ask me any more.”
上尉这一仗败了。 The Captain had lost his battle.
但他是个英勇的战士，当他起身做最后的告别时，他紧抿嘴唇，挺起胸膛。 But he was a gallant warrior, and when he rose to make his final adieu his mouth was grimly set and his shoulders were squared.
当晚他就乘火车北上。 He took the train for the North that night.
第二天晚上，他就回到了自己那个墙上挂着佩剑的房间。 On the next evening he was back in his room, where his sword was hanging against the wall.
他正在精心打着白色的领结，准备外出吃晚餐。 He was dressing for dinner, tying his white tie into a very careful bow.
同时，他忧郁地自言自语。 And at the same time he was indulging in a pensive soliloquy.
“说到底，我觉得西奥还是对的。 “’Pon my honour, I believe Theo was right, after all.
不可否认她的确是个美人，但是保守估计，她怎么也有28岁了。” Nobody can deny that she’s a peach, but she must be twenty-eight, at the very kindest calculation.”
要知道，上尉只有19岁，他的那把佩剑除了在查塔努加的检阅场以外，从未拔出来过，就像他也从未参加过美西战争一样。 For you see, the Captain was only nineteen, and his sword had never been drawn except on the parade ground at Chattanooga, which was as near as he ever got to the Spanish-American War.
