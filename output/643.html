布鲁斯-帕廷顿图纸[^1] The Advebture of The Bruce-Partington Plans
一八九五年十一月的第三个星期，浓重的黄雾在伦敦扎下了营盘。现在我依然怀疑，在那个星期，星期一到星期四的那段时间里，从我们贝克街寓所的窗子望出去，究竟有没有过能看见对面房子轮廓的时候。第一天，福尔摩斯打发时间的方法是替他那本大部头参考手册编制互见索引，第二天和第三天，他耐着性子研究中世纪的音乐，那是他新近培养起来的一个爱好。可是，到了第四天，我们又一次吃完早饭，把椅子从餐桌跟前挪开，又一次看见那股油乎乎、沉甸甸的黄褐色涡流飘过眼前，看见它在窗子玻璃上凝成一颗颗油汪汪的水珠，我这位天生急躁好动的室友终于对这种死水一潭的生活忍无可忍。无处发泄的精力驱使他没完没了地在客厅里踱来踱去，咬着指甲、敲着家具，为这种百无聊赖的局面大光其火。 In the third week of November, in the year 1895, a dense yellow fog settled down upon London. From the Monday to the Thursday I doubt whether it was ever possible from our windows in Baker Street to see the loom of the opposite houses. The first day Holmes had spent in cross-indexing his huge book of references. The second and third had been patiently occupied upon a subject which he had recently made his hobby—the music of the Middle Ages. But when, for the fourth time, after pushing back our chairs from breakfast we saw the greasy, heavy brown swirl still drifting past us and condensing in oily drops upon the window-panes, my comrade’s impatient and active nature could endure this drab existence no longer. He paced restlessly about our sitting-room in a fever of suppressed energy, biting his nails, tapping the furniture, and chafing against inaction.
“报纸上找不出什么有意思的东西吗，华生？”他说道。 “Nothing of interest in the paper, Watson?” he said.
我早就已经知道，福尔摩斯嘴里的“有意思”，意思就是有意思的罪案。报纸上讲到了一次革命，讲到了一场可能爆发的战争，还讲到了一个即将更迭的政府，只可惜，这些事情都不在我室友的考虑范围之内。我没能在报纸上找到什么奇案大案，福尔摩斯哀叹一声，没完没了的踱步再次开始。 I was aware that by anything of interest, Holmes meant anything of criminal interest. There was the news of a revolution, of a possible war, and of an impending change of government; but these did not come within the horizon of my companion. I could see nothing recorded in the shape of crime which was not commonplace and futile. Holmes groaned and resumed his restless meanderings.
“毫无疑问，伦敦的罪犯都是些庸碌之辈，”他说话的口气愤愤不平，活脱脱是一名找不到称心猎物的猎手。“你往窗子外面看看吧，华生。看看那些人影，看他们怎么渐渐浮现，模模糊糊地露个脸，转眼就再次消失在浓雾之中。这样的日子里，窃贼和凶手完全可以放开手脚嘛，他们可以在伦敦随意游荡，就像是密林之中的老虎，即便到了发起突袭的那一刻，也只有受害者能把他们看个清楚。” “The London criminal is certainly a dull fellow,” said he in the querulous voice of the sportsman whose game has failed him. “Look out of this window, Watson. See how the figures loom up, are dimly seen, and then blend once more into the cloud-bank. The thief or the murderer could roam London on such a day as the tiger does the jungle, unseen until he pounces, and then evident only to his victim.”
“报纸上登了啊，”我说道，“小偷小摸的事情多得很呢。” “There have,” said i, “been numerous petty thefts.”
福尔摩斯不屑一顾地哼了一声。 Holmes snorted his contempt.
“这么一个恢宏肃穆的舞台是为那些更有分量的演出准备的，”他说道，“我没有成为一名罪犯，这个社会真应该感到庆幸。” “This great and sombre stage is set for something more worthy than that,” said he. “it is fortunate for this community that i am not a criminal.”
“确实如此，千真万确！”我发自肺腑地表示同意。 “it is, indeed!” said i heartily.
“假设我是布鲁克斯或者伍德豪斯，或者是其他某个有充分理由取我性命的人，那样的人一共有五十个，那么，面临我自己的追杀，我能活多久呢？一张传票、一次虚假的约会，一切不就完结了嘛。那些暗杀成风的拉美国家没有雾天，倒还真是件好事哩。天哪！瞧，终于有点儿事情来打破这种死气沉沉的局面了。” “Suppose that I were Brooks or Woodhouse, or any of the fifty men who have good reason for taking my life, how long could i survive against my own pursuit? A summons, a bogus appointment, and all would be over. it is well they don’t have days of fog in the latin countries—the countries of assassination. By Jove! here comes something at last to break our dead monotony.”
原来是女仆送来了一封电报。福尔摩斯拆开电报，突然间大笑起来。 It was the maid with a telegram. Holmes tore it open and burst out laughing.
“好，好啊！接下来会是什么呢？”他说道，“眼下的事情是，我哥哥迈克罗夫特要来了。” “Well, well! What next?” said he. “Brother Mycroft is coming round.”
“这有什么好奇怪的呢？”我问道。 “Why not?” i asked.
“有什么好奇怪？这样的情形好比是一辆有轨电车[^2]出现在了乡村小路上。迈克罗夫特有他自己的轨道，轻易也不会脱轨。位于朴尔莫尔大街的寓所、第欧根尼俱乐部，再加上白厅[^3]，他生活的圈子不外如是。他来过这儿一次，也只有那么一次[^4]。眼下他居脱离了自己的轨道，到底是出了什么样的乱子呢？” “Why not? it is as if you met a tram-car coming down a country lane. Mycroft has his rails and he runs on them. His Pall Mall lodgings, the Diogenes Club, Whitehall—that is his cycle. once, and only once, he has been here. What upheaval can possibly have derailed him?”
“他自己没有说吗？” “Does he not explain?”
福尔摩斯把他哥哥的电报递给了我。 Holmes handed me his brother’s telegram.
> 因卡多根 ·韦斯特事亟需面晤。即往你处。 > Must see you over Cadogan West. Coming at once.
> 迈克罗夫特 > Mycroft.
“卡多根·韦斯特？我听说过这个名字。 ” “Cadogan West? i have heard the name.”
“我倒是一点儿印象也没有。不过，想想吧，迈克罗夫特竟然会以这么古怪的方式打破常规！简直就跟行星脱离了轨道差不多。对了，你知道迈克罗夫特是干什么的吗？” “It recalls nothing to my mind. But that Mycroft should break out in this erratic fashion! A planet might as well leave its orbit. By the way, do you know what Mycroft is?”
我依稀记得，在处理“希腊译员案”的时候，福尔摩斯曾经跟我说过这件事情。 I had some vague recollection of an explanation at the time of the Adventure of the Greek interpreter.
“你以前告诉过我，他是中央政府的一名小职员。” “You told me that he had some small office under the British government.”
福尔摩斯吃吃地笑了起来。 Holmes chuckled.
“那个时候，我还不怎么了解你呢。谈论国家大事的时候，不谨慎一点儿是不行的。你说他替中央政府做事，没错；如果你说他有些时候就是中央政府，从某种意义上说，也没错。” “I did not know you quite so well in those days. one has to be discreet when one talks of high matters of state. You are right in thinking that he is under the British government. You would also be right in a sense if you said that occasionally he *is*the British government.”
“亲爱的福尔摩斯！” “My dear Holmes!”
“我就知道你多半会大吃一惊。迈克罗夫特一年只挣四百五十镑，老老实实地待在幕僚的位置上，没有任何雄心壮志，得不到任何荣誉和头衔，即便如此，他仍然是这个国家里最不可或缺的人物。” “I thought i might surprise you. Mycroft draws four hundred and fifty pounds a year, remains a subordinate, has no ambitions of any kind, will receive neither honour nor title, but remains the most indispensable man in the country.”
“怎么叫不可或缺呢？” “But how?”
“这么说吧，他占据着一个独一无二的位置。这样的位置是他自己给自己打造的，以前不曾有过，将来也不会再有。他的头脑极度缜密、极有条理、极其擅长储存事实，世上无人可以匹敌。我用来侦破罪案的那些高超本领他也有，只不过被他用在了他自个儿的特殊行当上。政府各部的决议都会从他手里经过，他好比是情报交换中枢，好比是票据交换所，各个账户的盈亏都在他那里得到体现。其他的人都是擅长某一方面的专家，他的专长则是无所不知。打个比方说，某位大臣需要了解某个问题，那个问题同时牵扯到海军事务、印度事务、加拿大事务和金银复本位货币制度。在这种情况下，那位大臣固然可以从相关部门分别取得相关的材料，可是，只有迈克罗夫特同时掌握所有的材料，能够随口说出各种因素之间的相互影响。刚开始的时候，他们只是把他当成一条捷径、一道方便之门，眼下呢，他已经把自己变成了一个不可缺少的人物。他那颗了不起的头脑分门别类地储存着所有的材料，要用的时候就可以马上取出来。他的意见一次又一次地决定了国家的大政方针，这就是他全部的生活。别的事情他一概不想，只有在我向他求教的时候，他才会放松放松，想想我带去的那些小问题，权当是练练脑子。可是，这样一位大神却在今天从天而降，究竟会是什么意思呢？卡多根·韦斯特到底是谁，他跟迈克罗夫特又有什么关系呢？” “Well, his position is unique. He has made it for himself. There has never been anything like it before, nor will be again. He has the tidiest and most orderly brain, with the greatest capacity for storing facts, of any man living. The same great powers which i have turned to the detection of crime he has used for this particular business. The conclusions of every department are passed to him, and he is the central exchange, the clearing-house, which makes out the balance. All other men are specialists, but his specialism is omniscience. We will suppose that a minister needs information as to a point which involves the navy, india, Canada and the bimetallic question; he could get his separate advices from various departments upon each, but only Mycroft can focus them all, and say offhand how each factor would affect the other. They began by using him as a short-cut, a convenience; now he has made himself an essential. in that great brain of his everything is pigeon-holed and can be handed out in an instant. Again and again his word has decided the national policy. He lives in it. He thinks of nothing else save when, as an intellectual exercise, he unbends if i call upon him and ask him to advise me on one of my little problems. But Jupiter is descending to-day. What on earth can it mean? Who is Cadogan West, and what is he to Mycroft?”
“我知道了，”我嚷了一句，一头扎进了沙发上那堆乱七八糟的报纸。“没错，没错，他就在这儿，错不了！卡多根·韦斯特就是那个小伙子，星期二早晨，人们在地铁里发现了他的尸体。” “I have it,” i cried, and plunged among the litter of papers upon the sofa. “Yes, yes, here he is, sure enough! Cadogan West was the young man who was found dead on the underground on Tuesday morning.”
这句话立刻攫住了福尔摩斯的注意，他坐直身子，正在往嘴里送的烟斗停在了半空。 Holmes sat up at attention, his pipe halfway to his lips.
“这件事情一定是非常严重，华生。能让我哥哥改变自己的生活习惯，这样的死亡事件一定不一般。说到底，他跟这件事情能有什么关系呢？按我的记忆，这件案子并没有什么特异之处啊。这个小伙子显然是掉到了车厢外面，自个儿摔死的。他没有遭到抢劫，身上也没有明显的暴力伤害迹象，难道我说得不对吗？” “This must be serious, Watson. A death which has caused my brother to alter his habits can be no ordinary one. What in the world can he have to do with it? The case was featureless as i remember it. The young man had apparently fallen out of the train and killed himself. He had not been robbed, and there was no particular reason to suspect violence. is that not so?”
“死因调查已经结束了，”我说道，“他们又发现了很多新的情况。要我说，细看起来，这还真是件古怪的案子呢。” “There has been an inquest,” said i, “and a good many fresh facts have come out. looked at more closely, i should certainly say that it was a curious case.”
“从它对我哥哥造成的影响来看，我只能认为它非常不一般。”他窝进了他那把扶手椅。“好了，华生，把案情说来听听吧。” “Judging by its effect upon my brother, i should think it must be a most extraordinary one.” He snuggled down in his armchair. “now, Watson, let us have the facts.”
“这个人名叫阿瑟 ·卡多根 ·韦斯特，现年二十七岁，未婚，是伍利奇兵工厂[^5]的一名职员。” “The man’s name was Arthur Cadogan West. He was twenty-seven years of age, unmarried, and a clerk at Woolwich Arsenal.”
“原来他是政府雇员啊，瞧，这就跟迈克罗夫特老哥挂上钩了吧！” “Government employ. Behold the link with Brother Mycroft!”
“星期一晚上，他突然离开了伍利奇。最后一个见到他的人是他的未婚妻维奥莱特·韦斯特伯里小姐，当晚七点半左右，他突然独自离开，把他的未婚妻撇在了大雾之中。之前他俩并没有吵架，她想不出他为什么要这么做。再听到他的音信的时候，他已经变成了一具尸体，发现尸体的是一个名为梅森的养路工人，地点则是在伦敦的地铁里，紧挨着奥尔德盖特地铁站的地方。” “He left Woolwich suddenly on Monday night. Was last seen by his fiancée, Miss Violet Westbury, whom he left abruptly in the fog about 7:30 that evening. There was no quarrel between them and she can give no motive for his action. The next thing heard of him was when his dead body was discovered by a plate-layer named Mason, just outside Aldgate Station on the underground system in london.”
“时间呢？” “When?”
“发现尸体的时间是星期二早晨六点钟。尸体躺在铁轨外面，按东行方向来说是在铁轨左边，位置离车站很近，正好是在地铁线穿出隧道的地方。死者的脑袋已经碎裂，很可能是因为从车上掉下来的缘故。尸体只可能是从车上掉到地铁里的，要想从附近的街道搬过去的话，那就必须得经过站口的栅栏，栅栏旁边始终都站着一名验票员。看样子，这一点绝不会有什么疑问。” “The body was found at six on the Tuesday morning. it was lying wide of the metals upon the left hand of the track as one goes eastward, at a point close to the station, where the line emerges from the tunnel in which it runs. The head was badly crushed—an injury which might well have been caused by a fall from the train. The body could only have come on the line in that way. Had it been carried down from any neighbouring street, it must have passed the station barriers, where a collector is always standing. This point seems absolutely certain.”
“很好。案情可算是相当明确，这个人可能是自己掉下了地铁列车，也可能是被人推下去的，掉下去的时候可能还活着，也可能已经死了。这些我都听明白了。接着说吧。” “Very good. The case is definite enough. The man, dead or alive, either fell or was precipitated from a train. So much is clear to me. Continue.”
“在尸体旁边那些铁轨上行驶的都是东去的列车，有一些只在市内行驶，也有一些来自威尔斯登[^6]，或者是其他的一些外围车站。可以确定的是，这个小伙子死在深夜里，死在东去的旅途之中。不过，他上车的地点却完全无法确定。” “The trains which traverse the lines of rail beside which the body was found are those which run from west to east, some being purely Metropolitan, and some from Willesden and outlying junctions. it can be stated for certain that this young man, when he met his death, was travelling in this direction at some late hour of the night, but at what point he entered the train it is impossible to state.”
“看看他的车票，自然可以解决这个问题。” “His ticket, of course, would show that.”
“他的口袋里没有车票。” “There was no ticket in his pockets.”
“没有车票！天哪，华生，这可真是怪了。根据我的经验，不出示车票是进不了地铁站台的啊。如此说来，这个小伙子应该是有票的。难道是有人拿走了车票，打算掩盖他上车的地点吗？有可能。要不然，是他自己把车票丢在车厢里了吗？也有可能。不管怎么说，这一点终归让人非常好奇。我记得现场没有抢劫的迹象，对吗？” “No ticket! Dear me, Watson, this is really very singular. According to my experience it is not possible to reach the platform of a Metropolitan train without exhibiting one’s ticket. Presumably, then, the young man had one. Was it taken from him in order to conceal the station from which he came? it is possible. or did he drop it in the carriage? That also is possible. But the point is of curious interest. i understand that there was no sign of robbery?”
“显然是没有。这儿有一张清单，他的随身物品都列在里面。他身上有个钱包，里面装着两镑零十五先令，还有一本支票簿，抬头是都郡银行伍利奇分行，他的身份就是根据支票簿查出来的。此外还有两张事发当晚的伍利奇剧院二楼[^7]戏票，外加一小捆技术文件。 ” “Apparently not. There is a list here of his possessions. His purse contained two pounds fifteen. He had also a check-book on the Woolwich branch of the Capital and Counties Bank. Through this his identity was established. There were also two dress-circle tickets for the Woolwich Theatre, dated for that very evening. Also a small packet of technical papers.”
福尔摩斯心满意足地欢呼了一声。 Holmes gave an exclamation of satisfaction.
“咱们终于看清了其中的奥妙，华生！中央政府——伍利奇兵工厂——技术文件——迈克罗夫特老哥，所有的环节都凑齐啦。不过，我没弄错的话，这会儿他已经到了，咱们还是听听他自个儿怎么说吧。” “There we have it at last, Watson! British government— Woolwich Arsenal—technical papers—Brother Mycroft, the chain is complete. But here he comes, if i am not mistaken, to speak for himself.”
片刻之后，仆人将魁伟健硕的迈克罗夫特·福尔摩斯领了进来。看着他沉甸甸的庞大躯体，你难免会觉得他粗鲁笨拙、四体不勤，可是，这副臃肿的身板却顶着这样的一颗脑袋，眉宇之间的神色无比威严、深陷的钢灰色眼睛无比机警、嘴唇的线条无比坚毅，表情的变化也无比微妙，只需要看上一眼，你就会立刻忘掉他大而无当的身体，记得的只是他超卓不凡的心智。 A moment later the tall and portly form of Mycroft Holmes was ushered into the room. Heavily built and massive, there was a suggestion of uncouth physical inertia in the figure, but above this unwieldy frame there was perched a head so masterful in its brow, so alert in its steel-gray, deep-set eyes, so firm in its lips, and so subtle in its play of expression, that after the first glance one forgot the gross body and remembered only the dominant mind.
跟在他后面的是我俩在苏格兰场的那位老朋友，瘦削严肃的雷斯垂德。两个人都是脸色凝重，显然是碰上了大问题。探员走过来握了握手，一句话也没有说。迈克罗夫特·福尔摩斯则千辛万苦地脱掉大衣，慢吞吞地坐进了一把扶手椅。 At his heels came our old friend lestrade, of Scotland Yard—thin and austere. The gravity of both their faces foretold some weighty quest. The detective shook hands without a word. Mycroft Holmes struggled out of his overcoat and subsided into an armchair.
“这件事情真让人头疼，歇洛克，”他说道，“我最不愿意打破自己的习惯，那些当权的家伙却怎么也不让我推托。考虑到目前的暹罗[^8]局势，我实在是很不方便离开自己的办公室。话说回来，眼下的事情确实是一场危机。我从来没见过首相这么心烦，海军部就更不用说了，整个儿变成了一个捅翻了的马蜂窝。你读到这件案子的报道了吗？” “A most annoying business, Sherlock,” said he. “i extremely dislike altering my habits, but the powers that be would take no denial. In the present state of Siam it is most awkward that i should be away from the office. But it is a real crisis. i have never seen the Prime Minister so upset. As to the Admiralty— it is buzzing like an overturned bee-hive. Have you read up the case?”
“我俩刚刚读完。那些技术文件到底是什么呢？” “We have just done so. What were the technical papers?”
“咳，问题就在这里！幸运的是，事情还没有传扬出去。传扬出去的话，报界肯定会闹翻天的。那个倒霉的小伙子兜里的那些纸片，就是布鲁斯-帕廷顿潜艇的图纸。” “Ah, there’s the point! Fortunately, it has not come out. The press would be furious if it did. The papers which this wretched youth had in his pocket were the plans of the Bruce-Partington submarine.”
迈克罗夫特·福尔摩斯说话的语气十分沉重，说明他认为这件事情非同小可。我和他弟弟静静地坐在那里，等待着他的下文。 Mycroft Holmes spoke with a solemnity which showed his sense of the importance of the subject. His brother and I sat expectant.
“你肯定听说过它吧？我还以为所有的人都听说过它呢。 ” “Surely you have heard of it? i thought everyone had heard of it.”
“我听过的只是这个名字。 ” “only as a name.”
“这样东西的重要性，怎么形容都不算夸张，我国政府捂得最严实的秘密就是这样东西。我可以跟你打包票，在布鲁斯-帕廷顿潜艇的活动范围之内，海战根本就是不可能的事情。两年之前，政府偷偷摸摸地把一大笔款项塞进了财政预算，用它买下了潜艇的专利，然后就不遗余力地遮掩这个秘密。潜艇的图纸极其复杂，其中包括大概三十项单独的专利，每一项对潜艇的整体来说都是不可或缺。图纸存放在一个精心设计的保险柜里，地点是伍利奇兵工厂旁边一间保密的办公室，办公室的门窗都是防盗的。任何人不得以任何理由把图纸带出办公室，即便你是海军部的造船总工程师，要看图纸也只能到伍利奇的那间办公室里去看。没想到，眼下我们竟然在伦敦的心脏地带，在一名死去的低级职员兜里找到了图纸。从官方的角度来看，这事情只能说是糟糕透顶。” “Its importance can hardly be exaggerated. it has been the most jealously guarded of all government secrets. You may take it from me that naval warfare becomes impossible within the radius of a Bruce-Partington’s operation. Two years ago a very large sum was smuggled through the Estimates and was expended in acquiring a monopoly of the invention. Every effort has been made to keep the secret. The plans, which are exceedingly intricate, comprising some thirty separate patents, each essential to the working of the whole, are kept in an elaborate safe in a confidential office adjoining the arsenal, with burglar-proof doors and windows. under no conceivable circumstances were the plans to be taken from the office. if the chief constructor of the navy desired to consult them, even he was forced to go to the Woolwich office for the purpose. And yet here we find them in the pocket of a dead junior clerk in the heart of london. From an official point of view it’s simply awful.”
“可是，图纸不是找回来了吗？” “But you have recovered them?”
“没有，歇洛克，没有！糟糕就糟糕在这里。没有找回来。伍利奇丢失的图纸一共是十张，卡多根·韦斯特的兜里只有七张，最重要的三张不见了——让人偷了，没影儿了。你必须放下手头的所有事情，歇洛克。把你平日里那些地方法庭都管得了的小案子扔一边儿去吧，这次你要解决的可是个生死攸关的国际问题。卡多根·韦斯特为什么要拿走图纸，失踪的图纸去了哪里，他是怎么死的，尸体为什么会出现在那个地方，还有，这件祸事该怎么补救呢？只要你能把所有这些问题的答案找出来，就算是为你的祖国作出了巨大的贡献。” “No, Sherlock, no! Why did Cadogan West take the papers, where are the missing ones, how did he die, how came his body where it was found, how can the evil be set right? Find an answer to all these questions, and you will have done good service for your country.”
“你自己干吗不去找呢，迈克罗夫特？我看得到的，你也看得到啊。” “Why do you not solve it yourself, Mycroft? You can see as far as i.”

But it is a question of getting details. Give me your details, and from an armchair i will return you an excellent expert opinion. But to run here and run there, to cross-question railway guards, and lie on my face with a lens to my eye—it is not my *métier*. No, you are the one man who can clear the matter up. if you have a fancy to see your name in the next honours list——”
我朋友笑着摇了摇头。 My friend smiled and shook his head.
“我如果参加游戏，为的只是游戏本身的乐趣，”他说道，“话又说回来，这个问题确实有点儿意思，我倒是很有兴趣研究一下。麻烦你，再给我说点儿情况吧。” “I play the game for the game’s own sake,” said he. “But the problem certainly presents some points of interest, and i shall be very pleased to look into it. Some more facts, please.”

The actual official guardian of the papers is the famous government expert, Sir James Walter, whose decorations and sub-titles fill two lines of a book of reference. He has grown grey in the service, is a gentleman, a favoured guest in the most exalted houses, and, above all, a man whose patriotism is beyond suspicion. He is one of two who have a key of the safe. i may add that the papers were undoubtedly in the office during working hours on Monday, and that Sir James left for london about three o’clock taking his key with him. He was at the house of Admiral Sinclair at Barclay Square during the whole of the evening when this incident occurred.”
“这件事情核实了吗？” “Has the fact been verified?”
“核实了，他弟弟瓦伦丁·沃尔特上校证明他的确离开了伍利奇，辛克莱上将则证明他的确到了伦敦。由此看来，詹姆斯爵士跟这件案子没有直接的关联。” “Yes; his brother, Colonel Valentine Walter, has testified to his departure from Woolwich, and Admiral Sinclair to his arrival in london; so Sir James is no longer a direct factor in the problem.”
“另一个有钥匙的人是谁呢？” “Who was the other man with a key?”
“高级职员兼制图员西德尼·约翰逊先生，此人现年四十，已婚，有五个孩子。他是个性情乖僻的闷葫芦，总体说来却拥有无可挑剔的政府工作履历。他不受同事们的欢迎，工作倒是十分勤奋。按他自己的说法，星期一下班之后，他整晚都待在自己家里，还有呢，他那把钥匙一直都穿在他的表链上，从来不曾取下来。当然，他这些话的佐证仅仅是他妻子的话而已。” “The senior clerk and draughtsman, Mr. Sidney Johnson. He is a man of forty, married, with five children. He is a silent, morose man, but he has, on the whole, an excellent record in the public service. He is unpopular with his colleagues, but a hard worker. According to his own account, corroborated only by the word of his wife, he was at home the whole of Monday evening after office hours, and his key has never left the watch-chain upon which it hangs.”
“给我们讲讲卡多根·韦斯特吧。” “Tell us about Cadogan West.”
“他已经在政府部门工作了十年，表现相当不错，虽然是个出了名的急性子，人品却称得上正派诚实。我们没发现他有什么毛病。他在办公室里的地位仅次于西德尼·约翰逊，因为工作的原因，他每天都要直接接触图纸。其他的人都没有处置图纸的权力。” “He has been ten years in the service and has done good work. He has the reputation of being hot-headed and impetuous, but a straight, honest man. We have nothing against him. He was next Sidney Johnson in the office. His duties brought him into daily, personal contact with the plans. no one else had the handling of them.”
“当晚是谁把图纸锁起来的呢？” “Who locked the plans up that night?”
“就是那个高级职员，西德尼·约翰逊先生。 ” “Mr. Sidney Johnson, the senior clerk.”
“这样看来，图纸是谁拿的，答案不问可知。既然图纸实实在在地出现在了低级职员卡多根·韦斯特的身上，这个问题可算是有了定论，对吧？” “Well, it is surely perfectly clear who took them away. They are actually found upon the person of this junior clerk, Cadogan West. That seems final, does it not?”
“的确如此，歇洛克，可是，由此而来的问题还多着呢。首先，他干吗要拿图纸呢？” “It does, Sherlock, and yet it leaves so much unexplained. in the first place, why did he take them?”
“依我看，图纸应该很值钱吧？” “I presume they were of value?”
“他要是拿去卖的话，随随便便就可以弄个几千镑。” “He could have got several thousands for them very easily.”
“他把图纸拿到伦敦来，除了卖图纸还能有什么别的目的，你想得出吗？” “Can you suggest any possible motive for taking the papers to london except to sell them?”
“想不出，我想不出。” “No, I cannot.”
“既然如此，咱们只能暂时假定，拿走图纸的确实是韦斯特这个小子。好了，图纸既然是他拿的，那他肯定得有一把仿制的钥匙——” “Then we must take that as our working hypothesis. Young West took the papers. now this could only be done by having a false key——”
“应该说是几把仿制的钥匙，他还得打开楼门和房间门。” “Several false keys. He had to open the building and the room.”
“好吧，他有几把仿制的钥匙。他带着图纸到伦敦来，打算卖掉图纸当中的技术机密，毫无疑问，他还打算第二天一早就把图纸本身放回保险柜，免得被别人发现。来到伦敦之后，他在从事这项叛国活动的过程当中送掉了性命。” “He had, then, several false keys. He took the papers to london to sell the secret, intending, no doubt, to have the plans themselves back in the safe next morning before they were missed. While in london on this treasonable mission he met his end.”
“怎么送掉的呢？” “How?”
“咱们不妨假定，他是在返回伍利奇的途中被人杀死的，然后又被人扔下了列车。” “We will suppose that he was travelling back to Woolwich when he was killed and thrown out of the compartment.”
“发现尸体的地方是奥尔德盖特，已经远远地过了伦敦桥车站附近的那个站，他要是想回伍利奇的话，应该到伦敦桥车站去坐火车啊。” “Aldgate, where the body was found, is considerably past the station for london Bridge, which would be his route to Woolwich.”
“要解释他为什么坐过了伦敦桥车站附近的那个站，咱们可以设想出很多种情形。举例来说，咱们可以设想他在车厢里跟某个人谈话，而且谈得入了神。这次谈话导致了暴力事件，致使他送掉了性命。也有可能，他打算离开那节车厢，结果是摔死在了地铁里，另外那个人又把车门重新关好。外面的雾很大，什么也看不见。” “Many circumstances could be imagined under which he would pass london Bridge. There was someone in the carriage, for example, with whom he was having an absorbing interview. This interview led to a violent scene in which he lost his life. Possibly he tried to leave the carriage, fell out on the line, and so met his end. The other closed the door. There was a thick fog, and nothing could be seen.”
“从目前掌握的情况来看，咱们也拿不出什么更好的解释了。不过，歇洛克，想想吧，你没有给出解释的问题还多得很呢。为了把事情分析清楚，咱们不妨假定，卡多根·韦斯特这个小子确实打定了主意，要把图纸拿到伦敦来卖。这样的话，按道理他就应该跟某个外国间谍定下约会，把晚上的时间腾出来。可他没有这么做，反倒是买了两张戏票，还陪着他的未婚妻走到了去剧院的半道上，然后才突然消失。” “No better explanation can be given with our present knowledge; and yet consider, Sherlock, how much you leave untouched. We will suppose, for argument’s sake, that young Cadogan West *had*determined to convey these papers to london. He would naturally have made an appointment with the foreign agent and kept his evening clear. instead of that he took two tickets for the theatre, escorted his fiancée halfway there, and then suddenly disappeared.”
“幌子呗，”雷斯垂德说道。他一直坐在那里听兄弟俩说话，看样子是多少有点儿不耐烦。 “A blind,” said lestrade, who had sat listening with some impatience to the conversation.
“如果是幌子，这样的幌子未免太离奇了吧。这是第一个说不通的地方。第二：咱们假定他到了伦敦，并且见到了那个外国间谍。他必须赶在第二天一早把图纸放回去，要不然就会露出马脚。事实呢，他拿走了十张图纸，兜里却只有七张。另外三张到哪里去了呢？他肯定不会自愿交出那三张图纸。还有啊，他卖国换来的赏钱又在哪里呢？按道理说，他兜里应该有一大笔钱才对啊。” “A very singular one. That is objection no. 1. objection no. 2: We will suppose that he reaches london and sees the foreign agent. He must bring back the papers before morning or the loss will be discovered. He took away ten. only seven were in his pocket. What had become of the other three? He certainly would not leave them of his own free will. Then, again, where is the price of his treason? one would have expected to find a large sum of money in his pocket.”
“按我看，这件事情可说是一清二楚，”雷斯垂德说道，“我已经彻底看清了事情的经过，再没有半点儿疑问。他拿了图纸去卖，见到了那个间谍。发现价钱谈不拢之后，他开始往家里赶，没想到，那个间谍也跟着他上了地铁。那个间谍在车上杀死了他，拿走了比较重要的几张图纸，然后就把他的尸体扔出了车厢。这个解释可以涵盖所有的事实，不是吗？” “It seems to me perfectly clear,” said lestrade. “i have no doubt at all as to what occurred. He took the papers to sell them. He saw the agent. They could not agree as to price. He started home again, but the agent went with him. in the train the agent murdered him, took the more essential papers, and threw his body from the carriage. That would account for everything, would it not?”
“他身上为什么没有车票？” “Why had he no ticket?”
“车票会让别人知道那个间谍的住处离哪个车站最近，所以他就从死者的兜里掏走了车票。” “The ticket would have shown which station was nearest the agent’s house. Therefore he took it from the murdered man’s pocket.”
“很好，雷斯垂德，好极了，”福尔摩斯说道，“你的解释完全可以自圆其说。不过，照你这么说的话，这件案子也就没什么可办的啦。一方面，叛国者已经一命呜呼，另一方面，可想而知，布鲁斯-帕廷顿潜艇的图纸已经流到了欧洲大陆。咱们还能干什么呢？” “Good, lestrade, very good,” said Holmes. “Your theory holds together. But if this is true, then the case is at an end. on the one hand, the traitor is dead. on the other, the plans of the Bruce-Partington submarine are presumably already on the Continent. What is there for us to do?”
“行动啊，歇洛克——采取行动！”迈克罗夫特高声说道，一跃而起。“我所有的直觉都在提醒我，这种解释并不符合事实。把你的本事使出来！去犯罪现场看看！找相关的人谈谈！别放过任何线索！你干这行干了一辈子，眼下才是你报效国家的最好机会。” “To act, Sherlock—to act!” cried Mycroft, springing to his feet. “All my instincts are against this explanation. use your powers! Go to the scene of the crime! See the people concerned! leave no stone unturned! in all your career you have never had so great a chance of serving your country.”
“好吧，好吧！”福尔摩斯说道，耸了耸肩。“起身吧，华生！还有你，雷斯垂德，你能赏脸陪我们待那么一两个钟头吗？咱们的调查就从奥尔德盖特地铁站开始。再见，迈克罗夫特。天黑之前我就会给你一份报告，可我得把话说在头里，你千万别抱太大的希望。” “Well, well!” said Holmes, shrugging his shoulders. “Come, Watson! And you, lestrade, could you favour us with your company for an hour or two? We will begin our investigation by a visit to Aldgate Station. Good-bye, Mycroft. i shall let you have a report before evening, but i warn you in advance that you have little to expect.”
一个小时之后，福尔摩斯、雷斯垂德和我已经站在了地铁里，具体说就是铁轨穿出隧道、即将进入奥尔德盖特地铁站的地方。代表铁路公司接待我们的是一位彬彬有礼的红脸膛老先生。 An hour later Holmes, lestrade and i stood upon the underground railroad at the point where it emerges from the tunnel immediately before Aldgate Station. A courteous red-faced old gentleman represented the railway company.
“小伙子的尸体就是在那儿发现的，”老先生告诉我们，指了指离铁轨大约有三英尺的一个地方。“尸体不可能是从上面掉下来的，你们也看见了，上面都是光秃秃的墙壁。如此说来，尸体一定是从车上掉下来的，还有呢，根据我们的调查，出事的那趟车经过这里的时间一定是星期一夜里十二点左右。” “This is where the young man’s body lay,” said he, indicating a spot about three feet from the metals. “it could not have fallen from above, for these, as you see, are all blank walls. Therefore, it could only have come from a train, and that train, so far as we can trace it, must have passed about midnight on Monday.”
“你们检查过所有的车厢吗，有没有发现打斗的迹象呢？” “Have the carriages been examined for any sign of violence?”
“我们没有发现打斗的迹象，也没有找到车票。” “There are no such signs, and no ticket has been found.”
“有没有人报告车门没关呢？” “No record of a door being found open?”
“没有。” “None.”
“今天早上，我们了解到了一个新的情况，”雷斯垂德说道，“星期一夜里十一点四十左右，曾经有一趟普通的地铁列车从奥尔德盖特经过。那趟车上的一名乘客告诉我们，列车刚要进站的时候，他听到了一声沉重的闷响，似乎是有人掉到了地铁线上。只可惜当时雾很大，什么也看不见，所以他没有立刻报告这件事情。我说，福尔摩斯先生这是怎么啦？” “We have had some fresh evidence this morning,” said lestrade. “A passenger who passed Aldgate in an ordinary Metropolitan train about 11:40 on Monday night declares that he heard a heavy thud, as of a body striking the line, just before the train reached the station. There was dense fog, however, and nothing could be seen. He made no report of it at the time. Why, whatever is the matter with Mr. Holmes?”
我朋友站在一旁，死死地盯着铁轨蜿蜒穿出隧道的那个地方，表情极其专注。奥尔德盖特是一个中转站，铁轨上有一组列车转轨用的道岔。他那双探询的眼睛急切地注视着那些道岔，那张机警热切的脸庞也变成了我十分熟悉的模样，嘴唇紧绷、鼻翼微颤、浓密的双眉攒在了一起。 My friend was standing with an expression of strained intensity upon his face, staring at the railway metals where they curved out of the tunnel. Aldgate is a junction, and there was a network of points. on these his eager, questioning eyes were fixed, and i saw on his keen, alert face that tightening of the lips, that quiver of the nostrils, and concentration of the heavy, tufted brows which i knew so well.
“道岔，”他喃喃自语，“这些道岔。” “Points,” he muttered; “the points.”
“道岔怎么啦？你到底想说什么？” “What of it? What do you mean?”
“按我看，在这样的线路上，道岔的数目应该不算很多吧？” “I suppose there are no great number of points on a system such as this?”
“不多，实际上是非常少。” “No; there are very few.”
“还有啊，铁轨在这儿转了个弯。又有道岔，又有转弯。天哪！真要是这样的话。” “And a curve, too. Points, and a curve. By Jove! if it were only so.”
“真要是哪样，福尔摩斯先生？你有什么线索了吗？” “What is it, Mr. Holmes? Have you a clue?”
“我有个想法——只是个蒙蒙眬眬的想法，仅此而已。不过，这件案子确实是越来越有趣了。没见过，真是没见过，话又说回来，干吗不能是这样呢？照我看，地铁线上压根儿就没有血迹嘛。” “An idea—an indication, no more. But the case certainly grows in interest. unique, perfectly unique, and yet why not? i do not see any indications of bleeding on the line.”
“基本上没有。” “There were hardly any.”
“可是，我没记错的话，死者身上的伤很重啊。” “But I understand that there was a considerable wound.”
“他的骨头碎了，外伤倒是不怎么明显。” “The bone was crushed, but there was no great external injury.”
“即便如此，按理说也应该有点儿血迹的。那名乘客说他在大雾之中听见了重物落地的声音，我能检查一下他当时所在的那列地铁吗？” “And yet one would have expected some bleeding. Would it be possible for me to inspect the train which contained the passenger who heard the thud of a fall in the fog?”
“恐怕不行，福尔摩斯先生。那列地铁已经拆散，车厢也已经重新编挂。” “I fear not, Mr. Holmes. The train has been broken up before now, and the carriages redistributed.”
“我可以跟你打包票，福尔摩斯先生，”雷斯垂德说道，“我们已经仔细地检查过所有的车厢，这件事情是我亲自督办的。” “I can assure you, Mr. Holmes,” said lestrade, “that every carriage has been carefully examined. i saw to it myself.”
对于那些脑瓜子不如自己机灵的人，我朋友一向缺乏耐性，这是他最为明显的缺点之一。 It was one of my friend’s most obvious weaknesses that he was impatient with less alert intelligences than his own.
“你们多半是检查过，”他一边说，一边转身离去。“事实呢，我想要检查的东西并不是车厢。华生，咱们在这儿的事情已经办完了。我们不用再叨扰你啦，雷斯垂德先生。依我看，我们应该立刻把调查地点转移到伍利奇。” “Very likely,” said he, turning away. “As it happens, it was not the carriages which i desired to examine. Watson, we have done all we can here. We need not trouble you any further, Mr. lestrade. i think our investigations must now carry us to Woolwich.”
到了伦敦桥车站，福尔摩斯给他哥哥写了一封电报，发出去之前还给我看了看，电文如下： At london Bridge, Holmes wrote a telegram to his brother, which he handed to me before dispatching it. it ran thus:
> 案情已现曙光，惜其摇曳未定，尚有熄灭之虞。即请吩咐信差将政府所知英格兰境内外国间谍及国际特工之完整名单送往贝克街，名单应附各人详细地址，须在我返回之前送达。 > See some light in the darkness, but it may possibly flicker out. Meanwhile, please send by messenger, to await return at Baker Street, a complete list of all foreign spies or international agents known to be in England, with full address.
> 歇洛克 > Sherlock.
“这份名单应该能派上用场，华生，”我俩在开往伍利奇的火车上坐定之后，福尔摩斯说道，“说真的，咱们欠了迈克罗夫特老哥一个人情，因为他给咱们介绍了这么一件显然是极不寻常的案子。” “That should be helpful, Watson,” he remarked as we took our seats in the Woolwich train. “We certainly owe Brother Mycroft a debt for having introduced us to what promises to be a really very remarkable case.”
他那张热切的脸庞仍然显得极其专注、极其紧张，让我知道他一定是有了什么意味深长的新奇发现，由此打开了一条令人振奋的思路。瞧一瞧猎狐犬耷拉着耳朵、拖着尾巴在狗窝周围晃荡的光景，再瞧瞧它两眼放光、肌肉紧绷地全速追踪浓烈嗅迹[^9]的模样，两相对比，你就可以想象出福尔摩斯从晨间到此时的变化。短短几个小时之前，他还是一副有气无力、懒心无肠的模样，身上裹着一件鼠灰色的睡袍，在浓雾包围的房间里没完没了地逡巡游荡，到得此时，他已经完全变成了另外一个人。 His eager face still wore that expression of intense and high-strung energy, which showed me that some novel and suggestive circumstance had opened up a stimulating line of thought. See the foxhound with hanging ears and drooping tail as it lolls about the kennels, and compare it with the same hound as, with gleaming eyes and straining muscles, it runs upon a breast-high scent—such was the change in Holmes since the morning. He was a different man from the limp and lounging figure in the mouse-coloured dressing-gown who had prowled so restlessly only a few hours before round the fog-girt room.
“这案子还有文章可做，也还有转圜的余地，”他说道，“之前我竟然没有看到蕴藏其中的希望，真是迟钝得可以。” “There is material here. There is scope,” said he. “i am dull indeed not to have understood its possibilities.”
“即便到了现在，我还是看不到什么希望。” “Even now they are dark to me.”
“要说结局嘛，我也看不到，可我有了一个想法，兴许能带来很大的收获。这个人是在其他地方死的，尸体则被人放在了地铁列车的车顶。” “The end is dark to me also, but i have hold of one idea which may lead us far. The man met his death elsewhere, and his body was on the *roof* of a carriage.”
“车顶！” “On the roof!”
“挺不合常理的，对吗？可是，想想以下这些事实吧。尸体正好出现在铁轨上有道岔、列车必然会颠簸摇晃的地方，仅仅是一种巧合吗？车顶上的东西往下掉，按理说就该掉在这样的地方，不是吗？道岔可影响不到列车内部的东西。由此看来，尸体只可能是从车顶掉下来的，如其不然，咱们眼前的就是一种非常离奇的巧合。好了，再来看看血迹的问题。如果流血事件发生在其他地方的话，地铁线上自然不会有血迹。单个地看，这些事实已经算得上耐人寻味，合在一起的话，它们就有了相当可观的说服力。” “Remarkable, is it not? But consider the facts. is it a coincidence that it is found at the very point where the train pitches and sways as it comes round on the points? is not that the place where an object upon the roof might be expected to fall off? The points would affect no object inside the train. Either the body fell from the roof, or a very curious coincidence has occurred. But now consider the question of the blood. of course, there was no bleeding on the line if the body had bled elsewhere. Each fact is suggestive in itself. Together they have a cumulative force.”
“还有车票，车票也是一件！”我嚷嚷了一句。 “And the ticket, too!” i cried.
“一点儿不错。咱们本来解释不了死者为什么没有车票，这下子就可以解释了。所有的事情都对得上。” “Exactly. We could not explain the absence of a ticket. This would explain it. Everything fits together.”
“可是，就算你说得没错，咱们还是不知道他是怎么死的，这个谜题的答案还是跟以前一样遥远。说实在的，案情不但没有变得简单，反倒是更加离奇了哩。” “But suppose it were so, we are still as far as ever from unravelling the mystery of his death. indeed, it becomes not simpler but stranger.”
“兴许吧，”福尔摩斯若有所思地说道，“兴许。”他就此陷入了沉思，再也不曾开口，直到我们这趟慢腾腾的火车终于停在伍利奇车站为止。到站之后，他叫来一辆出租马车，把迈克罗夫特给他的那张纸片从兜里掏了出来。 “Perhaps,” said Holmes thoughtfully, “perhaps.” He relapsed into a silent reverie, which lasted until the slow train drew up at last in Woolwich Station. There he called a cab and drew Mycroft’s paper from his pocket.
“今天下午，咱们可有好几户人家需要走访呢，”他说道，“依我看，咱们的第一个拜访对象应该是詹姆斯·沃尔特爵士。 ” “We have quite a little round of afternoon calls to make,” said he. “I think that Sir James Walter claims our first attention.”
这位著名官员的住宅是一座漂亮的别墅，门前的葱绿草坪一直延伸到了泰晤士河畔。我俩赶到别墅门口的时候，浓雾渐渐散去，一抹惨淡稀薄的阳光突围而出。我俩拉响门铃，应门的是别墅的男管家。 The house of the famous official was a fine villa with green lawns stretching down to the Thames. As we reached it the fog was lifting, and a thin, watery sunshine was breaking through. A butler answered our ring.
“您找詹姆斯爵士啊，先生！”他神色肃穆地说道，“詹姆斯爵士今早去世了。” “Sir James, sir!” said he with solemn face. “Sir James died this morning.”
“天哪！”福尔摩斯惊叫一声。“他是怎么死的呢？” “Good heavens!” cried Holmes in amazement. “How did he die?”
“这个嘛，先生，你们要不要进来见见他弟弟瓦伦丁上校呢？” “Perhaps you would care to step in, sir, and see his brother, Colonel Valentine?”
“好吧，还是见一见比较好。” “Yes, we had best do so.”
管家把我俩领进了一个灯光暗淡的客厅，片刻之后，那位已故科学家的弟弟出来招呼我俩。他年约五十，身材很高，长相英俊，蓄着浅色的络腮胡子。只见他眼色惊惶、头发凌乱、双颊泪痕斑斑，全都在诉说突然降临的家门不幸。讲起这件事情的时候，他连话都说不利索了。 We were ushered into a dim-lit drawing-room, where an instant later we were joined by a very tall, handsome, light-bearded man of fifty, the younger brother of the dead scientist. His wild eyes, stained cheeks, and unkempt hair all spoke of the sudden blow which had fallen upon the household. He was hardly articulate as he spoke of it.
“怪就怪这桩可怕的丑闻，”他说道，“我哥哥詹姆斯爵士非常注重名誉，根本承受不了这样的事情。这桩丑闻打碎了他的心。他一向都为自己那个部门的效率深感自豪，对他来说，这桩丑闻完全是灭顶之灾。” “It was this horrible scandal,” said he. “My brother, Sir James, was a man of very sensitive honour, and he could not survive such an affair. it broke his heart. He was always so proud of the efficiency of his department, and this was a crushing blow.”
“我们这次来，原本还指望他给我们一点儿线索、帮助我们解决这件事情哩。” “We had hoped that he might have given us some indications which would have helped us to clear the matter up.”
“我可以跟你们保证，他跟你们、跟我们大家一样，压根儿就不知道这是怎么回事。他已经把他知道的情况全部告诉了警方。不用说，他完全确信这事情是卡多根·韦斯特干的。可是，其余的一切实在是让人没法想象。 ” “I assure you that it was all a mystery to him as it is to you and to all of us. He had already put all his knowledge at the disposal of the police. naturally he had no doubt that Cadogan West was guilty. But all the rest was inconceivable.”
“您能不能给我们一点儿新的线索呢？” “You cannot throw any new light upon the affair?”
“我自个儿什么都不知道，知道的都是些看来或者听来的东西。不是我不懂礼貌，福尔摩斯先生，不过您应该能够理解，我们眼下的处境非常狼狈，所以呢，我只能请你们赶快结束这次访问。” “I know nothing myself save what i have read or heard. i have no desire to be discourteous, but you can understand, Mr. Holmes, that we are much disturbed at present, and i must ask you to hasten this interview to an end.”
“这样的情况可真是出乎意料，”再次坐上马车之后，我朋友说道，“我倒想知道，这个可怜的老伙计究竟是自然死亡，还是自个儿把自个儿给杀了！如果是后一种情形的话，咱们能不能由此推测，他这是在谴责自己疏于职守呢？这个问题只能以后再说，眼下呢，咱们得去找卡多根·韦斯特的家人。” “This is indeed an unexpected development,” said my friend when we had regained the cab. “i wonder if the death was natural, or whether the poor old fellow killed himself! if the latter, may it be taken as some sign of self-reproach for duty neglected? We must leave that question to the future. now we shall turn to the Cadogan Wests.”
那位痛失爱子的母亲住在城郊，房子虽然不大，但却收拾得十分整洁。老太太伤心得神志不清，什么情况也提供不了。不过，她身边还坐着一位脸色苍白的年青女士。女士向我俩自报家门，原来，她就是死者的未婚妻维奥莱特·韦斯特伯里小姐，也是事发当晚最后一个见到死者的人。 A small but well-kept house in the outskirts of the town sheltered the bereaved mother. The old lady was too dazed with grief to be of any use to us, but at her side was a white-faced young lady, who introduced herself as Miss Violet Westbury, the fiancée of the dead man, and the last to see him upon that fatal night.
“我解释不了这件事情，福尔摩斯先生，”她说道，“祸事来了以后，我一天也没合过眼，没日没夜地想啊、想啊，想这件事情到底该怎么理解。阿瑟是这世上最忠诚可靠、最有男子气概、最爱国的男人，他宁愿砍掉自己的右手，也不会出卖国家托付给他的机密。只要是了解他的人，都会觉得这件事情岂有此理、绝无可能、荒唐透顶。” “I cannot explain it, Mr. Holmes,” she said. “i have not shut an eye since the tragedy, thinking, thinking, thinking, night and day, what the true meaning of it can be. Arthur was the most single-minded, chivalrous, patriotic man upon earth. He would have cut his right hand off before he would sell a State secret confided to his keeping. It is absurd, impossible, preposterous to anyone who knew him.”
“可是事实呢，韦斯特伯里小姐？” “But the facts, Miss Westbury?”
“是啊，是啊，我承认我解释不了。” “Yes, yes; i admit i cannot explain them.”
“他缺钱吗？” “Was he in any want of money?”
“不缺。他生活非常简朴，薪水完全够用。他攒下了几百镑，我俩本打算新年就结婚的。” “No; his needs were very simple and his salary ample. He had saved a few hundreds, and we were to marry at the new Year.”
“他有没有什么精神紧张的表现呢？说吧，韦斯特伯里小姐，别对我们隐瞒任何事情。” “No signs of any mental excitement? Come, Miss Westbury, be absolutely frank with us.”
我同伴目光如炬，早已注意到这位女士的神态发生了一点儿变化。听到他的问题，女士脸泛红晕，犹豫了一会儿。 The quick eye of my companion had noted some change in her manner. She coloured and hesitated.
“有的，”她终于开了口，“之前我确实觉得，他心里装着什么事情。” “Yes,” she said at last, “i had a feeling that there was something on his mind.”
“很久之前就开始了吗？” “For long?”
“大概也就是上个星期的事情。他显得心事重重，忧心忡忡。有一次我非要他说个明白，他才承认他心里确实有事，事情跟他的工作有关。他只是说：‘这事情极其严重，我不方便告诉别人，即便是你也不行。’别的我也问不出来了。” “Only for the last week or so. He was thoughtful and worried. once i pressed him about it. He admitted that there was something, and that it was concerned with his official life. ‘it is too serious for me to speak about, even to you,’ said he. i could get nothing more.”
福尔摩斯脸色一沉。 Holmes looked grave.
“接着说吧，韦斯特伯里小姐。就算您觉得这是在揭他的底儿，那也得接着说下去。您的话会引出怎样的结论，眼下还说不好呢。” “Go on, Miss Westbury. Even if it seems to tell against him, go on. We cannot say what it may lead to.”
“说真的，我没有什么可说的了。有那么一两次，我觉得他的话已经到了嘴边。还有一天晚上，他说起了那个机密的重要性，我隐约记得他当时说过，那些外国间谍肯定会出大价钱来收买那个机密。” “Indeed, i have nothing more to tell. once or twice it seemed to me that he was on the point of telling me something. He spoke one evening of the importance of the secret, and i have some recollection that he said that no doubt foreign spies would pay a great deal to have it.”
我朋友的脸色更加沉重。 My friend’s face grew graver still.
“还有别的吗？” “Anything else?”
“他还说，我们对这样的事情疏于防范，存心叛国的人很容易就可以弄到图纸。” “He said that we were slack about such matters—that it would be easy for a traitor to get the plans.”
“他只是最近才这么说吗？” “Was it only recently that he made such remarks?”
“是的，都是最近才说的。” “Yes, quite recently.”
“好了，给我们说说最后的那个晚上吧。” “Now tell us of that last evening.”
“我俩本来是要去剧院的。雾大坐不了马车，所以我俩就走着去。走到他办公室附近的时候，他突然拔腿就跑，消失在了大雾里面。” “We were to go to the theatre. The fog was so thick that a cab was useless. We walked, and our way took us close to the office. Suddenly he darted away into the fog.”
“一句话也没说吗？” “Without a word?”
“他只是惊叫了一声，别的就没了。我站在那里等他，可他始终没有回来，于是我就走回了家。第二天早上，办公室开了门，他们就跑来查问他的去向。大概十二点钟的时候，我们听到了那个可怕的消息。噢，福尔摩斯先生，您可千万、千万要替他挽回名誉啊！对他来说，名誉实在是太重要了。” “He gave an exclamation; that was all. i waited but he never returned. Then i walked home. next morning, after the office opened, they came to inquire. About twelve o’clock we heard the terrible news. oh, Mr. Holmes, if you could only, only save his honour! it was so much to him.”
福尔摩斯悲哀地摇了摇头。 Holmes shook his head sadly.
“走吧，华生，”他说道，“咱们得上别处去想办法。下一站，咱们必须得去丢失图纸的那间办公室。” “Come, Watson,” said he, “our ways lie elsewhere. our next station must be the office from which the papers were taken.
“形势本来就对这个小伙子非常不利，咱们的调查还让它雪上加霜，”马车辚辚开动的时候，他如是说道，“即将来临的婚事可以成为他的作案动机，因为他肯定会需要钱。他脑子里确实有这样的念头，因为他已经说了出来。还有啊，他差点儿就把那个姑娘变成了叛国的帮凶，因为他差点儿就把具体的计划告诉了她。这些情况都可以说是糟糕之极。” “It was black enough before against this young man, but our inquiries make it blacker,” he remarked as the cab lumbered off. “His coming marriage gives a motive for the crime. He naturally wanted money. The idea was in his head, since he spoke about it. He nearly made the girl an accomplice in the treason by telling her his plans. it is all very bad.”
“可是，福尔摩斯，人品总得占点儿分量吧？还有啊，他干吗要把姑娘扔在大街上、自个儿冲出去实施一项重大罪行呢？” “But surely, Holmes, character goes for something? Then, again, why should he leave the girl in the street and dart away to commit a felony?”
“没错！确实存在一些反证。不过，要靠它们把这件案子翻过来，恐怕是件难上加难的事情。” “Exactly! There are certainly objections. But it is a formidable case which they have to meet.”
高级职员西德尼·约翰逊先生在那间办公室里接待了我俩，态度也相当恭敬，正是人们见到我同伴名片之后的惯常反应。他是个身材瘦削、举止生硬的中年人，戴着一副眼镜，眼下他面色憔悴、双手颤抖，显然是承受着巨大的精神压力。 Mr. Sidney Johnson, the senior clerk, met us at the office and received us with that respect which my companion’s card always commanded. He was a thin, gruff, bespectacled man of middle age, his cheeks haggard, and his hands twitching from the nervous strain to which he had been subjected.
“糟糕啊，福尔摩斯先生，糟糕透了！您听说我们主管去世的事情了吗？” “It is bad, Mr. Holmes, very bad! Have you heard of the death of the chief?”
“我们刚刚才从他家那边过来。” “We have just come from his house.”
“这地方完全乱了套。主管死了，卡多根·韦斯特死了，我们的图纸也叫人偷了。可是，星期一傍晚关门的时候，我们的办公室还显得井井有条，可以跟任何一个政府部门媲美呢。我的天，想起来都让人害怕！世上这么多人，竟然是韦斯特做下了这种勾当！” “The place is disorganised. The chief dead, Cadogan West dead, our papers stolen. And yet, when we closed our door on Monday evening, we were as efficient an office as any in the government service. Good God, it’s dreadful to think of! That West, of all men, should have done such a thing!”
“这么说，您断定是他干的喽？” “You are sure of his guilt, then?”
“我看不到什么别的解释。话说回来，我真的相信他不是那种人，就跟相信我自个儿一样。” “I can see no other way out of it. And yet i would have trusted him as i trust myself.”
“星期一那天，你们这里是什么时间关的门呢？” “At what hour was the office closed on Monday?”
“五点钟。” “At five.”
“门是您关的吗？” “Did you close it?”
“我一向都是最后一个走的。” “I am always the last man out.”
“您走的时候，图纸在哪儿呢？” “Where were the plans?”
“就在那个保险柜里，我亲手放进去的。” “In that safe. I put them there myself.”
“楼里没有巡夜的人吗？” “Is there no watchman to the building?”
“有的，可他还得照看其他的几个部门。巡夜的是个老兵，人品绝对可靠。当晚他什么都没看见，当然喽，雾也确实非常大。” “There is, but he has other departments to look after as well. He is an old soldier and a most trustworthy man. He saw nothing that evening. of course the fog was very thick.”
“假设卡多根·韦斯特打算在下班之后闯进楼来，他得用上三把钥匙才能拿到图纸，对吗？” “Suppose that Cadogan West wished to make his way into the building after hours; he would need three keys, would he not, before he could reach the papers?”
“是的，确实得用上三把钥匙。楼门钥匙、办公室钥匙，还有保险柜的钥匙。” “Yes, he would. The key of the outer door, the key of the office, and the key of the safe.”
“只有您和詹姆斯·沃尔特爵士才有这些钥匙，对吗？” “Only Sir James Walter and you had those keys?”

之前我们已经把那具瘫软的躯体抬到了沙发上，到这会儿，我们的犯人坐了起来，神色惊惶地四下看了看，一只手捂住了额头，似乎是不敢相信自个儿的知觉。 We had carried the prostrate body to the sofa. now our prisoner sat up, looked round him with a horror-stricken face, and passed his hand over his forehead, like one who cannot believe his own senses.
“这是怎么回事？”他问道。“我是来拜访奥伯施泰因先生的。” “What is this?” he asked. “i came here to visit Mr. oberstein.”
“我们什么都知道了，沃尔特上校，”福尔摩斯说道，“我完全无法想象，一位英国绅士怎么能干出这样的事情。不过，我们已经掌握了你跟奥伯施泰因的关系和通信详情，也掌握了年青的卡多根·韦斯特遇害的经过。眼下我建议你忏悔罪行、从实招来，好歹为自己挽回一点儿局面，因为我们还有一些细节不太清楚，只能听你自个儿说。” “Everything is known, Colonel Walter,” said Holmes. “How an English gentleman could behave in such a manner is beyond my comprehension. But your whole correspondence and relations with oberstein are within our knowledge. So also are the circumstances connected with the death of young Cadogan West. let me advise you to gain at least the small credit for repentance and confession, since there are still some details which we can only learn from your lips.”
这个人哀叹一声，用双手捂住了脸。我们静静等待，可他还是一言不发。 The man groaned and sank his face in his hands. We waited, but he was silent.
“实话告诉你，”福尔摩斯说道，“基本的情况已经非常清楚。我们知道你迫切地需要钱，知道你复制了你哥哥掌管的钥匙，也知道你跟奥伯施泰因通信，而他通过《每日电讯报》的启事专栏来答复你。我们还知道，星期一晚间，你借着大雾的掩护去了办公室，可你没想到，年青的卡多根·韦斯特看见了你，而且跟在了你的后面，因为你多半是早就引起了他的疑心。他看见了你偷图纸，但却没法当场报警，怕的是万一你这是要把图纸送来伦敦、送到你哥哥那里去。作为一位正直的好公民，他置所有的个人得失于不顾，在大雾之中紧紧地跟着你，一直跟到了这座房子里面。到了这里，他出手干预，你呢，沃尔特上校，叛国之外，你又给自个儿揽上了更加可怕的谋杀罪名。” “I can assure you,” said Holmes, “that every essential is already known. We know that you were pressed for money; that you took an impress of the keys which your brother held; and that you entered into a correspondence with oberstein, who answered your letters through the advertisement columns of the *Daily Telegraph*. We are aware that you went down to the office in the fog on Monday night, but that you were seen and followed by young Cadogan West, who had probably some previous reason to suspect you. He saw your theft, but could not give the alarm, as it was just possible that you were taking the papers to your brother in london. leaving all his private concerns, like the good citizen that he was, he followed you closely in the fog and kept at your heels until you reached this very house. There he intervened, and then it was, Colonel Walter, that to treason you added the more terrible crime of murder.”
“我没有！我没有！老天在上，我发誓我没有杀他！”惨状可鄙的犯人叫了起来。 “I did not! i did not! Before God i swear that i did not!” cried our wretched prisoner.
“那你倒是说说，被你们搁到地铁车顶之前，卡多根·韦斯特是怎么死的。 ” “Tell us, then, how Cadogan West met his end before you laid him upon the roof of a railway carriage.”
“我说，我发誓我马上就说。其他的事情确实是我干的，我全都承认。事情跟您说的一样，我在股市上欠了债，迫切地需要钱，奥伯施泰因开出了五千镑的价码，我这是为了逃脱身败名裂的结局。不过，说到杀人的事情，我跟你们几位一样无辜。” “I will. i swear to you that i will. i did the rest. i confess it. It was just as you say. A Stock Exchange debt had to be paid. i needed the money badly. oberstein offered me five thousand. it was to save myself from ruin. But as to murder, i am as innocent as you.”
“那么，到底是怎么回事呢？” “What happened, then?”
“韦斯特一早就起了疑心，所以就跟在了我的后面，跟您说的一样。我一直走到了这座房子的门口，始终没发现他在盯我的梢。当时雾很大，三码之外就看不见东西。我敲了两下门，奥伯施泰因出来给我开门，那个小伙子冲了上来，质问我俩打算把那些图纸怎么样。奥伯施泰因有一根短短的防身手杖[^21]，时刻都带在身上。韦斯特跟着我俩闯进房子之后，奥伯施泰因就用手杖照他的脑袋来了一下。这一下足以致命，他不到五分钟就死了。他就那么躺在门厅里，我俩不知道如何是好。接下来，奥伯施泰因想到了他的后窗，想到列车经常会停在窗子下面。不过，搬运尸体之前，他先检查了一下我带去的那些图纸。检查完之后，他跟我说，他必须拿走其中的三张图纸，因为那三张尤其重要。‘你不能拿走，’我说。‘不还回去的话，伍利奇那边会闹翻天的。 ’‘我必须拿走，’他说，‘因为这些图纸太复杂，我来不及进行复制。’‘那我也没办法，今晚就得把所有的图纸一起还回去，’我说。他想了一会儿，然后就大叫一声，说他有了一个主意。‘我拿三张，’他说，‘其余的就塞到这个小伙子的口袋里。等他们找到尸体的时候，肯定会把所有的事情算到他的头上。’我看不到什么别的出路，我俩就照他说的做了。我俩在窗边等了半个小时，终于有一列地铁停了下来。外面的雾很大，不用怕别人看见，我俩没费什么周折就把韦斯特的尸体搁到了车顶。我知道的只有这些，别的我就不知道了。” “He had his suspicions before, and he followed me as you describe. I never knew it until i was at the very door. it was thick fog, and one could not see three yards. i had given two taps and oberstein had come to the door. The young man rushed up and demanded to know what we were about to do with the papers. oberstein had a short life-preserver. He always carried it with him. As West forced his way after us into the house oberstein struck him on the head. The blow was a fatal one. He was dead within five minutes. There he lay in the hall, and we were at our wit’s end what to do. Then oberstein had this idea about the trains which halted under his back window. But first he examined the papers which I had brought. He said that three of them were essential, and that he must keep them. ‘You cannot keep them,’ said I. ‘There will be a dreadful row at Woolwich if they are not returned.’ ‘I must keep them,’ said he, ‘for they are so technical that it is impossible in the time to make copies.’ ‘Then they must all go back together to-night,’ said i. He thought for a little, and then he cried out that he had it. ‘Three I will keep,’ said he. ‘The others we will stuff into the pocket of this young man. When he is found the whole business will assuredly be put to his account.’ i could see no other way out of it, so we did as he suggested. We waited half an hour at the window before a train stopped. It was so thick that nothing could be seen, and we had no difficulty in lowering West’s body on to the train. That was the end of the matter so far as i was concerned.”
“你哥哥呢？” “And your brother?”
“他什么也没说，可他有一次看见了我摆弄他的钥匙，所以我想，他应该是起了疑心。看他的眼神，他确实是起了疑心。你们也知道，从那以后，他再也没有抬起头来。” “He said nothing, but he had caught me once with his keys, and I think that he suspected. i read in his eyes that he suspected. As you know, he never held up his head again.”

“我还能做什么补救呢？” “What reparation can i make?”
“奥伯施泰因拿着图纸上哪儿去了呢？” “Where is oberstein with the papers?”
“我不知道。” “I do not know.”

“他说可以把信写到巴黎的卢浮宫酒店[^22]，会有人转交给他。” “He said that letters to the Hotel du louvre, Paris, would eventually reach him.”
“如此说来，你还有补救的机会，”歇洛克·福尔摩斯说道。 “Then reparation is still within your power,” said Sherlock Holmes.

目标确实上了钩！历史的真实是——我说的是一个国家的秘史，它往往会比那些公开的记录亲切得多也有趣得多——急于完成毕生伟业的奥伯施泰因直奔诱饵而来，跟着就在英国的监狱里踏踏实实地待了十五年。警方在他的行囊里找到了那三张价值无可估量的布鲁斯-帕廷顿图纸，落网之前，他已经把它们变成了拍卖商品，邀请欧洲大陆所有的海军强国参与竞价。 And it did! it is a matter of history—that secret history of a nation which is often so much more intimate and interesting than its public chronicles—that oberstein, eager to complete the coup of his lifetime, came to the lure and was safely engulfed for fifteen years in a British prison. in his trunk were found the invaluable Bruce-Partington plans, which he had put up for auction in all the naval centres of Europe.
刑期第二年的末尾，沃尔特上校死在了监狱里。福尔摩斯嘛，他又一次精神抖擞地拿起了纸笔，继续撰写他那篇关于拉叙斯复调圣歌的论文。那篇论文现已在私人圈子之中付梓流传，按照各位专家的看法，它是这个问题的盖棺论定之作。结案几个星期之后，我偶然得知我朋友去温莎镇[^24]待了一天，回来的时候还捎上了一枚精美异常的翡翠领针。我问他领针是不是买的，他回答说，领针是一位特别大方的女士送的，因为他曾经适逢其会，帮那位女士办过一件小小的事情。他没有再往下说，可我倒是觉得，我应该猜得出那位女士的尊贵姓名，同时我也绝不怀疑，那枚翡翠领针将会成为一个永远的留念，时时唤起我朋友对于布鲁斯-帕廷顿图纸一案的回忆。 Colonel Walter died in prison towards the end of the second year of his sentence. As to Holmes, he returned refreshed to his monograph upon the Polyphonic Motets of lassus, which has since been printed for private circulation, and is said by experts to be the last word upon the subject. Some weeks afterwards i learned incidentally that my friend spent a day at Windsor, whence he returned with a remarkably fine emerald tie-pin. When i asked him if he had bought it, he answered that it was a present from a certain gracious lady in whose interests he had once been fortunate enough to carry out a small commission. He said no more; but i fancy that i could guess at that lady’s august name, and I have little doubt that the emerald pin will forever recall to my friend’s memory the adventure of the Bruce-Partington plans.
