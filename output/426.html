“恐怖统治”时期的一名委员 A Committee-Man of ‘The Terror’
作者：托马斯·哈代 by Thomas Hardy
我们一直在谈论那个如今风光不再的海滨胜地在乔治王朝时期的辉煌。那里的建筑都是1800年的风格，由结实的赤褐色和暗褐色砖块建造而成，在今天看来就像索霍区或布鲁姆斯伯里区的某条街道的一侧被搬到了海边。那些对坚固构造毫无鉴赏力的现代游客看了只会付之一笑。 We had been talking of the Georgian glories of our old-fashioned watering-place, which now, with its substantial russet-red and dun brick buildings in the style of the year eighteen hundred, looks like one side of a Soho or Bloomsbury Street transported to the shore, and draws a smile from the modern tourist who has no eye for solidity of build.
笔者很年轻，只是坐在那里听着。 The writer, quite a youth, was present merely as a listener.
他们聊着聊着就从寻常话题谈到了一些特别的事情。H老夫人尽管已到耄耋之年，记忆力还跟从前一样好。她绘声绘色地讲起一个已经重复过许多遍的故事，把大家都给吸引住了。我们这位年迈的朋友从她母亲那里听到这个故事时还是个小女孩，这件事是当地一个充满戏剧性的事件，深深地影响了她母亲的一位朋友——法语家庭教师V小姐的一生。 The conversation proceeded from general subjects to particular, until old Mrs. H—, whose memory was as perfect at eighty as it had ever been in her life, interested us all by the obvious fidelity with which she repeated a story many times related to her by her mother when our aged friend was a girl—a domestic drama much affecting the life of an acquaintance of her said parent, one Mademoiselle V—, a teacher of French.
事情发生在1802到1803年间，那时正是我们国家与法国处于短暂和平之际，小镇一派繁荣兴盛之景。 The incidents occurred in the town during the heyday of its fortunes, at the time of our brief peace with France in 1802-3.
“几年前，我母亲刚去世不久时，我就将事情写成了故事，”H夫人说道， ‘I wrote it down in the shape of a story some years ago, just after my mother’s death,’ said Mrs. H—.
“现在它就锁在我的书桌里。” ‘It is locked up in my desk there now.’
“读读吧！”我们说。 ‘Read it!’ said we.
“不行，”她说，“光线不好，再说我也记得很清楚，字字句句都记得清清楚楚。” ‘No,’ said she; ‘the light is bad, and I can remember it well enough, word for word, flourishes and all.’
这样一来，我们也没有别的选择，她就开始讲了。 We could not be choosers in the circumstances, and she began.
“故事中当然有两位主人公，一男一女。她与他初次相识于九月的一个傍晚。 ‘There are two in it, of course, the man and the woman, and it was on an evening in September that she first got to know him.
海滨广场上一年到头都难得有如此盛大的聚会。 There had not been such a grand gathering on the Esplanade all the season.
乔治三世国王陛下携王妃公主和王室公爵们莅临盛会，300多位其他上流贵族和社会名流当时也齐聚镇上。 His Majesty King George the Third was present, with all the princesses and royal dukes, while upwards of three hundred of the general nobility and other persons of distinction were also in the town at the time.
从伦敦或别处过来的马车和其他车辆络绎不绝；其中一辆破旧的公共马车从黑文普尔来，一路沿海边的一条旁道行驶，最后在一家二流旅馆前停了下来，没怎么引起大家的注意。 Carriages and other conveyances were arriving every minute from London and elsewhere; and when among the rest a shabby stage-coach came in by a by-route along the coast from Havenpool, and drew up at a second-rate tavern, it attracted comparatively little notice.
从这辆落满灰尘的马车上下来一个男人，他将不多的行李暂时寄存在前台，之后就沿着街道走去，像是要找个落脚的地方。 ‘From this dusty vehicle a man alighted, left his small quantity of luggage temporarily at the office, and walked along the street as if to look for lodgings.
他45岁左右——也可能有50岁——穿着一件长外套，上等的布料已经褪了色，领子厚重，颈巾皱缩成一团。 ‘He was about forty-five—possibly fifty—and wore a long coat of faded superfine cloth, with a heavy collar, and a hunched-up neckcloth.
他似乎不想引起别人的注意。 He seemed to desire obscurity.
不过，聚会的阵势好像立刻吸引了他，他向街上遇见的一个乡下人打听发生了什么事情；听他说话的口音，英语发音对他来说挺困难。 ‘But the display appeared presently to strike him, and he asked of a rustic he met in the street what was going on; his accent being that of one to whom English pronunciation was difficult.
乡下人略有些惊讶地看着他，说道：‘乔治国王陛下和整个王室都来了。’ ‘The countryman looked at him with a slight surprise, and said, “King Jarge is here and his royal Cwort.”
外地人问及他们是否会呆很久。 ‘The stranger inquired if they were going to stay long.
‘不知道，先生。 ‘“Don’t know, Sir.
我猜跟从前一样。’ Same as they always do, I suppose.”
‘以前会呆多久？’ ‘“How long is that?”
‘呆到十月份的某个时候。 “‘Till some time in October.
从1789年开始，他们每年夏天都会来这里。’ They’ve come here every summer since eighty-nine.”
外地人沿着圣托马斯街继续向前，走到港湾回水处的桥上，那个地方跟现在一样，一端连着老镇，另一端连着更加现代的新区。 ‘The stranger moved onward down St. Thomas Street, and approached the bridge over the harbour backwater, that then, as now, connected the old town with the more modern portion.
在夕阳的笼罩下，一抹斜晖照亮了港湾，男子向西望去，阳光落在他帽檐下的脸庞上，照亮了他的双眼。 The spot was swept with the rays of a low sun, which lit up the harbour lengthwise, and shone under the brim of the man’s hat and into his eyes as he looked westward.
迎着落日，一个个身影从桥上与他迎面而过；其中就有后来与我母亲相识的V小姐。 Against the radiance figures were crossing in the opposite direction to his own; among them this lady of my mother’s later acquaintance, Mademoiselle V—.
她出身于法国一个古老的大户人家。那时她脸色苍白，28或30岁左右的样子，身材高挑，线条优美，穿着打扮倒是朴素寻常，那天傍晚（据她说）围着一条麦斯林纱的小披巾，按照那时流行的穿法绕过胸前，系在背后。 She was the daughter of a good old French family, and at that date a pale woman, twenty-eight or thirty years of age, tall and elegant in figure, but plainly dressed and wearing that evening (she said) a small muslin shawl crossed over the bosom in the fashion of the time, and tied behind.
一看到他的脸——她过去常常这么跟我们说——那张在夕照下异常清晰的脸，她便禁不住发出一声惊恐的低呼，其中的缘由很可怕，与她的过去有关。往前走了没几步她就倒在桥栏杆下，昏了过去。 ‘At sight of his face, which, as she used to tell us, was unusually distinct in the peering sunlight, she could not help giving a little shriek of horror, for a terrible reason connected with her history, and after walking a few steps further, she sank down against the parapet of the bridge in a fainting fit.
“那名外国男子正兀自出神，一开始没怎么注意到她，但她莫名其妙就昏倒在地，马上引起了他的注意。 ‘In his preoccupation the foreign gentleman had hardly noticed her, but her strange collapse immediately attracted his attention.
他立刻穿过车道去扶她起来，并将她抱到离桥最近的店铺里，解释说这位女士在外面突然病倒了。 He quickly crossed the carriageway, picked her up, and carried her into the first shop adjoining the bridge, explaining that she was a lady who had been taken ill outside.
她很快就苏醒过来了；但这位热心人显然困惑不已，因为他觉察到她依然害怕他，而且这种恐惧让她一时无法完全恢复自制。 ‘She soon revived; but, clearly much puzzled, her helper perceived that she still had a dread of him which was sufficient to hinder her complete recovery of self-command.
她紧张不安、语速飞快地嘱咐店主，请他叫辆马车来。 She spoke in a quick and nervous way to the shopkeeper, asking him to call a coach.
“店主出去叫马车了，留下V—小姐和外地人，两人都是局促不安、沉默不语。 ‘This the shopkeeper did, Mademoiselle V—and the stranger remaining in constrained silence while he was gone.
马车来了，她告诉车夫地址，然后就上车离开了。 The coach came up, and giving the man the address, she entered it and drove away.
‘那位女士是谁？’这位刚到此地不久的先生问道。 ‘“Who is that lady?” said the newly arrived gentleman.
‘她与你来自同一个国家，要是我猜得没错的话。’店主说。 ‘“She’s of your nation, as I should make bold to suppose,” said the shopkeeper.
他告诉这名男子那是V小姐，纽博尔德将军家的家庭教师，就住在这个镇上。 And he told the other that she was Mademoiselle V—, governess at General Newbold’s, in the same town.
‘你们这儿的外国人多吗？’外地男子问道。 ‘“You have many foreigners here?” the stranger inquired.
‘挺多的，不过大多是德国汉诺威人。 ‘“Yes, though mostly Hanoverians.
不过自英法和平以来，上流社会的人纷纷学习法语，还挺需要法语教员的。’ But since the peace they are learning French a good deal in genteel society, and French instructors are rather in demand.”
‘没错，我就教法语，’来访者说， ‘“Yes, I teach it,” said the visitor.
‘我希望能在哪所学校觅个教员的职位。’ “I am looking for a tutorship in an academy.”
这位英国市民给法国男人提供的信息似乎没法解释他那位法国女同胞的行为——也确实如此——于是他离开店铺，继续过了桥，沿南边的码头向老屋客栈走去，他在那里订了一间房。 ‘The information given by the burgess to the Frenchman seemed to explain to the latter nothing of his countrywoman’s conduct—which, indeed, was the case—and he left the shop, taking his course again over the bridge and along the south quay to the Old Rooms Inn, where he engaged a bedchamber.
那位女子一见到他就显得那么激动不安，自然让这个初来乍到的人思忖了好一会儿。 ‘Thoughts of the woman who had betrayed such agitation at sight of him lingered naturally enough with the newcomer.
尽管我说过，V小姐已年近30，但与他同是法国人，长得又非常秀美雅致，于是激起了这位中年男子异乎寻常的兴趣。她那双乌黑的大眼睛，睁开后却又躲闪着他，展现出一种惹人怜爱的美，几乎没有任何男人能够对此无动于衷。 Though, as I stated, not much less than thirty years of age, Mademoiselle V—, one of his own nation, and of highly refined and delicate appearance, had kindled a singular interest in the middle-aged gentleman’s breast, and her large dark eyes, as they had opened and shrunk from him, exhibited a pathetic beauty to which hardly any man could have been insensible.
第二天，写完几封信后他就出了门，到小镇《指南》和当地报纸的办事处登出消息，说有一名能教授法语和书法的教师新到小镇。他在书店也留了张卡片告知这个消息。 ‘The next day, having written some letters, he went out and made known at the office of the town “Guide” and of the newspaper, that a teacher of French and calligraphy had arrived, leaving a card at the bookseller’s to the same effect.
随后他就漫无目的地闲逛，但最后还是向人问起纽博尔顿将军家怎么走。 He then walked on aimlessly, but at length inquired the way to General Newbold’s.
到了门口，没有告知姓名，他就说想见见V小姐。有人将他领进了后面的一间小会客室，她过来时惊诧地盯着他。 At the door, without giving his name, he asked to see Mademoiselle V—, and was shown into a little back parlour, where she came to him with a gaze of surprise.
‘我的天哪！ ‘“My God!
你为什么擅自闯了进来，先生？’一看到他的脸，她就倒抽了口气，用法语喊道。 Why do you intrude here, Monsieur?” she gasped in French as soon as she saw his face.
‘你昨天突然病倒。 ‘“You were taken ill yesterday.
我帮了你。 I helped you.
要不是我扶你起来，你有可能已经被车子给碾过去了。 You might have been run over if I had not picked you up.
当然我的举动纯粹出于善意，但我想我应该过来问问你康复了没有？’ It was an act of simple humanity certainly; but I thought I might come to ask if you had recovered?”
她早就转过脸去，几乎没听清他说了什么。 ‘She had turned aside, and had scarcely heard a word of his speech.
‘我恨你，无耻的家伙！’她说， “I hate you, infamous man!” she said.
‘我不屑于你的帮助。 “I cannot bear your helping me.
你走吧！’ Go away!”
‘但我们素不相识啊。’ ‘“But you are a stranger to me.”
‘我对你可清楚得很！’ ‘“I know you too well!”
‘这样的话你有优势，小姐。 ‘“You have the advantage then, Mademoiselle.
我刚来这里。 I am a newcomer here.
在我的印象中以前从没见过你；我当然不会、也不可能去怨恨你。’ I never have seen you before to my knowledge; and I certainly do not, could not, hate you.”
‘你不就是B先生吗？’ ‘“Are you not Monsieur B—?”
他退缩了。 ‘He flinched.
‘我是——在巴黎是，’他说， “I am—in Paris,” he said.
‘但在这里，我是G先生。’ “But here I am Monsieur G—.”
‘这不重要。 ‘“That is trivial.
你就是我说的那个人。’ You are the man I say you are.”
‘小姐，你怎么知道我的真名？’ ‘“How did you know my real name, Mademoiselle?”
‘好多年前我就见过你，你那时没看见我。 ‘“I saw you in years gone by, when you did not see me.
你以前是国民公会下面救国委员会的成员。’ You were formerly Member of the Committee of Public Safety, under the Convention.”
‘是的。’ “I was.”
‘你将我的父亲、兄长和叔叔送上了断头台——几乎害了我全家人，我母亲也因此伤心欲绝。 ‘“You guillotined my father, my brother, my uncle—all my family, nearly, and broke my mother’s heart.
他们除了保持沉默可什么都没做。 They had done nothing but keep silence.
你们只是凭空猜测他们的想法。 Their sentiments were only guessed.
他们的无头尸体被随意丢弃在穆索公墓的沟渠里，用石灰给销毁了。’ Their headless corpses were thrown indiscriminately into the ditch of the Mousseaux Cemetery, and destroyed with lime.”
他点点头。 ‘He nodded.
‘你害得我无亲无故，如今一个人流落在异国他乡。’ ‘“You left me without a friend, and here I am now, alone in a foreign land.”
‘我很抱歉，’他说， ‘“I am sorry for you,” said he.
‘对造成的后果深表歉意，但我的意图没什么不对。 “Sorry for the consequence, not for the intent.
我只是凭良心行事，而且从某个角度上说，我做得对，尽管你难以理解。 What I did was a matter of conscience, and, from a point of view indiscernible by you, I did right.
我没有得到任何好处。 I profited not a farthing.
但我也不会为此争辩。 But I shall not argue this.
看到我也背井离乡，贫困潦倒，被同伴背弃，跟你一样无依无靠，这样你总该觉得高兴了吧。’ You have the satisfaction of seeing me here an exile also, in poverty, betrayed by comrades, as friendless as yourself.”
‘没什么可高兴的，先生。’ ‘“It is no satisfaction to me, Monsieur.”
‘嗯，事已至此，也无法改变了。 ‘“Well, things done cannot be altered.
我现在的问题是：你完全康复了吗？’ Now the question: are you quite recovered?”
‘对你还是感到厌恶和恐惧——要说身体，已经康复了。’ ‘“Not from dislike and dread of you—otherwise, yes.”
‘再见，小姐。’ ‘“Good morning, Mademoiselle.”
‘再见。’ ‘“Good morning.”
之后他们没有再见过彼此，直到有天晚上又在剧院里碰到（费好大劲才能劝服我母亲的这位朋友常常去剧院，以提高她的英文发音，那时她逗趣地想，以后能回自己国家当英文教师了）。 ‘They did not meet again till one evening at the theatre (which my mother’s friend was with great difficulty induced to frequent, to perfect herself in English pronunciation, the idea she entertained at that time being to become a teacher of English in her own country later on).
她发现他就坐在自己旁边，顿时脸色苍白，坐立难安。 She found him sitting next to her, and it made her pale and restless.
‘你仍然怕我吗？’ ‘“You are still afraid of me?”
‘是的。 ‘“I am.
哦，你就不明白吗！’ O cannot you understand!”
他表示自己明白。 ‘He signified the affirmative.
‘我觉得很难跟上剧情。’他紧接着说。 ‘“I follow the play with difficulty,” he said, presently.
‘我也很难跟上——尤其是现在。’她说。 ‘“So do I—now,” said she.
他久久地凝视着她，她也察觉到他的目光；虽然一直看着舞台，她的双眼却盈满了泪水。 ‘He regarded her long, and she was conscious of his look; and while she kept her eyes on the stage they filled with tears.
她仍旧坐着一动不动，能明显地看到眼泪从她脸颊滑落，尽管这出戏轻松欢快，正是谢里登先生的喜剧《情敌》，由S. 肯布尔先生饰演阿布所鲁特上尉。 Still she would not move, and the tears ran visibly down her cheek, though the play was a merry one, being no other than Mr. Sheridan’s comedy of “The Rivals,” with Mr. S. Kemble as Captain Absolute.
他觉察到了她的痛苦，知道她的心思已经不在表演上了。幕间休息时，他突然起身离开了剧院。 He saw her distress, and that her mind was elsewhere; and abruptly rising from his seat at candle-snuffing time he left the theatre.
虽然他住在老镇，她在新镇，两人却常常远远地就看到对方。 ‘Though he lived in the old town, and she in the new, they frequently saw each other at a distance.
有时是她在港湾北边，站在渡口旁等船来载她过河。 One of these occasions was when she was on the north side of the harbour, by the ferry, waiting for the boat to take her across.
他就站在对岸码头的科夫街旁。 He was standing by Cove Row, on the quay opposite.
船来了，她并没有上船，而是从码头后退几步；她想看看男子是否还在，就瞥见他手指着渡船。 Instead of entering the boat when it arrived she stepped back from the quay; but looking to see if he remained she beheld him pointing with his finger to the ferry-boat.
‘上船！’他喊道，声音大到她能够听见。 ‘“Enter!” he said, in a voice loud enough to reach her.
V小姐站着不动。 ‘Mademoiselle V—stood still.
‘上船！’他喊道，看她不动，又唤了一次。 ‘“Enter!” he said, and, as she did not move, he repeated the word a third time.
她确实是要过河，于是走上前去，踏进了船里。 ‘She had really been going to cross, and now approached and stepped down into the boat.
就是不抬眼她也知道他一直在看着她过河。 Though she did not raise her eyes she knew that he was watching her over.
要上岸时，她从帽檐下瞥见一只手伸了下来。 At the landing steps she saw from under the brim of her hat a hand stretched down.
上岸的台阶又陡又滑。 The steps were steep and slippery.
‘不用了，先生，’她说， ‘“No, Monsieur,” she said.
‘除非，你真正信仰上帝，并且为过去的罪恶忏悔！’ “Unless, indeed, you believe in God, and repent of your evil past!”
‘我很抱歉令你痛苦。 ‘“I am sorry you were made to suffer.
但我唯一的信仰是理性，我不会忏悔的。 But I only believe in the god called Reason, and I do not repent.
我只是履行了一项国家原则。 I was the instrument of a national principle.
你的朋友也不是因为我的任何利益而牺牲的。’ Your friends were not sacrificed for any ends of mine.”
于是她没有伸出手，而是自己登上了岸。 ‘She thereupon withheld her hand, and clambered up unassisted.
他继续朝前走，登上卢考特山，消失在了山顶。 He went on, ascending the Look-out Hill, and disappearing over the brow.
她也得朝那个方向走，因为她得负责将照看的两个小女孩送回家，而两个孩子都跑到悬崖上散步去了。 Her way was in the same direction, her errand being to bring home the two young girls under her charge, who had gone to the cliff for an airing.
在崖顶找到她们时，她看见更远处悬崖边男子孤独的身影，他正面朝大海静静地站着。 When she joined them at the top she saw his solitary figure at the further edge, standing motionless against the sea.
她跟学生们呆在那里时，他一直站着没有转身，似乎在望着锚地的护卫舰，但更有可能是在沉思，不知自己身在何处。 All the while that she remained with her pupils he stood without turning, as if looking at the frigates in the roadstead, but more probably in meditation, unconscious where he was.
离开的时候，一个孩子扔掉了吃剩的半块松糕饼干。 In leaving the spot one of the children threw away half a sponge-biscuit that she had been eating.
走近的时候他弯下腰来，小心地拾起饼干，放进了口袋。 Passing near it he stooped, picked it up carefully, and put it in his pocket.
V小姐往家走去，问自己道：‘他是饿坏了吗？’ ‘Mademoiselle V—came homeward, asking herself, “Can he be starving?”
那天之后，她很长时间没再见到他，她甚至想他是不是彻底离开了。 ‘From that day he was invisible for so long a time that she thought he had gone away altogether.
然而一天晚上，她收到了一封便笺，于是颤抖着打开了。 But one evening a note came to her, and she opened it trembling.
‘我病倒了，’信上写道，‘你也知道，我孤身一人。 ‘“I am here ill,” it said, “and, as you know, alone.

换句话说，既然放弃这份柔情毫无益处，何不沉溺其中。 In other words, why not indulge her tenderness, since its nullification could do no good.
她就这样坐在马车里不断自省，马车一路经过了卡斯特桥、肖特福特，到了梅尔彻斯特的白鹿镇，在那里，她所有的打算都破灭了。 ‘Thus she held self-communion in her seat in the coach, passing through Casterbridge, and Shottsford, and on to the White Hart at Melchester, at which place the whole fabric of her recent intentions crumbled down.
事已至此，还不如坚定不移。顺其自然、勇敢地嫁给这个让她如此刻骨铭心的男人吧。 Better be staunch having got so far; let things take their course, and marry boldly the man who had so impressed her.
他是多么伟大，而她又是多么渺小！ How great he was; how small was she!
她还擅自对他作出评判！ And she had presumed to judge him!
她轻率地坐上马车，现在又鲁莽地下了马车，站在那里一直等到马车开走。据她后来回忆，星空下坐在车厢外面的乘客渐渐远离的身影中，有什么东西令她心头一惊。 Abandoning her place in the coach with the precipitancy that had characterized her taking it, she waited till the vehicle had driven off, something in the departing shapes of the outside passengers against the starlit sky giving her a start, as she afterwards remembered.
紧接着回小镇的马车‘黎明先驱’进了城，她匆忙在马车顶层找了个位置。 Presently the down coach, “The Morning Herald,” entered the city, and she hastily obtained a place on the top.
‘我要坚定不移——我要成为他的妻子——即使这会让我的灵魂万劫不复！’她说。 ‘“I’ll be firm—I’ll be his—if it cost me my immortal soul!” she said.
她不安地喘息着，坐车沿原路返回。 And with troubled breathings she journeyed back over the road she had just traced.
破晓时分，她到达了我们的皇家海滨胜地，第一个念头就是回到自己租住了几天的房间。 ‘She reached our royal watering-place by the time the day broke, and her first aim was to get back to the hired room in which her last few days had been spent.
她紧张地叫门，女房东开门时，她尽力向她解释自己为何突然离开又突然回来；她提出要再租一天房间，女房东欣然答应。她上楼进了房间，坐下来时还气喘吁吁。 When the landlady appeared at the door in response to Mademoiselle V—’s nervous summons, she explained her sudden departure and return as best she could; and no objection being offered to her re-engagement of the room for one day longer she ascended to the chamber and sat down panting.
她又回来了，这次疯狂的出逃只与他有关，但她打算只字不提。 She was back once more, and her wild tergiversations were a secret from him whom alone they concerned.
壁炉架上有个密封的信件。 ‘A sealed letter was on the mantelpiece.
‘是的，是给您的信，小姐，’跟着进来的女人说， “Yes, it is directed to you, Mademoiselle,” said the woman who had followed her.
‘我们正在想该怎么处置呢。 “But we were wondering what to do with it.
你昨晚离开后，镇上的一名信差送过来的。’ A town messenger brought it after you had gone last night.”
女房东走后，V小姐拆开信读起来—— ‘When the landlady had left, Mademoiselle V—opened the letter and read—
‘我亲爱的、尊敬的朋友： “MY DEAR AND HONOURED FRIEND.
自我们相识以来，你对自己的担忧从来直言不讳。 —You have been throughout our acquaintance absolutely candid concerning your misgivings.
但我对自己的忧虑却有所保留。 But I have been reserved concerning mine.
这就是我们之间的不同。 That is the difference between us.
你可能不知道，你对我们婚姻的每一分疑虑，我心里都同样深切地感受到了。 You probably have not guessed that every qualm you have felt on the subject of our marriage has been paralleled in my heart to the full.
昨天你因为懊悔而不自觉地情绪爆发，尽管当着你的面我硬生生地反对你的想法，但我们的结合是否明智，对此我也心存疑虑，你的懊悔成了最后一根稻草，沉重得让我再也无法承受。 Thus it happened that your involuntary outburst of remorse yesterday, though mechanically deprecated by me in your presence, was a last item in my own doubts on the wisdom of our union, giving them a force that I could no longer withstand.
我回家了。经过再三考虑，既然我如此尊敬和爱慕你，我决定还你自由。 I came home; and, on reflection, much as I honour and adore you, I decide to set you free.
我这一生都奉献给了自由这项事业，也不妨说是为了自由而牺牲。我不能听任你的评判（这个评判很可能一辈子都不会改变）受到可能只是一时感情的左右，因此而得不到抒发。 “As one whose life has been devoted, and I may say sacrificed, to the cause of Liberty, I cannot allow your judgment (probably a permanent one) to be fettered beyond release by a feeling which may be transient only.
如果我亲口向你宣布这个决定，会让彼此都痛苦不堪。 “It would be no less than excruciating to both that I should announce this decision to you by word of mouth.
所以我选择写信，至少没有那么痛苦。 I have therefore taken the less painful course of writing.
可能信还没到你手上，我就已经坐上去伦敦的马车离开镇上了，至于会去哪个城市我不会向任何人透露。 Before you receive this I shall have left the town by the evening coach for London, on reaching which city my movements will be revealed to none.
小姐，你就当我已经死了吧，请你接受我再次向你允诺，我尊重你、牵挂你、深爱着你。’ “Regard me, Mademoiselle, as dead, and accept my renewed assurances of respect, remembrance, and affection.”
从震惊和悲伤中回过神来，她想起了拂晓时分那辆马车离开梅尔彻斯特时，星空下坐在车厢外面的乘客中有一个身影令她心头一颤，因为那像极了她的友人。 ‘When she had recovered from her shock of surprise and grief, she remembered that at the starting of the coach out of Melchester before dawn, the shape of a figure among the outside passengers against the starlit sky had caused her a momentary start, from its resemblance to that of her friend.
对彼此的打算毫不知情，在黑暗中也没有看见对方，他们两个就这样乘着同一辆马车离开了小镇。 Knowing nothing of each other’s intentions, and screened from each other by the darkness, they had left the town by the same conveyance.
‘伟大的他，坚定地离开了；而渺小的我，回来了！’她说。 “He, the greater, persevered; I, the smaller, returned!” she said.
从恍惚中回过神来，V小姐又想到了自己的雇主纽博尔德夫人，因为最近的事情两人都疏远了。 ‘Recovering from her stupor, Mademoiselle V—bethought herself again of her employer, Mrs. Newbold, whom recent events had estranged.
她真心诚意地回头去找那位夫人，向她解释了一切。 To that lady she went with a full heart, and explained everything.
纽博尔德夫人对这件事情什么都没说，重新接纳了这位被遗弃的新娘做家庭教师。 Mrs. Newbold kept to herself her opinion of the episode, and reinstalled the deserted bride in her old position as governess to the family.
后来她一直担任家庭教师直到去世。 ‘A governess she remained to the end of her days.
英法最终和解之后，她与我母亲相识，渐渐地将她的这些经历告诉了我母亲。 After the final peace with France she became acquainted with my mother, to whom by degrees she imparted these experiences of hers.
V小姐渐渐白了头发，憔悴了容颜，有时她会思忖自己的爱人会在世界的哪个角落，他是否还活着，自己是否还有可能再见到他。 As her hair grew white, and her features pinched, Mademoiselle V—would wonder what nook of the world contained her lover, if he lived, and if by any chance she might see him again.
她于19世纪20年代离世，走的时候年龄也不算老，那日黎明星空下的身影，成为这个她的家族仇人、曾经跟她订下婚约的丈夫留给她的最后记忆。” But when, some time in the ‘twenties, death came to her, at no great age, that outline against the stars of the morning remained as the last glimpse she ever obtained of her family’s foe and her once affianced husband.’
