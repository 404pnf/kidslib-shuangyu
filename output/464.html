太阳妹妹 The Sister of the Sun
作者：安德鲁·朗格 by Andrew Lang
很久以前，有一个年轻的王子，他最喜爱的玩伴是住在王宫庭园里的园丁的儿子。 A long time ago there lived a young prince whose favourite playfellow was the son of the gardener who lived in the grounds of the palace.
国王本来希望王子选择一个在宫里长大的青年侍从作为他的伙伴，但王子和他们没有共同语言，而且他已经被宠坏了，什么事都得依着他。而园丁的儿子性情温和且举止得体，所以得到国王恩准，无论早中晚都可以呆在王宫里。 The king would have preferred his choosing a friend from the pages who were brought up at court; but the prince would have nothing to say to them, and as he was a spoilt child, and allowed his way in all things, and the gardener’s boy was quiet and well-behaved, he was suffered to be in the palace, morning, noon, and night.
这两个孩子最喜爱的游戏就是射箭比赛，因为国王给了他们两张一模一样的弓，他们整天比试，看谁射得最高。 The game the children loved the best was a match at archery, for the king had given them two bows exactly alike, and they would spend whole days in trying to see which could shoot the highest.
这个游戏总是非常危险的，他们没把自己的眼珠给射出来简直是个奇迹，但他们总能设法避开危险。 This is always very dangerous, and it was a great wonder they did not put their eyes out; but somehow or other they managed to escape.
一天上午，王子做完功课就跑出去叫他的伙伴，两人便飞奔到他们平时玩耍的草地上。 One morning, when the prince had done his lessons, he ran out to call his friend, and they both hurried off to the lawn which was their usual playground.
他们从放玩具的小棚屋里取出弓，便开始比试，看谁射得最高。 They took their bows out of the little hut where their toys were kept, and began to see which could shoot the highest.
最后，两人恰巧同时放箭，当两支箭又落回到地上时，他们发现其中一支上卡着一根金鸡的尾羽。 At last they happened to let fly their arrows both together, and when they fell to earth again the tail feather of a golden hen was found sticking in one.
现在问题来了，两支箭一模一样，即便凑得再近些去看，也看不出有什么差别，那么是谁射出了那幸运的一箭呢？ Now the question began to arise whose was the lucky arrow, for they were both alike, and look as closely as you would you could see no difference between them.
王子说那支箭是他射出的，而园丁的儿子十分确信箭是自己射出的——他说得完全正确。但是，由于他们无法解决这件事，他们就直接去找国王。 The prince declared that the arrow was his, and the gardener’s boy was quite sure it was HIS—and on this occasion he was perfectly right; but, as they could not decide the matter, they went straight to the king.
国王听完事情的来龙去脉后，判定羽毛归自己的儿子，但另一个男孩不服气，坚持说羽毛是自己射下来的。 When the king had heard the story, he decided that the feather belonged to his son; but the other boy would not listen to this and claimed the feather for himself.
最后，国王不耐烦了，生气地说： At length the king’s patience gave way, and he said angrily:
“那好吧，既然你这么肯定羽毛是你的话，那它会是你的。但是，你必须找到那只尾巴上少一根羽毛的金鸡。 ‘Very well; if you are so sure that the feather is yours, yours it shall be; only you will have to seek till you find a golden hen with a feather missing from her tail.
如果你找不到，就砍下你的脑袋。” And if you fail to find her your head will be the forfeit.’
男孩需要鼓起所有的勇气才能默不作声地听完国王的话。 The boy had need of all his courage to listen silently to the king’s words.
他根本不知道金鸡会在哪里，而且即便他找到了金鸡，他也不知道怎么去捉。 He had no idea where the golden hen might be, or even, if he discovered that, how he was to get to her.
但他别无他法，只能服从国王的命令，而且他觉得，越早离开王宫越好。 But there was nothing for it but to do the king’s bidding, and he felt that the sooner he left the palace the better.
于是，他回到家，装了些食物在包里，便出发了，希望路上能撞上好运，为自己指明道路。 So he went home and put some food into a bag, and then set forth, hoping that some accident might show him which path to take.
走了几个小时后，他遇到了一只狐狸，狐狸看上去很友好，有了能说话的对象，男孩满心欢喜，便坐下和狐狸聊了起来。 After walking for several hours he met a fox, who seemed inclined to be friendly, and the boy was so glad to have anyone to talk to that he sat down and entered into conversation.
“你要去哪儿？”狐狸问。 ‘Where are you going?’ asked the fox.
“我要去找一只尾巴上掉了一根羽毛的金鸡，”男孩回答道，“但我不知道它在哪儿，也不知道怎么去抓它！” ‘I have got to find a golden hen who has lost a feather out of her tail,’ answered the boy; ‘but I don’t know where she lives or how I shall catch her!’
“哦，我能为你带路！”狐狸十分好心地说， ‘Oh, I can show you the way!’ said the fox, who was really very good-natured.
“在遥远的东边，就在那个方向，住着一个美丽的少女，人们叫她‘太阳妹妹’。 ‘Far towards the east, in that direction, lives a beautiful maiden who is called “The Sister of the Sun.”
她家里有三只金鸡。 She has three golden hens in her house.
说不定那根羽毛就是其中一只金鸡的。” Perhaps the feather belongs to one of them.’
男孩听到这个消息非常高兴，于是狐狸走在前面，男孩跟在后面，他们这样一起走了一整天。 The boy was delighted at this news, and they walked on all day together, the fox in front, and the boy behind.
夜幕降临时，他们就躺下来睡觉，把背包放在头下当枕头。 When evening came they lay down to sleep, and put the knapsack under their heads for a pillow.
大约在午夜时分，狐狸突然低嗥了一声，向同伴靠得更近了。 Suddenly, about midnight, the fox gave a low whine, and drew nearer to his bedfellow.
“兄弟，”他低声耳语道，“有人来了，他要从我这儿抢走背包。 ‘Cousin,’ he whispered very low, ‘there is someone coming who will take the knapsack away from me.
看那儿！” Look over there!’
男孩透过灌木丛，瞥见一个男人。 And the boy, peeping through the bushes, saw a man.
“哦，我倒认为他不会抢劫我们！”男孩说。等男人走近了，男孩把自己的遭遇告诉了他们，陌生男人对此非常感兴趣，便请求与他们同行，认为自己也许能派上用场。 ‘Oh, I don’t think he will rob us!’ said the boy; and when the man drew near, he told them his story, which so much interested the stranger that he asked leave to travel with them, as he might be of some use.
于是，当太阳升起时，他们又出发了，狐狸依旧走在前面，男人和男孩紧随其后。 So when the sun rose they set out again, the fox in front as before, the man and boy following.
几个小时后，他们到达了太阳妹妹的城堡，太阳妹妹养着许多宠物，其中就包括金鸡。 After some hours they reached the castle of the Sister of the Sun, who kept the golden hens among her treasures.
他们在城堡大门前停了下来，商量由谁进去面见太阳妹妹。 They halted before the gate and took counsel as to which of them should go in and see the lady herself.
“我认为最好是让我进去偷金鸡。”狐狸说；但男孩对此并不赞成。 ‘I think it would be best for me to enter and steal the hens,’ said the fox; but this did not please the boy at all.
“不，这是我的事情，应该由我去。”他接上狐狸的话。 ‘No, it is my business, so it is right that I should go,’ answered he.
“你会发现要捉到金鸡是很难的。”狐狸回答说。 ‘You will find it a very difficult matter to get hold of the hens,’ replied the fox.
“哦，包在我身上，绝不会有闪失。”男孩答道。 ‘Oh, nothing is likely to happen to me,’ returned the boy.
“好吧，那就去吧，”狐狸说，“但是务必小心，千万别出什么差错。 ‘Well, go then,’ said the fox, ‘but be careful not to make any mistake.
只偷那只尾巴上少了一根羽毛的金鸡，别管其他的。” Steal only the hen which has the feather missing from her tail, and leave the others alone.’
男人听着他们说话，并没有插嘴。于是，男孩走进了城堡的院子。 The man listened, but did not interfere, and the boy entered the court of the palace.
很快，他就看见三只骄傲的金鸡趾高气扬地踱来踱去，其实它们正焦急地寻找着地上是否有供它们享用的谷物。 He soon spied the three hens strutting proudly about, though they were really anxiously wondering if there were not some grains lying on the ground that they might be glad to eat.
当最后一只金鸡从男孩身边走过时，他发现它的尾巴上正好少了一根羽毛。 And as the last one passed by him, he saw she had one feather missing from her tail.
男孩见状立即向前扑去，抓住了金鸡的脖子，这样金鸡就不能挣扎了。 At this sight the youth darted forward and seized the hen by the neck so that she could not struggle.
然后，他将金鸡稳妥地夹在胳膊下，径直朝大门走去。 Then, tucking her comfortably under his arm, he made straight for the gate.
不幸的是，就在他即将走出大门时，他却回头看了一眼，从一扇敞开的门内瞥见了城堡里那宏伟壮观的景象。 Unluckily, just as he was about to go through it he looked back and caught a glimpse of wonderful splendours from an open door of the palace.
“反正也不着急走，”男孩心里嘀咕着，“既然已经来了，不妨进去瞧瞧。”于是他转过身，把金鸡忘得一干二净，金鸡就趁机从男孩的胳膊下逃走，跑去找自己的同伴们了。 ‘After all, there is no hurry,’ he said to himself; ‘I may as well see something now I AM here,’ and turned back, forgetting all about the hen, which escaped from under his arm, and ran to join her sisters.
从门口窥视到的一切美丽事物深深地吸引了男孩，他几乎都没注意到自己已经丢了战利品。当他看到睡在自己跟前床上的太阳妹妹时，他都忘了世上还有金鸡这回事。 He was so much fascinated by the sight of all the beautiful things which peeped through the door that he scarcely noticed that he had lost the prize he had won; and he did not remember there was such a thing as a hen in the world when he beheld the Sister of the Sun sleeping on a bed before him.
他痴痴地站着看了好一会儿，然后才猛然醒过神来，意识到自己在那里没什么事情可做，便悄悄溜走了，并再次幸运地抓住了那只金鸡，把它带到了大门口。 For some time he stood staring; then he came to himself with a start, and feeling that he had no business there, softly stole away, and was fortunate enough to recapture the hen, which he took with him to the gate.
刚要跨出门槛，他又停住了。 On the threshold he stopped again.
“我为什么不看看太阳妹妹呢？”他心想，“她睡着了，永远不会知道的。” ‘Why should I not look at the Sister of the Sun?’ he thought to himself; ‘she is asleep, and will never know.’
于是，他再次转身，走进寝宫，金鸡也像上次那样，扭动着身子逃走了。 And he turned back for the second time and entered the chamber, while the hen wriggled herself free as before.
男孩看后，心满意足，才走进院子又捉住了正在找玉米的金鸡。 When he had gazed his fill he went out into the courtyard and picked up his hen who was seeking for corn.
走近大门口时，他又停了下来。 As he drew near the gate he paused.
“我为什么没吻她一下呢？”他自言自语，“我永远都不会亲吻到如此美丽的姑娘了。” ‘Why did I not give her a kiss?’ he said to himself; ‘I shall never kiss any woman so beautiful.’
他懊悔地搓着手，所以金鸡落在地上又逃走了。 And he wrung his hands with regret, so that the hen fell to the ground and ran away.
“不过我还是可以这么做的！”他兴奋地叫道，匆匆回到寝宫，在熟睡的少女的额头上吻了一下。 ‘But I can do it still!’ he cried with delight, and he rushed back to the chamber and kissed the sleeping maiden on the forehead.
唉，可是当他再次出来时，他发现那只金鸡已经变得十分警觉，不让自己靠近它。 But, alas! when he came out again he found that the hen had grown so shy that she would not let him come near her.
而且，更糟糕的是，金鸡的同伴们开始咯咯咯地大声叫起来，太阳妹妹都被吵醒了。 And, worse than that, her sisters began to cluck so loud that the Sister of the Sun was awakened by the noise.
她急急忙忙地从床上跳起来，走到门口，对男孩说： She jumped up in haste from her bed, and going to the door she said to the boy:
“如果你不能从那个很远的城堡里，把我那被巨人掳走的姐姐救出来的话，你将永远、永远得不到我的金鸡。” ‘You shall never, never, have my hen till you bring me back my sister who was carried off by a giant to his castle, which is a long way off.’
男孩慢慢地离开了城堡，心里十分难过，他把事情的经过告诉了等在大门口的同伴们，告诉他们他是如何三次抓到金鸡，却最后还是让给它跑了。 Slowly and sadly the youth left the palace and told his story to his friends, who were waiting outside the gate, how he had actually held the hen three times in his arms and had lost her.
“我早就知道，我们不会那么容易就逃脱掉的，”狐狸摇了摇头，说道，“但是时间紧迫，耽误不得。 ‘I knew that we should not get off so easily,’ said the fox, shaking his head; ‘but there is no more time to waste.
我们赶紧出发，去找太阳妹妹的姐姐。 Let us set off at once in search of the sister.
幸好我认识路。” Luckily, I know the way.’
他们连续走了许多天，直到最后，一如既往在前面领路的狐狸突然停下来。 They walked on for many days, till at length the fox, who, as usual, was going first, stopped suddenly.
“巨人的城堡就在不远处，”它说，“但我们到那儿之后，你们俩必须留在外面，而我进去救出公主。 ‘The giant’s castle is not far now,’ he said, ‘but when we reach it you two must remain outside while I go and fetch the princess.
我一把她带出来，你们就必须抓紧她，跑得越快越好。我则返回城堡，去和那些巨人们周旋——因为他们人很多——所以也许不会注意到公主已经逃跑了。” Directly I bring her out you must both catch hold of her tight, and get away as fast as you can; while I return to the castle and talk to the giants—for there are many of them—so that they may not notice the escape of the princess.’
几分钟后，他们来到了城堡，狐狸以前经常去那里，所以轻而易举地就溜了进去。 A few minutes later they arrived at the castle, and the fox, who had often been there before, slipped in without difficulty.
大厅里有好几个巨人，有老有少，都正围着公主跳着舞。 There were several giants, both young and old, in the hall, and they were all dancing round the princess.
他们一看到狐狸就大声喊道：“过来一起跳舞吧，老狐狸，我们好久没见到你了。” As soon as they saw the fox they cried out: ‘Come and dance too, old fox; it is a long time since we have seen you.’
于是，狐狸直立起身子，跳了起来，舞步格外优美。但过了一会儿，它停下来说： So the fox stood up, and did his steps with the best of them; but after a while he stopped and said:
“我知道一种好看的新舞蹈，我想跳给你们看看。不过，这舞要两个人一起跳。 ‘I know a charming new dance that I should like to show you; but it can only be done by two people.
如果公主能赏脸跟我跳几分钟的话，你们很快就能看到这种舞是怎么跳的。” If the princess will honour me for a few minutes, you will soon see how it is done.’
“啊，太好了，我们想看点儿新鲜的。”巨人们答道，把公主交给了狐狸，狐狸张开双臂，搂住了公主。 ‘Ah, that is delightful; we want something new,’ answered they, and placed the princess between the outstretched arms of the fox.
一眨眼的工夫，狐狸就撞倒了大厅内用于照明的一大排灯盏，在黑暗中将公主带到了大门口。 In one instant he had knocked over the great stand of lights that lighted the hall, and in the darkness had borne the princess to the gate.
狐狸的同伴们如它交代的那样，抓住了公主，狐狸再次回到了大厅，没有人发觉它离开过。 His comrades seized hold of her, as they had been bidden, and the fox was back again in the hall before anyone had missed him.
它看到巨人们正忙着点火照明。但不一会儿就有人叫道： He found the giants busy trying to kindle a fire and get some light; but after a bit someone cried out:
“公主在哪儿？” ‘Where is the princess?’
“这儿，在我怀里呢，”狐狸答道， ‘Here, in my arms,’ replied the fox.
“别担心，她很安全。” ‘Don’t be afraid; she is quite safe.’
狐狸一直等着，直到估摸着同伴们已经走了好远，已经与巨人们相隔了至少五六座山。 And he waited until he thought that his comrades had gained a good start, and put at least five or six mountains between themselves and the giants.
这才撒腿跑出门去，边跑边喊：“公主在这儿呢，有本事就来抓啊！” Then he sprang through the door, calling, as he went: ‘The maiden is here; take her if you can!’
听到这话，巨人们这才明白，他们的战利品已经逃走了，他们立即撒开长腿，全力追赶狐狸，他们认为狐狸背着公主，所以应该很快就能追上它。 At these words the giants understood that their prize had escaped, and they ran after the fox as fast as their great legs could carry them, thinking that they should soon come up with the fox, who they supposed had the princess on his back.
而狐狸很聪明，当然不会选择与同伴们相同的路线，而是在树林里穿进穿出，直到最后连自己也累坏了，倒在一棵树下很快就睡着了。 The fox, on his side, was far too clever to choose the same path that his friends had taken, but would in and out of the forest, till at last even HE was tired out, and fell fast asleep under a tree.
白天的奔忙确实使狐狸精疲力竭了，它甚至都没听到巨人们已经接近了，他们已经伸手要去抓它的尾巴了。就在这时，狐狸睁开双眼，奋力一跳，又一次远远地把巨人们甩在身后。 Indeed, he was so exhausted with his day’s work that he never heard the approach of the giants, and their hands were already stretched out to seize his tail when his eyes opened, and with a tremendous bound he was once more beyond their reach.
整个后半夜，狐狸都在不停地奔跑。但当明艳的红色弥漫东边的天际时，它却停下来等着巨人们靠近。 All the rest of the night the fox ran and ran; but when bright red spread over the east, he stopped and waited till the giants were close upon him.
然后它转过身来，悄悄地说：“看，太阳妹妹在那儿！” Then he turned, and said quietly: ‘Look, there is the Sister of the Sun!’
巨人们随即抬眼去看，马上变成了一根根的石柱。 The giants raised their eyes all at once, and were instantly turned into pillars of stone.
狐狸向每根石柱深鞠一躬，就出发去找自己的同伴们了。 The fox then made each pillar a low bow, and set off to join his friends.
它知道许多通过小山的捷径，所以很快就追上了他们，于是四人日夜兼程，终于来到了太阳妹妹的城堡。 He knew a great many short cuts across the hills, so it was not long before he came up with them, and all four travelled night and day till they reached the castle of the Sister of the Sun.
看到原以为已经死去的公主活着回来了，整个城堡的人是多么的高兴啊！为了营救公主，男孩历尽艰险，大家都感激不尽。 What joy and feasting there was throughout the palace at the sight of the princess whom they had mourned as dead! and they could not make enough of the boy who had gone through such dangers in order to rescue her.
他马上就得到了金鸡，不仅如此，太阳妹妹还告诉他，不久之后，等他再长大一些，她会亲自去他家拜访，与他结为夫妻。 The golden hen was given to him at once, and, more than that, the Sister of the Sun told him that, in a little time, when he was a few years older, she would herself pay a visit to his home and become his wife.
当男孩听到这么美好的未来时，几乎不敢相信自己的耳朵，因为他的妻子将是世上最美丽的公主。无论多么幽深的黑暗，一见到她额前那颗星星的光芒，都会仓皇而逃。 The boy could hardly believe his ears when he heard what was in store for him, for his was the most beautiful princess in all the world; and however thick the darkness might be, it fled away at once from the light of a star on her forehead.
于是，在同伴们的陪同下，男孩启程回家。一想到公主的承诺，他就满心欢喜。 So the boy set forth on his journey home, with his friends for company; his heart full of gladness when he thought of the promise of the princess.
然而，他的同伴们一个接一个地在他们最初相遇的地方离开了他，当他回到家乡，来到王宫门前时，他已是孤身一人了。 But, one by one, his comrades dropped off at the places where they had first met him, and he was quite alone when he reached his native town and the gates of the palace.
他抱着金鸡去觐见国王，讲述了自己的冒险经历，并说他将迎娶一位十分美丽并且与众不同的公主为妻，她额前的星星能将黑夜变成白昼。 With the golden hen under his arm he presented himself before the king, and told his adventures, and how he was going to have for a wife a princess so wonderful and unlike all other princesses, that the star on her forehead could turn night into day.
国王默默地听着，当男孩说完后，他冷冷地说道：“如果我发现你说的不是真的，我就把你扔到沥青桶里去。” The king listened silently, and when the boy had done, he said quietly: ‘If I find that your story is not true I will have you thrown into a cask of pitch.’
“是真的，每个字都是真的。”男孩答道。他接着告诉国王，他的新娘前来找他的日子已经约定好了，甚至具体到几点钟。 ‘It is true—every word of it,’ answered the boy; and went on to tell that the day and even the hour were fixed when his bride was to come and seek him.
然而，随着约定的时间越来越近，公主依然杳无音讯，年轻人变得焦虑不安，尤其是当他听说大木桶里已经装满了沥青，底下堆满了木柴，准备点火烧开沥青。 But as the time drew near, and nothing was heard of the princess, the youth became anxious and uneasy, especially when it came to his ears that the great cask was being filled with pitch, and that sticks were laid underneath to make a fire to boil it with.
男孩整天站在窗前，俯瞰着公主必定要经过的那片大海。然而，海上没有任何她要来的迹象，连白帆的影子都没有。 All day long the boy stood at the window, looking over the sea by which the princess must travel; but there were no signs of her, not even the tiniest white sail.
当他站在那里时，士兵们就来了，他们抓住他，把他带到木桶前，大火在那里熊熊燃烧着，可怕的黑沥青已经沸腾了，冒着泡儿溢出了桶的边缘。 And, as he stood, soldiers came and laid hands on him, and led him up to the cask, where a big fire was blazing, and the horrid black pitch boiling and bubbling over the sides.
男孩看着木桶吓得发抖，但他无处可逃，因此他闭上眼睛，不愿看到这可怕的场景。 He looked and shuddered, but there was no escape; so he shut his eyes to avoid seeing.
国王命令他登上通向木桶最高处的台阶，就在这时，大家突然看到一些人拼命地跑了过来，边跑边喊着说一艘大船正鼓帆径直向城里驶来。 The word was given for him to mount the steps which led to the top of the cask, when, suddenly, some men were seen running with all their might, crying as they went that a large ship with its sails spread was making straight for the city.
没人知道那艘船是谁的，或者从何而来。国王下令，在船抵达之前，他是不会烧死男孩的，有的是时间处置他。 No one knew what the ship was, or whence it came; but the king declared that he would not have the boy burned before its arrival, there would always be time enough for that.
终于，大船安全到港，围观的人群中窃窃私语，说船上正是太阳妹妹，她是来履行承诺，与年轻的农民成婚。 At length the vessel was safe in port, and a whisper went through the watching crowd that on board was the Sister of the Sun, who had come to marry the young peasant as she had promised.
又过了一会儿，她上了岸，并叫人们给她带路，到她的新郎经常跟她描述的那个小屋去，而国王一看到那艘大船，就下令将男孩带回到那个小屋。 In a few moments more she had landed, and desired to be shown the way to the cottage which her bridegroom had so often described to her; and whither he had been led back by the king’s order at the first sign of the ship.
“你不认识我吗？”太阳妹妹俯身朝躺在地上的男孩问道，男孩由于惊吓，几乎神志不清了。 ‘Don’t you know me?’ asked the Sister of the Sun, bending over him where he lay, almost driven out of his senses with terror.
“不，不，我不认识你。”年轻人眼也没抬地答道。 ‘No, no; I don’t know you,’ answered the youth, without raising his eyes.
“吻我。”太阳妹妹说。年轻人照做了，但依然没有抬眼看。 ‘Kiss me,’ said the Sister of the Sun; and the youth obeyed her, but still without looking up.
“你现在还是不认识我吗？”她问道。 ‘Don’t you know me NOW?’ asked she.
“不，我不认识你——我不认识你。”他答道，说话的神情就像被吓疯了一样。 ‘No, I don’t know you—I don’t know you,’ he replied, with the manner of a man whom fear had driven mad.
见此情形，太阳妹妹越发觉得害怕，她索性从头跟他讲他与她相遇的故事以及她是如何千里迢迢赶来与他成婚的。 At this the Sister of the Sun grew rather frightened, and beginning at the beginning, she told him the story of his meeting with her, and how she had come a long way in order to marry him.
她刚讲完，国王就来了，他想看看男孩讲的是否真的属实。 And just as she had finished in walked the king, to see if what the boy had said was really true.
但他刚一打开小屋的门，就被屋内耀眼的光芒几乎照瞎了眼。他这才想起了男孩曾经告诉过他，公主额前有颗星星。 But hardly had he opened the door of the cottage when he was almost blinded by the light that filled it; and he remembered what he had been told about the star on the forehead of the princess.
他摇摇晃晃地往后退，仿佛被光芒击中了一样。接着，一种莫名的、从未有过的情感油然而生，他跪倒在太阳妹妹面前，央求她忘掉这个农家男孩，与自己共享王位。 He staggered back as if he had been struck, then a curious feeling took hold of him, which he had never felt before, and falling on his knees before the Sister of the Sun, he implored her to give up all thought of the peasant boy, and to share his throne.
但是，她笑了，说自己如果想要的话就会有个更好的王位，还说自己向来只做自己喜欢的事，除了这个男孩，她不会嫁给任何人，要不是因为国王，她永远都见不到他呢。 But she laughed, and said she had a finer throne of her own, if she wanted to sit on it, and that she was free to please herself, and would have no husband but the boy whom she would never have seen except for the king himself.
“我明天就嫁给他。”她最后说，并下令立即开始筹备婚礼。 ‘I shall marry him to-morrow,’ ended she; and ordered the preparations to be set on foot at once.
到了第二天，新郎的父亲却告诉公主，依照该国的法律，婚礼举行时必须要有国王在场，但他希望国王陛下不要迟迟不来。 When the next day came, however, the bridegroom’s father informed the princess that, by the law of the land, the marriage must take place in the presence of the king; but he hoped his majesty would not long delay his arrival.
一两个小时过去了，每个人都在翘首以盼。终于，喇叭声响了起来，街上浩浩荡荡地走来一支队伍。 An hour or two passed, and everyone was waiting and watching, when at last the sound of trumpets was heard and a grand procession was seen marching up the street.
一把铺着天鹅绒的椅子已经为国王准备好了，他坐了下来，环视聚集的人群，说： A chair covered with velvet had been made ready for the king, and he took his seat upon it, and, looking round upon the assembled company, he said:
“我无意阻拦这桩婚事。但是，在我允许可以庆祝婚礼之前，新郎必须完成三项任务，以此证明自己配得上这样一位新娘。 ‘I have no wish to forbid this marriage; but, before I can allow it to be celebrated, the bridegroom must prove himself worthy of such a bride by fulfilling three tasks.
第一件事就是他必须在一天之内砍倒整个森林里的树。” And the first is that in a single day he must cut down every tree in an entire forest.
年轻人被国王的话吓呆了。 The youth stood aghast as the king’s words.
他长这么大从来没有砍倒过一棵树，连怎么下手都不知道。 He had never cut down a tree in his life, and had not the least idea how to begin.
更何况是整个森林！ And as for a whole forest—!
但公主知道他在想什么，便悄悄对他说： But the princess saw what was passing in his mind, and whispered to him:
“别害怕。 ‘Don’t be afraid.
我的船上有把斧子，你带着它去森林。 In my ship you will find an axe, which you must carry off to the forest.
你用它砍倒一棵树，然后就说：‘让整个森林倒下’，所有的树都会马上倒下。 When you have cut down one tree with it just say: “So let the forest fall,” and in an instant all the trees will be on the ground.
但你要从砍倒的那棵树上捡起三片木屑，放到你的口袋里。” But pick up three chips of the tree you felled, and put them in your pocket.’
年轻人一五一十地照做了，而且很快就将三片木屑揣在外套里回来了。 And the young man did exactly as he was bid, and soon returned with the three chips safe in his coat.
第二天早上，公主说她考虑过这件事，说自己既然不是国王的臣民，就没有理由要受到他的法律的约束，她打算当天就成婚。 The following morning the princess declared that she had been thinking about the matter, and that, as she was not a subject of the king, she saw no reason why she should be bound by his laws; and she meant to be married that very day.
但新郎的父亲告诉她，她这么说是没问题的，但他儿子就不一样了，任何违背国王旨意的事情都会要了他的脑袋。 But the bridegroom’s father told her that it was all very well for her to talk like that, but it was quite different for his son, who would pay with his head for any disobedience to the king’s commands.
不过，考虑到前一天他儿子砍倒了整个森林，他希望国王陛下的心会软下来，而且他已经传消息给国王，说他们希望马上见到他。 However, in consideration of what the youth had done the day before, he hoped his majesty’s heart might be softened, especially as he had sent a message that they might expect him at once.
这对未婚夫妇只好耐心等候国王的到来。 With this the bridal pair had to be content, and be as patient as they could till the king’s arrival.
国王没有让他们久等，可他们一看他的脸色就知道等着他们的没什么好事。 He did not keep them long, but they saw by his face that nothing good awaited them.
“婚礼不能举行，”他简短地说道，“除非年轻人能让他昨天砍倒的树都站立起来。” ‘The marriage cannot take place,’ he said shortly, ‘till the youth has joined to their roots all the trees he cut down yesterday.’
这听起来比第一个任务难多了，年轻人绝望地向太阳妹妹求助。 This sounded much more difficult than what he had done before, and he turned in despair to the Sister of the Sun.
“没关系，”她悄悄地鼓励他， ‘It is all right,’ she whispered encouragingly.
“带上这些水，将它们洒在其中一棵倒下的树上，对它说：‘让森林里所有的树都直立起来’，一会儿，它们就都会直立起来。” ‘Take this water and sprinkle it on one of the fallen trees, and say to it: “So let all the trees of the forest stand upright,” and in a moment they will be erect again.’
年轻人按照她说的做了，他离开森林时，森林看起来和被砍之前一模一样。 And the young man did what he was told, and left the forest looking exactly as it had done before.
公主想，现在确实没有必要推迟婚礼了。于是，她命令第二天的婚礼事宜都必须准备妥当。 Now, surely, thought the princess, there was no longer any need to put off the wedding; and she gave orders that all should be ready for the following day.
但老人又一次打断说，没有国王的恩准，任何婚礼都不能举行。 But again the old man interfered, and declared that without the king’s permission no marriage could take place.
于是他们第三次请来了国王陛下，国王也第三次说他不会同意这桩婚事，除非新郎能杀死盘踞在城堡后面大河里的一条大蛇。 For the third time his majesty was sent for, and for the third time he proclaimed that he could not give his consent until the bridegroom should have slain a serpent which dwelt in a broad river that flowed at the back of the castle.
虽然没有人亲眼看到过那条可怕的大蛇，但人人都知道它的故事，时不时有孩子离家后迷路了，就再也没回来过，母亲们就不让其他孩子靠近那条两岸结着多汁的果实、开着美丽的花朵的大河了。 Everyone knew stories of this terrible serpent, though no one had actually seen it; but from time to time a child strayed from home and never came back, and then mothers would forbid the other children to go near the river, which had juicy fruits and lovely flowers growing along its banks.
因此，男孩听到自己面临的任务时颤抖不已、脸色苍白就不足为奇了。 So no wonder the youth trembled and turned pale when he heard what lay before him.
“这次你还是会成功的，”太阳妹妹握紧他的手，低声说，“我的船上有一把能斩断任何东西的神剑。 ‘You will succeed in this also,’ whispered the Sister of the Sun, pressing his hand, ‘for in my ship is a magic sword which will cut through everything.
到河边去把停泊在那儿的一只船解开，再把三片木屑扔入水中。 Go down to the river and unfasten a boat which lies moored there, and throw the chips into the water.
大蛇一竖起身子，你就一剑斩断它的三个脑袋。 When the serpent rears up its body you will cut off its three heads with one blow of your sword.
然后割下每颗头里的舌尖，明天早上把它们带到国王的厨房里。 Then take the tip of each tongue and go with it to-morrow morning into the king’s kitchen.
如果国王来了，你就对他说：‘为了回报你要求我完成的三项任务，我有三份礼物要送给你！’再把大蛇的舌尖扔向他，然后赶紧跑回大船上去，能跑多快就跑多快。 If the king himself should enter, just say to him: “Here are three gifts I offer you in return for the services you demanded of me!” and throw the tips of the serpent’s tongues at him, and hasten to the ship as fast as your legs will carry you.
但千万要小心，绝对不要回头看。” But be sure you take great care never to look behind you.’
年轻人完全按照公主的吩咐做了。 The young man did exactly what the princess had told him.
他扔入河里的三片木屑变成了一只小船，他驾着船过河时，蛇昂起了头，发出很大的嘶嘶声。 The three chips which he flung into the river became a boat, and, as he steered across the stream, the serpent put up its head and hissed loudly.
年轻人持剑准备就绪，瞬间，三颗头就漂浮在水面上。 The youth had his sword ready, and in another second the three heads were bobbing on the water.
他把船划到那三颗头旁边，俯身削掉三个舌尖，然后划回到对岸。 Guiding his boat till he was beside them, he stooped down and snipped off the ends of the tongues, and then rowed back to the other bank.
第二天早上，他带着舌尖来到国王的厨房，国王会来看看自己晚餐会吃什么，这是他的习惯，当他踏进厨房时，新郎就把舌尖扔到他的脸上，说：“为了回报你要求我完成的那些任务，我送你一样礼物。” Next morning he carried them into the royal kitchen, and when the king entered, as was his custom, to see what he was going to have for dinner, the bridegroom flung them in his face, saying: ‘Here is a gift for you in return for the services you asked of me.’
接着，他推开厨房的门，向大船跑去。 And, opening the kitchen door, he fled to the ship.
不幸的是他迷路了，由于心情激动，他跑来跑去，不知道要去哪里。 Unluckily he missed the way, and in his excitement ran backwards and forwards, without knowing whither he was going.
最后，他绝望地四处张望，惊奇地发现城市和王宫都消失得无影无踪。 At last, in despair, he looked round, and saw to his amazement that both the city and palace had vanished completely.
他把目光转向另一个方向，看见在很远很远的地方，大船已经乘着风扬帆而去。 Then he turned his eyes in the other direction, and, far, far away, he caught sight of the ship with her sails spread, and a fair wind behind her.
这可怕的一幕似乎令他失去了理智，整整一天，他都在四处游荡，不知道自己要去哪里。直到夜幕降临，他才注意到附近有个小草屋里冒出了缕缕炊烟。 This dreadful spectacle seemed to take away his senses, and all day long he wandered about, without knowing where he was going, till, in the evening, he noticed some smoke from a little hut of turf near by.
他径直走上前去，喊道：“啊，老妈妈，可怜可怜我，让我进去吧！” He went straight up to it and cried: ‘O mother, let me come in for pity’s sake!’
住在小草屋里的老妇人示意他进来，他一进去就又喊道：“啊，老妈妈，你能告诉我有关太阳妹妹的任何消息吗？” The old woman who lived in the hut beckoned to him to enter, and hardly was he inside when he cried again: ‘O mother, can you tell me anything of the Sister of the Sun?’
但老妇人只是摇摇头。 But the woman only shook her head.
“不，我对她一无所知。”她说。 ‘No, I know nothing of her,’ said she.
年轻人转身准备离开小草屋，但老妇人拦住了他，给了他一封信，请求他将信带给她的二姐，说：“如果你路上累了，就拿出信，把它抖得沙沙作响。” The young man turned to leave the hut, but the old woman stopped him, and, giving him a letter, begged him to carry it to her next eldest sister, saying: ‘If you should get tired on the way, take out the letter and rustle the paper.’
年轻人听了这个建议，感到很奇怪，因为他看不出来这样做能对他有什么帮助；但他也不答话，而是继续漫无目的地往前走。 This advice surprised the young man a good deal, as he did not see how it could help him; but he did not answer, and went down the road without knowing where he was going.
终于，他累得一步也走不动了；这时，他想起了老妇人跟他说过的话。 At length he grew so tired he could walk no more; then he remembered what the old woman had said.
他只抖了一次信纸，所有的疲劳感就都消失了，他跨着大步走过草地，终于来到另一个小草屋前。 After he had rustled the leaves only once all fatigue disappeared, and he strode over the grass till he came to another little turf hut.
“让我进去吧，求求你了，好心的老妈妈。”他喊道。 ‘Let me in, I pray you, dear mother,’ cried he.
门在他面前开了。 And the door opened in front of him.
“你妹妹让我捎给你这封信。”他说。接着又迅速补充道：“啊，老妈妈！你能告诉我有关太阳妹妹的任何消息吗？” ‘Your sister has sent you this letter,’ he said, and added quickly: ‘O mother! can you tell me anything of the Sister of the Sun?’
“不，我对她一无所知。”她答道。 ‘No, I know nothing of her,’ answered she.
但是，就在年轻人失望地转身离开之际，老妇人拦住了他。 But as he turned hopelessly away, she stopped him.
“如果你碰巧经过我大姐家的话，能帮我把这封信给她吗？”她说， ‘If you happen to pass my eldest sister’s house, will you give her this letter?’ said she.
“如果你路上累了，就从口袋里拿出这封信，把它抖得沙沙作响。” ‘And if you should get tired on the road, just take it out of your pocket and rustle the paper.’
于是，年轻人将信放入口袋，连日翻山越岭，终于来到一个小草屋，它和之前那两个小草屋一模一样。 So the young man put the letter in his pocket, and walked all day over the hills till he reached a little turf hut, exactly like the other two.
“让我进去吧，求求你了，好心的老妈妈。”他喊道。 ‘Let me in, I pray you, dear mother,’ cried he.
进门之后他说：“这是你妹妹的信，你能告诉我有关太阳妹妹的任何消息吗？” And as he entered he added: ‘Here is a letter from your sister and—can you tell me anything of the Sister of the Sun?’
“是的，我能，”老妇人答道， ‘Yes, I can,’ answered the old woman.
“她住在班卡岛的城堡里。 ‘She lives in the castle on the Banka.
就在几天前，她父亲吃了一场败仗，因为你偷走了他的神剑，太阳妹妹几乎悲痛欲绝。 Her father lost a battle only a few days ago because you had stolen his sword from him, and the Sister of the Sun herself is almost dead of grief.
但是，当你看到她时，就用一根针扎进她的手掌，并吸掉流出来的血滴。 But, when you see her, stick a pin into the palm of her hand, and suck the drops of blood that flow.
这样她就会变得更平静，并且再次认得你。 Then she will grow calmer, and will know you again.
但要小心，因为在你到达班卡岛的城堡之前，会发生可怕的事情。 Only, beware; for before you reach the castle on the Banka fearful things will happen.’
含着喜悦的泪水，他感谢老妇人带给他这个好消息，然后继续他的行程。 He thanked the old woman with tears of gladness for the good news she had given him, and continued his journey.
但没走多远，他在一个转弯处遇到了两兄弟，他们正在为一块布而争吵。 But he had not gone very far when, at a turn of the road, he met with two brothers, who were quarrelling over a piece of cloth.
“我的好兄弟，你们在争什么呀？”他说， ‘My good men, what are you fighting about?’ said he.
“那布看上去并不值钱啊！” ‘That cloth does not look worth much!’
“啊，它是很破旧了，”他们答道，“但它是我们的父亲遗留给我们的。如果哪个人披上它，就没有人能看到他；我们俩都想将它归为己有。” ‘Oh, it is ragged enough,’ answered they, ‘but it was left us by our father, and if any man wraps it round him no one can see him; and we each want it for our own.’
“让我披一会儿吧，”年轻人说，“然后我会告诉你们它应该归谁！” ‘Let me put it round me for a moment,’ said the youth, ‘and then I will tell you whose it ought to be!’
兄弟俩都觉得这个主意不错，就把布给了他；但他一把那块布披在自己的肩膀上，就消失得无影无踪了，仿佛他根本就没来过一样。 The brothers were pleased with this idea, and gave him the stuff; but the moment he had thrown it over his shoulder he disappeared as completely as if he had never been there at all.
与此同时，年轻人轻快地往前走着，直到他又遇到两个男人，他们在为一块桌布争吵。 Meanwhile the young man walked briskly along, till he came up with two other men, who were disputing over a table-cloth.
“怎么了？”他停在他们面前，问道。 ‘What is the matter?’ asked he, stopping in front of them.
“如果把这块桌布铺在桌子上，”他们答道，“桌子就会立即摆满了最美味的食物；我们都想得到它。” ‘If this cloth is spread on a table,’ answered they, ‘the table is instantly covered with the most delicious food; and we each want to have it.’
“让我试试这桌布，”年轻人说，“然后我会告诉你们它应该归谁。” ‘Let me try the table-cloth,’ said the youth, ‘and I will tell you whose it ought to be.’
两个男人都觉得这个主意不错，就把桌布递给了他。 The two men were quite pleased with this idea, and handed him the cloth.
于是，他急忙把第一块布披上肩，然后就消失了，两个男人为他们愚蠢的行为感到懊悔不已。 He then hastily threw the first piece of stuff round his shoulders and vanished from sight, leaving the two men grieving over their own folly.
年轻人没走多久，就又看到两个男人站在路旁，两人都抓住同一根结实的手杖不放，两人拉来拽去，互不相让。 The young man had not walked far before he saw two more men standing by the road-side, both grasping the same stout staff, and sometimes one seemed on the point of getting it, and sometimes the other.
“你们在吵什么？ ‘What are you quarrelling about?
你们可以各自从森林里砍来好多那样的棍子！”年轻人说。 You could cut a dozen sticks from the wood each just as good as that!’ said the young man.
他一说话，两人便停了下来，打量着他。 And as he spoke the fighters both stopped and looked at him.
“啊！你可以这样认为，”其中一人说，“但是用这根棍子的一端打一个人一下，那人就死了，而用另一端碰他一下，他就会起死回生。 ‘Ah! you may think so,’ said one, ‘but a blow from one end of this stick will kill a man, while a touch from the other end will bring him back to life.
再找一根这样的棍子可不容易啊！” You won’t easily find another stick like that!’
“是不容易，这倒是真的。”年轻人答道。 ‘No; that is true,’ answered the young man.
“让我看一下，然后我会告诉你们它应该归谁。” ‘Let me just look at it, and I will tell you whose it ought to be.’
两人都觉得这个主意不错，就把手杖递给了他。 The men were pleased with the idea, and handed him the staff.
“这当然很神奇，”他说，“但哪一端能让人起死回生啊？ ‘It is very curious, certainly,’ said he; ‘but which end is it that restores people to life?
毕竟，只要打得够狠，任何人都可能被棍子一下打死的！” After all, anyone can be killed by a blow from a stick if it is only hard enough!’
但他们把那一端指给他看以后，他把棍子扛在肩上就消失了。 But when he was shown the end he threw the stuff over his shoulders and vanished.
最后，他又看见两个人，他们在争着要一双鞋子。 At last he saw another set of men, who were struggling for the possession of a pair of shoes.
“为什么你们不把那双破鞋扔掉呢？”他说， ‘Why can’t you leave that pair of old shoes alone?’ said he.
“哎呀，穿着它们都走不出一码远！＂ ‘Why, you could not walk a yard in them!’
“是的，他们是很破旧，”他们回答说，“但是任何穿上这双鞋子的人想到哪里就能到哪里，瞬间就到。” ‘Yes, they are old enough,’ answered they; ‘but whoever puts them on and wishes himself at a particular place, gets there without going.’
“这听起来很不错，”年轻人说， ‘That sounds very clever,’ said the youth.
“让我试试，然后我就能告诉你们它们应该归谁。” ‘Let me try them, and then I shall be able to tell you whose they ought to be.’
两人都觉得这个主意不错，就把鞋子递给了他。但他一穿上鞋子，就喊道： The idea pleased the men, and they handed him the shoes; but the moment they were on his feet he cried:
“我希望我在班卡岛的城堡里！” ‘I wish to be in the castle on the Banka!’
他还没反应过来，就已经到那里了，并且发现悲伤过度的太阳妹妹已经奄奄一息了。 And before he knew it, he was there, and found the Sister of the Sun dying of grief.
他跪在她身边，将一根针扎进她的手掌，于是一滴血流了出来。 He knelt down by her side, and pulling a pin he stuck it into the palm of her hand, so that a drop of blood gushed out.
他按照老妇人吩咐的那样，吸了这滴血，公主立即苏醒了，她一下子就搂住了年轻人的脖子。 This he sucked, as he had been told to do by the old woman, and immediately the princess came to herself, and flung her arms round his neck.
接着，公主将她的遭遇都告诉了年轻人以及自从他错过那艘船之后都发生了什么。 Then she told him all her story, and what had happened since the ship had sailed away without him.
“但最糟糕的就是，”她补充道，“我父亲打了败仗，因为你与他的神剑一起消失了，他的军队几乎全军覆没。” ‘But the worst misfortune of all,’ she added, ‘was a battle which my father lost because you had vanished with his magic sword; and out of his whole army hardly one man was left.’
“带我去战场看看。”他说。 ‘Show me the battle-field,’ said he.
于是公主把他带到一块荒地上，那里遍野的死尸正等候着下葬。 And she took him to a wild heath, where the dead were lying as they fell, waiting for burial.
年轻人一个接一个地用手杖的一端碰了他们一下，直到最后，所有的人都在他面前站了起来。 One by one he touched them with the end of his staff, till at length they all stood before him.
整个王国欢呼雀跃。这一次，婚礼终于得以举行。 Throughout the kingdom there was nothing but joy; and THIS time the wedding was REALLY celebrated.
这对新婚夫妇在班卡岛的城堡里过着幸福的生活，直到他们的生命走到尽头。 And the bridal pair lived happily in the castle on the Banka till they died.
