铜指环 The Bronze Ring
作者：安德鲁•朗格 by Andrew Lang
从前有个国家，那里住着一位国王，他的宫殿被一个宽敞的花园包围着。 Once upon a time in a certain country there lived a king whose palace was surrounded by a spacious garden.
然而，尽管花园中有不少园丁，土壤也很肥沃，但这个花园就是不开花、不结果实，甚至连青草和茂盛的大树都没有。 But, though the gardeners were many and the soil was good, this garden yielded neither flowers nor fruits, not even grass or shady trees.
国王对此感到很绝望。这时，一个年长的智者对他说： The King was in despair about it, when a wise old man said to him:
“您的园丁不了解他们的工作，但您能期望这些人做得多好呢？他们的父亲不是修鞋匠就是木匠。 “Your gardeners do not understand their business: but what can you expect of men whose fathers were cobblers and carpenters?
他们怎么会料理您的花园呢？” How should they have learned to cultivate your garden?”
“你说得太对了！”国王喊道。 “You are quite right,” cried the King.
“所以，”长者继续说道，“您应该请一位父亲和祖父都是园丁的人来照看您的花园，这样不久之后，您的花园将会绿草如茵、鲜花芳菲，您还会吃到美味的果实。” “Therefore,” continued the old man, “you should send for a gardener whose father and grandfather have been gardeners before him, and very soon your garden will be full of green grass and gay flowers, and you will enjoy its delicious fruit.”
于是国王派出使者，走遍了他领地内的每一个城市与村庄，去寻找一个祖辈都曾是园丁的人。40天后，他们找到了一个合适的人选。 So the King sent messengers to every town, village, and hamlet in his dominions, to look for a gardener whose forefathers had been gardeners also, and after forty days one was found.
“跟我们走吧，去做国王的园丁。”他们对他说。 “Come with us and be gardener to the King,” they said to him.
“像我这样的可怜虫怎么能去见国王呢？”园丁说。 “How can I go to the King,” said the gardener, “a poor wretch like me?”
“这没关系，”他们答道， “That is of no consequence,” they answered.
“这是给你和你家人的新衣服。” “Here are new clothes for you and your family.”
“但是我欠了好几个人的钱。” “But I owe money to several people.”
“我们会替你还债。”他们说。 “We will pay your debts,” they said.
园丁禁不住劝说，带上妻子和儿子跟着使者一起走了。国王很高兴能找到一位真正的园丁，于是把他的花园托付给园丁照料。 So the gardener allowed himself to be persuaded, and went away with the messengers, taking his wife and his son with him; and the King, delighted to have found a real gardener, entrusted him with the care of his garden.
园丁很容易地就让国王的花园开出了花朵，结出了果实。一年过后，花园完全变了一个样，国王奖赏了他的新园丁很多礼物。 The man found no difficulty in making the royal garden produce flowers and fruit, and at the end of a year the park was not like the same place, and the King showered gifts upon his new servant.
你刚才已经听说了，这个园丁有一个儿子。他是个非常英俊的年轻人，举止端庄，彬彬有礼。他每天都把花园里结出的最好的果实给国王送去，把花园里开出的最美丽的花朵给公主送去。 The gardener, as you have heard already, had a son, who was a very handsome young man, with most agreeable manners, and every day he carried the best fruit of the garden to the King, and all the prettiest flowers to his daughter.
这位公主美丽绝伦，正值16岁的芳龄，所以国王开始考虑着是时候给她找个丈夫了。 Now this princess was wonderfully pretty and was just sixteen years old, and the King was beginning to think it was time that she should be married.
“我亲爱的孩子，”他说，“你到了该结婚的年龄了，因此我考虑把你嫁给我首相的儿子。” “My dear child,” said he, “you are of an age to take a husband, therefore I am thinking of marrying you to the son of my prime minister.”
“父亲，”公主答道，“我绝不会嫁给首相的儿子。” “Father,” replied the Princess, “I will never marry the son of the minister.”
“为什么不呢？”国王问道。 “Why not?” asked the King.
“因为我爱的是园丁的儿子。”公主答道。 “Because I love the gardener’s son,” answered the Princess.
听到公主的回答，国王起初非常生气，然后流了泪，叹了口气，表示这样的丈夫配不上他的女儿。然而年轻的公主下定决心，要嫁给园丁的儿子。 On hearing this the King was at first very angry, and then he wept and sighed, and declared that such a husband was not worthy of his daughter; but the young Princess was not to be turned from her resolution to marry the gardener’s son.
于是国王去和他的大臣们商量。 Then the King consulted his ministers.
“您得这么做，”他们说， “This is what you must do,” they said.
“要摆脱那个园丁，您必须派两位求婚者去一个非常遥远的国家，先回来的那一个将和您的女儿结婚。” “To get rid of the gardener you must send both suitors to a very distant country, and the one who returns first shall marry your daughter.”
国王采纳了这条建议，赐给首相的儿子一匹骏马和一个装满了金币的钱包，却给了园丁的儿子一匹跛足的老马和一个装满了铜币的钱包。所有人都认为园丁的儿子踏上了一条不归路。 The King followed this advice, and the minister’s son was presented with a splendid horse and a purse full of gold pieces, while the gardener’s son had only an old lame horse and a purse full of copper money, and every one thought he would never come back from his journey.
在他们动身的前一天，公主和她的情人见了面，对他说： The day before they started the Princess met her lover and said to him:
“勇敢些，要永远记住我爱你。 “Be brave, and remember always that I love you.
拿着这个装满珠宝的钱包，看在我爱你的分上，尽你所能地好好利用它，快快回来，然后向我求婚。” Take this purse full of jewels and make the best use you can of them for love of me, and come back quickly and demand my hand.”
这两位求婚者一起离开了城镇，但首相的儿子骑着良马疾驰而去，很快就消失在遥远的群山之后。 The two suitors left the town together, but the minister’s son went off at a gallop on his good horse, and very soon was lost to sight behind the most distant hills.
他连续行进了几天，不久之后，来到了一处喷泉。喷泉旁边，一位衣衫褴褛的老妇人坐在一块石头上。 He traveled on for some days, and presently reached a fountain beside which an old woman all in rags sat upon a stone.
“你好啊，年轻的旅行者。”她说。 “Good-day to you, young traveler,” said she.
但首相的儿子没有答话。 But the minister’s son made no reply.
“可怜可怜我吧，旅行者。”她又说了一句， “Have pity upon me, traveler,” she said again.
“就像你看到的这样，我快要饿死了，我在这里已经三天了，没有一个人给我任何东西吃。” “I am dying of hunger, as you see, and three days have I been here and no one has given me anything.”
“走开，老巫婆，”年轻人喊起来，“我什么都帮不了你。”他说完便继续出发了。 “Let me alone, old witch,” cried the young man; “I can do nothing for you,” and so saying he went on his way.
当天晚上，园丁的儿子也骑着灰色的跛足马到了那个喷泉处。 That same evening the gardener’s son rode up to the fountain upon his lame gray horse.
“你好啊，年轻的旅行者。”讨饭的女人说。 “Good-day to you, young traveler,” said the beggar-woman.
“你好，老婆婆。”他回答道。 “Good-day, good woman,” answered he.
“年轻的旅行者，可怜可怜我吧。” “Young traveler, have pity upon me.”
“拿着我的钱包，老婆婆，”他说道，“爬上马，坐在我后面吧，因为你的腿脚一定没什么力气。” “Take my purse, good woman,” said he, “and mount behind me, for your legs can’t be very strong.”
老妇人没等他说第二遍，就爬上了马背，坐在了他后面。他们就这样抵达了一个强国的第一大城市。 The old woman didn’t wait to be asked twice, but mounted behind him, and in this style they reached the chief city of a powerful kingdom.
首相的儿子下榻在一家大旅店，园丁的儿子和老妇人则下马，住在了乞丐住的客栈里。 The minister’s son was lodged in a grand inn, the gardener’s son and the old woman dismounted at the inn for beggars.
第二天，园丁的儿子听到街上传来一阵巨大的响声。国王的传令官经过这里，一边吹着各种乐器，一边喊道： The next day the gardener’s son heard a great noise in the street, and the King’s heralds passed, blowing all kinds of instruments, and crying:
“我们的国王陛下年迈体弱。 “The King, our master, is old and infirm.
如果有人能治好他的疾病，并让他变得和年轻时一样有力气，将会得到国王的重赏。” He will give a great reward to whoever will cure him and give him back the strength of his youth.”
于是，讨饭的老妇人对她的恩人说： Then the old beggar-woman said to her benefactor:
“要去领取国王承诺的奖赏，你必须得这么做： “This is what you must do to obtain the reward which the King promises.
从南门走出城市，你在那儿会看到三只不同颜色的小狗，第一只会是白色的，第二只是黑色的，第三只是红色的。 Go out of the town by the south gate, and there you will find three little dogs of different colors; the first will be white, the second black, the third red.
你得杀掉它们并把它们分开烧掉，然后把灰烬收集起来。 You must kill them and then burn them separately, and gather up the ashes.
你要把每条狗烧成的灰烬都放到和那条狗颜色相同的袋子里，然后走到王宫的门前，大声喊：‘阿尔巴尼亚国耶尼纳地区的名医来了。 Put the ashes of each dog into a bag of its own color, then go before the door of the palace and cry out, ‘A celebrated physician has come from Janina in Albania.
只有他能治好国王的病，并让他变得和年轻时一样有力气。’ He alone can cure the King and give him back the strength of his youth.’
国王的御医会说：‘这是一个江湖骗子，不是一个饱学之士’，而且他们会用各种方式刁难你，但最终你得克服这些困难，在生病的国王面前介绍自己。 The King’s physicians will say, ‘This is an impostor, and not a learned man,’ and they will make all sorts of difficulties, but you will overcome them all at last, and will present yourself before the sick King.
然后，你得要尽可能多的木材，有三头骡子能驮的那么多，还要一口大锅，接着必须把你自己和国王关在一间屋子内；当大锅的水烧开的时候，你得把国王扔进大锅，把他放在锅里煮，直到他的骨头和肉完全分开。 You must then demand as much wood as three mules can carry, and a great cauldron, and must shut yourself up in a room with the Sultan, and when the cauldron boils you must throw him into it, and there leave him until his flesh is completely separated from his bones.
然后，把他的骨头排列在合适的位置，从三个口袋里取出灰烬，撒在这些骨头上面。 Then arrange the bones in their proper places, and throw over them the ashes out of the three bags.
国王将会起死回生，并变得像他20岁时那样。 The King will come back to life, and will be just as he was when he was twenty years old.
你必须要那枚能够实现你所有愿望的铜指环作为奖赏。 For your reward you must demand the bronze ring which has the power to grant you everything you desire.
去吧，我的孩子，要牢记我告诉你的这些话。” Go, my son, and do not forget any of my instructions.”
年轻人按照讨饭的老妇人的话做了。 The young man followed the old beggar-woman’s directions.
他一出城门就看到了白狗、红狗和黑狗，他杀掉了它们，并把它们烧成了灰，把灰烬收集在三个袋子里。 On going out of the town he found the white, red, and black dogs, and killed and burnt them, gathering the ashes in three bags.
然后他跑到王宫，喊道： Then he ran to the palace and cried:
“阿尔巴尼亚国耶尼纳地区的名医来了。 “A celebrated physician has just come from Janina in Albania.
只有他能治好国王的病，并让他变得像年轻时一样有力气。” He alone can cure the King and give him back the strength of his youth.”
国王的御医一开始都嘲笑这个无名的旅行者，国王却下令让这个陌生人进去。 The King’s physicians at first laughed at the unknown wayfarer, but the Sultan ordered that the stranger should be admitted.
大锅和成担的木柴被搬了进来，很快国王就被煮掉了。 They brought the cauldron and the loads of wood, and very soon the King was boiling away.
快到中午的时候，园丁的儿子已经把骨头摆好了位置，他刚把灰烬撒到骨头上，老国王就复活了，变得年轻而健壮。 Toward mid-day the gardener’s son arranged the bones in their places, and he had hardly scattered the ashes over them before the old King revived, to find himself once more young and hearty.
“我该怎么报答你呢，我的恩人？”他喊道， “How can I reward you, my benefactor?” he cried.
“你想要我一半的财富吗？” “Will you take half my treasures?”
“不。”园丁的儿子答道。 “No,” said the gardener’s son.
“把我的女儿嫁给你如何？” “My daughter’s hand?”
“不。” “No.”
“我的半个国家？” “Take half my kingdom.”
“不。 “No.
我只想要那枚能立即实现我所有愿望的铜指环。” Give me only the bronze ring which can instantly grant me anything I wish for.”
“唉！”国王说，“我是极其珍视那枚奇妙的指环的，但你可以拿走它。” “Alas!” said the King, “I set great store by that marvelous ring; nevertheless, you shall have it.”
然后，国王把指环给了他。 And he gave it to him.
园丁的儿子回去和讨饭的老妇人道了别，接着，他对铜指环说： The gardener’s son went back to say good-by to the old beggar-woman; then he said to the bronze ring:
“为我准备一条华丽的大船，好让我继续旅程。 “Prepare a splendid ship in which I may continue my journey.
我要纯金做船体，白银做桅杆，织锦做船帆；还要12个外表高贵、穿戴得像国王一样的年轻人做船员。 Let the hull be of fine gold, the masts of silver, the sails of brocade; let the crew consist of twelve young men of noble appearance, dressed like kings.
让圣尼古拉斯来掌舵。 St. Nicholas will be at the helm.
船上载满了钻石、红宝石、绿宝石和红玉。” As to the cargo, let it be diamonds, rubies, emeralds, and carbuncles.”
海上立刻出现了一条船，每个细节都和园丁的儿子描述的一样。然后，园丁的儿子上了船，继续他的旅程。 And immediately a ship appeared upon the sea which resembled in every particular the description given by the gardener’s son, and, stepping on board, he continued his journey.
不久，他抵达了一个大城市，住在一座富丽堂皇的宫殿中。 Presently he arrived at a great town and established himself in a wonderful palace.
几天后，他碰到了自己的对手——首相的儿子。他花光了所有的钱，不得不从事清运垃圾和尘土这种低微的工作。 After several days he met his rival, the minister’s son, who had spent all his money and was reduced to the disagreeable employment of a carrier of dust and rubbish.
园丁的儿子对他说： The gardener’s son said to him:
“你叫什么名字？来自哪个家族？从哪个国家来？” “What is your name, what is your family, and from what country do you come?”
“我是一个大国首相的儿子，然而您看，我现在却被迫从事着一份多么低微的工作。” “I am the son of the prime minister of a great nation, and yet see what a degrading occupation I am reduced to.”
“听我说，虽然我对你的了解仅限于此，但我愿意帮助你。 “Listen to me; though I don’t know anything more about you, I am willing to help you.
我会给你一条船让你回到自己的国家，但你得答应我一个条件。” I will give you a ship to take you back to your own country upon one condition.”
“无论是什么条件，我都愿意接受。” “Whatever it may be, I accept it willingly.”
“跟我到我的宫殿里来。” “Follow me to my palace.”
首相的儿子跟着他，却没有认出来这位富有的陌生人是谁。 The minister’s son followed the rich stranger, whom he had not recognized.
到了宫殿，园丁的儿子向奴隶们示意，让他们脱光了新来者的衣服。 When they reached the palace the gardener’s son made a sign to his slaves, who completely undressed the new-comer.
“把这枚指环烧红，”主人下令道，“然后用它在这个人背后打个烙印。” “Make this ring red-hot,” commanded the master, “and mark the man with it upon his back.”
奴隶们照着做了。 The slaves obeyed him.
“现在，年轻人，”富有的陌生人说，“我给你一艘船，好让你回到自己的国家。” “Now, young man,” said the rich stranger, “I am going to give you a vessel which will take you back to your own country.”
然后，他走了出去，拿出铜指环，说道： And, going out, he took the bronze ring and said:
“铜指环，服从你的主人吧。 “Bronze ring, obey thy master.
给我准备一条船，半朽的木头要被涂成黑色，船帆要破破烂烂，水手全都老弱病残。 Prepare me a ship of which the half-rotten timbers shall be painted black, let the sails be in rags, and the sailors infirm and sickly.
第一个水手少一条腿，第二个缺一只胳膊，第三个是驼背，剩下的那个不是跛足、弯脚就是瞎子，并且大多数水手都相貌丑陋、伤痕累累。 One shall have lost a leg, another an arm, the third shall be a hunchback, another lame or club-footed or blind, and most of them shall be ugly and covered with scars.
快，执行我的命令吧。” Go, and let my orders be executed.”
首相的儿子登上了这条旧船，多亏了有顺风，他终于到达了自己的国家。 The minister’s son embarked in this old vessel, and thanks to favorable winds, at length reached his own country.
尽管他回国时处境可怜，但人们还是欣喜地迎接了他。 In spite of the pitiable condition in which he returned they received him joyfully.
“我是第一个回来的，”他对国王说道，“现在请兑现您的承诺，把公主嫁给我吧。” “I am the first to come back,” said he to the King; “now fulfil your promise, and give me the princess in marriage.”
因此，人们立刻开始准备婚礼盛典。 So they at once began to prepare for the wedding festivities.
而可怜的公主对此感到十分伤心和愤怒。 As to the poor princess, she was sorrowful and angry enough about it.
第二天拂晓时，一条华丽而鼓满风帆的大船停泊在城市前面。 The next morning, at daybreak, a wonderful ship with every sail set came to anchor before the town.
那时候，国王正好站在王宫的窗前。 The King happened at that moment to be at the palace window.
“多奇怪的一艘船啊，”他喊了起来，“船体是金的，桅杆是银的，船帆是绸的，开船的这些貌若王子的年轻人又是谁？ “What strange ship is this,” he cried, “that has a golden hull, silver masts, and silken sails, and who are the young men like princes who man it?
我看到的不会是圣尼古拉斯在掌舵吧？ And do I not see St. Nicholas at the helm?
立刻去请这条船的船长到我的宫殿里来。” Go at once and invite the captain of the ship to come to the palace.”
他的仆人按照他的命令做了。很快，一位英俊迷人的年轻王子走了进来，他身着艳丽的丝绸服饰，上面装饰着珍珠和钻石。 His servants obeyed him, and very soon in came an enchantingly handsome young prince, dressed in rich silk, ornamented with pearls and diamonds.
“年轻人，”国王说，“无论你是谁，我都欢迎你。 “Young man,” said the King, “you are welcome, whoever you may be.
只要你还在我的都城里，请赏脸做我的客人吧。” Do me the favor to be my guest as long as you remain in my capital.”
“非常感谢，陛下，”船长答道，“我接受您的邀请。” “Many thanks, sire,” replied the captain, “I accept your offer.”
“我的女儿要结婚了，”国王说道，“您能帮我送亲吗？” “My daughter is about to be married,” said the King; “will you give her away?”
“我感到非常荣幸，陛下。” “I shall be charmed, sire.”
片刻之后，公主和她的未婚夫进来了。 Soon after came the Princess and her betrothed.
“为什么，这是怎么回事？”年轻的船长喊了起来，“您要把您如此迷人的公主嫁给这样的一个男人吗？” “Why, how is this?” cried the young captain; “would you marry this charming princess to such a man as that?”
“但他是我的首相的儿子！” “But he is my prime minister’s son!”
“这有什么关系？ “What does that matter?
我没法给您的女儿送亲。 I cannot give your daughter away.
她的未婚夫是我的仆人。” The man she is betrothed to is one of my servants.”
“你的仆人？” “Your servant?”
“千真万确。 “Without doubt.
我在一个遥远的城市碰到了他，他已经沦落到给房子清运灰尘和垃圾的地步了。 I met him in a distant town reduced to carrying away dust and rubbish from the houses.
我可怜他，让他做了我的仆人。” I had pity on him and engaged him as one of my servants.”
“这不可能！”国王喊道。 “It is impossible!” cried the King.
“您想让我证明我说的话吗？ “Do you wish me to prove what I say?
这个年轻人乘着我给他的船回来了，那是一艘不适合航海的船，黑色的船体破旧不堪，水手都体弱且跛脚。” This young man returned in a vessel which I fitted out for him, an unseaworthy ship with a black battered hull, and the sailors were infirm and crippled.”
“一点儿都没错。”国王说。 “It is quite true,” said the King.
“这不是真的，”首相之子喊道， “It is false,” cried the minister’s son.
“我不认识这个人！” “I do not know this man!”
“陛下，”年轻的船长说，“让人脱下您女儿未婚夫的衣服，看看他背后是不是有我的指环烙下的印记。” “Sire,” said the young captain, “order your daughter’s betrothed to be stripped, and see if the mark of my ring is not branded upon his back.”
国王正准备下令，首相的儿子为了避免受辱，承认船长说的话是真的。 The King was about to give this order, when the minister’s son, to save himself from such an indignity, admitted that the story was true.
“现在，陛下，”年轻的船长说，“您还没认出我来吗？” “And now, sire,” said the young captain, “do you not recognize me?”
“我认出来了，”公主说道，“你是园丁的儿子，我一直爱着你，我希望嫁的人是你。” “I recognize you,” said the Princess; “you are the gardener’s son whom I have always loved, and it is you I wish to marry.”
“年轻人，你可以做我的女婿，”国王喊道， “Young man, you shall be my son-in-law,” cried the King.
“结婚盛典已经开始了，所以你今天就可以跟我的女儿结婚。” “The marriage festivities are already begun, so you shall marry my daughter this very day.”
于是，就在那一天，园丁的儿子娶了美丽的公主。 And so that very day the gardener’s son married the beautiful Princess.
几个月过去了。 Several months passed.
这对年轻夫妇过得非常幸福快乐，而国王也为招到了这样一位女婿而感到越来越满意。 The young couple were as happy as the day was long, and the King was more and more pleased with himself for having secured such a son-in-law.
然而，不久以后，这位金船船长觉得有必要进行一次远航，他温柔地拥抱了自己的妻子，然后上了船。 But, presently, the captain of the golden ship found it necessary to take a long voyage, and after embracing his wife tenderly he embarked.
然而，在都城的郊外住着一个老人，他一辈子都在学习妖术——包括炼金术、占星术、魔法和符咒。 Now in the outskirts of the capital there lived an old man, who had spent his life in studying black arts—alchemy, astrology, magic, and enchantment.
这个人发现，园丁的儿子之所以能成功地娶到公主，完全是借助了指环中魔仆的帮助，而魔仆听从铜指环的命令。 This man found out that the gardener’s son had only succeeded in marrying the Princess by the help of the genii who obeyed the bronze ring.
“那枚指环会是我的。”他自言自语地说。 “I will have that ring,” said he to himself.
因此，他向下走到海边，捉了几条红色的小鱼。 So he went down to the sea-shore and caught some little red fishes.
这几条小鱼确实非常漂亮。 Really, they were quite wonderfully pretty.
然后，他走回去，走过公主窗前，开始吆喝： Then he came back, and, passing before the Princess’s window, he began to cry out:
“谁想要些漂亮的小红鱼？” “Who wants some pretty little red fishes?”
公主听到了他的吆喝，派出她的奴隶，问这个年老的小贩： The Princess heard him, and sent out one of her slaves, who said to the old peddler:
“你想用你的小鱼换什么东西？” “What will you take for your fish?”
“一枚铜指环。” “A bronze ring.”
“一枚铜指环，老傻瓜！ “A bronze ring, old simpleton!
我去哪儿给你找一枚？” And where shall I find one?”
“公主房间的垫子下就有一枚。” “Under the cushion in the Princess’s room.”
这个奴隶回去见她的女主人。 The slave went back to her mistress.
“这个老疯子不要金也不要银。”她说。 “The old madman will take neither gold nor silver,” said she.
“那他想要什么？” “What does he want then?”
“藏在垫子下的一枚铜指环。” “A bronze ring that is hidden under a cushion.”
“找到这枚指环，拿给他吧。”公主说。 “Find the ring and give it to him,” said the Princess.
最后，奴隶找到了金船船长偶然忘记带在身边的铜指环，把他交给了老人，老人立刻带着指环匆匆离开了。 And at last the slave found the bronze ring, which the captain of the golden ship had accidentally left behind and carried it to the man, who made off with it instantly.
他一到自己的房子就拿出指环，说：“铜指环，服从你的主人。 Hardly had he reached his own house when, taking the ring, he said, “Bronze ring, obey thy master.
我想要让金船变成黑色的木头，船员都变成面目可憎的黑鬼；让圣尼古拉斯离开舵轮，船上唯一的货物是黑猫。” I desire that the golden ship shall turn to black wood, and the crew to hideous negroes; that St. Nicholas shall leave the helm and that the only cargo shall be black cats.”
铜指环里的魔仆听从了他的命令。 And the genii of the bronze ring obeyed him.
年轻的船长发现自己在海上的境况糟糕，意识到有人偷了他的铜指环。他大声哀叹着自己的不幸遭遇，却无济于事。 Finding himself upon the sea in this miserable condition, the young captain understood that some one must have stolen the bronze ring from him, and he lamented his misfortune loudly; but that did him no good.
“唉！”他自言自语道，“那个拿走了我的指环的人，也许也带走了我亲爱的妻子。 “Alas!” he said to himself, “whoever has taken my ring has probably taken my dear wife also.
回到我自己的国家又有什么用呢？” What good will it do me to go back to my own country?”
于是，他从一个岛屿航行到另一个岛屿，从一处海岸漂到另一处海岸，觉得自己无论走到哪里都遭到所有人的嘲笑。很快他就变得一贫如洗，他和他的船员以及可怜的黑猫只有草药和根茎可吃。 And he sailed about from island to island, and from shore to shore, believing that wherever he went everybody was laughing at him, and very soon his poverty was so great that he and his crew and the poor black cats had nothing to eat but herbs and roots.
四处漂泊了很长时间后，他到达了一个岛屿，岛上住着老鼠。 After wandering about a long time he reached an island inhabited by mice.
当这位船长上岸后，他开始对这个国家进行实地考察。 The captain landed upon the shore and began to explore the country.
那里到处都是老鼠，除了老鼠还是老鼠。 There were mice everywhere, and nothing but mice.
然而，一些跟着他上岸的黑猫已经几天没有进食了，它们饿到了极点，使鼠群元气大伤。 Some of the black cats had followed him, and, not having been fed for several days, they were fearfully hungry, and made terrible havoc among the mice.
于是，老鼠女王召开了会议。 Then the queen of the mice held a council.
“如果那个船长不把这些凶残的猫关起来，”她说道，“它们会把我们全部吃掉的。 “These cats will eat every one of us,” she said, “if the captain of the ship does not shut the ferocious animals up.
让我们派出由最勇敢的老鼠组成的代表团跟他谈谈。” Let us send a deputation to him of the bravest among us.”
几只老鼠自愿接受任务，动身去找年轻的船长。 Several mice offered themselves for this mission and set out to find the young captain.
“船长，”它们说，“赶快离开我们的岛屿吧，否则我们会死掉，我们每只老鼠都会死掉。” “Captain,” said they, “go away quickly from our island, or we shall perish, every mouse of us.”
“我愿意离开，”年轻的船长答道，“但你们得接受我一个条件。 “Willingly,” replied the young captain, “upon one condition.
那就是，你们必须先给我带回一枚铜指环，那是一个狡猾的魔法师从我这里偷走的。 That is that you shall first bring me back a bronze ring which some clever magician has stolen from me.
如果你们不这么做，我就把我所有的猫都放到你们的岛上，你们将会遭受灭顶之灾。” If you do not do this I will land all my cats upon your island, and you shall be exterminated.”
老鼠们惶恐万分地离开了。 The mice withdrew in great dismay.
“我们该怎么办呢？”老鼠女王说， “What is to be done?” said the Queen.
“我们怎么才能找到那枚铜指环呢？” “How can we find this bronze ring?”
她重新召开了一次会议，召集了世界每个角落的老鼠，但没有老鼠知道铜指环在哪里。 She held a new council, calling in mice from every quarter of the globe, but nobody knew where the bronze ring was.
突然，从一个非常遥远的国度来了三只老鼠。 Suddenly three mice arrived from a very distant country.
第一只是瞎眼的，第二只是跛脚的，第三只是耳朵残缺不全的。 One was blind, the second lame, and the third had her ears cropped.
“嗬，嗬，嗬！”新来的老鼠说， “Ho, ho, ho!” said the new-comers.
“我们从一个遥远的国家来。” “We come from a far distant country.”
“你们知道能让魔仆听令的铜指环在哪里吗？” “Do you know where the bronze ring is which the genii obey?”
“嗬，嗬，嗬！我们知道。一个魔法师占有了它，而且他现在白天把它放在口袋里，晚上放在嘴里。” “Ho, ho, ho! we know; an old sorcerer has taken possession of it, and now he keeps it in his pocket by day and in his mouth by night.”
“去从他那里把指环拿过来，尽快回来。” “Go and take it from him, and come back as soon as possible.”
于是，三只老鼠给自己造了一条小船，起航去魔法师的国家。 So the three mice made themselves a boat and set sail for the magician’s country.
等它们抵达王都，它们就上了岸，奔向王宫，只留下瞎眼的老鼠在岸边看船。 When they reached the capital they landed and ran to the palace, leaving only the blind mouse on the shore to take care of the boat.
然后，它们一直等到晚上。 Then they waited till it was night.
邪恶的老人躺到床上，把铜指环放在嘴里，很快就睡着了。 The wicked old man lay down in bed and put the bronze ring into his mouth, and very soon he was asleep.
“现在我们该怎么办呢？”这两只小动物商量道。 “Now, what shall we do?” said the two little animals to each other.
耳朵残缺不全的老鼠发现了一盏装满了油的灯和一个装满了胡椒的瓶子。 The mouse with the cropped ears found a lamp full of oil and a bottle full of pepper.
因此，她先把尾巴浸入灯油中，再放进胡椒中，接着把尾巴伸向了魔法师的鼻子。 So she dipped her tail first in the oil and then in the pepper, and held it to the sorcerer’s nose.
“阿嚏！阿嚏！”老人打起喷嚏来，但没有醒来，喷嚏让指环从他的嘴里蹦了出来。 “Atisha! atisha!” sneezed the old man, but he did not wake, and the shock made the bronze ring jump out of his mouth.
跛脚的老鼠飞快地抓住了这珍贵的宝贝，把它带到了船上。 Quick as thought the lame mouse snatched up the precious talisman and carried it off to the boat.
你能想象得到，当魔法师醒来，发现指环不见踪影，他是多么绝望！ Imagine the despair of the magician when he awoke and the bronze ring was nowhere to be found!
但这时候，我们的三只老鼠已经带着它们的战利品起航了。 But by that time our three mice had set sail with their prize.
顺风将它们送到了岛屿上，老鼠女王正在那里等着它们。 A favoring breeze was carrying them toward the island where the queen of the mice was awaiting them.
它们自然而然地谈起了铜指环。 Naturally they began to talk about the bronze ring.
“我们中间谁的功劳最大？”它们同声喊道。 “Which of us deserves the most credit?” they cried all at once.
“是我，”瞎眼的老鼠说，“如果不是我警惕地看着船，我们的船会漂向广阔的大海。” “I do,” said the blind mouse, “for without my watchfulness our boat would have drifted away to the open sea.”
“不是这样的，”耳朵残缺不全的老鼠喊道，“功劳是我的。 “No, indeed,” cried the mouse with the cropped ears; “the credit is mine.
难道不是我让指环从魔法师的嘴里蹦出来的吗？” Did I not cause the ring to jump out of the man’s mouth?”
“不，是我的，”跛脚的老鼠喊道，“是我带着指环跑掉的。” “No, it is mine,” cried the lame one, “for I ran off with the ring.”
它们吵着吵着，很快就开始动起手来，然后，哎呀！吵到最激烈的时候，指环掉进了海里。 And from high words they soon came to blows, and, alas! when the quarrel was fiercest the bronze ring fell into the sea.
“既然我们已经因为自己的愚蠢而丢了宝贝，让我们的同类陷入灭顶之灾，”三只老鼠说，“我们怎么回去见我们的女王呢？ “How are we to face our queen,” said the three mice, “when by our folly we have lost the talisman and condemned our people to be utterly exterminated?
我们没法回自己的国家了，我们就在这个荒岛登陆，并在这里过完我们悲惨的一生吧。” We cannot go back to our country; let us land on this desert island and there end our miserable lives.”
他们说做就做。 No sooner said than done.
小船到达了荒岛，老鼠们上了岸。 The boat reached the island, and the mice landed.
两只老鼠很快抛下了它们瞎眼的姐妹去捉苍蝇了，但当瞎老鼠悲伤地在海岸边独自游荡的时候，它找到了一条死鱼。它吃着吃着，发现了一个硬硬的东西。 The blind mouse was speedily deserted by her two sisters, who went off to hunt flies, but as she wandered sadly along the shore she found a dead fish, and was eating it, when she felt something very hard.
它喊了起来，另外两只老鼠跑了过来。 At her cries the other two mice ran up.
“是那枚铜指环！ “It is the bronze ring!
是那件宝贝！”它们欢呼起来，然后重新上了它们的小船，很快到达了鼠岛。 It is the talisman!” they cried joyfully, and, getting into their boat again, they soon reached the mouse island.
它们回来的正是时候，因为船长刚准备把船上的猫放上岸。这时，一队老鼠给他拿来了珍贵的铜指环。 It was time they did, for the captain was just going to land his cargo of cats, when a deputation of mice brought him the precious bronze ring.
“铜指环，”年轻人下令道，“服从你的主人吧。” “Bronze ring,” commanded the young man, “obey thy master.
让我的船恢复到原来的样子。” Let my ship appear as it was before.”
指环中的魔仆立刻照做了，老旧的黑船再一次变成了光彩夺目的金船，船帆又变回了织锦。英俊的海员跑向白银桅杆和丝绸绳索，很快他们就向王都起航了。 Immediately the genii of the ring set to work, and the old black vessel became once more the wonderful golden ship with sails of brocade; the handsome sailors ran to the silver masts and the silken ropes, and very soon they set sail for the capital.
啊！这些水手一边唱着歌，一边划过如镜的海面，多么愉快啊！ Ah! how merrily the sailors sang as they flew over the glassy sea!
最后，他们到达了港口。 At last the port was reached.
船长上了岸，跑向王宫，他发现邪恶的老人正在那里熟睡。 The captain landed and ran to the palace, where he found the wicked old man asleep.
公主紧紧地抱住她的丈夫，抱了很久。 The Princess clasped her husband in a long embrace.
魔法师企图逃走，但人们抓住了他，用牢固的绳索把他捆了起来。 The magician tried to escape, but he was seized and bound with strong cords.
第二天，魔法师被绑在了一头脾气暴躁、驮着坚果的骡子的尾巴上，被摔得粉碎，碎片和骡子背上的坚果一样多。[1] The next day the sorcerer, tied to the tail of a savage mule loaded with nuts, was broken into as many pieces as there were nuts upon the mule’s back. [1]
