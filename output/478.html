地下餐馆和玫瑰花 The Rathskeller and the Rose
作者：欧·亨利 by O. Henry
波希·卡林顿小姐获得了成功。 Miss Posie Carrington had earned her success.
她出生在那个叫做克兰伯里角的小镇，从来到这个世界的那一刻起，她就为“博格斯”这个家族姓氏所困扰。 She began life handicapped by the family name of “Boggs,” in the small town known as Cranberry Corners.
十八岁的时候，她改姓“卡林顿”，并在都市一家滑稽戏表演剧团的歌舞队里找到了工作。 At the age of eighteen she had acquired the name of “Carrington” and a position in the chorus of a metropolitan burlesque company.
此后她顺风顺水，轻松愉快地在演艺之路上步步高升：她是著名的“小鸟”八重唱组合成员之一，出演了大获成功的音乐喜剧《扯淡和伙伴》，担任《废话》土豆甲虫舞的领舞，最后在《国王的浴衣》那出戏里出演侍女“图瓦内特”一角。《国王的浴衣》获得了评论界的好评，也给了她成名的机会。 Thence upward she had ascended by the legitimate and delectable steps of “broiler,” member of the famous “Dickey-bird” octette, in the successful musical comedy, “Fudge and Fellows,” leader of the potato-bug dance in “Fol-de-Rol,” and at length to the part of the maid “’Toinette” in “The King’s Bath-Robe,” which captured the critics and gave her her chance.
当我们谈论卡林顿小姐的时候，她正声名鹊起、大红大紫；那个精明的经理蒂莫西·戈尔茨坦先生和她签下了合同，她将在下一季主演戴德·里奇的新剧《迷醉华灯下》 。 And when we come to consider Miss Carrington she is in the heydey of flattery, fame and fizz; and that astute manager, Herr Timothy Goldstein, has her signature to iron-clad papers that she will star the coming season in Dyde Rich’s new play, “Paresis by Gaslight.”
很快，有一个叫海史密斯的20世纪的青年实力派性格演员来找蒂莫西先生，恳请担任“索尔·海托塞”一角，也就是喜剧《迷醉华灯下》里的男主角。 Promptly there came to Herr Timothy a capable twentieth-century young character actor by the name of Highsmith, who besought engagement as “Sol Haytosser,” the comic and chief male character part in “Paresis by Gaslight.”
“老弟，”戈尔茨坦说，“如果你能搞得定，这个角色就归你。 “My boy,” said Goldstein, “take the part if you can get it.
卡林顿小姐不会听从我的任何建议。 Miss Carrington won’t listen to any of my suggestions.
她已经回绝了本市五六个演乡巴佬演得最好的演员。 She has turned down half a dozen of the best imitators of the rural dub in the city.
她声称，只有网罗到最好的‘海托塞’，她才肯登台。 She declares she won’t set a foot on the stage unless ‘Haytosser’ is the best that can be raked up.
你知道她是在乡村里长大的。百老汇的兰花在头上插根稻草，就想把自己说成是苜蓿，这可诓不了她。 She was raised in a village, you know, and when a Broadway orchid sticks a straw in his hair and tries to call himself a clover blossom she’s on, all right.
我曾有点儿半讽刺地问过她，依她看来，登曼·汤普森能否胜任这个角色。 I asked her, in a sarcastic vein, if she thought Denman Thompson would make any kind of a show in the part.
‘噢，不，’她这么回答， ‘Oh, no,’ says she.
‘我不要他，也不要约翰·德鲁或者吉姆·科比特，这些连萝卜缨和小白菜都分不清的大演员一个也不行。 ‘I don’t want him or John Drew or Jim Corbett or any of these swell actors that don’t know a turnip from a turnstile.
我要货真价实的家伙。’ I want the real article.’
所以，孩子，如果你想扮演索尔·海托塞，首先得说服卡林顿小姐。 So, my boy, if you want to play ‘Sol Haytosser’ you will have to convince Miss Carrington.
祝你好运。” Luck be with you.”
第二天，海史密斯乘火车去了克兰伯里角。 Highsmith took the train the next day for Cranberry Corners.
他在那个死气沉沉的偏僻小镇上呆了三天。 He remained in that forsaken and inanimate village three days.
他找到了博格斯家，把他们家祖辈和曾祖辈的那点儿历史都给打听出来了。　 He found the Boggs family and corkscrewed their history unto the third and fourth generation.
他收集了有关克兰伯里角的故事和风土人情。 He amassed the facts and the local color of Cranberry Corners.
小镇的发展速度赶不上卡林顿小姐的走红速度。 The village had not grown as rapidly as had Miss Carrington.
海史密斯估计，自从镇上唯一的泰斯庇斯门徒离开之后，小镇就压根没什么变化，正如舞台上“一晃过了四年”而实际上却没发生什么一样。 The actor estimated that it had suffered as few actual changes since the departure of its solitary follower of Thespis as had a stage upon which “four years is supposed to have elapsed.”
他吸收了克兰伯里角的一切，然后回到了那个千变万化的城市。 He absorbed Cranberry Corners and returned to the city of chameleon changes.
海史密斯在那个地下餐馆里迎来了他演艺生涯最辉煌的时刻。 It was in the rathskeller that Highsmith made the hit of his histrionic career.
我们无需提及餐馆的名字。只有在这家餐馆里，你才能指望碰到出演了一场《国王的浴衣》之后的波希·卡林顿小姐。 There is no need to name the place; there is but one rathskeller where you could hope to find Miss Posie Carrington after a performance of “The King’s Bath-Robe.”
他们占据了一张桌子，有说有笑，十分热闹，格外引人注目。 There was a jolly small party at one of the tables that drew many eyes.
首先要介绍的就是大红大紫的卡林顿小姐，她娇小可爱，丰满迷人，活力四射。 Miss Carrington, petite, marvellous, bubbling, electric, fame-drunken, shall be named first.
其次是戈尔茨坦先生，他是个大块头，嗓门很大，一头卷发，看样子有烦心事，神情就像是用爪子笨笨地抓住了一只蝴蝶的憨熊那样。 Herr Goldstein follows, sonorous, curly-haired, heavy, a trifle anxious, as some bear that had caught, somehow, a butterfly in his claws.
接下来是个郁郁不得志的记者，神情沮丧，貌似常被人追打而警觉的样子。他一边沉默地、自以为了不起地吃着纽伯格菜，一边分析抛向他的每一句话，留作日后新闻的谈资。 Next, a man condemned to a newspaper, sad, courted, armed, analyzing for press agent’s dross every sentence that was poured over him, eating his à la Newburg in the silence of greatness.
最后一个是梳分头的年轻人，他的名字对小报和餐馆账单来说相当于金钱。 To conclude, a youth with parted hair, a name that is ochre to red journals and gold on the back of a supper check.
这几个人占了一张桌子。餐馆里的乐师在演奏，侍者来来往往地表演着他们令人眼花缭乱的服务技巧，但留给需要他们服务的顾客的永远是背影。所有人都有种奇异和快乐的感觉，因为他们呆在离人行道地面九英尺深的地下。 These sat at a table while the musicians played, while waiters moved in the mazy performance of their duties with their backs toward all who desired their service, and all was bizarre and merry because it was nine feet below the level of the sidewalk.
差一刻12点时，一个人走进了地下餐馆。 At 11.45 a being entered the rathskeller.
第一小提琴手把本来是C调的地方拉低了半个音；吹单簧管的也把装饰音吹走了调；卡林顿小姐咯咯一笑；梳分头的年轻人囫囵吞下一颗橄榄仁。 The first violin perceptibly flatted a C that should have been natural; the clarionet blew a bubble instead of a grace note; Miss Carrington giggled and the youth with parted hair swallowed an olive seed.
刚进来的人带着一股毋庸置疑又无可非难的乡土气息。 Exquisitely and irreproachably rural was the new entry.
他是个不安而犹豫的年轻人，瘦高身材，一头淡黄色的头发，张着嘴，笨头笨脑的。餐厅里的灯光和人群令他有些局促不安。 A lank, disconcerted, hesitating young man it was, flaxen-haired, gaping of mouth, awkward, stricken to misery by the lights and company.
他穿着胡桃色的制服，打了鲜亮的蓝领带，骨瘦如柴的手腕和白袜子包裹的脚踝露出来四英寸。 His clothing was butternut, with bright blue tie, showing four inches of bony wrist and white-socked ankle.
他带翻了张椅子，在另一张上坐下来，脚蜷勾着一个桌角。侍者走了过来，他却有些怕怕的。 He upset a chair, sat in another one, curled a foot around a table leg and cringed at the approach of a waiter.
“那麻烦您给我来杯淡啤酒吧。”面对侍者周到的询问，他回答道。 “You may fetch me a glass of lager beer,” he said, in response to the discreet questioning of the servitor.
餐馆里的目光一下都聚集到他身上。 The eyes of the rathskeller were upon him.
他像芥蓝那样质朴，又像搂草机那样朴实。 He was as fresh as a collard and as ingenuous as a hay rake.
他睁大眼睛四处打量着，就像看到了一群猪闯进了自家的土豆地里一样。 He let his eye rove about the place as one who regards, big-eyed, hogs in the potato patch.
他的目光最后落在卡林顿小姐身上。 His gaze rested at length upon Miss Carrington.
年轻人咧开嘴笑了，一脸的灿烂，高兴而窘迫地红着脸站起来，朝她坐的桌子走去。 He rose and went to her table with a lateral, shining smile and a blush of pleased trepidation.
“你好，波希小姐,” “How’re ye, Miss Posie?”
他带着浓重的乡音说道, he said in accents not to be doubted.
“你还记得我吗——比尔·萨默斯——铁匠铺后面的萨默斯家？ “Don’t ye remember me—Bill Summers—the Summerses that lived back of the blacksmith shop?
我得说自从你离开克兰伯里角后，我长大了些。 I reckon I’ve growed up some since ye left Cranberry Corners.
“莉莎·佩里说我进城的时候可能会见到你。 “’Liza Perry ’lowed I might see ye in the city while I was here.
你知道莉莎嫁给了本尼·斯坦菲尔德，她说——” You know ’Liza married Benny Stanfield, and she says—”
“啊，什么！”卡林顿小姐来了兴致，打断道，“莉莎·佩里不可能结婚的——不可能啊！ “Ah, say!” interrupted Miss Carrington, brightly, “Lize Perry is never married—what!
她的雀斑呐！” Oh, the freckles of her!”
“六月结的婚，”八卦的年轻人咧嘴一乐，“现在住在塔特姆老院子里。 “Married in June,” grinned the gossip, “and livin’ in the old Tatum Place.
哈姆·赖利信教了；老太太布利塞斯把宅子卖给了斯普纳船长；沃特斯家的小女儿跟一个音乐老师跑了；三月份的一场火把法院大楼给烧喽；你叔叔威利当上了警长；玛蒂尔达·霍斯金斯死了，是因为手被根针扎了一下；汤姆·比德尔在追萨莉·莱斯罗普——他们说汤姆每晚都坐在萨利家的门口。” Ham Riley perfessed religion; old Mrs. Blithers sold her place to Cap’n Spooner; the youngest Waters girl run away with a music teacher; the court-house burned up last March; your uncle Wiley was elected constable; Matilda Hoskins died from runnin’ a needle in her hand, and Tom Beedle is courtin’ Sallie Lathrop—they say he don’t miss a night but what he’s settin’ on their porch.”
“那个斜眼的家伙！”卡林顿小姐愤恨地叫道， “The wall-eyed thing!” exclaimed Miss Carrington, with asperity.
“哼！汤姆·比德尔以前不是——哎，诸位，失陪一会儿——这是我的一位老朋友——是——你叫什么来着？ “Why, Tom Beedle once—say, you folks, excuse me a while—this is an old friend of mine—Mr.—what was it?
对了，萨默斯先生——这位是戈尔茨坦先生，里基茨先生——嗯，你是？ Yes, Mr. Summers—Mr. Goldstein, Mr. Ricketts, Mr.—Oh, what’s yours?
就叫你‘约翰尼’好了——上这边来，再多跟我聊聊。” ‘Johnny’’ll do—come on over here and tell me some more.”
她把他拖到僻静角落里的一张桌子旁。 She swept him to an isolated table in a corner.
戈尔茨坦先生耸了耸胖实的肩膀，招呼侍者过来。 Herr Goldstein shrugged his fat shoulders and beckoned to the waiter.
报社记者的兴致好了些，要了杯苦艾酒。 The newspaper man brightened a little and mentioned absinthe.
梳分头的年轻人突然郁闷起来。 The youth with parted hair was plunged into melancholy.
餐馆里的顾客们开怀畅饮，频频碰杯，欣赏着波希·卡林顿小姐在正式演出后招待他们的喜剧秀。 The guests of the rathskeller laughed, clinked glasses and enjoyed the comedy that Posie Carrington was treating them to after her regular performance.
几个玩世不恭的人边自得地笑着，边耳语道“纯属炒作”。 A few cynical ones whispered “press agent” and smiled wisely.
波希·卡林顿双手托着她那长着酒窝的漂亮脸颊，一点儿也不在意观众——为她赢得赞誉的正是这份本领。 Posie Carrington laid her dimpled and desirable chin upon her hands, and forgot her audience—a faculty that had won her laurels for her.
“我想不起来有谁叫比尔·萨默斯，”她边说边仔细打量起年轻的乡村小伙那双纯朴的蓝眼睛， “I don’t seem to recollect any Bill Summers,” she said, thoughtfully gazing straight into the innocent blue eyes of the rustic young man.
“不过，我还是知道萨默斯家的。 “But I know the Summerses, all right.
我猜老家没什么变化吧。 I guess there ain’t many changes in the old town.
最近你见过我的家人没？” You see any of my folks lately?”
于是海史密斯使出了他的杀手锏。 And then Highsmith played his trump.
“索尔·海托塞”一角除了能够表演喜剧之外，还需要会煽情。 The part of “Sol Haytosser” called for pathos as well as comedy.
应该让卡林顿小姐看到他在这个方面也能胜任。 Miss Carrington should see that he could do that as well.
“波希小姐，”比尔·萨默斯说，“两三天前，我还去了趟你家。 “Miss Posie,” said “Bill Summers,” “I was up to your folkeses house jist two or three days ago.
嗯，没什么变化好说的。 No, there ain’t many changes to speak of.
厨房窗户下的那丛紫丁香长到一英尺多高了，前院那棵榆树死了，不得不砍掉。 The lilac bush by the kitchen window is over a foot higher, and the elm in the front yard died and had to be cut down.
和以前相比，还是有点儿不同。” And yet it don’t seem the same place that it used to be.”
“我妈妈怎么样？”卡林顿小姐问。 “How’s ma?” asked Miss Carrington.
“我上次见她时，她正坐在门口，用钩针编灯座垫子，”“比尔”说， “She was settin’ by the front door, crocheting a lamp-mat when I saw her last,” said “Bill.”
“她比以前老了，波希小姐。 “She’s older’n she was, Miss Posie.
但是屋里的一切看上去都没变。 But everything in the house looked jest the same.
你母亲叫我坐下。 Your ma asked me to set down.
‘别碰那张柳条椅，威廉,’她说, ‘Don’t touch that willow rocker, William,’ says she.
‘波希走后始终没有动过，扶手上的那条围裙，她还没有缝好边，现在静静地放在那儿，就像当初她把它扔在那儿一样。 ‘It ain’t been moved since Posie left; and that’s the apron she was hemmin’, layin’ over the arm of it, jist as she flung it.
我一直希望，’她继续说道，‘波希有一天会把它缝好。’” I’m in hopes,’ she goes on, ‘that Posie’ll finish runnin’ out that hem some day.’”
卡林顿小姐果断地招呼一个侍者过来。 Miss Carrington beckoned peremptorily to a waiter.
“一品脱略带甜味的香槟，”她简短地吩咐到，“账单给戈尔茨坦先生。” “A pint of extra dry,” she ordered, briefly; “and give the check to Goldstein.”
“阳光洒在门口，”克兰伯里角来的编年史学家接着说，“你妈妈坐在阳光里。 “The sun was shinin’ in the door,” went on the chronicler from Cranberry, “and your ma was settin’ right in it.
我问她为什么不往后面挪挪。 I asked her if she hadn’t better move back a little.
‘威廉，’她说，‘我一坐下来看见这条路，就动不了了。 ‘William,’ says she, ‘when I get sot down and lookin’ down the road, I can’t bear to move.
每天如此，’她说，‘有空就在这儿坐着，瞅着，等着波希。 Never a day,’ says she, ‘but what I set here every minute that I can spare and watch over them palin’s for Posie.
她是晚上从这条路上走的，因为第二天我们在路上看到了她的小脚印。我有种感觉，当她厌倦了外面的世界，想起她老妈的时候，还会从那条路上回来的。’ She went away down that road in the night, for we seen her little shoe tracks in the dust, and somethin’ tells me she’ll come back that way ag’in when she’s weary of the world and begins to think about her old mother.’
“我离开的时候，”“比尔”最后说道，“在门前台阶那儿的灌木丛里把这个摘了下来。 “When I was comin’ away,” concluded “Bill,” “I pulled this off’n the bush by the front steps.
我估计到了城里兴许能见到你，我知道你会喜欢老家的东西。” I thought maybe I might see you in the city, and I knowed you’d like somethin’ from the old home.”
他从上衣口袋里掏出一朵黄玫瑰——芬芳柔美，有着天鹅绒般的光泽。它在地下餐馆污浊的空气中低垂着花蕾，宛如一个少女在古罗马竞技场上狮群热乎乎的呼吸面前，含胸垂首一样。 He took from his coat pocket a rose—a drooping, yellow, velvet, odorous rose, that hung its head in the foul atmosphere of that tainted rathskeller like a virgin bowing before the hot breath of the lions in a Roman arena.
卡林顿小姐悦耳的笑声穿透了乐队演奏的《风信子》的旋律。 Miss Carrington’s penetrating but musical laugh rose above the orchestra’s rendering of “Bluebells.”
“哎呀！”她高兴地喊了起来，“还有比那儿更无聊的地方吗？ “Oh, say!” she cried, with glee, “ain’t those poky places the limit?
我就知道让我在克兰伯里角呆上两个钟头，就够受的了。 I just know that two hours at Cranberry Corners would give me the horrors now.
嗯，非常高兴能见到你，萨默斯先生。 Well, I’m awful glad to have seen you, Mr. Summers.
我想，我现在要赶回旅馆睡个美容觉了。” Guess I’ll bustle around to the hotel now and get my beauty sleep.”
她把那朵黄玫瑰戳在她华美绸衣的前襟里，站起身，高傲地朝戈尔茨坦先生点点头。 She thrust the yellow rose into the bosom of her wonderful, dainty, silken garments, stood up and nodded imperiously at Herr Goldstein.
刚才陪同她的三个人和“比尔·萨默斯”一起送她上了马车。 Her three companions and “Bill Summers” attended her to her cab.
等到她的裙边和饰带都妥当地塞进车厢后，她才向他们道别，那明眸皓齿令他们心神荡漾。 When her flounces and streamers were all safely tucked inside she dazzled them with au revoirs from her shining eyes and teeth.
当那辆闪亮的马车疾驰而去的时候，她喊道：“你出城之前要到旅馆来看看我啊，比尔。” “Come around to the hotel and see me, Bill, before you leave the city,” she called as the glittering cab rolled away.
海史密斯装扮依旧，同戈尔茨坦走进一家小咖啡馆。 Highsmith, still in his make-up, went with Herr Goldstein to a café booth.
“不错的主意，是吧？”演员笑呵呵地问道, “Bright idea, eh?” asked the smiling actor.
“我应该能拿到‘索尔·海托塞’的角色了吧，你觉得呢？ “Ought to land ‘Sol Haytosser’ for me, don’t you think?
这位小姐始终没识破我。” The little lady never once tumbled.”
“我没有听到你们的谈话，”戈尔茨坦说，“不过你的化妆和表演还不赖。 “I didn’t hear your conversation,” said Goldstein, “but your make-up and acting was O. K.
来，敬你一杯，祝你成功。 Here’s to your success.
你最好明天一早就去找卡林顿小姐，把这个角色搞定。 You’d better call on Miss Carrington early to-morrow and strike her for the part.
