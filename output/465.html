林中的睡美人 The Sleeping Beauty in the Wood
作者：安德鲁·朗格 by Andrew Lang
从前有一位国王和一位王后，他们一直为没有孩子而伤心不已，忧愁得无以形容。 There were formerly a king and a queen, who were so sorry that they had no children; so sorry that it cannot be expressed.
他们走遍了世界上的所有地方，又起誓又朝圣，尝试了各种方法，但一直没有结果。 They went to all the waters in the world; vows, pilgrimages, all ways were tried, and all to no purpose.
然而，王后最终还是有了一个女儿。 At last, however, the Queen had a daughter.
王室为小公主举办了一场盛大的洗礼仪式；尽其所能地从全国请来了所有的仙女作为她的教母（一共有七位），按照当时的习俗，每一位仙女都要赠予公主一件礼物。 There was a very fine christening; and the Princess had for her god-mothers all the fairies they could find in the whole kingdom (they found seven), that every one of them might give her a gift, as was the custom of fairies in those days.
这样，公主将会拥有人们所能想到的各种优点。 By this means the Princess had all the perfections imaginable.
仪式结束后，所有参加的人都回到了国王的宫殿，在那里，国王为仙女们准备了盛大的晚宴。 After the ceremonies of the christening were over, all the company returned to the King’s palace, where was prepared a great feast for the fairies.
每一位仙女的面前都摆着一个精美华丽的托盘，上面放着一个金盒子，盒子里装着镶有钻石和红宝石的纯金勺子、刀叉等餐具。 There was placed before every one of them a magnificent cover with a case of massive gold, wherein were a spoon, knife, and fork, all of pure gold set with diamonds and rubies.
然而，就在他们全部就座于桌旁时，他们看见一位年老的仙女走进大厅。这位仙女并没有受到邀请，原因是她离开自己的城堡已经有50多年了，人们认为她不是死了，就是被施了魔法。 But as they were all sitting down at table they saw come into the hall a very old fairy, whom they had not invited, because it was above fifty years since she had been out of a certain tower, and she was believed to be either dead or enchanted.
国王下令给她拿来了一个托盘，但是却没有配备跟其他仙女一样的纯金餐具，因为他们仅仅为七位仙女准备了七份。 The King ordered her a cover, but could not furnish her with a case of gold as the others, because they had only seven made for the seven fairies.
老仙女觉得自己被怠慢了，于是嘴里含含糊糊地念叨着一些不好的话。 The old Fairy fancied she was slighted, and muttered some threats between her teeth.
坐在她旁边的一位年轻仙女偶然听到了她的抱怨，断定她必然会给小公主一些不幸的礼物。当大家起身离席时，她赶紧藏在了帘子后面，这样她可以最后一个送礼物，尽最大可能地消除老仙女可能造成的不幸。 One of the young fairies who sat by her overheard how she grumbled; and, judging that she might give the little Princess some unlucky gift, went, as soon as they rose from table, and hid herself behind the hangings, that she might speak last, and repair, as much as she could, the evil which the old Fairy might intend.
与此同时，所有的仙女开始给公主送礼物。 In the meanwhile all the fairies began to give their gifts to the Princess.
最年轻的仙女祝愿公主成长为世界上最漂亮的人；下一位祝愿她有天使般的睿智；第三个祝愿她所做的一切事情都能尽善尽美；第四个祝愿她能跳出最完美的舞蹈；第五位祝愿她拥有夜莺般的歌喉；第六位祝愿她能够极致完美地演绎出各式的音乐。 The youngest gave her for gift that she should be the most beautiful person in the world; the next, that she should have the wit of an angel; the third, that she should have a wonderful grace in everything she did; the fourth, that she should dance perfectly well; the fifth, that she should sing like a nightingale; and the sixth, that she should play all kinds of music to the utmost perfection.
该轮到老仙女送礼物了，她摇晃着头，更多的是出于恶意而非年迈，说公主会被一枚纱锭刺破手指而丧命。 The old Fairy’s turn coming next, with a head shaking more with spite than age, she said that the Princess should have her hand pierced with a spindle and die of the wound.
这份可怕的礼物使满座宾客惶恐战栗，大家为此难过地哭泣起来。 This terrible gift made the whole company tremble, and everybody fell a-crying.
在这紧要关头，那位年轻的仙女从帘子后面走出来，然后大声地说道： At this very instant the young Fairy came out from behind the hangings, and spake these words aloud:
“国王、王后，我向你们保证，公主不会死于这场灾难。 “Assure yourselves, O King and Queen, that your daughter shall not die of this disaster.
没错，我的魔力不能彻底改变那位老仙女的恶意诅咒。 It is true, I have no power to undo entirely what my elder has done.
公主的确会被纺锤刺伤，但是她不会死，而是会沉睡一百年，直到一位王子前来把她唤醒。” The Princess shall indeed pierce her hand with a spindle; but, instead of dying, she shall only fall into a profound sleep, which shall last a hundred years, at the expiration of which a king’s son shall come and awake her.”
为了避免发生不幸，国王立即宣布任何人都不能使用纺纱杆和纺锤，或者在家中藏有纺锤之类的东西，否则将被处以死刑。 The King, to avoid the misfortune foretold by the old Fairy, caused immediately proclamation to be made, whereby everybody was forbidden, on pain of death, to spin with a distaff and spindle, or to have so much as any spindle in their houses.
大概过了十五六年，国王和王后外出去度假。有一天，年轻的公主碰巧在宫殿里跑上跑下玩耍，她从一间屋子跑到上面另一间屋子，直到进入城堡顶处的一间小房间里，那里有一位慈祥的老妇人，正在用纺锤纺纱。 About fifteen or sixteen years after, the King and Queen being gone to one of their houses of pleasure, the young Princess happened one day to divert herself in running up and down the palace; when going up from one apartment to another, she came into a little room on the top of the tower, where a good old woman, alone, was spinning with her spindle.
这位慈祥的老人不知道国王下令禁止使用纺锤。 This good woman had never heard of the King’s proclamation against spindles.
公主问道：“老婆婆，您在这儿做什么？” “What are you doing there, goody?” said the Princess.
老人并不知道她是公主，回答说：“我可爱的姑娘，我正在纺纱啊。” “I am spinning, my pretty child,” said the old woman, who did not know who she was.
“哈！”公主高兴地说，“真好玩，您是怎样纺的？ “Ha!” said the Princess, “this is very pretty; how do you do it?
让我试试吧，我想看看自己能不能做。” Give it to me, that I may see if I can do so.”
她马上把它拿到手中，不知是拿得太快了，还是拿得不顺手，又或者是受到老仙女的诅咒，纺锤刺破了她的手，她昏倒在地，失去了知觉。 She had no sooner taken it into her hand than, whether being very hasty at it, somewhat unhandy, or that the decree of the Fairy had so ordained it, it ran into her hand, and she fell down in a swoon.
慈祥的老婆婆一时不知所措，大声求救。 The good old woman, not knowing very well what to do in this affair, cried out for help.
人们从各处赶来，他们把冷水洒到公主的脸上，解开她的衣服，拍打她的手掌……可这一切都不能使公主苏醒过来。 People came in from every quarter in great numbers; they threw water upon the Princess’s face, unlaced her, struck her on the palms of her hands, and rubbed her temples with Hungary-water; but nothing would bring her to herself.
这时，国王听到人们的嘈杂声，便赶了过来，他想起了仙女们的预言，断定这一切会印证她们的话，迟早会发生的。他命人把公主抬进王宫里一间最好的屋子，把她放在一张金银线绣成的床上。 And now the King, who came up at the noise, bethought himself of the prediction of the fairies, and, judging very well that this must necessarily come to pass, since the fairies had said it, caused the Princess to be carried into the finest apartment in his palace, and to be laid upon a bed all embroidered with gold and silver.
一定会有人把她当成一个小天使，她太美了；即使沉睡也丝毫没有影响她的美；她双颊红润，唇色粉红，尽管紧闭双眼，但你依然能听到她轻柔的呼吸声。她并没有死，这一切都使人很满意。 One would have taken her for a little angel, she was so very beautiful; for her swooning away had not diminished one bit of her complexion; her cheeks were carnation, and her lips were coral; indeed, her eyes were shut, but she was heard to breathe softly, which satisfied those about her that she was not dead.
国王命令任何人都不能打扰她，就让她安静地睡着，直到她醒来的那一刻。 The King commanded that they should not disturb her, but let her sleep quietly till her hour of awaking was come.
不幸发生的时候，那个让公主沉睡百年而使她得救的善良仙女，正在离王国一万两千里格远的马塔金国，但是她立刻从一个小矮人那里得到了这个消息，这个小矮人有一双七里格长的靴子，穿上这双靴子，每跨一步他可以步行七里格的距离。 The good Fairy who had saved her life by condemning her to sleep a hundred years was in the kingdom of Matakin, twelve thousand leagues off, when this accident befell the Princess; but she was instantly informed of it by a little dwarf, who had boots of seven leagues, that is, boots with which he could tread over seven leagues of ground in one stride.
这位仙女闻讯后立即出发，她乘着火龙战车，约一个小时后赶到了王宫。 The Fairy came away immediately, and she arrived, about an hour after, in a fiery chariot drawn by dragons.
国王扶她下了战车，她对他所做的一切表示赞许，但是由于她极富远见，考虑到当公主苏醒后会孤零零地呆在这个古老的王宫里，或许会不知所措，所以她用魔法棒点了点王宫里所有的人（除了国王和王后）——保姆、宫女、寝宫侍女、男仆、官员、管家、御厨、烹调师、厨房帮工、守卫、步兵；她同样点了点马厩里所有的马匹，各种猫猫狗狗，宫廷外的大狗以及可爱的小毛普思——公主的小宠物，它趴在床上，就在公主的旁边。 The King handed her out of the chariot, and she approved everything he had done, but as she had very great foresight, she thought when the Princess should awake she might not know what to do with herself, being all alone in this old palace; and this was what she did: she touched with her wand everything in the palace (except the King and Queen)—governesses, maids of honor, ladies of the bedchamber, gentlemen, officers, stewards, cooks, undercooks, scullions, guards, with their beefeaters, pages, footmen; she likewise touched all the horses which were in the stables, pads as well as others, the great dogs in the outward court and pretty little Mopsey too, the Princess’s little spaniel, which lay by her on the bed.
所有被点到的一切瞬间都酣睡过去，直到他们的女主人苏醒后才会醒过来，在她需要他们的时候，随时准备服侍。 Immediately upon her touching them they all fell asleep, that they might not awake before their mistress and that they might be ready to wait upon her when she wanted them.
此时，烤在炉火上的鹧鸪串和山鸡串也酣睡了，连火也睡着了。 The very spits at the fire, as full as they could hold of partridges and pheasants, did fall asleep also.
一瞬间所有的人和物都睡去了。 All this was done in a moment.
仙女们做事总是很快。 Fairies are not long in doing their business.
国王和王后亲吻了他们的爱女，但没有弄醒她，他们走出宫殿，宣布任何人都不能走近宫殿。 And now the King and the Queen, having kissed their dear child without waking her, went out of the palace and put forth a proclamation that nobody should dare to come near it.
然而，没有这项禁令也没有关系，因为不到一刻钟的时间里，皇家园林的四周就长出了大量大大小小的树木，还有灌木丛和荆棘，相互缠绕在一起，任何人或者野兽都不能进入。只有城堡的尖塔露出在树林之上，从远处可以望见。 This, however, was not necessary, for in a quarter of an hour’s time there grew up all round about the park such a vast number of trees, great and small, bushes and brambles, twining one within another, that neither man nor beast could pass through; so that nothing could be seen but the very top of the towers of the palace; and that, too, not unless it was a good way off.
毫无疑问，仙女创造了一件杰出的艺术品，这样公主就能一直睡着，无需担心受到任何好奇者的打扰。 Nobody; doubted but the Fairy gave herein a very extraordinary sample of her art, that the Princess, while she continued sleeping, might have nothing to fear from any curious people.
一百年过后，来自另一家族的一位王子外出打猎，路过公主的王宫，他问： When a hundred years were gone and passed the son of the King then reigning, and who was of another family from that of the sleeping Princess, being gone a-hunting on that side of the country, asked:
在大森林的中央，他看到的那些塔状物是什么？ What those towers were which he saw in the middle of a great thick wood?
每个人根据自己所听说的作出了不同的回答。 Everyone answered according as they had heard.
有人说， Some said:
那是一座毁灭的、经常闹鬼的老城堡。 That it was a ruinous old castle, haunted by spirits.
还有人说，全国所有的巫师在那里过安息日，或者半夜在那里集会。 Others, That all the sorcerers and witches of the country kept there their sabbath or night’s meeting.
普遍的说法是，那里住着一个吃人的妖怪，他会把所有能抓到的小孩都抓走，然后没事的时候把他们吃掉，没有人能够追上他，因为只有他具有穿过树林的魔力。 The common opinion was: That an ogre lived there, and that he carried thither all the little children he could catch, that he might eat them up at his leisure, without anybody being able to follow him, as having himself only the power to pass through the wood.
王子陷入了迷茫，不知道该相信谁的说法，这时有一位善良的村民告诉他说： The Prince was at a stand, not knowing what to believe, when a very good countryman spake to him thus:
“尊敬的王子殿下，请允许我说两句，五十年前我听我父亲说过，我的父亲也是听我的爷爷说的，传说那座城堡里有一位史上最美丽的公主，但她必须睡上一百年，直到有一位她等待的王子来唤醒她。” “May it please your royal highness, it is now about fifty years since I heard from my father, who heard my grandfather say, that there was then in this castle a princess, the most beautiful was ever seen; that she must sleep there a hundred years, and should be waked by a king’s son, for whom she was reserved.”
年轻的王子听完之后，顿时热情洋溢。没有权衡轻重，他便想着要完成这次难得的冒险。为了爱情和荣誉，他决定去城堡里一探究竟。 The young Prince was all on fire at these words, believing, without weighing the matter, that he could put an end to this rare adventure; and, pushed on by love and honor, resolved that moment to look into it.
他刚走进树林，所有高大的树木、灌木丛，还有荆棘都为他让路。他看见城堡就矗立在他所行进的大道尽头，便一鼓作气朝它走去。出乎他意料的是，没有一个随从跟着他进入树林，因为这些树木在他通过之后又自动合拢了。 Scarce had he advanced toward the wood when all the great trees, the bushes, and brambles gave way of themselves to let him pass through; he walked up to the castle which he saw at the end of a large avenue which he went into; and what a little surprised him was that he saw none of his people could follow him, because the trees closed again as soon as he had passed through them.
但是他仍然继续走着，追求爱情的年轻王子总是勇往直前的。 However, he did not cease from continuing his way; a young and amorous prince is always valiant.
他来到宫殿外一片非常宽阔的院子，即使是最无畏的人也会被这里的情景吓呆。 He came into a spacious outward court, where everything he saw might have frozen the most fearless person with horror.
一片死一般的沉寂，使人毛骨悚然，除了伸展开身体的人和动物外，什么都看不到，一切都仿佛都死了一般。 There reigned all over a most frightful silence; the image of death everywhere showed itself, and there was nothing to be seen but stretched-out bodies of men and animals, all seeming to be dead.
但他从士兵们有血色的脸和呼吸的鼻子知道，他们只是睡着了，他们的酒杯里还残有几滴酒，这表明他们是喝醉睡过去了。 He, however, very well knew, by the ruby faces and pimpled noses of the beefeaters, that they were only asleep; and their goblets, wherein still remained some drops of wine, showed plainly that they fell asleep in their cups.
随后他穿过铺有大理石的宫殿，走上楼梯进入守卫室，看见守卫们根据军衔一字站开，肩上扛着火枪，大声地打着酣。 He then crossed a court paved with marble, went up the stairs and came into the guard chamber, where guards were standing in their ranks, with their muskets upon their shoulders, and snoring as loud as they could.
之后，他又穿过几间屋子，里面是一些睡着的绅士和贵妇人，一些站着，一些坐着。 After that he went through several rooms full of gentlemen and ladies, all asleep, some standing, others sitting.
最后，他走近一间镀了金的房间，房间里有一张床，窗帘大开着，他看见了这辈子最美好的景象——一位美丽的公主躺在床上，大约十五六岁，楚楚动人、光彩夺目。 At last he came into a chamber all gilded with gold, where he saw upon a bed, the curtains of which were all open, the finest sight was ever beheld—a princess, who appeared to be about fifteen or sixteen years of age, and whose bright and, in a manner, resplendent beauty, had somewhat in it divine.
他激动地颤抖着，心生爱慕地走了过去，在她身边跪了下去。 He approached with trembling and admiration, and fell down before her upon his knees.
这时，诅咒解除了，公主苏醒了过来，她温柔地看着他，像是第一眼就爱上了他。 And now, as the enchantment was at an end, the Princess awaked, and looking on him with eyes more tender than the first view might seem to admit of:
“是你吗？我的王子？”她问道， “Is it you, my Prince?” said she to him.
“你等我很久了吧？” “You have waited a long while.”
王子完全被这些话迷住了，更沉迷于他们的交流方式，不知道怎么表达自己的喜悦和感激之情；他保证他爱她胜过爱自己；他们之间的交谈并没有那么连贯，并且更多的时候竟然哭了起来——只言片语，却包含了强烈的爱。 The Prince, charmed with these words, and much more with the manner in which they were spoken, knew not how to show his joy and gratitude; he assured her that he loved her better than he did himself; their discourse was not well connected, they did weep more than talk—little eloquence, a great deal of love.
相比于公主，王子更加不知所措，这也不足为奇；公主有足够的时间去考虑对他说什么，因为善良的仙女很可能给了她一段长久的美梦，尽管我们不知道内容是什么。 He was more at a loss than she, and we need not wonder at it; she had time to think on what to say to him; for it is very probable (though history mentions nothing of it) that the good Fairy, during so long a sleep, had given her very agreeable dreams.
总之，他们交谈了有四个小时，却连他们想说的一半都没有讲完。 In short, they talked four hours together, and yet they said not half what they had to say.
与此同时，整个宫殿里的人都苏醒了；每个人都想着自己的事，因为他们并没有坠入爱河，快要饿得不行了。 In the meanwhile all the palace awaked; everyone thought upon their particular business, and as all of them were not in love they were ready to die for hunger.
主管宫女跟其他人一样马上开始工作起来，变得非常没有耐心，大声地告诉公主晚餐已经准备好了。 The chief lady of honor, being as sharp set as other folks, grew very impatient, and told the Princess aloud that supper was served up.
王子伏公主起来；她完全装扮好了，非常楚楚动人，但是王子殿下小心地不告诉她，她的穿着很像他的祖母，高领上系着波点花纹的带子；但她仍然是那么迷人和美丽。 The Prince helped the Princess to rise; she was entirely dressed, and very magnificently, but his royal highness took care not to tell her that she was dressed like his great-grandmother, and had a point band peeping over a high collar; she looked not a bit less charming and beautiful for all that.
他们在镜子大厅里用餐，由公主的官员们服侍着，大厅里回荡着小提琴和双簧管弹奏的动听的古老旋律，尽管乐师已经有一百年没有弹奏过了。晚饭过后，王宫牧师立刻为他们在教堂里举行了婚礼，之后主管宫女拉上了帘子，好让他们休息一下。 They went into the great hall of looking-glasses, where they supped, and were served by the Princess’s officers, the violins and hautboys played old tunes, but very excellent, though it was now above a hundred years since they had played; and after supper, without losing any time, the lord almoner married them in the chapel of the castle, and the chief lady of honor drew the curtains.
他们基本上没有睡——公主已经不需要再睡了。第二天清早，王子就离开公主回到城里，因为他的父亲一定迫切想要见到他。 They had but very little sleep—the Princess had no occasion; and the Prince left her next morning to return to the city, where his father must needs have been in pain for him.
他告诉父亲： The Prince told him:
他在打猎的过程中迷失在森林里，只好睡在一个砍柴人的小木屋里，砍柴人还给了他奶酪和黑面包吃。 That he lost his way in the forest as he was hunting, and that he had lain in the cottage of a charcoal-burner, who gave him cheese and brown bread.
