四不像 Four Beasts In One—The Homo-Cameleopard
作者：爱伦·坡 by Edgar Allan Poe
每个人都有优点。 Chacun a ses vertus.
——克雷比永《薛西斯》 —Crebillon’s Xerxes.
安条克·伊皮法尼斯被普遍认为是先知以西结所预言的歌革。 Antiochus Epihanes is very generally looked upon as the Gog of the prophet Ezekiel.
然而，这一荣耀更适合归功于居鲁士大帝之子冈比西斯。 This honor is, however, more properly attributable to Cambyses, the son of Cyrus.
事实上，这位叙利亚王的威望根本无需任何外在的点缀。 And, indeed, the character of the Syrian monarch does by no means stand in need of any adventitious embellishment.
他在基督降世前171年继承王位，确切地说，是篡夺王权；他欲洗劫以弗所的狄安娜神庙的意图、他同犹太人势不两立的敌意、他对圣殿至圣所的亵渎以及他在动荡不安的11年统治后于塔巴的凄惨离世，这些都是令其名声大噪之事，他也因此备受同时代的历史学家们的关注，相比之下，构成他私生活和名声之和的那些邪恶的、卑鄙的、残酷的、愚蠢的以及荒唐的成就便被置之一旁了。 His accession to the throne, or rather his usurpation of the sovereignty, a hundred and seventy-one years before the coming of Christ; his attempt to plunder the temple of Diana at Ephesus; his implacable hostility to the Jews; his pollution of the Holy of Holies; and his miserable death at Taba, after a tumultuous reign of eleven years, are circumstances of a prominent kind, and therefore more generally noticed by the historians of his time than the impious, dastardly, cruel, silly, and whimsical achievements which make up the sum total of his private life and reputation.
亲爱的读者，让我们假设现在是3830年前的世界，并暂且一起想象，此时此刻我们正置身于那最奇特的人类居住地——大名鼎鼎的安条克城。 Let us suppose, gentle reader, that it is now the year of the world three thousand eight hundred and thirty, and let us, for a few minutes, imagine ourselves at that most grotesque habitation of man, the remarkable city of Antioch.
的确，除了我特别提及的这座城，在叙利亚和其他国家还有十六个城市也叫这个名字。 To be sure there were, in Syria and other countries, sixteen cities of that appellation, besides the one to which I more particularly allude.
但我们的这座城被唤作安条克·伊皮达夫尼，因它邻近达夫妮小村，那里耸立着一座崇奉那位达夫妮女神的庙宇。 But ours is that which went by the name of Antiochia Epidaphne, from its vicinity to the little village of Daphne, where stood a temple to that divinity.
安条克城是由亚历山大大帝的第一任继业者塞琉古·尼卡诺尔为纪念其父安条克所建（尽管此说尚有争议），并立刻成为了叙利亚塞琉古帝国的都城。 It was built (although about this matter there is some dispute) by Seleucus Nicanor, the first king of the country after Alexander the Great, in memory of his father Antiochus, and became immediately the residence of the Syrian monarchy.
在罗马帝国强盛时期，这里是东部行省行政长官的常驻地。此外，许多罗马皇帝（其中特别值得一提的是维鲁斯和瓦伦斯）在这里度过了他们的大部分人生。 In the flourishing times of the Roman Empire, it was the ordinary station of the prefect of the eastern provinces; and many of the emperors of the queen city (among whom may be mentioned, especially, Verus and Valens) spent here the greater part of their time.
但现在我感觉我们已经到了这座城市。 But I perceive we have arrived at the city itself.
就让我们登上城垛，将目光投向城镇和周边的乡村。 Let us ascend this battlement, and throw our eyes upon the town and neighboring country.
“那条河流江宽水急，携着无数瀑布，奔流过荒山野岭最后又淌过一大片荒芜的建筑群，那是什么河？” “What broad and rapid river is that which forces its way, with innumerable falls, through the mountainous wilderness, and finally through the wilderness of buildings?”
那是奥龙特斯河，除了在南边十二英里外绵延的、宛如一大面明镜的地中海，它是视野内唯一能见到的水域。 That is the Orontes, and it is the only water in sight, with the exception of the Mediterranean, which stretches, like a broad mirror, about twelve miles off to the southward.
人人都已见过地中海，但我告诉你，很少有人偷瞄过安条克城。 Every one has seen the Mediterranean; but let me tell you, there are few who have had a peep at Antioch.
我指的很少人是像你我一样，既受过现代教育又在这城市参观的人。 By few, I mean, few who, like you and me, have had, at the same time, the advantages of a modern education.
所以，别再盯着地中海了，全神贯注于我们脚下这片连甍接栋的房屋吧。 Therefore cease to regard that sea, and give your whole attention to the mass of houses that lie beneath us.
你会铭记现在是三千八百三十年前的世界。 You will remember that it is now the year of the world three thousand eight hundred and thirty.
若年代再近一点儿——比如，现在是公元1845年，我们应该见不到这样非凡的奇观。 Were it later—for example, were it the year of our Lord eighteen hundred and forty-five, we should be deprived of this extraordinary spectacle.
在19世纪，安条克城已是——说得更准确点儿，安条克将是——一片令人惋惜的衰败景象。 In the nineteenth century Antioch is—that is to say, Antioch will be—in a lamentable state of decay.
到那时候，这座城市已经历过三个不同的时代，并被接连而至的三场地震彻底摧毁。 It will have been, by that time, totally destroyed, at three different periods, by three successive earthquakes.
说实话，那时它应该大不如从前，留下的将是满目疮痍，一片废墟，以至于国王也将都城迁到了大马士革。 Indeed, to say the truth, what little of its former self may then remain, will be found in so desolate and ruinous a state that the patriarch shall have removed his residence to Damascus.
这很好。 This is well.
我知道你得益于我的建议，正用你大部分的时间观察我刚提到的这些房屋—— I see you profit by my advice, and are making the most of your time in inspecting the premises—in
瞧瞧这里 -satisfying your eyes
令这座城市大名鼎鼎的名胜古迹 With the memorials and the things of fame
一饱眼福吧—— That most renown this city.—
请原谅，我刚刚忘记了1750年后莎士比亚才负有盛名。 I beg pardon; I had forgotten that Shakespeare will not flourish for seventeen hundred and fifty years to come.
不过伊比达夫尼的城市风光不正可以说明我称之奇观是正确的吗？ But does not the appearance of Epidaphne justify me in calling it grotesque?
“这儿壁垒森严，而这一点既要归功于人工也要感激自然。” “It is well fortified; and in this respect is as much indebted to nature as to art.”
非常正确。 Very true.
“这里有无数富丽堂皇的宫殿。” “There are a prodigious number of stately palaces.”
是的。 There are.
“还有那些数不清的神庙，华丽且宏伟，一点儿也不亚于最广受称赞的古迹。” “And the numerous temples, sumptuous and magnificent, may bear comparison with the most lauded of antiquity.”
这一切我必须承认。 All this I must acknowledge.
但那里还有无穷无尽的泥棚屋和令人生厌的茅舍。 Still there is an infinity of mud huts, and abominable hovels.
我们不禁会联想到每一间陋室里的污秽肮脏，而且，若不是那敬神香火的浓烈烟气，我深信我们会闻到一股最无法忍受的恶臭。 We cannot help perceiving abundance of filth in every kennel, and, were it not for the over-powering fumes of idolatrous incense, I have no doubt we should find a most intolerable stench.
你曾见过狭窄得令人如此难以忍受的街道，或是高耸得令人出乎意料的房屋吗？ Did you ever behold streets so insufferably narrow, or houses so miraculously tall?
它们掷在地上的影子是多么阴暗！ What gloom their shadows cast upon the ground!
幸好那些望不到头的柱廊里的吊灯整天都亮着，否则埃及荒凉时代的黑暗之灾就该临到我们了。 It is well the swinging lamps in those endless colonnades are kept burning throughout the day; we should otherwise have the darkness of Egypt in the time of her desolation.
“这的确是个怪地方！ “It is certainly a strange place!
那边那个奇特的建筑是做什么用的？ What is the meaning of yonder singular building?
看！它比其他建筑都高，就在我认为是皇宫的建筑的东边。” See! it towers above all others, and lies to the eastward of what I take to be the royal palace.”
那是新的太阳神庙，太阳神在叙利亚广受崇拜并被称为埃拉·加巴卢斯。 That is the new Temple of the Sun, who is adored in Syria under the title of Elah Gabalah.
今后将有一位非常臭名昭著的罗马皇帝在罗马开创这种崇拜，并由此派生出一个姓氏——赫利奥加巴卢斯。 Hereafter a very notorious Roman Emperor will institute this worship in Rome, and thence derive a cognomen, Heliogabalus.
我敢说你想瞧瞧那神庙里的神。 I dare say you would like to take a peep at the divinity of the temple.
你不必往天上看；他的光辉不在那里——至少不是叙利亚人所崇拜的光辉。 You need not look up at the heavens; his Sunship is not there—at least not the Sunship adored by the Syrians.
在那边的建筑里就能感受到那种神性。 That deity will be found in the interior of yonder building.
受膜拜的神像是一根巨大石柱，顶端为圆锥形或金字塔形，借此来象征火。 He is worshipped under the figure of a large stone pillar terminating at the summit in a cone or pyramid, whereby is denoted Fire.
“听——快看！——那些可笑的家伙，那些半裸着身体、脸上涂着油彩、对着贱民们手舞足蹈大呼小叫的家伙是谁？” “Hark—behold!—who can those ridiculous beings be, half naked, with their faces painted, shouting and gesticulating to the rabble?”
他们中少数人是耍把戏的。 Some few are mountebanks.
其他一部分人具体地说应属于哲学家界。 Others more particularly belong to the race of philosophers.
而绝大多数——尤其是那些挥舞棍棒痛打群氓的人——是正在执行国王命令的宫中重臣，这些命令真是值得赞赏的滑稽行为。 The greatest portion, however—those especially who belabor the populace with clubs—are the principal courtiers of the palace, executing as in duty bound, some laudable comicality of the king’s.
“但我们听到的是什么？ “But what have we here?
天哪！这城里尽是野兽！ Heavens! the town is swarming with wild beasts!
多可怕的奇观！——多危险的怪景！” How terrible a spectacle!—how dangerous a peculiarity!”
你也许觉得糟糕透顶，但这一点儿也不危险。 Terrible, if you please; but not in the least degree dangerous.
若你不怕费心就仔细看看，每头野兽都安静地紧跟在它主人后面。 Each animal if you will take the pains to observe, is following, very quietly, in the wake of its master.
当然，有少部分野兽是脖颈上套着绳子被主人牵着，但这些主要都是个头矮小胆子也不大的种类。 Some few, to be sure, are led with a rope about the neck, but these are chiefly the lesser or timid species.
狮子、老虎和豹子完全无拘无束。 The lion, the tiger, and the leopard are entirely without restraint.
它们毫不费力地就被驯化来从事它们目前的职业，并服侍尊敬的主人，充当他们的贴身跟班。 They have been trained without difficulty to their present profession, and attend upon their respective owners in the capacity of valets-de-chambre.
的确，也有大自然维护自己被侵犯的主权的时候；——但在伊比达夫尼城，吞噬一个士兵，或勒死一只祭祀用的公牛，就是一眨眼的工夫，根本不足挂齿。 It is true, there are occasions when Nature asserts her violated dominions;—but then the devouring of a man-at-arms, or the throttling of a consecrated bull, is a circumstance of too little moment to be more than hinted at in Epidaphne.
“但我听到的是什么非同寻常的喧哗？ “But what extraordinary tumult do I hear?
这声音对安条克城来说无疑也是巨大的噪音！ Surely this is a loud noise even for Antioch!
这说明肯定发生了重大暴动。” It argues some commotion of unusual interest.”
是的——毋庸置疑。 Yes—undoubtedly.
国王已经下令开发某个新景点——竞技场的角斗士表演——或是屠杀西徐亚囚犯——也许是大火焚烧他的新宫殿——抑或是拆毁一座雄伟的神庙——或者，甚至是烧死一些犹太人。 The king has ordered some novel spectacle—some gladiatorial exhibition at the hippodrome—or perhaps the massacre of the Scythian prisoners—or the conflagration of his new palace—or the tearing down of a handsome temple—or, indeed, a bonfire of a few Jews.
骚动升级了。 The uproar increases.
欢笑的呐喊声响彻云际。 Shouts of laughter ascend the skies.
管乐器的声音在空气中显得格格不入，数百万民众的喧闹声令人生厌。 The air becomes dissonant with wind instruments, and horrible with clamor of a million throats.
我们去找点儿乐子吧，下去看看发生了什么事！ Let us descend, for the love of fun, and see what is going on!
这边走——小心点儿！ This way—be careful!
我们目前所在的就是主街道，名叫提马克斯街。 Here we are in the principal street, which is called the street of Timarchus.
人潮往这边涌来了，我们将很难在人流中逆行。 The sea of people is coming this way, and we shall find a difficulty in stemming the tide.
他们正奔涌过赫拉克利德斯小巷，这条小巷能直通皇宫；——所以国王极有可能就在这群骚乱者中。 They are pouring through the alley of Heraclides, which leads directly from the palace;—therefore the king is most probably among the rioters.
果然；——我听见皇宫信使用带有东方特色的浮夸措词高声宣布国王的驾临。 Yes;—I hear the shouts of the herald proclaiming his approach in the pompous phraseology of the East.
当他经过亚示玛神庙时我们就可以瞥见他的风采了。 We shall have a glimpse of his person as he passes by the temple of Ashimah.
我们就安坐在神庙门口，他马上就会过来。 Let us ensconce ourselves in the vestibule of the sanctuary; he will be here anon.

这是谁？ What is it?
噢！这正是亚示玛神。 Oh! it is the god Ashimah in proper person.
可是你看，他既不是羔羊，也不是山羊，不像萨特，也不大像阿卡迪亚的潘神。 You perceive, however, that he is neither a lamb, nor a goat, nor a satyr, neither has he much resemblance to the Pan of the Arcadians.
然而所有这些外貌都被——不好意思——将被——未来的学者赋予叙利亚人的亚示玛神。 Yet all these appearances have been given—I beg pardon—will be given—by the learned of future ages, to the Ashimah of the Syrians.
带上你的眼镜，并告诉我这是什么。 Put on your spectacles, and tell me what it is.

“天哪！是一只猿！” “Bless me! it is an ape!”
不错——是一只狒狒，但这绝没有减少一丝神性。 True—a baboon; but by no means the less a deity.
他的名字是希腊字类人猿的派生词——古生物研究者都是些愚蠢的大笨蛋！ His name is a derivation of the Greek Simia—what great fools are antiquarians!

除了伊皮法尼斯谁能称王？ Who is king but Epiphanes?
好极了！——棒极了！ Bravo!—bravo!
唯我伊皮法尼斯， There is none but Epiphanes,
无人——再无人： No—there is none:
能如此拆毁这些神庙， So tear down the temples,
熄灭那轮骄阳！ And put out the sun!
多么动听且激情的歌唱！ Well and strenuously sung!
人群爆发出欢呼声，不仅称他为“东方之光”、“宇宙之福”和“最杰出的长颈鹿”，还尊他为“诗坛巨头”。 The populace are hailing him ‘Prince of Poets,’ as well as ‘Glory of the East,’ ‘Delight of the Universe,’ and ‘Most Remarkable of Cameleopards.’
他们要求他再演唱一次，你听见了吗？——他又在唱那首歌了。 They have encored his effusion, and do you hear?—he is singing it over again.
当他到达竞技场时，他将被戴上诗人名号的花冠，以期待他在即将到来的奥林匹克竞赛上的胜利。 When he arrives at the hippodrome, he will be crowned with the poetic wreath, in anticipation of his victory at the approaching Olympics.
“但是，噢，天哪！我们身后这人群是怎么回事？” “But, good Jupiter! what is the matter in the crowd behind us?”
你刚刚说，在我们身后吗？——哦！啊！——我注意到了。 Behind us, did you say?—oh! ah!—I perceive.
我的朋友，还好你说的正是时候。 My friend, it is well that you spoke in time.
让我们尽快去个安全的地方。 Let us get into a place of safety as soon as possible.
就是这里啦！——让我们藏到渡槽的拱形门下面去，我一会儿就告诉你这场骚乱的起因。 Here!—let us conceal ourselves in the arch of this aqueduct, and I will inform you presently of the origin of the commotion.
它和我一直以来所预想的一样。 It has turned out as I have been anticipating.
看起来，是这长着人头的长颈鹿的怪异外表触犯了这座城市中驯养的野兽们一般能接受的礼节观念。 The singular appearance of the cameleopard and the head of a man, has, it seems, given offence to the notions of propriety entertained, in general, by the wild animals domesticated in the city.
暴动就是其造成的结果。然而，通常在这种情况下，人为力量对镇压暴徒根本无济于事。 A mutiny has been the result; and, as is usual upon such occasions, all human efforts will be of no avail in quelling the mob.
好几个叙利亚人已经被吞掉了，可那些四条腿的爱国者们的普遍呼声似乎是要把这头长颈鹿吃个精光。 Several of the Syrians have already been devoured; but the general voice of the four-footed patriots seems to be for eating up the cameleopard.
因此，“诗坛巨头”站立起来，正用后腿逃命。 ‘The Prince of Poets,’ therefore, is upon his hinder legs, running for his life.
他的大臣们都弃他于危难之中，他的爱妾们也都纷纷追随大臣们树立的好榜样。 His courtiers have left him in the lurch, and his concubines have followed so excellent an example.
“宇宙之福”，你陷入了悲哀的窘境啊！ ‘Delight of the Universe,’ thou art in a sad predicament!
“东方之光”，你面临着被撕碎的危险！ ‘Glory of the East,’ thou art in danger of mastication!
所以，绝对不要可怜巴巴地关心你那尾巴，它肯定会在泥垢里拖脏，但这也无可奈何。 Therefore never regard so piteously thy tail; it will undoubtedly be draggled in the mud, and for this there is no help.
那么，就不要回头看它那不可避免的落魄，而是鼓起勇气，奋力摆动双腿，全力飞奔向竞技场！ Look not behind thee, then, at its unavoidable degradation; but take courage, ply thy legs with vigor, and scud for the hippodrome!
记住你是安条克·伊皮法尼斯。 Remember that thou art Antiochus Epiphanes.
杰出的安条克！——也是“诗坛巨头”、“东方之光”、“宇宙之福”和“最杰出的长颈鹿”！ Antiochus the Illustrious!—also ‘Prince of Poets,’ ‘Glory of the East,’ ‘Delight of the Universe,’ and ‘Most Remarkable of Cameleopards!’
天哪！你所展示的速度能力是多么强大！ Heavens! what a power of speed thou art displaying!
你所显露的逃跑能力又是多么惊人！ What a capacity for leg-bail thou art developing!
跑呀，巨头！——棒极了，伊皮法尼斯！ Run, Prince!—Bravo, Epiphanes!
做得好，长颈鹿！——享誉盛名的安条克！——他跑！——他跳！——他飞！ Well done, Cameleopard!—Glorious Antiochus!—He runs!—he leaps!—he flies!
他如离弦之箭一般冲到了竞技场！ Like an arrow from a catapult he approaches the hippodrome!
他飞跃！——他尖叫！——他到了！ He leaps!—he shrieks!—he is there!
可是，“东方之光”，你若是到达竞技场大门时迟了半秒，伊皮达夫尼城的每一只幼熊都将细细啃咬你的尸体。 This is well; for hadst thou, ‘Glory of the East,’ been half a second longer in reaching the gates of the Amphitheatre, there is not a bear’s cub in Epidaphne that would not have had a nibble at thy carcase.
我们该离开了——我们出发吧！——因为我们将会发现，我们那脆弱的现代化耳朵将无法忍受即将开始的庆祝国王逃脱的巨大欢呼声。 Let us be off—let us take our departure!—for we shall find our delicate modern ears unable to endure the vast uproar which is about to commence in celebration of the king’s escape!
听哪！那声音已经开始了。 Listen! it has already commenced.
看哪！——整座城市都乱七八糟。 See!—the whole town is topsy-turvy.
“这无疑是东方人口最多的城市！ “Surely this is the most populous city of the East!
多么浩瀚的茫茫人海！男女老少和社会各阶级的人都乱成一团！如此多样的教派和种族！五花八门的衣装打扮！混乱繁杂的南腔北调！尖锐刺耳的野兽嘶吼！叮当作响的乐器声！还有一群扎堆的哲学家！ What a wilderness of people! what a jumble of all ranks and ages! what a multiplicity of sects and nations! what a variety of costumes! what a Babel of languages! what a screaming of beasts! what a tinkling of instruments! what a parcel of philosophers!”
来吧，我们走吧。 Come let us be off.
“等一会儿！ “Stay a moment!
我看见竞技场内一阵大骚动；请你告诉我，究竟发生了什么事？” I see a vast hubbub in the hippodrome; what is the meaning of it, I beseech you?”
那阵骚动？——哦，没什么！ That?—oh, nothing!
就如伊皮达夫尼城那高贵自由的公民们所宣布的一样，他们对他们国王的忠诚、勇猛、智慧和神威一直深信不疑。此外，他们又刚亲眼见证他宛如超人般的敏捷身手，认为他们的职责就是授予他（除诗人桂冠之外）赛跑比赛的胜利花环——一个显然在下届奥林匹克竞赛的庆典上他会获得的花环，现在他们提前颁给了他。 The noble and free citizens of Epidaphne being, as they declare, well satisfied of the faith, valor, wisdom, and divinity of their king, and having, moreover, been eye-witnesses of his late superhuman agility, do think it no more than their duty to invest his brows (in addition to the poetic crown) with the wreath of victory in the footrace—a wreath which it is evident he must obtain at the celebration of the next Olympiad, and which, therefore, they now give him in advance.
