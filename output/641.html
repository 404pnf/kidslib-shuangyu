绿宝石王冠[^1] The Adventure of the Beryl Coronet
一天早晨，我站在寓所的弧形凸肚窗旁边俯瞰下面的街道。“福尔摩斯，”我开口说道，“有个疯子正在下面的街道上跑呢。他的家人竟然让他单独出门，真是太凄惨啦。” “Holmes, ” said I as I stood one morning in our bow-window looking down the street, “here is a madman coming along. It seems rather sad that his relatives should allow him to come out alone.”
我朋友懒洋洋地从扶手椅上站了起来，双手插在睡袍的口袋里，隔着我的肩头往下方张望。这是个晴朗清新的二月早晨，昨天的雪依然厚厚地铺在地面，在冬日的阳光之中熠熠生辉。来往的马车在贝克街的街心犁出了一道满是碎屑的褐色沟槽，主路边缘和人行道的凸起街沿则依然是银装素裹，光景一如下雪之时。经过清扫的人行道露出了原来的灰色，走在上面却还是非常容易跌跤，这样一来，街上的行人就不像平时那么多。实际上，从地铁车站[^2]那边过来的行人只有一个，也就是那位行止古怪得让我不能不予以注意的绅士。 My friend rose lazily from his armchair and stood with his hands in the pockets of his dressing-gown, looking over my shoulder. It was a bright, crisp February morning, and the snow of the day before still lay deep upon the ground, shimmering brightly in the wintry sun. Down the centre of Baker Street it had been ploughed into a brown crumbly band by the traffic, but at either side and on the heaped-up edges of the foot-paths it still lay as white as when it fell. The grey pavement had been cleaned and scraped, but was still dangerously slippery, so that there were fewer passengers than usual. Indeed, from the direction of the Metropolitan Station no one was coming save the single gentleman whose eccentric conduct had drawn my attention.
那人大概五十岁左右，又高又胖，块头大得引人注目，宽大的脸膛棱角分明，整个人显得气派非凡。他的衣着十分庄重，同时又相当奢华，其中包括一件黑色的礼服大衣、一顶光闪闪的礼帽、一双款式优雅的褐色长统松紧靴，以及一条裁剪精良的珠灰色长裤。另一方面，他的举止却与他威仪十足的打扮和长相形成了一种滑稽可笑的对比，因为他正在拼命奔跑，中间还时不时地往上蹿一蹿，腿脚完全不习惯奔波劳碌的人跑得累了，表现就是他这个样子。奔跑的过程当中，他的双手像抽风似地上上下下抖个不停，脑袋摇来晃去，面容也扭曲到了匪夷所思的程度。 He was a man of about fifty, tall, portly, and imposing, with a massive, strongly marked face and a commanding figure. He was dressed in a sombre yet rich style, in black frock-coat, shining hat, neat brown gaiters, and well-cut pearlgrey trousers. Yet his actions were in absurd contrast to the dignity of his dress and features, for he was running hard, with occasional little springs, such as a weary man gives who is little accustomed to set any tax upon his legs. As he ran he jerked his hands up and down, waggled his head, and writhed his face into the most extraordinary contortions.
“他究竟有什么事情呢？”我问道。“他还在看房子的门牌号码哩。” “What on earth can be the matter with him?” I asked. “He is looking up at the numbers of the houses.”
“依我看，他是要上咱们这儿来，”福尔摩斯摩拳擦掌地说道。 “I believe that he is coming here,” said Holmes, rubbing his hands.
“咱们这儿？” “Here?”
“没错，而且我认为，他来的目的多半是找我咨询专业上的事情。要我说，我认得出他身上的那些征候。哈！我没说错吧？”他话音未落，那人已经呼哧呼哧地冲到了我们的家门口，把门铃拉得咣啷啷响彻整座房屋。 “Yes; I rather think he is coming to consult me professionally. I think that I recognise the symptoms. Ha! Did I not tell you?” As he spoke, the man, puffing and blowing, rushed at our door and pulled at our bell until the whole house resounded with the clanging.
片刻之后，他已经走进了我们的房间，依然气喘吁吁、依然双手乱舞。可是，他眼睛里的悲哀与绝望实在是太过凝重，我们脸上的笑意立刻变成了心里的惊骇与怜悯。有那么一小会儿，他根本说不出话，只是一个劲儿地在那里摇晃身子、拉扯头发，似乎他的理性已经被逼到了崩溃的边缘。接下来，他突然身子一纵，开始用自个儿的脑袋去撞墙壁，力道大得让我俩不得不赶紧冲了过去，把他拖到了房间中央。再下来，歇洛克·福尔摩斯把他摁进了那张安乐椅，然后就坐在他的身边，拍了拍他的手，开始用自己无比擅长的那种舒缓安神的语调跟他交谈。 A few moments later he was in our room, still puffing, still gesticulating, but with so fixed a look of grief and despair in his eyes that our smiles were turned in an instant to horror and pity. For a while he could not get his words out, but swayed his body and plucked at his hair like one who has been driven to the extreme limits of his reason. Then, suddenly springing to his feet, he beat his head against the wall with such force that we both rushed upon him and tore him away to the centre of the room. Sherlock Holmes pushed him down into the easy-chair and, sitting beside him, patted his hand and chatted with him in the easy, soothing tones which he knew so well how to employ.
“您来是想跟我讲您的事情，对吧？”福尔摩斯说道，“您走得这么匆忙，一定是累坏了吧。您先歇会儿，好让自己缓过劲儿来，到时候，不管您把什么样的小小问题交给我，我都会非常乐意帮您解决。” “You have come to me to tell your story, have you not?” said he. “You are fatigued with your haste. Pray wait until you have recovered yourself, and then I shall be most happy to look into any little problem which you may submit to me.”
来人静静地坐了一分多钟，胸脯起伏不停，努力控制着自己的情绪。这之后，他掏出手帕擦了擦自己的额头，紧紧地绷住嘴唇，把脸转向了我俩这边。 The man sat for a minute or more with a heaving chest, fighting against his emotion. Then he passed his handkerchief over his brow, set his lips tight, and turned his face towards us.
“你们肯定是觉得我疯了吧？”他说道。 “No doubt you think me mad?” said he.
“我觉得您肯定是遇上了非常大的麻烦，”福尔摩斯回答道。 “I see that you have had some great trouble,” responded Holmes.
“老天作证，我确实遇上了麻烦！这麻烦无比突然，无比恐怖，足以把理性从我的脑子里驱赶出去。我可能得面对当众出丑的结局，尽管我这个人从来不曾有过品格上的污点。我还得承受家庭事务带来的折磨，这样的折磨虽然每个人都会有，可是，它跟前面那件事情加在一起，具体的形式又是如此可怕，确实是让我失魂落魄。此外，有麻烦的还不只是我自己，如果不能从这件骇人听闻的事情当中找到一条出路的话，我们这片土地上最尊贵的人物也会受到牵累的。” “God knows I have!—A trouble which is enough to unseat my reason, so sudden and so terrible is it. Public disgrace I might have faced, although I am a man whose character has never yet borne a stain. Private affliction also is the lot of every man; but the two coming together, and in so frightful a form, have been enough to shake my very soul. Besides, it is not I alone. The very noblest in the land may suffer unless some way be found out of this horrible affair.”
“您千万得冷静下来，先生，”福尔摩斯说道，“好让我知道您是谁，遇上的麻烦又是什么。” “Pray compose yourself, sir,” said Holmes, “and let me have a clear account of who you are and what it is that has befallen you.”
“我的名字，”我们的客人回答道，“你们兴许会觉得耳熟。我是亚历山大·霍尔德，在针线街的霍尔德-史蒂文森银行任职。” “My name,” answered our visitor, “is probably familiar to your ears. I am Alexander Holder, of the banking firm of Holder & Stevenson, of Threadneedle Street.”
这名字我俩确实非常熟悉，因为名字的主人是故城里第二大私人银行集团的主要合伙人。那么，能够让伦敦的一位头等公民沦落到如此凄惨的境地，究竟会是什么样的事情呢？我俩满心好奇地等着下文，不过，他又经过了一番挣扎，这才打起精神，开始讲述他的故事。 The name was indeed well known to us as belonging to the senior partner in the second largest private banking concern in the City of London. What could have happened, then, to bring one of the foremost citizens of London to this most pitiable pass? We waited, all curiosity, until with another effort he braced himself to tell his story.
“我觉得眼下的形势刻不容缓，”他说道，“所以呢，警局的那位督察刚刚建议我来寻求您的帮助，我就十万火急地赶到了这里。我觉得马车在这样的雪地里跑不快，所以就坐地铁到了贝克街，然后又徒步从车站那边冲了过来，结果就把自己搞得上气不接下气，因为我很少锻炼身体。现在我感觉已经好些了，接下来，我会尽量简单明了地把事情告诉你们。 “I feel that time is of value,” said he; “that is why I hastened here when the police inspector suggested that I should secure your cooperation. I came to Baker Street by the Underground and hurried from there on foot, for the cabs go slowly through this snow. That is why I was so out of breath, for I am a man who takes very little exercise. I feel better now, and I will put the facts before you as shortly and yet as clearly as I can.
“当然，你们应该很清楚，要想让银行生意取得成功，我们一方面得为自己的资金找到回报丰厚的投资渠道，一方面还得多拉储户，增进与储户之间的关系。我们最赚钱的投资渠道之一就是出借贷款，前提是还款的保障无可挑剔。过去几年当中，我们的贷款业务非常兴旺，许多显贵家庭都用藏画、藏书或者贵重器皿作为抵押，从我们这里借走了大笔现款。 “It is, of course, well known to you that in a successful banking business as much depends upon our being able to find remunerative investments for our funds as upon our increasing our connection and the number of our depositors. One of our most lucrative means of laying out money is in the shape of loans, where the security is unimpeachable. We have done a good deal in this direction during the last few years, and there are many noble families to whom we have advanced large sums upon the security of their pictures, libraries, or plate.
“昨天上午，我在银行的办公室里坐着，一名职员给我送来了一张卡片。卡片上的名字把我吓了一跳，因为来找我的不是别人，正是——呃，照我看，即便是在你们面前，我仍然是少说为妙。我只能这么说，来人的名字在全世界都是家喻户晓，也是全英格兰最显赫、最高贵、最尊荣的名字之一。我感到受宠若惊，在他进来的时候也急于表白这样的心情，可他一上来就直奔主题，看架势是觉得手头的事情并不让人愉快，越早了结越好。 “Yesterday morning I was seated in my office at the bank when a card was brought in to me by one of the clerks. I started when I saw the name, for it was that of none other than—well, perhaps even to you I had better say no more than that it was a name which is a household word all over the earth—one of the highest, noblest, most exalted names in England. I was overwhelmed by the honour and attempted, when he entered, to say so, but he plunged at once into business with the air of a man who wishes to hurry quickly through a disagreeable task.
“‘霍尔德先生，’他说，‘我听说你们经常做贷款生意。’ “‘Mr. Holder,’ said he, ‘I have been informed that you are in the habit of advancing money.’
“‘只要有可靠的抵押，我们银行的确会做这样的生意，’我回答他。 “‘The firm does so when the security is good,’ I answered.
“‘我需要立刻拿到五万镑现金，’他说，‘这事情对我来说极端重要。当然，这不过是个区区之数，即便十倍于此，我也可以从朋友们那里借来。不过，我非常倾向于把它变成一件单纯的生意，而且要由我本人亲自经手。处在我的位置，你应该很容易理解，随便接受人家的恩惠并不明智。’ “‘It is absolutely essential to me,’ said he, ‘that I should have fifty thousand pounds at once. I could, of course, borrow so trifling a sum ten times over from my friends, but I much prefer to make it a matter of business and to carry out that business myself. In my position you can readily understand that it is unwise to place one’s self under obligations.’
“‘我能不能问一问，这笔款子您打算用多长时间呢？’我问他。 “‘For how long, may I ask, do you want this sum?’ I asked.
“‘下周一我会有一大笔进账，到时就肯定会归还借款，再加上你们认为合适的任何利息。不过，最要紧的是，我必须立刻拿到这笔钱。’ “‘Next Monday I have a large sum due to me, and I shall then most certainly repay what you advance, with whatever interest you think it right to charge. But it is very essential to me that the money should be paid at once.’
“‘不用您劳神多说，我也非常乐意从我私人的口袋里掏钱借给您，’我说，‘只不过，这么大的一份荣耀，我的口袋未免消受不起。另一方面，要是以银行的名义来做这件事情的话，我就必须对我的合伙人负责，照章采取所有的保障措施，即便是您，也不能够例外。’ “‘I should be happy to advance it without further parley from my own private purse,’ said I, ‘were it not that the strain would be rather more than it could bear. If, on the other hand, I am to do it in the name of the firm, then in justice to my partner I must insist that even in your case, every business like precaution should be taken.’
“‘我倒是非常倾向于照章办事，’他一边说，一边把他放在椅子旁边的一只黑色的摩洛哥皮方盒子拿了起来。‘毫无疑问，你应该听说过绿宝石王冠吧？’ “‘I should much prefer to have it so,’ said he, raising up a square, black morocco case which he had laid beside his chair. ‘You have doubtless heard of the Beryl Coronet?’
“‘听说过，它是整个帝国最宝贵的公共财产之一，’我说。 “‘One of the most precious public possessions of the empire,’ said I.
“‘的确如此。’他打开了盒子，盒子的衬里是柔软的肉色丝绒，安放在丝绒之上的正是他刚刚提到的那件稀世珍宝。‘这上面有三十九颗巨大的绿宝石，’他说道，‘黄金镂雕的价值也是无可估量。按照最保守的估计，这个王冠的价值也是我所借数目的两倍。我可以把它留在你这里，作为我的借款抵押。’ “‘Precisely.’ He opened the case, and there, imbedded in soft, flesh-coloured velvet, lay the magnificent piece of jewellery which he had named. ‘There are thirty-nine enormous beryls,’ said he, ‘and the price of the gold chasing is incalculable. The lowest estimate would put the worth of the coronet at double the sum which I have asked. I am prepared to leave it with you as my security.’
“我把那个贵重的宝盒捧在手里，看看它，又看看这位声名赫奕的主顾，心里多少有点儿犯难。 “I took the precious case into my hands and looked in some perplexity from it to my illustrious client.
“‘你怀疑它的价值吗？’他问我。 “‘You doubt its value?’ he asked.
“‘绝不怀疑。我只是怀疑——’ “‘Not at all. I only doubt—’
“‘怀疑我把它留在这里合不合适。这一点你完全可以放心，如果没有绝对的把握在四天之内把它赎回去的话，我是无论如何也不会这么做的。这次的抵押仅仅是个形式而已。我交的抵押够了吗？’ “‘The propriety of my leaving it. You may set your mind at rest about that. I should not dream of doing so were it not absolutely certain that I should be able in four days to reclaim it. It is a pure matter of form. Is the security sufficient?’
“‘太够了。’ “‘Ample.’
“你得明白，霍尔德先生，这件事情充分证明了我对你的信任，信任来自我之前听说的关于你的一切情况。我对你的期望不仅仅是谨慎从事、防止关于此事的任何流言，更重要的是，你一定要万无一失地把这个王冠保管好，不用我说，你自己也应该知道，要是它有了什么闪失，无疑就意味着一桩千夫所指的巨大丑闻。任何伤损的后果都跟彻底遗失相去无几，因为世上没有能跟它们媲美的绿宝石，要想替换是不可能的事情。不过，把它留在你这里，我还是拥有充分的信心。周一上午，我亲自来取。’ “‘You understand, Mr. Holder, that I am giving you a strong proof of the confidence which I have in you, founded upon all that I have heard of you. I rely upon you not only to be discreet and to refrain from all gossip upon the matter but, above all, to preserve this coronet with every possible precaution because I need not say that a great public scandal would be caused if any harm were to befall it. Any injury to it would be almost as serious as its complete loss, for there are no beryls in the world to match these, and it would be impossible to replace them. I leave it with you, however, with every confidence, and I shall call for it in person on Monday morning.’
“我看出这位主顾急于抽身，所以就没有再说什么，只是叫来我的出纳，吩咐他向主顾支付五十张一千镑的钞票[^3]。可是，再一次独坐在办公室里的时候，我看着桌上这只贵重的宝盒，禁不住觉得责任重大、诚惶诚恐。毫无疑问，王冠既然是国之重宝，任何闪失都会造成可怕的丑闻。当时我就已经开始后悔，觉得自己压根儿就不该同意保管这件东西。然而，事情至此已经无法改变，于是我把它锁进自己的私人保险柜，重新投入了自己的工作。 “Seeing that my client was anxious to leave, I said no more; but, calling for my cashier, I ordered him to pay over fifty thousand-pound notes. When I was alone once more, however, with the precious case lying upon the table in front of me, I could not but think with some misgivings of the immense responsibility which it entailed upon me. There could be no doubt that, as it was a national possession, a horrible scandal would ensue if any misfortune should occur to it. I already regretted having ever consented to take charge of it. However, it was too late to alter the matter now, so I locked it up in my private safe and turned once more to my work.
“黄昏时候，我突然觉得，要是把如此贵重的东西留在办公室里，实在不能算是小心谨慎。银行家的保险柜遭人撬窃的事情屡见不鲜，我的保险柜就能够例外吗？万一被人撬了的话，我的处境将会可怕到何种程度！因此我决定，接下来的几天里面，不管上班下班，我都要把这个盒子带在身边，不让它离开我伸手可及的范围。打定主意之后，我叫来一辆出租马车，带着宝盒回到了我在斯特里特姆街区的家里。我拿着它上了楼，又把它锁进了更衣室的衣橱，这才觉得呼吸顺畅了些。 “When evening came I felt that it would be an imprudence to leave so precious a thing in the office behind me. Bankers’ safes had been forced before now, and why should not mine be? If so, how terrible would be the position in which I should find myself! I determined, therefore, that for the next few days I would always carry the case backward and forward with me, so that it might never be really out of my reach. With this intention, I called a cab and drove out to my house at Streatham, carrying the jewel with me. I did not breathe freely until I had taken it upstairs and locked it in the bureau of my dressing-room.
“好了，现在我打算说说我家里的情况，福尔摩斯先生，好让您对眼下的形势有一个全面的了解。我的马夫和听差都没有睡在我那座房子里，他们的情况可以略过不提。女仆当中有三个都已经跟了我好些年，可说是绝对忠诚可靠。另有一个名叫露西·帕尔的粗使丫头，虽然只来了几个月，但却拥有无可挑剔的品性证明[^4]，做事也总是让我感到称心满意。她长得非常漂亮，偶尔就会有一些仰慕她的人到我家附近来转悠。这一点让我们觉得美中不足，除此之外，无论从哪方面来说，我们都相信她是个非常不错的姑娘。 “And now a word as to my household, Mr. Holmes, for I wish you to thoroughly understand the situation. My groom and my page sleep out of the house, and may be set aside altogether. I have three maid-servants who have been with me a number of years and whose absolute reliability is quite above suspicion. Another, Lucy Parr, the second waiting-maid, has only been in my service a few months. She came with an excellent character, however, and has always given me satisfaction. She is a very pretty girl and has attracted admirers who have occasionally hung about the place. That is the only drawback which we have found to her, but we believe her to be a thoroughly good girl in every way.
“仆人的情况就是这些。至于我的家庭成员嘛，他们的数目非常有限，要不了多久就能讲完。我是个鳏夫，只有一个儿子，名字叫做阿瑟。他是我的伤心事，福尔摩斯先生，伤透了我的心。毫无疑问，这事情只能怪我自己。大家都说我把他惯坏了，多半也没说错。我亲爱的妻子去世之后，我就把全部的爱放在了他的身上，看到笑容从他脸上消失，哪怕是一瞬间我也受不了。我对他从来都是有求必应。要是我严厉一点的话，我们两个的情况可能都会好一些，可是，我这么宠他也完全是为了他好。 “So much for the servants. My family itself is so small that it will not take me long to describe it. I am a widower and have an only son, Arthur. He has been a disappointment to me, Mr. Holmes—a grievous disappointment. I have no doubt that I am myself to blame. People tell me that I have spoiled him. Very likely I have. When my dear wife died I felt that he was all I had to love. I could not bear to see the smile fade even for a moment from his face. I have never denied him a wish. Perhaps it would have been better for both of us had I been sterner, but I meant it for the best.
“我的愿望自然是让他接手我的生意，可他并没有做生意的本事。他没有规矩，由着性子胡来，说实话，我真不放心让他经手大笔的钱财。年纪还小的时候，他就加入了一个贵族俱乐部。他的风度举止讨人喜欢，所以很快就跟一些挥霍成性的阔少交上了朋友。他学会了打牌赌钱，赌得也很大，还把钱浪费在马场里，结果就一次又一次地跑来找我预支他的花销，为的是清偿债务、保住面子。他不止一次想要跟那些危险的伙伴断绝关系，但每一次又都会受他朋友乔治·伯恩韦尔爵士的蛊惑，继续跟那些人来往。 “It was naturally my intention that he should succeed me in my business, but he was not of a business turn. He was wild, wayward, and, to speak the truth, I could not trust him in the handling of large sums of money. When he was young he became a member of an aristocratic club, and there, having charming manners, he was soon the intimate of a number of men with long purses and expensive habits. He learned to play heavily at cards and to squander money on the turf, until he had again and again to come to me and implore me to give him an advance upon his allowance, that he might settle his debts of honour. He tried more than once to break away from the dangerous company which he was keeping, but each time the influence of his friend, Sir George Burnwell, was enough to draw him back again.
“说真的，他会受乔治·伯恩韦尔爵士这种人的蛊惑，我倒是一点儿也不奇怪，因为他经常把爵士带到家里来，连我自己都很难抗拒爵士的翩翩风采。爵士比阿瑟大一些，从头到脚都是个谙熟世情的人物，什么地方都去过，什么事情都见过，口才非常好，仪表也十分出众。不过，他不在场的时候，我可以摆脱他那种迷人风度的影响，冷静地掂量他的为人。根据他愤世嫉俗的言辞，还有他落在我眼里的一些眼神，我断定他这个人完全不堪信任。我这么想，我的小玛丽也这么想，因为她身为女人，对他人的性情有一种敏锐的直觉。 “And, indeed, I could not wonder that such a man as Sir George Burnwell should gain an influence over him, for he has frequently brought him to my house, and I have found myself that I could hardly resist the fascination of his manner. He is older than Arthur, a man of the world to his finger-tips, one who had been everywhere, seen everything, a brilliant talker, and a man of great personal beauty. Yet when I think of him in cold blood, far away from the glamour of his presence, I am convinced from his cynical speech and the look which I have caught in his eyes that he is one who should be deeply distrusted. So I think, and so, too, thinks my little Mary, who has a woman’s quick insight into character.
“到现在，还没讲到的人也只剩她了。她本来是我的侄女，可惜我兄弟在五年之前与世长辞，抛下她一个人孤苦度日，于是我收养了她，待她像自己的亲生女儿。她是我家里的阳光，心地善良，美丽可人，非常善于料理家务，温婉娴静的品性也不输给任何女人。她是我的左膀右臂，没有她我真不知道该怎么办。只有一件事情，她没有顺我的意。我儿子爱她爱得很是痴心，向她求了两次婚，两次却都遭到了她的拒绝。我觉得，要说有哪个人能把我儿子拉回正道的话，这个人就只能是她，他俩如果结了婚，他的人生就会迎来翻天覆地的转变。现在可好，唉！来不及了，再也来不及了！ “And now there is only she to be described. She is my niece; but when my brother died five years ago and left her alone in the world I adopted her, and have looked upon her ever since as my daughter. She is a sunbeam in my house—sweet, loving, beautiful, a wonderful manager and housekeeper, yet as tender and quiet and gentle as a woman could be. She is my right hand. I do not know what I could do without her. In only one matter has she ever gone against my wishes. Twice my boy has asked her to marry him, for he loves her devotedly, but each time she has refused him. I think that if anyone could have drawn him into the right path it would have been she, and that his marriage might have changed his whole life; but now, alas! it is too late—forever too late!
“好了，福尔摩斯先生，您已经知道了我家的屋檐下都有些什么人，接下来，我要说的就是那件悲惨的事情。 “Now, Mr. Holmes, you know the people who live under my roof, and I shall continue with my miserable story.
“昨天晚上，吃完饭在客厅里喝咖啡的时候，我跟阿瑟和玛丽讲了我白天的经历，还讲了眼下放在家里的那件珍宝，唯一没讲的就是主顾的名字。端咖啡进来的是露西·帕尔，不过我敢肯定，我讲的时候她已经离开了房间。话说回来，房间的门关没关我可不敢保证。玛丽和阿瑟都对这件事情很感兴趣，都想看看那个著名的王冠，不过我没有同意，因为我觉得，还是别去动它比较好。 “When we were taking coffee in the drawing-room that night after dinner, I told Arthur and Mary my experience, and of the precious treasure which we had under our roof, suppressing only the name of my client. Lucy Parr, who had brought in the coffee, had, I am sure, left the room; but I cannot swear that the door was closed. Mary and Arthur were much interested and wished to see the famous coronet, but I thought it better not to disturb it.
“‘您把它放在什么地方呢？’阿瑟问我。 “‘Where have you put it?’ asked Arthur.
“‘在我自己的衣橱里。’ “‘In my own bureau.’
“‘是吗，老天保佑，夜里可别有人到家里来偷东西啊，’他说。 “‘Well, I hope to goodness the house won’t be burgled during the night,’ said he.
“‘衣橱是上了锁的，’我回答他。 “‘It is locked up,’ I answered.
“‘噢，您那个衣橱啊，随便哪把旧钥匙都打得开。我小的时候就打开过，用的是储藏室里那个碗柜的钥匙。’ “‘Oh, any old key will fit that bureau. When I was a youngster I have opened it myself with the key of the box-room cupboard.’
“‘他经常都这么胡说八道，所以我也没当回事。可是，等我回房的时候，他跟到了我的房间里，脸色非常阴郁。 “He often had a wild way of talking, so that I thought little of what he said. He followed me to my room, however, that night with a very grave face.
“‘听我说，爸爸，’说话的时候，他眼睛望着地面，‘您能给我两百镑吗？’ “‘Look here, dad,’ said he with his eyes cast down, ‘can you let me have two hundred pounds?’
“‘不行，我不能！’我厉声回答他。‘在钱的事情上，我对你实在是慷慨过头了。’ “‘No, I cannot!’ I answered sharply. ‘I have been far too generous with you in money matters.’
“‘您对我确实非常慷慨，’他说，‘可我必须拿到这笔钱，要不然，我就再没有脸面去俱乐部了。’ “‘You have been very kind,’ said he, ‘but I must have this money, or else I can never show my face inside the club again.’
“‘不去最好！’我嚷了一声。 “‘And a very good thing, too!’ I cried.
“‘好是好，可您总不能让我赖着账离开那里吧，’他说，‘我可丢不起这种人。我无论如何也得想办法筹到这笔钱，您不帮我的话，那我就只能去找别的门路了。’ “‘Yes, but you would not have me leave it a dishonoured man,’ said he. ‘I could not bear the disgrace. I must raise the money in some way, and if you will not let me have it, then I must try other means.’
“当时我非常生气，因为他这个月已经是第三次问我要钱了。‘我一个子儿也不会给你，’我吼了一句。听了这话，他没有再说什么，鞠了个躬就出去了。 “I was very angry, for this was the third demand during the month. ‘You shall not have a farthing from me,’ I cried, on which he bowed and left the room without another word.
“他走了之后，我打开衣橱，确定那件宝物安然无恙，然后就重新锁上了柜门。接下来，我开始在屋子里巡视，看看门窗是不是都关严实了。这件工作通常都是由玛丽来做的，只不过，当晚我觉得还是自己来做比较好。下楼的时候，我看见玛丽站在大厅侧窗的旁边。我走过去的时候，她关上窗子，插好了插销。 “When he was gone I unlocked my bureau, made sure that my treasure was safe, and locked it again. Then I started to go round the house to see that all was secure—a duty which I usually leave to Mary but which I thought it well to perform myself that night. As I came down the stairs I saw Mary herself at the side window of the hall, which she closed and fastened as I approached.
“‘告诉我，爸爸，’她对我说。按我的感觉，她的神色有点儿慌张，‘那个叫露西的女仆今天晚上出去，是您同意的吗？’ “‘Tell me, dad,’ said she, looking, I thought, a little disturbed, ‘did you give Lucy, the maid, leave to go out tonight?’
“‘当然不是。’ “‘Certainly not.’
“‘她刚刚才从屋子的后门进来。我完全肯定，刚才她只不过是到院子的边门去见某个人而已，可我还是觉得，这样终归不太安全，咱们应该制止她。’ “‘She came in just now by the back door. I have no doubt that she has only been to the side gate to see someone, but I think that it is hardly safe and should be stopped.’
“‘那你明天早上跟她讲吧，我也可以去讲，如果你觉得我讲比较好的话。你确定门窗都关好了吗？’ “‘You must speak to her in the morning, or I will if you prefer it. Are you sure that everything is fastened?’
“‘非常确定，爸爸。’ “‘Quite sure, dad.’
“‘那好，晚安。’我吻了她，然后上楼回房，不一会儿就睡着了。 “‘Then, good-night.’ I kissed her and went up to my bedroom again, where I was soon asleep.
“我尽量讲得详细一点儿，福尔摩斯先生，不落下任何一件可能会跟案子有关系的事情。如果有哪一点讲得不清楚，您一定得问我才行。” “I am endeavouring to tell you everything, Mr. Holmes, which may have any bearing upon the case, but I beg that you will question me upon any point which I do not make clear.”
“恰恰相反，您讲得清楚极了。” “On the contrary, your statement is singularly lucid.”
“尤其是接下来的这个部分，我希望能讲得特别清楚。我睡觉本来也不怎么沉，毫无疑问，心里装着这样的担忧，我睡得就更不踏实了。这么着，大概凌晨两点的时候，我就被屋里的什么声音给吵醒了。没等我完全清醒过来，那声音已经消失了，可它给我留下了一种印象，似乎是什么地方有人在轻轻地关窗子。我躺在床上，支起耳朵使劲儿地听。突然之间，我觉得万分惊骇，因为我清清楚楚地听见，隔壁的房间里传来了轻轻悄悄的脚步声。我吓得心惊肉跳，赶紧溜下床去，站在更衣室的门边往里面看。 “I come to a part of my story now in which I should wish to be particularly so. I am not a very heavy sleeper, and the anxiety in my mind tended, no doubt, to make me even less so than usual. About two in the morning, then, I was awakened by some sound in the house. It had ceased ere I was wide awake, but it had left an impression behind it as though a window had gently closed somewhere. I lay listening with all my ears. Suddenly, to my horror, there was a distinct sound of footsteps moving softly in the next room. I slipped out of bed, all palpitating with fear, and peeped round the corner of my dressing-room door.
“‘阿瑟！’我忍不住尖叫起来，‘你这个无赖！你这个贼！连那个王冠你也敢碰，你哪来的胆子？’ “‘Arthur!’ I screamed, ‘You villain! You thief! How dare you touch that coronet?’
“睡觉之前我没灭煤气灯，特意让它半亮着，这会儿就看见我那个悖时的孩子站在灯的旁边，身上只穿着衬衫和长裤，双手捧着那个王冠。他似乎正在扭它，要不就是在扳它，总之是使足了全身的力气。听到我的叫喊，他的脸变得像死人一样煞白，手里的王冠也掉到了地上。我赶紧把它捡了起来，仔仔细细地看了看。金制的王冠少了一个尖角，一起少掉的还有镶在那个尖角上的三颗绿宝石。 “The gas was half up, as I had left it, and my unhappy boy, dressed only in his shirt and trousers, was standing beside the light, holding the coronet in his hands. He appeared to be wrenching at it, or bending it with all his strength. At my cry he dropped it from his grasp and turned as pale as death. I snatched it up and examined it. One of the gold corners, with three of the beryls in it, was missing.
“‘你这个下流坯！’我吼了起来，气得丧失了理智。‘你把它搞坏了！你让我这一辈子都抬不起头了！你把那些宝石偷到哪儿去了？’ “‘You blackguard!’ I shouted, beside myself with rage. ‘You have destroyed it! You have dishonoured me forever! Where are the jewels which you have stolen?’
“‘您说我偷！’他嚷了一句。 “‘Stolen!’ he cried.
“‘没错，你是个贼！’我一边大吼大叫，一边抓着他的肩膀使劲儿地晃。 “‘Yes, thief!’ I roared, shaking him by the shoulder.
“‘宝石没有不见，不会不见的，’他这么说。 “‘There are none missing. There cannot be any missing,’ said he.
“‘有三颗不见了，你肯定知道它们在哪儿。管你叫贼都不够，你还非得让我管你叫骗子吗？刚刚我亲眼看到你还想再掰一个角下来，这样你还要抵赖吗？’ “‘There are three missing. And you know where they are. Must I call you a liar as well as a thief? Did I not see you trying to tear off another piece?’
“‘您骂我骂够了吧，’他说，‘反正我是不会再忍了。既然您这么侮辱我，这件事情我就不会再说一个字。明天早上我就离开您的房子，自个儿去闯世界。’ “‘You have called me names enough,’ said he; ‘I will not stand it any longer. I shall not say another word about this business, since you have chosen to insult me. I will leave your house in the morning and make my own way in the world.’
“‘离开也只能让警察押着离开！’我吼了一声，心里又痛又恼，几乎是已经疯了。‘我会让他们把这件事情查个水落石出的。’ “‘You shall leave it in the hands of the police!’ I cried, half-mad with grief and rage. ‘I shall have this matter probed to the bottom.’
“‘我什么也不会跟您说的，’说这话的时候他显得极其激动，按我看完全不像他的天性。‘您要报警就去报好了，看那些警察能找出些什么来吧。’ “‘You shall learn nothing from me,’ said he with a passion such as I should not have thought was in his nature. ‘If you choose to call the police, let the police find what they can.’
“到了这个时候，整座房子都已经骚动起来，因为我怒不可遏，嗓门儿提得很高。第一个冲进我房间的就是玛丽，她看到王冠，又看到阿瑟的脸色，一下子明白了所有的事情，跟着就尖叫一声，晕倒在地。我立刻打发上房女仆去报警，把这件事情交到了警方手里。阿瑟把双臂抱在胸前，沉着脸站在原地。等那位督察带着一名警员走进房子的时候，阿瑟就问我，是不是打算告他盗窃。我回答说，既然弄坏了的王冠是国家财产，这事情就不再是什么家庭纠纷，已经变成了一件公共事务。我已经拿定了主意，一切都得按法律来办。 “By this time the whole house was astir, for I had raised my voice in my anger. Mary was the first to rush into my room, and, at the sight of the coronet and of Arthur’s face, she read the whole story and, with a scream, fell down senseless on the ground. I sent the house-maid for the police and put the investigation into their hands at once. When the inspector and a constable entered the house, Arthur, who had stood sullenly with his arms folded, asked me whether it was my intention to charge him with theft. I answered that it had ceased to be a private matter, but had become a public one, since the ruined coronet was national property. I was determined that the law should have its way in everything.
“‘最低限度，’他说，‘您还是让他们等一会儿再逮捕我吧。让我去屋子外面待五分钟，对您对我都有好处。’ “‘At least,’ said he, ‘you will not have me arrested at once. It would be to your advantage as well as mine if I might leave the house for five minutes.’
“‘那样你就会趁机逃走，也没准儿，你是想趁机藏匿偷来的东西，’我这么回答他。接下来，我意识到了自己面临的可怕处境，于是就央求他不要忘了，面临名誉威胁的不光是我，还有一位比我显赫得多的人物。我还告诉他，他这么做，等于是在制造一起震动全国的丑闻，另一方面，只要他肯把那三颗失踪宝石的下落告诉我，一切都还有挽回的余地。 “‘That you may get away, or perhaps that you may conceal what you have stolen,’ said I. And then, realising the dreadful position in which I was placed, I implored him to remember that not only my honour but that of one who was far greater than I was at stake; and that he threatened to raise a scandal which would convulse the nation. He might avert it all if he would but tell me what he had done with the three missing stones.
“‘你不如勇敢地面对这件事情，’我说，‘既然你已经被我当场抓住，坦白也不会让你的过错显得更加可耻。只要你愿意作出力所能及的补救，把绿宝石的下落告诉我们，一切我都可以原谅，都可以当作没有发生。’ “‘You may as well face the matter,’ said I; ‘you have been caught in the act, and no confession could make your guilt more heinous. If you but make such reparation as is in your power, by telling us where the beryls are, all shall be forgiven and forgotten.’
“‘把您的原谅留给那些想要的人吧，’他这么应了一句，然后就冷笑一声，从我面前走开了。我看他已经铁了心肠，再说什么都不会管用，于是也就别无选择，只好把那位督察叫了进来，把他交给了警察。他们立刻展开搜索，不光搜了他的身，还搜了他的房间，搜遍了屋子里每一个可能藏匿宝石的地方。可是，宝石还是无影无踪，我那个遭殃的孩子也还是死活不肯开口，我们怎么连哄带吓都没有用。今天早上，他们把他送进了监狱。办完警方的那一大套手续之后，我急急忙忙地赶到了您这里，恳求您用您的本领来解决这个问题。警方已经公开承认，眼下他们什么头绪也没有。费用多少不是问题，您只管去花就是，我已经为这些宝石开了一千镑的赏格。老天爷，我该怎么办哪！一夜之间，我就失去了我的名誉、我的宝石，还有我的儿子。噢，我该怎么办哪！” “‘Keep your forgiveness for those who ask for it,’ he answered, turning away from me with a sneer. I saw that he was too hardened for any words of mine to influence him. There was but one way for it. I called in the inspector and gave him into custody. A search was made at once not only of his person but of his room and of every portion of the house where he could possibly have concealed the gems; but no trace of them could be found, nor would the wretched boy open his mouth for all our persuasions and our threats. This morning he was removed to a cell, and I, after going through all the police formalities, have hurried round to you to implore you to use your skill in unravelling the matter. The police have openly confessed that they can at present make nothing of it. You may go to any expense which you think necessary. I have already offered a reward of a thousand pounds. My God, what shall I do! I have lost my honour, my gems, and my son in one night. Oh, what shall I do!”
他用双手捧住自己的脑袋，身子来回摇晃，自个儿冲自个儿哼哼起来，模样就像是一个悲痛得无法言喻的孩子。 He put a hand on either side of his head and rocked himself to and fro, droning to himself like a child whose grief has got beyond words.
歇洛克·福尔摩斯一声不吭地坐了几分钟，眉头紧锁，眼睛直盯着壁炉里的火焰。 Sherlock Holmes sat silent for some few minutes, with his brows knitted and his eyes fixed upon the fire.
“您家里客人多吗？”他开口问道。 “Do you receive much company?” he asked.
“不多，只有我那位合伙人和他的家人，再就是阿瑟偶尔带回来的朋友。最近这段时间，乔治·伯恩韦尔爵士来过几次。按我的记忆，别的也就没什么人了。” “None save my partner with his family and an occasional friend of Arthur’s. Sir George Burnwell has been several times lately. No one else, I think.”
“您经常出去应酬吗？” “Do you go out much in society?”
“阿瑟经常去，玛丽和我总是待在家里，我俩都不喜欢应酬。” “Arthur does. Mary and I stay at home. We neither of us care for it.”
“年青姑娘这样可不多见啊。” “That is unusual in a young girl.”
“她生来就喜欢安静，还有啊，她已经二十四岁，也不算特别年青了。” “She is of a quiet nature. Besides, she is not so very young. She is four-and-twenty.”
“这件事情，按您刚才的说法，似乎也对她造成了很大的震动。” “This matter, from what you say, seems to have been a shock to her also.”
“大极了！她甚至比我还要震惊。” “Terrible! She is even more affected than I.”
“事情是您儿子干的，你们俩都觉得这一点毫无疑问，对吗？” “You have neither of you any doubt as to your son’s guilt?”
“还能有什么疑问，我亲眼看见他把王冠拿在手里。” “How can we have when I saw him with my own eyes with the coronet in his hands.”
“我可不认为这是一条确凿无疑的证据。王冠的其余部分有损坏吗？” “I hardly consider that a conclusive proof. Was the remainder of the coronet at all injured?”
“是的，王冠被扭弯了。” “Yes, it was twisted.”
“那么，您有没有想过，当时他可能是在把它扳直呢？” “Do you not think, then, that he might have been trying to straighten it?”
“愿上帝保佑您！谢谢您尽量维护他，也等于是维护我。只不过，您这个任务实在是太艰巨了。不说别的，他为什么要到那里去呢？如果心里没有鬼的话，他干吗不说呢？” “God bless you! You are doing what you can for him and for me. But it is too heavy a task. What was he doing there at all? If his purpose were innocent, why did he not say so?”
“您说到点子上了。如果心里有鬼的话，他干吗不编个理由出来呢？按我看，他拒绝开口的事情可以从两个方向去解释。现在看来，这件案子包含着几个非常特别的地方。关于吵醒您的那个声音，警方是怎么说的呢？” “Precisely. And if it were guilty, why did he not invent a lie? His silence appears to me to cut both ways. There are several singular points about the case. What did the police think of the noise which awoke you from your sleep?”
“他们说，那可能是阿瑟把自个儿卧室的门关上的声音。” “They considered that it might be caused by Arthur’s closing his bedroom door.”
“说得真对！就跟一个正在作大案的人非得使劲儿关门吵醒全家似的。好了，关于那些失踪的宝石，他们又是怎么说的呢？” “A likely story! As if a man bent on felony would slam his door so as to wake a household. What did they say, then, of the disappearance of these gems?”
“他们仍然在敲墙跺地、翻箱倒柜，希望能把宝石找回来。” “They are still sounding the planking and probing the furniture in the hope of finding them.”
“他们想没想到去屋子外面找呢？” “Have they thought of looking outside the house?”
“想到了，他们找得特别卖力，已经把整个花园仔仔细细地搜了一遍。” “Yes, they have shown extraordinary energy. The whole garden has already been minutely examined.”
“到了现在，亲爱的先生，”福尔摩斯说道，“您有没有明显地感觉到，这件案子要比您和警方刚开始的推断复杂得多呢？您可能觉得这案子非常简单，可我却觉得它特别复杂。好了，咱们来看看您那个假设到底是怎么回事。按您的假设，您的儿子从床上爬起来，冒着极大的风险跑进您的更衣室，打开您的衣橱，拿出您那个王冠，使出吃奶的劲儿掰下一个小小的尖角，然后就跑到别的某个地方，藏起三十九颗宝石之中的三颗，藏得还非常巧妙，谁都没法找到，再下来，他又拿着剩下的三十六颗宝石跑回原来那个房间，给自己招来事情败露的莫大风险。现在我想问问您，这样的假设站得住脚吗？” “Now, my dear sir,” said Holmes, “is it not obvious to you now that this matter really strikes very much deeper than either you or the police were at first inclined to think? It appeared to you to be a simple case; to me it seems exceedingly complex. Consider what is involved by your theory. You suppose that your son came down from his bed, went, at great risk, to your dressing-room, opened your bureau, took out your coronet, broke off by main force a small portion of it, went off to some other place, concealed three gems out of the thirty-nine, with such skill that nobody can find them, and then returned with the other thirty-six into the room in which he exposed himself to the greatest danger of being discovered. I ask you now, is such a theory tenable?”
“可是，其他的假设又在哪里呢？”银行家叫了起来，做了个绝望的手势。“如果他去那里是出于某种善良的目的，他为什么不说出来呢？” “But what other is there?” cried the banker with a gesture of despair. “If his motives were innocent, why does he not explain them?”
“我们的任务就是去搞清楚这个问题，”福尔摩斯回答道，“所以呢，您没意见的话，霍尔德先生，咱们现在就一起去一趟斯特里特姆，花上个把钟头的时间，把案子的细节再看得稍微仔细一些。” “It is our task to find that out,” replied Holmes; “so now, if you please, Mr. Holder, we will set off for Streatham together, and devote an hour to glancing a little more closely into details.”
我朋友竭力邀请我一起去，而我自己也非常想去，原因在于，刚刚听到的故事已经让我产生了极大的好奇与同情。说实话，当时我也跟那位不幸的银行家一样，认为他儿子的罪行的确是一目了然，另一方面，我又对福尔摩斯的判断力拥有无比的信心，因此就觉得，只要他还对这个大家公认的解释不满意，银行家儿子的事情就一定有转圜的余地。前往南郊那个街区的路上，福尔摩斯几乎一句话也没说，只是自顾自地坐在那里，下巴贴着胸膛，帽沿儿盖着眼睛，沉浸在深不可测的思绪之中。至于我们的主顾嘛，眼前既然出现了一点希望的微光，他似乎打起了一点精神，以至于东拉西扯地跟我聊了聊他生意上的一些事情。经过一段短短的火车旅程，再加上一段路程更短的步行，我们就来到了这位大银行家的简朴寓所，费尔班宅邸。 My friend insisted upon my accompanying them in their expedition, which I was eager enough to do, for my curiosity and sympathy were deeply stirred by the story to which we had listened. I confess that the guilt of the banker’s son appeared to me to be as obvious as it did to his unhappy father, but still I had such faith in Holmes’s judgment that I felt that there must be some grounds for hope as long as he was dissatisfied with the accepted explanation. He hardly spoke a word the whole way out to the southern suburb, but sat with his chin upon his breast and his hat drawn over his eyes, sunk in the deepest thought. Our client appeared to have taken fresh heart at the little glimpse of hope which had been presented to him, and he even broke into a desultory chat with me over his business affairs. A short railway journey and a shorter walk brought us to Fairbank, the modest residence of the great financier.
费尔班宅邸是一座相当大的方形房屋，房子是用白石砌成的，离马路稍微有点儿距离。房子正面的庭院里是一片白雪皑皑的草坪，一条双行的马车道穿过草坪蜿蜒而下，直抵庭院入口处的两扇大铁门。大门的右侧有一道小小的边门，边门里面是两排修剪整齐的树篱，树篱之间的小径是商贩出入的通道，从马路一直通到宅子的厨房门口。大门的左侧是通往马厩的巷道，巷道并不在庭院的范围之内，而是一条公用的大路，只不过很少有人走而已。到了屋子门口，福尔摩斯撇下我俩在那里站着，开始自顾自地在庭院里四处逡巡，走得非常缓慢。他走过屋子的正面，沿着商贩使用的那条小径走了一阵，又从屋子后面的花园绕进了通往马厩的巷道。他去得实在是太久，我和霍尔德先生便径直走进餐厅，坐在壁炉旁边等他回来。我俩默默地坐在那里，餐厅的门却突然开了，一位年青的女士走了进来。她个子中等偏上，身材苗条，完全没有血色的脸庞让黑色的头发和眼睛更显乌亮。现在想来，我从来都没有在其他女人脸上看到过如此惨白的颜色。她的嘴唇也没有一丝血色，眼睛却哭得红红的。她悄无声息地走进了房间，给我的印象是她承受着巨大的悲痛，程度比这位银行家今天早上的表现有过之而无不及。格外让人吃惊的是，这样的神情竟然会出现在她的身上，因为她显然是一个非常坚强的女子，拥有十分惊人的自控能力。进来之后，她没有理会我的存在，径直走到叔叔跟前，伸出手去抚摸了一下叔叔的头，动作之中充满了女性的温柔。 Fairbank was a good-sized square house of white stone, standing back a little from the road. A double carriage-sweep, with a snow-clad lawn, stretched down in front to two large iron gates which closed the entrance. On the right side was a small wooden thicket, which led into a narrow path between two neat hedges stretching from the road to the kitchen door, and forming the tradesmen’s entrance. On the left ran a lane which led to the stables, and was not itself within the grounds at all, being a public, though little used, thoroughfare. Holmes left us standing at the door and walked slowly all round the house, across the front, down the tradesmen’s path, and so round by the garden behind into the stable lane. So long was he that Mr. Holder and I went into the dining-room and waited by the fire until he should return. We were sitting there in silence when the door opened and a young lady came in. She was rather above the middle height, slim, with dark hair and eyes, which seemed the darker against the absolute pallor of her skin. I do not think that I have ever seen such deadly paleness in a woman’s face. Her lips, too, were bloodless, but her eyes were flushed with crying. As she swept silently into the room she impressed me with a greater sense of grief than the banker had done in the morning, and it was the more striking in her as she was evidently a woman of strong character, with immense capacity for self-restraint. Disregarding my presence, she went straight to her uncle and passed her hand over his head with a sweet womanly caress.
“您已经吩咐他们把阿瑟放了，对吗，爸爸？”她问道。 “You have given orders that Arthur should be liberated, have you not, dad?” she asked.
“没有，不行啊，我的姑娘，这事情必须得查个水落石出才行。” “No, no, my girl, the matter must be probed to the bottom.”
“可我非常肯定，他跟这事情没有关系。您也知道，女人的直觉是很灵的。我知道他没做什么坏事，您对他这么严厉，以后会后悔的。” “But I am so sure that he is innocent. You know what woman’s instincts are. I know that he has done no harm and that you will be sorry for having acted so harshly.”
“可是，他要是无辜的话，为什么不肯开口呢？” “Why is he silent, then, if he is innocent?”
“谁知道呢？也许是因为您怀疑他，他觉得非常生气吧。” “Who knows? Perhaps because he was so angry that you should suspect him.”
“我实实在在地看到他把王冠拿在手里，怎么可能不怀疑呢？” “How could I help suspecting him, when I actually saw him with the coronet in his hand?”
“噢，可他只是把它捡起来看看，没什么别的意图。噢，求您了，求您相信我的话吧，他真的是无辜的。这事情就这么算了吧，以后也别再提了。想到我们亲爱的阿瑟竟然待在监狱里，真是太可怕啦！” “Oh, but he had only picked it up to look at it. Oh, do, do take my word for it that he is innocent. Let the matter drop and say no more. It is so dreadful to think of our dear Arthur in prison!”
“宝石没找回来，我绝对不能就这么算了——绝对不能，玛丽！你喜欢阿瑟，所以就没有意识到，眼下我面临着一些多么严重的后果。我不但没想把这件事情捂住，还从伦敦请来了一位先生，让他进行更加深入的调查。” “I shall never let it drop until the gems are found—never, Mary! Your affection for Arthur blinds you as to the awful consequences to me. Far from hushing the thing up, I have brought a gentleman down from London to inquire more deeply into it.”
“这位先生吗？”她问道，转过脸来看着我。 “This gentleman?” she asked, facing round to me.
“不是，是这位先生的朋友。他说他打算自个儿去转一转，这会儿已经走进了马厩门口的那条巷道。” “No, his friend. He wished us to leave him alone. He is round in the stable lane now.”
“马厩门口的巷道？”她挑了挑乌黑的眉毛。“那儿有什么可找的呢？噢！刚刚进来的这位，依我看，就是他吧。我相信，先生，您肯定会成功地证明，真相就是我确信无疑的那件事情，也就是说，我堂兄阿瑟跟这桩罪行没有关系。” “The stable lane?” She raised her dark eyebrows. “What can he hope to find there? Ah! this, I suppose, is he. I trust, sir, that you will succeed in proving, what I feel sure is the truth, that my cousin Arthur is innocent of this crime.”
“我完全同意您的观点，而且我相信，您和我，咱俩可以一起来证明这件事情，”福尔摩斯一边回答，一边走回门口，就着垫子蹭自己鞋子上的雪。“我没想错的话，屈尊听我进言的应该就是玛丽·霍尔德小姐吧。我可以问您一两个问题吗？” “I fully share your opinion, and I trust, with you, that we may prove it,” returned Holmes, going back to the mat to knock the snow from his shoes. “I believe I have the honour of addressing Miss Mary Holder. Might I ask you a question or two?”
“请吧，先生，只要有助于澄清这件可怕的事情就行。” “Pray do, sir, if it may help to clear this horrible affair up.”
“昨天夜里，您本人什么也没听见吗？” “You heard nothing yourself last night?”
“没听见，后来才听见我叔叔大声说话。听见他的声音，我就下了楼。” “Nothing, until my uncle here began to speak loudly. I heard that, and I came down.”
“昨天晚上，门窗都是您关的。所有窗子您都关好了吗？” “You shut up the windows and doors the night before. Did you fasten all the windows?”
“是的。” “Yes.”
“今天早晨，所有的窗子都还是关得好好的吗？” “Were they all fastened this morning?”
“是的。” “Yes.”
“您家里的一名女仆正在谈恋爱，对吧？昨晚您还跟您叔叔提过，她曾经出去找她的爱人，我没说错吧？” “You have a maid who has a sweetheart? I think that you remarked to your uncle last night that she had been out to see him?”
“是的，那个姑娘是在客厅里伺候的，也许听见了叔叔说王冠的事情。” “Yes, and she was the girl who waited in the drawing-room, and who may have heard uncle’s remarks about the coronet.”
“我明白了。您的意思是，她可能趁着出去的机会把这件事情告诉了她的爱人，窃案可能是他俩一块儿谋划的。” “I see. You infer that she may have gone out to tell her sweetheart, and that the two may have planned the robbery.”
“我已经跟你们说了，我亲眼看见阿瑟把王冠拿在手里，”银行家不胜其烦地叫了起来，“既然如此，这些捕风捉影的假设还有什么用呢？” “But what is the good of all these vague theories,” cried the banker impatiently, “When I have told you that I saw Arthur with the coronet in his hands?”
“稍安毋躁，霍尔德先生，回头我们就会讲到阿瑟的事情。咱们接着说这位姑娘的事情吧，霍尔德小姐。您看见她从厨房的门回来的，对吧？” “Wait a little, Mr. Holder. We must come back to that. About this girl, Miss Holder. You saw her return by the kitchen door, I presume?”
“是的，当时我去看门有没有关好，正好碰见她溜进来。我还看见那个男的躲在暗处呢。” “Yes; when I went to see if the door was fastened for the night I met her slipping in. I saw the man, too, in the gloom.”
“您认识他吗？” “Do you know him?”
“噢，怎么不认识！他就是给我家送蔬菜的那个菜贩子，名叫弗朗西斯·普罗斯珀。” “Oh, yes! he is the green-grocer who brings our vegetables round. His name is Francis Prosper.”
“当时，”福尔摩斯说道，“他站在门的左边——我是说，他站在小路上比较远的地方，够不到厨房的门，对吧？” “He stood,” said Holmes, “to the left of the door—that is to say, further up the path than is necessary to reach the door?”
“是的，的确如此。” “Yes, he did.”
“他还装了一条木腿，对吧？” “And he is a man with a wooden leg?”
年青女士那双会说话的眼睛里闪出了一抹类似恐惧的神色。“哎哟，您简直跟个巫师似的，”她说道，“这您是怎么知道的呢？”说到这里，她微微一笑，福尔摩斯的瘦削脸庞却依旧是那副全神贯注的模样，并没有用微笑来回应她。 Something like fear sprang up in the young lady’s expressive black eyes. “Why, you are like a magician,” said she. “How do you know that?” She smiled, but there was no answering smile in Holmes’s thin, eager face.
“我非常希望现在就到楼上去看一看，”他说道，“或许还会再上屋子外面去看一看。又或许，我应该先看看一楼的窗子，然后再到楼上去。” “I should be very glad now to go upstairs,” said he. “I shall probably wish to go over the outside of the house again. Perhaps I had better take a look at the lower windows before I go up.”
他飞快地走过一扇又一扇窗子，其间只在餐厅里对着马厩巷道的那扇大窗子跟前停了一停。他打开那扇窗子，用他的高倍放大镜仔仔细细地检查了一遍窗台。“好了，咱们一块儿到楼上去吧。”好一会儿之后，他终于说道。 He walked swiftly round from one to the other, pausing only at the large one which looked from the hall onto the stable lane. This he opened and made a very careful examination of the sill with his powerful magnifying lens. “Now we shall go upstairs,” said he at last.
银行家的更衣室是一个装潢简朴的小房间，里面铺着一张灰色的地毯，还摆着一个硕大的衣橱和一架长长的穿衣镜。福尔摩斯径直走到了衣橱跟前，紧紧地盯着衣橱的锁。 The banker’s dressing-room was a plainly furnished little chamber, with a grey carpet, a large bureau, and a long mirror. Holmes went to the bureau first and looked hard at the lock.
“打开衣橱的人用的是哪把钥匙呢？”他问道。 “Which key was used to open it?” he asked.
“就是我儿子说的那一把，储藏室里那个碗柜的钥匙。” “That which my son himself indicated—that of the cupboard of the lumber room.”
“钥匙在这儿吗？” “Have you it here?”
“梳妆台上的那把就是。” “That is it on the dressing-table.”
歇洛克·福尔摩斯拿起钥匙打开了衣橱。 Sherlock Holmes took it up and opened the bureau.
“这把锁没有声音，”他说道，“没把您吵醒也很正常。据我看，这就是装王冠的那个盒子吧。咱们得好好地看一看。”他打开盒子，取出王冠，把它摆在了梳妆台上。出现在我眼前的是珠宝工艺的一个巅峰典范，镶在上面的三十六颗宝石也是我生平仅见的绝佳样板。在王冠的一侧，有一个镶有三颗宝石的尖角已经被人掰掉，留下了一个缺口。 “It is a noiseless lock,” said he. “It is no wonder that it did not wake you. This case, I presume, contains the coronet. We must have a look at it.” He opened the case, and taking out the diadem he laid it upon the table. It was a magnificent specimen of the jeweller’s art, and the thirty-six stones were the finest that I have ever seen. At one side of the coronet was a cracked edge, where a corner holding three gems had been torn away.
“好了，霍尔德先生，”福尔摩斯说道，“您瞧，这个尖角跟不幸失踪的那个尖角是对称的。麻烦您把它掰下来给我看看。” “Now, Mr. Holder,” said Holmes, “here is the corner which corresponds to that which has been so unfortunately lost. Might I beg that you will break it off.”
银行家惊骇不已地往后面缩了一缩。“我做梦也不会去尝试这样的事情。”他说道。 The banker recoiled in horror. “I should not dream of trying,” said he.
“那就让我来试试好了。”说完之后，福尔摩斯猛然运足力气掰了一下，王冠却没有丝毫变化。“我感觉它稍微有点儿变形，”他说道，“不过，我手指上的力道虽然超出常人，要把它掰下来也得费上不少工夫。普通人应该是掰不动的。好了，霍尔德先生，如果我把它掰下来的话，您知道会怎么样吗？动静会大得跟手枪开火一样。您说说，如果这样的事情发生在离您的床几码之内的地方，您有可能无知无觉吗？” “Then I will.” Holmes suddenly bent his strength upon it, but without result. “I feel it give a little,” said he; “but, though I am exceptionally strong in the fingers, it would take me all my time to break it. An ordinary man could not do it. Now, what do you think would happen if I did break it, Mr. Holder? There would be a noise like a pistol shot. Do you tell me that all this happened within a few yards of your bed and that you heard nothing of it?”
“我不知道该怎么说，我眼前完全是一抹黑。” “I do not know what to think. It is all dark to me.”
“咱们走着瞧好了，黑地儿兴许会渐渐亮起来的。霍尔德小姐，您怎么看呢？” “But perhaps it may grow lighter as we go. What do you think, Miss Holder?”
“坦白说，即便是到了现在，我还是跟我叔叔一样困惑。” “I confess that I still share my uncle’s perplexity.”
“您看见儿子的时候，他没有穿鞋或者拖鞋吗？” “Your son had no shoes or slippers on when you saw him?”
“他只穿了衬衫和长裤，别的什么也没穿。” “He had nothing on save only his trousers and shirt.”
“谢谢您。咱们这次调查的运气显然是非常之好，要是还不能澄清这件事情的话，咱们就只能怪自己没用了。您允许的话，霍尔德先生，我这就到屋子外面去继续调查。” “Thank you. We have certainly been favoured with extraordinary luck during this inquiry, and it will be entirely our own fault if we do not succeed in clearing the matter up. With your permission, Mr. Holder, I shall now continue my investigations outside.”

“他心爱的女人还在现场的时候，他不能采取任何行动，否则就会让她的行径遭到可耻的暴露。不过，她的身影刚刚消失，他立刻意识到，这件祸事会对您造成多么巨大的打击、亡羊补牢又是多么地必要。他连鞋都顾不上穿，光着脚冲到楼下，打开窗子，跳进外面的雪地，看到月光下的巷道里有一个黑影，于是就沿着巷道追了上去。这么着，阿瑟逮住了正要逃跑的乔治·伯恩韦尔爵士，两个人厮打起来。您的孩子抓着王冠的一端，他的对头抓着另一端。混战之中，您的儿子一拳打中了乔治爵士的眼睛。紧接着，啪的一声，有什么东西突然断掉了，您的儿子发现王冠已经到了自己手里，这才赶紧跑回家里，关好窗子，上楼走进了您的房间。他发现王冠在刚才的扭打当中变了形，所以想把它扳直，他正在扳的时候，您出现在了现场。” “As long as she was on the scene he could not take any action without a horrible exposure of the woman whom he loved. But the instant that she was gone he realised how crushing a misfortune this would be for you, and how important it was to set it right. He rushed down, just as he was, in his bare feet, opened the window, sprang out into the snow, and ran down the lane, where he could see a dark figure in the moonlight. Sir George Burnwell tried to get away, but Arthur caught him, and there was a struggle between them, your lad tugging at one side of the coronet and his opponent at the other. In the scuffle, your son struck Sir George and cut him over the eye. Then something suddenly snapped, and your son, finding that he had the coronet in his hands, rushed back, closed the window, ascended to your room, and had just observed that the coronet had been twisted in the struggle and was endeavouring to straighten it when you appeared upon the scene.”
“真的是这样吗？”银行家倒吸了一口凉气。 “Is it possible?” gasped the banker.
“那个时刻，他觉得自己理应得到您最诚挚的感谢，您却用辱骂激起了他的怒火。他没法向您说明真相，因为他不想出卖另一个人。毫无疑问，那个人并不值得他手下留情，可他还是采取了一种更有骑士风范的立场，替她守住了秘密。” “You then roused his anger by calling him names at a moment when he felt that he had deserved your warmest thanks. He could not explain the true state of affairs without betraying one who certainly deserved little enough consideration at his hands. He took the more chivalrous view, however, and preserved her secret.”
“所以她才会一看到王冠就尖叫着晕了过去。”霍尔德先生叫道，“噢，我的天哪！我真是个瞎了眼的蠢货！当时他还求我让他去外面待五分钟！这个好孩子是想去看少掉的一块在不在打斗现场啊。我那样误会他，实在是太残忍了！” “And that was why she shrieked and fainted when she saw the coronet,” cried Mr. Holder. “Oh, my God! what a blind fool I have been! And his asking to be allowed to go out for five minutes! The dear fellow wanted to see if the missing piece were at the scene of the struggle. How cruelly I have misjudged him!”
“刚到您屋子跟前的时候，”福尔摩斯接着说道，“我小心翼翼地绕着屋子转了一圈，想看看雪地里有没有什么可以帮助破案的痕迹，因为我知道前一天傍晚雪就停了，还知道之后的那场严霜可以让痕迹得到完好的保存。我沿着商贩出入的小径走了一遍，发现它已经被践踏得无从辨识。不过，刚刚跨出那条小径，我立刻辨认出来，有一男一女曾经站在厨房门口远离正门的那一侧交谈，男人的脚印有一个是圆的，说明他装着一条木腿。我甚至可以看出他俩受到了惊扰，因为地上有脚尖深脚跟浅的足印，说明那个女的飞快地跑回了门里。木腿男人则在原地等了一小会儿，然后也离开了那里。我当即推断，这两个人可能就是您提过的那个女仆和她的爱人，后来的调查也证实了我的看法。这之后，我绕着花园走了一圈，只可惜没有任何收获。花园里只有一些乱七八糟的脚印，据我看，脚印的主人应该都是警方人员。还好，走进通往马厩的那条巷道之后，我立刻发现，眼前的雪地上书写着一个情节复杂的漫长故事。 “When I arrived at the house,” continued Holmes, “I at once went very carefully round it to observe if there were any traces in the snow which might help me. I knew that none had fallen since the evening before, and also that there had been a strong frost top reserve impressions. I passed along the tradesmen’s path, but found it all trampled down and indistinguishable. Just beyond it, however, at the far side of the kitchen door, a woman had stood and talked with a man, whose round impressions on one side showed that he had a wooden leg. I could even tell that they had been disturbed, for the woman had run back swiftly to the door, as was shown by the deep toe and light heel marks, while Wooden-leg had waited a little, and then had gone away. I thought at the time that this might be the maid and her sweetheart, of whom you had already spoken to me, and inquiry showed it was so. I passed round the garden without seeing anything more than random tracks, which I took to be the police; but when I got into the stable lane a very long and complex story was written in the snow in front of me.
“雪地上有两行足迹，属于一个穿靴子的男人，另外两行呢，我欣喜地看到，属于一个赤脚的男人。根据您之前告诉我的情况，我立刻断定，赤脚的男人就是您的儿子。穿靴子的男人走着来又走着去，赤脚的男人则跑得飞快，他的脚印跟靴子男人的脚印有一些重叠，显然是在追赶后者。我顺着靴子男人的足迹往上捋，发现它一直延伸到了大厅的窗子下面，靴子男人肯定是在那里站了很久，因为他的靴子已经穿透了积雪。接下来，我走到了足迹的另一端。另一端在巷道上离屋子一百码开外的地方，靴子男人在那里转过了脸。那里的积雪七零八落，看样子是发生过一场打斗。到最后，我看到了几点血迹，关于打斗的推测由此得到了确证。打斗之后，靴子男人顺着巷道跑了开去，而他逃跑的路线上又出现了一点血迹，说明他才是打斗之中的伤者。循着他的足迹走上大路之后，我发现那里的人行道已经有人打扫过了，这条线索由此中断。 “There was a double line of tracks of a booted man, and a second double line which I saw with delight belonged to a man with naked feet. I was at once convinced from what you had told me that the latter was your son. The first had walked both ways, but the other had run swiftly, and as his tread was marked in places over the depression of the boot, it was obvious that he had passed after the other. I followed them up and found they led to the hall window, where Boots had worn all the snow away while waiting. Then I walked to the other end, which was a hundred yards or more down the lane. I saw where Boots had faced round, where the snow was cut up as though there had been a struggle, and, finally, where a few drops of blood had fallen, to show me that I was not mistaken. Boots had then run down the lane, and another little smudge of blood showed that it was he who had been hurt. When he came to the highroad at the other end, I found that the pavement had been cleared, so there was an end to that clue.
“不过，您应该还记得，进屋之后，我用放大镜检查过大厅那扇窗子的窗台和窗框。当时我立刻断定，有人曾经从那扇窗子跳出去，原因在于，一只打湿了的脚曾经从同一扇窗子爬进来，我可以认出脚背在窗台上蹭出的痕迹。这样一来，我就对之前发生的事情有了一个概念：有个男人在窗子外面等着，另一个人把宝石王冠拿给了他，您的儿子碰巧看见了这一幕，于是就跑出去追那个窃贼，还跟窃贼打了一架。两个人都抓着王冠不放，结果呢，两个人的合力就对王冠造成了任何一人都无法单独造成的破坏。您的儿子拿着战利品跑了回来，却把战利品的零头留在了对头手里。以上这些情形我已经了如指掌，剩下的问题就是，等在外面的男人是谁？把王冠拿给他的人又是谁？ “On entering the house, however, I examined, as you remember, the sill and framework of the hall window with my lens, and I could at once see that someone had passed out. I could distinguish the outline of an instep where the wet foot had been placed in coming in. I was then beginning to be able to form an opinion as to what had occurred. A man had waited outside the window; someone had brought the gems; the deed had been overseen by your son; he had pursued the thief, had struggled with him; they had each tugged at the coronet, their united strength causing injuries which neither alone could have effected. He had returned with the prize, but had left a fragment in the grasp of his opponent. So far I was clear. The question now was, who was the man and who was it brought him the coronet?
“我有一条奉行多年的格言，也就是说，如果你已经排除了所有不可能的情形，剩下的情形就必然是事情的真相，不管它看上去有多么地不合常理。好了，我已经知道把王冠送出去的人不可能是您自己，剩下的就只有您的侄女和那些女仆。可是，要说是那些女仆的话，您的儿子干吗要替她们顶罪呢？我根本找不出任何理由来解释那样的情形。反过来，既然他爱着自己的堂妹，他替她保守秘密的举动就可以得到完美的解释，鉴于她那个秘密并不光彩，他这种举动只能说是越发地符合情理。想到您曾经看见她站在那扇窗子旁边，又想到她一看见王冠就晕了过去，我就对自己的推测有了十足的把握。 “It is an old maxim of mine that when you have excluded the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth. Now, I knew that it was not you who had brought it down, so there only remained your niece and the maids. But if it were the maids, why should your son allow himself to be accused in their place? There could be no possible reason. As he loved his cousin, however, there was an excellent explanation why he should retain her secret—the more so as the secret was a disgraceful one. When I remembered that you had seen her at that window, and how she had fainted on seeing the coronet again, my conjecture became a certainty.
“那么，她的同伙又会是谁呢？显然是她的情人，除了情人，还有谁的分量能超过她对您必然会有的爱和感激呢？我已经知道你们很少出去应酬，也知道你们的朋友圈子非常狭小。可惜的是，这个狭小的圈子当中恰恰包括了乔治·伯恩韦尔爵士。我以前就听说过这个家伙，知道他在女人的事情上声名狼藉。如此说来，留下靴子脚印、拿走失踪宝石的人只能是他，不会是什么别人。尽管知道自己已经被阿瑟发现，他多半还是沾沾自喜，觉得自己可以高枕无忧，因为他知道小伙子没法开口，一开口就会伤害自己的家人。 “And who could it be who was her confederate? A lover evidently, for who else could outweigh the love and gratitude which she must feel to you? I knew that you went out little, and that your circle of friends was a very limited one. But among them was Sir George Burnwell. I had heard of him before as being a man of evil reputation among women. It must have been he who wore those boots and retained the missing gems. Even though he knew that Arthur had discovered him, he might still flatter himself that he was safe, for the lad could not say a word without compromising his own family.
“听到这里，您自个儿也可以据理推测，接下来我会采取什么样的行动。我扮成流浪汉去了乔治爵士的寓所，设法跟爵士的贴身男仆搭上了关系，由此听说他的主人头天晚上伤到了脑袋。再下来，我付给那个男仆六个先令，万无一失地买到了一双爵士扔弃不穿的旧靴子。这之后，我带着靴子赶到了斯特里特姆，发现它们跟地上的足迹完全相符。” “Well, your own good sense will suggest what measures I took next. I went in the shape of a loafer to Sir George’s house, managed to pick up an acquaintance with his valet, learned that his master had cut his head the night before, and, finally, at the expense of six shillings, made all sure by buying a pair of his cast-off shoes. With these I journeyed down to Streatham and saw that they exactly fitted the tracks.”
“昨天晚上，我确实看到过一个衣衫褴褛的流浪汉在那条巷道上转悠。”霍尔德先生说道。 “I saw an ill-dressed vagabond in the lane yesterday evening,” said Mr. Holder.
“一点儿不错，那个流浪汉就是我。搞清楚我要对付的这个伙计是谁之后，我回家换了身衣服。到了这个时候，我必须扮演的是一个相当不好拿捏的角色，因为我非常清楚，要想避免丑闻，咱们就不能把他送上法庭，同时也非常清楚，这个恶棍十分精明，肯定能看出咱们缚手缚脚的艰难处境。这么着，我跑去找了他。刚开始的时候，他当然是一概不认，等我把前前后后所有细节讲给他听了之后，他就把墙上的一根防身手杖[^9]拿了下来，打算把我吓住。然而，我完全清楚我这个伙计的路数，于是就抢先用手枪顶住了他的脑袋，没给他留下动手的机会。这一来，他总算是比先前多了那么一点点通情达理的意思。我告诉他，我们可以花钱买回他手里的宝石，一千镑一颗。听了这话，他才破天荒第一次表露出了些许悔恨。‘什么，真是见了鬼！’他这么说，‘三颗一起我才卖了六百镑！’我跟他保证我们不会告他，很快就从他嘴里打听到了收赃的人住在哪里。接下来，我赶到了收赃的人那里，跟他来来回回磨了半天，这才按一千镑一颗的价钱买回了咱们的宝石。再下来，我跑去看了看您的儿子，告诉他一切风波都已过去，最后才在约莫凌晨两点的时候上床就寝，结束了按我看绝对算得上勤勤恳恳的一天。” “Precisely. It was I. I found that I had my man, so I came home and changed my clothes. It was a delicate part which I had to play then, for I saw that a prosecution must be avoided to avert scandal, and I knew that so astute a villain would see that our hands were tied in the matter. I went and saw him. At first, of course, he denied everything. But when I gave him every particular that had occurred, he tried to bluster and took down a life-preserver from the wall. I knew my man, however, and I clapped a pistol to his head before he could strike. Then he became a little more reasonable. I told him that we would give him a price for the stones he held—a thousand pounds apiece. That brought out the first signs of grief that he had shown. ‘Why, dash it all!’ said he, ‘I’ve let them go at six hundred for the three!’ I soon managed to get the address of the receiver who had them, on promising him that there would be no prosecution. Off I set to him, and after much chaffering I got our stones at £1000 apiece. Then I looked in upon your son, told him that all was right, and eventually got to my bed about two o’clock, after what I may call a really hard day’s work.”
“何止如此，您还在这一天挽救了英格兰，让它逃脱了一桩震惊公众的巨大丑闻[^10]。”银行家一边说，一边站起身来。“先生，我没法用言辞表达我心里的谢意，可您千万不要误会，以为我会对您所做的一切不知感激。实实在在地说，您的本领比我听过的所有传闻还要高明。不过，眼下我必须火速赶到我亲爱的孩子身边，为我让他蒙受的冤屈向他道歉。至于可怜的玛丽，听了您说的那些事情，我真的是伤透了心。您的本领虽然非常地了不起，恐怕也没法让我知道她现在的下落吧。” “A day which has saved England from a great public scandal,” said the banker, rising. “Sir, I cannot find the words to thank you, but you shall not find me ungrateful for what you have done. Your skill has indeed exceeded all that I have heard of it. And now I must fly to my dear boy to apologise to him for the wrong which I done him. As to what you tell me of poor Mary, it goes to my very heart. Not even your skill can inform me where she is now.”
“依我看，咱们可以十拿九稳地说，”福尔摩斯回答道，“乔治·伯恩韦尔爵士在哪里，她也就在哪里。同样十拿九稳的是，不管她犯的是什么样的过错，加倍的惩罚都会迅速来临。” “I think that we may safely say,”returned Holmes,“that she is wherever Sir George Burnwell is. It is equally certain, too, that whatever her sins are, they will soon receive a more than sufficient punishment.”
