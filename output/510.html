一个非洲故事 An African Story
作者：欧内斯特·海明威 by Ernest Hemingway
他在静候着月亮升起，抚摩着基博，让它别出声，手下感受到狗的毛发都竖了起来。他和狗都注视着，倾听着，直到月亮升起，投射出他们的影子。 He was waiting for the moon to rise and he felt Kibo’s hair rise under his hand as he stroked him to be quiet and they both watched and listened as the moon came up and gave them shadows.
他搂着狗的脖子，能感觉到它浑身在颤抖。 His arm was around the dog’s neck now and he could feel him shivering.
所有夜籁都已静止。 All of the night sounds had stopped.
他们没有听到大象的声音。直到狗转过头，差点儿撞到他，戴维才发现了大象。 They did not hear the elephant and David did not see him until the dog turned his head and seemed to settle into David.
随即，大象的影子笼罩住他们，悄无声息地从他们面前走过。他们从山上吹来的微风中闻到了大象的气味。 Then the elephant’s shadow covered them and he moved past making no noise at all and they smelled him in the light wind that came down from the mountain.
那气味很浓烈，陈腐酸臭。当它走过去之后，戴维发现它左边的象牙长得似乎快碰到地面了。 He smelled strong but old and sour and when he was past David saw that the left tusk was so long it seemed to reach the ground.
他们等了一会儿，但是再没有别的大象经过，于是戴维和狗开始在月光下奔跑起来。 They waited but no other elephants came by and then David and the dog started off running in the moonlight.
狗紧紧地跟在戴维的脚后，以至于他一停，狗鼻子就撞上了他的膝盖弯。 The dog kept close behind him and when David stopped the dog pressed his muzzle into the back of his knee.
戴维非得再看一眼那头公象，终于他们在森林边上赶上了它。 David had to see the bull again and they came up on him at the edge of the forest.
大象正朝着山的方向行进，迎着徐徐晚风缓缓而行。 He was traveling toward the mountain and slowly moving into the steady night breeze.
戴维走得很近，以至于又被大象的身影笼罩。他又闻到了那陈年的酸臭味，但就是看不到右边的象牙。 David came close enough to see him cut off the moon again and to smell the sour oldness but he could not see the right tusk.
他不敢带着狗继续靠近，于是顺着风向把狗带回到一棵树脚下，强迫它蹲下，试图让它领会其中的意思。 He was afraid to work closer with the dog and he took him back with the wind and pushed him down against the base of a tree and tried to make him understand.
他想那狗应该会呆在那里的，那狗也确实留下了，可当他朝那头公象走去的时候，却又感觉到狗湿湿的鼻头撞到了他的膝弯。 He thought the dog would stay and he did but when David moved up toward the bulk of the elephant again he felt the wet muzzle against the hollow of his knee.
他俩跟随着大象，直到来到树林中的一块空地上。 The two of them followed the elephant until he came to an opening in the trees.
大象站在那里甩动着它的大耳朵。 He stood there moving his huge ears.
庞大的身躯罩在树影里，但是月光仍然照在了它的头上。 His bulk was in the shadow but the moonlight would be on his head.
戴维来到大象身后，用手轻轻地把狗的嘴巴合上，然后屏住呼吸，侧身擦着迎面的晚风缓缓地向大象的右边移动，感觉到风拂面而过。戴维紧贴着大象的身躯前移，几乎不留一点儿空隙，终于看到大象的头和缓慢甩动的巨大耳朵。 David reached behind him and closed the dog’s jaws gently with his hand and then moved softly and unbreathing to his right along the edge of the night breeze, feeling it on his cheek, edging with it, never letting it get between him and the bulk until he could see the elephant’s head and the great ears slowly moving.
它右边的象牙就和戴维的大腿一样粗，向下弯曲几乎要触到地面了。 The right tusk was as thick as his own thigh and it curved down almost to the ground.
戴维和基博退了回来，这时风都吹到脖子上了。他们沿原路返回走出森林，来到了狩猎区空旷的野地。 He and Kibo moved back, the wind on his neck now, and they back-tracked out of the forest and into the open park country.
这个时候，狗跑在了戴维的前面，然后在两支猎矛处停了下来，这猎矛是戴维在跟随大象足迹时留下的。 The dog was ahead of him now and he stopped where David had left the two hunting spears by the trail when they had followed the elephant.
他提起猎矛上的皮圈皮套，往肩上一背，手上拿着从不离手的、最称心的长矛，带上狗开始循着大象的足迹向庄地行进。 He swung them over his shoulder in their thong and leather cup harness and, with his best spear that he had kept with him all the time in his hand, they started on the trail for the shamba.
月亮已经升得很高了，为什么庄地却没有鼓声？他感到很疑惑。 The moon was high now and he wondered why there was no drumming from the shamba.
如果父亲在那里却没有鼓声，那就很奇怪了。 Something was strange if his father was there and there was no drumming.
当他们再次找到象迹时，戴维已感觉浑身疲惫。 David had felt the tiredness as soon as they had picked up the trail again.
一直以来他比两个大人的体力都好，更精力充沛，看到他们慢步行进，父亲每小时都定点停息，戴维总是不耐烦。 For a long time he had been fresher and in better shape than the two men and impatient with their slow trailing and the regular halts his father made each hour on the hour.
他本可以走得比朱玛和他父亲快很多，但当他开始感到疲累的时候，他们却一如往常，面不改色，中午也只是照常休息了五分钟，并且他发现朱玛的步伐似乎加快了一些。 He could have moved ahead much faster than Juma and his father but when he started to tire they were the same as ever and at noon they took only the usual five-minute rest and he had seen that Juma was increasing the pace a little.
或许他并没有加快。 Perhaps he wasn’t.
或许只是看上去快了些，但是此时的象粪已经新鲜多了，尽管摸上去没有一点儿热气。 Perhaps it had only seemed faster but the elephant dung was fresher now although it was not warm yet to the touch.
当他们来到最后一堆象粪前时，朱玛把步枪交给戴维背。可过了一个钟头，朱玛看了看他，又把枪拿了回去。 Juma gave him the rifle to carry after they came upon the last pile of dung but after an hour he looked at him and took it back.
他们本是沿着一个山坡平稳地向上爬，但是现在象迹向下延伸去，透过森林的缝隙，他看到前面起伏不平的冈峦。 They had been climbing steadily across a slope of the mountain but now the trail went down and from a gap in the forest he saw broken country ahead.
“戴维，从这儿开始路就难走了。” “Here’s where the tough part starts, Davey.”
父亲说道。 his father said.
他这才意识到，当他带他们找到象迹的时候，他就应该被打发回庄地的。 It was then he knew that he should have been sent back to the shamba once he had put them on the trail.
这一点朱玛早就知道了。 Juma had known it for a long time.
他的父亲现在也明白了，但却也无计可施。 His father knew it now and there was nothing to be done.
他又犯了个错误，现在除了冒险也别无选择。 It was another of his mistakes and there was nothing to do now except gamble.
戴维低头看着那又圆又大的、扁平的大象足迹，只见欧洲蕨都被踩倒了，还有一颗踏断的杂草都快要干枯了。 David looked down at the big flattened circle of the print of the elephant’s foot and saw where the bracken had been pressed down and where a broken stem of a weed was drying.
朱玛捡起杂草，然后看了看太阳。 Juma picked it up and looked at the sun.
他把断草递给了戴维的父亲，戴维的父亲将草绕在了手指上。 Juma handed the broken weed to David’s father and his father rolled it in his fingers.
戴维注意到草茎上的白花都蔫掉了，快要死了。 David noticed the white flowers that were drooped and dying.
但是它们还没有被太阳晒枯，花瓣也没有掉落。 But they still had not dried in the sun nor shed their petals.
“太好了，”他父亲说， “It’s going to be a bitch,” his father said.
“我们快跟上。” “Let’s get going.”
直到傍晚时分他们仍然在崎岖的山路上追踪着。 Late in the afternoon they were still tracking through the broken country.
戴维已经昏昏欲睡很久了，当他看向那两个大人时，他意识到困倦才是他真正的敌人。他跟随着他们的步伐，努力地挪着脚步，以此来驱逐令他变得迟钝的睡意。 He had been sleepy now for a long time and as he watched the two men he knew that sleepiness was his real enemy and he followed their pace and tried to move through and out of the sleep that deadened him.
两个大人每小时轮流替换在前追寻象迹，后面的人每隔一定时间就回头看看他有没有跟上。 The two men relieved each other tracking on the hour and the one who was in second place looked back at him at regular intervals to check if he was with them.
夜晚，当他们在这森林的无水地露营时，戴维一坐下来就睡着了，醒来时就看到朱玛拎着鹿皮靴，抚摸着光脚看有没有水泡。 When they made a dry camp at dark in the forest he went to sleep as soon as he sat down and woke with Juma holding his moccasins and feeling his bare feet for blisters.
父亲已经将外套盖在了他身上，正坐在他旁边，手里拿着一块冷的熟肉和两块饼干， His father had spread his coat over him and was sitting by him with a piece of cold cooked meat and two biscuits.
递给他一个装着冷茶的水瓶。 He offered him a water bottle with cold tea.
“大象也要吃东西的，戴维，”父亲说， “He’ll have to feed, Davey,” his father said.
“你的脚没事。 “Your feet are in good shape.
就和朱玛的一样结实。 They’re as sound as Juma’s.
你慢慢吃，然后喝点儿茶再睡觉吧。 Eat this slowly and drink some tea and go to sleep again.
我们不会有什么问题的。” We haven’t any problems.”
“很抱歉，我实在是太困了。” “I’m sorry I was so sleepy.”
“昨晚你和基博寻找象迹奔走了整个晚上。 “You and Kibo hunted and traveled all last night.
怎么会不累呢？ Why shouldn’t you be sleepy?
你想吃的话就多吃点儿肉吧。” You can have a little more meat if you want it.”
“我不饿。” “I’m not hungry.”
“好吧。 “Good.
我们可以坚持三天。 We’re good for three days.
明天又可以找到水源了。 We’ll hit water again tomorrow.
山上有充足的泉水。” Plenty of creeks come off the mountain.”
“大象是要去哪呢？” “Where’s he going?”
“朱玛觉得他大概知道。” “Juma thinks he knows.”
“那情况糟糕吗？” “Isn’t it bad?”
“不会很糟糕的，戴维。” “Not too bad, Davey.”
“我又想睡觉了，”戴维说道， “I’m going back to sleep,” David had said.
“我不需要你的外套。” “I don’t need your coat.”
“我和朱玛能对付，”父亲说道， “Juma and I are all right,” his father said.
“你知道，我睡觉总是不怕冷的。” “I always sleep warm you know.”
父亲还没来得及说晚安，戴维他就睡着了。 David was asleep even before his father said good night.
后来他又醒了一次，发现月光洒在自己的脸上，他想起了大象在森林中的场景：煽动着巨耳，头因象牙的重量而往下垂着。 Then he woke once with the moonlight on his face and he thought of the elephant with his great ears moving as he stood in the forest, his head hung down with the weight of the tusks.
戴维觉得夜里想到大象时所感觉到的空虚，是由于睡醒时的饥饿造成的。 David thought then in the night that the hollow way he felt as he remembered him was from waking hungry.
但事实并非如此，他在接下来的三天中才意识到。 But it was not and he found that out in the next three days.
第二天情况很糟糕，因为还远没到中午，他就意识到大人跟孩子的差别不仅仅是需要多睡会儿的问题。 The next day was very bad because long before noon he knew that it was not just the need for sleep that made the difference between a boy and men.
头三个小时戴维比他们有精神，他请求朱玛将点三零三口径的枪给他背，但是朱玛摇了摇头。 For the first three hours he was fresher than they were and he asked Juma for the .303 rifle to carry but Juma shook his head.
脸上没有一丝笑容，他可一直是戴维最好的朋友，还教会了戴维打猎。 He did not smile and he had always been David’s best friend and had taught him to hunt.
戴维心想，昨天他还把枪交给我背了，而且我今天的精神比昨天好多了。 He offered it to me yesterday, David thought, and I’m in better shape today than I was then.
他的精神的确是好多了，但是才到十点他也就意识到今天可能跟昨天一样糟，甚至比昨天更糟。 He was, too, but by ten o’clock he knew the day would be as bad or worse than the day before.
对他来说，想跟上父亲的步伐，就跟他想和父亲干上一架一样，是个十分愚蠢的想法。 It was as silly for him to think that he could trail with his father as to think he could fight with him.
他也知道那并不仅仅是因为他们是大人。 He knew too that it was not just that they were men.
他们是专业猎人，他现在知道为什么朱玛连一个笑容都很吝啬了。 They were professional hunters and he knew now that was why Juma would not even waste a smile.
他们知道大象的一举一动，不用言语，只需用手指一指就能让对方知道是大象的足迹，当追踪变得困难时，父亲总是听朱玛的。 They knew everything the elephant had done, pointed out the signs of it to each other without speaking, and when the tracking became difficult his father always yielded to Juma.
当他们在泉边停下装水时，父亲说：“只要够今天喝就行了，戴维。” When they stopped to fill the water bottles at a stream his father said, “Just last the day out, Davey.”
后来崎岖的山路终于走完了，他们开始向森林攀爬，这时，大象的足迹转向了右边，通到一条旧的象迹上。 Then when they were past the broken country and climbing toward the forest the tracks of the elephant turned off to the right onto an old elephant trail.
他看到朱玛和父亲在说着什么，当他走近时，朱玛回头看了看他们来时的路，然后又望了望远处无水地带几座如僻岩孤岛般的小山，似乎正在以远处地平线上的三座青山顶来测定方位。 He saw his father and Juma talking and when he got up to them Juma was looking back over the way they had come and then at a far distant stony island of hills in the dry country and seemed to be taking a bearing of this against the peaks of three far blue hills on the horizon.
“现在朱玛知道大象往哪儿去了，”父亲解释说， “Juma knows where he’s going now,” his father explained.
“他本来以为自己知道，但是大象却转向绕了这一大圈。” “He thought he knew before but then he dropped down into this stuff.”
父亲回头望了望他们整整一天所走过的路。 He looked back at the country they had come through all day.
“现在大象所走的路比较平坦好走了，但是我们还得爬坡。” “Where he’s headed now is pretty good going but we’ll have to climb.”
他们一直爬着山坡，直到天黑，然后又就地露营。 They climbed until it was dark and then made another dry camp.
就在日落前不久，一小群鹧鸪从象径上横穿而过，戴维用弹弓射中了两只。 David killed two spur fowl with his slingshot out of a small flock that had walked across the trail just before the sunset.
那群胖墩墩的鹧鸪潇洒地踏上了老象径，整齐阔步地走着，扬起了阵阵尘土。其中一只小鹧鸪的背被石子打断了，它拍打着翅膀开始挣扎扑腾，另一只鹧鸪跑过来想用嘴救它，戴维又装上一颗石子，往后一拉，打中这只鹧鸪的肋骨。 The birds had come into the old elephant trail to dust, walking neatly and plumply, and when the pebble broke the back of one and the bird began to jerk and toss with its wings thumping, another bird ran forward to peck at it and David pouched another pebble and pulled it back and sent it against the ribs of the second bird.
当他跑过去捡的时候，其他的鹧鸪呼呼地飞走了。 As he ran forward to put his hand on it the other birds whirred off.
朱玛回过头来看了看，这次还露出了笑容。戴维捡起那两只鸟，都是胖墩墩、热乎乎的，羽毛还很平整。他用猎刀刀柄敲了几下鸟的脑袋。 Juma had looked back and smiled this time and David picked up the two birds, warm and plump and smoothly feathered, and knocked their heads against the handle of his hunting knife.
到了他们夜晚露营的地方，父亲说：“我从来没见过这么大的鹧鸪。 Now where they were camped for the night his father said, “I’ve never seen that type of francolin quite so high.
你能连中两只，干得真不错！” You did very well to get a double on them.”
朱玛将鹧鸪串在枝条上，放在很小的炭火上烤。 Juma cooked the birds spitted on a stick over the coals of a very small fire.
他和父亲躺在那里，看着朱玛烤鹧鸪，父亲往长颈瓶瓶盖里倒了些威士忌，惨了点儿水，在那里喝着。 His father drank a whiskey and water from the cup top on his flask as they lay and watched Juma cook.
后来朱玛把胸脯肉和鹧鸪心分给他们一人一份，自己吃了两份颈背和鹧鸪腿。 Afterward Juma gave them each a breast with the heart in it and ate the two necks and backs and the legs himself.
“这可帮了大忙，戴维，”父亲说道， “It makes a great difference, Davey,” his father said.
“这下我们的干粮就很宽裕了。” “We’re very well off on rations now.”
“我们离大象还有多远？” “How far are we behind him?”
戴维问道。 David asked.
“已经很近了，”父亲说， “We’re quite close,” his father said.
“这要看大象在月亮出来后还走不走。 “it depends on whether he travels when the moon comes up.
今天晚上月亮上山比昨天晚一个钟头，比你找到它那天晚了两个钟头。” It’s an hour later tonight and two hours later than when you found him.”
“为什么朱玛会认为他知道大象的去向呢？” “Why does Juma think he knows where he’s going?”
“他就在离这儿不远的地方打伤过大象，而且还杀死了它的‘部下’。” “He wounded him and killed his askari not too far from here.”
“什么时候？” “When?”
“他说是五年前， “Five years ago, he says.
也可能是另外的某个时候。 That may mean anytime.
他说那时你还是一个娃娃。” When you were still a toto he says.”
“从那时起他就没再见过那头大象吗？” “Has he been alone since then?”
“他是这样说的， “He says so.
再也没有见过它， He hasn’t seen him.
只是听说过它。” Only heard of him.”
“他说那头大象有多大？” “How big does he say he is?”
“接近两百磅。 “Close to two hundred.
比我所见过的所有动物都大。 Bigger than anything I’ve ever seen.
他说比这还大的大象只有过一只，也出没在这附近。” He says there’s only been one greater elephant and he came from near here too.”
“我还是睡觉吧，”戴维说， “I’d better get to sleep,” David said.
“希望我明天会好些。” “I hope I’ll be better tomorrow.”
“你今天已经做得很好了，”父亲说， “You were splendid today,” his father said.
“我真为你感到骄傲！ “I was very proud of you.
朱玛也一样！” So was Juma.”
夜晚，当他醒来时，月亮已经升得很高了，他确信他们并不为自己感到骄傲，也许只是除了他敏捷地抓住了两只鹧鸪之外。 In the night when he woke after the moon was up he was sure they were not proud of him except perhaps for his dexterity in killing the two birds.
他在夜里发现了大象，跟踪它，看到了它的两只大象牙都还在，然后回来找到了父亲和朱玛，带着他们踏上了象径。 He had found the elephant at night and followed him to see that he had both of his tusks and then returned to find the two men and put them on the trail.
戴维知道他们也为那也让他们感到骄傲。 David knew they were proud of that.
然而艰难的追踪一开始，他就成了他们的累赘，甚至会对他们的成功构成威胁，就像他夜里想靠近大象时，基博会妨碍他一样。而且他知道他们两个肯定都在埋怨自己，在可以让他回去的时候没有叫他回去。 But once the deadly following started he was useless to them and a danger to their success just as Kibo had been to him when he had gone up close to the elephant in the night, and he knew they must each have hated themselves for not having sent him back when there was time.
那头大象的每只象牙都有两百磅重。 The tusks of the elephant weighed two hundred pounds apiece.
当象牙长到超过一定长度以后，那头大象就因为它的牙而一直遭到猎杀。而现在他们三个想杀它，也就是为了象牙。 Ever since these tusks had grown beyond their normal size the elephant had been hunted for them and now the three of them would kill him for them.
戴维确信他们这次会杀了它，因为戴维他已经坚持了一整天，尽管在快到中午的时候自己已经累垮了，他还是紧跟着他们的步子。 David was sure that they would kill him now because he, David, had lasted through the day and kept up after the pace had destroyed him by noon.
所以他们可能也因为他坚持下来了而感到骄傲吧。 So they probably were proud of him doing that.
但是他对这次猎杀行动毫无用处，况且如果没有他，他们可能会做得好得多。 But he had brought nothing useful to the hunt and they would have been far better off without him.

那天晚上，当戴维坐在火堆旁，望着脸上缝了许多针、肋骨也断了几根的朱玛，心里寻思着，大象是不是认出了朱玛，所以才想干掉他呢？ That evening as David had sat by the fire he had looked at Juma with his stitched-up face and his broken ribs and wondered if the elephant had recognized him when he had tried to kill him.
他希望如此。 He hoped he had.
现在大象成了他心目中的英雄了，正如长久以来父亲在他心中的英雄形象一样。他心想：那头大象已经那么老，那么累，还敢这么做，简直难以置信。 The elephant was his hero now as his father had been for a long time and he had thought, I didn’t believe he could do it when he was so old and tired.
它本来是可以杀死朱玛的。 He would have killed Juma, too.
但是它看我的眼神看里，似乎并没有要伤害我的意思。 But he didn’t look at me as though he wanted to kill me.
它只是和我一样流露出难过的样子。 He only looked sad the same way I felt.
在临死的那天，它还看望了它的老朋友。 He visited his old friend on the day he died.
戴维不会忘记，那头大象一旦失去生命的活力，它那副尊贵的威严也就随之消逝了。他也不会忘记，等到他和父亲找到背包回来时，大象已经开始变肿，即便是在这凉爽的夜。 David remembered how the elephant lost all dignity as soon as his eye had ceased to be alive and how when his father and he had returned with the packs the elephant had already started to swell, even in the cool evening.
哪里还看得出那是头大象啊！剩下的只是它那皮皱肉肿的尸体和那黄褐斑斑的大象牙，正是这对象牙害它送了命。 There was no more true elephant; only the gray wrinkled swelling dead body and the huge mottled brown and yellow tusks that they had killed him for.
粘在象牙上的血迹已经凝固了。他像刮干了的火漆一样，用拇指指甲刮了点儿血迹下来，放进了衬衣的口袋里。 The tusks were stained with dried blood and he scraped some off with his thumbnail like a dried piece of sealing wax and put it in the pocket of his shirt.
除了刮下来的这些血块，他什么也没要，倒是这大象给了他一种孤寂之感。 That was all he took from the elephant except the beginning of the knowledge of loneliness.
那晚，宰割取完牙后，父亲在火堆旁想要和他说说话。 After the butchery his father tried to talk to him that night by the fire.
“戴维，大象是个杀人狂，”他说， “He was a murderer you know, Davey,” he had said.
“朱玛说谁也不知道它到底害死了多少人。” “Juma says nobody knows how many people he has killed.”
“他们不都想猎杀它吗？” “They were all trying to kill him, weren’t they?”
“那当然，”父亲说道，“那对象牙谁都想要。” “Naturally,” his father had said, “with that pair of tusks.”
“那怎么能说它爱杀人呢？!” “How could he be a murderer then?”
“你爱怎么想就怎么想吧，”父亲说， “Just as you like,” his father had said.
“我很遗憾你对它的看法这么糊涂。” “I’m sorry you got so mixed up about him.”
“我真希望它那时能杀了朱玛。”戴维说。 “I wish he’d killed Juma,” David said.
“我觉得你说得有点儿过分了，”父亲说道， “I think that’s carrying it a little far,” his father said.
“要知道，朱玛是你的好朋友。” “Juma’s your friend, you know.”
“他再也不是我的朋友了。” “Not any more.”
“这种话可别对他说！” “No need to tell him so.”
“不说他也知道。”戴维说。 “He knows it,” David had said.
“我看你是误会他了，”父亲说。谈到这里，也就没再说下去了。 “I think you misjudge him,” his father said and they had left it there.
后来，经过种种波折，他们终于带着象牙安全地回来了。两只大象牙在泥糊枝编的房子外靠着墙。象牙尖头挨着尖头斜靠在一起，那么高，那么粗，人们用手摸着都不敢相信。两个尖头上都有一个向里的弯，谁也够不到那弯的顶，就连他的父亲也不行。顿时，朱玛和他们父子俩都成了英雄，基博也成了英雄的狗，连那些扛象牙的人也都成了英雄。他们本来就已经微醉了，后来醉得更厉害了。也就在这时，父亲说：“戴维，我们和解好吗？” Then when they were finally back safely with the tusks after all the things that had happened and the tusks were propped against the wall of the stick and mud house, leaning there with their points touching, the tusks so tall and thick that no one could believe them even when they touched them and no one, not even his father, could reach to the top of the bend where they curved in for the points to meet, there when Juma and his father and he were heroes and Kibo was a hero’s dog and the men who had carried the tusks were heroes, already slightly drunk heroes and to be drunker, his father had said, “Do you want to make peace, Davey?”
“好吧。”他说。因为他知道，这是他决定再也不把心里话告诉别人的开始。 “All right,” he said because he knew this was the start of the never telling that he had decided on.
“太好了，”父亲说， “I’m so glad,” his father said.
“这样事情就简单多了，感觉也好多了。” “It’s so much simpler and better.”
于是，他们坐在无花果树树阴下的长者凳上喝起啤酒来。大象牙依然在茅屋的墙上靠着，他们用葫芦杯喝着酒，那些酒杯是一个姑娘和她的弟弟送来的，他们可是为英雄而服务的仆人，也跟英雄的那头神犬一起坐在地上，那位英雄养了一只老公鸡，那只老公鸡也被升格为英雄心爱的大雄鸡。 Then they sat on old men’s stools under the shade of the fig tree with the tusks against the wall of the hut and drank beer from gourd cups that were brought by a young girl and her younger brother, the servant of heroes, sitting in the dust by the heroic dog of a hero who held an old cockerel, newly promoted to the standing of the heroes’ favorite rooster.
他们就坐在那里喝着啤酒，大鼓开始敲起来了，恩戈麦鼓也擂了起来。 They sat there and drank beer while the big drum started and the ngoma began to build.
