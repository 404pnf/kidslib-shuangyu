晚到的骑兵 Enter a Dragoon
作者：托马斯·哈代 by Thomas Hardy
不久前，我经历了一件不太愉快的事情（这是一位先生的讲述，他可以为这个故事的真实性负责）。 I lately had a melancholy experience (said the gentleman who is answerable for the truth of this story).
那天，我去察看一座即将不复存在的房屋。长久以来，我很熟悉那座房子的外观，由于它年久失修，下个星期就要被拆除了。 It was that of going over a doomed house with whose outside aspect I had long been familiar—a house, that is, which by reason of age and dilapidation was to be pulled down during the following week.
破破烂烂的茅草屋顶有如干蘑菇的褐色菌褶。事实上，在我走到这座房子这里之前，那屋顶已被拆掉了一部分。 Some of the thatch, brown and rotten as the gills of old mushrooms, had, indeed, been removed before I walked over the building.
这房子非常小，人们往往称这样的房子为“农家小屋”。它坐落在一个僻静的小村庄里，最多不过有一百年的历史。因此，当我走过一间间空荡荡的屋子，看到龟裂的墙壁和倾斜的地板时，不禁开始猜测，这里发生过多少家庭变故——只算我知道的那些。 Seeing that it was only a very small house—which is usually called a ‘cottage-residence’—situated in a remote hamlet, and that it was not more than a hundred years old, if so much, I was led to think in my progress through the hollow rooms, with their cracked walls and sloping floors, what an exceptional number of abrupt family incidents had taken place therein—to reckon only those which had come to my own knowledge.
毫无疑问，还有许多变故是我闻所未闻的。 And no doubt there were many more of which I had never heard.
房子坐落在果园的尽头，果园一直延伸到街边。小街两侧是一片隐蔽的民居，属于麦尔斯托克教区。 It stood at the top of a garden stretching down to the lane or street that ran through a hermit-group of dwellings in Mellstock parish.
果园的较矮入口的大门是绿色的，门上方的荆棘树篱由于常常修剪，形成了一个拱顶。一条碎石小径向上延伸到房子前门，两侧是曾修剪得整整齐齐的覆盆子、草莓以及蔬菜地。 From a green gate at the lower entrance, over which the thorn hedge had been shaped to an arch by constant clippings, a gravel path ascended between the box edges of once trim raspberry, strawberry, and vegetable plots, towards the front door.
矮门的绿漆旧得泛白，用手指就能蹭掉。门上有一只年代久远的黄铜小门环，门环的缝隙间覆满了铜锈。 This was in colour an ancient and bleached green that could be rubbed off with the finger, and it bore a small long-featured brass knocker covered with verdigris in its crevices.
近些年来，这座宅子日益破败，被分成两间，供田里干活的人居住，直至如今面临被拆除的命运。但在它的全盛时期，这座房子毫无疑问是整洁、漂亮而优雅的。 For some years before this eve of demolition the homestead had degenerated, and been divided into two tenements to serve as cottages for farm labourers; but in its prime it had indisputable claim to be considered neat, pretty, and genteel.
前面提到的种种变故，主要是由于先后租住在这里的家庭与当地的普通家庭有所不同。而当地人大都乐天知命，任由命运安排自己的境遇、地位和出身。 The variety of incidents above alluded to was mainly owing to the nature of the tenure, whereby the place had been occupied by families not quite of the kind customary in such spots—people whose circumstances, position, or antecedents were more or less of a critical happy-go-lucky cast.
我想讲的这个故事就是关于其中一家的。这家的主人雅各布·帕多克先生是个果农，他和妻子以及成年的女儿在这里住过一些年头。 And of these residents the family whose term comprised the story I wish to relate was that of Mr. Jacob Paddock the market-gardener, who dwelt there for some years with his wife and grown-up daughter.
一 I
帕多克家显然正处于骚动之中，房子前边的菜地传出忙乱的声音，如同受到惊扰的蜂巢。 An evident commotion was agitating the premises, which jerked busy sounds across the front plot, resembling those of a disturbed hive.
每个出现在门口的家庭成员，都是一副心不在焉、忧心忡忡的神情。 If a member of the household appeared at the door it was with a countenance of abstraction and concern.
夜幕降临了。小村庄里的其他居民纷纷出来提水，村里公用的水井就在帕多克家房子以及果园对面的那条公路上。 Evening began to bend over the scene; and the other inhabitants of the hamlet came out to draw water, their common well being in the public road opposite the garden and house of the Paddocks.
村民们把各自的桶装满水摇上来后，依然流连在井边，意味深长地交谈着。 Having wound up their bucketsfull respectively they lingered, and spoke significantly together.
从他们的谈话中，任何无心的听者都能推断出发生了什么事。 From their words any casual listener might have gathered information of what had occurred.
故事的大半内容是由住得离当事人最近的伐木工讲述的。 The woodman who lived nearest the site of the story told most of the tale.
对面帕多克夫妇的女儿塞利娜，这天下午意外地收到了昔日未婚夫的来信。那小伙子从前是个下士，如今是一名骑兵军士长，而塞利娜一直以为他已在两三年前的阿尔马河战役中阵亡了。 Selina, the daughter of the Paddocks opposite, had been surprised that afternoon by receiving a letter from her once intended husband, then a corporal, but now a sergeant-major of dragoons, whom she had hitherto supposed to be one of the slain in the Battle of the Alma two or three years before.
“大伙儿都知道，她不顾她爹的意思选了他，那时候他连军衔都没有哩，”“情报员”伐木工继续说道， ‘She picked up wi’en against her father’s wish, as we know, and before he got his stripes,’ their informant continued.
“不过，他倒跟咱们伦敦南边的小伙子一样，是个实诚人。 ‘Not but that the man was as hearty a feller as you’d meet this side o’ London.
可是你们也知道，雅各布想让她找个更好的，这个可以理解。 But Jacob, you see, wished her to do better, and one can understand it.
可她那时候下定决心非要跟他在一起。后来发生的事情也不能全怪她，俩人眼看就要结婚了，结果打起仗来了，一切都完了。” However, she was determined to stick to him at that time; and for what happened she was not much to blame, so near as they were to matrimony when the war broke out and spoiled all.’
“就连婚礼用的猪都杀好了，”一个女人说道，“啤酒也订了一桶。 ‘Even the very pig had been killed for the wedding,’ said a woman, ‘and the barrel o’ beer ordered in.
唉，那小伙子倒是真心实意的。 O, the man meant honourable enough.
可他两天之后就要去国外打仗了，她爹让他们等他回来再结婚，也是很自然的事儿。” But to be off in two days to fight in a foreign country—’twas natural of her father to say they should wait till he got back.’
“可他再也没回来。”树阴下有个人咕哝道。 ‘And he never came,’ murmured one in the shade.
“仗打完了，可她男人再也没露面。 ‘The war ended but her man never turned up again.
她不确定他是不是死了，又不肯去找他，不是因为好面子，就是因为不敢。” She was not sure he was killed, but was too proud, or too timid, to go and hunt for him.’
“她爹弄明白是怎么回事儿之后，倒是原谅了她。他当时明明白白说了，原谅她的一个原因就是他挺喜欢那小伙子，看得出他是真心实意的。 ‘One reason why her father forgave her when he found out how matters stood was, as he said plain at the time, that he liked the man, and could see that he meant to act straight.
所以尽管于事无补，老夫妇俩还是尽力而为，把闺女留在身边。别的爹妈可不一定做得到这一点。 So the old folks made the best of what they couldn’t mend, and kept her there with ’em, when some wouldn’t.
时间证明他好像确实是认真的，因为他写信给她说他要回来了。 Time has proved seemingly that he did mean to act straight, now that he has writ to her that he’s coming.
我觉得，要不是冒出来另一个人，她肯定一直对他忠心耿耿的呢。” She’d have stuck to him all through the time, ’tis my belief; if t’other hadn’t come along.’
“他追求她的时候，”伐木工继续说道，“他的兵团正驻扎在卡斯特桥兵营。他要花一便士买她爹果园里那棵苹果树上早熟的苹果，他俩就这样认识了——不过据说他看见苹果的时候，就发现篱笆后面的她了。 ‘At the time of the courtship,’ resumed the woodman, ‘the regiment was quartered in Casterbridge Barracks, and he and she got acquainted by his calling to buy a penn’orth of rathe-ripes off that tree yonder in her father’s orchard—though ’twas said he seed her over hedge as well as the apples.
他说他很喜欢这种苹果，于是就每天来买一便士的，直到树上的果子都被摘光。 He declared ’twas a kind of apple he much fancied; and he called for a penn’orth every day till the tree was cleared.
然后，他就开始追求她了。” It ended in his calling for her.’
“真遗憾他们没马上结婚，结果什么都完了。” ‘’Twas a thousand pities they didn’t jine up at once and ha’ done wi’ it.
“唉，都说晚来总比不来好，要真是这样，他现在还是能娶她的。 ‘Well; better late than never, if so be he’ll have her now.
可是，天哪，当年他没回来，她就确信他死了，像咱们教堂墓地里最底下埋着的人一样，确确实实死了。 But, Lord, she’d that faith in ’en that she’d no more belief that he was alive, when a’ didn’t come, than that the undermost man in our churchyard was alive.
要不是这样，她心里也绝对不会有别人——唉，绝不会！” She’d never have thought of another but for that—O no!’
“总之，现在情况对她来说有点儿尴尬。” ‘’Tis awkward, altogether, for her now.’
“她还没有跟另一个人成婚。 ‘Still she hadn’t married wi’ the new man.
不过，他们下周本来就要结婚了，据说连结婚证都领到了。她这回不发结婚公告了，因为上次太倒霉了。” Though to be sure she would have committed it next week, even the licence being got, they say, for she’d have no banns this time, the first being so unfortunate.’
“没准儿军士长会觉得这是个解脱，然后就从哪儿来回哪儿去了呢。” ‘Perhaps the sergeant-major will think he’s released, and go as he came.’
“唉，我估计不会。 ‘O, not as I reckon.
士兵尤其不会这样想，再说她还是那么漂亮的一个小可人儿。 Soldiers bain’t particular, and she’s a tidy piece o’ furniture still.
她肯定会选择士兵，跟那个造车轮的师傅分手，什么结婚证不结婚证的——她要不这样，我倒奇怪了。” What will happen is that she’ll have her soldier, and break off with the master-wheelwright, licence or no—daze me if she won’t.’
正当大家胡乱猜测之时，又一个乡邻的身影从暗处出现了。 In the progress of these desultory conjectures the form of another neighbour arose in the gloom.
她一边穿过帕多克先生家的大门，朝他家房门走去，一边冲井边的人们点点头。人们纷纷回应：“晚上好，斯通太太。” She nodded to the people at the well, who replied ‘G’d night, Mrs. Stone,’ as she passed through Mr. Paddock’s gate towards his door.
斯通太太是帕多克一家的好朋友。人们目送她走上小径，经过窗子。那些窗子已被屋内的蜡烛照得透亮。 She was an intimate friend of the latter’s household, and the group followed her with their eyes up the path and past the windows, which were now lighted up by candles inside.
二 II
斯通夫人在门前停下，敲敲门，塞利娜的母亲给她开了门，马上把她迎进左边的客厅，那里摆放着一张餐桌，不过晚餐还没有完全备好。 Mrs. Stone paused at the door, knocked, and was admitted by Selina’s mother, who took her visitor at once into the parlour on the left hand, where a table was partly spread for supper.
客厅的布置十分平常，唯一能吸引当地陌生来客眼球的大概就是墙边“食柜”上摆着的一样东西了。那是一个葡萄干大蛋糕，像奇珍异宝似的被玻璃罩保护着。玻璃罩是博物馆里常见的那种，呈方形，背面是木质的，一般用来装有珍稀羽毛或毛皮的动物标本。 On the ‘beaufet’ against the wall stood probably the only object which would have attracted the eye of a local stranger in an otherwise ordinarily furnished room, a great plum-cake guarded as if it were a curiosity by a glass shade of the kind seen in museums—square, with a wooden back like those enclosing stuffed specimens of rare feather or fur.
这个已经风化的蛋糕原来是为塞利娜和那位士兵的婚宴准备的。尽管后来发生了不幸，塞利娜还是虔诚而静心地把这个蛋糕保存了起来，以证明自己的心意。我们后面会讲到她的不幸。 This was the mummy of the cake intended in earlier days for the wedding-feast of Selina and the soldier, which had been religiously and lovingly preserved by the former as a testimony to her intentional respectability in spite of an untoward subsequent circumstance, which will be mentioned.
这件纪念品如今干得像是一块砖，看上去像是史前文明留下来的。 This relic was now as dry as a brick, and seemed to belong to a pre-existent civilization.
塞利娜习惯了每天在这个蛋糕跟前停一会儿，回想那突如其来的不幸，也就是井边提水的村民们所说的那件事。这个习惯一直持续到前不久。那件事发生后，她的人生便被蒙上了阴影。那天早上，突然传来消息说骑兵团即将奉令开拔，离开的日子就在两天后。当时，他们第二次结婚公告已经通过，却等不到第三次了，于是匆忙征求意见该怎么办。最后，大家认为在如此忙乱的情形下举行结婚仪式是不明智的，况且仪式来不来得及办还很难说。 Till quite recently, Selina had been in the habit of pausing before it daily, and recalling the accident whose consequences had thrown a shadow over her life ever since—that of which the water-drawers had spoken—the sudden news one morning that the Route had come for the—th Dragoons, two days only being the interval before departure; the hurried consultation as to what should be done, the second time of asking being past but not the third; and the decision that it would be unwise to solemnize matrimony in such haphazard circumstances, even if it were possible, which was doubtful.
此时，这位年轻女子正坐在炉火前的一张矮凳上，静静地出神。一个蹒跚学步的小男孩在她身畔的地板上玩耍着。 Before the fire the young woman in question was now seated on a low stool, in the stillness of reverie, and a toddling boy played about the floor around her.
“啊，斯通太太！”塞利娜边说边慢慢站起身来， ‘Ah, Mrs. Stone!’ said Selina, rising slowly.
“你来了真是太好了。 ‘How kind of you to come in.
等吃了晚饭再走吧？ You’ll bide to supper?
妈妈肯定已经把那个奇怪的消息告诉你了吧？” Mother has told you the strange news, of course?’
“还没有。 ‘No.
但我听外边的人说了，说你收到克拉克先生的一封信——他们说他现在是军士长了——他要来跟你重续前缘。” But I heard it outside, that is, that you’d had a letter from Mr. Clark—Sergeant-Major Clark, as they say he is now—and that he’s coming to make it up with ’ee.’
“是的，他今晚就来——大老远从他驻扎的英格兰北部赶来。 ‘Yes; coming to-night—all the way from the north of England where he’s quartered.
他要来了，我不知道我是高兴，还是——害怕。 I don’t know whether I’m happy or—frightened at it.
当然，我一直认为如果他还活着，肯定会信守他对我的神圣誓言，回来找我。 Of course I always believed that if he was alive he’d come and keep his solemn vow to me.
但是，当时他阵亡的消息都白纸黑字印在纸上了——我还能指望什么呢？” But when it is printed that a man is killed—what can you think?’
“印在纸上了？” ‘It was printed?’
“唉，是呀。 ‘Why, yes.
阿尔马河战役之后，伤亡人员名册就钉在卡斯特桥市政厅门口。 After the Battle of the Alma the book of the names of the killed and wounded was nailed up against Casterbridge Town Hall door.
那是个星期六，我走到那儿，想亲眼看看名册，因为我听说那上面有他的名字。 ’Twas on a Saturday, and I walked there o’ purpose to read and see for myself; for I’d heard that his name was down.
名册旁边围了一群人，都在找自己亲属的名字。看得出来，他们一看见我就给我让路——因为大家都知道我们本来马上就要结婚了，也知道我——可以说是已经属于他了。 There was a crowd of people round the book, looking for the names of relations; and I can mind that when they saw me they made way for me—knowing that we’d been just going to be married—and that, as you may say, I belonged to him.
唉，于是我就抬起胳膊，一页页翻阅名册，在‘阵亡名单’一栏下，我看到了他的姓，但名字不是‘约翰’，而是‘詹姆斯’。我想他们是把他的名字印错了，那肯定是他。 Well, I reached up my arm, and turned over the farrels of the book, and under the “killed” I read his surname, but instead of “John” they’d printed “James,” and I thought ’twas a mistake, and that it must be he.
谁能想到一个兵团里有两个名字那么相近的人呢。” Who could have guessed there were two nearly of one name in one regiment.’
“唉——他可能就是来跟你履行婚约的吧。所以不要担心了，亲爱的。 ‘Well—he’s coming to finish the wedding of ’ee as may be said; so never mind, my dear.
结果好就是好。” All’s well that ends well.’
“他信里似乎是这个意思。 ‘That’s what he seems to say.
但他写信时还不知道米勒先生的事呢，我怕的就是这一点。 But then he has not heard yet about Mr. Miller; and that’s what rather terrifies me.
幸好我和米勒先生下周的婚事只办了结婚证书，而不像和约翰那回是发结婚公告，所以知道的人没那么多。 Luckily my marriage with him next week was to have been by licence, and not banns, as in John’s case; and it was not so well known on that account.
但我心里还是没个主意。” Still, I don’t know what to think.’
“你的事好像总是好事多磨，对不对，帕多克小姐？ ‘Everything seems to come just ’twixt cup and lip with ’ee, don’t it now, Miss Paddock.
两桩婚事都吹了——这可不多见！ Two weddings broke off—’tis odd!
你怎么会接受米勒先生呢，亲爱的？” How came you to accept Mr. Miller, my dear?’
“他一直对我那么好，那么忠诚！ ‘He’s been so good and faithful!
他知道实情，却一点儿也不介意我有孩子。 Not minding about the child at all; for he knew the rights of the story.
要知道，他非常喜欢约翰尼——把他当成自己的儿子——是不是，宝贝儿？ He’s dearly fond o’ Johnny, you know—just as if ’twere his own—isn’t he, my duck?
米勒先生爱不爱你？” Do Mr. Miller love you or don’t he?’
“爱！ ‘Iss!
我也爱米勒先生！”小娃娃答道。 An’ I love Mr. Miller,’ said the toddler.
“唔，你瞧，斯通太太，米勒先生说会给我一个舒适的家，而且他比我富裕多了，我觉得这对约翰尼是件好事。我最后终于接受了他，一个寡妇还能指望什么呢——自从我认定约翰的名字在阵亡名单上，我就一直把自己当个寡妇。 ‘Well, you see, Mrs. Stone, he said he’d make me a comfortable home; and thinking ’twould be a good thing for Johnny, Mr. Miller being so much better off than me, I agreed at last, just as a widow might—which is what I have always felt myself; ever since I saw what I thought was John’s name printed there.
但愿约翰能原谅我！” I hope John will forgive me!’
“他会原谅你的，因为对他来说你一点儿也没做错。 ‘So he will forgive ’ee, since ’twas no manner of wrong to him.
他本该写封信给你的，告诉你阵亡名单上是另一个人。” He ought to have sent ’ee a line, saying ’twas another man.’
塞利娜的母亲走了进来。 Selina’s mother entered.
“我们知道这消息还不到一个钟头呢，斯通太太，”她说， ‘We’ve not known of this an hour, Mrs. Stone,’ she said.
“信今天下午才到，是一个上学的孩子从下麦尔斯托克邮局捎来的。 ‘The letter was brought up from Lower Mellstock Post-office by one of the school children, only this afternoon.
米勒先生今晚正要过来商定婚礼的安排。 Mr. Miller was coming here this very night to settle about the wedding doings.
听！ Hark!
是你爸爸回来了吗？ Is that your father?
还是米勒先生已经来了？” Or is it Mr. Miller already come?’
门廊上响起了脚步声，接着是在门口地垫上蹭鞋子的声音。房门开了，一个三十岁左右、面色红润的男子出现在门口，他有一副技术工人的强壮身躯，而且明显脾气不错。 The footsteps entered the porch; there was a brushing on the mat, and the door of the room sprung back to disclose a rubicund man about thirty years of age, of thriving master-mechanic appearance and obviously comfortable temper.
来人一看到孩子，还没来得及注意到屋里的大人们，就一边模仿公鸡打鸣，一边像拍打翅膀似的拍打着两臂。约翰尼对这种出场方式喜欢得不得了。 On seeing the child, and before taking any notice whatever of the elders, the comer made a noise like the crowing of a cock and flapped his arms as if they were wings, a method of entry which had the unqualified admiration of Johnny.
“是的——是他来了。”塞利娜说着，不自然地迎上前去。 ‘Yes—it is he,’ said Selina constrainedly advancing.

三 III
他可以算是那时候长期服役老兵的一个典型：长相颇为英俊，又带着几分含蓄和严肃。有人可能会说，这种印象一部分是由于他还围着高高的宽大硬领巾，使得制服的领子十分挺。 He was a good specimen of the long-service soldier of those days; a not unhandsome man, with a certain undemonstrative dignity, which some might have said to be partly owing to the stiffness of his uniform about his neck, the high stock being still worn.
比起和塞利娜分别的时候，现在的他结实多了。 He was much stouter than when Selina had parted from him.

米勒几乎是欣然接受了晚餐邀请，几个邻居也被请了过来，好增添些快活的气氛。 Miller, with very little pressure indeed, accepted an invitation to supper, and a few neighbours were induced to come in to make up a cheerful party.

可谈话完全是围绕不久前的战争展开的。尽管塞利娜用角制酒杯喝了半杯军士长带来的烈性啤酒，胆子壮了些，但她仍然决定还是等到晚餐结束后，再找个更好的时机私下把实情告诉他。 But the talk ran entirely upon the late war; and though fortified by half a horn of the strong ale brought by the sergeant-major she decided that she might have a better opportunity when supper was over of revealing the situation to him in private.
晚饭后，克拉克自在地靠在椅背上，环顾四周。 Having supped, Clark leaned back at ease in his chair and looked around.

“是的，我一会儿就都告诉约翰！”塞利娜插嘴道。一听这话，塞利娜的父亲意识到，即使他不愿藏着这个秘密，他还是得继续藏下去。他住了嘴，脸上表现出明显的不悦。 ‘Yes, I’ll tell John all about that by and by!’ interposed Selina; at which, perceiving that the secret which he did not like keeping was to be kept even yet, her father held his tongue with some show of testiness.
既然提到了跳舞，大家就觉得应该把这个想法付诸实践。 The subject of a dance having been broached, to put the thought in practice was the feeling of all.
很快，热心的人们就把对面房间的桌椅搬到了这间屋子，两位村民还差人回家去取小提琴和小手鼓。这时，大多数人便踩着这偏僻村庄里流行的舞步，跳起舞来。 Soon after the tables and chairs were borne from the opposite room to this by zealous hands, and two of the villagers sent home for a fiddle and tambourine, when the majority began to tread a measure well known in that secluded vale.
塞利娜自然是和军士长跳舞。她父亲对此不甚满意，她母亲更是十分不安。两位家长的本意是，等女儿和克拉克之前的轻率婚约得到教堂的法令认可之后再举行庆祝活动。 Selina naturally danced with the sergeant-major, not altogether to her father’s satisfaction, and to the real uneasiness of her mother, both of whom would have preferred a postponement of festivities till the rashly anticipated relationship between their daughter and Clark in the past had been made fact by the church’s ordinances.
然而，两人都没有明确表示反对，因为帕多克先生记得，正是因为他起初强烈反对塞利娜和军人结婚，婚事才被耽搁了，最终没有结成——而后果又是出乎意料地严重，他对此十分自责。自这场因他干涉而起的不幸之后，他便对一切都撒手不管，听天由命了。 They did not, however, express a positive objection, Mr. Paddock remembering, with self-reproach, that it was owing to his original strongly expressed disapproval of Selina’s being a soldier’s wife that the wedding had been delayed, and finally hindered—with worse consequences than were expected; and ever since the misadventure brought about by his government he had allowed events to steer their own courses.

“亲爱的约翰，你还说呢！ ‘Dear John, to say that!
当时你为什么没回来呀？” Why didn’t you?’

“我的老天！ ‘Sakes alive!

“真的…… ‘Really!...
哎呀，塞利娜——” Why, Selina—’

舞会开始得随意，结束得也突然。客人们仍在房子里呆着，等医生来。 The dancing-party ended as unceremoniously as it had begun; but the guests lingered round the premises till the doctor should arrive.
等医生赶来时，军士长的四肢已经凉了。毫无疑问，他几乎是一坐下就死了。 When he did come the sergeant-major’s extremities were already cold, and there was no doubt that death had overtaken him almost at the moment that he had sat down.
郁郁寡欢的塞利娜认为是她吐露的消息导致了克拉克的猝死，医生则认为一点儿关系都没有。 The medical practitioner quite refused to accept the unhappy Selina’s theory that her revelation had in any way induced Clark’s sudden collapse.
而后赶来的验尸官发现猝死的直接原因是心脏衰竭。两人都认为塞利娜的想法没有事实依据。 Both he and the coroner afterwards, who found the immediate cause to be heart-failure, held that such a supposition was unwarranted by facts.
他们断定，体验过克里米亚半岛冬天物质缺乏和其他生活中的挣扎经历之后，克拉克的心脏出现了脂肪变性，变得十分衰弱，再加上一整天的旅程，匆忙赶路，还有累人的跳舞，足以造成猝死。这不幸的事件恰恰在她吐露实情后发生，纯属巧合。 They asserted that a long day’s journey, a hurried drive, and then an exhausting dance, were sufficient for such a result upon a heart enfeebled by fatty degeneration after the privations of a Crimean winter and other trying experiences, the coincidence of the sad event with any disclosure of hers being a pure accident.
然而，这个结论仍没有打消塞利娜的念头，她仍认为她的一番话是最直接的打击，摧毁了军士长孱弱的身躯。 This conclusion, however, did not dislodge Selina’s opinion that the shock of her statement had been the immediate stroke which had felled a constitution so undermined.
五 V
那时期，卡斯特桥兵营仍是骑兵的营房，被改造成炮兵营地则是若干年之后的事了。 At this date the Casterbridge Barracks were cavalry quarters, their adaptation to artillery having been effected some years later.
正是因为约翰·克拉克服役的骑兵团恰巧驻扎在那里，塞利娜才认识了他。 It had been owing to the fact that the—the Dragoons, in which John Clark had served, happened to be lying there that Selina made his acquaintance.
克拉克死时，兵营已被灰骑兵团占据。但当军士长惨死的消息传到镇上时，灰骑兵的军官派出了他们技艺精湛的簧管乐手及铜管乐手，以在葬礼上凸显他作为军人应有的荣耀。 At the time of his death the barracks were occupied by the Scots Greys, but when the pathetic circumstances of the sergeant-major’s end became known in the town the officers of the Greys offered the services of their fine reed and brass band, that he might have a funeral marked by due military honours.
于是，他的遗体被运到了灰骑兵的营房，次日下午，又从那里被运去安葬到杜罗维尔区的教堂墓地。灰骑兵营里资格最老、最温顺的战马披上黑纱，在送葬过程中代表克拉克的坐骑。 His body was accordingly removed to the barracks, and carried thence to the churchyard in the Durnover quarter on the following afternoon, one of the Greys’ most ancient and docile chargers being blacked up to represent Clark’s horse on the occasion.
塞利娜的故事家喻户晓，人人都同情她。 Everybody pitied Selina, whose story was well known.
她作为唯一一位送葬者，跟在灵柩后面。克拉克在英格兰南部没有亲人，与他的兵团通了消息后，也没有亲人远道而来。 She followed the corpse as the only mourner, Clark having been without relations in this part of the country, and a communication with his regiment having brought none from a distance.

灵柩下葬后，连发敬礼枪，送葬队列就回程了。让塞利娜有些震惊的是，护卫的士兵们竟然跟着《她走了！》的轻快曲调齐步行进，好像随着刚刚卡宾枪的发射，一切因军士长而起的忧思也都终结了。 When the interment had taken place, the volleys been fired, and the return journey begun, it was with something like a shock that she found the military escort to be moving at a quick march to the lively strains of ‘Off she goes!’ as if all care for the sergeant-major was expected to be ended with the late discharge of the carbines.
巧合的是，他猝死前，他们跳的正是这支曲子。塞利娜无法忍受这样的调子，忙叫车夫在前方停车。 It was, by chance, the very tune to which they had been footing when he died, and unable to bear its notes, she hastily told her driver to drop behind.
军乐团和士兵们沿着镇子的主街向北而行，渐渐消失，而塞利娜则转身走上斯旺桥，往麦尔斯托克的家走去。 The band and military party diminished up the High Street, and Selina turned over Swan bridge and homeward to Mellstock.
在一模一样的境遇下，她又重新开始了军士长回来之前的那种生活。可她面对这些境遇的心境已经变得多么不同！ Then recommenced for her a life whose incidents were precisely of a suit with those which had preceded the soldier’s return; but how different in her appreciation of them!
塞利娜的父母本来希望她通过那桩迟来的婚事挽回名声，结果又是功败垂成，两人为此很是愤怒。塞利娜才服了一两周的丧，就发现几乎无法忍受跟父母在一起的日子了。 Her narrow miss of the recovered respectability they had hoped for from that tardy event worked upon her parents as an irritant, and after the first week or two of her mourning her life with them grew almost insupportable.
她冲动地穿上了寡妇的丧服，因为她觉得自己就是个寡妇，并且也给小约翰尼穿上了丧服。 She had impulsively taken to herself the weeds of a widow, for such she seemed to herself to be, and clothed little Johnny in sables likewise.
她认为自己同死者之间有着道义上的关系，仅仅是由于两次意外事件，才没有成为法律上的关系。这种想法使得两位老人一看到她的衣服，就少不了讽刺她几句。尽管一直以来对他们而言，说这些话比她听这些话还难受。 This assumption of a moral relationship to the deceased, which she asserted to be only not a legal one by two most unexpected accidents, led the old people to indulge in sarcasm at her expense whenever they beheld her attire, though all the while it cost them more pain to utter than it gave her to hear it.
她之前住在家里，已经熟悉了父亲的生意。有一天，她突然带着孩子去了乔克－牛顿，伊夫尔镇那边的一个小村子，开了一家小小的蔬果店，并把她种植的农产品拿到伊夫尔的市场上卖。这让她的父母大吃一惊。 Having become accustomed by her residence at home to the business carried on by her father, she surprised them one day by going off with the child to Chalk-Newton, in the direction of the town of Ivell, and opening a miniature fruit and vegetable shop, attending Ivell market with her produce.
她的生意做大了些，很快就足以让自己和儿子过上舒适的日子了。 Her business grew somewhat larger, and it was soon sufficient to enable her to support herself and the boy in comfort.
从离家那天起，她就自称“约翰·克拉克太太”，并把这个名字写在商店招牌上——没人阻止她这样做。 She called herself ‘Mrs. John Clark’ from the day of leaving home, and painted the name on her signboard—no man forbidding her.
在新的环境里，她渐渐忘记了自己的痛苦处境，人们也慢慢接受了她，认为她就是一位龙骑兵军士长的遗孀——她低调、忧伤的举止似乎足以证明她的身份。在宁静的生活中，她幻想着假如约翰还活着，带她去了新西兰，她的未来将会是什么样子。她的心在这忧伤而又愉悦的白日梦中得到了滋养。 By degrees the pain of her state was forgotten in her new circumstances, and getting to be generally accepted as the widow of a sergeant-major of dragoons—an assumption which her modest and mournful demeanour seemed to substantiate—her life became a placid one, her mind being nourished by the melancholy luxury of dreaming what might have been her future in New Zealand with John, if he had only lived to take her there.
她所有的外出活动仅限于在集市的日子去趟伊夫尔，再就是每隔两周去一次安葬克拉克的教堂墓地，在约翰尼的帮助下照料她在他墓前种下的花儿——寡妇们通常都这么做。 Her only travels now were a journey to Ivell on market-days, and once a fortnight to the churchyard in which Clark lay, there to tend, with Johnny’s assistance, as widows are wont to do, the flowers she had planted upon his grave.
军士长意外死亡后约一年半的一天，巴塞洛缪·米勒拜访了塞利娜位于小店楼上的住处，她吃了一惊。 On a day about eighteen months after his unexpected decease, Selina was surprised in her lodging over her little shop by a visit from Bartholomew Miller.
他之前来拜访过她一两次，也用大家熟知的“克拉克太太”这个名字来称呼她，并不作评论。 He had called on her once or twice before, on which occasions he had used without a word of comment the name by which she was known.
“我这次来，”他说，“不是正好顺路，而是来请求你的。你大概猜得到我请求什么，克拉克太太。 ‘I’ve come this time,’ he said, ‘less because I was in this direction than to ask you, Mrs. Clark, what you mid well guess.
简单说吧，我是来求婚的。” I’ve come o’ purpose, in short.’
她笑了。 She smiled.
“是再次请求我嫁给你吗？” ‘’Tis to ask me again to marry you?’
“对啊，当然了。 ‘Yes, of course.
你瞧，他回来找你，恰恰证明了你的尊严，我一直相信你，尽管其他人不信。 You see, his coming back for ’ee proved what I always believed of ’ee, though others didn’t.
你已经表明了你的独立，你信任他的承诺，并付诸行动。现在，没有人不欢迎你回到我们教区了。 There’s nobody but would be glad to welcome you to our parish again, now you’ve showed your independence and acted up to your trust in his promise.
好啦，亲爱的，你会回来吗？” Well, my dear, will you come?’
“我想，我还是宁愿继续当克拉克太太，”她回答道， ‘I’d rather bide as Mrs. Clark, I think,’ she answered.
“我一点儿也不为我的身份感到羞耻，因为老天承认我是约翰的遗孀。” ‘I am not ashamed of my position at all; for I am John’s widow in the eyes of Heaven.’
“我很赞同——这也是我为什么会来。 ‘I quite agree—that’s why I’ve come.
但是，你也不愿意总这么辛苦地开店、赶集吧，再说，如果你能什么都不做一心照料约翰尼的话，对他也更好。” Still, you won’t like to be always straining at this shop-keeping and market-standing; and ’twould be better for Johnny if you had nothing to do but tend him.’
他这番话击中了塞利娜拒绝同他结婚的唯一软肋——为孩子好。 He here touched the only weak spot in Selina’s resistance to his proposal—the good of the boy.
为了孩子，要是有别的她不爱的男人向她求婚，她说不定也会随便嫁了。然而，尽管她从小就认识眼前这个可敬的男人，此时想到要成为米勒太太，却还是开心不起来。 To promote that there were other men she might have married offhand without loving them if they had asked her to; but though she had known the worthy speaker from her youth, she could not for the moment fancy herself happy as Mrs. Miller.
他沉默了片刻。 He paused awhile.
“我得告诉你，克拉克太太，”一会儿，他又说道，“对我来说，结婚已经是个很紧迫的问题了。 ‘I ought to tell ’ee, Mrs. Clark,’ he said by and by, ‘that marrying is getting to be a pressing question with me.
这丝毫不是为了我自己。 Not on my own account at all.
实话实说，我妈妈上了年纪，我又经常不在家，所以，除了我之外，家里几乎必须得再有一个人陪着她。 The truth is, that mother is growing old, and I am away from home a good deal, so that it is almost necessary there should be another person in the house with her besides me.
这个实际问题迫使我不得不考虑娶个妻子，而且我也是真心想娶你，你知道的，这世上我最在乎的，除了你再没有别人了。” That’s the practical consideration which forces me to think of taking a wife, apart from my wish to take you; and you know there’s nobody in the world I care for so much.’
她说世上还有比她强得多的女人，还说了些其他这种场合下自然要说的客套话。但她要他相信，她非常感激他对她的感情，事实上她也是真的很感激。 She said something about there being far better women than she, and other natural commonplaces; but assured him she was most grateful to him for feeling what he felt, as indeed she sincerely was.
然而，塞利娜还是不愿意到他舒适的家里去当那个有用的第三人——至少此时此刻不愿意。 However, Selina would not consent to be the useful third person in his comfortable home—at any rate just then.
他同她喝过茶就走了。她的道别没有让他感到太多希望。 He went away, after taking tea with her, without discerning much hope for him in her good-bye.
六 VI
那晚之后，她很久没再见过他，也没听到过他的任何消息。 After that evening she saw and heard nothing of him for a great while.
只要天气允许，她还是每两周带孩子去军士长的墓地。她想，米勒先生肯定知道她的这个习惯。 Her fortnightly journeys to the sergeant-major’s grave were continued, whenever weather did not hinder them; and Mr. Miller must have known, she thought, of this custom of hers.
然而，尽管从他家到墓地比从他家到她在乔克－牛顿的店近得多，他却从来没有像通常的恋人那样，假装同她不期而遇。 But though the churchyard was not nearly so far from his homestead as was her shop at Chalk-Newton, he never appeared in the accidental way that lovers use.
不久，她从她母亲的一封来信中得到了解释。信中不经意间提到，巴塞洛缪·米勒先生去了肖茨福德农场的另一边，娶了他认识的一个富裕奶牛场主的女儿。 An explanation was forthcoming in the shape of a letter from her mother, who casually mentioned that Mr. Bartholomew Miller had gone away to the other side of Shottsford-Forum to be married to a thriving dairyman’s daughter that he knew there.
据说他结婚的主要动机不是爱情，而是想给他年迈的母亲找个伴儿。 His chief motive, it was reported, had been less one of love than a wish to provide a companion for his aged mother.
塞利娜足够现实，知道这样一来，她便失去了一个安顿下来的好机会——而且很有可能是唯一的机会。有那么一会儿，她对自己的独立感到后悔了。 Selina was practical enough to know that she had lost a good and possibly the only opportunity of settling in life after what had happened, and for a moment she regretted her independence.
但细想之后，她便平静下来。为了增强自己走下去的决心，那天下午她便启程去给军士长扫墓，感受那种一如既往的、庄重的快乐。 But she became calm on reflection, and to fortify herself in her course started that afternoon to tend the sergeant-major’s grave, in which she took the same sober pleasure as at first.
到了墓地，她像往常一样转过一个拐角，往军士长的墓走去。这时，她惊奇地发现另一个女人正站在克拉克墓前的草地上，俯身用伞尖铲掉一些常春藤的根。常春藤是塞利娜满心虔诚种在那里的，好让坟上有个常青的保护罩。那女人显然也是个体面的寡妇，身边跟着一个小男孩。 On reaching the churchyard and turning the corner towards the spot as usual, she was surprised to perceive another woman, also apparently a respectable widow, and with a tiny boy by her side, bending over Clark’s turf, and spudding up with the point of her umbrella some ivy-roots that Selina had reverently planted there to form an evergreen mantle over the mound.
“你把我的常春藤挖出来干什么！”塞利娜叫道。她激动地冲上前去，拉着约翰尼的手猛地一拽，孩子被一座墓绊倒了。 ‘What are you digging up my ivy for!’ cried Selina, rushing forward so excitedly that Johnny tumbled over a grave with the force of the tug she gave his hand in her sudden start.
“你的常春藤？”那个体面女人问道。 ‘Your ivy?’ said the respectable woman.
“嗨，当然了！ ‘Why yes!
是我种在那儿的——种在我丈夫坟上的。” I planted it there—on my husband’s grave.’
“你丈夫！” ‘Your husband’s!’
“对。 ‘Yes.
就是已故的克拉克军士长。 The late Sergeant-Major Clark.
反正他实际上等于是我丈夫，因为他死前我们马上就要结婚了。” Anyhow, as good as my husband, for he was just going to be.’
“真的？ ‘Indeed.
可谁又是我丈夫呢，除了他？ But who may be my husband, if not he?
我是唯一的约翰·克拉克太太，这位已故骑兵军士长的遗孀，这孩子是他唯一的子嗣。” I am the only Mrs. John Clark, widow of the late Sergeant-Major of Dragoons, and this is his only son and heir.’
“怎么可能呢？”塞利娜结结巴巴地说。她开始意识到这女人说的话有可能是真的，喉头一阵发紧， ‘How can that be?’ faltered Selina, her throat seeming to stick together as she just began to perceive its possibility.
“他正要——再跟我办一次婚事——而且我们要一起去新西兰。” ‘He had been—going to marry me twice—and we were going to New Zealand.’
“啊——我想起你来了，”那合法的寡妇平静而不失礼貌地答道， ‘Ah!—I remember about you,’ returned the legitimate widow calmly and not unkindly.
“你一定是塞利娜吧。他时不时会说起你，说他和你的关系将一直是他良心上的负担。 ‘You must be Selina; he spoke of you now and then, and said that his relations with you would always be a weight on his conscience.
好吧，下面我来给你讲讲我和他的故事。 Well; the history of my life with him is soon told.
他从克里米亚回来后，在我北部的家乡认识了我，我们相识不到一个月就结婚了。 When he came back from the Crimea he became acquainted with me at my home in the north, and we were married within a month of first knowing each other.
不幸的是，共同生活几个月后，我们发现彼此合不来。有一次我们吵得特别厉害，那次可能大部分是我的错——我不介意在他坟墓旁边承认这一点。之后，他便离开了我，说他要拿到退伍许可，移民到新西兰去，再也不回我身边了。 Unfortunately, after living together a few months, we could not agree; and after a particularly sharp quarrel, in which, perhaps, I was most in the wrong—as I don’t mind owning here by his graveside—he went away from me, declaring he would buy his discharge and emigrate to New Zealand, and never come back to me any more.
随后我就听说他突然死在了麦尔斯托克某次低俗的狂欢宴会上。鉴于他离开我时那么生气，再也不肯和我共同生活，我也不肯来参加他的葬礼，或者做任何跟他有关的事情。 The next thing I heard was that he had died suddenly at Mellstock at some low carouse; and as he had left me in such anger to live no more with me, I wouldn’t come down to his funeral, or do anything in relation to him.
是我任性，我知道，但实情就是如此。 ’Twas temper, I know, but that was the fact.
即使我们是友好分手，跑300英里赶到这儿来也是一大笔开销，因为他可没给我剩下多少钱……我连根拔了你的常春藤，真是抱歉，不过在我们北方人看来，这常见的常春藤就是一种杂草。” Even if we had parted friends it would have been a serious expense to travel three hundred miles to get there, for one who wasn’t left so very well off...I am sorry I pulled up your ivy-roots; but that common sort of ivy is considered a weed in my part of the country.’
