Chapter I 第一章
The interior of St. James’s Church, in Havenpool Town, was slowly darkening under the close clouds of a winter afternoon. 一个冬日的下午，阴云密布，黑文普尔镇上圣詹姆斯教堂的室内慢慢暗了下来。
It was Sunday: service had just ended, the face of the parson in the pulpit was buried in his hands, and the congregation, with a cheerful sigh of release, were rising from their knees to depart. 那是个星期天。祷告仪式刚刚结束，布道坛上的牧师双手捂着脸，会众们发出一声解脱后的愉悦叹息，站起身准备离开。
For the moment the stillness was so complete that the surging of the sea could be heard outside the harbour-bar. 此时，教堂内一片寂静，甚至海港沙洲外汹涌的浪涛声都能听到。
Then it was broken by the footsteps of the clerk going towards the west door to open it in the usual manner for the exit of the assembly. 接着，教堂执事的脚步声打破了沉寂。他像往常一样走向西门，准备打开门让众人离开。
Before, however, he had reached the doorway, the latch was lifted from without, and the dark figure of a man in a sailor’s garb appeared against the light. 然而，他还没走到门口，门闩就被人从外面打开了。一个水手装束的黑色人影出现在光亮下。
The clerk stepped aside, the sailor closed the door gently behind him, and advanced up the nave till he stood at the chancel-step. 教堂执事让开路，水手轻轻关上门，穿过教堂中殿，一直走到高坛的台阶上站定。
The parson looked up from the private little prayer which, after so many for the parish, he quite fairly took for himself; rose to his feet, and stared at the intruder. 牧师本来正默默为自己作简短的祷告。他为教区的人们作了那么多祷告，为自己作一次也是合情合理。此时，他抬起头，站起身，盯着这位不速之客。
‘I beg your pardon, sir,’ said the sailor, addressing the minister in a voice distinctly audible to all the congregation. “打扰了，先生，”水手说道。他虽是和牧师说话，声音却很响亮，所有的会众都听得见，
‘I have come here to offer thanks for my narrow escape from shipwreck. “我来这里，是为了表达我的感恩之情，因为我侥幸从海难中生还。
I am given to understand that it is a proper thing to do, if you have no objection?’ 我一向认为我应该这样做，如果您不反对的话。”
The parson, after a moment’s pause, said hesitatingly, ‘I have no objection; certainly. 牧师沉默了片刻，迟疑地说道：“我当然不反对。
It is usual to mention any such wish before service, so that the proper words may be used in the General Thanksgiving. 这种感恩的意愿通常要在祷告仪式之前提出，这样我为大家统一感恩祈祷时便能选择合适的话语。
But, if you wish, we can read from the form for use after a storm at sea.’ 不过，如果你愿意，我们也可以诵读海上风暴过后常用的现成祷文。”
‘Ay, sure; I ain’t particular,’ said the sailor. “嗯，没问题，我不介意。”水手说道。
The clerk thereupon directed the sailor to the page in the prayer-book where the collect of thanksgiving would be found, and the rector began reading it, the sailor kneeling where he stood, and repeating it after him word by word in a distinct voice. 于是，教堂执事让水手翻到祷告书上收录了这种感恩祷文的那一页，牧师开始诵读祷文。水手跪在他原先站着的地方，跟着牧师一字一句地大声念。
The people, who had remained agape and motionless at the proceeding, mechanically knelt down likewise; but they continued to regard the isolated form of the sailor who, in the precise middle of the chancel-step, remained fixed on his knees, facing the east, his hat beside him, his hands joined, and he quite unconscious of his appearance in their regard. 整个过程中，教堂的会众一直目瞪口呆，一动不动。现在，他们也机械地跟着跪了下来，但眼睛仍注视着水手孤零零的轮廓。他面朝东跪在高坛台阶的正中央，帽子放在身旁，双手紧握，完全没有意识到自己在众人眼中的形象。
When his thanksgiving had come to an end he rose; the people rose also, and all went out of church together. 水手作完感恩祈祷后，站了起来。其他人也站起身，一同走出教堂。
As soon as the sailor emerged, so that the remaining daylight fell upon his face, old inhabitants began to recognize him as no other than Shadrach Jolliffe, a young man who had not been seen at Havenpool for several years. 水手一走出去，夕阳的余晖照到他脸上，常年居住在这里的人们便渐渐认出，他就是谢德拉克·乔利夫。这个年轻人已经好些年没在黑文普尔露面了。
A son of the town, his parents had died when he was quite young, on which account he had early gone to sea, in the Newfoundland trade. 乔利夫出生在黑文普尔镇，很小的时候便失去了双亲。因此，他很早就成了水手，到纽芬兰做生意。
He talked with this and that townsman as he walked, informing them that, since leaving his native place years before, he had become captain and owner of a small coasting-ketch, which had providentially been saved from the gale as well as himself. 他一边走，一边和镇上的人左一句右一句地交谈。他告诉他们，几年前离开家乡后，他成了船长，拥有了一艘沿岸航行的小双桅纵帆船。多亏上帝保佑，他自己还有他的船才从大风暴中获救。
Presently he drew near to two girls who were going out of the churchyard in front of him; they had been sitting in the nave at his entry, and had watched his doings with deep interest, afterwards discussing him as they moved out of church together. 不一会儿，他靠近两个姑娘。这两个姑娘在他前面，正要走出教堂的院子。他进入教堂时，她们正坐在中殿里，饶有兴味地观察了他的一举一动。之后她们一起走出教堂，便聊起了他。
One was a slight and gentle creature, the other a tall, large-framed, deliberative girl. 其中一个姑娘身材纤细，温和文雅，另外一个则身材高大，体格健壮，做事很慎重。
Captain Jolliffe regarded the loose curls of their hair, their backs and shoulders, down to their heels, for some time. 乔利夫船长注视了她们好一会儿，从松松的发卷、后背、肩膀，一直打量到脚后跟。
‘Who may them two maids be?’ he whispered to his neighbour. “这两位姑娘是？”他轻声问周围的乡邻。
‘The little one is Emily Hanning; the tall one Joanna Phippard.’ “小个子的是埃米莉·汉宁，高个子的是乔安娜·菲帕德。”
‘Ah! “噢！
I recollect ’em now, to be sure.’ 我现在想起来了，是她俩。”
He advanced to their elbow, and genially stole a gaze at them. 他赶上她们，快活地偷偷瞅了她们一眼。
‘Emily, you don’t know me?’ said the sailor, turning his beaming brown eyes on her. “埃米莉，你不认识我了吗？”水手用含着笑意的棕色眼睛看着她，说道。
‘I think I do, Mr. Jolliffe,’ said Emily shyly. “我想我还记得你，乔利夫先生。”埃米莉羞怯地回答。
The other girl looked straight at him with her dark eyes. 另外一个姑娘则用她那黑亮的眼睛直直地看着他。
‘The face of Miss Joanna I don’t call to mind so well,’ he continued. “我对乔安娜小姐的长相记不太清了，”他继续说，
‘But I know her beginnings and kindred.’ “不过我见过她小的时候，也认识她的亲戚。”
They walked and talked together, Jolliffe narrating particulars of his late narrow escape, till they reached the corner of Sloop Lane, in which Emily Hanning dwelt, when, with a nod and smile, she left them. 他们边走边聊。乔利夫细细讲述着他不久前那次九死一生的经历，直到他们走到单桅船巷的拐角。埃米莉·汉宁就住在那里。她点了点头，微微一笑，先回了家。
Soon the sailor parted also from Joanna, and, having no especial errand or appointment, turned back towards Emily’s house. 很快，水手和乔安娜也告了别。由于没有什么事要做，也没有约，他便回转身，朝埃米莉家走去。
She lived with her father, who called himself an accountant, the daughter, however, keeping a little stationery-shop as a supplemental provision for the gaps of his somewhat uncertain business. 埃米莉和父亲一起住。她父亲自称是个会计师，但埃米莉却开了一个小小的文具店，以贴补父亲那那时好时坏的生意。
On entering Jolliffe found father and daughter about to begin tea. 一进门，乔利夫就看到父女俩正准备开始喝茶。
‘O, I didn’t know it was tea-time,’ he said. “哦，没想到是下午茶时间了，”他说，
‘Ay, I’ll have a cup with much pleasure.’ “嗯，我很乐意喝一杯。”
He remained to tea and long afterwards, telling more tales of his seafaring life. 他喝了茶，又呆了很久，讲述了更多他航海生活中的故事。
Several neighbours called to listen, and were asked to come in. 几个邻居过来串门，应邀加入聊天。
Somehow Emily Hanning lost her heart to the sailor that Sunday night, and in the course of a week or two there was a tender understanding between them. 那个周日的晚上，埃米莉·汉宁不知怎的对水手动了心。不出一两周，两人之间便暗生情愫。
One moonlight evening in the next month Shadrach was ascending out of the town by the long straight road eastward, to an elevated suburb where the more fashionable houses stood—if anything near this ancient port could be called fashionable—when he saw a figure before him whom, from her manner of glancing back, he took to be Emily. 次月，一个月光皎洁的晚上，谢德拉克顺着一条长长的笔直大路朝东走出黑文普尔镇，一直走到郊区。那里地势较高，聚集了比较气派的房子——如果这个古老港口附近有称得上气派的东西的话——这时，他看到前方有一个身影，从那人回头张望的姿势来看，他觉得是埃米莉。
But, on coming up, he found she was Joanna Phippard. 不过，走近后他才发现，那是乔安娜·菲帕德。
He gave a gallant greeting, and walked beside her. 他殷勤地打了个招呼，和她并肩行走。
‘Go along,’ she said, ‘or Emily will be jealous!’ “你快走吧，”她说，“不然埃米莉会吃醋的！”
He seemed not to like the suggestion, and remained. 他似乎不喜欢这个提议，依然走在她旁边。
What was said and what was done on that walk never could be clearly recollected by Shadrach; but in some way or other Joanna contrived to wean him away from her gentler and younger rival. 那次散步途中说的话、做的事，谢德拉克怎么也记不清了；而乔安娜则想尽办法，试图让谢德拉克疏远那位比她更年轻、更温柔的情敌。
From that week onwards, Jolliffe was seen more and more in the wake of Joanna Phippard and less in the company of Emily; and it was soon rumoured about the quay that old Jolliffe’s son, who had come home from sea, was going to be married to the former young woman, to the great disappointment of the latter. 从那一周起，人们越来越频繁地看到乔利夫和乔安娜·菲帕德在一起，而他和埃米莉相处的时间则越来越少。不久，整个港口都风传老乔利夫的儿子，也就是那个刚刚出海回来的水手，要和前者结婚，让后者大失所望。
Just after this report had gone about, Joanna dressed herself for a walk one morning, and started for Emily’s house in the little cross-street. 就在谣言传开之后的一个早上，乔安娜收拾妥当，准备去小街上的埃米莉家走一遭。
Intelligence of the deep sorrow of her friend on account of the loss of Shadrach had reached her ears also, and her conscience reproached her for winning him away. 她也听说了她的朋友由于失去谢德拉克而伤心欲绝的消息。她为自己的横刀夺爱，受到良心上的谴责。
Joanna was not altogether satisfied with the sailor. 乔安娜对水手并非十分满意。
She liked his attentions, and she coveted the dignity of matrimony; but she had never been deeply in love with Jolliffe. 她喜欢他的殷勤照顾，也向往体面的婚姻生活，但她从未深深爱上乔利夫。
For one thing, she was ambitious, and socially his position was hardly so good as her own, and there was always the chance of an attractive woman mating considerably above her. 首先，她有更高的追求，而他的社会地位却还不及她自己。漂亮的女人总是有机会嫁给地位比自己高得多的男士。
It had long been in her mind that she would not strongly object to give him back again to Emily if her friend felt so very badly about him. 她反复思考，觉得如果埃米莉确实对乔利夫爱得死心塌地，那她倒也不怎么介意将他拱手还给她的朋友。
To this end she had written a letter of renunciation to Shadrach, which letter she carried in her hand, intending to send it if personal observation of Emily convinced her that her friend was suffering. 为此，她写了一封给谢德拉克的绝交信。她把信攥在手里，打算如果经过亲自观察，确定埃米莉饱受煎熬，那她就把信寄出去。
Joanna entered Sloop Lane and stepped down into the stationery-shop, which was below the pavement level. 乔安娜走进单桅船巷，向下迈进低于人行道的文具店。
Emily’s father was never at home at this hour of the day, and it seemed as though Emily were not at home either, for the visitor could make nobody hear. 这个时间，埃米莉的父亲向来不在家，埃米莉这天似乎也不在，因为没人回应登门拜访的乔安娜。
Customers came so seldom hither that a five minutes’ absence of the proprietor counted for little. 小店门可罗雀，不常有顾客光临，因此店主出去五分钟，也不打紧。
Joanna waited in the little shop, where Emily had tastefully set out—as women can—articles in themselves of slight value, so as to obscure the meagreness of the stock-in-trade; till she saw a figure pausing without the window apparently absorbed in the contemplation of the sixpenny books, packets of paper, and prints hung on a string. 埃米莉充分发挥女性的天赋，精心摆放了店里那些本身不甚值钱的商品，尽量掩盖东西本身的寒酸。乔安娜在小店里等着，突然看到一个人停在窗外，看上去正全神贯注地打量着六便士一本的廉价书籍、小捆纸和悬挂在细绳上的画片。
It was Captain Shadrach Jolliffe, peering in to ascertain if Emily were there alone. 来人正是谢德拉克·乔利夫船长。他正透过窗子观察店里，以确定是否只有埃米莉一个人在。
Moved by an impulse of reluctance to meet him in a spot which breathed of Emily, Joanna slipped through the door that communicated with the parlour at the back. 乔安娜突然觉得她不愿在一个散发着埃米莉气息的地方见到乔利夫，于是她悄悄溜进通往后面会客室的门。
She had frequently done so before, for in her friendship with Emily she had the freedom of the house without ceremony. 她以前经常这么干，因为凭着她和埃米莉的交情，她在这个家里可以自由自在，不需客气。
Jolliffe entered the shop. 乔利夫走进小店。
Through the thin blind which screened the glass partition she could see that he was disappointed at not finding Emily there. 透过遮住玻璃隔墙的薄百叶窗，乔安娜能看出，乔利夫发现埃米莉不在家后，很是失望。
He was about to go out again, when Emily’s form darkened the doorway, hastening home from some errand. 他刚要离开时，埃米莉的身影出现在门口。她因为一些事匆匆赶回来。
At sight of Jolliffe she started back as if she would have gone out again. 一看到乔利夫，她吃惊地倒退一步，仿佛要再次离开。
‘Don’t run away, Emily; don’t!’ said he. “不要走，埃米莉。别走！”他说，
‘What can make ye afraid?’ “你怕什么呢？”
‘I’m not afraid, Captain Jolliffe. “我不怕，乔利夫船长。
Only—only I saw you all of a sudden, and—it made me jump!’ 只是——只是我突然看到你，吓了一跳！”
Her voice showed that her heart had jumped even more than the rest of her. 从她的声音可以听出，她内心的反应比她表现出来的还要强烈。
‘I just called as I was passing,’ he said. “我路过，正好进来看看。”他说。
‘For some paper?’ “要买纸吗？”
She hastened behind the counter. 她匆匆走到柜台后面。
‘No, no, Emily; why do ye get behind there? “不，不用，埃米莉。你为什么站到柜台后面？
Why not stay by me? 干吗不和我站在一起？
You seem to hate me.’ 你似乎讨厌我。”
‘I don’t hate you. “我不讨厌你。
How can I?’ 我怎么会讨厌你呢？”
‘Then come out, so that we can talk like Christians.’ “那你出来，这样我们就能正经聊聊天。”
Emily obeyed with a fitful laugh, till she stood again beside him in the open part of the shop. 埃米莉呵呵地笑了一声，顺从地走了出去，走到店里的空处，再次站在乔利夫身边。
‘There’s a dear,’ he said. “真是个可人儿。”他说。
‘You mustn’t say that, Captain Jolliffe; because the words belong to somebody else.’ “别这么说，乔利夫船长。这些话属于另外一个人。”
‘Ah! “唉！
I know what you mean. 我明白你的意思。
But, Emily, upon my life I didn’t know till this morning that you cared one bit about me, or I should not have done as I have done. 可是，埃米莉，我以生命担保，直到今天早上，我才意识到你心里是有我的，否则我绝不会那样做。
I have the best of feelings for Joanna, but I know that from the beginning she hasn’t cared for me more than in a friendly way; and I see now the one I ought to have asked to be my wife. 我对乔安娜非常中意，可我知道，从一开始她对我就只能算是朋友关系。我现在想清楚了到底该向哪一位求婚。
You know, Emily, when a man comes home from sea after a long voyage he’s as blind as a bat—he can’t see who’s who in women. 埃米莉，你知道，经过长途航行出海归来的男人就像只蝙蝠，是个睁眼瞎，分辨不清女人。
They are all alike to him, beautiful creatures, and he takes the first that comes easy, without thinking if she loves him, or if he might not soon love another better than her. 女人对他而言都是一样的，是一种美丽的生物。他会追求容易接近的女人，不管她是否爱自己，也不管他自己是否不久会爱上更胜于她的另一个。
From the first I inclined to you most, but you were so backward and shy that I thought you didn’t want me to bother ’ee, and so I went to Joanna.’ 最开始我更中意你，可你总是忸怩害羞，我以为你不希望我打扰你，于是就开始追求乔安娜。”
‘Don’t say any more, Mr. Jolliffe, don’t!’ said she, choking. “别说了，乔利夫先生，别说了！”她哽咽着说道，
‘You are going to marry Joanna next month, and it is wrong to—to—’ “你下个月就要和乔安娜结婚了，不——不应该——”
‘O, Emily, my darling!’ he cried, and clasped her little figure in his arms before she was aware. “唉，我亲爱的埃米莉！”他喊了一声。她还没有反应过来，他就紧紧拥住了她那弱小的身子。
Joanna, behind the curtain, turned pale, tried to withdraw her eyes, but could not. 乔安娜躲在帘子后，脸色变得苍白，她努力想移开视线，却做不到。
‘It is only you I love as a man ought to love the woman he is going to marry; and I know this from what Joanna has said, that she will willingly let me off! “只有你，让我像男人爱自己要娶的女人那样去爱。我从乔安娜的话中明白了这一点，并且明白了她很愿意放开我！
She wants to marry higher I know, and only said “Yes” to me out of kindness. 我知道她希望嫁给更体面的人。她答应我的求婚，不过是出于善意。
A fine, tall girl like her isn’t the sort for a plain sailor’s wife: you be the best suited for that.’ 像她那样漂亮高挑的女孩子，本不适合做平凡水手的妻子，你才是最适合的。”
He kissed her and kissed her again, her flexible form quivering in the agitation of his embrace. 他一遍遍吻着她，她柔软的身子在他的拥抱中激动得发抖。
‘I wonder—are you sure—Joanna is going to break off with you? “我不知道——你确定——乔安娜会和你分手吗？
O, are you sure? 唉，你真的确定吗？
Because—’ 因为——”
‘I know she would not wish to make us miserable. “我相信她不希望让我们俩痛苦。
She will release me.’ 她会让我解脱的。”
‘O, I hope—I hope she will! “唉，我希望——我希望如此！
Don’t stay any longer, Captain Jolliffe!’ 你现在走吧，乔利夫船长！”
He lingered, however, till a customer came for a penny stick of sealing-wax, and then he withdrew. 然而他一直呆着，直到一个顾客来买一便士的封口蜡时，他才恋恋不舍地离去。
Green envy had overspread Joanna at the scene. 看到此情此景，乔安娜妒火中烧。
She looked about for a way of escape. 她环顾四周，寻找逃脱的路。
To get out without Emily’s knowledge of her visit was indispensable. 她必须在不惊动埃米莉的情况下，离开她家。
She crept from the parlour into the passage, and thence to the front door of the house, where she let herself noiselessly into the street. 她从会客室慢慢挪到走廊，又潜行到房子前门，然后悄无声息地离开埃米莉家，走上街道。
The sight of that caress had reversed all her resolutions. 看到那两人拥抱后，她的决定完全改变了。
She could not let Shadrach go. 她不会放手谢德拉克。

But they are rich, and we are poor still!’ 可他们富得流油，而我们还是一贫如洗！”
The greater part of a year was desultorily spent. 大半年时间在不经意间就过去了。
She moved sadly about the house and shop, and the boys were still occupying themselves in and around the harbour. 乔安娜在家里和店里难过地转来转去。孩子们依然在海港做着各种工作。

‘My boys will have to live by steering the ships that the Lesters own; and I was once above her!’ “我儿子要靠给莱斯特家开船谋生，而我曾经过着比她优越的生活！”

She turned pale. 乔安娜的脸色变得煞白。

‘’Twould treble what I should get from the venture single-handed. “那会是我一个人去所赚钱的三倍。
Under my eye they would be as good as two more of myself.’ 有我照看，他们会像我自己一样毫发无损的。”
Later on she said: ‘Tell me more about this.’ 过了一阵，她又说：“再给我说说出海的事吧。”

‘O, well, there be risks. “唔，这个嘛，会有风险的。
Still...’ 不过……”
The idea grew and magnified, and the mother’s heart was crushed and stifled by it. 这个不断膨胀的想法充斥了乔安娜的心，压得她喘不过气。
Emmy was growing too patronizing; it could not be borne. 埃米莉越来越显得高人一等，实在让人无法忍受。
Shadrach’s wife could not help nagging him about their comparative poverty. 谢德拉克的妻子总是不停地向他抱怨自家比埃米莉家穷。
The young men, amiable as their father, when spoken to on the subject of a voyage of enterprise, were quite willing to embark; and though they, like their father, had no great love for the sea, they became quite enthusiastic when the proposal was detailed. 年轻的孩子们像他们父亲一样随和。当父母跟他们提起出海做生意的事时，他们非常乐意参与，尽管他们也和父亲一样，并不是很热爱航海，但随着这个计划的细节确定下来，他们的兴致也高了很多。
Everything now hung upon their mother’s assent. 现在，一切都等乔安娜点头了。
She withheld it long, but at last gave the word: the young men might accompany their father. 她克制了很久，最后还是开口了：孩子们可以陪着他们父亲一起去。
Shadrach was unusually cheerful about it: Heaven had preserved him hitherto, and he had uttered his thanks. 谢德拉克对此显得格外开心，迄今为止，上帝一直在保佑他，他也表达了对上帝的感恩。
God would not forsake those who were faithful to him. 上帝不会遗弃那些忠于他的子民的。
All that the Jolliffes possessed in the world was put into the enterprise. 乔利夫变卖了所有家产，都投进了这次生意。
The grocery stock was pared down to the least that possibly could afford a bare sustenance to Joanna during the absence, which was to last through the usual ‘New-f’nland spell.’ 杂货店的存货尽量削减，只留下出海期间够乔安娜维持基本生计的部分。而出海通常持续整整一个“纽芬兰季”。
How she would endure the weary time she hardly knew, for the boys had been with her formerly; but she nerved herself for the trial. 乔安娜不知道要怎样消磨漫漫长日。之前还有孩子们陪着她，不过她还是鼓起勇气，准备接受考验。
The ship was laden with boots and shoes, ready-made clothing, fishing-tackle, butter, cheese, cordage, sailcloth, and many other commodities; and was to bring back oil, furs, skins, fish, cranberries, and what else came to hand. 船上装满靴子、鞋子、成衣、捕鱼用具、黄油、奶酪、绳索、帆布以及其他各种货物；回来时，船上会载着石油、毛皮、兽皮、鱼、蔓越橘，以及其他任何能弄到手的东西。
But much trading to other ports was to be undertaken between the voyages out and homeward, and thereby much money made. 出海和回来的路上，他们还要在其他港口进行许多交易，因此会赚到大钱。
Chapter III 第三章
The brig sailed on a Monday morning in spring; but Joanna did not witness its departure. 春季的一个星期一早上，横帆船起航了，不过乔安娜没有去送行。
She could not bear the sight that she had been the means of bringing about. 她不忍面对自己一手促成的分离场景。
Knowing this, her husband told her overnight that they were to sail some time before noon next day hence when, awakening at five the next morning, she heard them bustling about downstairs, she did not hasten to descend, but lay trying to nerve herself for the parting, imagining they would leave about nine, as her husband had done on his previous voyage. 谢德拉克知道这些，因此离开的前一夜告诉她，他们大约在第二天中午之前出发。因此，第二天早上5点钟乔安娜醒来时，她听到他们在楼下来回忙碌，却没有急着下楼，而是躺着，试图鼓起勇气面对离别，她想着他们大约会在9点钟离开，就像她丈夫上一次出海时那样。
When she did descend she beheld words chalked upon the sloping face of the bureau; but no husband or sons. 后来，她终于下了楼，看到写字台的斜面上有粉笔写的字，可她丈夫和儿子们都不在了。
In the hastily-scrawled lines Shadrach said they had gone off thus not to pain her by a leave-taking; and the sons had chalked under his words: ‘Good-bye, mother!’ 谢德拉克匆匆地写了几句，说他们为了不让她因为告别难过，已经出发了，孩子们在谢德拉克的字下面写着：“再见，妈妈！”
She rushed to the quay, and looked down the harbour towards the blue rim of the sea, but she could only see the masts and bulging sails of the Joanna; no human figures. 乔安娜匆匆赶到码头，站在港口俯视着蔚蓝的海面，可是除了“乔安娜号”的桅杆和鼓起的船帆，她什么也看不到，一个人影也没有。
‘’Tis I have sent them!’ she said wildly, and burst into tears. “我就这样送走他们了！”她发狂般地说着，眼泪哗哗地流了出来。
In the house the chalked ‘Good-bye’ nearly broke her heart. 房间里用粉笔写的“再见”几乎让她的心都碎了。
But when she had re-entered the front room, and looked across at Emily’s, a gleam of triumph lit her thin face at her anticipated release from the thraldom of subservience. 可是再次进入前屋后，她看着对面埃米莉家，想到要从低人一等的境况中解脱出来了，瘦削的脸庞上也流露出了一丝胜利的表情。
To do Emily Lester justice, her assumption of superiority was mainly a figment of Joanna’s brain. 为了对埃米莉·莱斯特公平起见，我们必须指出，乔安娜认为埃米莉高高在上的想法，基本上都是她自己的臆想。
That the circumstances of the merchant’s wife were more luxurious than Joanna’s, the former could not conceal; though whenever the two met, which was not very often now, Emily endeavoured to subdue the difference by every means in her power. 埃米莉身为商人的妻子，生活条件比乔安娜优越，这是她无法隐藏的事实。然而，二人无论什么时候碰面（事实上现在她们也不常碰面了），埃米莉都尽力缩小两人之间的差距。
The first summer lapsed away; and Joanna meagrely maintained herself by the shop, which now consisted of little more than a window and a counter. 第一个夏季无声无息地过去了，乔安娜靠着杂货店勉强维持生计，店里现在除了窗户和柜台外，几乎没有其他东西了。
Emily was, in truth, her only large customer; and Mrs. Lester’s kindly readiness to buy anything and everything without questioning the quality had a sting of bitterness in it, for it was the uncritical attitude of a patron, and almost of a donor. 事实上，埃米莉是她唯一的大主顾。莱斯特太太不问质量，欣然购买任何东西，这种善意本身却带着一种令人心酸的刺痛，因为这种毫不挑剔的态度显示出她是个恩主，甚至可以说是个施舍者。
The long dreary winter moved on; the face of the bureau had been turned to the wall to protect the chalked words of farewell, for Joanna could never bring herself to rub them out; and she often glanced at them with wet eyes. 漫长而凄凉的冬季姗姗来临，写字台的正面现在朝着墙，以保护粉笔写的告别语，因为乔安娜怎么也不忍心把那些字擦掉。她常常看着那些字，眼里便蓄满了泪水。
Emily’s handsome boys came home for the Christmas holidays; the University was talked of for them; and still Joanna subsisted as it were with held breath, like a person submerged. 埃米莉家一表人才的男孩子们回来过圣诞节了，他们常常聊起上大学的事，而乔安娜依然小心翼翼地生活着，连呼吸都谨慎得仿佛一个溺水的人。
Only one summer more, and the ‘spell’ would end. 再过一个夏天，“纽芬兰季”就要结束了。
Towards the close of the time Emily called on her quondam friend. 归期快到时，埃米莉去拜访了她旧时的朋友。
She had heard that Joanna began to feel anxious; she had received no letter from husband or sons for some months. 埃米莉听说乔安娜开始感到焦虑了，她有几个月都没有收到丈夫和儿子们的信了。
Emily’s silks rustled arrogantly when, in response to Joanna’s almost dumb invitation, she squeezed through the opening of the counter and into the parlour behind the shop. 埃米莉一边回应乔安娜几近麻木的邀请，一边挤过柜台的开口，进入商店后面的客厅。她的丝绸衣服沙沙响着，显得颇为傲慢。
‘You are all success, and I am all the other way!’ said Joanna. “你什么都顺顺当当，而我却什么都不如意！”乔安娜说。
‘But why do you think so?’ said Emily. “你怎么会这样想呢？”埃米莉说，
‘They are to bring back a fortune, I hear.’ “我听说他们会赚大钱回来的。”
‘Ah! will they come? “呵，他们会回来吗？
The doubt is more than a woman can bear. 这种担惊受怕可不是一个女人能承受的。
All three in one ship—think of that! 三个人在一条船上——想想吧！
And I have not heard of them for months!’ 更别提我好几个月没有收到他们的消息了！”
‘But the time is not up. “可是还不到他们回来的时间。
You should not meet misfortune half-way.’ 你不会中途遭到厄运的。”
‘Nothing will repay me for the grief of their absence!’ “如果他们回不来了，那什么也不能弥补我的难过！”
‘Then why did you let them go? “那你为什么还放他们去呢？
You were doing fairly well.’ 你们家日子本来也不错。”
‘I made them go!’ she said, turning vehemently upon Emily. “是我让他们去的！”她说着，猛地向埃米莉发作了，
‘And I’ll tell you why! “让我告诉你为什么！
I could not bear that we should be only muddling on, and you so rich and thriving! 我受不了我们只是勉强度日，而你们家却过得红红火火！
Now I have told you, and you may hate me if you will!’ 我现在都告诉你了，你想讨厌我，随你便吧！”
‘I shall never hate you, Joanna.’ “我永远都不会讨厌你的，乔安娜。”
And she proved the truth of her words afterwards. 埃米莉后来证明了自己的话。
The end of autumn came, and the brig should have been in port; but nothing like the Joanna appeared in the channel between the sands. 秋季过去，帆船应该回港口了，可是沙洲间的水道里却看不到“乔安娜号”的影子。
It was now really time to be uneasy. 现在，乔安娜真的坐立不安了。
Joanna Jolliffe sat by the fire, and every gust of wind caused her a cold thrill. 她坐在炉火边，每一阵寒风吹过，都会打个寒战。
She had always feared and detested the sea; to her it was a treacherous, restless, slimy creature, glorying in the griefs of women. 乔安娜素来畏惧大海，厌恶大海，她觉得大海是个凶险、虚伪而永不满足的怪物，以给女人带来悲伤为荣。
‘Still,’ she said, ‘they must come!’ “不过，”她说，“他们肯定会回来！”
She recalled to her mind that Shadrach had said before starting that if they returned safe and sound, with success crowning their enterprise, he would go as he had gone after his shipwreck, and kneel with his sons in the church, and offer sincere thanks for their deliverance. 她记得谢德拉克出发前说过，如果他们平安归来，并且是满载而归，他要像那次船只失事后那样去教堂，和孩子们跪在那里，为得救而虔诚地感谢上帝。
She went to church regularly morning and afternoon, and sat in the most forward pew, nearest the chancel-step. 乔安娜每天上下午都按时去教堂，她总是坐在第一排靠背长凳上，那里离高坛的台阶最近。
Her eyes were mostly fixed on that step, where Shadrach had knelt in the bloom of his young manhood: she knew to an inch the spot which his knees had pressed twenty winters before; his outline as he had knelt, his hat on the step beside him. 她总是盯着那级台阶，那是当年风华正茂的谢德拉克跪过的台阶，乔安娜非常清楚地记得20年前谢德拉克双膝跪下的地方，记得他跪着时的轮廓，他的帽子就放在身旁的台阶上。
God was good. 上帝是仁慈的。
Surely her husband must kneel there again: a son on each side as he had said; George just here, Jim just there. 她丈夫肯定还会再次跪在那里，像他说过的那样，两个儿子跪在他身边，一边是乔治，另一边是吉姆。
By long watching the spot as she worshipped it became as if she saw the three returned ones there kneeling; the two slim outlines of her boys, the more bulky form between them; their hands clasped, their heads shaped against the eastern wall. 乔安娜做礼拜时长时间盯着那级台阶，她仿佛看到父子三人已经回来，正跪在那里：两个修长的身躯是她的儿子，还有中间那更为粗壮的身影，三人的手紧紧握着，头部的轮廓映在东边的墙上。
The fancy grew almost to an hallucination: she could never turn her worn eyes to the step without seeing them there. 这个想法简直成了幻觉，她疲倦的眼神一落到那级台阶，就总会看到父子三人跪在那里。
Nevertheless they did not come. 然而，他们并没有回来。
Heaven was merciful, but it was not yet pleased to relieve her soul. 上天是仁慈的，可它却不愿让乔安娜的灵魂得到解脱。
This was her purgation for the sin of making them the slaves of her ambition. 她把父子三人变成她自己野心的奴隶，这是她应得的报应。
But it became more than purgation soon, and her mood approached despair. 可这很快就不只是报应了，乔安娜的情绪接近崩溃了。
Months had passed since the brig had been due, but it had not returned. 帆船的归期过了好几个月，船却依然没有回来。
Joanna was always hearing or seeing evidences of their arrival. 乔安娜总觉得自己听到或者看到了什么，证明他们回来了。
When on the hill behind the port, whence a view of the open Channel could be obtained, she felt sure that a little speck on the horizon, breaking the eternally level waste of waters southward, was the truck of the Joana’s mainmast. 有时她在港口后面的小山上，从那里看着开阔的海峡，她确信地平线上有个小点打破了永远平静的南流之水，她觉得那是“乔安娜号”主桅的桅杆帽。
Or when indoors, a shout or excitement of any kind at the corner of the Town Cellar, where the High Street joined the Quay, caused her to spring to her feet and cry: ‘’Tis they!’ 有时候她在家，只要听到主街与码头相接之处的镇酒窖拐角传来大叫声或者喧闹声，就会匆匆站起，高喊道：“是他们！”
But it was not. 然而却不是。
The visionary forms knelt every Sunday afternoon on the chancel-step, but not the real. 每周日下午，她都幻想着他们跪在高坛的台阶上，事实上他们却是无迹可寻。
Her shop had, as it were, eaten itself hollow. 她的杂货店可以说是已经坐吃山空了。
In the apathy which had resulted from her loneliness and grief she had ceased to take in the smallest supplies, and thus had sent away her last customer. 由于寂寞与悲伤，她对开店已经漠不关心，一点儿货也不再进，于是最后一个客人也不来了。
In this strait Emily Lester tried by every means in her power to aid the afflicted woman; but she met with constant repulses. 在这种情况下，埃米莉·莱斯特竭尽全力接济这个孤苦的女人，可每次都被断然拒绝。
‘I don’t like you! “我讨厌你！
I can’t bear to see you!’ 我不能忍受看到你！”
Joanna would whisper hoarsely when Emily came to her and made advances. 埃米莉来看望乔安娜的时候，乔安娜总是这么嘶哑着嗓子，低声朝她怒吼。
‘But I want to help and soothe you, Joanna,’ Emily would say. “可我想帮助你，安慰你，乔安娜。”埃米莉总是这样回答。
‘You are a lady, with a rich husband and fine sons! “你是个阔太太，你丈夫有钱，你儿子有出息！
What can you want with a bereaved crone like me!’ 你想从我这个失去亲人的老女人这里得到什么！”
‘Joanna, I want this: I want you to come and live in my house, and not stay alone in this dismal place any longer.’ “乔安娜，我想这样：我想让你住到我家去，不要再一个人住在这个凄凉的地方了。”
‘And suppose they come and don’t find me at home? “万一他们回来了找不到我怎么办？
You wish to separate me and mine! 你想把我和我的家人分开！
No, I’ll stay here. 不，我要留在这里。
I don’t like you, and I can’t thank you, whatever kindness you do me!’ 我讨厌你，不管你怎么帮我，我都不会感谢你的！”
However, as time went on Joanna could not afford to pay the rent of the shop and house without an income. 然而，随着时间的流逝，乔安娜因为没有收入，付不起商店和房子的租金了。
She was assured that all hope of the return of Shadrach and his sons was vain, and she reluctantly consented to accept the asylum of the Lesters’ house. 她已经认命了，知道谢德拉克和孩子们回来的希望很渺茫，最终不情愿地接受帮助，同意住到莱斯特家去。
Here she was allotted a room of her own on the second floor, and went and came as she chose, without contact with the family. 她在三楼得到一间属于她自己的房间，来去自如，也不和莱斯特家的人来往。
Her hair greyed and whitened, deep lines channeled her forehead, and her form grew gaunt and stooping. 她的头发渐渐变成花白，最终全白了，额头上皱纹横生，身体也变得骨瘦如柴，驼背得厉害。
But she still expected the lost ones, and when she met Emily on the staircase she would say morosely: ‘I know why you’ve got me here! 可她依然期盼着父子三人回来，有时在楼梯上遇到埃米莉，她就会愠怒地说：“我知道你为什么让我住在这儿！
They’ll come, and be disappointed at not finding me at home, and perhaps go away again; and then you’ll be revenged for my taking Shadrach away from ’ee!’ 他们回到家里找不到我，会很失望，或许会再次离开，那样你就报了我把谢德拉克从你手中抢走的仇了！”
Emily Lester bore these reproaches from the grief-stricken soul. 埃米莉·莱斯特总是默默承受着这个悲痛欲绝的人发出的指责。
She was sure—all the people of Havenpool were sure—that Shadrach and his sons could not return. 她确信——实际上黑文普尔镇的所有人都确信——谢德拉克和他的儿子们不可能回来了。
For years the vessel had been given up as lost. 好几年过去了，人们已经对那艘失踪的船不抱希望了。
Nevertheless, when awakened at night by any noise, Joanna would rise from bed and glance at the shop opposite by the light from the flickering lamp, to make sure it was not they. 然而，每当夜里被一丁点儿响动吵醒，乔安娜总会从床上跳起来，借着忽明忽暗的灯光看一眼对面的杂货店，以确定是不是那父子三人。
It was a damp and dark December night, six years after the departure of the brig Joanna. 那是一个12月的夜里，潮湿漆黑，距“乔安娜号”帆船出海已经有六年了。
The wind was from the sea, and brought up a fishy mist which mopped the face like moist flannel. 风从海上吹来，带来一阵薄雾，散发着鱼腥味，拂到脸上就像湿润的法兰绒。
Joanna had prayed her usual prayer for the absent ones with more fervour and confidence than she had felt for months, and had fallen asleep about eleven. 乔安娜像往常一样为久别未归的三人做了祈祷，不过这次带着数月来都没有过的虔诚和信心，11点左右，她就睡着了。
It must have been between one and two when she suddenly started up. 不到2点钟的时候，乔安娜突然惊醒了。
She had certainly heard steps in the street, and the voices of Shadrach and her sons calling at the door of the grocery shop. 她确信自己听到了街上的脚步声，还有谢德拉克和孩子们在杂货店门口叫门的声音。
She sprang out of bed, and, hardly knowing what clothing she dragged on herself; hastened down Emily’s large and carpeted staircase, put the candle on the hall-table, unfastened the bolts and chain, and stepped into the street. 她从床上一跃而起，来不及披好衣服，就匆匆下了埃米莉家宽敞的、铺着地毯的楼梯。她把蜡烛放在大厅的桌子上，打开门闩和门链，走到街上。
The mist, blowing up the street from the Quay, hindered her seeing the shop, although it was so near; but she had crossed to it in a moment. 从码头方向蔓延而来的薄雾笼罩在街道上，虽然杂货店近在眼前，乔安娜却看不清楚。不过她很快就穿过街道，向杂货店走过去。
How was it? 怎么样呢？
Nobody stood there. 空无一人。
The wretched woman walked wildly up and down with her bare feet—there was not a soul. 这个可怜的女人光着脚，发疯般地走来走去，却一个人影也没有。
She returned and knocked with all her might at the door which had once been her own—they might have been admitted for the night, unwilling to disturb her till the morning. 她回到曾经属于她的杂货店门口，用尽力气敲着门——或许他们不愿意夜里打扰她，获准在店里过夜，要到第二天早上才告诉她。
It was not till several minutes had elapsed that the young man who now kept the shop looked out of an upper window, and saw the skeleton of something human standing below half-dressed. 不一会儿，如今拥有杂货店的年轻人从楼上的窗户里探出头来。他看到一个衣衫不整的瘦削人影站在下面。
‘Has anybody come?’ asked the form. “有人来吗？”那身影问道。
‘O, Mrs. Jolliffe, I didn’t know it was you,’ said the young man kindly, for he was aware how her baseless expectations moved her. “哦，乔利夫太太，我不知道是你，”年轻人和蔼地说道，因为他知道乔安娜是被虚幻的期待驱使着，
‘No; nobody has come.’ “没有，没有人来。”
