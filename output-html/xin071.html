What the Rose did to the Cypress[1] 玫瑰对柏树做了什么[1]
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
Once upon a time a great king of the East, named Saman-lalposh,[2] had three brave and clever sons—Tahmasp, Qamas, and Almas-ruh-baksh．[3] 从前，东方有一位伟大的国王，名叫萨曼—拉波什[2]，他有三个勇敢又聪明的儿子——塔玛斯普、喀玛斯和阿玛斯—鲁—巴克什[3]。
One day, when the king was sitting in his hall of audience, his eldest son, Prince Tahmasp, came before him, and after greeting his father with due respect, said: ‘O my royal father! 一天，国王正坐在觐会厅里，他的大儿子塔玛斯普王子来到他面前。他先向父王恭恭敬敬地问了安，接着说道：“哦，父王！
I am tired of the town; if you will give me leave, I will take my servants to-morrow and will go into the country and hunt on the hill-skirts; and when I have taken some game I will come back, at evening-prayer time.’ 我在城里呆厌了，如果您恩准，我明天要带着随从们去乡下，到山边打猎。等我打着了猎物，会在傍晚祷告的时候回来的。”
His father consented, and sent with him some of his own trusted servants, and also hawks, and falcons, hunting dogs, cheetahs and leopards. 他父王同意了，派了自己的几个可靠随从跟王子一起去，还给他带上鹰、隼、猎犬、猎豹和花豹。
At the place where the prince intended to hunt he saw a most beautiful deer. 在准备打猎的地方，王子看到一头特别美的鹿。
He ordered that it should not be killed, but trapped or captured with a noose. 他下令不准杀死那头鹿，只准设陷阱或者用套索活捉。
The deer looked about for a place where he might escape from the ring of the beaters, and spied one unwatched close to the prince himself. 那头鹿环顾四周，想找个地方逃出驱猎者的包围。它发现王子身边有个没人把守的口子。
It bounded high and leaped right over his head, got out of the ring, and tore like the eastern wind into the waste. 它高高跳起，跃过王子头顶，冲出了包围圈，像一阵东风似的飞奔到了原野上。
The prince put spurs to his horse and pursued it; and was soon lost to the sight of his followers. 王子策马追逐，很快他就在随从们的视野中消失了。
Until the world-lighting sun stood above his head in the zenith he did not take his eyes off the deer; suddenly it disappeared behind some rising ground, and with all his search he could not find any further trace of it. 王子紧盯着鹿不放，一直到普照世间的太阳悬在头顶、高挂中天，他也没把眼睛挪开过。突然，那鹿消失在一块高地后面，王子四处搜寻，再没找到鹿的踪迹。
He was now drenched in sweat, and he breathed with pain; and his horse’s tongue hung from its mouth with thirst. 王子现在浑身是汗，连呼吸都生疼。他的马渴得往外耷拉着舌头。
He dismounted and toiled on, with bridle on arm, praying and casting himself on the mercy of heaven. 他下了马艰难前行，胳膊拽着马笼头，边走边祈祷上天保佑。
Then his horse fell and surrendered its life to God. 这时，他的马倒下了，一命呜呼。
On and on he went across the sandy waste, weeping and with burning breast, till at length a hill rose into sight. 他流着眼泪在沙地里一直走啊，走啊，胸口火辣辣地疼。终于，一座小山出现在他眼前。
He mustered his strength and climbed to the top, and there he found a giant tree whose foot kept firm the wrinkled earth, and whose crest touched the very heaven. 王子强打精神爬上山顶，见到一棵巨树，树根牢牢扎在沟壑纵横的土地里，树冠触到了天空。
Its branches had put forth a glory of leaves, and there were grass and a spring underneath it, and flowers of many colours. 巨树枝叶繁茂，树下绿草丛生，一条溪流涓涓而过，鲜花姹紫嫣红。
Gladdened by this sight, he dragged himself to the water’s edge, drank his fill, and returned thanks for his deliverance from thirst. 王子见了很高兴，他拖着身子走到水边喝了个饱，为自己能不再干渴而感恩。
He looked about him and, to his amazement, saw close by a royal seat. 他环顾四周，惊奇地发现身边有一个王座。
While he was pondering what could have brought this into the merciless desert, a man drew near who was dressed like a faqir, and had bare head and feet, but walked with the free carriage of a person of rank. 他正想着这王座怎么会在荒寂无情的沙漠里的时候，有个人走来了。那人穿得像个苦行僧，光着头，还光着脚，可走起路来却是一副毫不拘谨的贵族仪态。
His face was kind, and wise and thoughtful, and he came on and spoke to the prince. 他一脸和气，看起来睿智深沉。然后他走了过来，同王子说道：
‘O good youth! how did you come here? “哦，好小伙！你如何到此？
Who are you? 你是何人？
Where do you come from?’ 来自何处？”
The prince told everything just as it had happened to him, and then respectfully added: ‘I have made known my own circumstances to you, and now I venture to beg you to tell me your own. 王子把发生的事情一五一十地告诉了他，接着恭敬地说道：“我已告知您我的境遇。现在，我冒昧请求您，告诉我您的境况。
Who are you? 您是何人？
How did you come to make your dwelling in this wilderness?’ 如何前来居留于此荒野之中？”
To this the faqir replied: ‘O youth! it would be best for you to have nothing to do with me and to know nothing of my fortunes, for my story is fit neither for telling nor for hearing.’ 那僧人答道：“哦，年轻人！于我，你不识为好；于我的际遇，你不知为好。我所经之事，不宜讲，亦不宜听。”
The prince, however, pleaded so hard to be told, that at last there was nothing to be done but to let him hear. 可是王子苦苦哀求，最后僧人实在别无他法，只好说给他听。
‘Learn and know, O young man! that I am King Janangir[4] of Babylon, and that once I had army and servants, family and treasure; untold wealth and belongings. “哦，听着，年轻人！我是巴比伦的贾南吉尔国王。我曾拥有军队和仆从，有家庭和财富。我的财产多得无人能说清。
The Most High God gave me seven sons who grew up well versed in all princely arts. 至高无上的主赐予我七个儿子，个个成才，精通各门高雅技艺。
My eldest son heard from travellers that in Turkistan, on the Chinese frontier, there is a king named Quimus, the son of Timus, and that he has an only child, a daughter named Mihr-afruz, [5] who, under all the azure heaven, is unrivalled for beauty. 我的大儿子从旅人那里听说，在中国边境上的土耳其斯坦，有位国王名叫古伊穆斯，是提穆斯之子，他有个独生女儿，名叫米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹[5]，她的美貌天下无双。
Princes come from all quarters to ask her hand, and on one and all she imposes a condition. 各地的王子都向她求婚，而她向每个人都提出了一个条件。
She says to them: “I know a riddle; and I will marry anyone who answers it, and will bestow on him all my possessions. 她对他们说：‘我有一个谜语，谁能答出这个谜语我就嫁给谁，我的所有财产也全都归他。
But if a suitor cannot answer my question I cut off his head and hang it on the battlements of the citadel.” 不过，要是求婚之人答不出我的问题，我就砍下他的头挂在城垛上。’
The riddle she asks is, “What did the rose do to the cypress?” 她出的谜语是‘玫瑰对柏树做了什么？’
‘Now, when my son heard this tale, he fell in love with that unseen girl, and he came to me lamenting and bewailing himself. “我儿子听了这个故事，就爱上了那个素未谋面的姑娘，还在我面前自哀自戚。
Nothing that I could say had the slightest effect on him. 不管我说什么，对他都起不了一点儿作用。
I said: “Oh my son! if there must be fruit of this fancy of yours, I will lead forth a great army against King Quimus. 我说：‘哦，我的孩子！若你这痴想定要有个结果，我将率大军前去拜会古伊穆斯国王。
If he will give you his daughter freely, well and good; and if not, I will ravage his kingdom and bring her away by force.” 他若自愿将女儿嫁给你，那甚合我意，一切都好。他若不肯，我将攻掠其国，强夺其女。’
This plan did not please him; he said: “It is not right to lay a kingdom waste and to destroy a palace so that I may attain my desire. 这计划并不合他心意，他说：‘为得我所欲，而灭国毁都，此为不义。
I will go alone; I will answer the riddle, and win her in this way.” 我要独自一人前去。我将解答谜题，以此得到她。’
At last, out of pity for him, I let him go. 最后，因对他怜悯，我放他去了。
He reached the city of King Quimus. 他到了古伊穆斯国王的都城。
He was asked the riddle and could not give the true answer; and his head was cut off and hung upon the battlements. 那个谜语被提出来要他解答，可他没能答对。他的头被砍了下来，挂在了城垛上。
Then I mourned him in black raiment for forty days. 而后，我为他身着黑衣，哀悼四十日。
“After this another and another of my sons were seized by the same desire, and in the end all my seven sons went, and all were killed. “此后，我的儿子们一个接一个地被同样的渴慕所俘。最后，我那七个儿子都去了，全都被杀死了。
In grief for their death I have abandoned my throne, and I abide here in this desert, withholding my hand from all State business and wearing myself away in sorrow.’ 我因他们的死悲恸不已，遂抛弃王位，居于这沙漠之中，不再过问国事，任凭悲伤将我磨蚀。”
Prince Tahmasp listened to this tale, and then the arrow of love for that unseen girl struck his heart also. 塔玛斯普王子听了这故事，内心也被爱情之箭射中，爱上了那个素未谋面的姑娘。
Just at this moment of his ill-fate his people came up, and gathered round him like moths round a light. 就在这厄运降临的时刻，他的随从们赶来了，像飞蛾绕灯火似的朝王子围拢过来。
They brought him a horse, fleet as the breeze of the dawn; he set his willing foot in the stirrup of safety and rode off. 他们给他牵来一匹疾如晨风的马。王子开心地踩上马镫，骑上马平安离开了。
As the days went by the thorn of love rankled in his heart, and he became the very example of lovers, and grew faint and feeble. 日子一天天过去，爱情的刺总挠着他的心，他就像所有相思之人一样，变得虚弱无力。
At last his confidants searched his heart and lifted the veil from the face of his love, and then set the matter before his father, King Saman-lal-posh. 最后他的密友探询他的心事，掀开了他心里那爱情的面纱，并将此事禀报他父亲，萨曼—拉波什国王。
‘Your son, Prince Tahmasp, loves distractedly the Princess Mihr-afruz, daughter of King Quimus, son of Timus.’ “您的儿子，塔玛斯普王子，疯狂爱着米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹公主，提穆斯之子古伊穆斯国王的女儿。”
Then they told the king all about her and her doings. 接着，他们告诉国王关于她的一切和她的所作所为。
A mist of sadness clouded the king’s mind, and he said to his son: ‘If this thing is so, I will in the first place send a courier with friendly letters to King Quimus, and will ask the hand of his daughter for you. 一团愁云浮上了国王心头，他对他儿子说道：“若果真如此，我先派使者给古伊穆斯国王送上文牒与他交好，并请他将女儿许配给你。
I will send an abundance of gifts, and a string of camels laden with flashing stones and rubies of Badakhsham. In this way I will bring her and her suite, and I will give her to you to be your solace. 我会送上许多赠礼，带上一队骆驼，载满炫光之石和巴达克沙的红宝石。这样，我会把她和她的侍从带回来，让她与你相伴。
But if King Quimus is unwilling to give her to you, I will pour a whirlwind of soldiers upon him, and I will bring to you, in this way, that most consequential of girls.’ 不过，若是古伊穆斯国王不愿将她许给你，我旋即派兵大举进攻，这样，我会把那最重要的姑娘带来给你。”
But the prince said that this plan would not be right, and that he would go himself, and would answer the riddle. 可是王子说这个计划不合道义，还说他要自己去，他要去解答那个谜。
Then the king’s wise men said: ‘This is a very weighty matter; it would be best to allow the prince to set out accompanied by some persons in whom you have confidence. 国王的谋士们说道：“此事重大，还是准许王子由您信赖之人陪同前去为好。
Maybe he will repent and come back.’ 或许他将反悔而后折返。”
So King Saman ordered all preparations for the journey to be made, and then Prince Tahmasp took his leave and set out, accompanied by some of the courtiers, and taking with him a string of two-humped and raven-eyed camels laden with jewels, and gold, and costly stuffs. 于是萨曼国王下令为旅途作好一切准备。接着，塔玛斯普王子便辞行启程，由几位使者陪同，带了一队眼睛乌亮的双峰骆驼，满载着珠宝、黄金和价值连城的宝贝。
By stage after stage, and after many days’ journeying, he arrived at the city of King Quimus. 走了一程又一程，经过许多天的长途跋涉，他到达了古伊穆斯国王的都城。
What did he see? 他看到了什么？
A towering citadel whose foot kept firm the wrinkled earth, and whose battlements touched the blue heaven. 一座高耸的城堡牢牢竖立在沟壑纵横的土地上，城垛触到了蓝天。
He saw hanging from its battlements many heads, but it had not the least effect upon him that these were heads of men of rank; he listened to no advice about laying aside his fancy, but rode up to the gate and on into the heart of the city. 他看到城垛上挂着很多人头，全是王公贵族们的头，可这一点儿也影响不了他。那些要他放下痴想的劝告，他一句也不听，反而骑着马到了城门口，接着骑到了城中央。
The place was so splendid that the eyes of the ages have never seen its like, and there, in an open square, he found a tent of crimson satin set up, and beneath it two jewelled drums with jewelled sticks. 这里富丽堂皇，连上了年纪的人也从没见过像这样的地方。王子看见一个露天广场上搭着一顶深红色的绸缎帐篷，下边是两面镶了珠宝的鼓，配着镶了珠宝的鼓槌。
These drums were put there so that the suitors of the princess might announce their arrival by beating on them, after which some one would come and take them to the king’s presence. 这些鼓摆在那里，是用来让公主的求婚者击鼓宣告自己的到来的，击完鼓就会有人来把他们带到国王面前。
The sight of the drums stirred the fire of Prince Tahmasp’s love. 塔玛斯普王子看到这些鼓，心中燃起爱火。
He dismounted, and moved towards them; but his companions hurried after and begged him first to let them go and announce him to the king, and said that then, when they had put their possessions in a place of security, they would enter into the all important matter of the princess. 他下马走向它们。可随行的人们赶忙跟来，求他先让他们去向国王禀报他的到来，还说等他们把自己的东西放到了安全的地方，就会着手商讨关于公主的这桩大事。
The prince, however, replied that he was there for one thing only; that his first duty was to beat the drums and announce himself as a suitor, when he would be taken, as such, to the king, who would then give him proper lodgment. 可是王子回答说，他来这里只为一件事。他的首要任务便是击鼓以表明自己求婚者的身份，接着他会以此被带去见国王，而国王会给他安排合适的住处。
So he struck upon the drums, and at once summoned an officer who took him to King Quimus. 于是他击了鼓，马上就召来一个大臣，把他带到了古伊穆斯国王面前。
When the king saw how very young the prince looked, and that he was still drinking of the fountain of wonder, he said: ‘O youth! leave aside this fancy which my daughter has conceived in the pride of her beauty. 国王见王子还很年轻，且依旧耽于幻想，便对他说：“哦，年轻人！放下对我那女儿的痴想吧，她不过是自恃美貌才有了那念头。
No one can answer her riddle, and she has done to death many men who had had no pleasure in life nor tasted its charms. 没人能解答她的谜题。她已经让很多生无所悦亦难察其美之人断送了性命。
God forbid that your spring also should be ravaged by the autumn winds of martyrdom.’ 主不容汝之春荣同摧于殉亡之秋风。”
All his urgency, however, had no effect in making the prince withdraw. 国王的一切劝告都没能让王子退却。
At length it was settled between them that three days should be given to pleasant hospitality and that then should follow what had to be said and done. 最后，他们约定，国王将盛情款待王子三日，三日之后，再行那必说必做之事。
Then the prince went to his own quarters and was treated as became his station. 接着王子便去往自己的住处，受着与他地位相当的礼遇。
King Quimus now sent for his daughter and for her mother, Gul-rukh, [6] and talked to them. 古伊穆斯国王派人传来他的女儿和她母后，古丽—鲁克[6]，并同她们谈话。
He said to Mihr-afruz: ‘Listen to me, you cruel flirt! 他对米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹说道：“听我说，你这卖俏的无情种！
Why do you persist in this folly? 你为何固守这蠢念不放？
Now there has come to ask your hand a prince of the east, so handsome that the very sun grows modest before the splendour of his face; he is rich, and he has brought gold and jewels, all for you, if you will marry him. 如今有一位东方的王子前来向你求婚，他美得连太阳见了他那俊脸都得逊让三分。他很富有，还带来了黄金和珠宝，你若嫁给他，那些全是给你的。
A better husband you will not find.’ 你再也找不出比他更好的丈夫了。”
But all the arguments of father and mother were wasted, for her only answer was: ‘O my father! 可父王和母后说什么道理都是白费口舌，她唯一的回答只是：“哦，我的父王！
I have sworn to myself that I will not marry, even if a thousand years go by, unless someone answers my riddle, and that I will give myself to that man only who does answer it.’ 我已对自己起誓，纵使一千年过去，我也不会出嫁，除非有人能答出我的谜，我只嫁给解开谜题的那个人。”
The three days passed; then the riddle was asked: ‘What did the rose do to the cypress?’ 约定的三日之期过去了。而后，公主提出了谜语：“玫瑰对柏树做了什么？”
The prince had an eloquent tongue, which could split a hair, and without hesitation he replied to her with a verse: ‘Only the Omnipotent has knowledge of secrets; if any man says, “I know” do not believe him.’ 王子素来能言善辩，舌灿莲花。他毫不迟疑，以一节经文回答她：“唯万能之主方知奥秘。若有人道：‘我知，’不可信他。”
Then a servant fetched in the polluted, blue-eyed headsman, who asked: ‘Whose sun of life has come near its setting?’ took the prince by the arm, placed him upon the cloth of execution, and then, all merciless and stony hearted, cut his head from his body and hung it on the battlements. 然后，一名侍从带来了一个一身污脏、长着蓝眼的刽子手。那刽子手问道：“何人之命，日薄西山？”他抓起王子的胳膊，把他撂在行刑布上，接着便冷酷无情地砍下了他的头，挂到了城垛上。
The news of the death of Prince Tahmasp plunged his father into despair and stupefaction. 塔玛斯普王子死去的消息让他的父王陷入绝望，心神恍惚。
He mourned for him in black raiment for forty days; and then, a few days later, his second son, Prince Qamas, extracted from him leave to go too; and he, also, was put to death. 他身着黑衣为儿子哀悼了四十天。过了些日子，他的二儿子，喀玛斯王子，也获准前去。他也被杀死了。
One son only now remained, the brave, eloquent, happy-natured Prince Almas-ruh-bakhsh. 现在，国王只剩下英勇无畏、能言善辩又生性达观的阿玛斯—鲁—巴克什王子这一个儿子了。
One day, when his father sat brooding over his lost children, Almas came before him and said: ‘O father mine! the daughter of King Quimus has done my two brothers to death; I wish to avenge them upon her.’ 一天，国王正坐着为自己死去的儿子们黯然神伤。这时，阿玛斯来到他面前，说道：“哦，我的父王！古伊穆斯国王的女儿杀死了我的两个哥哥，我愿为他们向她报仇。”
These words brought his father to tears. 听了这话，国王流下泪来。
‘O light of your father!’ he cried, ‘I have no one left but you, and now you ask me to let you go to your death.’ “哦！为父的命中之光啊！”国王喊道，“我只剩下你了，可如今连你也要我放你去赴死。”
‘Dear father!’ pleaded the prince, ‘until I have lowered the pride of that beauty, and have set her here before you, I cannot settle down or indeed sit down off my feet.’ “亲爱的父王！”王子哀求道，“我若不能挫了那美人的傲气，不能把她带到您面前，就寝食难安，不得片刻安宁。”
In the end he, too, got leave to go; but he went without a following and alone. 最后，他也获准前去了。不过他没有带一个随从，孤身一人上了路。
Like his brothers, he made the long journey to the city of Quimus the son of Timus; like them he saw the citadel, but he saw there the heads of Tahmasp and Qamas. 和他的哥哥们一样，他长途跋涉到达了提穆斯之子古伊穆斯的都城。他也一样看到了那城垛，不过，他在那上面看见了塔玛斯普和喀玛斯的人头。
He went about in the city, saw the tent and the drums, and then went out again to a village not far off. 他在城里四处走动，见到了那顶帐篷和那两面鼓，接着他又出了城，到了不远处的一个村子里。
Here he found out a very old man who had a wife 120 years old, or rather more. 他在这里找到一位年纪很大的老人。老人的妻子有一百二十岁了，也许还不止。
Their lives were coming to their end, but they had never beheld face of child of their own. 他们活不了多久了，却没能有过一个自己的孩子。
They were glad when the prince came to their house, and they dealt with him as with a son. 王子到他们家里的时候，两位老人很高兴，他们像待亲儿子一样待他。
He put all his belongings into their charge, and fastened his horse in their out-house. 王子把他的所有东西都交给他们照管，把马拴在外屋。
Then he asked them not to speak of him to anyone, and to keep his affairs secret. 他让他们不要向任何人提起自己，不要泄露他的事。
He exchanged his royal dress for another, and next morning, just as the sun looked forth from its eastern oratory, he went again into the city. 他换下了自己的贵族衣衫。第二天一早，太阳刚从东边探出头，王子又进城去了。
He turned over in his mind without ceasing how he was to find out the meaning of the riddle, and to give them a right answer, and who could help him, and how to avenge his brothers. 他心里不停琢磨着，要怎么才能弄明白那个谜语的意思，并给出正解，还思量着有谁能帮他，又要怎么给哥哥们报仇。
He wandered about the city, but heard nothing of service, for there was no one in all that land who understood the riddle of Princess Mihr-afruz. 他在城里到处走，可是没听到一点儿有用的事。因为在那个地方，没有人明白米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹公主的谜。
One day he thought he would go to her own palace and see if he could learn anything there, so he went out to her garden-house. 有一天，王子心想，他要到公主的宫殿里去，看看在那里能不能找到点儿什么。于是他出了门，往她的花园宅邸去了。
It was a very splendid place, with a wonderful gateway, and walls like Alexander’s ramparts. 那是个很美的地方，大门建得华丽雄伟，围墙好似亚历山大大帝的城墙。
Many gate-keepers were on guard, and there was no chance of passing them. 大门由众多侍卫把守着，根本不可能通行。
His heart was full of bitterness, but he said to himself: ‘All will be well! it is here I shall get what I want.’ 王子满心苦涩，但是他对自己说：“一切都会好的！我会在这儿找到我想要的东西的。”
He went round outside the garden wall hoping to find a gap, and he made supplication in the Court of Supplications and prayed, ‘O Holder of the hand of the helpless! show me my way.’ 他绕着花园外墙走着，希望能找到个缺口。他在祈愿殿里祈求道：“哦，无助之人的恩主啊！为我指明道路吧！”
While he prayed he bethought himself that he could get into the garden with a stream of inflowing water. 他祈祷的时候想着自己能顺着流入花园的水道进去。
He looked carefully round, fearing to be seen, stripped, slid into the stream and was carried within the great walls. 他仔细地察看四周，生怕被人看见，接着便脱掉衣服，溜进河里。水流把他带到了巨大的围墙之内。
There he hid himself till his loin cloth was dry. 他在那里一直藏到缠腰布干透了为止。
The garden was a very Eden, with running water amongst its lawns, with flowers and the lament of doves and the jug-jug of nightingales. 这花园就像个伊甸园，绿草遍地，流水淙淙，鲜花满园，有鸽子喃喃，有夜莺啾啾。
It was a place to steal the senses from the brain, and he wandered about and saw the house, but there seemed to be no one there. 这里美得勾人心神。王子四处走着，看到了宅邸，可看上去好像没人在里头。
In the forecourt was a royal seat of polished jasper, and in the middle of the platform was a basin of purest water that flashed like a mirror. 宅邸前庭有一张光洁的碧玉宝座，平台中央的池盆里装着澄净的水，水面像明镜一样闪闪发光。
He pleased himself with these sights for a while, and then went back to the garden and hid himself from the gardeners and passed the night. 他在这景致里享受了一会儿，又回到花园里，躲开园丁藏了起来，就这么过了一夜。
Next morning he put on the appearance of a madman and wandered about till he came to a lawn where several pert-faced girls were amusing themselves. 第二天一早，他装成疯子四处游荡，直到他来到一片草地上，看见几个面容俏丽的姑娘正在那里嬉戏。
On a throne, jewelled and overspread with silken stuffs, sat a girl the splendour of whose beauty lighted up the place, and whose ambergris and attar perfumed the whole air. 在一张镶着宝石、铺满绫罗绸缎的宝座上，坐着一位姑娘，她的美貌照亮了四周，她搽的龙涎香和花精油熏香了整个空气。
‘That must be Mihrafruz,’ he thought, ‘she is indeed lovely.’ “那个一定就是米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹了，”王子心想，“她确实很美。”
Just then one of the attendants came to the water’s edge to fill a cup, and though the prince was in hiding, his face was reflected in the water. 就在那时，一个侍女拿着杯子到水边舀水。王子虽然藏起来了，脸却映在水里。
When she saw this image she was frightened, and let her cup fall into the stream, and thought, ‘Is it an angel, or a peri, or a man?’ 那侍女见了倒影吓了一跳，连杯子都掉进了河里，她心想：“这是天使，是精灵，还是人？”
Fear and trembling took hold of her, and she screamed as women scream. 她吓得发抖，尖声叫了起来。
Then some of the other girls came and took her to the princess who asked: ‘What is the matter, pretty one?’ 另外几个姑娘过来了，把她带到公主面前，公主问道：“出什么事了，美人？”
‘O princess! “哦，公主！
I went for water, and I saw an image, and I was afraid.’ 我去取水，看到一个影子，我害怕。”
So another girl went to the water and saw the same thing, and came back with the same story. 于是另一个姑娘走到水边，也看到同样的景象，回来说的也是同样的事。
The princess wished to see for herself; she rose and paced to the spot with the march of a prancing peacock. 公主想亲自去看看。她站起身，像只神气的孔雀一样大步走到那里。
When she saw the image she said to her nurse: ‘Find out who is reflected in the water, and where he lives.’ 当她看到了水中的倒影，便对她的女佣说：“去查清楚映在水里的是什么人，住在什么地方。”
Her words reached the prince’s ear, he lifted up his head; she saw him and beheld beauty such as she had never seen before. 她的话传到了王子的耳朵里。他抬起头来，公主看到了他，见到了她从未见过的俊美面容。
She lost a hundred hearts to him, and signed to her nurse to bring him to her presence. 她对王子一见倾心，并示意女佣带他到自己面前来。
The prince let himself be persuaded to go with the nurse, but when the princess questioned him as to who he was and how he had got into her garden, he behaved like a man out of his mind—sometimes smiling, sometimes crying, and saying: ‘I am hungry,’Or words misplaced and random, civil mixed with the rude. 王子乖乖地跟着那女佣去了，可是一到公主问他是谁、是怎么进花园的时候，他就一副疯子模样——一会儿笑，一会儿哭，还说：“我饿了，”或者语无伦次地胡说，一会儿彬彬有礼，一会儿又粗鲁野蛮。
‘What a pity!’ said the princess, ‘he is mad!’ “真可惜！”公主说，“他疯了！”
As she liked him she said: ‘He is my madman; let no one hurt him.’ 公主喜欢他，所以说：“这疯子是我的，谁也不许伤害他。”
She took him to her house and told him not to go away, for that she would provide for all his wants. 她把他带到自己的房里，告诉他不要走，她会给他所有他想要的东西。
The prince thought, ‘It would be excellent if here, in her very house, I could get the answer to her riddle; but I must be silent, on pain of death.’ 王子心想：“如果能在她这房里找到谜底，那就太好了。不过我绝对不能说出来，说了就是死路一条。”
Now in the princess’s household there was a girl called Dil-aram[7]; she it was who had first seen the image of the prince. 在公主的房里有个叫迪丽—阿拉姆[7]的姑娘，她就是第一个看到王子倒影的人。
She came to love him very much, and she spent day and night thinking how she could make her affection known to him. 她深深爱上了王子，日夜思索着要如何让他明白自己的爱慕之情。
One day she escaped from the princess’s notice and went to the prince, and laid her head on his feet and said: ‘Heaven has bestowed on you beauty and charm. 有一天，她躲开公主的注意，跑去找王子。她把头伏在他脚上，对他说：“上天赐予了你美貌与魅力。
Tell me your secret; who are you, and how did you come here? 告诉我你的秘密吧。你是谁，又是怎么到这儿来的？
I love you very much, and if you would like to leave this place I will go with you. 我深爱着你，你若要离开这儿，我就跟你走。
I have wealth equal to the treasure of the miserly Qarun.’ 我拥有的财富和守财的戈伦一样多。”
But the prince only made answer like a man distraught, and told her nothing. 可是王子只是像个神志不清的人那样作答，什么也没有告诉她。
He said to himself, ‘God forbid that the veil should be taken in vain from my secret; that would indeed disgrace me.’ 他对自己说：“主不容我的机密徒然为人所知。那必将使我蒙羞。”
So, with streaming eyes and burning breast, Dil-aram arose and went to her house and lamented and fretted. 于是，迪丽—阿拉姆流着眼泪、胸口火烫地起身走回自己房里，又心痛又心焦。
Now whenever the princess commanded the prince’s attendance, Dil-aram, of all the girls, paid him attention and waited on him best. 每次只要公主命人照顾王子，迪丽—阿拉姆就特别照料他。所有姑娘里头，就数她服侍得最周全。
The princess noticed this, and said: ‘O Dil-aram! you must take my madman into your charge and give him whatever he wants.’ 公主注意到了这事，说道：“哦，迪丽—阿拉姆！你就负责照顾我的疯子吧，他要什么你就给他什么。”
This was the very thing Dilaram had prayed for. 这可是迪丽—阿拉姆求之不得的事。
A little later she took the prince into a private place and she made him take an oath of secrecy, and she herself took one and swore, ‘By Heaven! 不久，她把王子带到一个隐秘的地方。她先要王子发誓保守秘密，她自己也起誓道：“我以上天之名起誓！
I will not tell your secret. 我绝不会泄露你的秘密。
Tell me all about yourself so that I may help you to get what you want.’ 把你的境遇都告诉我吧，这样我也许能帮你得到你想要的。”
The prince now recognised in her words the perfume of true love, and he made compact with her. 王子从她的话里嗅到了真爱的芬芳，于是他同她立下约定。
‘O lovely girl! “哦！可爱的姑娘！
I want to know what the rose did to the cypress. 我想知道玫瑰对柏树做了什么。
Your mistress cuts off men’s heads because of this riddle; what is at the bottom of it, and why does she do it?’ 你那女主人为了这谜砍下了许多人的头。这究竟是因何而起，她为何这么做？”
Then Dil-aram answered: ‘If you will promise to marry me and to keep me always amongst those you favour, I will tell you all I know, and I will keep watch about the riddle.’ 迪丽—阿拉姆回答道：“你若许诺娶我为妻，永远视我为最爱之一，我就把我所知道的统统告诉你，也会时时留心这个谜。”
‘O lovely girl,’ rejoined he, ‘if I accomplish my purpose, so that I need no longer strive for it, I will keep my compact with you. “哦，可爱的姑娘，”王子回答，“若我达成目的，不必再为之力争，我会遵守你我的约定。
When I have this woman in my power and have avenged my brothers, I will make you my solace.’ 待我将这女人置于我掌控之下，为我的哥哥们报了仇，我就让你与我为伴。”
‘O wealth of my life and source of my joy!’ responded Dil-aram, ‘I do not know what the rose did to the cypress; but so much I know that the person who told Mihr-afruz about it is a negro whom she hides under her throne. “哦！我的命中之宝！我的喜乐之源！”迪丽—阿拉姆答道，“我不知道玫瑰对柏树做了什么，可我知道是谁把这个谜告诉米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹的，就是她藏在宝座下面的那个黑人。
He fled here from Waq of the Caucasus—it is there you must make inquiry; there is no other way of getting at the truth.’ 他是从高加索的沃格逃到这儿来的，你必须到那儿去探寻答案。要得到真相，只有这一条路可走。”
On hearing these words, the prince said to his heart, ‘O my heart! your task will yet wear away much of your life.’ 听了这话，王子对自己的心说：“哦！我的心啊！你的任务可还要大大耗去你的生命啊。”
He fell into long and far thought, and Dil-aram looked at him and said: ‘O my life and my soul! do not be sad. 王子久久深思，迪丽—阿拉姆看着他，对他说：“哦！我的魂灵、我的命啊！不要难过。
If you would like this woman killed, I will put poison into her cup so that she will never lift her head from her drugged sleep again.’ 你若想杀了这女人，我就在她杯里投毒，她将从此长眠，永远不再抬起头。”
‘O Dil-aram! such a vengeance is not manly. “哦，迪丽—阿拉姆！这般报复非大丈夫所为。
I shall not rest till I have gone to Waq of the Caucasus and have cleared up the matter.’ 我若不到高加索的沃格去探明真相，就永不停歇。”
Then they repeated the agreement about their marriage, and bade one another goodbye. 然后他俩将婚约重述，并向彼此道别。
The prince now went back to the village, and told the old man that he was setting out on a long journey, and begged him not to be anxious, and to keep safe the goods which had been entrusted to him. 王子回到村子里，告诉那老人自己要出远门了，请他不必担心，并要他保管好自己托他照看的东西。
The prince had not the least knowledge of the way to Waq of the Caucasus, and was cast down by the sense of his helplessness. 王子完全不知道去高加索的沃格的路，他因无助而陷入沮丧。
He was walking along by his horse’s side when there appeared before him an old man of serene countenance, dressed in green and carrying a staff, who resembled Khizr.[8] 他正牵着马走着，这时眼前走来一位神态安详的老者，他身着绿衣，手持拐杖，样子很像黑德尔[8]。
The prince thanked heaven, laid the hands of reverence on his breast and salaamed. 王子感谢上天，崇敬地将双手放在胸前行礼。
The old man returned the greeting graciously, and asked: ‘How fare you? 那老者慈祥地回了礼，并问道：“你一路可好？
Whither are you bound? 将去往何方？
You look like a traveller.’ 你貌若旅人。”
‘O revered saint! “哦，尊敬的圣人！
I am in this difficulty: I do not know the way to Waq of the Caucasus.’ 我遇到了难题：我不知道去往高加索的沃格的路。”
The old man of good counsel looked at the young prince and said: ‘Turn back from this dangerous undertaking. 善导的老者看着年轻的王子说道：“从这凶险之事中抽身吧。
Do not go; choose some other task! 万勿前往。另择他事！
If you had a hundred lives you would not bring one out safe from this journey.’ 纵使你身有百命，也无一可安然而返。”
But his words had no effect on the prince’s resolve. 可是，他的话没能动摇王子的决心。
‘What object have you,’ the old man asked, ‘in thus consuming your life?’ “你这般不惜性命，”老者问道，“所为何事？”
‘I have an important piece of business to do, and only this journey makes it possible. “我有一桩重要的事要做，只有走了这一遭才有可能完成。
I must go; I pray you, ill God’s name, tell me the way.’ 我必须去。我求您，纵使渎主之名，请为我指明道路。”
When the saint saw that the prince was not to be moved, he said: ‘Learn and know, O youth! that Waq of Qaf is in the Caucasus and is a dependency of it. 圣人见王子心意已决，不可动摇，于是说道：“哦，听着，年轻人！那盖夫的沃格就在高加索，是其属地。
In it there are jins, demons, and peris. 那里有妖怪、魔鬼，还有精灵。
You must go on along this road till it forks into three; take neither the right hand nor the left, but the middle path. 你须沿此路前行，直至道分三岔。不可走右边的路，亦不可左行，而要走中间的路。
Follow this for a day and a night. 沿着这路走一天一夜。
Then you will come to a column on which is a marble slab inscribed with Cufic characters. 而后你会行至一根柱下，那柱上嵌有一块大理石板，板上的铭文以古体刻就。
Do what is written there; beware of disobedience.’ 做那板上所写之事，务必遵命。”
Then he gave his good wishes for the journey and his blessing, and the prince kissed his feet, said good-bye, and, with thanks to the Causer of Causes, took the road. 接着圣人为这旅程而祝佑。王子亲吻他的双脚，向他道别。王子感恩于万因之主，就此上路了。
After a day and a night he saw the column rise in silent beauty to the heavens. 走了一天一夜，他见到了那根柱子。它高高地耸入天空，静默而美丽。
Everything was as the wise old man had said it would be, and the prince, who was skilled in all tongues, read the following Cufic inscription: ‘O travellers! be it known to you that this column has been set up with its tablet to give true directions about these roads. 一切都像那智慧的老者说过的那样。精通各种语言的王子看着如下的古体铭文念道：“哦，旅人！你可知晓，此柱与碑立于此地，是为你在这道路中指准方向。
If a man would pass his life in ease and pleasantness, let him take the right-hand path. 若要命中安逸自在，行右方之路。
If he take the left, he will have some trouble, but he will reach his goal without much delay. 若行左方之路，麻烦将至，但终可得偿所愿，无需耽延。
Woe to him who chooses the middle path! if he had a thousand lives he would not save one; it is very hazardous; it leads to the Caucasus, and is an endless road. 行正中之路者将有祸！纵有千命，无一保全。此行凶险。此路通高加索，永无尽头。
Beware of it!’ 谨记！”
The prince read and bared his head and lifted his hands in supplication to Him who has no needs, and prayed, ‘O Friend of the traveller! 王子读了这话，取下头巾，举起双手向无欲无求之主祈求道：“哦，旅人之善主啊！
I, Thy servant, come to Thee for succour. 在下，汝之奴仆，求汝帮扶。
My purpose lies in the land of Qaf and my road is full of peril. 我欲为之事，位于盖夫之地。我所行之路，险境重重。
Lead me by it.’ 请引我于途。”
Then he took a handful of earth and cast it on his collar, and said: ‘O earth! be thou my grave; and O vest! tee thou my winding-sheet!’ 接着，他抓了一把土撒在衣领上，说道：“哦，土地！为我作坟；哦，衣衫！裹我尸身！”
Then he took the middle road and went along it, day after day, with many a silent prayer, till he saw trees rise from the weary waste of sand. 然后他踏上中间那条路，沿着它前行，一路默默祈祷着走了一天又一天，直到他看见毫无生气的荒漠里有树生长。
They grew in a garden, and he went up to the gate and found it a slab of beautifully worked marble, and that near it there lay sleeping, with his head on a stone, a negro whose face was so black that it made darkness round him. 那些树长在一个花园里。王子走到门前，看见一块打磨精细的大理石板，旁边躺着个黑人。他把头枕在一块石头上睡着，脸黑得连他周围都显暗了。
His upper lip, arched like an eyebrow, curved upwards to his nostrils and his lower hung down like a camel’s. 他的上唇像眉毛一样弯着，一直拱到鼻孔，而下唇像骆驼嘴唇一样耷拉下来。
Four millstones formed his shield, and on a box-tree close by hung his giant sword. 他的盾是由四块磨石制成的，旁边的一棵黄杨树上挂着他的巨剑。
His loin-cloth was fashioned of twelve skins of beasts, and was bound round his waist by a chain of which each link was as big as an elephant’s thigh. 他的缠腰布以十二块兽皮缝制而成，用链子系在腰上，那链子每一环都像大象腿一样粗大。
The prince approached and tied up his horse near the negro’s head. 王子走过去，把马系在靠近那黑人脑袋的地方。
Then he let fall the Bismillah from his lips, entered the garden and walked through it till he came to the private part, delighting in the great trees, the lovely verdure, and the flowery borders. 接着，他说了声“以主之名”，就进了花园，穿行其中，直至走到隐秘处。这里巨树参天，草木葱茏，鲜花环绕盛开，这一切都让王子满心欢喜。
In the inner garden there were very many deer. 花园内苑有很多鹿。
These signed to him with eye and foot to go back, for that this was enchanted ground; but he did not understand them, and thought their pretty gestures were a welcome. 这些鹿用眼睛和脚示意王子回去，因为这是个被施了魔咒的地方。可王子没有明白它们的意思，他以为那些鹿漂亮的姿势是在表示欢迎。
After a while he reached a palace which had a porch more splendid than Caesar’s, and was built of gold and silver bricks. 不久他走到一座宫殿前头。那带着门廊的宫殿比恺撒的宫殿还要富丽堂皇，以金砖和银砖砌成。
In its midst was a high seat, overlaid with fine carpets, and into it opened eight doors, each having opposite to it a marble basin. 殿中央是一张高椅，上面铺着毯子。有八扇门通往殿内，每扇门对面都有一个大理石池盆。
Banishing care, Prince Almas walked on through the garden, when suddenly a window opened and a girl, who was lovely enough to make the moon writhe with jealousy, put out her head. 王子打消顾虑，继续在花园里穿行。忽然，有扇窗户打开了，一个美得连月亮都嫉妒得打滚的姑娘探出头来。
She lost her heart to the good looks of the prince, and sent her nurse to fetch him so that she might learn where he came from and how he had got into her private garden where even lions and wolves did not venture. 她对王子的英俊模样一见倾心，便派她的女佣把他带上前来，好知道他从哪里来、是怎么进入自己的私人花园的，因为这花园连狮子和狼都进不来。
The nurse went, and was struck with amazement at the sun-like radiance of his face; she salaamed and said: ‘O youth! welcome! the lady of the garden calls you; come!’ 女佣得令前去，见了王子那太阳一样光芒万丈的面容，大吃一惊。她向王子行了礼，说道：“哦，小伙子！欢迎你！花园的女主人唤你前去。跟我来！”
He went with her and into a palace which was like a house in Paradise, and saw seated on the royal carpets of the throne a girl whose brilliance shamed the shining sun. 王子跟着她走进一座仿佛置于天堂的宫殿，看见宝座的王毯上坐着一位美得连闪耀的太阳都自愧不如的姑娘。
He salaamed; she rose, took him by the hand and placed him near her. 他向她行了礼，她起身握住他的手，让他坐在自己身边。
‘O young man! who are you? “哦，年轻人！你是谁？
Where do you come from? 你从哪里来？
How did you get into this garden?’ 你是怎么进到这个花园里来的？”
He told her his story from beginning to end, and Lady Latifa[9] replied: ‘This is folly! 王子把自己的故事从头到尾讲给她听，拉蒂法[9]小姐答道：“这真是太傻了！
It will make you a vagabond of the earth, and lead you to destruction. 这会让你四处漂泊流浪，终将你毁灭。
Come, cease such talk! 来，别说这样的话了。
No one can go to the Caucasus. 没有人能到得了高加索。
Stay with me and be thankful, for here is a throne which you can share with me, and in my society you can enjoy my wealth. 跟我在一起吧，你要心存感激，因为在这儿你可以与我共享宝座。在我的地盘上，你可以享有我的财富。
I will do whatever you wish; I will bring here King Quimus and his daughter, and you can deal with them as you will.’ 我会为你做一切你想做的事。我会把古伊穆斯国王和他女儿带来，任凭你处置。”
‘O Lady Latifa,’ he said, ‘I have made a compact with heaven not to sit down off my feet till I have been to Waq of Qaf and have cleared up this matter, and have taken Mihrafruz from her father, as brave men take, and have put her in prison. “哦，拉蒂法小姐，”王子说道，“我已对天发誓，我若未到盖夫的沃格查清此事，未如勇者般将米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹从她父亲身边带走并打入监牢，就绝不停步歇息。
When I have done all this I will come back to you in state and with a great following, and I will marry you according to the law.’ 待我完成这一切，我将带领大批随从，隆重地回来找你，并依照律法娶你为妻。”
Lady Latifa argued and urged her wishes, but in vain; the prince was not to be moved. 拉蒂法小姐极力劝阻，要王子从她所愿，可是没有用，王子毫不动摇。
Then she called to the cupbearers for new wine, for she thought that when his head was hot with it he might consent to stay. 接着她叫侍者拿新的酒来，心想等王子喝得头脑发热了，兴许就会答应留下。
The pure, clear wine was brought; she filled a cup and gave to him. 侍者端来纯净澄澈的酒，拉蒂法斟满一杯递给王子。
He said: ‘O most enchanting sweetheart! it is the rule for the host to drink first and then the guest.’ 王子说道：“哦！最迷人的甜心！按规矩应该是主人先喝，然后才轮到客人。”
So to make him lose his head, she drained the cup; then filled it again and gave him. 于是，为了能让王子喝得头脑发昏，她喝干了这杯酒，然后又斟满了递给王子。
He drank it off, and she took a lute from one of the singers and played upon it with skill which witched away the sense of all who heard. 王子一饮而尽。她又从一名歌者手里拿来鲁特琴，用特别的手法弹奏它，所有听了这琴声的人都会神志不清。
But it was all in vain; three days passed in such festivities, and on the fourth the prince said: ‘O joy of my eyes! 可这一切都是徒劳。欢宴持续了三天，到了第四天，王子说：“哦！我眼中的至宝！
I beg now that you will bid me farewell, for my way is long and the fire of your love darts flame into the harvest of my heart. 我现在求你为我送别，因前路漫漫，而你的爱火投予我满心光焰。
By heaven’s grace I may accomplish my purpose, and, if so, I will come back to you.’ 若上天保佑，成我所欲，我会回来找你。”
Now she saw that she could not in any way change his resolve, she told her nurse to bring a certain casket which contained, she said, something exhilarating which would help the prince on his journey. 拉蒂法见自己没法改变王子的决心，就吩咐女佣取来一个匣子，说是里头装着使人振奋的东西，能在路上助王子一臂之力。
The box was brought, and she divided off a portion of what was within and gave it to the prince to eat. 匣子取来了，她把里面的东西分出一部分递给王子吃。
Then, and while he was all unaware, she put forth her hand to a stick fashioned like a snake; she said some words over it and struck him so sharply on the shoulder that he cried out; then he made a pirouette and found that he was a deer. 然后，趁王子没注意，她伸手取出一根蛇形手杖。她对着手杖说了些什么，然后猛地击向他的肩膀，疼得他叫了出来。紧接着，王子单脚旋转一圈，发现自己变成了一头鹿。
When he knew what had been done to him he thought, ‘All the threads of affliction are gathered together; I have lost my last chance!’ 等他明白过来发生在自己身上的事，他心想：“一切苦难之线缠拢了，我失去了最后的机会！”
He tried to escape, but the magician sent for her goldsmith, who, coming, overlaid the deer-horns with gold and jewels. 他设法逃跑，可女巫拉蒂法派人请来金匠，给鹿角镀上黄金，又镶上珠宝。
The kerchief which that day she had had in her hand was then tied round its neck, and this freed it from her attentions. 她把那天拿着的手巾系在鹿脖子上，一时没有留心它。
The prince-deer now bounded into the garden and at once sought some way of escape. 这时王子所变的鹿跃进了花园，立刻开始找逃出去的路。
It found none, and it joined the other deer, which soon made it their leader. 可是它没有找到。它加入到其他的鹿当中，很快成了头领。
Now, although the prince had been transformed into the form of a deer, he kept his man’s heart and mind. 虽然王子现在变作了鹿的样子，不过他还保有人的心智。
He said to himself, ‘Thank heaven that the Lady Latifa has changed me into this shape, for at least deer are beautiful.’ 他对自己说：“感谢上天让拉蒂法小姐把我变成这个样子，至少鹿还长得挺美。”
He remained for some time living as a deer amongst the rest, but at length resolved that an end to such a life must be put ill some way. 他留在那里，以鹿的模样在鹿群里生活了一段时间。不过最后他决定，必须要想办法结束这种生活。
He looked again for some place by which he could get out of the magic garden. 他又开始找能逃出这魔法花园的地方。
Following round the wall he reached a lower part; he remembered the Divine Names and flung himself over, saying, ‘Whatever happens is by the will of God.’ 他绕墙走着，走到一段比别处低矮的墙边。他向神致意，而后纵身越过，口中念道：“一切皆为神的旨意。”
When he looked about he found that he was in the very same place he had jumped from; there was the palace, there the garden and the deer! 他看向四周，发现自己就在刚刚跳离的地方。他看见了宫殿、花园，还有那些鹿！
Eight times he leaped over the wall and eight times found himself where he had started from; but after the ninth leap there was a change, there was a palace and there was a garden, but the deer were gone. 他八次越过墙，八次都发现自己还在原来的地方。可是到了第九次，变化发生了，宫殿还在，花园还在，但是鹿不见了。
Presently a girl of such moon-like beauty opened a window that the prince lost to her a hundred hearts. 这时，一个美如明月的姑娘打开了窗户，王子对她一见倾心。
She was delighted with the beautiful deer, and cried to her nurse: ‘Catch it! if you will I will give you this necklace, every pearl of which is worth a kingdom.’ 她见了这头漂亮的鹿很高兴，朝她的女佣喊道：“捉住它！你要是捉住了我就把这条项链给你，这上面每一颗珍珠都能抵一个王国。”
The nurse coveted the pearls, but as she was three hundred years old she did not know how she could catch a deer. 那女佣很想要珍珠，可她已经三百岁了，不知道要怎么才能捉住一头鹿。
However, she went down into the garden and held out some grass, but when she went near the creature ran away. 不过，她还是下了楼走到花园里，伸手递过去一些草。可她一走近，那鹿就跑开了。
The girl watched with great excitement from the palace window, and called: ‘O nurse, if you don’t catch it, I will kill you!’ 那姑娘很兴奋地从宫殿窗户里往外看着，喊道：“哦，阿嬷，你要是捉不到它，我就杀了你！”
‘I am killing myself,’ shouted back the old woman. “我正搭上老命呢。”老阿嬷喊道。
The girl saw that nurse tottering along and went down to help, marching with the gait of a prancing peacock. 那姑娘见阿嬷走得踉踉跄跄，就下来帮忙，她走起路来像只神气的孔雀。
When she saw the gilded horns and the kerchief she said: ‘It must be accustomed to the hand, and be some royal pet!’ 她见了那镀金的鹿角和系着的手巾，说道：“这鹿一定是家养的，它一定是哪个王室的宠物！”
The prince had it in mind that this might be another magician who could give him some other shape, but still it seemed best to allow himself to be caught. 王子心想，这大概是另一个女巫，能把自己变成另一个模样，不过看样子最好还是让自己被她捉住。
So he played about the girl and let her catch him by the neck. 于是他在那姑娘身边嬉耍，任她抱着脖子把自己捉到了手。
A leash was brought, fruits were given, and it was caressed with delight. 仆人取来绳子绑住它，端来水果喂给它吃，那姑娘高兴地抚摸着它。
It was taken to the palace and tied at the foot of the Lady Jamila’s raised seat, but she ordered a longer cord to be brought so that it might be able to jump up beside her. 它被带到宫殿里，拴在贾米拉小姐的高椅椅脚上，不过她下令取一根更长的绳子来，那样它就可以在她身边跳跃。
When the nurse went to fix the cord she saw tears falling from its eyes, and that it was dejected and sorrowful. ‘O Lady Jamila! this is a wonderful deer, it is crying; I never saw a deer cry before.’ 阿嬷过来系绳子，见那鹿眼里流着泪，一脸沮丧悲伤。“哦！贾米拉小姐！这真是头奇鹿，它在哭呢。我从来没见过鹿哭。”
Jamila darted down like a flash of lightning, and saw that it was so. 贾米拉像闪电一样冲下来看，发现鹿真的在哭。
It rubbed its head on her feet and then shook it so sadly that the girl cried for sympathy. 它用头蹭蹭她的脚，接着悲伤地摇摇头，贾米拉怜悯地哭了。
She patted it and said: ‘Why are you sad, my heart? 她拍拍它，说道：“你为什么难过，我心爱的？”
Why do you cry, my soul? 你为什么哭，我的魂？
Is it because I have caught you? 是不是因为我把你捉来了？
I love you better than my own life.’ 我爱你要胜过爱我自己的生命。”
But, spite of her comforting, it cried the more. 可是，她一安慰，它却哭得更厉害了。
Then Jamila said: ‘Unless I am mistaken, this is the work of my wicked sister Latifa, who by magic art turns servants of God into beasts of the field.’ 这时贾米拉说道：“要是我没猜错，这是我那个邪恶的姐姐拉蒂法干的，她总用魔法把主的仆从变作地上的走兽。”
At these words the deer uttered sounds, and laid its head on her feet. 听了这些话，那头鹿叫出声来，把头搁在她脚上。
Then Jamila was sure it was a man, and said: ‘Be comforted, I will restore you to your own shape.’ 于是贾米拉确信这是个人。她说道：“放心，我会让你恢复原形的。”
She bathed herself and ordered the deer to be bathed, put on clean raiment, called for a box which stood in an alcove, opened it and gave a portion of what was in it to the deer to eat. 她去为自己沐浴，并下令让鹿也接受沐浴。接着，她换上干净的衣服，叫人取来放在壁龛里的一个盒子。她打开盒子，把里面的东西取了一部分给鹿吃。
Then she slipped her hand under her carpet and produced a stick to which she said something. 然后她把手伸到毯子底下，取出一根手杖，对着手杖说了些什么。
She struck the deer hard, it pirouetted and became Prince Almas. 她重重地击向那鹿，鹿单脚旋转后又变回了阿玛斯王子。
The broidered kerchief and the jewels lay upon the ground. 绣花手巾和珠宝都掉在了地上。
The prince prostrated himself in thanks to heaven and Jamila, and said: ‘O delicious person! 王子伏倒在地，感谢上天，又感谢贾米拉，并说道：“哦，最可心的人！
O Chinese Venus! how shall I excuse myself for giving you so much trouble? 哦，中国的维纳斯！给你带来这么多麻烦，我要如何宽恕自己？
With what words can I thank you?’ 我要说些什么才能感谢你？”
Then she called for a clothes-wallet and chose out a royal dress of honour. 然后贾米拉叫人取来一包衣物，挑出了一件有王室气派的衣服。
Her attendants dressed him in it, and brought him again before the tender-hearted lady. 她的侍从们给王子穿上衣服，又把他带到善心的贾米拉小姐跟前。
She turned to him a hundred hearts, took his hand and seated him beside her, and said: ‘O youth! tell me truly who you are and where you come from, and how you fell into the power of my sister.’ 她对王子一见倾心，握住他的手，让他坐在自己身边，说道：“哦，少年人！同我说实话，你是谁，你从哪里来，还有你是怎么落在我姐姐手里的。”
Even when he was a deer the prince had much admired Jamila now he thought her a thousand times more lovely than before. 王子还是头鹿的时候就对贾米拉很是爱慕，现在他觉得她要比之前可爱一千倍。
He judged that in truth alone was safety, and so told her his whole story. 他断定只有实话实说才是安全之策，于是把自己的整个经历都告诉了她。
Then she asked: ‘O Prince Almas-ruh-bakhsh, do you still wish so much to make this journey to Waq of Qaf? 然后她问道：“哦，阿玛斯—鲁—巴克什王子，你还是那么渴望到盖夫的沃格去吗？
What hope is there in it? 这趟旅程有何希望可言？
The road is dangerous even near here, and this is not yet the borderland of the Caucasus. 即使只是在这附近，路途也充满危险，而这里连高加索的边境都未到。
Come, give it up! 算了，放弃吧！
It is a great risk, and to go is not wise. 这太冒险了，绝非明智之举。
It would be a pity for a man like you to fall into the hands of jins and demons. 你这般人物，若落入妖怪和魔鬼之手，实在可惜。
Stay with me, and I will do whatever you wish.’ 和我在一起吧，我会为你做一切你想要做的事。”
‘O most delicious person!’ he answered, ‘you are very generous, and the choice of my life lies in truth in your hands; but I beg one favour of you. “哦！最可心的人！”王子答道，“你如此仁厚，我的性命抉择当真就在你手中。但我有一事相求。
If you love me, so do I too love you. 你若爱我，那我也爱着你。
If you really love me, do not forbid me to make this journey, but help me as far as you can. 你若真爱我，不要阻止我上路，而要尽你所能地帮我。
Then it may be that I shall succeed, and if I return with my purpose fulfilled I will marry you according to the law, and take you to my own country, and we will spend the rest of our lives together in pleasure and good companionship. 而后或许我得以成事。若能如愿而返，我将依照律法娶你为妻，带你到我的国度，我们相濡以沫，欢度余生。
Help me, if you can, and give me your counsel.’ 如果可以，请你帮我，并赠我以良策。”
‘O very stuff of my life,’ replied Jamila ‘I will give you things that are not in kings’ treasuries, and which will be of the greatest use to you. “哦！我的命中之宝啊，”贾米拉答道，“我要给你连国王的宝藏里也没有的东西，都是对你最有用的。
First, there are the bow and arrows of his Reverence the Prophet Salih. 第一件，是尊敬的先知撒立哈的弓和箭。
Secondly, there is the Scorpion of Solomon (on whom be peace), which is a sword such as no king has; steel and stone are one to it; if you bring it down on a rock it will not be injured, and it will cleave whatever you strike. 第二件，是所罗门之蝎（愿他安息），是一柄任何国王都没有的剑。钢与石在其中合为一体。你若用它砍击岩石，它也丝毫无损。它能劈开你砍击的所有东西．
Thirdly, there is the dagger which the sage Timus himself made; this is most useful, and the man who wears it would not bend under seven camels’ loads. 第三件，是圣者提穆斯自己铸造的匕首。这是最能派上用场的，拥有它的人，身压七匹骆驼所负的重物也不会屈身。
What you have to do first is to get to the home of the Simurgh, [10] and to make friends with him. 你要做的第一件事，是去西木尔格[10]的家，同他交朋友。
If he favours you, he will take you to Waq of Qaf; if not, you will never get there, for seven seas are on the way, and they are such seas that if all the kings of the earth, and all their wazirs, and all their wise men considered for a thousand years, they would not be able to cross them.’ 他若喜欢你，就会带你去盖夫的沃格，否则，你永远也到不了那里。因为一路上横亘着七片海，就算世上所有国王，连同他们的大臣和谋士想上一千年，也不能渡过那些海。”
‘O most delicious person! where is the Simurgh’s home? “哦！最可心的人！西木尔格的家在哪儿？
How shall I get there?’ 我要如何才能到达？”
‘O new fruit of life! you must just do what I tell you, and you must use your eyes and your brains, for if you don’t you will find yourself at the place of the negroes, who are a bloodthirsty set; and God forbid they should lay hands on your precious person.’ “哦！我命中的新生之果啊！你得按我说的做，你要用眼看，用心想，不然，你会身陷嗜血成性的黑人的地盘。主不容你这等珍贵的人落入他们手中。”
Then she took the bow and quiver of arrows, the sword, and the dagger out of a box, and the prince let fall a Bismillah, and girt them all on. 接着，她取来弓和箭袋，拿来宝剑，并从盒子里取出匕首。王子说了声“以主之名”，把它们都佩在了身上。
Then Jamila of the houri-face, produced two saddle-bags of ruby-red silk, one filled with roasted fowl and little cakes, and the other with stones of price. 美若天人的贾米拉又拿出两个宝石红的丝缎鞍囊，一个装着烤鸟肉和小面饼，另一个装着珍贵的宝石。
Next she gave him a horse as swift as the breeze of the morning, and she said: ‘Accept all these things from me; ride till you come to a rising ground, at no great distance from here, where there is a spring. 然后，她送给王子一匹疾如晨风的马，并说道：“收下我给你的这些东西。你要骑着马一直走，直到你到达一片有泉水的高地上，那就在离这儿不远的地方。
It is called the Place of Gifts, and you must stay there one night. 那里叫做赠礼之地，你必须在那儿停留一晚。
There you will see many wild beasts—lions, tigers, leopards, apes, and so on. 你会见到很多野兽——狮子、老虎、花豹、猿等等。
Before you get there you must capture some game. 到那儿之前，你得先打点儿猎物。
On the long road beyond there dwells a lion-king, alla if other beasts did not fear him they would ravage the whole country and let no one pass. 在另一边的一条长路上，住着一位狮王，其他野兽都畏惧他，不然他们早就毁了整个国度，禁止任何人通行了。
The lion is a red transgressor, so when he comes rise and do him reverence; take a cloth and rub the dust and earth from his face, then set the game you have taken before him, well cleansed, and lay the hands of respect on your breast. 那狮王生性暴烈不羁。他走过来的时候，你要起身向他行礼，取出一块布为他拭去脸上的尘土，再把清洗干净的猎物放到他面前，把双手恭敬地放在胸前。
When he wishes to eat, take your knife and cut pieces of the meat and set them before him with a bow. 待他想吃的时候，你要拿刀把肉切成片，再躬身放到他面前。
In this way you will enfold that lion-king in perfect friendship, and he will be most useful to you, and you will be safe from molestation by the negroes. 这样，你就能同狮王建立深厚交情，他对你最为有用，有了他你就能免受黑人侵扰。
When you go on from the Place of Gifts, be sure you do not take the right-hand road; take the left, for the other leads by the negro castle, which is known as the Place of Clashing Swords, and where there are forty negro captains each over three thousand or four thousand more. 你出了赠礼之地，继续前行，万万不可走右边的路。要走左边那条，因为另一条路通往黑人的城堡，那里被唤作撞剑之地，住着四十位黑人首领，每人统领三四千人，甚至更多。
Their chief is Taramtaq.[11] 他们的头领叫塔拉姆塔格[11]。
Further on than this is the home of the Simurgh.’ 从那儿再往前走，便是西木尔格的家了。”
Having stored these things in the prince’s memory, she said: ‘You will see everything happen just as I have said.’ 贾米拉嘱咐王子记住这些事，又说：“你会看到，一切都如我所言。”
Then she escorted him a little way; they parted, and she went home to mourn his absence. 接着，她护送王子走了一小段路，然后就分开了。贾米拉回到家，为王子的离开而哀伤。
Prince Almas, relying on the Causer of Causes, rode on to the Place of Gifts and dismounted at the platform. 阿玛斯王子在万因之主的护佑下一路骑到了赠礼之地，在平台上下了马。
Everything happened just as Jamila had foretold; when one or two watches of the night had passed, he saw that the open ground around him was full of such stately and splendid animals as he had never seen before. 一切正如贾米拉预言的那样。到了晚上，过了一二更，他便看见身边的空地上满是那些他从没见过的高贵美丽的动物。
By-and-by, they made way for a wonderfully big lion, which was eighty yards from nose to tail-tip, and was a magnificent creature. 过了一会儿，它们都为一头巨大的狮子让出道来。那狮子从鼻子到尾梢有八码长，是头伟岸的巨兽。
The prince advanced and saluted it; it proudly drooped its head and forelocks and paced to the platform. 王子上前向它行礼。那狮子高傲地低了低头，鬃毛也跟着垂了下去，接着便走到平台上去了。
Seventy or eighty others were with it, and now encircled it at a little distance. 他身边有七八十头别的动物，它们同它隔出一小段距离，将它环绕在当中。
It laid its right paw over its left, and the prince took the kerchief Jamila had given him for the purpose, and rubbed the dust and earth from its face; then brought forward the game he had prepared, and crossing his hands respectfully on his breast stood waiting before it. 那狮子把右爪搁在了左爪上。王子掏出贾米拉特意给的那块手帕，为狮王拭去脸上的尘土，接着取出准备好的猎物，把双手恭敬地交叉在胸前，站在它跟前等候。
When it wished for food he cut off pieces of the meat and put them in its mouth. 等它要吃东西的时候，王子把肉一片片切下来，放进它嘴里。
The serving lions also came near and the prince would have stayed his hand, but the king-lion signed to him to feed them too. 狮子侍从们也走上前来，王子本没停下正伺候狮王的手，不过狮王示意他也把肉给侍从们吃。
This he did, laying the meat on the platform. 王子照办了，把肉放在了平台上。
Then the king-lion beckoned the prince to come near and said: ‘Sleep at ease; my guards will watch.’ 然后，狮王示意王子走近，对他说道：“安心睡吧。我的侍卫们会守着的。”
So, surrounded by the lion-guard, he slept till dawn, when the king lion said good-bye, and gave him a few of his own hairs and said: ‘When you are in any difficulty, burn one of these and I will be there.’ 于是，在狮子侍卫们的围绕下，王子一觉睡到清晨。这时，狮王同他道别，又把自己的几根狮毛送给他，并说道：“有难的时候点燃一根，我便现身。”
Then it went off into the jungle. 接着，狮王便走入丛林，离开了。
Prince Almas immediately started; he rode till he came to the parting of the ways. 阿玛斯王子即刻启程，一口气骑到了岔路口。
He remembered quite well that the right-hand way was short and dangerous, but he bethought himself too that whatever was written on his forehead would happen, and took the forbidden road. 他牢牢记着，右边的路虽短却危险重重。但他也想到，写在额上的诫命必会应验。于是，他踏上了那条禁忌之路。
By-and-by he saw a castle, and knew from what Jamila had told him that it was the Place of Clashing Swords. 走了没多久，王子便看到一座城堡，他知道，依贾米拉所说，那便是撞剑之地了。
He would have liked to go back by the way he had come, but courage forbade, and he said, ‘What has been preordained from eternity will happen to me,’ and went on towards the castle. 他本想原路返回，却没有勇气。他自语道：“永世注定之事将降临于我。”然后继续朝着城堡前行。
He was thinking of tying his horse to a tree which grew near the gate when a negro came out and spied him. 他刚想着要把马系在大门边的一棵树上，这时一个黑人走出来，发现了他。
‘Ha!’ said the wretch to himself, ‘this is good; Taram-taq has not eaten man-meat for a long time, and is craving for some. “哈！”那恶人自语道，“这可正好。塔拉姆—塔格很久没吃人肉了，正急着想吃呢。
I will take this creature to him.’ 我要把这家伙带去给他。”
He took hold of the prince’s reins, and said: ‘Dismount, man-child! 他一把抓住王子的马缰绳，说道：“下来，小家伙！
Come to my master. 到我主人那儿去。
He has wanted to eat man-meat this long time back.’ 他早就想着要吃人肉了。”
‘What nonsense are you saying?’ said the prince, and other such words. “你在胡说八道些什么？”王子这么说了几句。
When the negro understood that he was being abused, he cried: ‘Come along! 等那黑人明白过来，知道王子是觉得自己受了羞辱，他便喊道：“过来！
I will put you into such a state that the birds of the air will weep for you.’ 我会让你体体面面的，让空中所有的鸟都为你哭。”
Then the prince drew the Scorpion of Solomon and struck him—struck him on the leathern belt and shore him through so that the sword came out on the other side. 这时，王子拔出所罗门之蝎，一剑砍向他——砍在他的皮腰带上，把他刺穿了，剑从另一面穿了出来。
He stood upright for a little while, muttered some words, put out his hand to seize the prince, then fell in two and surrendered his life. 那黑人直挺挺地站了一会儿，嘟哝了几句，伸手要来抓王子，然后就断成了两截，一命呜呼。
There was water close at hand, and the prince made his ablution, and then said: ‘O my heart! a wonderful task lies upon you.’ 近旁有水，王子洗净了身子，然后说道：“哦，我的心啊！有桩了不起的事等着你去做。”
A second negro came out of the fort, and seeing what had been done, went back and told his chief. 又有个黑人从堡垒里出来，见了发生的事，便回去禀告他的长官。
Others wished to be doubled, and went out, and of every one the Scorpion of Solomon made two. 其他人也想来挑战这能把人一切为二的剑，就都出来了。所罗门之蝎把他们都砍成了两半。
Then Taram-taq sent for a giant negro named Chil-maq, who in the day of battle was worth three hundred, and said to him: ‘I shall thank you to fetch me that man.’ 然后，塔拉姆—塔格找来一个名叫奇勒—马格的黑巨人，他在战斗时能以一抵三百。塔拉姆—塔格跟他说：“你若替我把那人抓来，我必谢你。”
Chil-maq went out, tall as a tower, and bearing a shield of eight millstones, and as he walked he shouted: ‘Ho! blunder-head! by what right do you come to our country and kill our people? 奇勒—马格出来了，他高得像座塔，背着用八块磨石制成的盾牌，边走边喊：“嗬！蠢货！你凭什么到我们国家来，还杀了我们的族人？
Come! make two of me.’ 来啊！来把我砍成两半。”
As the prince was despicable in his eyes, he tossed aside his club and rushed to grip him with his hands. 他视王子为卑劣小人，把棍子扔在一边，冲过来用手捉他。
He caught him by the collar, tucked him under his arm and set off with him to Taram-taq. 他抓着王子的衣领，把他塞在胳膊底下，挟他去见塔拉姆—塔格。
But the prince drew the dagger of Timus and thrust it upwards through the giant’s armpit, for its full length. 可是王子拔出提穆斯的匕首，朝上直刺巨人腋窝，把整个匕首都插了进去。
This made Chil-maq drop him and try to pick up his club; but when he stooped the mighty sword shore him through at the waist. 奇勒—马格被这么一刺，丢下王子就要去捡棍子。就在他弯腰的时候，那威力无比的剑就把他拦腰砍穿了。
When news of his champion’s death reached Taram-taq he put himself at the head of an army of his negroes and led them forth. 塔拉姆—塔格得知同伴之死，便亲自打头阵，率领黑人军队前进。
Many fell before the magic sword, and the prince laboured on in spite of weakness and fatigue till he was almost worn out. 很多人都倒在魔剑之下。尽管王子已经虚弱疲乏，他依旧继续拼杀，直至几乎精疲力竭。
In a moment of respite from attack he struck his fire-steel and burned a hair of the king-lion; and he had just succeeded in this when the negroes charged again and all but took him prisoner. 在攻击的间隙，他划着了打火器，点燃了狮王的一根毛。他才刚点着，黑人们就再次发起攻击，差一点儿就要俘虏他了。
Suddenly from behind the distant veil of the desert appeared an army of lions led by their king. 忽然，沙漠深处远远地出现了一队狮子，由它们的王带领着。
‘What brings these scourges of heaven here?’ cried the negroes. “什么风把这群天上的祸害吹到这里来了？”黑人们叫嚷道。
They came roaring up, and put fresh life into the prince. 他们大吼着冲上前，要同王子拼命。
He fought on, and when he struck on a belt the wearer fell in two, and when on a head he cleft to the waist. 王子继续战斗。他一砍到对手的腰带，那人就断成了两截；他一砍到对手的脑袋，那人整个上身就裂成了两半。
Then the ten thousand mighty lions joined the fray and tore in pieces man and horse. 此时，一万只力大无穷的狮子加入了厮杀，把人和马都撕成碎片。
Taram-taq was left alone; he would have retired into his fort, but the prince shouted: ‘Whither away, accursed one? 塔拉姆—塔格成了孤身一人。他本想退回堡垒，可是王子大喊：“该死的人，你往哪里逃？
Are you fleeing before me?’ 你是要当着我的面逃跑吗？”
At these defiant words the chief shouted back, ‘Welcome, man! 听了这挑衅的话，那头领回喊道：“来吧，小子！
Come here and I will soften you to wax beneath my club.’ 上这儿来，我要让你在我棍下软成蜡泥。”
Then he hurled his club at the prince’s head, but it fell harmless because the prince had quickly spurred his horse forward. 然后，他猛地把棍子朝王子头上抡过去，可是没伤着他，因为王子已经迅速赶着马朝前跑了。
The chief, believing he had hit him, was looking down for him, when all at once he came up behind and cleft him to the waist and sent him straight to hell. 那头领确信自己击中了王子，正低头找他，王子却突然来到他身后，一下子把他从头劈到腰，直接送他下了地狱。
The king-lion greatly praised the dashing courage of Prince Almas. 狮王对阿玛斯王子拼劲十足的勇气大大地赞扬了一番。
They went together into the Castle of Clashing Swords and found it adorned and fitted in princely fashion. 他们一同走进撞剑城堡，只见这里装饰得富丽堂皇。
In it was a daughter of Taram taq, still a child. She sent a message to Prince Almas saying, ‘O king of the world! choose this slave to be your handmaid. 塔拉姆—塔格的女儿在城堡里，她还是个孩子。她带话给阿玛斯王子，说道：“哦！世间之王！将这奴隶选作您的侍女吧。
Keep her with you; where you go, there she will go!’ 将她带在身边吧。您去往何处，她就去往何处！”
He sent for her and she kissed his feet and received the Mussulman faith at his hands. 王子派人把她请来，她亲吻了王子的脚，从王子手中接受了穆斯林的信仰。
He told her he was going a long journey on important business, and that when he came back he would take her and her possessions to his own country, but that for the present she must stay in the castle. 王子告诉她，他正为了一件重要的事而远行，等他回来了，会带着她和她的财产到他自己的国家去，不过眼下她必须呆在城堡里。
Then he made over the fort and all that was in it to the care of the lion, saying: ‘Guard them, brother! let no one lay a hand on them.’ 接着，王子把堡垒和那里的一切转交给狮子看管，说道：“要守护它们，兄弟！不可让任何人触碰。”
He said goodbye, chose a fresh horse from the chief’s stable and once again took the road. 王子道了别，从那头领的马厩里挑了一匹新的马，便又上路了。
After travelling many stages and for many days, he reached a plain of marvellous beauty and refreshment. 走了许多程、许多天，他来到了一片奇美无比、让人心旷神怡的平原上。
It was carpeted with flowers—roses, tulips, and clover; it had lovely lawns, and amongst them running water. 这里鲜花遍野——有玫瑰、郁金香，还有苜蓿。草地葱茏可爱，其间还有淙淙流水。
This choicest place of earth filled him with wonder. 这块世间上选之地让王子惊叹不已。
There was a tree such as he had never seen before; its branches were alike, but it bore flowers and fruit of a thousand kinds. 这里长着一棵王子从未见过的树。那树的枝条都长得差不多，上面却开着几千种花，结了几千种果。
Near it a reservoir had been fashioned of four sorts of stone—touchstone, pure stone, marble, and loadstone. 旁边是一个水池，这池子由四种石头打造而成——试金石、净石、大理石和磁石。
In and out of it flowed water like attar. 池水像花精油一样流进流出。
The prince felt sure this must be the place of the Simurgh.’ he dismounted, turned his horse loose to graze, ate some of the food Jamila had given him, drank of the stream and lay down to sleep. 王子确信，这一定就是西木尔格的属地了。他下了马，把马放开吃草，自己吃了些贾米拉给他的东西，又从溪里喝了点儿水，就躺下睡了。
He was still dozing when he was aroused by the neighing and pawing of his horse. 他还正打着盹，就被马的嘶鸣声和跺蹄声吵醒了。
When he could see clearly he made out a mountain-like dragon whose heavy breast crushed the stones beneath it into putty. 等他清醒过来定睛一看，是一头大得像山一样的龙，它肥厚的前胸把身下的石头都碾成了灰。
He remembered the Thousand Names of God and took the bow of Salih from its case and three arrows from their quiver. 王子诵念着千神之名，从弓匣里取出撒立哈之弓，又从箭袋里抽出三支箭。
He bound the dagger of Timus firmly to his waist and hung the scorpion of Solomon round his neck. 他把提穆斯的匕首紧紧绑在腰上，又把所罗门之蝎挂在脖子上。
Then he set an arrow on the string and released it with such force that it went in at the monster’s eye right up to the notch. 接着他搭箭上弦，用力射了出去，一箭直插那怪物的眼凹。
The dragon writhed on itself, and belched forth an evil vapour, and beat the ground with its head till the earth quaked. 那龙扭动翻滚着，喷出恶臭的水蒸气，不住地用头撞地，撞得土地都震动起来了。
Then the prince took a second arrow and shot into its throat. 王子又取出第二支箭，直射它的咽喉。
It drew in its breath and would have sucked the prince into its maw, but when he was within striking distance he drew his sword and, having committed himself to God, struck a mighty blow which cut the creature’s neck down to the gullet. 那龙呼吸急促，要把王子吞进肚里。正当它近在咫尺之时，王子拔出了剑，誓忠于主，然后奋力一击，砍中那怪物的脖子，一剑深入咽喉。
The foul vapour of the beast and horror at its strangeness now overcame the prince, and he fainted. 王子这时终于承受不住那怪物喷出的污浊恶气和恐怖的骇人模样，晕了过去。
When he came to himself he found that he was drenched in the gore of the dead monster. 等他苏醒过来，发现那怪物已死，自己浑身都浸透在那死兽的血里。
He rose and thanked God for his deliverance. 他站起身，感谢主的解救。
The nest of the Simurgh was in the wonderful tree above him, and in it were young birds; the parents were away searching for food. 西木尔格的巢就在他头顶的奇树上，巢里住着小雏鸟。鸟爸爸和鸟妈妈出去找吃的了。
They always told the children, before they left them, not to put their heads out of the nest; but, to-day, at the noise of the fight below, they looked down and so saw the whole affair. 它们出门之前总嘱咐孩子们，不要把头探出巢去。不过，今天，小鸟们听见底下打斗的声音，都往下看了，看到了整件事。
By the time the dragon had been killed they were very hungry and set up a clamour for food. 那头龙被杀死的时候，它们已经饿极了，都嚷嚷着要吃东西。
The prince therefore cut up the dragon and fed them with it, bit by bit, till they had eaten the whole. 于是王子切下龙肉，一点儿一点儿地喂它们吃，直到它们把整头龙全吃掉了。
He then washed himself and lay down to rest, and he was still asleep when the Simurgh came home. 然后他把自己洗干净，躺下休息了。他还睡着的时候，西木尔格回家了。
As a rule, the young birds raised a clamour of welcome when their parents came near, but on this day they were so full of dragon-meat that they had no choice, they had to go to sleep. 照惯例，爸爸妈妈一飞近，小鸟们都会叫嚷着迎接它们，可是今天它们实在吃了太多龙肉，除了睡觉别无选择。
As they flew nearer, the old birds saw the prince lying under the tree and no sign of life in the nest. 大鸟们飞近了，看见王子躺在树下，而巢里没有一点儿生命迹象。
They thought that the misfortune which for so many earlier years had befallen them had again happened and that their nestlings had disappeared. 它们以为之前连年降临在自己身上的不幸又发生了，孩子们又不见了。
They had never been able to find out the murderer, and now suspected the prince. 它们一直没能找出凶手，现在便怀疑是王子干的。
‘He has eaten our children and sleeps after it; he must die,’ said the father-bird, and flew back to the hills and clawed up a huge stone which he meant to let fall on the prince’s head. “他吃了我们的孩子就睡了。他非死不可。”鸟爸爸说道。它飞回山上，抓起一块巨石，打算砸在王子头上。

How is this? 这是怎么了？
Has sickness touched you? 你病了吗？
Tell me that I may seek a cure.” 告诉我，我会想办法医治你。’
Alas! there was now no way of concealing the mingled delight and anguish of our love; from secret it became known. 唉！事到如今，我们这苦乐交杂的爱是藏不住的了。它被发现了，不再是秘密。
I was put in prison and the world grew dark to my rose, bereft of her lover. 我被关进牢里，我的玫瑰姑娘失去了她的爱人，世界成了一片黑暗。

His demons and peris. collected amber-wood and made a pile, and would have set me on it, when I remembered the word of life which the two peris I had rescued had breathed into my ear,and I asked that my body might be rubbed with oil to release me the sooner from torture. 他手下的魔鬼和精灵搬来琥珀木，堆在一起。他们刚要把我放上去的时候，我想起救过的两个精灵悄悄对我说的保命的话。于是我请求在身上抹油，好让我早些从痛苦中解脱。
This was allowed, and those two contrived to be the anointers. 这请求被准许了，而那两个精灵设法前来为我抹油。
I was put into the fire and it was kept up for seven days and nights. 我被放在火里烧了七天七夜。
By the will of the Great King it left no trace upon me. 上主保佑，那火没有在我身上留下痕迹。
At the end of a week the pert-king ordered the ashes to be cast upon the dust-heap, and I was found alive and unharmed. 到一星期结束时，精灵王下令把灰烬撒到土堆上去，却发现我毫发无伤地活着。
‘Peris who had seen Gul consumed by her love for me now interceded with the king, and said: “It is clear that your daughter’s fortunes are bound up with his, for the fire has not hurt him. “那些眼看着古丽因爱我而日渐憔悴的精灵这时便向国王求情道：‘事情已经很明白了，您女儿的命运是同他的绑在一起的，连火都伤不了他。
It is best to give him the girl, for they love one another. 最好的办法还是把姑娘嫁给他，因为他们彼此相爱。
He is King of Waq of Qaf, and you will find none better.” 他是盖夫的沃格的国王，您找不出比他更好的人选了。’
‘To this the king agreed, and made formal marriage between Gul and me. “精灵王同意了，并为我和古丽举行了正式婚礼。
You now know the price I paid for this faithless creature. 现在你知道了，我曾为这不忠的东西付出了什么样的代价。
O prince! remember our compact.’ 哦，王子！记着我们的约定。”
‘I remember,’ said the prince; ‘but tell me what brought Queen Gul to her present pass?’ “我记着，”王子说，“可是，告诉我，是什么事让古丽皇后落入如今的境地？”
‘One night,’ continued King Sinaubar,’I was aroused by feeling Gul’s hands and feet, deadly cold, against my body. “有一天夜里，”国王接着说，“古丽的手脚触到了我的身体，死一般冰凉，我被惊醒了。
I asked her where she had been to get so cold, and she said she had had to go out. 我问她上哪儿去了，怎么身上这么凉，她说她不得不出去了一趟。
Next morning, when I went to my stable I saw that two of my horses, Windfoot and Tiger, were thin and worn out. 第二天早上，我到马厩里去，看到我的两匹马——旋风和猛虎——变得很瘦，显得筋疲力尽。
I reprimanded the groom and beat him. 我训斥马夫，还打了他一顿。
He asked where his fault lay, and said that every night my wife took one or other of these horses and rode away, and came back only just before dawn. 他问他何错之有，还说，每天夜里我妻子都要牵走其中一匹骑着出门，直到快要清晨了才回来。
A flame kindled in my heart, and I asked myself where she could go and what she could do. 我心里燃起一团火。我问自己，她会去哪里，会做些什么。
I told the groom to be silent, and when next Gul took a horse from the stable to saddle another quickly and bring it to me. 我让马夫不要声张，等下次古丽从马厩里牵了马，就立刻给另一匹上鞍，然后牵来给我。
That day I did not hunt, but stayed at home to follow the matter up. 那天我没有去打猎，而是呆在家里，要把这事查个究竟。
I lay down as usual at night and pretended to fall asleep. 晚上，我像往常一样躺下，然后假装睡着了。
When I seemed safely off Gul got up and went to the stable as her custom was. 等我看起来完全睡熟了，古丽爬起来，照例去了马厩。
That night it was Tiger’s turn. 那天晚上轮到猛虎。
She rode off on him, and I took Windfoot and followed. 她骑着猛虎离开了，我骑上旋风跟着。
With me went that dog you see, a faithful friend who never left me. 跟我一起去的就是你看到的那条狗，这狗是我忠实的朋友，从未离开过我。
‘When I came to the foot of those hills which lie outside the city I saw Gul dismount and go towards a house which some negroes have built there. “我骑到城外那片山下，看到古丽下了马，朝着一群黑人建在那儿的一所房子走去。
Over against the door was a high seat, and on it lay a giant negro, before whom she salaamed. 一张高椅正对着门放着，上面躺着一个黑巨人，她向他行了礼。
He got up and beat her till she was marked with weals, but she uttered no complaint. 那黑人起身就打她，打得她满是伤痕，可她一声不吭。
I was dumfounded, for once when I had struck her with a rose stalk she had complained and fretted for three days! 我愣住了。有一次我用玫瑰枝打了她，她就恼得发了三天牢骚！
Then the negro said to her: “How now, ugly one and shaven head! 接着那黑人对她说：‘怎么着，你这个丑秃子！
Why are you so late, and why are you not wearing wedding garments?” 为什么这么晚？还有，你怎么不穿嫁衣？’
She answered him: “That person did not go to sleep quickly, and he stayed at home all day, so that I was not able to adorn myself. 她回答他：‘那人没有很快睡着，而且他今天一整天都呆在家里，所以我没法梳妆打扮。
I came as soon as I could.” 我已经尽快赶来了。’
In a little while he called her to sit beside him; but this was more than I could bear. 不一会儿，他叫她坐到他身边去。这让我忍无可忍。
I lost control of myself and rushed upon him. 我失去自控，朝他冲了过去。
He clutched my collar and we grappled in a death struggle. 他抓着我的领子，我们扭打在一起，要决一死战。
Suddenly she came behind me, caught my feet and threw me. 古丽忽然走到我身后，抓着我的脚把我撂倒了。
While he held me on the ground, she drew out my own knife and gave it to him. 那黑人把我按在地上，这时古丽拔出我的刀递给他。
I should have been killed but for that faithful dog which seized his throat and pulled him down and pinned him to the ground. 要不是那条忠实的狗咬住了他的喉咙，又把他扑倒在地死死按住，我本已被杀死了。
Then I got up and despatched the wretch. 然后我起身杀了那混蛋。
There were four other negroes at the place; three I killed and the fourth got away, and has taken refuge beneath the throne of Mihr-afruz, daughter of King Quimus. 那里还有另外四个黑人，有三个被我杀了，第四个跑了，藏身在古伊穆斯国王的女儿米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹的宝座下。
I took Gul back to my palace, and from that time till now I have treated her as a dog is treated, and I have cared for my dog as though it were my wife. 我把古丽带回了宫。从那时起，我就像待一条狗一样待她，却像爱护自己妻子一样爱护我的狗。
Now you know what the rose did to the cypress; and now you must keep compact with me.’ 现在你知道玫瑰对柏树做了什么了。你要信守与我的约定。”
‘I shall keep my word,’ said the prince; ‘but may a little water be taken to the roof so that I may make my last ablution?’ “我会说话算话，”王子说，“不过，可以拿些水到屋顶上去，好让我最后洗洗身子吗？”
To this request the king consented. 国王同意了这个请求。
The prince mounted to the roof, and, getting into a corner, struck his fire-steel and burned one of the Simurgh’s feathers in the flame. 王子爬上屋顶，走到角落里，划着打火器点燃了西木尔格的一根羽毛。
Straightway it appeared, and by the majesty of its presence made the city quake. 它立刻出现了，气撼全城。
It took the prince on its back and soared away to the zenith. 它背着王子直飞天顶。
After a time King Sinaubar said: ‘That young man is a long time on the roof; go and bring him here.’ 过了一会儿，国王说道：“那年轻人在屋顶上呆了很久了，去把他带下来。”
But there was no sign of the prince upon the roof; only, far away in the sky, the Simurgh was seen carrying him off. 可屋顶上没有王子的踪影，只能远远看到西木尔格带着他飞在空中。
When the king heard of his escape he thanked heaven that his hands were clean of this blood. 国王听说王子跑了，便感谢上天让自己的双手免沾了这鲜血。
Up and up flew the Simurgh, till earth looked like an egg resting on an ocean. 西木尔格越飞越高，直到地球看起来像一个浮在海上的蛋。
At length it dropped straight down to its own place, where the kind prince was welcomed by the young birds and most hospitably entertained. 最后，它径直飞落到自己的领地上。小鸟们欢迎善良的王子，他受到了最热情的款待。
He told the whole story of the rose and the cypress, and then, laden with gifts which the Simurgh had gathered from cities far and near, he set his face for the Castle of Clashing Swords. 他原原本本地讲述了玫瑰和柏树的故事，然后背上西木尔格从远近各城收集的礼物，朝着撞剑之城出发了。
The king-lion came out to meet him; he took the negro chief’s daughter—whose name was also Gul—in lawful marriage, and then marched with her and her possessions and her attendants to the Place of Gifts. 狮王出来接见他。他依照律法娶了那黑人头领的女儿——她的名字也叫古丽，然后带着她和她的财产还有侍从们，前往赠礼之地。
Here they halted for a night, and at dawn said good-bye to the king-lion and set out for Jamila’s country. 他们在那里歇了一晚，一早便同狮王告别，启程前往贾米拉的国度。
When the Lady Jamila heard that Prince Almas was near, she went out, with many a fair handmaid, to give him loving reception. 贾米拉小姐一听说阿玛斯王子来了，便带着许多漂亮的侍女走了出来，充满爱意地迎接他。
Their meeting was joyful, and they went together to the garden-palace. 他们彼此相见，满心欢喜，一起到花园王宫里去了。
Jamila summoned all her notables, and in their presence her marriage with the prince was solemnised. 贾米拉请来了所有对自己很重要的人，在他们的见证下，她和王子举行了庄严的婚礼。
A few days later she entrusted her affairs to her wazir, and made preparation to go with the prince to his own country. 几天以后，她把事情都托付给大臣们照料，准备跟着王子到他的国度去。
Before she started she restored all the men whom her sister, Latifa, had bewitched, to their own forms, and received their blessings, and set them forward to their homes. 出发之前，她把所有被她姐姐拉蒂法施了魔法的人都恢复了原形，并接受了他们的祝福，然后让他们回家了。
The wicked Latifa herself she left quite alone in her garden-house. 邪恶的拉蒂法被一个人孤零零地留在她的花园宅邸里头。
When all was ready they set out with all her servants and slaves, all her treasure and goods, and journeyed at ease to the city of King Quimus. 等一切准备就绪，他们便带上她所有的仆人、奴隶、宝物和财产，轻轻松松地朝着古伊穆斯国王的都城出发了。
When King Quimus heard of the approach of such a great company, he sent out his wazir to give the prince honourable meeting, and to ask what had procured him the favour of the visit. 古伊穆斯国王听说来了这么一大队人马，就派出大臣隆重地接见王子，并问他因何事大驾光临。
The prince sent back word that he had no thought of war, but he wrote: ‘Learn and know, King Quimus, that I am here to end the crimes of your insolent daughter who has tyrannously done to death many kings and kings sons, and has hung their heads on your citadel. 王子回话说自己无意开战，但是他写道：“听着，古伊穆斯国王，你那傲慢的女儿已暴虐杀害众多国王与王子，并将其头颅悬于你城堡之上，我是前来终结她的罪恶的。
I am here to give her the answer to her riddle.’ 我来告知她那谜题的答案。”
Later on he entered the city, beat boldly on the drums, and was conducted to the presence. 不久，王子进了城，无所畏惧地击鼓，然后被领到国王面前。
The king entreated him to have nothing to do with the riddle, for that no man had come out of it alive. 国王求他不要管那个谜了，因为企图解答的人没有一个能活命。
‘O king!’ replied the prince, ‘it is to answer it that I am here; I will not withdraw.’ “哦，国王！”王子回答，“我就是来揭开谜底的，我绝不退缩。”
Mihr-afruz was told that one man more had staked his head on her question, and that this was one who said he knew the answer. 米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹被告知，又有人拿自己的人头冒险来解答她的问题了，而且这个人说他知道答案。
At the request of the prince, all the officers and notables of the land were summoned to hear his reply to the princess. 在王子的要求下，这里的所有官员和重要人物都被召来听他回答公主的谜题。
All assembled, and the king and his queen Gul-rakh, and the girl and the prince were there. 所有人聚集在一起，国王和他的王后古丽—拉克、公主和王子都到场了。
The prince addressed Mihr-afruz: ‘What is the question you ask?’ 王子对米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹说：“你的谜题是什么？”
‘What did the rose do to the cypress?’ she rejoined. “玫瑰对柏树做了什么？”她回答。
The prince smiled, and turned and addressed the assembly. 王子微微一笑，转过身去，对众人说话。
‘You who are experienced men and versed in affairs, did you ever know or hear and see anything of this matter?’ “你们都是经验丰富、熟谙世事的人，有谁听过或看过任何关于这个谜的事吗？”
‘No!’ they answered, ‘no one has ever known or heard or seen aught about it; it is an empty fancy.’ “没有！”他们回答，“从未有人知晓、听说抑或看见过这事，这只是凭空想象之事。”
‘From whom, then, did the princess hear of it? “那么，公主又是从谁那里听说这件事的？
This empty fancy it is that has done many a servant of God to death!’ 这凭空想象之事已让许多真主的仆从丧命了！”　
All saw the good sense of his words and showed their approval. 所有人都认为王子说的有理，纷纷表示赞同。
Then he turned to the princess: ‘Tell us the truth, princess; who told you of this thing? 他又转身面向公主说：“告诉我们真相，公主。是谁把这事告诉你的？
I know it hair by hair, and in and out; but if I tell you what I know, who is there that can say I speak the truth? 这事我里里外外知道得一清二楚。不过，我若是跟你说了我知道的那些，有谁能断言我说的是事实呢？
You must produce the person who can confirm my words.’ 你必须把那个能证实我所说属实的人带出来。”
Her heart sank, for she feared that her long-kept secret was now to be noised abroad. 公主心中一沉，她害怕自己长久隐藏的秘密就要被传扬出去了。
But she said merely: ‘Explain yourself.’ 可她只是说：“你把话说清楚。”
‘I shall explain myself fully when you bring here the negro whom you hide beneath your throne.’ “我会把话讲得明明白白的，只要你把藏在你宝座下的那个黑人带到这里来。”
Here the king shouted in wonderment: ‘Explain yourself, young man! 听到这里，国王惊叫：“你说清楚了，年轻人！
What negro does my daughter hide beneath her throne?’ 我女儿在宝座底下藏了个什么黑人？”
‘That,’ said the prince, ‘you will see if you order to be brought here the negro who will be found beneath the throne of the princess.’ “那个啊，”王子说，“你只要下令把公主宝座之下找到的那个黑人带到这里来，你就知道了。”
Messengers were forthwith despatched to the garden house, and after awhile they returned bringing a negro whom they had discovered in a secret chamber underneath the throne of Mihr-afruz, dressed in a dress of honour, and surrounded with luxury. 国王即刻派使臣到公主的花园宅邸去。不久，他们带着一个黑人回来了，这黑人是在米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹的宝座之下的一间密室里发现的，他身着华服，尽享奢侈。
The king was overwhelmed with astonishment, but the girl had taken heart again. 国王惊呆了。不过，公主又壮起胆来。
She had had time to think that perhaps the prince had heard of the presence of the negro, and knew no more. 她趁机想了一会儿，觉得王子可能只是听说了那个黑人的事，并不知道别的。
So she said haughtily: ‘Prince! you have not answered my riddle.’ 于是她傲慢地说：“王子！你还没有解出我的谜。”
‘O most amazingly impudent person,’ cried he, ‘do you not yet repent?’ “哦，你这厚颜无耻的家伙，”王子喊道，“你还不知悔改？”
Then he turned to the people, and told them the whole story of the rose and the cypress, of King Sinaubar and Queen Gul. 然后他转身面向众人，把玫瑰和柏树——古丽王后和锡诺巴国王——的故事，原原本本地讲给他们听。
When he came to the killing of the negroes, he said to the one who stood before them: ‘You, too, were present.’ 讲到国王杀死黑人的时候，他对面前站着的那个黑人说：“你，当时也在场。”
‘That is so; all happened as you have told it!’ “没错，我在。一切都像你说的那样！”
There was great rejoicing in the court and all through the country over the solving of the riddle, and because now no more kings and princes would be killed. 谜底揭开了，再也不会有国王和王子为此丧命了，朝野上下一片欢腾。
King Quimus made over his daughter to Prince Almas, but the latter refused to marry her, and took her as his captive. 古伊穆斯国王将女儿许配给阿玛斯王子，可王子拒绝娶她，而是把她作为俘虏。
He then asked that the heads should be removed from the battlements and given decent burial. 接着，王子请求把城垛上的人头取下来，体面地下葬。
This was done. 这事照办了。
He received from the king everything that belonged to Mihr-afruz; her treasure of gold and silver; her costly stuffs and carpets; her household plenishing; her horses and camels; her servants and slaves. 国王把米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹的所有东西都给了他，她的金银财宝、奢华物件、地毯、家具摆设、马和骆驼，还有她的仆人和奴隶。
Then he returned to his camp and sent for Dil-aram, who came bringing her goods and chattels, her gold and her jewels. 然后王子回到自己的帐篷里，叫来迪丽—阿拉姆。她带着自己的家当和黄金珠宝来了。
When all was ready, Prince Almas set out for home, taking with him Jamila, and Dil-aram and Gul, daughter of Taram-taq, and the wicked Mihr-afruz, and all the belongings of the four, packed on horses and camels, and in carts without number. 等一切就绪，阿玛斯王子带着贾米拉、迪丽—阿拉姆、塔拉姆—塔格的女儿古丽、坏心眼的米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹四个人，用数不清的马、骆驼和马车载着她们的所有家当，启程回家了。
As he approached the borders of his father’s country word of his coming went before him, and all the city came forth to give him welcome. 快走到他父王的国土边境的时候，他回来的消息已经先传到家了。每个城市的人都前来迎接他。
King Saman-lal-posh—Jessamine, wearer of rubies—had so bewept the loss of his sons that he was now blind. 萨曼—拉波什国王——他这名字的意思是茉莉和身戴红宝石之人——因为失去儿子们而时常流泪，现在已经瞎了。
When the prince had kissed his feet and received his blessing, he took from a casket a little collyrium of Solomon, which the Simurgh had given him, and which reveals the hidden things of earth, and rubbed it on his father’s eyes. 王子亲吻父亲的双脚，接受他的祝福，然后从一个匣子里取出一点儿所罗门的眼药。这是西木尔格给他的，能让埋藏于地下的东西显露出来。王子把药涂在父王的眼睛上。　
Light came, and the king saw his son. 光明重现，国王见到了自己的儿子。
Mihr-afruz was brought before the king, and the prince said: ‘This is the murderer of your sons; do with her as you will.’ 米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹被带到国王面前，王子说：“这就是杀害您儿子的凶手，任您处置。”
The king fancied that the prince might care for the girl’s beauty, and replied: ‘You have humbled her; do with her as you will.’ 国王猜想着王子也许会喜欢这姑娘的美貌，就回答说：“你已挫了她的傲气，还是你来处置吧。”
Upon this the prince sent for four swift and strong horses, and had the negro bound to each one of them; then each was driven to one of the four quarters, and he tore in pieces like muslin. 听了这话，王子派人找来四匹速度快、力气大的马，把那黑人和这些马绑在一起。接着，四匹马被赶着奔往四个方向，那黑人像穆斯林一样被撕成了碎片。
This frightened Mihr-afruz horribly, for she thought the same thing might be done to herself. 这场面把米赫尔—阿弗鲁兹吓坏了，她心想同样的事也许会发生在自己身上。
She cried out to the prince: ‘O Prince Almas! what is hardest to get is most valued. 她朝王子大叫：“哦，阿玛斯王子！最难以得到的便是最珍贵的。
Up till now I have been subject to no man, and no man had had my love. 我至今未曾臣服于任何人，也没有人得到过我的爱。
The many kings and kings sons who have died at my hands have died because it was their fate to die like this. 死在我手下的国王和王子是命中注定要以这种方式死去。
In this matter I have not sinned. 于此，我并未犯下罪孽。
That was their fate from eternity; and from the beginning it was predestined that my fate should be bound up with yours.’ 那是他们永世的命运。而我的命运，最初就已注定要同你的绑在一起。”
The prince gave ear to the argument from pre-ordainment, and as she was a very lovely maiden he took her too in lawful marriage. 王子听了她这番关于命中注定的辩解，又因为她是个很可爱的姑娘，便也依照律法娶了她。
She and Jamila, set up house together, and Dil-aram and Gul set up theirs; and the prince passed the rest of his life with the four in perfect happiness, and in pleasant and sociable entertainment. 她和贾米拉住在一起，迪丽—阿拉姆和古丽住在一起。王子同她们四个人一起，舒心、和睦、欢娱又幸福地度过了余生。
Now has been told what the rose did to the cypress. 现在，这个“玫瑰对柏树做了什么”的故事就讲完了。
Finished, finished, finished! 结束了，结束了，结束了！
