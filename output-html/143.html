一位健康的老人 A Fine Old Man
作者：马克·吐温 by Mark Twain
最近，约翰·瓦格纳，布法罗最年长的人——一百零四岁高龄的——在两周内步行了一英里半路程。 John Wagner, the oldest man in Buffalo—one hundred and four years old—recently walked a mile and a half in two weeks.
他精力充沛，思维敏捷，就像报纸上长篇累牍、不厌其烦介绍的其他长寿老人一样，而且他无论在哪一方面都引人注目。 He is as cheerful and bright as any of these other old men that charge around so persistently and tiresomely in the newspapers, and in every way as remarkable.
去年十一月，他曾冒着暴雨，只拿一把雨伞穿过五个街区（没有别的遮挡物），去给格兰特投上一票。他还说自己已经为美国四十七位总统投过票——这可是瞎话。 Last November he walked five blocks in a rainstorm, without any shelter but an umbrella, and cast his vote for Grant, remarking that he had voted for forty-seven presidents—which was a lie.
昨天，他那头棕色浓密的“第二茬庄稼”从纽约运来，他还有一套新牙也快要从费城运来。 His “second crop” of rich brown hair arrived from New York yesterday, and he has a new set of teeth coming from Philadelphia.
下周，他将和一名一百零二岁的姑娘完婚。那位姑娘还在从事洗衣的工作。 He is to be married next week to a girl one hundred and two years old, who still takes in washing.
实际上，他们八十年前就已经订婚了，但他们的父母一直反对他们的婚事，直到三天前才同意。 They have been engaged eighty years, but their parents persistently refused their consent until three days ago.
现在，约翰·瓦格纳比罗德岛上的寿星还大两岁。不过他这辈子可是没沾过一滴酒——除非——除非你把威士忌也当成酒。 John Wagner is two years older than the Rhode Island veteran, and yet has never tasted a drop of liquor in his life—unless—unless you count whisky.
