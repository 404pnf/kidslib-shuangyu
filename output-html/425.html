Chapter I 第一章
The person who, next to the actors themselves, chanced to know most of their story, lived just below ‘Top o’ Town’ (as the spot was called) in an old substantially-built house, distinguished among its neighbours by having an oriel window on the first floor, whence could be obtained a raking view of the High Street, west and east, the former including Laura’s dwelling, the end of the Town Avenue hard by (in which were played the odd pranks hereafter to be mentioned), the Port-Bredy road rising westwards, and the turning that led to the cavalry barracks where the Captain was quartered. 演员们旁边的那个人碰巧知道他们的大部分故事。这个人住在“城高地”（人们如是称呼这个地方）正下方一处建筑完好的老房子里，这座房子因其二楼的凸肚窗而与邻里的房子相区分开来，透过窗子可以斜视商业大街及其东西两侧。劳拉的住处在西侧，紧挨着城镇大街的尽头（下文将要讲到的恶作剧就是在这里发生的），还有越向西越高的布列德港路以及通往上尉曾经驻扎骑兵营房的转弯处。
Looking eastward down the town from the same favoured gazebo, the long perspective of houses declined and dwindled till they merged in the highway across the moor. 从这同一处令人喜爱的露台向东望向下面的城镇，连绵的房屋越来越低，越来越小，最后与荒原对面的公路融为一体。
The white riband of road disappeared over Grey’s Bridge a quarter of a mile off, to plunge into innumerable rustic windings, shy shades, and solitary undulations up hill and down dale for one hundred and twenty miles till it exhibited itself at Hyde Park Corner as a smooth bland surface in touch with a busy and fashionable world. 白色缎带般的公路在距格雷斯桥四分之一英里处的地方消失，融入数不清的乡村弯路和幽暗的树阴里，再沿着山谷上下起伏120英里，直到它如同一个光滑柔和的平面展现在海德公园角，与一个忙碌而时髦的世界相连接。
To the barracks aforesaid had recently arrived the—th Hussars, a regiment new to the locality. 刚刚提到的那个营房迎来了第X轻骑兵，这个兵团对当地人而言还很陌生。
Almost before any acquaintance with its members had been made by the townspeople, a report spread that they were a ‘crack’ body of men, and had brought a splendid band. 几乎在市民与兵团里的任何成员相识之前，就有一则传言说他们是“一流”的男子，还带来了一个绝佳的乐队。
For some reason or other the town had not been used as the headquarters of cavalry for many years, the various troops stationed there having consisted of casual detachments only; so that it was with a sense of honour that everybody—even the small furniture-broker from whom the married troopers hired tables and chairs—received the news of their crack quality. 由于这样那样的原因，这座城镇已经多年没有被用作骑兵的总部了，在那里驻扎的军队只是些非正式的分遣队罢了。因此，带着一丝荣誉感，城里的人——甚至包括帮助已婚骑兵租用桌椅的小家具经纪人——都听说他们品质一流。
In those days the Hussar regiments still wore over the left shoulder that attractive attachment, or frilled half-coat, hanging loosely behind like the wounded wing of a bird, which was called the pelisse, though it was known among the troopers themselves as a ‘sling-jacket.’ 那时，轻骑兵团的成员左肩上佩戴着引人注目的配饰，或披着饰有褶边的短大衣。大衣随意搭在身后，像鸟儿受伤的翅膀。人们把这种大衣叫做皮制上衣，而骑兵们则称它为“悬吊夹克”。
It added amazingly to their picturesqueness in women’s eyes, and, indeed, in the eyes of men also. 这种上衣大大增加了骑兵在女性眼中独特的魅力，而且，在男性的眼中亦是如此。
The burgher who lived in the house with the oriel window sat during a great many hours of the day in that projection, for he was an invalid, and time hung heavily on his hands unless he maintained a constant interest in proceedings without. 住在带有凸肚窗房子里的这位市民，一天里的大部分时间都坐在窗前眺望，因为他是个残疾人。如果他不对外面的事物保持长久的兴趣，那么，时间就会过得缓慢而无聊。
Not more than a week after the arrival of the Hussars his ears were assailed by the shout of one schoolboy to another in the street below. 轻骑兵到达城里还不到一个星期，他的耳朵里就充斥着下面街道上一个学生对另一个学生大喊的内容。
‘Have ’ee heard this about the Hussars? “你听说那些轻骑兵了吗？
They are haunted! 他们被鬼缠身了！
Yes—a ghost troubles ’em; he has followed ’em about the world for years.’ 没错——有个幽灵一直折磨着他们，已经跟着他们满世界转悠好多年了。”
A haunted regiment: that was a new idea for either invalid or stalwart. 闹鬼的兵团，不管是对残疾人还是健全人来说，这都是个新说法。
The listener in the oriel came to the conclusion that there were some lively characters among the—th Hussars. 凸肚窗里的听众得出结论：第X轻骑兵里肯定有些活跃的角色。
He made Captain Maumbry’s acquaintance in an informal manner at an afternoon tea to which he went in a wheeled chair—one of the very rare outings that the state of his health permitted. 在一次下午茶的时候，他以一种非正式的方式结识了莫姆布里上尉。他是坐着轮椅去的——这是在他身体条件允许下为数不多的外出之一。
Maumbry showed himself to be a handsome man of twenty-eight or thirty, with an attractive hint of wickedness in his manner that was sure to make him adorable with good young women. 莫姆布里上尉是个28岁或30岁的帅气男子，举止中透露出一种迷人的邪恶，这让他在年轻淑女中备受喜爱。
The large dark eyes that lit his pale face expressed this wickedness strongly, though such was the adaptability of their rays that one could think they might have expressed sadness or seriousness just as readily, if he had had a mind for such. 一对浓黑的大眼睛使得他那苍白的脸有了容光，充分展现了这种邪恶，虽说他的眼神善于变化，让人觉得只要他愿意，他的眼中随时可以表现出悲伤或严肃的情绪。
An old and deaf lady who was present asked Captain Maumbry bluntly: ‘What’s this we hear about you? 在场的一位耳聋的老太太直截了当地问莫姆布里上尉：“关于你们的传言是怎么回事？
They say your regiment is haunted.’ 他们说你的兵团闹鬼。”
The Captain’s face assumed an aspect of grave, even sad, concern. 上尉的脸上浮现出暗淡甚至是悲伤的担忧情绪。
‘Yes,’ he replied, ‘it is too true.’ “没错，”他回答道，“传言一点儿都没错。”
Some younger ladies smiled till they saw how serious he looked, when they looked serious likewise. 一些年轻一点儿的女士们笑了起来，直到她们看到他的表情如此严肃，于是她们的表情也变得严肃起来了。
‘Really?’ said the old lady. “真的吗？”老太太说。
‘Yes. “是的。
We naturally don’t wish to say much about it.’ 我们自然不想多谈这个话题。”
‘No, no; of course not. “是，是，那是当然。
But—how haunted?’ 不过——怎么个闹鬼法？”
‘Well; the—thing, as I’ll call it, follows us. “嗯，那个——东西，就这么称呼吧，一直跟着我们。
In country quarters or town, abroad or at home, it’s just the same.’ 不管是在乡下营地还是在城镇里，不管在国内还是在国外，都一直跟着我们。”
‘How do you account for it?’ “你们对此怎么解释呢？”
‘H’m.’ “嗯，”
Maumbry lowered his voice. 莫姆布里压低声音，
‘Some crime committed by certain of our regiment in past years, we suppose.’ “我们猜测，大概是兵团里的某些骑兵以前犯下的罪过吧。”
‘Dear me...How very horrid, and singular!’ “天哪……多么恐怖，多么奇怪啊！”
‘But, as I said, we don’t speak of it much.’ “不过，就像我刚才说的，我们并不怎么谈论这个话题。”
‘No...no.’ “当然……当然。”
When the Hussar was gone, a young lady, disclosing a long-suppressed interest, asked if the ghost had been seen by any of the town. 那位轻骑兵离开之后，一位年轻的女士按捺不住压抑已久的好奇心，问起城里是否有人见过那个幽灵。
The lawyer’s son, who always had the latest borough news, said that, though it was seldom seen by any one but the Hussars themselves, more than one townsman and woman had already set eyes on it, to his or her terror. 律师的儿子总是有城里的最新消息。他说，尽管除了轻骑兵团的人之外，极少有人看到那个幽灵，不过，已经有不止一个城镇的人看见了，非常恐怖。
The phantom mostly appeared very late at night, under the dense trees of the town-avenue nearest the barracks. 幽灵大都只在深夜才会出现在营房附近城镇大街浓密的树丛下。
It was about ten feet high; its teeth chattered with a dry naked sound, as if they were those of a skeleton; and its hip-bones could be heard grating in their sockets. 它大约有十英尺高，牙齿格格作响，发出干涩的、不加掩饰的声音，好似骷髅的牙齿。你甚至还能听到臀骨摩擦骨窝的声音。
During the darkest weeks of winter several timid persons were seriously frightened by the object answering to this cheerful description, and the police began to look into the matter. 在冬季最黑的几个星期，几个胆小的人被这描述生动的鬼吓得不轻，于是警察开始着手调查。
Whereupon the appearances grew less frequent, and some of the Boys of the regiment thankfully stated that they had not been so free from ghostly visitation for years as they had become since their arrival in Casterbridge. 后来，幽灵出现的次数就越来越少了，兵团里的一些骑兵感激地说，自从到达卡斯特桥市之后，他们已经好多年没有像现在这样不受幽灵的侵扰了。
This playing at ghosts was the most innocent of the amusements indulged in by the choice young spirits who inhabited the lichened, red-brick building at the top of the town bearing ‘W.D.’ and a broad arrow on its quoins. 这段有关幽灵的轶事是这些年轻人沉迷的最无害的消遣了。他们住在城镇高处一座长满地衣的红砖楼里，隅石上刻着“W.D.”字样和一支宽箭。
Far more serious escapades—levities relating to love, wine, cards, betting—were talked of, with no doubt more or less of exaggeration. 其他更过分的恶作剧涉及爱情、酒、纸牌、赌博，当然或多或少都有夸张的成分。
That the Hussars, Captain Maumbry included, were the cause of bitter tears to several young women of the town and country is unquestionably true, despite the fact that the gaieties of the young men wore a more staring colour in this old-fashioned place than they would have done in a large and modern city. 毫无疑问的是，包括莫姆布里上尉在内的轻骑兵团是城里和乡下几个年轻女士苦涩的泪水之源，而事实上，这些年轻人华丽的服饰在这个陈旧落后的地方远比在现代化大城市要显眼得多。
Chapter II 第二章
Regularly once a week they rode out in marching order. 他们每周定期列队出行一次。
Returning up the town on one of these occasions, the romantic pelisse flapping behind each horseman’s shoulder in the soft south-west wind, Captain Maumbry glanced up at the oriel. 有一次行军归来回城的时候，每个轻骑兵背上的皮制上衣都在柔和的西南风中轻摆，莫姆布里上尉向上瞥了一眼那个凸肚窗。
A mutual nod was exchanged between him and the person who sat there reading. 他和坐在那里读书的人相互点头示意。
The reader and a friend in the room with him followed the troop with their eyes all the way up the street, till, when the soldiers were opposite the house in which Laura lived, that young lady became discernible in the balcony. 读书的人和房里的一个朋友用目光尾随着街上前行的队伍，直到骑兵们到达劳拉住所的对面。阳台上可以隐约分辨出这位年轻女士的身影。
‘They are engaged to be married, I hear,’ said the friend. “我听说，他们已经订婚了。”朋友说道。
‘Who—Maumbry and Laura? “谁——莫姆布里和劳拉？
Never—so soon?’ 怎么可能——这么快？”
‘Yes.’ “是的。”
‘He’ll never marry. “他绝不会结婚的。
Several girls have been mentioned in connection with his name. 人们已经提到有好几个女孩跟他有关系了。
I am sorry for Laura.’ 我为劳拉感到遗憾。”
‘Oh, but you needn’t be. “噢，不过你不需要感到遗憾。
They are excellently matched.’ 他们是天作之合。”
‘She’s only one more.’ “她只是又一个这样的女孩而已。”
‘She’s one more, and more still. “她是又一个女孩，而且不止如此。
She has regularly caught him. 他经常为她着迷。
She is a born player of the game of hearts, and she knew how to beat him in his own practices. 劳拉是天生玩弄感情的好手，她知道怎么用他自己的行为打败他。
If there is one woman in the town who has any chance of holding her own and marrying him, she is that woman.’ 如果整个城里只有一个能坚持不懈并与他结婚的女子，那就是劳拉。”
This was true, as it turned out. 后来，事实证明，这是正确的。
By natural proclivity Laura had from the first entered heart and soul into military romance as exhibited in the plots and characters of those living exponents of it who came under her notice. 受一种自然的癖好驱使，劳拉的内心和灵魂从一开始就被浪漫的军旅色彩占据，从引起她注意的那些活生生的人物性格和故事情节便可见一斑。
From her earliest young womanhood civilians, however promising, had no chance of winning her interest if the meanest warrior were within the horizon. 从最早被她的女性气质折服的市民开始，无论这人多么前途无量，只要哪怕是最卑劣的士兵一出现在她的视线中，这个人就再也引不起她的兴趣了。
It may be that the position of her uncle’s house (which was her home) at the corner of West Street nearest the barracks, the daily passing of the troops, the constant blowing of trumpet-calls a furlong from her windows, coupled with the fact that she knew nothing of the inner realities of military life, and hence idealized it, had also helped her mind’s original bias for thinking men-at-arms the only ones worthy of a woman’s heart. 这可能是因为她叔叔的房子（也就是她家）在西大街拐角处靠近营房的地方，每天都有军队经过，持续不断的号角声从离她窗户一弗隆的地方传来。事实上，她对真实的军旅生活一无所知，于是便将其理想化了。这一切都加深了她头脑中原有的偏见，即军人是唯一值得女人芳心暗许的对象。
Captain Maumbry was a typical prize; one whom all surrounding maidens had coveted, ached for, angled for, wept for, had by her judicious management become subdued to her purpose; and in addition to the pleasure of marrying the man she loved, Laura had the joy of feeling herself hated by the mothers of all the marriageable girls of the neighbourhood. 莫姆布里上尉就是这样典型的一流人物，一个周围所有姑娘都梦寐以求、渴望已久、竞相追逐、为之黯然落泪的对象。劳拉通过她高超的手段征服了他，达到了自己的目的。除了嫁给自己所爱的男人之外，还让劳拉高兴的是，她感到自己被邻里间所有待嫁女儿的母亲所憎恶。
The man in the oriel went to the wedding; not as a guest, for at this time he was but slightly acquainted with the parties; but mainly because the church was close to his house; partly, too, for a reason which moved many others to be spectators of the ceremony; a subconsciousness that, though the couple might be happy in their experiences, there was sufficient possibility of their being otherwise to colour the musings of an onlooker with a pleasing pathos of conjecture. 凸肚窗里的人也参加了婚礼，不过他不是以客人的身份去的，因为那时他跟新婚夫妇还不是很熟。他去的主要原因是教堂离他家很近，另外一个原因也是驱使了众多观众参加这一仪式的原因，即虽然这对新婚夫妇可能会拥有幸福美满的生活，不过人们潜意识里还是觉得他们很可能会有不幸的遭遇，这为旁观者平添几分看点，让他们有机会做一番愉快却又饱含怜悯的揣测。
He could on occasion do a pretty stroke of rhyming in those days, and he beguiled the time of waiting by pencilling on a blank page of his prayer-book a few lines which, though kept private then, may be given here:- 那时，他偶尔会作几行漂亮的押韵诗，为了消磨等待的时光，他用铅笔在祈祷书的空白页上写了几行诗。虽然那时这些诗只有他自己知道，不过现在我们可以看一下：
AT A HASTY WEDDING 在一个草率的婚礼上
(Triolet) （八行两韵诗）
If hours be years the twain are blest, 时光转岁月，新人得祝福，
For now they solace swift desire 成全当时愿，
By lifelong ties that tether zest 但结一世缘。

Nor pallid ashes follow fire. 火尽无灰生。

As if, however, to falsify all prophecies, the couple seemed to find in marriage the secret of perpetuating the intoxication of a courtship which, on Maumbry’s side at least, had opened without serious intent. 不过，好像是为了证明所有预言都是假的，这对新人似乎在婚姻中找到了永葆求爱时那种迷醉感觉的秘密，至少对莫姆布里来说，这段感情并非以严肃认真的意图开端。
During the winter following they were the most popular pair in and about Casterbridge—nay in South Wessex itself. 在接下来的冬天，他们在整个卡斯特桥市及其周边——还有整个南威塞克斯——都是最受欢迎的一对。
No smart dinner in the country houses of the younger and gayer families within driving distance of the borough was complete without their lively presence; Mrs. Maumbry was the blithest of the whirling figures at the county ball; and when followed that inevitable incident of garrison-town life, an amateur dramatic entertainment, it was just the same. 只要是这一区域内驾车能到的地方，没有一个年轻快乐的乡村家庭里的丰盛晚宴缺少这两位出席。莫姆布里太太旋转的身影是乡村舞会上最快乐无忧的，她难免被问到乡下的军旅生活，这对外行人来说是极好的消遣，而她的回答也一样快乐无忧。
The acting was for the benefit of such and such an excellent charity—nobody cared what, provided the play were played—and both Captain Maumbry and his wife were in the piece, having been in fact, by mutual consent, the originators of the performance. 有的表演是为了某些卓著的慈善团体而举办的——没人关心是哪些慈善团体，只要有演出就行——莫姆布里上尉夫妇都会参与，事实上，两人已经同意作为演出的发起人。
And so with laughter, and thoughtlessness, and movement, all went merrily. 于是，在无忧无虑的欢笑声和表演中，一切都快乐地进行着。
There was a little backwardness in the bill-paying of the couple; but in justice to them it must be added that sooner or later all owings were paid. 这对夫妻在付账单的时候有点儿拖欠。不过，说句公道话，这里必须要补充一下，他们的欠款或早或晚都已经还清了。
Chapter III 第三章
At the chapel-of-ease attended by the troops there arose above the edge of the pulpit one Sunday an unknown face. 一个星期天，在骑兵们去的小教堂里，讲道坛旁边出现了一个陌生的面孔。
This was the face of a new curate. 这是一个新的助理牧师。
He placed upon the desk, not the familiar sermon book, but merely a Bible. 他放在桌子上的不是大家熟悉的布道书，而只是一本《圣经》。
The person who tells these things was not present at that service, but he soon learnt that the young curate was nothing less than a great surprise to his congregation; a mixed one always, for though the Hussars occupied the body of the building, its nooks and corners were crammed with civilians, whom, up to the present, even the least uncharitable would have described as being attracted thither less by the services than by the soldiery. 讲述这些故事的人当时并不在现场，但他很快就了解到这位年轻的助理牧师让教堂里的信徒大吃一惊。教堂里的信徒一直都形形色色，尽管轻骑兵占据了教堂的主要部分，但边边角角处都挤满了市民。到现在为止，即使是最虔诚的市民都会说，比起教堂仪式，他们更为那些军人所吸引。
Now there arose a second reason for squeezing into an already overcrowded church. 如今，有另一个理由让人们涌向本已拥挤不堪的教堂。
The persuasive and gentle eloquence of Mr. Sainway operated like a charm upon those accustomed only to the higher and dryer styles of preaching, and for a time the other churches of the town were thinned of their sitters. 对于那些只习惯一种高高在上、枯燥无味的布道方式的人们来说，塞恩韦先生极具说服力而又温文尔雅的口才如同一记魔咒，城镇里其他教堂的人数一度大大减少。
At this point in the nineteenth century the sermon was the sole reason for churchgoing amongst a vast body of religious people. 在19世纪的这个时期，对于绝大多数的宗教信徒来说，听布道是他们去教堂的唯一理由。
The liturgy was a formal preliminary, which, like the Royal proclamation in a court of assize, had to be got through before the real interest began; and on reaching home the question was simply: Who preached, and how did he handle his subject? 礼拜仪式是正式的预备阶段，正如巡回法院上的《皇家宣言》一样，是在真正产生兴趣之前不得不完成的过程。人们回到家时脑子里想的问题仅仅是：是谁布道的？如何布道的？
Even had an archbishop officiated in the service proper nobody would have cared much about what was said or sung. 就算是大主教本人主持布道，也没人会关心他说了或唱了些什么内容。

Chapter V 第五章
Casterbridge had known many military and civil episodes; many happy times, and times less happy; and now came the time of her visitation. 卡斯特桥市有许多军人和百姓的轶事，有开心的时刻，也有不开心的时刻。如今，不开心的时刻到了。

Mixen Lane, in the Durnover quarter, and in Maumbry’s parish, was where the blow fell most heavily. 位于德恩诺夫区的米克森巷属于莫姆布里的教区，受到的冲击最为沉重。

She was now free. 现在，她自由了。
She shut up his house at Durnover Cross and returned to her lodgings at Creston. 她关上房门，离开了她丈夫这座位于德恩诺夫交叉路口的小屋，回到了自己在克雷斯顿的住处。
Soon she had a letter from Vannicock, and six weeks after her husband’s death her lover came to see her. 很快，她收到了瓦尼考克的来信。丈夫去世六周之后，她的情人来看她了。
‘I forgot to give you back this—that night,’ he said presently, handing her the little bag she had taken as her whole luggage when leaving. “我忘记把这个还给你了——那天晚上。”他说着，把离开时那个装着她全部行李的小包给她。
Laura received it and absently shook it out. 劳拉接过来，心不在焉地抖开了包裹。
There fell upon the carpet her brush, comb, slippers, nightdress, and other simple necessaries for a journey. 掉落在地板上的是她的牙刷、梳子、拖鞋、睡衣和其他旅行必备的简单物品。
They had an intolerably ghastly look now, and she tried to cover them. 如今，这些物品看上去让人讨厌、难以容忍，于是她设法把它们盖起来。
‘I can now,’ he said, ‘ask you to belong to me legally—when a proper interval has gone—instead of as we meant.’ “现在，我，”他说，“可以合法地向你求婚了——间隔的时间足够长了——我们不用像原先打算的那样私奔了。”
There was languor in his utterance, hinting at a possibility that it was perfunctorily made. 他的话语中有一丝倦怠，可能暗示着这些话只是敷衍。
Laura picked up her articles, answering that he certainly could so ask her—she was free. 劳拉收拾着她的东西，回答说他当然可以这么做——她已经自由了。
Yet not her expression either could be called an ardent response. 不过，她的回答也不能说是热烈。
Then she blinked more and more quickly and put her handkerchief to her face. 然后，她的眼眨动得越来越快，接着她把手帕放在了脸上。
She was weeping violently. 她开始痛哭流涕。
He did not move or try to comfort her in any way. 他没动，也没用任何方式安慰她。
What had come between them? 他们之间怎么了？
No living person. 人已逝。
They had been lovers. 他们曾是情人。
There was now no material obstacle whatever to their union. 他们之间再也没有实质性障碍。
But there was the insistent shadow of that unconscious one; the thin figure of him, moving to and fro in front of the ghastly furnace in the gloom of Durnover Moor. 挥之不去的阴影却一直存在于潜意识中。他瘦弱的身影，在阴郁的德恩诺夫沼泽，在可怕的火炉前来回走动。
Yet Vannicock called upon Laura when he was in the neighbourhood, which was not often; but in two years, as if on purpose to further the marriage which everybody was expecting, the—st Foot returned to Budmouth Regis. 瓦尼考克要是在附近的话，就会去看望劳拉，不过并不经常。两年后，好像是为了故意满足人们对这桩姻缘的期待，圣富特军团回到了巴德摩斯里吉斯。
Thereupon the two could not help encountering each other at times. 于是，两人难免不时碰面。
But whether because the obstacle had been the source of the love, or from a sense of error, and because Mrs. Maumbry bore a less attractive look as a widow than before, their feelings seemed to decline from their former incandescence to a mere tepid civility. 然而，不管是因为两人之间的障碍是爱情之源，或是一种错误的感觉，或者因为变成寡妇的莫姆布里夫人不如以前有魅力了，他们之间的感觉似乎从之前的炽热浓烈变成了如今的不冷不热。
What domestic issues supervened in Vannicock’s further story the man in the oriel never knew; but Mrs. Maumbry lived and died a widow. 瓦尼考克后来是否成家，凸肚窗里的人不知道。不过，莫姆布里夫人一直到死都是寡妇。
