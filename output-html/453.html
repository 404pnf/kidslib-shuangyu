The Forty Thieves 四十大盗
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
In a town in Persia there dwelt two brothers, one named Cassim, the other Ali Baba. 在波斯的一座城镇里住着两兄弟，一个叫卡西姆，另一个叫阿里巴巴。
Cassim was married to a rich wife and lived in plenty, while Ali Baba had to maintain his wife and children by cutting wood in a neighboring forest and selling it in the town. 卡西姆娶了一位家境殷实的妻子，过着丰衣足食的生活，然而阿里巴巴为了养活妻子和孩子，不得不去邻近的森林里砍柴，之后拿到镇上去卖。
One day, when Ali Baba was in the forest, he saw a troop of men on horseback, coming toward him in a cloud of dust. 一天，阿里巴巴正在森林里砍柴，他看到前面尘土飞扬，一支骑着马的队伍正向他靠近。
He was afraid they were robbers, and climbed into a tree for safety. 阿里巴巴担心他们是强盗，为了安全，他爬到了一棵树上。
When they came up to him and dismounted, he counted forty of them. 当队伍来到他附近时，这些人从马上下来，阿里巴巴数了一下人数，一共有四十个人。
They unbridled their horses and tied them to trees. 他们把马的辔头取下，将马拴在了树上。
The finest man among them, whom Ali Baba took to be their captain, went a little way among some bushes, and said: “Open, Sesame!” [1] so plainly that Ali Baba heard him. 他们当中有一位衣着最华丽的人，阿里巴巴认为他应该就是首领，在灌木丛中稍微向前走了几步，说道：“芝麻，开门！”【1】阿里巴巴清晰地听到了这句话。
A door opened in the rocks, and having made the troop go in, he followed them, and the door shut again of itself. 岩石上的一扇门打开了，这群人走了进去，而首领跟在他们后面。当他们进去之后，门又自动关上了。
They stayed some time inside, and Ali Baba, fearing they might come out and catch him, was forced to sit patiently in the tree. 他们在里面呆了一段时间，阿里巴巴怕他们出来后抓住他，不得不耐心地坐在树上等着。
At last the door opened again, and the Forty Thieves came out. 终于，门再一次打开了，四十个强盗从里面走了出来。
As the Captain went in last he came out first, and made them all pass by him; he then closed the door, saying: “Shut, Sesame!” 由于首领最后进的山洞，所以他最先走了出来，所有人都从他身边走过，最后他说道：“芝麻，关门！”门便又关上了。
Every man bridled his horse and mounted, the Captain put himself at their head, and they returned as they came. 每个人都给马套上辔头后，骑上了马，首领走在最前面，他们沿着原路返回了。
Then Ali Baba climbed down and went to the door concealed among the bushes, and said: “Open, Sesame!” and it flew open. 在他们离开之后，阿里巴巴从树上下来，走到了隐藏在树丛间的石门前，说道：“芝麻，开门！”门突然打开了。
Ali Baba, who expected a dull, dismal place, was greatly surprised to find it large and well lighted, hollowed by the hand of man in the form of a vault, which received the light from an opening in the ceiling. 阿里巴巴本以为那里是一个阴暗的地方，但他出乎意料地发现那里宽敞明亮，是一个被人挖空的储藏室，光线从洞顶的通气孔射进来。
He saw rich bales of merchandise—silk, stuff-brocades, all piled together, and gold and silver in heaps, and money in leather purses. 他看到大包大包的货物——丝绸和锦缎堆积在一起，成堆的金银财宝和成皮袋的钞票。
He went in and the door shut behind him. 他走了进去，身后的门便关上了。
He did not look at the silver, but brought out as many bags of gold as he thought his asses, which were browsing outside, could carry, loaded them with the bags, and hid it all with fagots. 阿里巴巴对银子并不感兴趣，而是尽可能地多拿几袋金币出来，因为他想到正在外面吃草的驴子能够驮几袋回去，他把金币全藏在柴火里面。
Using the words: “Shut, Sesame!” he closed the door and went home. “芝麻，关门！”他关上了洞门朝家走去。
Then he drove his asses into the yard, shut the gates, carried the money-bags to his wife, and emptied them out before her. 阿里巴巴赶着驴子走进了院子，他关上大门，把装着钱的袋子拿到妻子面前，倒出了金币。
He bade her keep the secret, and he would go and bury the gold. 他吩咐妻子要保守秘密，之后便想把金币埋起来。
“Let me first measure it,” said his wife. “先让我称一称它们，”妻子说，
“I will go borrow a measure of someone, while you dig the hole.” “我去向别人借一个量器，你来挖洞好了。”
So she ran to the wife of Cassim and borrowed a measure. 于是她便去找卡西姆的妻子，向她借了量器。
Knowing Ali Baba’s poverty, the sister was curious to find out what sort of grain his wife wished to measure, and artfully put some suet at the bottom of the measure. 卡西姆的妻子知道阿里巴巴很贫穷，因此很好奇阿里巴巴的妻子想测量哪种谷物的重量，于是她巧妙地在量器底部刷了一些板油。
Ali Baba’s wife went home and set the measure on the heap of gold, and filled it and emptied it often, to her great content. 阿里巴巴的妻子回到家后开始称重，量器被反反复复地满上、清空了好几次，这使她得到了极大的满足。
She then carried it back to her sister, without noticing that a piece of gold was sticking to it, which Cassim’s wife perceived directly her back was turned. 之后她便把量器还了回去，但并没注意到上面粘着一枚金币，而卡西姆的妻子立刻发现了这枚金币。
She grew very curious, and said to Cassim when he came home: “Cassim, your brother is richer than you. 她对此非常疑惑，等卡西姆回家之后她便对他说：“卡西姆，你的弟弟比你还富有。
He does not count his money, he measures it.” 他不用数的方法，而是用量器量自己有多少钱。”
He begged her to explain this riddle, which she did by showing him the piece of money and telling him where she found it. 他恳求妻子向他解释这究竟是怎么一回事，于是她向卡西姆展示了那枚金币，并且告诉他是在哪里找到的。
Then Cassim grew so envious that he could not sleep, and went to his brother in the morning before sunrise. 卡西姆嫉妒万分，以至于彻夜未眠。第二天天还未亮时，他就去找自己的兄弟。
“Ali Baba,” he said, showing him the gold piece, “you pretend to be poor and yet you measure gold.” “阿里巴巴，”他说，并向他展示那枚金币，“你假装自己很贫穷，但竟然用斗量金币。”
By this Ali Baba perceived that through his wife’s folly Cassim and his wife knew their secret, so he confessed all and offered Cassim a share. 阿里巴巴这才意识到，是妻子的粗心大意使得卡西姆和嫂子知道了他们的秘密，所以他坦白了一切，并分给卡西姆一部分金币。
“That I expect,” said Cassim; “but I must know where to find the treasure, otherwise I will discover all, and you will lose all.” “我猜到会是这样，”卡西姆说，“但是我必须知道在哪里可以找到宝藏，否则我会揭发你，你将失去一切。”
Ali Baba, more out of kindness than fear, told him of the cave, and the very words to use. 更多是出于善良而非恐惧，阿里巴巴向哥哥坦白了山洞的位置和咒语。
Cassim left Ali Baba, meaning to be beforehand with him and get the treasure for himself. 卡西姆离开了阿里巴巴的家，他打算先阿里巴巴一步，独自去找宝藏。
He rose early next morning, and set out with ten mules loaded with great chests. 第二天一大早，卡西姆出发去寻找山洞，他赶着十匹骡子，上面还放着大箱子。
He soon found the place, and the door in the rock. 他很快就发现了那个地方和石头上的门。
He said: “Open, Sesame!” and the door opened and shut behind him. 他说道：“芝麻，开门！”门开了，在他进去之后便关上了。
He could have feasted his eyes all day on the treasures, but he now hastened to gather together as much of it as possible; but when he was ready to go he could not remember what to say for thinking of his great riches. 他本可以一整天在这里尽情地观赏这些财宝，但现在他却急着尽可能多地拿财宝；当他准备离开时，由于他惦记着这不计其数的财富，早已忘记咒语是什么了。
Instead of “Sesame,” he said: “Open, Barley!” and the door remained fast. 他把“芝麻，开门”说成了“大麦，开门”，因此门依然紧闭。
He named several different sorts of grain, all but the right one, and the door still stuck fast. 卡西姆说了几种不同的谷物，就是没有说出正确的那个，门依旧死死地关着。
He was so frightened at the danger he was in that he had as much forgotten the word as if he had never heard it. 他恐惧万分，因为此时他就像之前没听过那句咒语一样，完全忘记了该说什么。
About noon the robbers returned to their cave, and saw Cassim’s mules roving about with great chests on their backs. 不久，强盗回到了山洞，他们看到卡西姆的骡子在外面徘徊，背上还有巨大的箱子。
This gave them the alarm; they drew their sabres, and went to the door, which opened on their Captain’s saying: “Open, Sesame!” 这让他们警觉起来；他们取出刀剑，来到了洞门。首领说着咒语：“芝麻，开门！”门便打开了。
Cassim, who had heard the trampling of their horses’ feet, resolved to sell his life dearly, so when the door opened he leaped out and threw the Captain down. 卡西姆听到了他们的马蹄声，决定殊死一搏，所以当洞门打开的时候，他冲了出来，把首领撞倒了。
In vain, however, for the robbers with their sabres soon killed him. 然而，这是徒劳的，这些手拿刀剑的强盗很快就将他杀死了。
On entering the cave they saw all the bags laid ready, and could not imagine how anyone had got in without knowing their secret. 他们走进山洞，看到满地是准备抬出去的袋子，很难想象不知道咒语的外人究竟是怎样进来的。
They cut Cassim’s body into four quarters, and nailed them up inside the cave, in order to frighten anyone who should venture in, and went away in search of more treasure. 他们把卡西姆的身体大卸八块，然后钉挂在洞内，以此来警戒那些进来冒险的人，之后他们便离开去寻找更多的宝藏。
As night drew on Cassim’s wife grew very uneasy, and ran to her brother-in-law, and told him where her husband had gone. 随着夜晚的到来，卡西姆的妻子变得十分不安，她跑到小叔子那里，告诉他卡西姆去了哪里。
Ali Baba did his best to comfort her, and set out to the forest in search of Cassim. 阿里巴巴竭力安慰她，然后动身前往森林去寻找卡西姆。
The first thing he saw on entering the cave was his dead brother. 在他进入山洞后，首先映入眼帘的就是他哥哥的尸体。
Full of horror, he put the body on one of his asses, and bags of gold on the other two, and, covering all with some fagots, returned home. 阿里巴巴害怕极了，他把尸体放在其中一头驴身上，然后把几袋金币放在了另外两头身上，用柴火盖在上面返回了家里。
He drove the two asses laden with gold into his own yard, and led the other to Cassim’s house. 他把身上运载金币的两匹驴子牵回了家，把另外一头带到了卡西姆家里。
The door was opened by the slave Morgiana, whom he knew to be both brave and cunning. 家里的仆人摩吉妮娜打开门，阿里巴巴知道她是个既勇敢又聪明的人。
Unloading the ass, he said to her: “This is the body of your master, who has been murdered, but whom we must bury as though he had died in his bed. 阿里巴巴把尸体从驴子身上卸下来，告诉她说：“这个是你主人的尸首，他是被人谋杀的，但是我们必须埋了他，好像他是在床上死去了一样。
I will speak with you again, but now tell your mistress I am come.” 我之后再和你说，但现在你去告诉你的女主人我回来了。”
The wife of Cassim, on learning the fate of her husband, broke out into cries and tears, but Ali Baba offered to take her to live with him and his wife if she would promise to keep his counsel and leave everything to Morgiana; whereupon she agreed, and dried her eyes. 卡西姆的妻子听到丈夫的死讯后放声大哭，但是阿里巴巴愿意让她与自己和妻子一起生活，只要她承诺保守这个秘密，并且把所有的东西留给摩吉妮娜；她擦干眼泪，答应了，
Morgiana, meanwhile, sought an apothecary and asked him for some lozenges. 与此同时，摩吉妮娜找到了一位药师，向他要了一些锭剂。
“My poor master,” she said, “can neither eat nor speak, and no one knows what his distemper is.” “我可怜的主人，”她说，“既不能吃饭又不能说话，没人知道他得了哪种病。”
She carried home the lozenges and returned next day weeping, and asked for an essence only given to those just about to die. 她把锭剂带回了家，第二天又哭着向药师要了一服只给快死的人才开的药。
Thus, in the evening, no one was surprised to hear the wretched shrieks and cries of Cassim’s wife and Morgiana, telling everyone that Cassim was dead. 就这样到了晚上，卡西姆的妻子和摩吉妮娜大声地哭喊，十分可怜，没有人对此感到惊讶，大家都知道卡西姆死去了。
The day after Morgiana went to an old cobbler near the gates of the town who opened his stall early, put a piece of gold in his hand, and bade him follow her with his needle and thread. 第二天，摩吉妮娜在离城门不远处找到了一个很早就开始营业的老鞋匠，摩吉妮娜给了他一枚金币，让他拿着针线跟自己走。
Having bound his eyes with a handkerchief, she took him to the room where the body lay, pulled off the bandage, and bade him sew the quarters together, after which she covered his eyes again and led him home. 老鞋匠的眼睛被蒙上一块手帕，他被摩吉妮娜带到了陈放尸体的地方，她把手帕解开，要求老鞋匠把这些一块块的尸体缝合起来，在缝好之后她又把他蒙住，送他回家。
Then they buried Cassim, and Morgiana his slave followed him to the grave, weeping and tearing her hair, while Cassim’s wife stayed at home uttering lamentable cries. 于是，他们把卡西姆给埋了，卡西姆的仆人摩吉妮娜来到主人的坟墓，一边哭一边撕扯自己的头发，而此时，卡西姆的妻子呆在家里悲伤地哭泣。
Next day she went to live with Ali Baba, who gave Cassim’s shop to his eldest son. 第二天，她就搬去和阿里巴巴住在一起，阿里巴巴把卡西姆的店铺交给了他的大儿子。
The Forty Thieves, on their return to the cave, were much astonished to find Cassim’s body gone and some of their money-bags. 四十个强盗返回他们的山洞，震惊地发现卡西姆的尸体和他们的几袋钱币都不见了。
“We are certainly discovered,” said the Captain, “and shall be undone if we cannot find out who it is that knows our secret. “我们肯定是被别人发现了，”首领说，“如果我们找不到是谁知道这个秘密，那我们就前功尽弃了。
Two men must have known it; we have killed one, we must now find the other. 一定有两个人知道了这件事；我们已经杀了一个，现在必须找到另一个。
To this end one of you who is bold and artful must go into the city dressed as a traveler, and discover whom we have killed, and whether men talk of the strange manner of his death. 为了找到这个人，你们当中必须有一个勇敢机智的人，装扮成旅行者前往镇上，找出我们杀死的那个家伙是谁以及人们是否谈论他奇怪的死因。
If the messenger fails he must lose his life, lest we be betrayed.” 如果被派遣的人失败了，那么他必须死，以免背叛我们。”
One of the thieves started up and offered to do this, and after the rest had highly commended him for his bravery he disguised himself, and happened to enter the town at daybreak, just by Baba Mustapha’s stall. 其中一个强盗请缨前往，在大家高度称赞他勇敢。他乔装打扮，在黎明时分前往镇上，恰巧路过了巴巴穆斯塔法的货摊。
The thief bade him good-day, saying: “Honest man, how can you possibly see to stitch at your age?” 强盗问候他早安，说：“诚实的人，你这么大年岁了怎么可能看得见针？”
“Old as I am,” replied the cobbler, “I have very good eyes, and will you believe me when I tell you that I sewed a dead body together in a place where I had less light than I have now.” “我虽然老了，”老鞋匠回答说，“但我的眼睛很好，你相信吗，我在一个比现在还要黑的地方把一个死人的尸体缝合在一起。”
The robber was overjoyed at his good fortune, and, giving him a piece of gold, desired to be shown the house where he stitched up the dead body. 强盗对自己的好运感到欣喜若狂，他给了老鞋匠一枚金币，希望他能带自己前往他缝合尸体的地方。
At first Mustapha refused, saying that he had been blindfolded; but when the robber gave him another piece of gold he began to think he might remember the turnings if blindfolded as before. 一开始穆斯塔法拒绝这样做，说自己当时眼睛被蒙上了；但是当强盗给他另一枚金币时，他开始思考如果像之前一样双眼被蒙上，或许自己会记得那些弯路。
This means succeeded; the robber partly led him, and was partly guided by him, right in front of Cassim’s house, the door of which the robber marked with a piece of chalk. 这就意味着这件事能成功了；强盗一会儿引导他，一会儿跟随他，正好来到了卡西姆的家门前，强盗用粉笔在门上做了一个记号。
Then, well pleased, he bade farewell to Baba Mustapha and returned to the forest. 于是，强盗感到十分开心，和巴巴穆斯塔法告别后返回到森林。
By and by Morgiana, going out, saw the mark the robber had made, quickly guessed that some mischief was brewing, and fetching a piece of chalk marked two or three doors on each side, without saying anything to her master or mistress. 后来摩吉妮娜出门，看到了强盗做的记号，她马上猜到有不好的事情要发生。于是她拿来了一根粉笔，在几家的门上做了记号，但她并没有把这件事告诉她的男主人或女主人。
The thief, meantime, told his comrades of his discovery. 与此同时，强盗告诉了同伙自己的发现。
The Captain thanked him, and bade him show him the house he had marked. 首领十分感谢他，并要求他带着自己前往他先前做记号的房子那里。
But when they came to it they saw that five or six of the houses were chalked in the same manner. 但是当他们到达之后，发现有五六家的房子上都用粉笔做了同样的记号。
The guide was so confounded that he knew not what answer to make, and when they returned he was at once beheaded for having failed. 带路的强盗十分困惑，他不知道该说些什么，当他们返回之后，马上就因为自己的失败被砍了头。
Another robber was dispatched, and, having won over Baba Mustapha, marked the house in red chalk; but Morgiana being again too clever for them, the second messenger was put to death also. 另一个强盗也被派遣出去，在说服了巴巴穆斯塔法之后，他在房子上用红色的粉笔做了记号；但是摩吉妮娜太聪明了，第二个派遣者最终也被杀了。
The Captain now resolved to go himself, but, wiser than the others, he did not mark the house, but looked at it so closely that he could not fail to remember it. 现在首领决定自己前往，但由于他聪明绝顶，他不需要在房子上做记号，只是很近地观察了这个房子便记住了。
He returned, and ordered his men to go into the neighboring villages and buy nineteen mules, and thirty-eight leather jars, all empty except one, which was full of oil. 他回去之后，要求手下去邻村买了十九头骡子和三十八个皮罐子，除了其中一个灌满了油之外，其他都是空的。
The Captain put one of his men, fully armed, into each, rubbing the outside of the jars with oil from the full vessel. 首领让他的手下全副武装，钻进皮罐子里，再从装满油的罐子里取些油，在每个罐子的外表面上涂擦。
Then the nineteen mules were loaded with thirty-seven robbers in jars, and the jar of oil, and reached the town by dusk. 于是，十九头骡子驮着藏在罐子里的强盗和装满油的罐子，在黄昏时分到达了镇上。
The Captain stopped his mules in front of Ali Baba’s house, and said to Ali Baba, who was sitting outside for coolness: “I have brought some oil from a distance to sell at to-morrow’s market, but it is now so late that I know not where to pass the night, unless you will do me the favor to take me in.” 首领在阿里巴巴家门前停下了骡子，此时阿里巴巴正在外面乘凉，首领对他说：“我从远处带来了一些油，准备在明天的集市上卖，但是今天太晚了，除非你好心收留我，否则我都不知道今夜在哪里度过。”
Though Ali Baba had seen the Captain of the robbers in the forest, he did not recognize him in the disguise of an oil merchant. 尽管阿里巴巴曾在森林里见过首领，但并没有认出伪装成油商的他。
He bade him welcome, opened his gates for the mules to enter, and went to Morgiana to bid her prepare a bed and supper for his guest. 阿里巴巴对他表示欢迎，把门打开让骡子进来，同时让摩吉妮娜为客人准备一张床和晚餐。
He brought the stranger into his hall, and after they had supped went again to speak to Morgiana in the kitchen, while the Captain went into the yard under pretense of seeing after his mules, but really to tell his men what to do. 他带着这位陌生人走进门厅，等他们吃完晚饭之后，阿里巴巴再一次出去和正在厨房的摩吉妮娜说话，就在此时，首领以看骡子为借口来到院子里，但实际上是想告诉他的手下应该怎么做。
Beginning at the first jar and ending at the last, he said to each man: “As soon as I throw some stones from the window of the chamber where I lie, cut the jars open with your knives and come out, and I will be with you in a trice.” 他从第一个罐子走到最后一个，依次告诉大家：“当我从休息的房间窗户内扔出几块石头时，你们马上用刀把罐子切开出来，而我会迅速地来到你们身边。”
He returned to the house, and Morgiana led him to his chamber. 首领回到屋里后，摩吉妮娜便带他去住的房间。
She then told Abdallah, her fellow-slave, to set on the pot to make some broth for her master, who had gone to bed. 摩吉妮娜告诉她的同伴阿卜杜拉，用锅为主人做一些肉汤，而这时阿里巴巴已经去睡觉了。
Meanwhile her lamp went out, and she had no more oil in the house. 与此同时，她的灯熄灭了，屋子里也没有多余的油来点亮灯。
“Do not be uneasy,” said Abdallah; “go into the yard and take some out of one of those jars.” “别担心，”阿卜杜拉说，“去院子里从其中一个罐子里取一些油。”
Morgiana thanked him for his advice, took the oil pot, and went into the yard. 摩吉妮娜感谢他出的主意，拿着油壶来到了院子里。
When she came to the first jar the robber inside said softly: “Is it time?” 当她走到第一个罐子时，里面的强盗小声地说：“是时候了吗？”
Any other slave but Morgiana, on finding a man in the jar instead of the oil she wanted, would have screamed and made a noise; but she, knowing the danger her master was in, bethought herself of a plan, and answered quietly: “Not yet, but presently.” 除了摩吉妮娜以外，任何一个仆人如果在罐子里发现一个人而并非她想要的油，都会尖叫起来，吵吵闹闹一番；但是，摩吉妮娜知道她的主人身陷危险之中，便想了一个计划，于是她轻声回答道：“还没呢，快了。”
She went to all the jars, giving the same answer, till she came to the jar of oil. 她走到所有罐子旁边，说了相同的答案，直到她来到了装有油的罐子旁。
She now saw that her master, thinking to entertain an oil merchant, had let thirty-eight robbers into his house. 她现在知道了，主人本想招待这个油商，实际上是让三十八个强盗进了家里。
She filled her oil pot, went back to the kitchen, and, having lit her lamp, went again to the oil jar and filled a large kettle full of oil. 她装好自己的油壶，走回厨房，点燃油灯，又回到油罐旁装满了一大壶的油。
When it boiled she went and poured enough oil into every jar to stifle and kill the robber inside. 她把油煮开之后，将足够多的油倒进了每个罐子里，这样里面的强盗就被闷死了。
When this brave deed was done she went back to the kitchen, put out the fire and the lamp, and waited to see what would happen. 当她完成这一勇敢的行动后，她又回到了厨房，把火和灯熄灭，等待着会有什么事情发生。
In a quarter of an hour the Captain of the robbers awoke, got up, and opened the window. 十五分钟后，强盗的首领醒来了，于是他打开了窗户。
As all seemed quiet, he threw down some little pebbles which hit the jars. 一切看起来都好像很平静，他朝罐子上扔了一些小石子。
He listened, and as none of his men seemed to stir he grew uneasy, and went down into the yard. 他听着声音，好像没有一个手下开始活动起来，这使得他感到不安，于是走到了院子里。
On going to the first jar and saying, “Are you asleep?” he smelt the hot boiled oil, and knew at once that his plot to murder Ali Baba and his household had been discovered. 当他来到第一个罐子时问道：“你睡了吗？”他闻到了一股热油味儿，马上就知道他密谋杀害阿里巴巴和他全家的计划被发现了。
He found all the gang was dead, and, missing the oil out of the last jar, became aware of the manner of their death. 他发现所有的强盗都已经死了，同时最后一个罐子里的油漏了出来，这让他明白了他们的死因。
He then forced the lock of a door leading into a garden, and climbing over several walls made his escape. 于是他用力推开了通向花园的门，翻过了几座墙逃跑了。
Morgiana heard and saw all this, and, rejoicing at her success, went to bed and fell asleep. 摩吉妮娜听到并看到了这一切，为自己的成功感到高兴，于是回到床上睡觉去了。
At daybreak Ali Baba arose, and, seeing the oil jars still there, asked why the merchant had not gone with his mules. 到了黎明，阿里巴巴起床后看到所有的油罐子依然在那里，便问为什么商人没有牵自己的骡子就离开了。
Morgiana bade him look in the first jar and see if there was any oil. 摩吉妮娜让他看看第一个罐子里还有没有油。
Seeing a man, he started back in terror. 看到里面有个男人，他吓了一跳。
“Have no fear,” said Morgiana; “the man cannot harm you: he is dead.” “不要害怕，”摩吉妮娜说，“他伤害不了你，他已经死了。”
Ali Baba, when he had recovered somewhat from his astonishment, asked what had become of the merchant. 当阿里巴巴从惊吓中稍回过神之后，便询问商人怎么样了。
“Merchant!” said she, “he is no more a merchant than I am!” and she told him the whole story, assuring him that it was a plot of the robbers of the forest, of whom only three were left, and that the white and red chalk marks had something to do with it. “商人！”她说，“他和我一样都不是商人！”她告诉了阿里巴巴所有的故事，让他相信这是森林里强盗们的一个密谋，只有三个强盗离开了，而白色和红色的粉笔记号与这件事有关。
Ali Baba at once gave Morgiana her freedom, saying that he owed her his life. 阿里巴巴马上给了摩吉妮娜自由，说她可以过自己的生活。
They then buried the bodies in Ali Baba’s garden, while the mules were sold in the market by his slaves. 于是他们把这些尸体埋在了阿里巴巴家的花园里，而骡子由他的仆人在集市上卖掉了。
The Captain returned to his lonely cave, which seemed frightful to him without his lost companions, and firmly resolved to avenge them by killing Ali Baba. 首领回到了他荒凉的山洞，由于他的同伴都死了，山洞看起来很可怕。他下决心要为他们报仇，把阿里巴巴杀死。
He dressed himself carefully, and went into the town, where he took lodgings in an inn. 他精心地装扮一番，来到了镇上，暂住在一家小旅馆里。
In the course of a great many journeys to the forest he carried away many rich stuffs and much fine linen, and set up a shop opposite that of Ali Baba’s son. 他多次回到森林，把许多值钱的东西和亚麻细布搬到镇上，在阿里巴巴儿子家的对面开了一家店铺。
He called himself Cogia Hassan, and as he was both civil and well dressed he soon made friends with Ali Baba’s son, and through him with Ali Baba, whom he was continually asking to sup with him. 他给自己起名叫科吉亚汉森，由于他彬彬有礼、穿着得体，很快就和阿里巴巴的儿子成了朋友，又通过他和阿里巴巴成了朋友，并且经常邀请阿里巴巴和他一起吃饭。
Ali Baba, wishing to return his kindness, invited him into his house and received him smiling, thanking him for his kindness to his son. 阿里巴巴为了回报他的好意，邀请他去自己家，满脸笑容地接待他，感谢他对自己儿子的帮助。
When the merchant was about to take his leave Ali Baba stopped him, saying: “Where are you going, sir, in such haste? 当商人要离开的时候，阿里巴巴叫住了他，说道：“你要去哪里啊，先生，怎么这么着急？
Will you not stay and sup with me?” 你不留下和我一起吃饭吗？”
The merchant refused, saying that he had a reason; and, on Ali Baba’s asking him what that was, he replied: “It is, sir, that I can eat no victuals that have any salt in them.” 商人拒绝了他，说有自己的原因；阿里巴巴问他是什么原因时，他回答道：“先生，是因为我不能吃任何有咸味的食物。”
“If that is all,” said Ali Baba, “let me tell you that there shall be no salt in either the meat or the bread that we eat to-night.” “如果这就是全部的原因，”阿里巴巴说，“那让我告诉你，我们今晚吃的肉和面包里都不会有盐。”
He went to give this order to Morgiana, who was much surprised. 他把这条指令下达给摩吉妮娜，摩吉妮娜感到非常吃惊。
“Who is this man,” she said, “who eats no salt with his meat?” “这个人是谁，”她说，“谁吃肉不加盐啊？”
“He is an honest man, Morgiana,” returned her master; “therefore do as I bid you.” “他是一个正直的人，摩吉妮娜，”她的主人回答道，“所以按照我说的那样做。”
But she could not withstand a desire to see this strange man, so she helped Abdallah to carry up the dishes, and saw in a moment that Cogia Hassan was the robber Captain, and carried a dagger under his garment. 但是她忍不住想看看这个奇怪的人，所以她帮助阿卜杜拉端盘子，马上就认出科吉亚汉森是那个强盗首领，而且他还藏了一把匕首在衣服下面。
“I am not surprised,” she said to herself, “that this wicked man, who intends to kill my master, will eat no salt with him; but I will hinder his plans.” “我并不感到惊讶，”她自言自语道，“这个打算杀死我主人的邪恶男人想要吃不加盐的食物，但是我会阻碍他的计划。”
She sent up the supper by Abdallah, while she made ready for one of the boldest acts that could be thought on. 她把阿卜杜拉做好的晚饭送上去，这时她准备采取能想到的最大胆的行动。
When the dessert had been served, Cogia Hassan was left alone with Ali Baba and his son, whom he thought to make drunk and then to murder them. 当甜点送上去时，科吉亚汉森独自与阿里巴巴和他儿子呆在一起。他想要灌醉他们，然后把他们杀掉。
Morgiana, meanwhile, put on a head-dress like a dancing-girl’s, and clasped a girdle round her waist, from which hung a dagger with a silver hilt, and said to Abdallah: “Take your tabor, and let us go and divert our master and his guest.” 与此同时，摩吉妮娜戴上了一条类似于舞娘一样的头巾，腰部扣紧了一条腰带，腰带上挂着一把银柄的匕首，对阿卜杜拉说：“拿着你的手鼓，我们俩过去让我们的主人和客人娱乐一下。”
Abdallah took his tabor and played before Morgiana until they came to the door, where Abdallah stopped playing and Morgiana made a low courtesy. 阿卜杜拉拿着他的手鼓在摩吉妮娜前面演奏，直到他们来到了门口。这时，阿卜杜拉停止了演奏，摩吉妮娜礼貌地问候了一下。
“Come in, Morgiana,” said Ali Baba, “and let Cogia Hassan see what you can do”; and, turning to Cogia Hassan, he said: “She’s my slave and my housekeeper.” “进来吧，摩吉妮娜，”阿里巴巴说，“让科吉亚汉森看看你们的本领。”接着他转向科吉亚汉森，对他说：“这是我的仆人兼管家。”
Cogia Hassan was by no means pleased, for he feared that his chance of killing Ali Baba was gone for the present; but he pretended great eagerness to see Morgiana, and Abdallah began to play and Morgiana to dance. 科吉亚汉森并没有感到开心，因为他害怕自己现在已经失去了谋杀阿里巴巴的机会，但是他假装有极大的兴趣，想看看摩吉妮娜和阿卜杜拉的舞蹈和演奏。
After she had performed several dances she drew her dagger and made passes with it, sometimes pointing it at her own breast, sometimes at her master’s, as if it were part of the dance. 在她表演了几支舞蹈后，她拿出自己的匕首在空中舞动，有时把刀指向自己的胸膛，有时指向主人，好像这是舞蹈的一部分。
Suddenly, out of breath, she snatched the tabor from Abdallah with her left hand, and, holding the dagger in her right hand, held out the tabor to her master. 突然，摩吉妮娜气喘吁吁，她用左手夺过阿卜杜拉的手，用右手握住匕首，把手鼓伸向她的主人。
Ali Baba and his son put a piece of gold into it, and Cogia Hassan, seeing that she was coming to him, pulled out his purse to make her a present, but while he was putting his hand into it Morgiana plunged the dagger into his heart. 阿里巴巴和他的儿子向里面放了一枚金币。科吉亚汉森看到摩吉妮娜来到他面前，便拿出他的钱包要给她一个礼物，但是就在他把手放进去的时候，摩吉妮娜把匕首刺向了他的心脏。
“Unhappy girl!” cried Ali Baba and his son, “what have you done to ruin us?” “煞风景的女孩！”阿里巴巴和他的儿子大喊，“你怎么能这么做来破坏我们的宴会？”
“It was to preserve you, master, not to ruin you,” answered Morgiana. “我是要保护你们，主人，不是要破坏你们，”摩吉妮娜说，
“See here,” opening the false merchant’s garment and showing the dagger; “see what an enemy you have entertained! “看看这儿，”她掀开了假商人的衣服，拿出了他的匕首，“看看你们招待了怎样一个敌人！
Remember, he would eat no salt with you, and what more would you have? 想想吧，他都不和你们一起吃带盐的食物，他还不知道要对你们做什么呢。
Look at him! he is both the false oil merchant and the Captain of the Forty Thieves.” 看看他！他既是假冒的油商，又是那四十个强盗的首领。”
Ali Baba was so grateful to Morgiana for thus saving his life that he offered her to his son in marriage, who readily consented, and a few days after the wedding was celebrated with greatest splendor. 阿里巴巴十分感谢摩吉妮娜救了自己，因此他让自己的儿子与她成婚，儿子也马上答应了。几天之后，他们举行了盛大的婚礼。
At the end of a year Ali Baba, hearing nothing of the two remaining robbers, judged they were dead, and set out to the cave. 年末的时候，阿里巴巴没有听过关于剩下两个强盗的任何事情，断定他们是死了，决定出发前往山洞。
The door opened on his saying: “Open Sesame!” 洞门在他说了“芝麻，开门！”之后就开了。
He went in, and saw that nobody had been there since the Captain left it. 他走了进去，发现自从首领离开之后就没有人到过这里。
He brought away as much gold as he could carry, and returned to town. 他尽可能多地拿走了金子，然后返回到镇上。
He told his son the secret of the cave, which his son handed down in his turn, so the children and grandchildren of Ali Baba were rich to the end of their lives. [2] 他告诉了他的儿子这个关于山洞的秘密，接着由他的儿子把这个秘密传了下去，所以到了阿里巴巴的孩子和孙儿女们时，他们仍旧很富裕。【2】
[1]Sesame is a kind of grain. 【1】芝麻是一种谷物。
[2]Arabian Nights. 【2】《天方夜谭》
