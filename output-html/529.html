The Three Dogs 三只狗
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
There was once upon a time a shepherd who had two children, a son and a daughter. 从前有一个牧羊人，他有两个孩子，一个男孩，一个女孩。
When he was on his death-bed he turned to them and said, ‘I have nothing to leave you but three sheep and a small house; divide them between you, as you like, but don’t quarrel over them whatever you do.’ 当他躺在床上奄奄一息的时候，他对自己的两个孩子说：“除了三只羊和一个小屋，我没有什么东西能留给你们了。你们两个人想怎么分就怎么分吧，但无论如何都不要吵架。”
When the shepherd was dead, the brother asked his sister which she would like best, the sheep or the little house; and when she had chosen the house he said, ‘Then I’ll take the sheep and go out to seek my fortune in the wide world. 牧羊人死去后，弟弟问姐姐想要羊还是想要小屋，姐姐选了小屋，于是弟弟就说：“那我就要羊吧，我要去外面广阔的世界里碰碰运气。
I don’t see why I shouldn’t be as lucky as many another who has set out on the same search, and it wasn’t for nothing that I was born on a Sunday.’ 我觉得我会和许多同样出门碰运气的人一样幸运的，我可不是白白生在星期天的。”
And so he started on his travels, driving his three sheep in front of him, and for a long time it seemed as if fortune didn’t mean to favour him at all. 说完，他就把三只羊赶到前面，开始了他的旅程。但是，过了很长时间，好运似乎并没有眷顾他。
One day he was sitting disconsolately at a cross road, when a man suddenly appeared before him with three black dogs, each one bigger than the other. 一天，他正闷闷不乐地坐在十字路口，这时，一个男人突然出现在他面前，身边还带着三只狗，一只比一只大。
‘Hullo, my fine fellow,’ said the man, ‘I see you have three fat sheep. “喂，小伙子，”这个人说，“我看到你有三只肥羊。
I’ll tell you what; if you’ll give them to me, I’ll give you my three dogs.’ 这样吧，如果你把它们给我，我就把我的三只狗给你。”
In spite of his sadness, the youth smiled and replied, ‘What would I do with your dogs? 年轻人虽然很忧伤，但还是笑着回答说：“我要你的狗有什么用呢？
My sheep at least feed themselves, but I should have to find food for the dogs.’ 我的羊至少可以喂饱它们自己，但狗还得我给它们找吃的。”
‘My dogs are not like other dogs,’ said the stranger; ‘they will feed you instead of you them, and will make your fortune. “我的狗不像别的狗，”陌生人说道，“它们不但不用你喂，反倒能喂饱你，还能让你发大财。
The smallest one is called “Salt,” and will bring you food whenever you wish; the second is called “Pepper,” and will tear anyone to pieces who offers to hurt you; and the great big strong one is called “Mustard,” and is so powerful that it will break iron or steel with its teeth.’ 最小的那只叫‘盐’，只要你想吃东西，它就会给你弄到吃的；大一点儿的那只叫‘胡椒’，如果有人想伤害你，它就会把他们撕成碎片；最大、最强壮的那只叫‘芥末’，它力大无穷，能用牙咬碎钢铁。”
The shepherd at last let himself be persuaded, and gave the stranger his sheep. 年轻的牧羊人最终被说服了，把他的三只羊给了陌生人。
In order to test the truth of his statement about the dogs, he said at once, ‘Salt, I am hungry,’ and before the words were out of his mouth the dog had disappeared, and returned in a few minutes with a large basket full of the most delicious food. 他想知道陌生人说的是不是真的，就立刻说道：“盐，我饿了。”话还没有说完，那只狗就消失了，几分钟后就叼着满满一大篮子的美味佳肴回来了。
Then the youth congratulated himself on the bargain he had made, and continued his journey in the best of spirits. 年轻人暗自庆幸自己做了这么划算的一笔交易，就高高兴兴地继续上路了。
One day he met a carriage and pair, all draped in black; even the horses were covered with black trappings, and the coachman was clothed in crape from top to toe. 一天，他遇到了一辆双驾马车，马车通体都被黑色的帷幔遮盖着，连拉车的马身上的马饰都是黑色的，而赶车的马夫更是从头到脚都裹着黑纱。
Inside the carriage sat a beautiful girl in a black dress crying bitterly. 马车里坐着一个身穿黑色裙子的美丽女孩，女孩正伤心地痛哭着。
The horses advanced slowly and mournfully, with their heads bent on the ground. 拉车的马儿头低向地面，缓慢而忧伤地向前行进着。
‘Coachman, what’s the meaning of all this grief?’ asked the shepherd. “车夫，你们为什么这么难过啊？”牧羊人问道。
At first the coachman wouldn’t say anything, but when the youth pressed him he told him that a huge dragon dwelt in the neighbourhood, and required yearly the sacrifice of a beautiful maiden. 刚开始，车夫什么也不愿意说，但在牧羊人的一再追问下，他终于告诉牧羊人，这一带住着一条巨龙，巨龙每年都要他们献上一位美丽的少女。
This year the lot had fallen on the King’s daughter, and the whole country was filled with woe and lamentation in consequence. 今年轮到了国王的女儿，因此，整个国家都沉浸在悲痛和哀悼的气氛中。
The shepherd felt very sorry for the lovely maiden, and determined to follow the carriage. 牧羊人很同情这位可爱的少女，就决定跟着马车。
In a little it halted at the foot of a high mountain. 不一会儿，马车就停在了一座高山的山脚下。
The girl got out, and walked slowly and sadly to meet her terrible fate. 女孩下了马车，缓慢而忧伤地走向她悲惨的命运。
The coachman perceived that the shepherd wished to follow her, and warned him not to do so if he valued his life; but the shepherd wouldn’t listen to his advice. 车夫发现牧羊人想跟着她，就警告他说，如果他还珍惜自己性命的话，就不要那么做，但牧羊人不肯听从他的忠告。
When they had climbed about half-way up the hill they saw a terrible-looking monster with the body of a snake, and with huge wings and claws, coming towards them, breathing forth flames of fire, and preparing to seize its victim. 爬到差不多半山腰的地方，他们看到一个长得很吓人的怪兽，它有着蛇一样的身体、巨大的翅膀和爪子。怪兽向他们冲来，呼出一团团的火焰，想抓住它的祭品。
Then the shepherd called, ‘Pepper, come to the rescue,’ and the second dog set upon the dragon, and after a fierce struggle bit it so sharply in the neck that the monster rolled over, and in a few moments breathed its last. 这时，牧羊人大喊了一声“胡椒，快来救我”，大一点儿的那只狗就跳到了龙身上，和它激烈地打斗起来，最后在恶龙的脖子上狠狠咬了一口。那头猛兽疼得打了个滚，过了一会儿就死掉了。
Then the dog ate up the body, all except its two front teeth, which the shepherd picked up and put in his pocket. 狗吃掉了它的身体，只留下两颗门牙，牧羊人把它们捡起来，放进了自己的口袋里。
The Princess was quite overcome with terror and joy, and fell fainting at the feet of her deliverer. 那位公主心里充满了极度的恐惧和喜悦，于是晕倒在了她救命恩人的脚边。
When she recovered her consciousness she begged the shepherd to return with her to her father, who would reward him richly. 清醒过来后，她就恳求牧羊人和她一起回到自己父亲身边，说父亲会好好奖赏牧羊人的。
But the youth answered that he wanted to see something of the world, and that he would return again in three years, and nothing would make him change this resolve. 但年轻的牧羊人回答说，他想见识见识世界。他承诺三年后会再回来，但现在，什么也不能让他改变自己的决心。
The Princess seated herself once more in her carriage, and, bidding each other farewell, she and the shepherd separated, she to return home, and he to see the world. 公主重新坐回马车里，并向牧羊人道了别。两个人就此分开，公主回了家，牧羊人则重新踏上了见识世界的旅程。
But while the Princess was driving over a bridge the carriage suddenly stood still, and the coachman turned round to her and said, ‘Your deliverer has gone, and doesn’t thank you for your gratitude. 但公主乘坐的马车在过桥时突然停下了，车夫转身对公主说：“你的救命恩人走了，而且也没有感谢你的知恩图报。
It would be nice of you to make a poor fellow happy; therefore you may tell your father that it was I who slew the dragon, and if you refuse to, I will throw you into the river, and no one will be any the wiser, for they will think the dragon has devoured you.’ 如果能让一个穷人幸福，那你就真的是太好了。所以，你要告诉你父亲，是我杀了巨龙，如果你不这么做的话，我就把你扔到河里，没有人会发现的，他们会认为是巨龙把你吃掉了。”
The maiden was in a dreadful state when she heard these words; but there was nothing for her to do but to swear that she would give out the coachman as her deliverer, and not to divulge the secret to anyone. 听到这些话的时候，少女心里很害怕，但是她什么也做不了，只能发誓说会告诉人们车夫是她的救命恩人，绝不会向任何人泄露这个秘密。
So they returned to the capital, and everyone was delighted when they saw the Princess had returned unharmed; the black flags were taken down from all the palace towers, and gay-coloured ones put up in their place, and the King embraced his daughter and her supposed rescuer with tears of joy, and, turning to the coachman, he said, ‘You have not only saved the life of my child, but you have also freed the country from a terrible scourge; therefore, it is only fitting that you should be richly rewarded. 就这样，他们回到了都城。大家发现他们的公主毫发无损地回来了，都很高兴。所有宫殿里的塔上的黑旗都被降下来了，国王流着泪拥抱了他的女儿和她的冒牌救命恩人，然后对车夫说：“你不仅救了我的孩子，还让整个国家免于一场可怕的灾难，因此，我一定要好好奖赏你。
Take, therefore, my daughter for your wife; but as she is still so young, do not let the marriage be celebrated for another year.’ 这样吧，我把女儿嫁给你，不过，她还太小了，一年以后再举行婚礼吧。”
The coachman thanked the King for his graciousness, and was then led away to be richly dressed and instructed in all the arts and graces that befitted his new position. 车夫感谢了国王的好意，就被仆从领去换上了华贵的衣服，并接受各种艺术和礼仪方面的指导，以配得上他的新地位。
But the poor Princess wept bitterly, though she did not dare to confide her grief to anyone. 可怜的公主却伤心地痛哭着，然而，她不敢向任何人倾诉她的悲伤。
When the year was over, she begged so hard for another year’s respite that it was granted to her. 一年过去了，她苦苦哀求父亲延缓一年，国王答应了。
But this year passed also, and she threw herself at her father’s feet, and begged so piteously for one more year that the King’s heart was melted, and he yielded to her request, much to the Princess’s joy, for she knew that her real deliverer would appear at the end of the third year. 但这一年也过去了，公主伏倒在父亲的脚边，可怜巴巴地恳求父亲再延迟一年，国王心软了，就答应了她的请求。公主很开心，因为她知道，她真正的救命恩人会在第三年结束的时候出现。
And so the year passed away like the other two, and the wedding-day was fixed, and all the people were prepared to feast and make merry. 这一年像其他两年一样过去了，结婚的日子也定好了，所有人都准备迎接盛大的宴会，并尽情欢乐。
But on the wedding-day it happened that a stranger came to the town with three black dogs. 但是，在举行婚礼那一天，刚好有一个陌生人来到了镇上，还带着三只狗。
He asked what the meaning of all the feasting and fuss was, and they told him that the King’s daughter was just going to be married to the man who had slain the terrible dragon. 他询问为什么会有这样盛大的宴席和忙乱的景象，人们告诉他，那是因为国王的女儿就要嫁给那个杀死可怕巨龙的勇士了。
The stranger at once denounced the coachman as a liar; but no one would listen to him, and he was seized and thrown into a cell with iron doors. 陌生人立刻揭发了车夫，说他是个骗子，但没有人听他的，他被抓了起来，关进了一间铁门紧闭的牢房里。
While he was lying on his straw pallet, pondering mournfully on his fate, he thought he heard the low whining of his dogs outside; then an idea dawned on him, and he called out as loudly as he could, ‘Mustard, come to my help,’ and in a second he saw the paws of his biggest dog at the window of his cell, and before he could count two the creature had bitten through the iron bars and stood beside him. 他躺在简陋的稻草床铺上，想着自己的命运，感到十分难过。这时，他感觉似乎听到他的狗在外面低声呜呜叫。牧羊人灵机一动，用尽全身力气大声喊道：“芥末，来帮帮我”，然后，他立刻就看到，最大的那只狗的爪子趴在他的牢房的窗子上，他还没反应过来，那只狗就咬穿了铁栏，站在了他身边。
Then they both let themselves out of the prison by the window, and the poor youth was free once more, though he felt very sad when he thought that another was to enjoy the reward that rightfully belonged to him. 他们从窗子逃出了牢房，这个可怜的年轻人又自由了，但是，想到另一个人将得到本该属于他的奖赏时，他就感到非常伤心。
He felt hungry too, so he called his dog ‘Salt,’ and asked him to bring home some food. 这时，他也饿了，于是，他喊了一声“盐”，让那只狗拿些吃的来。
The faithful creature trotted off, and soon returned with a table-napkin full of the most delicious food, and the napkin itself was embroidered with a kingly crown. 这只忠实的狗立刻离开了，很快就带回来满满一大包用餐巾包着的美味佳肴，餐巾上绣着国王的皇冠的图案。
The King had just seated himself at the wedding-feast with all his Court, when the dog appeared and licked the Princess’s hand in an appealing manner. 国王刚和他的大臣们在婚宴前就座，这时，突然出现了一条狗，恳求般地舔了舔公主的手。
With a joyful start she recognised the beast, and bound her own table-napkin round his neck. 公主吓了一跳，但很开心，因为她认出了这条狗。然后，公主把自己的餐巾系在了狗的脖子上。
Then she plucked up her courage and told her father the whole story. 她鼓起勇气，把一切都告诉了她的父亲。
The King at once sent a servant to follow the dog, and in a short time the stranger was led into the Kings presence. 国王立刻派了一名仆从去跟着狗，很快，牧羊人就被带到了国王面前。
The former coachman grew as white as a sheet when he saw the shepherd, and, falling on his knees, begged for mercy and pardon. 那个马车夫看到牧羊人时，脸变得像纸一样白，他跪倒在地上，乞求仁慈和原谅。
The Princess recognized her deliverer at once, and did not need the proof of the two dragon’s teeth which he drew from his pocket. 公主立刻认出了她的救命恩人，根本不用牧羊人拿口袋里的两颗龙的牙齿来证明。
The coachman was thrown into a dark dungeon, and the shepherd took his place at the Princess’s side, and this time, you may be sure, she did not beg for the wedding to be put off. 车夫被关入一间阴暗的地牢，而牧羊人取代了他在公主身边的位置，这一次我们可以肯定，公主并没有再请求国王推迟婚礼。
The young couple lived for some time in great peace and happiness, when suddenly one day the former shepherd bethought himself of his poor sister and expressed a wish to see her again, and to let her share in his good fortune. 这对年轻的夫妇平静快乐地生活了一段时间，突然有一天，牧羊人想起了自己可怜的姐姐，想要见到她，并和她分享自己的好运。
So they sent a carriage to fetch her, and soon she arrived at the court, and found herself once more in her brother’s arms. 因此，他们派了一辆马车去接她，很快，她就到了皇宫里，并再一次和她弟弟团聚了。
Then one of the dogs spoke and said, ‘Our task is done; you have no more need of us. 这时，其中一只狗说：“我们的任务完成了，你不再需要我们了。
We only waited to see that you did not forget your sister in your prosperity.’ 我们只是等着看你在荣华富贵的时候还能不能记得你的姐姐。”
And with these words the three dogs became three birds and flew away into the heavens. 说完这些话，三只狗就变成三只小鸟，飞向了天空。
