法官逢“英勇少妇” The Judge's "Spirited Woman"
作者：马克·吐温 by Mark Twain
“现在，我坐在这里,”法官说，“在这个古老的讲道台上，开庭审理，我们现在审判的是一名身材高大、长相凶恶的西班牙暴徒，他杀死了一位聪明、美丽的墨西哥女人的丈夫。那是一个懒洋洋的夏日，并且十分地漫长，证人们都觉得沉闷不堪。对于这次审判，我们都没有兴趣，不过那个紧张、心神不安的墨西哥女恶魔除外，因为你知道他们夫妇是如何去爱的，又是如何去恨的，她过去倾尽全力地爱她丈夫，现在这些爱都已变成了憎恨，她站在这里，双眼满是愤恨地瞪着眼前这个西班牙人。我跟你说，她那如夏日闪电的眼神，偶尔都能搅得我心神不宁。嗯，我脱下外套，踮起脚后跟，懒洋洋地倚靠着，浑身冒着汗，还抽着一支卷心菜雪茄，在那个时代，旧金山人认为那已经是很好的雪茄了；律师们也脱下了外套，抽着烟，觉得烦躁不堪，证人们也是一样，就连那个被告也是如此。说起来，实际上，没有人对这场谋杀审判感兴趣，因为被告总是以“无罪”的名义带上庭，陪审团期望有一天这人也能这样对待他们；并且，尽管所有的证据都直指那个西班牙人，但我们知道，我们要判他有罪，就难免会看似过于专横，而且我们还得顾及社区里的各位先生们；因为当时既没有四轮马车，也没有车马出租行，所以唯一“时兴”的，就是给自己留一块私人墓地。但是，那个女人似乎已经下定决心要让西班牙人处以绞刑；你们真应该来看看，她当时怒气冲冲地瞪了被告一分钟，然后又带着怜悯恳求的眼神望向我，接着她又转过头去，用五分钟的时间搜索陪审团的一张张面孔，之后，她很快地低下头，双手捂了一小会儿脸，好像做好了准备要放弃似的；可是，她立即又抬起头来，看上去还是跟以前一样精力充沛、焦虑不安。但是在陪审团宣告判决——无罪——而我对那名犯人说，他被宣判无罪、可以离开时，那个女人站了起来，直到她看着像是一艘装载着七十四门大炮的舰艇一样高大雄伟时，她说： “I was sitting here,” said the judge, “in this old pulpit, holding court, and we were trying a big, wicked-looking Spanish desperado for killing the husband of a bright, pretty Mexican woman.It was a lazy summer day, and an awfully long one, and the witnesses were tedious.None of us took any interest in the trial except that nervous, uneasy devil of a Mexican woman because you know how they love and how they hate, and this one had loved her husband with all her might, and now she had boiled it all down into hate, and stood here spitting it at that Spaniard with her eyes; and I tell you she would stir me up, too, with a little of her summer lightning, occasionally.Well, I had my coat off and my heels up, lolling and sweating, and smoking one of those cabbage cigars the San Francisco people used to think were good enough for us in those times; and the lawyers they all had their coats off, and were smoking and whittling, and the witnesses the same, and so was the prisoner.Well, the fact is, there warn’t any interest in a murder trial then, because the fellow was always brought in ‘not guilty,’ the jury expecting him to do as much for them some time; and, although the evidence was straight and square against this Spaniard, we knew we could not convict him without seeming to be rather high-handed and sort of reflecting on every gentleman in the community; for there warn’t any carriages and liveries then, and so the only ‘style’ there was, was to keep your private graveyard.But that woman seemed to have her heart set on hanging that Spaniard; and you’d ought to have seen how she would glare on him a minute, and then look up at me in her pleading way, and then turn and for the next five minutes search the jury’s faces, and by and by drop her face in her hands for just a little while as if she was most ready to give up; but out she’d come again directly, and be as live and anxious as ever.But when the jury announced the verdict—Not Guilty—and I told the prisoner he was acquitted and free to go, that woman rose up till she appeared to be as tall and grand as a seventy-four-gun ship, and says she:
“‘法官，我是否能这么理解您，这个男人在我跟年幼的孩子眼前，无缘由地杀害了我丈夫，是无罪的？对这个人的判决，就是司法和法律所能做的一切了？’ “‘Judge, do I understand you to say that this man is not guilty that murdered my husband without any cause before my own eyes and my little children’s, and that all has been done to him that ever justice and the law can do?’
“‘确实。’我回答。 “‘The same,’ says I.
“你猜她之后做了什么？啊，她像野猫一样，转向那个在傻笑的西班牙蠢货，掏出一把‘海军左轮手枪’，公开地在法庭上把他打死了！” “And then what do you reckon she did?Why, she turned on that smirking Spanish fool like a wildcat, and out with a ‘navy’ and shot him dead in open court!”
“真是有气魄，我不得不承认。” “That was spirited, I am willing to admit.”
“然而，不是吗？”法官钦佩不已地说道。 “Wasn’t it, though?” said the judge admiringly.
“不管为了什么，我都不想错过那一幕。我当场宣布休庭，然后我们穿上外套，走出法庭，开始为那个女人和她的孩子募捐，最后把她们送过山去她们的朋友那里了。啊，她真是个英勇的少妇！” “I wouldn’t have missed it for anything.I adjourned court right on the spot, and we put on our coats and went out and took up a collection for her and her cubs, and sent them over the mountains to their friends.Ah, she was a spirited wench!”
