黄狗自传 Memoirs of a Yellow Dog
作者：欧·亨利 by O. Henry
我想，读一篇动物写的文章不会将你们人类击败吧。吉卜林先生和其他很多人已经证实这样的事实：动物可以用英语来表达自己，并且可以得到回报。现在，任何一份杂志，除了那些仍然刊登着布赖恩和蒙·佩利的恐怖画的老式月刊，如果没有一篇动物故事在里面，是不会刊印的。 I don’t suppose it will knock any of you people off your perch to read a contribution from an animal.Mr. Kipling and a good many others have demonstrated the fact that animals can express themselves in remunerative English, and no magazine goes to press nowadays without an animal story in it, except the old-style monthlies that are still running pictures of Bryan and the Mont Pélee horror.
但是，在我的文章里，你不需要寻找那些丛林小说谈到的比如熊卵、熊、蛇卵、蛇、坦慕卵和老虎之类的自命不凡的文学。一只在纽约一间廉价的公寓里度过了它大半生的黄狗，蜷在角落里，睡在一件旧的棉缎衬裙上（就是在郎肖曼夫人的宴会上她把波尔图葡萄酒洒在上面的那件），别期望它会用言语去欺骗人。 But you needn’t look for any stuck-up literature in my piece, such as Bearoo, the bear, and Snakoo, the snake, and Tammanoo, the tiger, talk in the jungle books.A yellow dog that’s spent most of his life in a cheap New York flat, sleeping in a corner on an old sateen underskirt (the one she spilled port wine on at the Lady Longshoremen’s banquet), mustn’t be expected to perform any tricks with the art of speech.
我生下来就是一只小黄狗；出生日期、地点、血统和重量不明。我可以回忆起的第一件事情是，在百老汇和第二十三街的街道口，一个老太太把我装在篮子里，设法把我卖给一个肥胖的女人。老妈妈哈伯德吹捧我，说我的叫声可以打败乐队，是一只纯正的博美——汉布东尼安——红毛爱尔兰——交趾支那——贪吃的——波吉斯猎狐犬。这个胖女人在那个装着一堆罗缎棉织法兰绒样品的购物袋里，试图找一张五元钞票，直到把它翻个底朝天，她才放弃。从那刻起，我成了一只宠物——妈妈的心肝宝贝。哎，和善的读者，你是否曾经有过这种经历：一个200磅重的女人，呼气时带有一种卡门贝奶酪和西班牙起绒织物的味道，她把你抱起，用她的鼻子在你身上来回拱，还不停地用埃玛·埃姆斯的腔调说：“哦，oo’s um oodlun，doodlum，woodlum， toodlum，bitsy-witsy skoodlums（女人哄爱犬时发出的类似唱歌的声音）？” I was born a yellow pup; date, locality, pedigree and weight unknown.The first thing I can recollect, an old woman had me in a basket at Broadway and Twenty-third trying to sell me to a fat lady.Old Mother Hubbard was boosting me to beat the band as a genuine Pomeranian-Hambletonian-Red-Irish-Cochinchina-Stoke-Pogis fox terrier.The fat lady chased a V around among the samples of gros grain flannelette in her shopping bag till she cornered it, and gave up.From that moment I was a pet—a mamma’s own wootsey squidlums.Say, gentle reader, did you ever have a 200-pound woman breathing a flavour of Camembert cheese and Peau d’Espagne pick you up and wallop her nose all over you, remarking all the time in an Emma Eames tone of voice: “Oh, oo’s um oodlum, doodlum, woodlum, toodlum, bitsy-witsy skoodlums?”
从一个有来历的小黄狗，我长成了一只无名的黄色杂种狗，看起来像是安哥拉猫和一盒柠檬交配出来的杂种。但是我的女主人从来没有意识到。她认为那两只被诺亚追赶到方舟上的远古小狗只是我祖先的一个旁系分支。两个警察阻止她，才没让她把我带进麦迪逊广场花园参加西伯利亚猎犬大奖赛。 From a pedigreed yellow pup I grew up to be an anonymous yellow cur looking like a cross between an Angora cat and a box of lemons.But my mistress never tumbled.She thought that the two primeval pups that Noah chased into the ark were but a collateral branch of my ancestors.It took two policemen to keep her from entering me at the Madison Square Garden for the Siberian bloodhound prize.
我说一下我住的那套房子吧。这个房子是纽约市的普通房，门廊里铺着帕罗斯大理石，底楼上铺着鹅卵石。我们的房子登三阶才能上去——但是，不是楼梯。我的女主人把它租了出去，没有什么家具设备，放置了一些常见的东西——客厅里有一套1903年的古式的、加了布面的家具，一幅彩色石印画，画面中有几个日本艺妓呆在一家哈莱姆茶室，有一棵橡胶树和一个丈夫。 I’ll tell you about that flat.The house was the ordinary thing in New York, paved with Parian marble in the entrance hall and cobblestones above the first floor.Our flat was three—well, not flights—climbs up.My mistress rented it unfurnished, and put in the regular things—1903 antique upholstered parlour set, oil chromo of geishas in a Harlem tea house, rubber plant and husband.
天哪！还有一个两足动物，我为他感到难过。他个头不高，淡棕色头发，胡须很像我身上的毛发。怕老婆啊？——哎，巨嘴鸟、火烈鸟和鹈鹕鸟都在他身上啄来啄去。他擦干盘子，听我的女主人唠叨着二楼穿着松鼠皮大衣的女人把那些便宜的、破旧的衣服晾在她的绳子上的事情。每个晚上，当她做晚饭的时候，她都让他用绳子牵着我出去溜溜。 By Sirius!there was a biped I felt sorry for.He was a little man with sandy hair and whiskers a good deal like mine.Henpecked?—well, toucans and flamingoes and pelicans all had their bills in him.He wiped the dishes and listened to my mistress tell about the cheap, ragged things the lady with the squirrel-skin coat on the second floor hung out on her line to dry.And every evening while she was getting supper she made him take me out on the end of a string for a walk.
如果男人知道当女人们孤单的时候是怎样消磨时间的话，他们就永远不会结婚了。劳拉·利恩·吉比，她脖子上沾着点花生糖和一点杏仁酪，碟子没洗，和卖冰的聊半个小时，翻看一包以前的信件，吃几块泡菜，吃两瓶麦芽膏，透过遮光帘上的洞，目光穿过通风井，偷看对面的那套房子——就这么多了。在他下班回来前20分钟，她清理一下房屋，戴好她的发垫以免露馅，拿出一大堆针线活，忙活十分钟，欺骗她的丈夫。 If men knew how women pass the time when they are alone they’d never marry.Laura Lean Jibbey, peanut brittle, a little almond cream on the neck muscles, dishes unwashed, half an hour’s talk with the iceman, reading a package of old letters, a couple of pickles and two bottles of malt extract, one hour peeking through a hole in the window shade into the flat across the air-shaft—that’s about all there is to it.Twenty minutes before time for him to come home from work she straightens up the house, fixes her rat so it won’t show, and gets out a lot of sewing for a ten-minute bluff.
我在那个房子里过着狗的生活。大部分时间我都躺在那个属于我的角落，看着那个胖女人消磨时间。我有时候睡觉，也幻想着正如狗所期望的，跑出去追逐猫到地下室，朝着戴黑色连指手套的老妇人们叫几声。然后她就猛地抓住我，愚蠢地说一大堆奉承狮子狗的话，接着亲亲我的鼻子——但是我能做什么呢？狗又不能咀嚼丁香叶。 I led a dog’s life in that flat.Most all day I lay there in my corner watching that fat woman kill time.I slept sometimes and had pipe dreams about being out chasing cats into basements and growling at old ladies with black mittens, as a dog was intended to do.Then she would pounce upon me with a lot of that drivelling poodle palaver and kiss me on the nose—but what could I do?A dog can’t chew cloves.
我开始为哈比感到难过。如果我不能追赶猫，它就得去。我们看起来如此相像，以至于当我们出去的时候，人们就会注意到这点；因此，我们常常跑出摩根的出租马车经过的街道，开始到那些穷人居住区的街道上爬去年十二月留下的雪堆。 I began to feel sorry for Hubby, dog my cats if I didn’t.We looked so much alike that people noticed it when we went out; so we shook the streets that Morgan’s cab drives down, and took to climbing the piles of last December’s snow on the streets where cheap people live.
一天晚上，当我们这样散步的时候，我设法使自己看起来像是一只得了奖的圣伯纳狗，这个老头努力使自己看起来不像是可能谋杀第一个在街头弹奏门德尔松的《婚礼进行曲》的手风琴演奏者，我抬头看着他，用自己的语言说： One evening when we were thus promenading, and I was trying to look like a prize St. Bernard, and the old man was trying to look like he wouldn’t have murdered the first organ-grinder he heard play Mendelssohn’s wedding-march, I looked up at him and said, in my way:
“为什么你看起来愁眉不展的，你这个草包傻瓜？她不会亲你。你不必要坐在她的大腿上听她说话，那些话可以让一本歌舞喜剧听起来像爱比克泰德的格言。你应该感到欣慰的是，你不是只狗。打起精神，本尼迪克，命令那些沮丧走开。” “What are you looking so sour about, you oakum trimmed lobster?She don’t kiss you.You don’t have to sit on her lap and listen to talk that would make the book of a musical comedy sound like the maxims of Epictetus.You ought to be thankful you’re not a dog.Brace up, Benedick, and bid the blues begone.”
饱受婚姻之苦的老头俯看一下我，脸上几乎露出了犬类的智慧。 The matrimonial mishap looked down at me with almost canine intelligence in his face.
“喂，小狗，”他说，“乖狗狗。你看起来几乎像是你会说话一样。说什么啊，小狗——猫？” “Why, doggie,” says he, “good doggie.You almost look like you could speak.What is it, doggie—Cats?”
猫！能说话！ Cats!Could speak!
但是，当然，他不会理解。人类否认了动物的语言能力。狗和人在一起唯一能交流的共同点是在小说里。 But, of course, he couldn’t understand.Humans were denied the speech of animals.The only common ground of communication upon which dogs and men can get together is in fiction.
大厅对面的房子住着一个女人，养了一只黑毛褐纹狗。每天晚上她丈夫都用绳子牵着它，带它出来，但是他总是很高兴地吹着口哨回家。一天我和黑毛褐纹狗在大厅碰了碰鼻子，我撞了一下它，让它解释一下。 In the flat across the hall from us lived a lady with a black-and-tan terrier.Her husband strung it and took it out every evening, but he always came home cheerful and whistling.One day I touched noses with the black-and-tan in the hall, and I struck him for an elucidation.
“看这儿，欢欢，”我说，“你知道在公共场合当狗的保姆可不是一个真正男人的本性啊。我从来没有见过哪个被狗牵绊着的人，看起来不像是乐意舔一下别的看他一眼的人。但是你的老板每天进来都是得意洋洋的，像是一个业余魔术师，正在玩变鸡蛋的戏法。他是怎么做到的？别告诉我他喜欢这样。” “See, here, Wiggle-and-Skip,” I says, “you know that it ain’t the nature of a real man to play dry nurse to a dog in public.I never saw one leashed to a bow-wow yet that didn’t look like he’d like to lick every other man that looked at him.But your boss comes in every day as perky and set up as an amateur prestidigitator doing the egg trick.How does he do it?Don’t tell me he likes it.”
“他？”这只黑毛褐纹狗说，“哦，他用了自然疗伤法。他总是略带醉意。最初当我们出去的时候，他害羞得就像轮船上那个玩牌的人。每当他们累积一大笔赌注的时候，他都宁愿出佩德罗纸牌游戏中的五张王牌。那个时候我们已经去了八个酒吧，他不在乎绳子的另一端是只狗还是条鲶鱼。为了躲避那些摇晃的门，我的尾巴已经少了两英寸了。” “Him?” says the black-and-tan.“Why, he uses Nature’s Own Remedy.He gets spifflicated.At first when we go out he’s as shy as the man on the steamer who would rather play pedro when they make ’em all jackpots.By the time we’ve been in eight saloons he don’t care whether the thing on the end of his line is a dog or a catfish.I’ve lost two inches of my tail trying to sidestep those swinging doors.”
黑毛褐纹狗的话让我得到了启示——沃德维尔请记下来——我开始思考。 The pointer I got from that terrier—Vaudeville please copy—set me to thinking.
一天晚上，大约六点钟，我的女主人命令他忙起来，带“宝贝”出去呼吸新鲜空气。我一直隐瞒到现在，但是她就是这样叫我的。那只黑毛褐纹狗叫“吱吱”。我认为，就追兔子来说，我比黑毛褐纹狗有优势。“宝贝”还算是一个能让人感到自尊的命名吧。 One evening about 6 o’clock my mistress ordered him to get busy and do the ozone act for Lovey.I have concealed it until now, but that is what she called me.The black-and-tan was called “Tweetness.”I consider that I have the bulge on him as far as you could chase a rabbit.Still “Lovey” is something of a nomenclatural tin can on the tail of one’s self respect.
在一条安全的街道上，有个僻静的地方，在一个吸引人的、优雅的酒吧门前，我绷紧了看管我的人手中的绳子。我直向前地冲向门，就像一只狗在新闻电讯中哀声叫唤，让他的家人知道小艾丽斯在小溪边采百合花的时候掉进泥沼里了。 At a quiet place on a safe street I tightened the line of my custodian in front of an attractive, refined saloon.I made a dead-ahead scramble for the doors, whining like a dog in the press despatches that lets the family know that little Alice is bogged while gathering lilies in the brook.
“哎呀，可恶的眼睛，”老人咧着嘴笑着说，“可恶的眼睛，我那个用柠檬汁染过毛发的黄毛儿子不正是要我进去喝一杯嘛。让我看看——自从我把鞋皮保存在歇脚板上以来有多长时间了？我想我要——” “Why, darn my eyes,” says the old man, with a grin; “darn my eyes if the saffron-coloured son of a seltzer lemonade ain’t asking me in to take a drink.Lemme see—how long’s it been since I saved shoe leather by keeping one foot on the foot-rest?I believe I’ll—”
我知道老头中了我的套了。坐在一张台前，他喝着热的苏格兰威士忌。一个小时内，他不停地让服务生上坎贝尔葡萄酒。我坐在他旁边，边用尾巴拍打着服务员，边吃着免费午餐，例如，在爸爸回家前八分钟，妈妈在家里，用在熟食店买来的东西在家做的饭是无法与之相比的。 I knew I had him.Hot Scotches he took, sitting at a table.For an hour he kept the Campbells coming.I sat by his side rapping for the waiter with my tail, and eating free lunch such as mamma in her flat never equalled with her homemade truck bought at a delicatessen store eight minutes before papa comes home.
当苏格兰的产品全喝光了，只剩黑麦面包的时候，老人将我从桌腿那儿解开，在外面耍弄我，就像钓鱼者耍弄一条大麻哈鱼一样。在那儿，他拿掉我的领圈，把它扔向街道。 When the products of Scotland were all exhausted except the rye bread the old man unwound me from the table leg and played me outside like a fisherman plays a salmon.Out there he took off my collar and threw it into the street.
“可怜的小狗，”他说，“乖狗狗，她将再不会啃你啦。这真是一件该死的羞耻的事情。乖狗狗，走吧，让街道上的车把你碾死吧，高兴点。” “Poor doggie,” says he; “good doggie.She shan’t kiss you any more.’S a darned shame.Good doggie, go away and get run over by a street car and be happy.”
我拒绝离开。我在老头的腿边跳着蹦着，快乐得就像一只哈巴狗在地毯上撒欢一样。 I refused to leave.I leaped and frisked around the old man’s legs happy as a pug on a rug.
“你这个满头虱子抓土拨鼠的老头，”我对他说——“你这只叫着去追月亮、追野兔、偷鸡蛋的老猎犬，难道你看不出来我不想离开你吗？难道你看不出我们两个都是森林里的小狗、女主人是那个拿着擦碗布追着你打，给我擦防虱油并在我尾巴上系个粉色蝴蝶结的残酷的家长吗？为什么不抛开一切，永远和我为伴？” “You old flea-headed woodchuck-chaser,” I said to him—“you moon-baying, rabbit-pointing, egg-stealing old beagle, can’t you see that I don’t want to leave you?Can’t you see that we’re both Pups in the Wood and the missis is the cruel uncle after you with the dish towel and me with the flea liniment and a pink bow to tie on my tail.Why not cut that all out and be pards forever more?”
也许你会说他不理解——也许不理解。但是他确实紧抓着热苏格兰威士忌，静止不动地站了一分钟，思考着。 Maybe you’ll say he didn’t understand—maybe he didn’t.But he kind of got a grip on the Hot Scotches, and stood still for a minute, thinking.
“小狗，”最后他说，“在这个世上我们不可能有十几条命活，我们中间很少有人能活到300多岁。如果我再看到那套房子，我就是一个傻子，如果你看到，你比我更傻；这可不是奉承。我出价60比1，打赌我们向西跑，我能赢你一只腊肠犬的距离。” “Doggie,” says he, finally, “we don’t live more than a dozen lives on this earth, and very few of us live to be more than 300.If I ever see that flat any more I’m a flat, and if you do you’re flatter; and that’s no flattery.I’m offering 60 to 1 that Westward Ho wins out by the length of a dachshund.”
没有绳子牵着我，但是我陪同我的主人一直嬉戏，来到了二十三街道的渡口。沿途的猫们应该感谢我那四只能捕捉的爪子没有伸向它们。 There was no string, but I frolicked along with my master to the Twenty-third street ferry.And the cats on the route saw reason to give thanks that prehensile claws had been given them.
在泽西岛的一边，我的主人对一个站着吃葡萄干小面包的陌生人说： On the Jersey side my master said to a stranger who stood eating a currant bun:
“我和我的狗，我们要去落基山。” “Me and my doggie, we are bound for the Rocky Mountains.”
但是让我最高兴的，是老头拉我的耳朵直到我叫起来的时候，那时他说： But what pleased me most was when my old man pulled both of my ears until I howled, and said:
“你这只普通的、顽皮的、长着老鼠尾巴的、黄色的小受气包，你知道我会叫你什么吗？” “You common, monkey-headed, rat-tailed, sulphur-coloured son of a door mat, do you know what I’m going to call you?”
我想到了“宝贝”，因此我悲哀地叫着。 I thought of “Lovey,” and I whined dolefully.
“我将叫你‘皮特’。”我的主人说。就算我长有五条尾巴，在这种时刻，那也是不够我摇的。 “I’m going to call you ‘Pete,’” says my master; and if I’d had five tails I couldn’t have done enough wagging to do justice to the occasion.
