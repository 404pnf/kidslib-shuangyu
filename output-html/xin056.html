The Husband of the Rat’s Daughter 老鼠嫁女
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
Once upon a time there lived in Japan a rat and his wife who came of an old and noble race, and had one daughter, the loveliest girl in all the rat world. 从前，在日本住着一对老鼠夫妇，妻子有着古老且高贵的血统。他们有个女儿，是老鼠国里最可爱的女老鼠。
Her parents were very proud of her, and spared no pains to teach her all she ought to know. 老鼠夫妇很为女儿感到骄傲，竭尽全力地教会女儿应该掌握的所有知识。
There was not another young lady in the whole town who was as clever as she was in gnawing through the hardest wood, or who could drop from such a height on to a bed, or run away so fast if anyone was heard coming. 在整个鼠国里，没有第二个年轻女鼠像他们的女儿一样，聪明得能啃穿最硬的木头，能从很高的地方跳到床上，听到有人来了能飞快地逃跑。
Great attention, too, was paid to her personal appearance, and her skin shone like satin, while her teeth were as white as pearls, and beautifully pointed. 她的长相也很有吸引力：皮肤像缎子那样闪闪发亮，牙齿不但如珍珠一般洁白，而且还很锋利。
Of course, with all these advantages, her parents expected her to make a brilliant marriage, and, as she grew up, they began to look round for a suitable husband. 女儿有这么多优点，父母当然期望她能得个好姻缘。随着她慢慢长大，老鼠夫妇开始到处给女儿寻找能配得上她的丈夫。
But here a difficulty arose. 但是难题来了。
The father was a rat from the tip of his nose to the end of his tail, outside as well as in, and desired that his daughter should wed among her own people. 鼠爸爸是一只地地道道的老鼠，于是他希望自己的女儿跟老鼠结婚。
She had no lack of lovers, but her father’s secret hopes rested on a fine young rat, with moustaches which almost swept the ground, whose family was still nobler and more ancient than his own. 女儿不缺追求者，但是鼠爸爸私下里看中了一位优秀的年轻老鼠，他长着几乎能拖到地上的胡子，家族也更古老、更高贵。
Unluckily, the mother had other views for her precious child. 不幸的是，鼠妈妈对宝贝女儿的婚事另有打算。
She was one of those people who always despise their own family and surroundings, and take pleasure in thinking that they themselves are made of finer material than the rest of the world. 她与一些人的想法一样，这种人总瞧不起自己的出身和周围的环境，喜欢把自己想得比世上其他任何人都出色。
‘HER daughter should never marry a mere rat,’she declared, holding her head high.‘With her beauty and talents she had a right to look for someone a little better than THAT.’ So she talked, as mothers will, to anyone that would listen to her. “我们女儿不会嫁给一只微不足道的老鼠，”她昂着头宣布，“她那么美丽，那么有才华，所以她有资格找比老鼠更好的丈夫。”作为妈妈，她跟任何听众都是这么说的。
What the girl thought about the matter nobody knew or cared—it was not the fashion in the rat world. 没人知道或者在意老鼠女儿对婚事的想法——鼠国里也不兴这个。
Many were the quarrels which the old rat and his wife had upon the subject, and sometimes they bore on their faces certain marks which looked as if they had not kept to words only. 老鼠夫妇为这件事吵了好多次，有时他们脸上还有一道道明显的伤痕，看起来他们好像还为此事打过架。
‘Reach up to the stars is MY motto,’ cried the lady one day, when she was in a greater passion than usual. ‘My daughter’s beauty places her higher that anything upon earth’, she cried; ‘and I am certainly not going to accept a son-in-law who is beneath her.’ “我的宗旨是把女儿嫁给星星，”一天鼠妈妈这样叫起来，她比以往更激动，“我女儿比地球上的所有事物都要美，我当然不可能接受比她差的女婿。”
‘Better offer her in marriage to the sun,’ answered her husband impatiently, “As far as I know there is nothing greater than he.” “那最好把她嫁给太阳，”鼠爸爸不耐烦地说，“就我所知，他是最伟大的。”
‘Well, I WAS thinking of it,’ replied the wife, ‘and as you are of the same mind, we will pay him a visit to-morrow.’ “好吧，我确实也考虑过，”鼠妈妈说，“既然你我想法一样，我们明天去拜访他吧。”
So the next morning, the two rats, having spent hours in making themselves smart, set out to see the sun, leading their daughter between them. 于是，第二天早上，老鼠夫妇先是花了好长时间把自己打扮得时髦以后，才出发去见太阳。女儿走在他们中间，夫妇俩牵着她。
The journey took some time, but at length they came to the golden palace where the sun lived. 走了一段时间，最终，他们来到太阳住的黄金宫殿。
‘Noble king,’ began the mother, ‘behold our daughter! “尊贵的王，”鼠妈妈首先说，“请看看我们的女儿！
She is so beautiful that she is above everything in the whole world. 她是如此漂亮，超过了世间一切。
Naturally, we wish for a son-in-law who, on his side, is greater than all. 自然，我们也希望女婿是世界上最伟大的。
Therefore we have come to you.’ 所以我们来找你了。”
‘I feel very much flattered,’ replied the sun, who was so busy that he had not the least wish to marry anybody, ‘you do me great honour by your proposal. “我真是受宠若惊，”太阳说，他很忙，一点儿也不想结婚，“你来求婚是我极大的荣耀。
Only, in one point you are mistaken, and it would be wrong of me to take advantage of your ignorance. 但是，有一点您错了，如果我利用您的失误，则是我的不对。
There is something greater than I am, and that is the cloud. 还有比我更伟大的，那就是云。
Look!’ 看！”
And as he spoke a cloud spread itself over the sun’s face, blotting out his rays. 他正说着话，一片云遮住了太阳的脸，挡住了他的光线。
‘Oh, well, we will speak to the cloud,’ said the mother. “哦，那好吧，我们去跟云说说。”鼠妈妈讲。
And turning to the cloud she repeated her proposal. 接着她转向了云，重复了求婚的事。
‘Indeed I am unworthy of anything so charming,’ answered the cloud; ‘but you make a mistake again in what you say. “事实上，我没这么大的魅力，”云回答，“你这么说又犯了个错。
There is one thing that is even more powerful than I, and that is the wind. 还有比我更强大的，那就是风。
Ah, here he comes, you can see for yourself.’ 啊，他来了，你们自己看。”
And she DID see, for catching up the cloud as he passed, he threw it on the other side of the sky. 她的确看到，风经过的时候，就把云带走了，甩到天空的另一边。
Then, tumbling father, mother and daughter down to the earth again, he paused for a moment beside them, his foot on an old wall. 然后，老鼠一家三口被风卷起，打着滚回到了地面，风在他们身旁停留了一会儿，站到一堵老墙上。
When she had recovered her breath, the mother began her little speech once more. 等她喘过气，鼠妈妈又开始跟风求婚。
‘The wall is the proper husband for your daughter,’ answered the wind, whose home consisted of a cave, which he only visited when he was not rushing about elsewhere; ‘you can see for yourself that he is greater than I, for he has power to stop me in my flight.’ “墙才配得上你女儿，”风说，他的家是个山洞，不四处乱飞的时候才回家，“你自己也看到了，他比我更强大，他能阻止我飞。”
And the mother, who did not trouble to conceal her wishes, turned at once to the wall. 鼠妈妈并不掩饰自己的愿望，立刻向墙求婚。
Then something happened which was quite unexpected by everyone. 这时，发生了所有人都没想到的事。
‘I won’t marry that ugly old wall, which is as old as my grandfather,’ sobbed the girl, who had not uttered one word all this time. I would have married the sun, or the cloud, or the wind, because it was my duty, although I love the handsome young rat, and him only. “我不会嫁给那堵又丑又老的墙，他跟我爷爷一样老，”一直都没讲话的女儿啜泣着说，“我可以嫁给太阳、云彩，或者是风，因为这是我的责任，尽管我爱那位年轻帅老鼠，只爱他。
But that horrid old wall—I would sooner die!’ 但是要我嫁给那堵可怕的老墙——我情愿马上死！”
And the wall, rather hurt in his feelings, declared that he had no claim to be the husband of so beautiful a girl. 墙虽然感到伤心，但还是表明自己无意做这个漂亮女孩的丈夫。
‘It is quite true,’ he said, ‘that I can stop the wind who can part the clouds who can cover the sun; but there is someone who can do more than all these, and that is the rat. “的确，”他说，“云能遮住太阳，风能吹散云，而我能挡住风，但是还有人比我更厉害，那就是老鼠。
It is the rat who passes through me, and can reduce me to powder, simply with his teeth. 老鼠可以啃穿我，仅用牙齿就能让我化为粉末。
If, therefore, you want a son-in-law who is greater than the whole world, seek him among the rats.’ 所以，如果你想找一个世上最强的人当你女婿，就在老鼠中选吧。”
‘Ah, what did I tell you?’ cried the father. “啊，我跟你说什么来着？”鼠爸爸叫道。
And his wife, though for the moment angry at being beaten, soon thought that a rat son-in-law was what she had always desired. 此刻，鼠妈妈虽然很生气败给了鼠爸爸，但是转念一想，老鼠就是她一直希望找到的女婿。
So all three returned happily home, and the wedding was celebrated three days after. 所以，一家三口开心地回了家，三天后举办了婚礼。
