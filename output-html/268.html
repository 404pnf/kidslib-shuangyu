胡桃树 The Walnut-Tree
路旁矗立着一棵胡桃树，树上结满了果实。为了吃这些果实，路人用石头砸、用棍棒打，将胡桃树的树枝折断了。胡桃树哀怨地叫道：“噢，我真不幸啊！我为人们献出了自己的果实，却换来他们的一顿痛打！” A Walnut-Tree standing by the roadside bore an abundant crop of fruit.For the sake of the nuts, the passers-by broke its branches with stones and sticks.The Walnut-Tree piteously exclaimed, “O wretched me! that those whom I cheer with my friut should repay me with these painful requitals!”
蚊子和狮子 The Gnat and the Lion
一只蚊子飞过来，对狮子说：“我一点都不怕你，你并不比我强大。你有什么力量呢？你只会用爪子抓、牙齿咬，像女人吵架一样。我再说一遍，我比你强大得多。如果你不相信，我们就来比试一下，看谁能获胜。”鸣响完号角的蚊子牢牢地趴在狮子身上，然后叮咬他的鼻孔以及脸上没有毛的地方。狮子想要拍死蚊子，就用爪子在身上抓来抓去，最后把自己弄得遍体鳞伤。最终，蚊子战胜了狮子，嗡嗡地哼着凯歌飞走了。可没过多久，蚊子就被一个蜘蛛网粘住，让一只蜘蛛给吃掉了。他深深地哀叹自己的命运：“真是可悲啊！我能成功地战胜最强大的野兽，却被这只最不起眼的昆虫——蜘蛛给消灭了！” A Gnat came and said to a Lion, “I do not in the least fear you, nor are you stronger than I am.For in what does your strength consist?You can scratch with your claws and bite with your teeth as a woman in her quarrels.I repeat that I am altogether more powerful than you; and if you doubt it, let us fight and see who will conquer.”The Gnat, having sounded his horn, fastened himself upon the Lion and stung him on the nostrils and the parts of the face devoid of hair.While trying to crush him, the Lion tore himself with his claws, until he punished himself severely.The Gnat thus prevailed over the Lion, and, buzzing about in a song of triumph, flew away.But shortly afterwards he became entangled in the meshes of a cobweb and was eaten by a spider.He greatly lamented his fate, saying, “Woe is me! that I, who can wage war successfully with the hugest beasts, should perish myself from this spider, the most inconsiderable of insects!”
猴子和海豚 The Monkey and the Dolphin
一个水手要进行一次长途航行，便带了一只猴子上船，以供消遣。当他驶出希腊海岸时，一场猛烈的暴风雨袭来，船沉了。这个水手、他的猴子，以及所有的船员都不得不跳水逃生。一只海豚看到猴子正在与海浪搏斗，以为猴子是人（人们总说海豚是朋友），就游过来把它驮在自己的背上，把它安全地送往岸边。海豚把猴子驮到离雅典不远、能看见陆地的地方时，它问猴子是不是雅典人。猴子回答说自己是雅典人，并且自称是雅典最显贵的一个家族的后裔。海豚又问猴子是否知道比雷埃夫斯（雅典的著名海港）。猴子以为海豚说的是人，就回答说它非常了解他。他是自己的挚友。海豚发现猴子骗它，非常愤怒，于是把猴子沉入水中，将它淹死了。 A sailor, bound on a long voyage, took with him a Monkey to amuse him while on shipboard.As he sailed off the coast of Greece, a violent tempest arose in which the ship was wrecked and he, his Monkey, and all the crew were obliged to swim for their lives.A Dolphin saw the Monkey contending with the waves, and supposing him to be a man (whom he is always said to be friend), came and placed himself under him, to convey him on his back in safety to the shore.When the Dolphin arrived with his burden in sight of land not far from Athens, he asked the Monkey if he were an Athenian.The latter replied that he was, and that he was descended from one of the most noble families in that city.The Dolphin then inquired if he knew the Piraeus (the famous harbor of Athens).Supposing that a man was meant, the Monkey answered that he knew him very well and that he was an intimate friend.The Dolphin, indignant at these falsehoods, dipped the Monkey under the water and drowned him.
寒鸦和鸽子 The Jackdaw and the Doves
一只寒鸦看到鸽棚里有许多食物，便把自己涂白，然后混进鸽群里，以分享鸽子丰饶的食物。只要寒鸦不出声，鸽子就会误以为他是他们中的一员，让他进棚里吃食。可是有一天，寒鸦忘了自己的身份，突然叫出声来。鸽子发现了他的真面目，就用嘴啄他，把他赶了出来。寒鸦没法从鸽子那里得到食物了，便返回到寒鸦群中。寒鸦群看到这只寒鸦的羽毛是白色的，没认出他，便也不让他和他们一起住。寒鸦两头都想要，结果什么都没得到。 A Jackdaw, seeing some Doves in a cote abundantly provided with food, painted himself white and joined them in order to share their plentiful maintenance.The Doves, as long as he was silent, supposed him to be one of themselves and admitted him to their cote.But when one day he forgot himself and began to chatter, they discovered his true character and drove him forth, pecking him with their beaks.Failing to obtain food among the Doves, he returned to the Jackdaws.They too, not recognizing him on account of his color, expelled him from living with them. So desiring two ends, he obtained neither.
马和牡鹿 The Horse and the Stag
从前，马曾经独自占领整个草原。后来，一只牡鹿闯入了马的领地，并分享着它的牧草。马想要报复这个陌生的闯入者，就去问一个人是否可以帮它惩罚牡鹿。人说，只要马愿意套上马嚼子，并同意驮他，他就答应设计有力的武器来对付牡鹿。马答应了人的要求，让他骑了上来。从那一刻起，马才发现自己不但没有成功报复牡鹿，反而让自己变成了人的奴隶。 At one time the Horse had the plain entirely to himself.Then a Stag intruded into his domain and shared his pasture.The Horse, desiring to revenge himself on the stranger, asked a man if he were willing to help him in punishing the Stag.The man replied that if the Horse would receive a bit in his mouth and agree to carry him, he would contrive effective weapons against the Stag.The Horse consented and allowed the man to mount him.From that hour he found that instead of obtaining revenge on the Stag, he had enslaved himself to the service of man.
孩子和狼 The Kid and the Wolf
一个孩子，在从牧场回来的路上，被一匹狼追赶着，无人保护。孩子知道自己逃脱不了，便转过身来对狼说：“狼朋友，我知道我肯定是你的囊中之物了。但是在我死之前，希望你能帮我一个忙，你能为我吹首曲子，让我跳支舞吗？”狼同意了，吹奏起来，孩子也跟着起舞。几只猎狗闻声跑上来，开始追赶狼。狼转过头对孩子说：“我活该得到这样的报应，我明明只是一个屠夫，本来就不该变成一个吹笛者来取悦于你。” A Kid, returning without protection from the pasture, was pursued by a Wolf.Seeing he could not escape, he turned round, and said: “I know, friend Wolf, that I must be your prey, but before I die I would ask of you one favor you will play me a tune to which I may dance.”The Wolf complied, and while he was piping and the Kid was dancing, some hounds hearing the sound ran up and began chasing the Wolf.Turning to the Kid, he said, “It is just what I deserve; for I, who am only a butcher, should not have turned piper to please you.”
狐狸和猴子 The Fox and the Monkey
一只狐狸和一只猴子在路上结伴同行。途中，他们经过一个满是纪念碑的公墓。“你看到的所有这些纪念碑，”猴子说，“都是为纪念我的祖先而建造的。当时，他们可都是享有盛誉的自由民和公民。”狐狸回答说：“你也太会说谎了吧，你的这些祖先当然不能来反驳你啦。” A Fox and a Monkey were traveling together on the same road.As they journeyed, they passed through a cemetery full of monuments.“All these monuments which you see,” said the Monkey, “are erected in honor of my ancestors, who were in their day freedmen and citizens of great renown.”The Fox replied, “You have chosen a most appropriate subject for your falsehoods, as I am sure none of your ancestors will be able to contradict you.”
虚假的故事常常自相矛盾。 A false tale often betrays itself.
预言家 The Prophet
一个术士正坐在集市里给路人算命。这时，一个人匆匆忙忙地跑过来告诉他，说术士家的房门被撬了，东西全被偷走了。术士重重地叹了一口气，以最快的速度跑回家去了。一个邻居见此情形，就说：“噢！你这个家伙！你说你能预知别人的命运，那你怎么没能预见你自己的命运呢？” A wizard, sitting in the marketplace, was telling the fortunes of the passers-by when a person ran up in great haste, and announced to him that the doors of his house had been broken open and that all his goods were being stolen.He sighed heavily and hastened away as fast as he could run.A neighbor saw him running and said, “Oh! you fellow there! you say you can foretell the fortunes of others; how is it you did not foresee your own?”
小偷和看家狗 The Thief and the Housedog
一天夜里，一个小偷潜入一座房子偷东西。为了防止狗叫，惊醒房主，他带了几块肉来喂狗，好让狗安静下来。当小偷朝狗扔肉时，狗说：“如果你认为这样就能堵住我的嘴，那你就大错特错了。你手中这突如其来的好意只会让我更加警觉，免得你给我这些意外的好处是别有用心，为了获得你自己的利益而损害到我的主人。” A Thief came in the night to break into a house.He brought with him several slices of meat in order to pacify the Housedog, so that he would not alarm his master by barking.As the Thief threw him the pieces of meat, the Dog said, “If you think to stop my mouth, you will be greatly mistaken.This sudden kindness at your hands will only make me more watchful, lest under these unexpected favors to myself, you have some private ends to accomplish for your own benefit, and for my master’s injury.”
人、马、牛和狗 The Man, the Horse, the Ox, and the Dog
马、牛和狗受不了严寒的折磨，便向人寻求庇护之所和保护。人亲切地接待了它们，还生了一堆火，让它们取暖。他让马随便吃燕麦，分给牛大量的干草，还把自己饭桌上的肉喂狗。这些动物对此心存感激，于是决定竭尽全力来回报人。为此，它们把人的生命划分为三个时期，并把各自的典型特征分别赋予人的每个生命时期。马选择了人的生命初期，并把自己的品性赋予了这个时期的人。因此，每个人年轻时都很冲动、任性、固执己见。牛负责人生命的下个时期。所以，人在中年时期都很热爱工作，兢兢业业，一心积累财富，且勤俭节约。人的晚年时期留给了狗。因此，老人常常脾气暴躁、易怒、难以取悦、自私，只能容忍自己的家人，但厌恶陌生人，以及所有对他的舒适生活或基本需求不能给予帮助的人。 A Horse, Ox, and Dog, driven to great straits by the cold, sought shelter and protection from Man.He received them kindly, lighted a fire, and warmed them.He let the Horse make free with his oats, gave the Oxan abundance of hay, and fed the Dog with meat from his own table.Grateful for these favors, the animals determined to repay him to the best of their ability.For this purpose, they divided the term of his life between them, and each endowed one portion of it with the qualities which chiefly characterized himself.The Horse chose his earliest years and gave them his own attributes: hence every man is in his youth impetuous, headstrong, and obstinate in maintaining his own opinion.The Ox took under his patronage the next term of life, and therefore man in his middle age is fond of work, devoted to labor, and resolute to amass wealth and to husband his resources.The end of life was reserved for the Dog, wherefore the old man is often snappish, irritable, hard to please, and selfish, tolerant only of his own household, but averse to strangers and to all who do not administer to his comfort or to his necessities.
猿猴和两个旅行者 The Apes and the Two Travelers
有两个人一起旅行，一个总是说实话，而另一个总是说假话。他们偶然间闯入了猿猴的领地。其中一个自称是国王的猿猴命令手下把他们抓起来，带到自己面前。这样，它或许就能了解人类对它的看法了。同时，猴王命令所有的猿猴在它左右各排成长长的一列，并为它安放一个宝座，就像人类的风俗习惯一样。一切准备妥当之后，它示意手下将这两个人带到它面前，并用这样的问候语来跟他们打招呼：“噢，陌生人，在你们看来，我是个什么样的国王？”那个总说谎话的旅行者回答道：“在我看来，您是最强大的国王。”“那你怎么评价我身边的这些手下呢？”“这些，”他回答道，“是您杰出的随从，至少能胜任大使和军队统帅的职位。”猴王和所有朝臣都对这个谎言十分满意，于是下令赏给这位奉承者一份厚礼。见此情形，那个总说真话的旅行者心想：“如果给一个说谎者都这么大的奖赏，要是我依照我的一贯作风说真话，不知道会奖励我怎样的厚礼呢？”猴王立即转向他。“请问，在你看来，我和我身边的这些朋友怎么样呢？”“你是，”他说，“一只最为杰出的猿猴。你的所有随从以你为榜样，也都是优秀的猿猴。”听了这些实话，猴王勃然大怒，把他交由随从的尖牙利爪处置了。 Two men, one who always spoke the truth and the other who told nothing but lies, were traveling together and by chance came to the land of Apes.One of the Apes, who had raised himself to be king, commanded them to be seized and brought before him, that he might know what was said of him among men.He ordered at the same time that all the Apes be arranged in a long row on his right hand and on his left, and that a throne be placed for him, as was the custom among men.After these preparations he signified that the two men should be brought before him, and greeted them with this salutation: “What sort of a king do I seem to you to be, O strangers?”The Lying Traveler replied, “You seem to me a most mighty king.”“And what is your estimate of those you see around me?”“These,” he made answer, “are worthy companions of yourself, fit at least to be ambassadors and leaders of armies.”The Ape and all his court, gratified with the lie, commanded that a handsome present be given to the flatterer.On this the truthful Traveler thought to himself, “If so great a reward be given for a lie, with what gift may not I be rewarded, if, according to my custom, I tell the truth?” The Ape quickly turned to him.“And pray how do I and these my friends around me seem to you?”“Thou art,” he said, “a most excellent Ape, and all these thy companions after thy example are excellent Apes too.”The King of the Apes, enraged at hearing these truths, gave him over to the teeth and claws of his companions.
狼和牧羊人 The Wolf and the Shepherd
一匹狼跟了一群羊很久，却没有试图伤害其中任何一只羊。一开始，牧羊人提防着这只狼，就像严防敌人一样，对狼的一举一动保持严密的监视，。但是日复一日，狼每天和羊为伴，却丝毫没有显露出要猎食羊的意图。于是，牧羊人开始把它看作羊群的守卫者，而不是与羊群为敌的邪恶的阴谋家。一天，他需要进城办事，就将羊群完全交给狼看管。狼既然得到了这个机会，便袭击了羊群，吃掉了大部分羊。当牧羊人回来发现羊群被毁时，他惊叫道：“我真是活该，谁叫我将羊群托付给一匹狼呢？” A Wolf followed a flock of sheep for a long time and did not attempt to injure one of them.The Shepherd at first stood on his guard against him, as against an enemy, and kept a strict watch over his movements.But when the Wolf, day after day, kept in the company of the sheep and did not make the slightest effort to seize them, the Shepherd began to look upon him as a guardian of his flock rather than as a plotter of evil against it; and when occasion called him one day into the city, he left the sheep entirely in his charge.The Wolf, now that he had the opportunity, fell upon the sheep, and destroyed the greater part of the flock.When the Shepherd returned to find his flock destroyed, he exclaimed: “I have been rightly served; why did I trust my sheep to a Wolf?”
野兔和狮子 The Hares and the Lions
野兔在集会上高谈阔论，主张所有动物应该平等。狮子这样回答道：“噢，野兔！你们的话很精彩，但他们既没有利爪，也没有尖牙，而这些我们都有。” The Hares harangued the assembly, and argued that all should be equal.The Lions made this reply: “Your words, O Hares! are good; but they lack both claws and teeth such as we have.”
云雀和小云雀 The Lark and Her Young Ones
初春，一只云雀在嫩绿的小麦上筑了一个巢。等一窝小云雀几乎都已长得羽翼丰满，并且学会了如何利用翅膀和全身的羽毛时，田地的主人来察看他成熟的庄稼，说道：“时机到了，我必须请我所有的邻居来帮忙收庄稼了。”一只小云雀听到他的话后，就去告诉母亲，问母亲它们该搬到什么地方去才安全。“还不到搬家的时候呢，我的儿子，”它母亲回答道，“那个人只是去邀请他的朋友来帮忙收割，说明他不是很认真。”几天后，田地的主人又来了，看见麦粒正从熟透了的麦穗上往下掉。他说：“明天我要亲自和劳工们一起来，还要尽量多租用一些收割机，我要开始收割庄稼了。”云雀听到这些话，便对它的孩子们说：“现在是离开的时候了，我的小家伙们，因为这个人这次是认真的，他不再委托他的朋友们，而是要亲自收割庄稼了。” A Lark had made her nest in the early spring on the young green wheat.The brood had almost grown to their full strength and attained the use of their wings and the full plumage of their feathers, when the owner of the field, looking over his ripe crop, said, “The time has come when I must ask all my neighbors to help me with my harvest.”One of the young Larks heard his speech and related it to his mother, inquiring of her to what place they should move for safety.“There is no occasion to move yet, my son,” she replied; “the man who only sends to his friends to help him with his harvest is not really in earnest.”The owner of the field came again a few days later and saw the wheat shedding the grain from excess of ripeness.He said, “I will come myself tomorrow with my laborers, and with as many reapers as I can hire, and will get in the harvest.”The Lark on hearing these words said to her brood, “It is time now to be off, my little ones, for the man is in earnest this time; he no longer trusts his friends, but will reap the field himself.”
自助是最好的帮助。 Self-help is the best help.
狐狸和狮子 The Fox and the Lion
有一只狐狸之前从未见过狮子。当他第一次在森林里偶遇狮子时，他非常恐惧，差点吓死了。第二次见到狮子时，狐狸仍然非常惊慌，但是没有第一次那样害怕了。等第三次见到狮子，狐狸胆量大增，竟然走上前去，开始和狮子闲谈了起来。 When a Fox who had never yet seen a Lion, fell in with him by chance for the first time in the forest, he was so frightened that he nearly died with fear.On meeting him for the second time, he was still much alarmed, but not to the same extent as at first.On seeing him the third time, he so increased in boldness that he went up to him and commenced a familiar conversation with him.
熟悉可以淡化偏见。 Acquaintance softens prejudices.
黄鼠狼和老鼠 The Weasel and the Mice
一只黄鼠狼，因年老体衰而行动缓慢，不能像从前那样抓老鼠了。于是，他在自己身上裹了一层面粉，然后躺在一个阴暗的角落里。有只老鼠以为那是食物，便跳到黄鼠狼身上，结果立即就被黄鼠狼抓住，被捏死了。另一只老鼠也以同样的方式死掉了，接着是第三只，随后还有很多只步其后尘。一只曾经逃脱过许多陷阱和圈套的年迈的老鼠，从安全的距离处观察着这个狡猾敌人的把戏，说道：“哎呀！躺在那边的家伙，祝愿你能像你假装的那个面团一样，也能成比例地强壮起来！” A Weasel, inactive from age and infirmities, was not able to catch mice as he once did.He therefore rolled himself in flour and lay down in a dark corner.A Mouse, supposing him to be food, leaped upon him, and was instantly caught and squeezed to death.Another perished in a similar manner, and then a third, and still others after them.A very old Mouse, who had escaped many a trap and snare, observed from a safe distance the trick of his crafty foe and said, “Ah! you that lie there, may you prosper just in the same proportion as you are what you pretend to be!”
驴和狼 The Ass and the Wolf
一头驴正在牧场上吃草，看见一只狼正走过来要抓他，驴便马上装作瘸腿的样子。狼走上前来，问驴瘸腿的原因。驴回答说自己在过树篱时，脚踩到了一根尖刺。驴请求狼替他把刺拔出来，以免狼吃他的时候刺伤了喉咙。狼同意了，他提起驴那只瘸了的脚，全神贯注地寻找那根刺。这时，驴用蹄子把狼的牙齿踢进狼嘴里，然后飞奔而去。狼就这样被驴严重踢伤了，他说：“我真是自作自受。父亲只教过我怎么做一个屠夫，我为什么要去尝试医术呢？” An Ass feeding in a meadow saw a Wolf approaching to seize him, and immediately pretended to be lame. The Wolf, coming up, inquired the cause of his lameness.The Ass replied that passing through a hedge he had trod with his foot upon a sharp thorn.He requested that the Wolf pull it out, lest when he ate him it should injure his throat.The Wolf consented and lifted up the foot, and was giving his whole mind to the discovery of the thorn, when the Ass, with his heels, kicked his teeth into his mouth and galloped away.The Wolf, being thus fearfully mauled, said, “I am rightly served, for why did I attempt the art of healing, when my father only taught me the trade of a butcher?”
卖神像的人 The Seller of Images

有个鞋匠，靠自己的手艺难以维持生计，被穷困逼得走投无路了，便去了一个没人认识的镇上行医。他卖一种药剂，谎称它能解百毒，并通过喋喋不休的吹捧和宣传让自己名声大噪。一天，鞋匠恰巧自己身染了一种重病，镇长决定趁机试试他的医术。为此，镇长叫人拿来了一个杯子。倒水时，镇长假装把毒药和鞋匠的解药混在一起放了进去，然后要求鞋匠把它喝掉，并许诺会奖励他。鞋匠怕死，只好承认自己根本不懂医术，只是拜群众的愚蠢叫嚣所赐才出了名。于是，镇长召集公众集会，并对民众说：“看看你们犯了多么愚蠢的错误啊？你们居然毫不犹豫地轻信了一个甚至连鞋子都没有人愿意雇他做的鞋匠。” A Cobbler unable to make a living by his trade and made desperate by poverty, began to practice medicine in a town in which he was not known.He sold a drug, pretending that it was an antidote to all poisons, and obtained a great name for himself by long-winded puffs and advertisements.When the Cobbler happened to fall sick himself of a serious illness, the Governor of the town determined to test his skill.For this purpose he called for a cup, and while filling it with water, pretended to mix poison with the Cobbler’s antidote, commanding him to drink it on the promise of a reward.The Cobbler, under the fear of death, confessed that he had no knowledge of medicine, and was only made famous by the stupid clamors of the crowd.The Governor then called a public assembly and addressed the citizens: “Of what folly have you been guilty?You have not hesitated to entrust your heads to a man, whom no one could employ to make even the shoes for their feet.”

有两个男人，彼此是死对头，他们坐在同一条船上。他们决定尽可能隔得远些，其中一个坐在船尾，另一个坐在船头。一阵剧烈的暴风雨袭来，船处于沉没的极大危险之中，坐在船尾的人问船夫，船的两端中哪一端会先沉下去。船夫回答说，他觉得应该是船头。坐在船尾的人说：“要是能看到我的敌人比我先死，死亡对我来说也不痛苦了。” Two men, deadly enemies to each other, were sailing in the same vessel.Determined to keep as far apart as possible, the one seated himself in the stem, and the other in the prow of the ship.A violent storm arose, and with the vessel in great danger of sinking, the one in the stem inquired of the pilot which of the two ends of the ship would go down first.On his replying that he supposed it would be the prow, the Man said, “Death would not be grievous to me, if I could only see my Enemy die before me.”

Hence it is that when she appears they look to her as knowing all things, while she no longer gives them advice, but in solitude laments their past folly.

一只被鹰突袭的兔子抽泣得十分厉害，发出像小孩一样的哭声。一只麻雀责骂她说：“你那快得不同反响的步伐而今何在？为什么你的脚下的速度这么慢？”正当麻雀这样说着，一只鹰突然抓住他，杀死了他。野兔对自己的死感到宽慰了，快断气时说：“啊！你不久前还以为自己很安全，对我幸灾乐祸，现在你该悲叹类似的不幸了。” A hare pounced upon by an eagle sobbed very much and uttered cries like a child.A Sparrow upbraided her and said, “Where now is thy remarkable swiftness of foot?Why were your feet so slow?” While the Sparrow was thus speaking, a hawk suddenly seized him and killed him.The Hare was comforted in her death, and expiring said, “Ah! you who so lately, when you supposed yourself safe, exulted over my calamity, have now reason to deplore a similar misfortune.”

墨丘利和工匠 Mercury and the Workmen
有个工匠在河边砍树，他的斧头不小心掉进一个很深的水塘里。这样他就失去了谋生的工具，他坐在河岸边，为他的苦命悲恸不已。墨丘利出现了，问他流眼泪的原因。当他告诉墨丘利自己的不幸后，墨丘利钻入河里，带上来一把金斧头，问他这是不是他丢失的那把。他说这不是他的之后，墨丘利就第二次消失在水中，然后手中拿着一把银斧头上岸了，又一次问他这把是不是他的。这位工匠说这不是，墨丘利又第三次潜入水中，拿出那把他丢失的斧头。工人认领了斧头，并表达了失而复得的高兴之情。墨丘利对他的诚实很满意，将金斧、银斧连同原本属于他的斧头都给了他。工匠回到自己家中，向他的同伴们讲述了发生的一切。其中一人立刻决定要去试试，想得到同样的好运气。他跑到河边，故意将自己的斧头丢进池塘的同一个地方，便坐在河岸边哭起来。墨丘利正如他希望的那样出现了。了解了他悲伤的原因之后，墨丘利投身于水流中，带上来一把金斧，问是不是他丢失的。工人贪婪地抓住金斧，声称这正是他丢失的那把斧头。墨丘利对他的欺骗行为感到生气，不仅拿走了金斧，而且拒绝为他找回他丢进水塘里的那把斧头。 A workman, felling wood by the side of a river, let his axe drop—by accident into a deep pool.Being thus deprived of the means of his livelihood, he sat down on the bank and lamented his hard fate.Mercury appeared and demanded the cause of his tears.After he told him his misfortune, Mercury plunged into the stream, and, bringing up a golden axe, inquired if that were the one he had lost.On his saying that it was no this, Mercury disappeared beneath the water a second time, returned with a silver axe in his hand, and again asked the Workman if it were his.When the Workman said it was not, he dived into the pool for the third time and brought up the axe that had been lost.The Workman claimed it and expressed his joy at its recovery. Mercury, pleased with his honesty, gave him the golden and silver axes in addition to his own.The Workman, on his return to his house, related to his companions all that had happened.One of them at once resolved to try and secure the same good fortune for himself.He ran to the river and threw his axe on purpose into the pool at the same place, and sat down on the bank to weep.Mercury appeared to him just as he hoped he would; and having learned the cause of his grief, plunged into the stream and brought up a golden axe, inquiring if he had lost it.The Workman seized it greedily, and declared that truly it was the very same axe that he had lost.Mercury, displeased at his knavery, not only took away the golden axe, but refused to recover for him the axe he had thrown into the pool.
鹰和寒鸦 The Eagle and the Jackdaw
一只鹰从它高高岩石上的栖息处飞下，抓住一只羊羔，用自己的爪子抓着它在空中飞。一只寒鸦目击了羊羔被抓的过程，它很嫉妒，决心赶上鹰的力量和飞行高度。它飞来飞去，翅膀发出很大的呼呼声，它选定一头大公羊，企图把它抓走，但是它的爪子缠到了公羊的羊毛里，尽管它尽力挥动着它的翅膀，还是不能挣脱。羊倌看到所发生的一切，跑过去抓住了它。他马上剪掉寒鸦的翅膀，晚上将它带回家，把它送给了自己的孩子们。孩子们说：“爸爸，它是一种什么鸟啊？”父亲回答道：“据我所知，它是一只寒鸦，但是它希望你们把它看作一只鹰。” An Eagle, flying down from his perch on a lofty rock, seized upon a lamb and carried him aloft in his talons.A Jackdaw, who witnessed the capture of the lamb, was stirred with envy and determined to emulate the strength and flight of the Eagle.He flew around with a great whir of his wings and settled upon a large ram, with the intention of carrying him off, but his claws became entangled in the ram’s fleece and he was not able to release himself, although he fluttered with his feathers as much as he could.The shepherd, seeing what had happened, ran up and caught him.He at once clipped the Jackdaw’s wings, and taking him home at night, gave him to his children.On their saying, “Father, what kind of bird is it?” he replied, “To my certain knowledge he is a Daw; but he would like you to think an Eagle.”
