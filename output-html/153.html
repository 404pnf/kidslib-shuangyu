第一章 汤姆和哈克应邀前来 Chapter I. An Invitation for Tom and Huck
（按：故事中的事件虽非同寻常，但并非虚构，而是确有其事——甚至被告的公开忏悔都是事实。这些事件取材于一个古老的瑞典刑事案件，我改变了其中的角色，并将其背景转换至美国。我还加入了些细节，但只有少数是重要的。——马克·吐温） (Note: Strange as the incidents of this story are, they are not inventions, but facts—even to the public confession of the accused. I take them from an old-time Swedish criminal trial, change the actors, and transfer the scenes to America. I have added some details, but only a couple of them are important ones.—M.T.)
嗯，就在我和汤姆·索耶将黑人老吉姆释放之后的下一个春天，他被视为逃跑未遂的奴隶而被拴在汤姆的叔叔赛拉斯在阿肯色州的农场上。严寒正慢慢地从地面和空气中散去，离赤脚的时节也一天天地临近了；而接下来将陆续是玩弹珠、玩掷刀游戏、玩铁圈以及放风筝的时候，接着就马上到了夏季，可以去游泳了。这样的展望恰恰让一个小男孩犯了思乡症，并且对遥远的夏天抱有憧憬。是的，这让他长叹一口气，而且一脸愁容，有些事情在困扰着他，而他自己也不知道是什么事。　　　但即便如此，他还是会独自外出，郁郁沉思；他最常去山顶上树林边，找一个人烟稀少的地方，在那里呆着，看着山下的密西西比河环绕着树林向着远方延伸着，直到看见看起来模糊不清的树林被层层烟雾笼罩，遥远而寂静，一切看上去如此庄严肃穆，就如同你所敬爱的人过世时那样，而这时你所想的就是和他们一起死去，离开这个世界。 Well, it was the next spring after me and Tom Sawyer set our old nigger Jim free, the time he was chained up for a runaway slave down there on Tom’s uncle Silas’s farm in Arkansaw.The frost was working out of the ground, and out of the air, too, and it was getting closer and closer onto barefoot time every day; and next it would be marble time, and next mumbletypeg, and next tops and hoops, and next kites, and then right away it would be summer and going in a-swimming.It just makes a boy homesick to look ahead like that and see how far off summer is.Yes, and it sets him to sighing and saddening around, and there’s something the matter with him, he don’t know what.But anyway, he gets out by himself and mopes and thinks; and mostly he hunts for a lonesome place high up on the hill in the edge of the woods, and sets there and looks away off on the big Mississippi down there a-reaching miles and miles around the points where the timber looks smoky and dim it’s so far off and still, and everything’s so solemn it seems like everybody you’ve loved is dead and gone, and you most wish you was dead and gone too, and done with it all.
难道你不知道那是什么吗？那就是春倦症。这就是它的名称。一旦染上它，你就会想做某事——噢，实际上也许你根本不清楚到底想做什么，但那却让你的心都痛了，你是如此得想到它！对于你而言，你所想要的就是脱身罢了；从你所司空见惯的老套而又令人厌倦的东西中脱身，去寻找新的东西。这就是你的想法；你想离开去当一个流浪者；你想去一个遥远而又陌生的国度流浪，在那里任何东西都是神秘、美妙而浪漫的。如果这些你无法做到，你将退而求其次；你将去能到达的任何地方，只要能让自己脱身便可，并也将对此机遇心存感激。 Don’t you know what that is?It’s spring fever.That is what the name of it is.And when you’ve got it, you want—oh, you don’t quite know what it is you DO want, but it just fairly makes your heart ache, you want it so!It seems to you that mainly what you want is to get away; get away from the same old tedious things you’re so used to seeing and so tired of, and set something new.That is the idea; you want to go and be a wanderer; you want to go wandering far away to strange countries where everything is mysterious and wonderful and romantic.And if you can’t do that, you’ll put up with considerable less; you’ll go anywhere you CAN go, just so as to get away, and be thankful of the chance, too.
好吧，我和汤姆·索耶也严重地染上了春倦症；但想象汤姆尝试逃脱是没用的，正如他所说的，他姑姑波利绝不会允许他辍学去四处溜达、浪费时间，所以我们很沮丧。有一天黄昏，当我们坐在门口的台阶处时，波利姑姑手里拿着一封信走过来对我们说： Well, me and Tom Sawyer had the spring fever, and had it bad, too; but it warn’t any use to think about Tom trying to get away, because, as he said, his Aunt Polly wouldn’t let him quit school and go traipsing off somers wasting time; so we was pretty blue.We was setting on the front steps one day about sundown talking this way, when out comes his aunt Polly with a letter in her hand and says:
“汤姆，我认为你该收拾行李去阿肯色州了——萨莉婶婶想让你去她那儿。” “Tom, I reckon you’ve got to pack up and go down to Arkansaw—your aunt Sally wants you.”
我当时简直欣喜若狂。我本以为汤姆会奔向他姑姑，并紧紧抱住她；可是如果您相信我的话，他还是坐在那儿稳如磐石，一言不发。在这样一个绝好机会前，他表现得如此愚笨，我差点儿叫出声来。为什么这么说，因为如果他这样一声不吭，不表达他的感激之情，我们很可能会因此而丢掉这个机会。可他就一直在那一动不动，沉思着沉思着，这让我很犯愁，不知道该怎么办；而接下来他平静地说了句话，听了那话，我很想揍他： I ’most jumped out of my skin for joy.I reckoned Tom would fly at his aunt and hug her head off; but if you believe me he set there like a rock, and never said a word.It made me fit to cry to see him act so foolish, with such a noble chance as this opening up.Why, we might lose it if he didn’t speak up and show he was thankful and grateful.But he set there and studied and studied till I was that distressed I didn’t know what to do; then he says, very ca’m, and I could a shot him for it:
“好吧，”他说，“波利姑姑，十分抱歉，现在——我想离开一会儿。” “Well,” he says, “I’m right down sorry, Aunt Polly, but I reckon I got to be excused—for the present.”
波利姑姑对他如此冷漠无礼的行为而感到诧异，半分钟内都没说出话来，不过这倒也让我有机会轻轻碰了下汤姆，低声告诉他： His aunt Polly was knocked so stupid and so mad at the cold impudence of it that she couldn’t say a word for as much as a half a minute, and this gave me a chance to nudge Tom and whisper:
“难道你真的不明白？你真打算就这么浪费并丢掉这么好的机会吗？” “Ain’t you got any sense?Sp’iling such a noble chance as this and throwing it away?”
但他似乎并不为此所困扰。他咕哝着回应我： But he warn’t disturbed.He mumbled back:
“哈克·费恩，难道你想我让她知道自己是多么迫切地想去那吗？何必要这样，这样她会马上怀疑并想象许多疾病、危险和障碍，然后你便会看到她收回成命。让我自己呆会儿；我想我知道该怎么对付她。” “Huck Finn, do you want me to let her SEE how bad I want to go?Why, she’d begin to doubt, right away, and imagine a lot of sicknesses and dangers and objections, and first you know she’d take it all back.You lemme alone; I reckon I know how to work her.”
到目前为止我确实没像他这么考虑过。不过他是对的。汤姆·索耶总是对的——他是我所见过的人中头脑最冷静的一个，而且最能保持真我，并能对突如其来的事有所准备。这回他姑姑再次直接地告诉他，并对他发了脾气。她说： Now I never would ‘a’ thought of that.But he was right.Tom Sawyer was always right—the levelest head I ever see, and always AT himself and ready for anything you might spring on him.By this time his aunt Polly was all straight again, and she let fly.She says:
“你可以离开！你完全可以！嗯，我还从没听过有人这样说话！就像你刚对我说的那些！快闭嘴，赶紧去收拾行李；要是让我再听到诸如你想离开或者你不想去的话，我发誓你必须要吃一顿鞭子才能获得我的原谅。” “You’ll be excused!YOU will!Well, I never heard the like of it in all my days!The idea of you talking like that to ME!Now take yourself off and pack your traps; and if I hear another word out of you about what you’ll be excused from and what you won’t, I lay I’LL excuse you—with a hickory!”
她戴着针箍的手正准备狠敲他脑袋，我们躲闪开了，他假装呜咽着和我跑上楼。他一上房间便抱住了我，因为他可以去旅行了，所以高兴得有点儿失去理智。然后他说： She hit his head a thump with her thimble as we dodged by, and he let on to be whimpering as we struck for the stairs.Up in his room he hugged me, he was so out of his head for gladness because he was going traveling.And he says:
“在我们离开前，她会希望她没有应许过让我离开，但现在她已经无法改变现实了。一旦说出了口，她的自尊心就不会允许自己把说出的话再收回了。” “Before we get away she’ll wish she hadn’t let me go, but she won’t know any way to get around it now.After what she’s said, her pride won’t let her take it back.”
汤姆在十分钟内就收拾好了行李，除了剩下的一些由他姑姑和玛丽为他打点；然后我们又等了十多分钟让她冷静下来，让她恢复慈祥的样子；因为汤姆说如果她没完全生气一般只需十分钟就能恢复镇定，一旦她完全生气则需要二十分钟才能恢复，而她这回看来是完全生气了。然后我们下了楼，急于想知道信的内容。 Tom was packed in ten minutes, all except what his aunt and Mary would finish up for him; then we waited ten more for her to get cooled down and sweet and gentle again; for Tom said it took her ten minutes to unruffle in times when half of her feathers was up, but twenty when they was all up, and this was one of the times when they was all up.Then we went down, being in a sweat to know what the letter said.
她坐在那儿陷入沉思，信封就在她的腿上。我们一坐下，她便说： She was setting there in a brown study, with it laying in her lap.We set down, and she says:
“他们在那儿遇到了许多麻烦，而你和哈克对他们来说能转换一下气氛，用他们的话说就是——‘安慰’。我认为，他们会从你和哈克·费恩身上得到不少的放松。她有一个邻居叫布雷斯·邓拉普，在过去的三个多月里，他一直想娶他们的本尼，最后他们就直截了当地告诉他那是不可能的；因此布雷斯·邓拉普对他们的态度就变差了，他们一直在担心此事。我认为对于布雷斯·邓拉普，他们应该都想获得他的青睐，因为他们曾经想讨好过他，在农场几乎无力承受的情况下，他们雇佣了他的一个一无是处的兄弟在农场上干活，虽然他们也不想让他呆在这里。这邓拉普一家是谁呢？” “They’re in considerable trouble down there, and they think you and Huck’ll be a kind of diversion for them—‘comfort,’ they say.Much of that they’ll get out of you and Huck Finn, I reckon.There’s a neighbor named Brace Dunlap that’s been wanting to marry their Benny for three months, and at last they told him point blank and once for all, he COULDN’T; so he has soured on them, and they’re worried about it.I reckon he’s somebody they think they better be on the good side of, for they’ve tried to please him by hiring his no-account brother to help on the farm when they can’t hardly afford it, and don’t want him around anyhow.Who are the Dunlaps?”
“他们住在离赛拉斯叔叔家一英里远的地方，波利姑姑——所有的农民都住在离这里大约一英里远的地方——而布雷斯·邓拉普比其他人富有，还拥有许多自己的黑奴。他是一位鳏夫，三十六岁，无儿无女，他常因为有钱而颇为得意，还很专横跋扈，以至于每个人都对他有些畏惧。我认为，他觉得只要自己想要的话，他可以得到任何他喜欢的女孩，而当他发现不能得到本尼时，他就受到了不小的挫折。为什么会这样，因为本尼才只有他一半的年纪，你们见了就知道了——她很甜美可爱。可怜的赛拉斯叔叔——为什么要通过这种拍马屁的方式来取得好感，他这样子真可怜——这样子过于强求而且很可悲，何况还请了没用的丘比特·邓拉普去取悦他那个脾气暴躁的兄弟。” “They live about a mile from Uncle Silas’s place, Aunt Polly—all the farmers live about a mile apart down there—and Brace Dunlap is a long sight richer than any of the others, and owns a whole grist of niggers.He’s a widower, thirty-six years old, without any children, and is proud of his money and overbearing, and everybody is a little afraid of him.I judge he thought he could have any girl he wanted, just for the asking, and it must have set him back a good deal when he found he couldn’t get Benny.Why, Benny’s only half as old as he is, and just as sweet and lovely as—well, you’ve seen her.Poor old Uncle Silas—why, it’s pitiful, him trying to curry favor that way—so hard pushed and poor, and yet hiring that useless Jubiter Dunlap to please his ornery brother.”
“多么有意思的名字——丘比特！他这名字哪来的？” “What a name—Jubiter!Where’d he get it?”
“这只不过是个绰号罢了。我觉得他们在很久以前就忘了他的真实名字了。他今年二十七岁，而从他第一次游泳开始他便有了这个绰号。一个学校老师在他没穿衣服时看到他左腿膝盖上有一个银币大小的褐色胎块，而在其四周还有四个稍微小点儿的印记，那老师说这让他想起了木星和其卫星；那群小孩子觉得很好玩，所以他们开始叫他丘比特，此后人们便一直称他丘比特。他人长得高，为人懒惰，行事狡猾，手段卑鄙而且胆小懦弱，但还是挺和善的。他留着一头棕色长发但没留胡子。他当时身无分文，布雷斯免费给他提供膳宿，拿自己的旧衣服给他穿,不过常奚落他。丘比特是双胞胎之一。” “It’s only just a nickname.I reckon they’ve forgot his real name long before this.He’s twenty-seven, now, and has had it ever since the first time he ever went in swimming.The school teacher seen a round brown mole the size of a dime on his left leg above his knee, and four little bits of moles around it, when he was naked, and he said it minded him of Jubiter and his moons; and the children thought it was funny, and so they got to calling him Jubiter, and he’s Jubiter yet.He’s tall, and lazy, and sly, and sneaky, and ruther cowardly, too, but kind of good-natured, and wears long brown hair and no beard, and hasn’t got a cent, and Brace boards him for nothing, and gives him his old clothes to wear, and despises him.Jubiter is a twin.”
“那另一个孪生兄弟长什么样子？” “What’s t’other twin like?”
“正如他们说的——他长得很像丘比特；不管怎么说，他过去是这个样子的，但大家已经七年没看到他了。他十九、二十岁时参与了抢劫，然后他们把他关进监狱；但他越狱往北方某个地方逃跑了。他们以前常常听到关于他抢劫盗窃的事，不过那是很多年前的事了。现在他已经死了。至少他们是这么说的。他们再也没听到他的消息了。” “Just exactly like Jubiter—so they say; used to was, anyway, but he hain’t been seen for seven years.He got to robbing when he was nineteen or twenty, and they jailed him; but he broke jail and got away—up North here, somers.They used to hear about him robbing and burglaring now and then, but that was years ago.He’s dead, now.At least that’s what they say.They don’t hear about him any more.”
“他叫什么名字？” “What was his name?”
“杰克。” “Jake.”
过了好一阵姑姑都没说话；她似乎在想着些什么。最后她说： There wasn’t anything more said for a considerable while; the old lady was thinking.At last she says:
“丘比特·邓拉普常让你叔叔发火，这事现在最让你萨莉婶婶担心。” “The thing that is mostly worrying your aunt Sally is the tempers that that man Jubiter gets your uncle into.”
汤姆感到很惊讶，我也是一样。汤姆说： Tom was astonished, and so was I.Tom says:
“发脾气？赛拉斯叔叔？天啊，您一定是在开玩笑吧！我从不知道他会发脾气。” “Tempers?Uncle Silas?Land, you must be joking!I didn’t know he HAD any temper.”
“萨莉婶婶说他会让你叔叔勃然大怒；她还说，甚至有时候你叔叔像要真揍那小子似的。” “Works him up into perfect rages, your aunt Sally says; says he acts as if he would really hit the man, sometimes.”
“波利姑姑，这和我以前所听说的怎么不一样。为什么会成这个样子，他一向举止文雅。” “Aunt Polly, it beats anything I ever heard of.Why, he’s just as gentle as mush.”
“是的，不管怎样，她还是很担心。她说每次争吵，赛拉斯叔叔就像变了一个人一样。而邻居们也在讨论这事，可都把它怨在你叔叔身上，当然这是因为他是个牧师，以前都不会吵架。萨莉婶婶说赛拉斯叔叔不喜欢去那个让他觉得羞耻的教堂讲坛上；于是人们便开始对他态度冷淡，渐渐地他不再像以前那么受欢迎了。” “Well, she’s worried, anyway.Says your uncle Silas is like a changed man, on account of all this quarreling.And the neighbors talk about it, and lay all the blame on your uncle, of course, because he’s a preacher and hain’t got any business to quarrel.Your aunt Sally says he hates to go into the pulpit he’s so ashamed; and the people have begun to cool toward him, and he ain’t as popular now as he used to was.”
“嗯，难道这不奇怪吗？”为什么会成这个样子，波利姑姑，他总是很友善，很和蔼，有点儿健忘，傻乎乎的，惹人喜爱——为什么会成这样，他简直就是一位天使！你有没有想过在他身上到底发生了什么事？” “Well, ain’t it strange?Why, Aunt Polly, he was always so good and kind and moony and absent-minded and chuckle-headed and lovable—why, he was just an angel!What CAN be the matter of him, do you reckon?”
第二章 杰克·邓拉普 Chapter II. Jake Dunlap
我们运气很好；因为我们有机会坐上艉明轮船，准备从北部的其中一个海湾口离开或者从一条沿着路易斯安那州的小河离开，所以我们可以不用在圣路易斯换乘汽船，而直接一路沿着密西西比河上游和下游到达那个位于阿肯色州的农场。这发动机一启动就至少能开一千英里。　　　　 We had powerful good luck; because we got a chance in a stern-wheeler from away North which was bound for one of them bayous or one-horse rivers away down Louisiana way, and so we could go all the way down the Upper Mississippi and all the way down the Lower Mississippi to that farm in Arkansaw without having to change steamboats at St. Louis; not so very much short of a thousand miles at one pull.
船上人很少；只有几个乘客，而且都是些上了年纪的人，他们分散于四周，坐着打瞌睡，十分安静。我们花了四天时间才驶出“上游”，因为我们的船经常搁浅。但这不会乏味——当然对于在旅行中的男孩子来说是不会乏味的。 A pretty lonesome boat; there warn’t but few passengers, and all old folks, that set around, wide apart, dozing, and was very quiet.We was four days getting out of the “upper river,” because we got aground so much.But it warn’t dull—couldn’t be for boys that was traveling, of course.
从一开始我和汤姆就认为我们的隔壁包舱里有人生病了，因为总会有伙计把饭菜送进去。不久我们问了这事——是汤姆去问的，伙计说那是个男的，不过他看起来并不像生病的样子。 From the very start me and Tom allowed that there was somebody sick in the stateroom next to ourn, because the meals was always toted in there by the waiters.By and by we asked about it—Tom did and the waiter said it was a man, but he didn’t look sick.
“噢，难道他没病？” “Well, but AIN’T he sick?”
“我不清楚；他可能是生病了，不过我觉得他不过是在假装罢了。” “I don’t know; maybe he is, but ‘pears to me he’s just letting on.”
“你为什么这么想？” “What makes you think that?”
“因为要是他真生病的话，迟早会脱掉衣服的——难道你不认为他应该这么做吗？可是，他就不这么做。总之，他至少从不脱掉他的靴子。” “Because if he was sick he would pull his clothes off SOME time or other—don’t you reckon he would?Well, this one don’t.At least he don’t ever pull off his boots, anyway.”
“这家伙真没这么做！甚至在上床睡觉时也没脱掉？” “The mischief he don’t!Not even when he goes to bed?”
“是的。” “No.”
汤姆·索耶对一种事情很着迷——神秘的事情。如果你把一件神秘的事和一个馅饼同时放在我和他面前供我们选择，你不用非得说作出选择，因为这是显而易见的事。因为以我的天性，我会选择馅饼，而他生来就会选择去探索奥秘。大家生来就是有所不同。而这恰恰是最好的方式。汤姆对伙计说： It was always nuts for Tom Sawyer—a mystery was.If you’d lay out a mystery and a pie before me and him, you wouldn’t have to say take your choice; it was a thing that would regulate itself.Because in my nature I have always run to pie, whilst in his nature he has always run to mystery.People are made different.And it is the best way.Tom says to the waiter:
“那人叫什么名字？” “What’s the man’s name?”
“菲利普斯。” “Phillips.”
“他是在哪儿上船的？” “Where’d he come aboard?”
“我想他是在亚历山大上船的，在爱荷华州那段流域。” “I think he got aboard at Elexandria, up on the Iowa line.”
“你觉得他这是在玩哪一出？” “What do you reckon he’s a-playing?”
“我不知道——我从没想过这事。” “I hain’t any notion—I never thought of it.”
我心里偷偷告诉自己，这里又多了一个会选择馅饼的人了。 I says to myself, here’s another one that runs to pie.

“没了——没有别的了，除了他常看起来很惊慌，不管白天还是晚上总是紧闭着门，而当你敲门时，他只会把门打开一条缝，看看是谁在敲门，否则他不会让你进门。” “No—nothing, except he seems so scary, and keeps his doors locked night and day both, and when you knock he won’t let you in till he opens the door a crack and sees who it is.”
“哎呀，这真是太有趣了！我打算去看看他。这样吧——下回你去他那儿时，难道你没想过可以推开门然后——” “By jimminy, it’s int’resting!I’d like to get a look at him.Say—the next time you’re going in there, don’t you reckon you could spread the door and—”

他没怎么睡，他很迫切地想去那里了解关于菲利普斯的秘密。此外，他彻夜对此事进行了很多的猜测，可这些猜测不会有什么用的，因为你若即将揭开一件事的谜底，那花费很多精力去猜测后发现都不是真的又有何用？我睡不着觉。我对自己说，我才不想知道在菲利普斯身上发生了什么事。 He didn’t sleep much, he was in such a sweat to get in there and find out the mystery about Phillips; and moreover he done a lot of guessing about it all night, which warn’t no use, for if you are going to find out the facts of a thing, what’s the sense in guessing out what ain’t the facts and wasting ammunition?I didn’t lose no sleep.I wouldn’t give a dern to know what’s the matter of Phillips, I says to myself.
好吧，一早我们便穿上了围裙，推着一辆装着些托盘的餐车到他那里，汤姆上前敲了敲门。那个人把门打开一条缝，然后让我们进去并马上把门给关上了。天哪，当我们看到他时，差点儿把盘子都掉了！汤姆说： Well, in the morning we put on the aperns and got a couple of trays of truck, and Tom he knocked on the door.The man opened it a crack, and then he let us in and shut it quick.By Jackson, when we got a sight of him, we ‘most dropped the trays! and Tom says:
“丘比特·邓拉普，为什么是你，你是从哪里冒出来的？” “Why, Jubiter Dunlap, where’d YOU come from?”
当然，那个人感到很惊讶；首先他看起来并不清楚自己是该表现得害怕，高兴，或者是两者都表现出来，还是其他表现，但最终他安静了下来，并喜上眉梢；虽然刚才他脸色一下子变得苍白，可过了一会儿他脸上又有了血色。所以在他吃早餐时，我们就在一起聊天了。然后他说： Well, the man was astonished, of course; and first off he looked like he didn’t know whether to be scared, or glad, or both, or which, but finally he settled down to being glad; and then his color come back, though at first his face had turned pretty white.So we got to talking together while he et his breakfast.And he says:
“我可不是丘比特·邓拉普。我其实也不是菲利普斯，如果你发誓可以不说出去，我马上会告诉你我是谁。” “But I aint Jubiter Dunlap.I’d just as soon tell you who I am, though, if you’ll swear to keep mum, for I ain’t no Phillips, either.”
汤姆说： Tom says:
“我们不会说出去，但是你如果不是丘比特·邓拉普，也不需要告诉我们你是谁了。” “We’ll keep mum, but there ain’t any need to tell who you are if you ain’t Jubiter Dunlap.”
“为什么？” “Why?”
“因为如果你不是他的话，那你就是双胞胎中的另一个，杰克。你和丘比特长得一模一样。” “Because if you ain’t him you’re t’other twin, Jake.You’re the spit’n image of Jubiter.”
“好吧，我就是杰克。喂，那你是怎么知道我们邓拉普兄弟的呢？” “Well, I’m Jake.But looky here, how do you come to know us Dunlaps?”
汤姆便告诉他我们去年夏天在赛拉斯叔叔那里的历险。当他从汤姆的话里没有听到任何关于他家人的消息，或者是关于他和那件事有关的消息时——其实我们倒真的一无所知——他便打破沉默并饶有兴趣地侃侃而谈起来。他对于自己的事情并不是很坦率；他说他以前命很苦，至今仍过得很苦，还认为即使到终老之时也还是会很苦。他说这当然是充满危险的一生，然后——他突然倒抽一口气，摆出一副聆听的样子。我们没吭声，所以都沉默了片刻，此刻没有任何声音，只剩下船上木造部分发出的吱吱声和机器发出的嘎嚓声。 Tom told about the adventures we’d had down there at his uncle Silas’s last summer, and when he see that there warn’t anything about his folks—or him either, for that matter—that we didn’t know, he opened out and talked perfectly free and candid.He never made any bones about his own case; said he’d been a hard lot, was a hard lot yet, and reckoned he’d be a hard lot plumb to the end.He said of course it was a dangerous life, and—He give a kind of gasp, and set his head like a person that’s listening.We didn’t say anything, and so it was very still for a second or so, and there warn’t no sounds but the screaking of the woodwork and the chug-chugging of the machinery down below.
其后我们让他再次安下心来，并告诉他关于家人的事，既有布雷斯的妻子在三年前是如何去世的，也有布雷斯迫切地想娶本尼为妻却又如何遭到她的拒绝，还有丘比特是如何为赛拉斯叔叔工作的以及为何他和赛拉斯叔叔总是吵架——而他也很放松并被逗乐了。　 Then we got him comfortable again, telling him about his people, and how Brace’s wife had been dead three years, and Brace wanted to marry Benny and she shook him, and Jubiter was working for Uncle Silas, and him and Uncle Silas quarreling all the time—and then he let go and laughed.
他说：“天哪！这就像以前在听那些闲言碎语一样，让我感觉棒极了。我已经七年多没听到这些消息了。那现在他们都是如何谈论关于我的事呢？” “Land!” he says, “it’s like old times to hear all this tittle-tattle, and does me good.It’s been seven years and more since I heard any.How do they talk about me these days?”
“你说是谁在谈论？” “Who?”
“农民——以及家里人。” “The farmers—and the family.”
“嗨，他们已经不会谈论你的事了——仅仅只是很长时间里偶尔提到几回你罢了。” “Why, they don’t talk about you at all—at least only just a mention, once in a long time.”
他诧异地说：“天哪！为什么会这样？” “The nation!” he says, surprised; “why is that?”
“因为他们以为你已经不在人世很久了。” “Because they think you are dead long ago.”
“不！你现在说的是真的，——敢保证是千真万确吗？”他兴奋地跳了起来。 “No!Are you speaking true?—honor bright, now.”He jumped up, excited.
“我当然敢保证。没有人会想到你还活着。” “Honor bright.There ain’t anybody thinks you are alive.”
“看来我有救了，我确实有救了！我要回去。他们会把我藏起来，他们会救我的。你不要说出去。你发誓不要说出去——发誓永远也不能说出去，永远不告发我。啊，孩子们，就可怜可怜我这样一个每天被到处追查、不能露脸的可怜人吧！我永远也不会伤害你们；看在上帝分上，我永远不会那么做；请你发誓要帮我个忙，帮忙救我一命。” “Then I’m saved, I’m saved, sure!I’ll go home.They’ll hide me and save my life.You keep mum.Swear you’ll keep mum—swear you’ll never, never tell on me.Oh, boys, be good to a poor devil that’s being hunted day and night, and dasn’t show his face!I’ve never done you any harm; I’ll never do you any, as God is in the heavens; swear you’ll be good to me and help me save my life.”
就算他是无赖我们也会发这个誓；之后我们也的确这样做了。好吧，这可怜的家伙，对我们喜爱和感激得无以复加；他所能做的就是给我们一个拥抱。 We’d a swore it if he’d been a dog; and so we done it.Well, he couldn’t love us enough for it or be grateful enough, poor cuss; it was all he could do to keep from hugging us.
我们继续聊着，然后他拿出了一个小手提袋，开始慢慢打开并让我们转过身去。我们按他的话照做了，而当他让我们再转回身子时，与刚刚相比他发生了非常大的变化。他戴上了一副蓝色的护目镜，一条看起来浑然天成的褐色长络腮胡子以及你经常见到的长髭。他的亲生母亲可能也认不出他。他问我们现在这个样子会不会像他的兄弟丘比特。 We talked along, and he got out a little hand-bag and begun to open it, and told us to turn our backs.We done it, and when he told us to turn again he was perfectly different to what he was before.He had on blue goggles and the naturalest-looking long brown whiskers and mustashes you ever see.His own mother wouldn’t ’a’ knowed him.He asked us if he looked like his brother Jubiter, now.
“不像，”汤姆说，“现在除了你的一头长发，你身上没有一样东西和他相似了。” “No,” Tom said; “there ain’t anything left that’s like him except the long hair.”
“好的，在回去前，我会把头发剪得很短；届时丘比特和布雷斯会保守这个秘密，我将会以一个陌生人的身份和他们住在一起，这样那些邻居们就不可能猜出我是谁了。你觉得这主意怎样？” “All right, I’ll get that cropped close to my head before I get there; then him and Brace will keep my secret, and I’ll live with them as being a stranger, and the neighbors won’t ever guess me out.What do you think?”
汤姆想了一会儿，然后说： Tom he studied awhile, then he says:
“嗯，当然我和哈克不会把这事说出去，但是如果你自己说出去，这就会有些风险了——风险可能不会很大，但至少会有些。我意思是，如果你说话，难道人们就不会注意到你的声音和丘比特很相似吗；不过人们也有可能想起那个大家都以为已经死掉的孪生兄弟，并认为或许他并没有死，隐姓埋名活到现在呢？” “Well, of course me and Huck are going to keep mum there, but if you don’t keep mum yourself there’s going to be a little bit of a risk—it ain’t much, maybe, but it’s a little.I mean, if you talk, won’t people notice that your voice is just like Jubiter’s; and mightn’t it make them think of the twin they reckoned was dead, but maybe after all was hid all this time under another name?”
“天啊，”他说，“你真是机灵！你完全是正确的。到时如果旁边有邻居，我就会装聋作哑。要是我要回家时忘了这些细枝末节的事——无论如何，我就不会回去了。我会找一个那些要抓我的人找不到的地方停下来，然后戴上这些伪装用具，穿上不同的衣服，并且——” “By George,” he says, “you’re a sharp one!You’re perfectly right.I’ve got to play deef and dumb when there’s a neighbor around.If I’d a struck for home and forgot that little detail—However, I wasn’t striking for home.I was breaking for any place where I could get away from these fellows that are after me; then I was going to put on this disguise and get some different clothes, and—”
他突然跳到外面的门边，把耳朵贴在门上听，此刻他面色苍白而且像在喘息。不久他低声说： He jumped for the outside door and laid his ear against it and listened, pale and kind of panting.Presently he whispers:
“这听起来像是扣扳机的声音！天啊，这下该怎么办！” “Sounded like cocking a gun!Lord, what a life to lead!”
然后他无力地瘫坐在椅子上，擦了擦他脸上的汗珠，露出一副病怏怏的样子。 Then he sunk down in a chair all limp and sick like, and wiped the sweat off of his face.

从那时开始，我们就几乎一直和他在一起，我们中至少有一个会睡在他上铺。他说过去颇感寂寞，而有人陪伴并在烦恼时有倾诉的对象，让他很欣慰。　我们很想了解他的秘密，但汤姆说最好的方法就是装作毫无好奇心，然后他自己可能就会在话中透露他的秘密，如果我们一直追问，他反而会心生戒备，守口如瓶。事实的确如此。我们很容易看出他往往想谈某些事，可每当所讲的事和其秘密有沾上点儿边，他就不敢再往下讲，而去扯些其他的事情。　事情是这样的：他看似漫不经心地问我们关于甲板上乘客的事。我们俩就告诉他一些乘客的事。可他始终不满意；觉得我们描述得不够详细。他让我们更准确地描述。后来汤姆照做了。最后，当汤姆描述完那些人中举止最粗鲁、衣着最破烂的一个人时，他不禁打了个冷战，倒抽一大口气说： From that time out, we was with him ’most all the time, and one or t’other of us slept in his upper berth.He said he had been so lonesome, and it was such a comfort to him to have company, and somebody to talk to in his troubles.We was in a sweat to find out what his secret was, but Tom said the best way was not to seem anxious, then likely he would drop into it himself in one of his talks, but if we got to asking questions he would get suspicious and shet up his shell.It turned out just so.It warn’t no trouble to see that he WANTED to talk about it, but always along at first he would scare away from it when he got on the very edge of it, and go to talking about something else.The way it come about was this: He got to asking us, kind of indifferent like, about the passengers down on deck.We told him about them.But he warn’t satisfied; we warn’t particular enough.He told us to describe them better.Tom done it.At last, when Tom was describing one of the roughest and raggedest ones, he gave a shiver and a gasp and says:

汤姆又马上描述了另外一些乘客。当他说到甲板上另一个举止粗鲁的乘客时，他再次打了个冷战，并说： Presently when Tom was describing another mangy, rough deck passenger, he give that shiver again and says:
“就是他！——他就是另外一个。如果哪个晚上碰上暴风雨，我一定要设法上岸。你瞧，他们已经在暗中监视我了。他们已经在靠近我了，就在前面的柜台买饮料，然后趁机贿赂搬运工，擦靴工或者其他人来严密监视我。如果我趁没人注意悄悄溜上岸，他们一定会在一小时内就知道情况。” “That’s him!—that’s the other one.If it would only come a good black stormy night and I could get ashore.You see, they’ve got spies on me.They’ve got a right to come up and buy drinks at the bar yonder forrard, and they take that chance to bribe somebody to keep watch on me—porter or boots or somebody.If I was to slip ashore without anybody seeing me, they would know it inside of an hour.”

“那些钻石很对得起这个价钱。” “Every cent of it.”
“后来你们得手了？” “And you fellows got away with them?”

这让汤姆·索耶很反感，他说这是他所听过的最下流、最卑鄙的伎俩。但是杰克·邓拉普说这种事在他这行里太普遍了。他还说当一个人在干这行时，他只会关心自己的利益，不会有任何人来为你做任何事。然后他又继续。他说： It disgusted Tom Sawyer, and he said it was the orneriest, low-downest thing he ever heard of.But Jake Dunlap said it warn’t unusual in the profession.Said when a person was in that line of business he’d got to look out for his own intrust, there warn’t nobody else going to do it for him.And then he went on.He says:

“那这里就废话少说吧，确切地说，直到那天中午我们都还没离开那里。在那段时间内我就一直藏在这间包舱。可就在吃早饭前，我看到一个人从远处慢慢走过来，而最让我不爽的就是越看那个人的走姿就越像哈尔·克莱顿。我想，如果他发现我在这艘船上，他就能轻而易举地抓住我。他所需要做的就是暗中监视我，并等待——等我偷偷溜上岸的那天，并幻想他在千里之外而偷溜，然后一直跟踪我，找个好地方迫使我交出钻石，最后他就会——天啊，我知道他会对我做什么！　难道这不可怕吗——这简直太可怕了！现在另外一个人也上船了！噢，孩子们，这不就是背运嘛——这不就是倒霉嘛！但我知道你们会救我的，对吧？——噢，孩子们，就救救我这样一个被到处追杀的可怜鬼吧——我会对你们感激涕零！” “Well, to cut the tale short, we never left there till plumb noon; and long before that I was hid in this stateroom; for before breakfast I see a man coming, away off, that had a gait like Hal Clayton’s, and it made me just sick.I says to myself, if he finds out I’m aboard this boat, he’s got me like a rat in a trap.All he’s got to do is to have me watched, and wait—wait till I slip ashore, thinking he is a thousand miles away, then slip after me and dog me to a good place and make me give up the di’monds, and then he’ll—oh, I know what he’ll do!Ain’t it awful—awful!And now to think the OTHER one’s aboard, too!Oh, ain’t it hard luck, boys—ain’t it hard!But you’ll help save me, WON’T you?—oh, boys, be good to a poor devil that’s being hunted to death, and save me—I’ll worship the very ground you walk on!”
我们也没再说话了，并尽量让他平静下来，告诉他别害怕，我们会帮他，还会给他制定好下一步计划；不久他脸上的愁云就烟消云散了，然后他就旋下他的鞋跟铁片，拿起他的钻石左右打量，不难看出他对钻石的欣赏和喜爱。的确，在光线的照射下，它们相当美丽，耀眼的光芒从内部散裂开来，夺目异常。　　　但即便是这样，我还是认为他真的很傻。我要是他的话，就会把钻石直接交给那些家伙，让他们上岸离开，并把我留在这里。　　但是他可不一样。他说这是他的全部家当，他是不可能接受我这个建议的。 We turned in and soothed him down and told him we would plan for him and help him, and he needn’t be so afeard; and so by and by he got to feeling kind of comfortable again, and unscrewed his heelplates and held up his di’monds this way and that, admiring them and loving them; and when the light struck into them they WAS beautiful, sure; why, they seemed to kind of bust, and snap fire out all around.But all the same I judged he was a fool.If I had been him I would a handed the di’monds to them pals and got them to go ashore and leave me alone.But he was made different.He said it was a whole fortune and he couldn’t bear the idea.
有一天夜里我们的船第二次停下来进行维修，而这次停了好一阵子。不过当时天还不太黑，他还不敢在这个时候溜走。　　但是到了我们的船进行第三次维修时，这可是个绝佳机会。那晚我们的船在一个乡下的堆木场停留，估计那里离赛拉斯叔叔家有四十英里远。那晚云层变得越来越厚，雾越来越浓，眼看着一场暴风雨即将到来。　所以杰克已经准备好抓住这次机会逃跑。很快就已经大雨倾盆，狂风大作。当然每一个船夫在担柴时，都会固定一个麻袋，而戴上它后就像戴一顶无边呢帽，因此我们给杰克准备了这样东西。后来他带着手提袋悄悄溜到船尾，和其他人一样踩着沉重的步子往前走，并和他们一起上了岸。在我们看到他慢慢离开火把照亮之处，身影渐渐消失在黑暗中时，我们总算松了一口气，那感觉真是棒极了。但没过多久。我猜应该是有人向他的同伙告密；仅仅过了八到十分钟左右，那两个家伙看起来几近疯狂地往岸上飞奔狂追，后来也消失了。在破晓前我们一直在等待他们回来，并对此还抱有希望，可终究还是没有见到人影。我们觉得实在对不起他，一直提不起精神。我们所希望的就是杰克能摆脱那两个家伙的跟踪而安全启程，顺利到达他兄弟那里，找到安全的藏身之所。 Twice we stopped to fix the machinery and laid a good while, once in the night; but it wasn’t dark enough, and he was afeard to skip.But the third time we had to fix it there was a better chance.We laid up at a country woodyard about forty mile above Uncle Silas’s place a little after one at night, and it was thickening up and going to storm.So Jake he laid for a chance to slide.We begun to take in wood.Pretty soon the rain come a-drenching down, and the wind blowed hard.Of course every boat-hand fixed a gunny sack and put it on like a bonnet, the way they do when they are toting wood, and we got one for Jake, and he slipped down aft with his hand-bag and come tramping forrard just like the rest, and walked ashore with them, and when we see him pass out of the light of the torch-basket and get swallowed up in the dark, we got our breath again and just felt grateful and splendid.But it wasn’t for long.Somebody told, I reckon; for in about eight or ten minutes them two pals come tearing forrard as tight as they could jump and darted ashore and was gone.We waited plumb till dawn for them to come back, and kept hoping they would, but they never did.We was awful sorry and low-spirited.All the hope we had was that Jake had got such a start that they couldn’t get on his track, and he would get to his brother’s and hide there and be safe.
他告诉我们他将会沿着河边小径走，还让我们去看看布雷斯和丘比特是否在家，是否有陌生人在他们那，让我们在夕阳西下时出来告诉他情况。他还说会在一小丛悬铃木那等我们，那里人迹罕至的，刚好是位于赛拉斯叔叔在河边小径的一块烟草地后面。　　　　　　　　　　　　　　 He was going to take the river road, and told us to find out if Brace and Jubiter was to home and no strangers there, and then slip out about sundown and tell him.Said he would wait for us in a little bunch of sycamores right back of Tom’s uncle Silas’s tobacker field on the river road, a lonesome place.
我们在那坐了很久，讨论他逃脱的几率。汤姆说只要那俩家伙沿着河往上游追，而不是往下游追，杰克就会没事的。但这基本是不可能的，因为他们应该都知道他的家乡在哪儿，所以一定会往那个方向追，并在天黑时杀了他，把靴子取走。　　　　　所以刹那间，我们心情很沉重。 We set and talked a long time about his chances, and Tom said he was all right if the pals struck up the river instead of down, but it wasn’t likely, because maybe they knowed where he was from; more likely they would go right, and dog him all day, him not suspecting, and kill him when it come dark, and take the boots.So we was pretty sorrowful.
第五章 树林里的悲剧 Chapter V. A Tragedy in the Woods
那天下午，我们很晚才离开，直到那时船都还没修理好。正因为这样，我们到家时就已经快日落了，所以一路上也顾不得停下休息，马不停蹄地赶到悬铃木丛。我们想告诉杰克迟到的原因，并希望他能等我们去布雷斯那里看看情况后回来。　　　　　　　　　　　　　　跑了一大段路后，我们终于转过树林拐角处，那时已经是气喘吁吁、大汗淋漓，而且天色也愈来愈暗。眼看着悬铃木就在我们前方三十码左右，突然间有几个人跑进草丛里，之后从那里传来了两三声十分可怕的尖叫呼救声。　　“他们肯定杀了可怜的杰克。”我们说。我们当时非常害怕，于是就赶紧躲进烟草地里，身体在不停地发抖，都快把衣服抖下来了。就在我们藏起来的时候，又有几个人从我们旁边飞奔冲进草丛，没多久四个人又先后从草丛里跳了出来冲到大路上，后面的俩人拼命地追着前面的那两个家伙。　　　 We didn’t get done tinkering the machinery till away late in the afternoon, and so it was so close to sundown when we got home that we never stopped on our road, but made a break for the sycamores as tight as we could go, to tell Jake what the delay was, and have him wait till we could go to Brace’s and find out how things was there.It was getting pretty dim by the time we turned the corner of the woods, sweating and panting with that long run, and see the sycamores thirty yards ahead of us; and just then we see a couple of men run into the bunch and heard two or three terrible screams for help.“Poor Jake is killed, sure,” we says.We was scared through and through, and broke for the tobacker field and hid there, trembling so our clothes would hardly stay on; and just as we skipped in there, a couple of men went tearing by, and into the bunch they went, and in a second out jumps four men and took out up the road as tight as they could go, two chasing two.
我们趴了下来，感到虚弱而不舒服，想听听看是不是还有更多的声响，但是过了很久还是什么都没听到，只是听到了各自的心跳声。我们当时在想躺在那丛悬铃木里的可怕的西，霎时间觉得和幽灵原来是近在咫尺，这不禁让我打了个冷战。月亮缓缓地爬上来了，又大又圆又亮，在浓郁的树冠背后时隐时现，就像一张晃动在监狱铁栅栏后的囚徒的脸。月光在阴暗的地面上缓缓移动着。四周冷冷清清，凉风飕飕，就像在墓园里一样，很阴森。忽然间汤姆嘀咕道： We laid down, kind of weak and sick, and listened for more sounds, but didn’t hear none for a good while but just our hearts.We was thinking of that awful thing laying yonder in the sycamores, and it seemed like being that close to a ghost, and it give me the cold shudders.The moon come a-swelling up out of the ground, now, powerful big and round and bright, behind a comb of trees, like a face looking through prison bars, and the black shadders and white places begun to creep around, and it was miserable quiet and still and night-breezy and graveyardy and scary.All of a sudden Tom whispers:
“你快看！——那是什么东西？” “Look!—what’s that?”
“我才不看！”我说。“你别想用这方式吓唬人了。即使你不这么做，我也快被吓死了。” “Don’t!” I says.“Don’t take a person by surprise that way.I’m ’most ready to die, anyway, without you doing that.”
“我说，你快看啊。有东西从悬铃木那里冒出来。” “Look, I tell you.It’s something coming out of the sycamores.”
“汤姆，别闹了！” “Don’t, Tom!”
“他好高！” “It’s terrible tall!”
“噢，天啊！让我们——” “Oh, lordy-lordy! let’s—”
“别说话——他正往这边过来。” “Keep still—it’s a-coming this way.”
他太兴奋了，差点儿连低声嘀咕都缓不过气。我必须看一看。我实在没办法控制住自己。这个时候我们两个人都两膝跪地，下巴紧贴着篱笆，目不转睛地望着那里——是的，我们也在不断喘气。有个东西在沿街的树影中移动，并向我们靠近，但是看得不怎么清楚。它不断地向我们靠近，直到身边的一缕月光照亮了它，这下我们都惊悚地缩回了身子，躲入隐蔽处——是杰克·邓拉普的鬼魂！　　　　　我们心里都是这么想的。 He was so excited he could hardly get breath enough to whisper.I had to look.I couldn’t help it.So now we was both on our knees with our chins on a fence rail and gazing—yes, and gasping too.It was coming down the road—coming in the shadder of the trees, and you couldn’t see it good; not till it was pretty close to us; then it stepped into a bright splotch of moonlight and we sunk right down in our tracks—it was Jake Dunlap’s ghost!That was what we said to ourselves.
我们在这短短的一两分钟内完全不敢颤抖；后来他就离开了。我们低声讨论此事。　汤姆说： We couldn’t stir for a minute or two; then it was gone.We talked about it in low voices.Tom says:
“这类东西总是很模糊很朦胧，但多半只是雾气造成的错觉而已，但这次好像没那么简单。” “They’re mostly dim and smoky, or like they’re made out of fog, but this one wasn’t.”
我应道：“这个的确像是真的，我清楚地看到了他的络腮胡和护目镜。” “No,” I says; “I seen the goggles and the whiskers perfectly plain.”
“没错，还有他那套颜色很土气的节日礼服——特别是那条格子呢短裤，既有绿色又有黑色——” “Yes, and the very colors in them loud countrified Sunday clothes—plaid breeches, green and black—”
“还有人造棉平绒，火红色和黄色的方格——” “Cotton velvet westcot, fire-red and yaller squares—”
“那条长至裤腿的皮带，其中一边由于扣子解开而悬在半空——” “Leather straps to the bottoms of the breeches legs and one of them hanging unbottoned—”
“对了，还有那顶帽子——” “Yes, and that hat—”
“居然连幽灵也会戴帽子！” “What a hat for a ghost to wear!”
这个就是人们在春天都戴的那种帽子——黑色硬边烟筒样式的高帽，做工粗糙，上面还有一个像棒棒糖一样的圆顶。 You see it was the first season anybody wore that kind—a black stiff-brim stove-pipe, very high, and not smooth, with a round top—just like a sugar-loaf.
“哈克，不知道你有没有注意到他们的头发是否一样？” “Did you notice if its hair was the same, Huck?”
“不能确定——我好像留意了，好像又没有。” “No—seems to me I did, then again it seems to me I didn’t.”
“我也没留意；不过我注意到它还有个袋子。” “I didn’t either; but it had its bag along, I noticed that.”
“我也看到了。汤姆，怎么连幽灵都有个袋子？” “So did I.How can there be a ghost-bag, Tom?”
“当然是这样！哈克·费恩，我要是你的话，我才不会那么无知。不管幽灵有什么，那都是幽灵界的东西。它们也像常人一样，会有自己的一套穿着打扮。　　　　　就像我们要穿衣服一样，幽灵也有用于遮体的东西。好吧，那么为什么他的袋子没有也变成幽灵的袋子？　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　当然那个袋子也已经是了。” “Sho!I wouldn’t be as ignorant as that if I was you, Huck Finn.Whatever a ghost has, turns to ghost-stuff.They’ve got to have their things, like anybody else.You see, yourself, that its clothes was turned to ghost-stuff.Well, then, what’s to hender its bag from turning, too?Of course it done it.”
这么说是有道理的。我也确实觉得这里面有蹊跷。这时比尔·威瑟斯和他弟弟杰克一路上说着话往这边走过来。杰克说： That was reasonable.I couldn’t find no fault with it.Bill Withers and his brother Jack come along by, talking, and Jack says:
“你觉得他背的是什么东西？” “What do you reckon he was toting?”
“我不知道。不过看起来挺重的。” “I dunno; but it was pretty heavy.”
“是的，他几乎用尽全力在背。我认为那黑鬼从赛拉斯牧师那儿偷走了东西。” “Yes, all he could lug.Nigger stealing corn from old Parson Silas, I judged.”
“我也是这么想。所以我还是装作没看到他吧。” “So did I.And so I allowed I wouldn’t let on to see him.”
“我也打算这么做。” “That’s me, too.”
后来他们都笑了，渐渐地走远了，基本听不见他们在说些什么了。由此看得出赛拉斯叔叔现在在这里确实不受欢迎。他们不允许黑人偷任何人的玉米，却不对这个偷东西的黑人作任何惩罚。 Then they both laughed, and went on out of hearing.It showed how unpopular old Uncle Silas had got to be now.They wouldn’t ’a’ let a nigger steal anybody else’s corn and never done anything to him.
我们听到一些声音叽里咕噜地朝我们这边靠近，声音也越来越大，而且还不时地发出了咯咯的笑声。　他们是莱姆·毕比和吉姆·莱恩。吉姆·莱恩说： We heard some more voices mumbling along towards us and getting louder, and sometimes a cackle of a laugh.It was Lem Beebe and Jim Lane.Jim Lane says:
“谁？——是丘比特·邓拉普？” “Who?—Jubiter Dunlap?”
“没错。” “Yes.”
“噢，我不知道。我是这么想的。就在一小时前我还看到他在某块地上用铁锹在翻土，没错，就在日落前——当时和他一起的还有牧师。他说他猜测今晚会去不了，但如果真要找他的话，把他的狗抓来就行了。” “Oh, I don’t know.I reckon so.I seen him spading up some ground along about an hour ago, just before sundown—him and the parson.Said he guessed he wouldn’t go to-night, but we could have his dog if we wanted him.”
“我想他应该是太累了。” “Too tired, I reckon.”
“没错——他工作太辛苦了！” “Yes—works so hard!”
“噢，当然了！” “Oh, you bet!”
他们为此又笑了，后来就走了。汤姆说我们现在还是别躲这里，最好跟着他们，因为他们正好和我们顺路，要是万一再碰到幽灵的话也比较安心。所以我们就跟上去了，没多久就顺利到家了。 They cackled at that, and went on by.Tom said we better jump out and tag along after them, because they was going our way and it wouldn’t be comfortable to run across the ghost all by ourselves.So we done it, and got home all right.
那晚是九月二日的晚上——周六。我永远也不会忘记。你很快就会知道这是为什么了。 That night was the second of September—a Saturday.I sha’n’t ever forget it.You'll see why,pretty soon..
第六章 保护钻石计划 Chapter VI. Plans to Secure the Diamonds
我们顺着吉姆和莱姆留下的脚步走下去，到了一间小屋的后门台阶。以前老汤姆就是被关在这间小屋的，我们也正是在那时将他释放。此刻，突然冒出了一条狗，一直绕着我们转以示好，我们还注意到远方的房子有些许微弱光线，所以我们也不再害怕了。然而正当我要攀爬过去时，汤姆说：　　　　　　　　　　　　　　 We tramped along behind Jim and Lem till we come to the back stile where old Jim’s cabin was that he was captivated in, the time we set him free, and here come the dogs piling around us to say howdy, and there was the lights of the house, too; so we warn’t afeard any more, and was going to climb over, but Tom says:

当那个黑人离开时，他便从椅子上站了起来，拨弄着头发，并焦虑地来回走动，口中还不时地喃喃自语。看到他这样子真让人觉得很可怜。萨莉婶婶低声告诉我们别一直注意他，这会让他很尴尬。她说他自从碰上麻烦后，最近老在冥思苦想，而在他深思时她也不试着去打探究竟。她还说，最近他晚上起床梦游的症状比以前严重得多，有时候会在屋子里四下游荡，有时甚至会跑到屋子外面去。如果我们在那个时候发现了他，最好别打扰他，也别理睬他。　　　她觉得这对他无害，可能还在某种程度对他有好处。她说在最近这段时间内，本尼是唯一一个给他不少帮助的人。　　她说本尼似乎总是知道要在什么时候安慰他，也知道什么时候不要打扰他。 And when the nigger was gone he got up and walked the floor, backwards and forwards, mumbling and muttering to himself and plowing his hands through his hair.It was real pitiful to see him.Aunt Sally she whispered to us and told us not to take notice of him, it embarrassed him.She said he was always thinking and thinking, since these troubles come on, and she allowed he didn’t more’n about half know what he was about when the thinking spells was on him; and she said he walked in his sleep considerable more now than he used to, and sometimes wandered around over the house and even outdoors in his sleep, and if we catched him at it we must let him alone and not disturb him.She said she reckoned it didn’t do him no harm, and may be it done him good.She said Benny was the only one that was much help to him these days.Said Benny appeared to know just when to try to soothe him and when to leave him alone.
他就这样一直在房里踱步，嘴里还一直嘀嘀咕咕，不久后他似乎真的累了，才停下来；然后本尼走过去依偎在他身边，一只手还挽在赛拉斯叔叔腰间陪着他走；他则低下头，朝本尼略微一笑，并弓下腰亲吻了她。就这样，他的愁容消失了，本尼就劝他回房间休息。　父女俩在一起的动作很亲昵，确实很少能见到他们这个样子。　　 So he kept on tramping up and down the floor and muttering, till by and by he begun to look pretty tired; then Benny she went and snuggled up to his side and put one hand in his and one arm around his waist and walked with him; and he smiled down on her, and reached down and kissed her; and so, little by little the trouble went out of his face and she persuaded him off to his room.They had very petting ways together, and it was uncommon pretty to see.
萨莉婶婶此刻则忙着安顿孩子们去睡觉；过了不久我和汤姆就觉得这太无聊了，着实令人厌倦。于是借着月光，我和汤姆跑到了一片西瓜地，吃了个西瓜，并在那里聊了很久。　　　汤姆说他敢确定这次争吵是丘比特的错，等到时机成熟，他自然会出现。如果事实真是这样的话，他就会尽其所能让赛拉斯叔叔把他辞退掉。　 Aunt Sally she was busy getting the children ready for bed; so by and by it got dull and tedious, and me and Tom took a turn in the moonlight, and fetched up in the watermelon-patch and et one, and had a good deal of talk.And Tom said he’d bet the quarreling was all Jubiter’s fault, and he was going to be on hand the first time he got a chance, and see; and if it was so, he was going to do his level best to get Uncle Silas to turn him off.
在此后大约两个钟头内，我们一直聊着天，吸烟，还吃西瓜吃到差点撑死。后来由于太晚，我们回家时四周一片漆黑，犹如死寂一般，大家也已进入梦乡。　　　 And so we talked and smoked and stuffed watermelons much as two hours, and then it was pretty late, and when we got back the house was quiet and dark, and everybody gone to bed.
汤姆总是能注意到任何事情，而这回他发现那件旧绿色台面呢工作服居然不翼而飞了。他觉得这很怪，因为据他说，在他出去时衣服还在那里，不过后来我们还是上床睡觉了。 Tom he always seen everything, and now he see that the old green baize work-gown was gone, and said it wasn’t gone when he went out; so he allowed it was curious, and then we went up to bed.
本尼的房间就在我们隔壁，所以我们可以听到她一直在房内不停走动。猜想，她应该是因为很担心她父亲所以睡不着吧。我们发现其实我们俩也睡不着。因此我们坐了很久，一边吸烟，一边压着声音聊着天，觉得很烦躁而且情绪低落。　因为我们一直在反复地聊着那起谋杀事件以及幽灵的事，感到毛骨悚然，所以根本就睡不着。 We could hear Benny stirring around in her room, which was next to ourn, and judged she was worried a good deal about her father and couldn’t sleep.We found we couldn’t, neither.So we set up a long time, and smoked and talked in a low voice, and felt pretty dull and down-hearted.We talked the murder and the ghost over and over again, and got so creepy and crawly we couldn’t get sleepy nohow and noway.
慢慢地，夜色渐深，任何声音在夜间都越发肃穆安详。汤姆用肘轻轻碰了我，低声叫我快看前方。我顺着前方看过去，发现有个人正在地里面乱翻乱找，可似乎连他自己都不知道想做什么。可是确实由于当时太暗了，我们没法看清楚他到底是谁。然后他往台阶处走，就在那时，月亮从云间钻了出来。我们看见他肩上扛着一把长柄的铁锹，还注意到了那件旧工作服上有白色补丁。所以汤姆说： By and by, when it was away late in the night and all the sounds was late sounds and solemn, Tom nudged me and whispers to me to look, and I done it, and there we see a man poking around in the yard like he didn’t know just what he wanted to do, but it was pretty dim and we couldn’t see him good.Then he started for the stile, and as he went over it the moon came out strong, and he had a long-handled shovel over his shoulder, and we see the white patch on the old work-gown.So Tom says:
“他正在梦游。我希望我们最好还是跟着他，看看他到底要去哪里。看，他拐进烟草地里了。现在就完全看不到他了。他连休息都没法休息好，真是太可怜了。” “He’s a-walking in his sleep.I wish we was allowed to follow him and see where he’s going to.There, he’s turned down by the tobacker-field.Out of sight now.It’s a dreadful pity he can’t rest no better.”
我们等了很久，可最后他还是没有回来，或者他真的回来的话，那肯定是从其他路走了。所以最后我们俩都筋疲力尽，便径直去睡觉，期间还做了无数个噩梦。但在黎明破晓前我们又醒了过来，一场暴风雨突然来袭，可怕的电闪雷鸣让人不寒而栗。狂风肆虐，狠狠地摇晃着大树，大雨滂沱，水沟里溢出的雨水在地面上汇聚成条条的细流。汤姆说： We waited a long time, but he didn’t come back any more, or if he did he come around the other way; so at last we was tuckered out and went to sleep and had nightmares, a million of them.But before dawn we was awake again, because meantime a storm had come up and been raging, and the thunder and lightning was awful, and the wind was a-thrashing the trees around, and the rain was driving down in slanting sheets, and the gullies was running rivers.Tom says:
“哈克，快看，我等会告诉你一件很奇怪的事。在我们昨晚出来前，家里人都还不知道杰克·邓拉普已经被谋杀了。可现在那个在追哈尔·克莱顿和巴德·迪克逊的人只需半小时就能把这条消息传播开，到时候所有听说这个事情的街坊邻居都会争相走动于各个农场，争取第一时间把这个消息告诉给他人。天啊，他们已经快三十年没碰到这么爆炸性的新闻了！哈克，这很奇怪，我就是没法理解。” “Looky here, Huck, I’ll tell you one thing that’s mighty curious.Up to the time we went out last night the family hadn’t heard about Jake Dunlap being murdered.Now the men that chased Hal Clayton and Bud Dixon away would spread the thing around in a half an hour, and every neighbor that heard it would shin out and fly around from one farm to t’other and try to be the first to tell the news.Land, they don’t have such a big thing as that to tell twice in thirty year!Huck, it’s mighty strange; I don’t understand it.”
雨在慢慢减弱，而他越发显得坐立不安。这时我们也可以出动去看看是否能遇上些什么人，看看他们会不会跟我们说一些和那有关的事情。他还说，如果他们确实跟我们说这事，我们必须表现得很震惊。 So then he was in a fidget for the rain to let up, so we could turn out and run across some of the people and see if they would say anything about it to us.And he said if they did we must be horribly surprised and shocked.
雨一停下来，我们就离开了。后来天气就很好了。我们在路上闲逛时，总会不时地遇到一些人。然后就会停下和他们打打招呼，告诉他们我们是什么时候来的，还会呆多久之类的事，但是并没有人向我们提到那件事。这倒是让我们很吃惊，但这也在情理之内。汤姆说他相信如果现在去悬铃木那里，还是会看到那具尸体孤零零地躺在那里，周围一个人都没有。他还说他相信追那两个贼的人可能跑到丛林深处时，被贼抓住了机会，反过来被解决掉了，很可能最后是两败俱伤没有一个活下来，所以到现在还没有人把这事传开。 We was out and gone the minute the rain stopped.It was just broad day then.We loafed along up the road, and now and then met a person and stopped and said howdy, and told them when we come, and how we left the folks at home, and how long we was going to stay, and all that, but none of them said a word about that thing; which was just astonishing, and no mistake.Tom said he believed if we went to the sycamores we would find that body laying there solitary and alone, and not a soul around.Said he believed the men chased the thieves so far into the woods that the thieves prob’ly seen a good chance and turned on them at last, and maybe they all killed each other, and so there wasn’t anybody left to tell.
他一路上一直念叨着，如果我们真的是最早知道这事的人，那么我们对于现在悬铃木丛情况的判断就应该没错。这时一股股寒气在我的背部流淌，如果不是汤姆一直劝我，我几乎都迈不开步子了。但他还是没法控制住自己；他还是想去看看到底那双靴子是否还完好地穿在他身上。所以他悄悄地往里面走——过了一会儿，他出来了，看起来很兴奋，瞪大着眼睛说： First we knowed, gabbling along that away, we was right at the sycamores.The cold chills trickled down my back and I wouldn’t budge another step, for all Tom’s persuading.But he couldn’t hold in; he’d GOT to see if the boots was safe on that body yet.So he crope in—and the next minute out he come again with his eyes bulging he was so excited, and says:
“哈克，他不见了！” “Huck, it’s gone!”
这让我惊讶！我说： I WAS astonished!I says:
“汤姆，你不是当真的吧。” “Tom, you don’t mean it.”
“他真的不见了。这里他没留下一点儿痕迹。　　这里的地面像有被人踏过。如果这里有任何血迹的话，早已经被这场暴风雨洗干净了，这里现在只剩下些水坑和稀泥浆了。” “It’s gone, sure.There ain’t a sign of it.The ground is trampled some, but if there was any blood it’s all washed away by the storm, for it’s all puddles and slush in there.”
最后我被说服了，亲自走过去看了看。结果正如汤姆所说的那样——这里没有任何尸体的痕迹。 At last I give in, and went and took a look myself; and it was just as Tom said—there wasn’t a sign of a corpse.
“天啊”，我说，“钻石不见了。汤姆，难道你就没想过那些贼会偷偷溜回来把他拖走？” “Dern it,” I says, “the di’monds is gone.Don’t you reckon the thieves slunk back and lugged him off, Tom?”
“事情有可能是这样的。假设事情就是这样吧。那你觉得他们现在会把他藏哪儿呢？” “Looks like it.It just does.Now where’d they hide him, do you reckon?”
一想到尸体，我便反感地说：“我不知道。再说我才不想管它。我关心的就是他们已经把靴子抢走了。在我把他找出来之前，他可能已经在这片树林里某处躺了足够长时间了。” “I don’t know,” I says, disgusted, “and what’s more I don’t care.They’ve got the boots, and that’s all I cared about.He’ll lay around these woods a long time before I hunt him up.”
汤姆现在就只想知道他的下落，其他的他一概不感兴趣了。他说我们现在要低调并且潜伏起来，总会有那么一天，嗅觉灵敏的狗或者什么人会发现他。　 Tom didn’t feel no more intrust in him neither, only curiosity to know what come of him; but he said we’d lay low and keep dark and it wouldn’t be long till the dogs or somebody rousted him out.
后来我们便回去吃早餐，整个早餐期间。都觉得前所未有的不安和失望。　　而在此之前，我也从未因为一具尸体而那么情绪低落过。 We went back home to breakfast ever so bothered and put out and disappointed and swindled.I warn’t ever so down on a corpse before.

我们吃早饭时都打不起精神。萨莉婶婶似乎变老了，而且看起来很疲惫。她放任着孩子们不管，随他们喊叫和大吵大闹，似乎并不关心他们在做什么，可这并不是她以往的风格；汤姆和我即使不说话，还是有很多事情等着我们去思考。本尼看上像没有睡够，不论她什么时候抬起头偷瞄她爸爸，你总能看到她的眼角闪烁着泪光；至于那个老头子，盘子里的食物他压根儿就没碰过，我猜他可能连自己都不知道盘子里有食物。由于他老是在想事情，所以他几乎没说话，甚至没尝一下食物。　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　 It warn’t very cheerful at breakfast.Aunt Sally she looked old and tired and let the children snarl and fuss at one another and didn’t seem to notice it was going on, which wasn’t her usual style; me and Tom had a plenty to think about without talking; Benny she looked like she hadn’t had much sleep, and whenever she’d lift her head a little and steal a look towards her father you could see there was tears in her eyes; and as for the old man, his things stayed on his plate and got cold without him knowing they was there, I reckon, for he was thinking and thinking all the time, and never said a word and never et a bite.
过了一会儿，就在房间里最安静的时候，那个黑人又在门口探了一下头，他小心谨慎地说他的主人布雷斯正因为主人丘比特而万分焦虑，因为到现在他还没回家，烦请主人赛拉斯——他一看到赛拉斯叔叔，就说不出话了，好像剩下的话都被卡住一样。只见赛拉斯叔叔颤抖地站了起来，用手指顶在桌上以稳住自己，然后盯着那个黑人，不断地在倒吸冷气，还好几次把另一只手抬到他的咽喉处以使自己平息，最后他开始说话了，吼道：　　　 By and by when it was stillest, that nigger’s head was poked in at the door again, and he said his Marse Brace was getting powerful uneasy about Marse Jubiter, which hadn’t come home yet, and would Marse Silas please—He was looking at Uncle Silas, and he stopped there, like the rest of his words was froze; for Uncle Silas he rose up shaky and steadied himself leaning his fingers on the table, and he was panting, and his eyes was set on the nigger, and he kept swallowing, and put his other hand up to his throat a couple of times, and at last he got his words started, and says:
“他——他——他怎么想就怎么做吧！告诉他——告诉他——”接着他便瘫坐在他的椅子上，看上去毫无精神又虚弱无力，接着又喊道：“快滚——快滚！”可这次声音几乎虚弱得听不到了。 “Does he—does he—think—WHAT does he think!Tell him—tell him—” Then he sunk down in his chair limp and weak, and says, so as you could hardly hear him: “Go away—go away!”
那个黑人似乎被吓坏了，马上就消失的无影无踪，这让我们感觉到——事实上我也并不知道我们到底是什么感觉，但难受极了，那个老头子还是呼吸急促，大口地喘气，他的眼神和将死之人一样。此时没有一个人敢走动；只有本尼缓缓地走近他身边，流着泪。她搂着他白发苍苍的头，用手抚摸着，充满了慈爱；她向我们点点头示意我们先离开，我们于是先离开了，轻声地走出了房间，就好像不想惊动屋里的死者那样。 The nigger looked scared and cleared out, and we all felt—well, I don’t know how we felt, but it was awful, with the old man panting there, and his eyes set and looking like a person that was dying.None of us could budge; but Benny she slid around soft, with her tears running down, and stood by his side, and nestled his old gray head up against her and begun to stroke it and pet it with her hands, and nodded to us to go away, and we done it, going out very quiet, like the dead was there.
汤姆和我都想回归树林去享受片刻的安静；我们都在感叹，这里的变化真大。去年夏天我们在这里时，一切都那么安详，那么快乐，每个人都很喜欢赛拉斯叔叔，他曾是一个简单善良而又快乐的人，甚至还带着点儿傻气呢——你再看看现在的他。如果他并没有疯掉，那也快变成那样了。我们就是这么想的。 Me and Tom struck out for the woods mighty solemn, and saying how different it was now to what it was last summer when we was here and everything was so peaceful and happy and everybody thought so much of Uncle Silas, and he was so cheerful and simple-hearted and pudd’n-headed and good—and now look at him.If he hadn’t lost his mind he wasn’t much short of it.That was what we allowed.
今天阳光明媚，天气很好；我们穿过山峦，往大草原方向走得越远，我们就越觉得花草树木越发美丽，在一个如此美丽的世界里，竟然会被麻烦困住，真是无比奇怪。　 突然间我屏住呼吸，抓住汤姆的手，感觉我的五脏六腑都蹦出体外了。　 It was a most lovely day now, and bright and sunshiny; and the further and further we went over the hills towards the prairie the lovelier and lovelier the trees and flowers got to be and the more it seemed strange and somehow wrong that there had to be trouble in such a world as this.And then all of a sudden I catched my breath and grabbed Tom’s arm, and all my livers and lungs and things fell down into my legs.
“就在这儿！”我喊道。我们跳到一堆灌木丛背后直打哆嗦，然后汤姆说： “There it is!” I says.We jumped back behind a bush shivering, and Tom says:
“嘘！——别出声。” “Sh!—don’t make a noise.”
我想，声音是从这小片草地边上的一块木头上发出来的。我试着叫汤姆离开，但他不同意，于是我自己也没走。他说可能不会再有第二次机会可以瞧见这样的东西。就算会有丧命的危险，他也要去探个究竟好满足他的好奇心。因此我也跟过去看了，尽管觉得自己就像跟屁虫一样。汤姆此时必须得说话，但他只是低声在说。他说道： It was setting on a log right in the edge of a little prairie, thinking.I tried to get Tom to come away, but he wouldn’t, and I dasn’t budge by myself.He said we mightn’t ever get another chance to see one, and he was going to look his fill at this one if he died for it.So I looked too, though it give me the fan-tods to do it.Tom he HAD to talk, but he talked low.He says:
“可怜的杰克，就像他所说的那样，他已经把他准备好的所有东西都穿上了。现在你应该知道我们不确定的就是——他的头发。他看起来离这里不会很远：正如他之前所说的那样，他已经把他的头发都剪短了，几乎快剪成光头了。哈克，我从没见过任何东西能比它还逼真。” “Poor Jakey, it’s got all its things on, just as he said he would.NOW you see what we wasn’t certain about—its hair.It’s not long now the way it was: it’s got it cropped close to its head, the way he said he would.Huck, I never see anything look any more naturaler than what It does.”
我答道：“是的，我也从没见过能比这更逼真的东西了。不论在哪里，我都认得出它。” “Nor I neither,” I says; “I’d recognize it anywheres.”

他似乎忘了自己正在讲话，便一路独自踏着重重的步伐，边走边沉思。他又开始兴奋起来，很快便说： He forgot he was talking, and went tramping along, studying.He begun to get excited again, and pretty soon he says:
“哈克，在大家都放弃寻找的情况下，如果我们能找到那具尸体，而且还能进一步抓到凶手，这会是一件非常美妙的事情。这不仅仅对我们而言意味着荣耀，赛拉斯叔叔也会觉得很骄傲，因为这些事都是我们做的。这会让他再次得到大家的尊重，如果不信的话，到时你就会明白。”　　　 “Huck, it’ll be the bulliest thing that ever happened if we find the body after everybody else has quit looking, and then go ahead and hunt up the murderer.It won’t only be an honor to us, but it’ll be an honor to Uncle Silas because it was us that done it.It’ll set him up again, you see if it don’t.”
可当我们到了老杰夫·胡克的铁匠铺并告诉他我们的来意时，他居然对我们的全盘计划泼冷水。　　　　　　　　 But Old Jeff Hooker he throwed cold water on the whole business when we got to his blacksmith shop and told him what we come for.
他说：“你可以把这条狗带走，但你不会找到任何尸体的，因为这儿根本就没有尸体。每个人都放弃搜寻尸体了，他们都是对的。他们一想，就很快意识到这里并不存在什么尸体。我马上会告诉你这到底是为什么。汤姆·索耶——你回答我，一个人杀另一个人的动机是什么？” “You can take the dog,” he says, “but you ain’t a-going to find any corpse, because there ain’t any corpse to find.Everybody’s quit looking, and they’re right.Soon as they come to think, they knowed there warn’t no corpse.And I’ll tell you for why.What does a person kill another person for, Tom Sawyer?—answer me that.”
“为什么呢，他——” “Why, he—er—”
“快回答！你又不是傻瓜。他到底是因何要杀他呢？” “Answer up!You ain’t no fool.What does he kill him FOR?”
“那个，有些时候可能是因为复仇，和——” “Well, sometimes it’s for revenge, and—”
“等会儿。一次一个想法就可以了。你刚说复仇；如果你是正确的话。那么有谁想从这个微不足道、一无是处的可怜虫身上得到些东西呢？你觉得是谁想要杀那个该死的家伙呢？——那个窝囊废！” “Wait.One thing at a time.Revenge, says you; and right you are.Now who ever had anything agin that poor trifling no-account?Who do you reckon would want to kill HIM?—that rabbit!”
汤姆被这个问题问住了。我认为他之前应该从未想过一个人还必须要有动机才会去杀人，而现在他明白了不可能有任何人会对像丘比特·邓拉普这样温顺的人结下深仇大恨。过了一会儿，那个铁匠说： Tom was stuck.I reckon he hadn’t thought of a person having to have a REASON for killing a person before, and now he sees it warn’t likely anybody would have that much of a grudge against a lamb like Jubiter Dunlap.The blacksmith says, by and by:
“你应该也明白了这个寻仇的想法是站不住脚的吧。那好，下一个动机会是什么呢？抢劫？天啊，汤姆，一定是为了抢劫！是的，先生们，我想这回我们应该对了吧。有些家伙想得到他裤子背带上的搭钩，所以他——” “The revenge idea won’t work, you see.Well, then, what’s next?Robbery?B’gosh, that must ’a’ been it, Tom!Yes, sirree, I reckon we’ve struck it this time.Some feller wanted his gallus-buckles, and so he—”
但让人莫名其妙的是，他忽然间开始大笑起来，而且一直持续这个样子，直到他似乎要笑死时才停了下来，而汤姆看起来有些不太高兴。我知道他开始觉得为来这里而难为情，希望自己没来过。　　　　　　　　　但老胡克可没停止羞辱他。他重提一个人会谋杀另一个人的每种动机，即使是任何傻瓜也都看得出没有任何动机符合这件事。他其实就是想无休止地取笑整件事，以及这次所有参与搜索尸体的人；后来他说： But it was so funny he busted out laughing, and just went on laughing and laughing and laughing till he was ‘most dead, and Tom looked so put out and cheap that I knowed he was ashamed he had come, and he wished he hadn’t.But old Hooker never let up on him.He raked up everything a person ever could want to kill another person about, and any fool could see they didn’t any of them fit this case, and he just made no end of fun of the whole business and of the people that had been hunting the body; and he said:
“如果他们有动脑子的话，他们应当知道那个懒散的家伙只不过是溜走了，因为在干完所有这些活后，他不过是想去外面闲荡一段时间罢了。几周内他应该就会闲逛回来，那么现在你们觉得怎么样呢？但是，但愿上帝保佑你们，把狗带走吧，去找他身上的残留物吧。汤姆，去吧。” “If they’d had any sense they’d ’a’ knowed the lazy cuss slid out because he wanted a loafing spell after all this work.He’ll come pottering back in a couple of weeks, and then how’ll you fellers feel?But, laws bless you, take the dog, and go and hunt his remainders.Do, Tom.”
然后他突然又爆发出一阵狂笑，持续了好几分钟。刚刚老胡克所作的这些不可能让汤姆放弃，所以他说：“好了，松开它吧”；铁匠就把它给松开了，此后我们就开始启程回去，而那老头子还在那里狂笑不止。 Then he busted out, and had another of them forty-rod laughs of hisn.Tom couldn’t back down after all this, so he said, “All right, unchain him;” and the blacksmith done it, and we started home and left that old man laughing yet.
这是一条可爱的狗。这里没有别的狗会比寻血犬更讨人喜欢了，而这条狗看上去不仅知晓我们，还喜欢我们俩。它十分雀跃地打着转，很友善，显然对这次获得解放，能享受一次假期而感到十分开心。但汤姆则很难过，对它根本提不起任何兴趣。他说要是当初在干这件蠢事之前能三思就好了。他还说老杰夫·胡克会把这个事到处传，而我们却都还蒙在鼓里。 It was a lovely dog.There ain’t any dog that’s got a lovelier disposition than a bloodhound, and this one knowed us and liked us.He capered and raced around ever so friendly, and powerful glad to be free and have a holiday; but Tom was so cut up he couldn’t take any intrust in him, and said he wished he’d stopped and thought a minute before he ever started on such a fool errand.He said old Jeff Hooker would tell everybody, and we’d never hear the last of it.
所以我们一路从小路闲逛回家，心里闷闷不乐的，也没怎么讲话。我们经过烟草地远端的角落时，只听见那条狗长嚎一声。当我们到达那儿时，寻血犬就在地上使劲挖，又时不时地侧着他的脑袋高声嚎叫。 So we loafed along home down the back lanes, feeling pretty glum and not talking.When we was passing the far corner of our tobacker field we heard the dog set up a long howl in there, and we went to the place and he was scratching the ground with all his might, and every now and then canting up his head sideways and fetching another howl.
这是一块较长的正方形，像墓地一样的地方；而大雨则让它下陷，慢慢显露出了形状。等我们一到达，站在那里时，我们看着对方，一句话都说不出来。当狗仅仅挖了几英寸时，他似乎抓住了什么东西往上拉，事实上那是一只手臂和一条袖子。汤姆喘着气，好不容易说出几个字： It was a long square, the shape of a grave; the rain had made it sink down and show the shape.The minute we come and stood there we looked at one another and never said a word.When the dog had dug down only a few inches he grabbed something and pulled it up, and it was an arm and a sleeve.Tom kind of gasped out, and says:
“哈克，快过来——找到了。” “Come away, Huck—it’s found.”
那个时候我觉得太可怕了。我们冲上大路，把路上几个行人拉了过来。他们从食槽里拿了把锹并把尸体挖了出来，你永远也不会看到那么激动人心的时刻。从尸体的脸上根本判定不出什么了，但是我们也不需要这么去做。每个人都叹道： I just felt awful.We struck for the road and fetched the first men that come along.They got a spade at the crib and dug out the body, and you never see such an excitement.You couldn’t make anything out of the face, but you didn’t need to.Everybody said:
“可怜的丘比特；这就是他衣服最后的碎片！” “Poor Jubiter; it’s his clothes, to the last rag!”
有些人赶紧把这个事情传开，去告诉治安官并进行调查，而我和汤姆则迅速回家。当我们冲进屋子里时，赛拉斯叔叔、萨莉婶婶和本尼都在那儿，当时汤姆激动极了，几乎快喘不过起来了。汤姆大喊： Some rushed off to spread the news and tell the justice of the peace and have an inquest, and me and Tom lit out for the house.Tom was all afire and ’most out of breath when we come tearing in where Uncle Silas and Aunt Sally and Benny was.Tom sung out:
“在所有人都退出并放弃寻找尸体后，是我和哈克完全靠我们自己的力量用寻血犬找到丘比特·邓拉普的；如果不是我们俩，尸体可能永远也不会被找到；而他恰恰就是被谋杀的——他们就是用棍棒或者其他类似的东西杀掉他的；接下来，我就要准备去找出凶手，我敢肯定我能做到！” “Me and Huck’s found Jubiter Dunlap’s corpse all by ourselves with a bloodhound, after everybody else had quit hunting and given it up; and if it hadn’t a been for us it never WOULD ’a’ been found; and he WAS murdered too—they done it with a club or something like that; and I’m going to start in and find the murderer, next, and I bet I’ll do it!”
此刻萨莉婶婶和本尼被吓得脸色突然间都变苍白了，而赛拉斯叔叔则直接从他的椅子上摔了下来，呻吟道： Aunt Sally and Benny sprung up pale and astonished, but Uncle Silas fell right forward out of his chair on to the floor and groans out:
“噢，我的上帝，你居然现在找到他了！” “Oh, my God, you’ve found him NOW!”

他这些可怕的话让我们都完全僵住了。在将近半分钟内，我们的手脚都几乎不能动弹。后来我们稍微清醒了点儿，然后去把老头子扶了起来，让他坐回他的椅子上，而本尼和萨莉婶婶则想通过爱抚和吻来安慰他；可是那个老头子，这消息几乎让他目瞪口呆，他感到十分害怕，他也几乎不知道他们在做什么。对汤姆而言，这太糟糕了；他被吓到了，甚至觉得是他让他的叔叔陷入了比以往严重几千倍的麻烦当中，如果他没这么野心勃勃地想出名，如果他不曾掺和到寻找那具尸体的事件中，这些可能都不会发生了。但很快，他再次回过神来，并说： Them awful words froze us solid.We couldn’t move hand or foot for as much as half a minute.Then we kind of come to, and lifted the old man up and got him into his chair, and Benny petted him and kissed him and tried to comfort him, and poor old Aunt Sally she done the same; but, poor things, they was so broke up and scared and knocked out of their right minds that they didn’t hardly know what they was about.With Tom it was awful; it ’most petrified him to think maybe he had got his uncle into a thousand times more trouble than ever, and maybe it wouldn’t ever happened if he hadn’t been so ambitious to get celebrated, and let the corpse alone the way the others done.But pretty soon he sort of come to himself again and says:
“赛拉斯叔叔，不要再说那样的话了。这很危险，那些都不是真的。” “Uncle Silas, don’t you say another word like that.It’s dangerous, and there ain’t a shadder of truth in it.”
萨莉婶婶和本尼听到他这么说觉得很欣慰，她们俩也都这么说；但此时泪珠从老头子的脸颊上滚落了下来，他伤心而又无助地摇着头说： Aunt Sally and Benny was thankful to hear him say that, and they said the same; but the old man he wagged his head sorrowful and hopeless, and the tears run down his face, and he says;
“不——是我干的；可怜的丘比特是我杀的！” “No—I done it; poor Jubiter, I done it!”
他这么说把我们都吓呆了。他又接着说，这事就发生在我和汤姆来的那天——那时快要日落了。他说丘比特一直在纠缠着他，他被激怒了，简直快发疯了；他抓起一把棍子往丘比特头上使劲地打了一下，随后他便倒下了。后来他吓坏了并觉得十分抱歉，便蹲下来把他的头抬起来，祈求他说话并告诉他还活着；不久后他也就醒过来，而当他看到赛拉斯叔叔正抱着他的头，他就像快被吓死一样跳了起来，越过栅栏，冲进树林里，此后便不见踪影。他只能祈祷他没有受太严重的伤。 It was dreadful to hear him say it.Then he went on and told about it, and said it happened the day me and Tom come—along about sundown.He said Jubiter pestered him and aggravated him till he was so mad he just sort of lost his mind and grabbed up a stick and hit him over the head with all his might, and Jubiter dropped in his tracks.Then he was scared and sorry, and got down on his knees and lifted his head up, and begged him to speak and say he wasn’t dead; and before long he come to, and when he see who it was holding his head, he jumped like he was ‘most scared to death, and cleared the fence and tore into the woods, and was gone.So he hoped he wasn’t hurt bad.
他说：“上帝啊，其实是恐惧让他迸发出自己剩下的一丁点儿力量逃跑，当然，他最后筋疲力尽地躺在灌木丛中，当时没有人可以帮他，所以他就死了。” “But laws,” he says, “it was only just fear that gave him that last little spurt of strength, and of course it soon played out and he laid down in the bush, and there wasn’t anybody to help him, and he died.”
后来老头子非常难过，泣不成声地说他就是凶手，他有罪，他给自己的家庭丢脸了，警察很快就会找到他并将他处死。　　　　可是汤姆说： Then the old man cried and grieved, and said he was a murderer and the mark of Cain was on him, and he had disgraced his family and was going to be found out and hung.But Tom said:
“不，你不会被找到的。你并没有杀他。一棍打下去是不可能将他杀掉的。这是其他人干的。” “No, you ain’t going to be found out.You DIDN’T kill him.ONE lick wouldn’t kill him.Somebody else done it.”
“噢，是的，”他应道，“是我干的——不是其他人做的。除了我以外，有谁会与他有仇呢？又有哪个人可能会和他有仇呢？” “Oh, yes,” he says, “I done it—nobody else.Who else had anything against him?Who else COULD have anything against him?”
他抬头看着我们，那样子似乎在希望着我们能说出一个与这个无辜而微不足道的家伙有仇的人的名字。但当然这样也无济于事，我们一个字都说不出来。他注意到了目前的情况，又再度陷入悲伤之中，我从没见过一张这么不幸而又令人生怜的脸。汤姆突然间有了个主意，说道： He looked up kind of like he hoped some of us could mention somebody that could have a grudge against that harmless no-account, but of course it warn’t no use—he HAD us; we couldn’t say a word.He noticed that, and he saddened down again, and I never see a face so miserable and so pitiful to see.Tom had a sudden idea, and says:
“但是等一下！——是其他人把他埋掉的。那么会是谁——” “But hold on!—somebody BURIED him.Now who—”
他突然间不说话了。我知道这是什么原因。当他讲这话时，我着实打了个冷战，因为那时我马上想起那天深夜我们看到赛拉斯叔叔拿着长柄的铲子在蹑足行走。而我知道本尼也看到他了，因为有一天她有在说这件事。就在汤姆停止讲话时，他赶紧转换了话题，恳求赛拉斯叔叔不要说出去，而我们其余的人也在帮着腔。我们劝赛拉斯叔叔不要声张，因为这不关他的事。倘若他不说出去，就不会有人会知道这件事情，但如果这件事情被查出来了，那么给他带来的任何伤害都将让这个家庭心碎，就相当于也杀了他们。这对大家都没有任何好处。所以最终他答应了我们。我们都松了一口气放心下来了，于是便开始努力让老头子提起精神来。我们告诉他目前他所需要做的就是保持冷静，不用多久这个事情就会平息并很快会被人淡忘。我们都说这里不会有人会去怀疑赛拉斯叔叔，也不会有人会去这么想，因为他一直都是这么友好，品格高尚，“而且为人热忱”，汤姆应和道： He shut off sudden.I knowed the reason.It give me the cold shudders when he said them words, because right away I remembered about us seeing Uncle Silas prowling around with a long-handled shovel away in the night that night.And I knowed Benny seen him, too, because she was talking about it one day.The minute Tom shut off he changed the subject and went to begging Uncle Silas to keep mum, and the rest of us done the same, and said he MUST, and said it wasn’t his business to tell on himself, and if he kept mum nobody would ever know; but if it was found out and any harm come to him it would break the family’s hearts and kill them, and yet never do anybody any good.So at last he promised.We was all of us more comfortable, then, and went to work to cheer up the old man.We told him all he’d got to do was to keep still, and it wouldn’t be long till the whole thing would blow over and be forgot.We all said there wouldn’t anybody ever suspect Uncle Silas, nor ever dream of such a thing, he being so good and kind, and having such a good character; and Tom says, cordial and hearty, he says:
“为什么呢，就想一会儿，想一下。这就是赛拉斯叔叔，一位资深教士，从没有花过人们一分钱；这些年他总是尽其所能地为大家做好事，所有的支出都是靠他自己；大家一直都很爱戴他；他也一直恪尽职守，待人温和，是这整个教区唯一一个能让大家都敞开心扉倾诉的人，这是众所周知的。怀疑他？当然不，不可能有比——” “Why, just look at it a minute; just consider.Here is Uncle Silas, all these years a preacher—at his own expense; all these years doing good with all his might and every way he can think of—at his own expense, all the time; always been loved by everybody, and respected; always been peaceable and minding his own business, the very last man in this whole deestrict to touch a person, and everybody knows it.Suspect HIM?Why, it ain’t any more possible than—”
“经阿肯色州当局批准，你因涉嫌谋杀丘比特·邓拉普被捕了。”门外传来了治安警察的叫唤声。 “By authority of the State of Arkansaw, I arrest you for the murder of Jubiter Dunlap!” shouts the sheriff at the door.
这真是糟透了。这时萨莉婶婶和本尼都拥在赛拉斯叔叔身上，哭喊着；她们紧紧地抱着他，萨莉婶婶喊着让他们离开，她不会放手的，而他们也不要妄想能带他走；黑仆们也挤上来对着门口哭——哎，我也有些承受不住了，这个场景足以让人心碎。于是我走了出去。 It was awful.Aunt Sally and Benny flung themselves at Uncle Silas, screaming and crying, and hugged him and hung to him, and Aunt Sally said go away, she wouldn’t ever give him up, they shouldn’t have him, and the niggers they come crowding and crying to the door and—well, I couldn’t stand it; it was enough to break a person’s heart; so I got out.
他们把他关在村里的一个小监狱里，我们都一起去和他道别；此时汤姆十分庄严地告诉我：“哈克，我们将会有很多时间，也会碰到很多危险在某个黑夜把他救出去。这事将会被广为流传，到那时我们就会出名。”但就在他低声告诉我那刻，老头子就把我们的方案给喊出来了。他说这不行，不管法律对他如何惩罚，他都必须承担，这是他的义务，即使这里没有门，他也会一直呆在监狱里直到最后把他放出去。这让汤姆很失望，也让他很气恼，但他必须得忍受住。 They took him up to the little one-horse jail in the village, and we all went along to tell him good-bye; and Tom was feeling elegant, and says to me, “We’ll have a most noble good time and heaps of danger some dark night getting him out of there, Huck, and it’ll be talked about everywheres and we will be celebrated;” but the old man busted that scheme up the minute he whispered to him about it.He said no, it was his duty to stand whatever the law done to him, and he would stick to the jail plumb through to the end, even if there warn’t no door to it.It disappointed Tom and graveled him a good deal, but he had to put up with it.
但是他觉得他有责任，也一定要让赛拉斯叔叔重获自由；他告诉萨莉婶婶，不要再担心了，因为他会去上诉，然后会没日没夜地调查，找出真凶，最终让赛拉斯叔叔无罪释放；听到汤姆这么说，萨莉婶婶很感动，也很感激，她说她知道汤姆一定会尽全力做好的。她还告诉我们要帮助本尼看好屋子，照顾好孩子们，最后当我们要分别时都抱在一起哭泣，后来我们回到了农场，而萨莉婶婶则要留在监狱里一个月陪着赛拉斯叔叔直到十月份的审判。 But he felt responsible and bound to get his uncle Silas free; and he told Aunt Sally, the last thing, not to worry, because he was going to turn in and work night and day and beat this game and fetch Uncle Silas out innocent; and she was very loving to him and thanked him and said she knowed he would do his very best.And she told us to help Benny take care of the house and the children, and then we had a good-bye cry all around and went back to the farm, and left her there to live with the jailer’s wife a month till the trial in October.

唉，这样的一个月对我们来说都是一种煎熬。可怜的本尼，努力撑着保持好状态，而我和汤姆也一直尝试着让整个屋子的气氛轻松快乐起来，但是很明显那丝毫不起作用。在监狱里的情况也是一样的。每天我们起床后都会去看老头子，但气氛总是很压抑，因为赛拉斯叔叔没怎么睡，而且在睡觉时又常起来梦游，所以他看上去很疲劳，让人觉得很难过，而他也变得神志不清，我们都很怕这件事情会彻底打垮他，甚至会杀了他。每当我们劝他打起精神来的时候，他也只是摇着头说，如果我们也有一个谋杀犯的罪名扣在头上，我们就不会这样说话了。汤姆和我们所有人都一直告诉他那根本不是谋杀，只不过是过失杀人！但我们的劝说对他一点儿效果都没有——那就是谋杀，他始终坚持这样认为。实际上他已经开始直接又坚决地表示，到时在庭审的时候他会承认他有预谋要杀那个人。为什么要这样，你应该知道这样情况会很糟糕。这让事情变得糟糕五十倍，也让萨莉婶婶和本尼更加惶恐不安。但他答应我们在其他人在场时，他不会对他们透漏任何有关他谋杀的话，这倒是让我们高兴了一下。 Well, that was a hard month on us all.Poor Benny, she kept up the best she could, and me and Tom tried to keep things cheerful there at the house, but it kind of went for nothing, as you may say.It was the same up at the jail.We went up every day to see the old people, but it was awful dreary, because the old man warn’t sleeping much, and was walking in his sleep considerable and so he got to looking fagged and miserable, and his mind got shaky, and we all got afraid his troubles would break him down and kill him.And whenever we tried to persuade him to feel cheerfuler, he only shook his head and said if we only knowed what it was to carry around a murderer’s load in your heart we wouldn’t talk that way.Tom and all of us kept telling him it WASN’T murder, but just accidental killing! but it never made any difference—it was murder, and he wouldn’t have it any other way.He actu’ly begun to come out plain and square towards trial time and acknowledge that he TRIED to kill the man.Why, that was awful, you know.It made things seem fifty times as dreadful, and there warn’t no more comfort for Aunt Sally and Benny.But he promised he wouldn’t say a word about his murder when others was around, and we was glad of that.
整整一个月来，汤姆·索耶一直在绞尽脑汁看看能否想出办法救出赛拉斯叔叔，好多个晚上他都把我叫醒，然后几乎整夜在做这个累人的工作，但他看上去并没有找到正确的方法。就我而言，我想我们可能都已经放弃了，因为这一切看起来都那么令人失望，我的情绪也很低落；可是他从来都不会。他毫无懈怠地关注着案子的进展，不停地谋划着，思考着，竭尽所能地在想办法。 Tom Sawyer racked the head off of himself all that month trying to plan some way out for Uncle Silas, and many’s the night he kept me up ‘most all night with this kind of tiresome work, but he couldn’t seem to get on the right track no way.As for me, I reckoned a body might as well give it up, it all looked so blue and I was so downhearted; but he wouldn’t.He stuck to the business right along, and went on planning and thinking and ransacking his head.
最后在十月中旬，庭审还是来了，我们都在法庭上出席了。当然，那个地方很拥挤。可怜的赛拉斯叔叔看上去更像一个死人而不是大活人，他的双眼深深的凹陷，看起来十分瘦削，让人好悲痛。本尼和萨莉婶婶分别坐在赛拉斯叔叔的两侧，她们都戴着面纱，处于深深的忧愁之中。而汤姆坐在我们的律师身旁，对案子的每个细节，他都理所应当地掺和着发表意见。律师允许他这么做，法官也允许他这么做。他有时几乎把律师手头的活都揽过来自己做了；这样也好，因为那只是个资历尚浅的律师，正如俗语说的，干事没心眼，缺乏自辩能力。 So at last the trial come on, towards the middle of October, and we was all in the court.The place was jammed, of course.Poor old Uncle Silas, he looked more like a dead person than a live one, his eyes was so hollow and he looked so thin and so mournful.Benny she set on one side of him and Aunt Sally on the other, and they had veils on, and was full of trouble.But Tom he set by our lawyer, and had his finger in everywheres, of course.The lawyer let him, and the judge let him.He ‘most took the business out of the lawyer’s hands sometimes; which was well enough, because that was only a mud-turtle of a back-settlement lawyer and didn’t know enough to come in when it rains, as the saying is.
他们在陪审团面前一起宣了誓，然后控方律师先站起来，开始陈词。他对着老头子作了一段很糟糕的演说，这不仅让赛拉斯叔叔不断呻吟，也让本尼和萨莉婶婶都哭了。他描述的这起谋杀案让我们都十分震惊，就像把我们都当成了傻瓜，因为那和老头子描述的完全不一样。他说他有两个目击证人亲眼见到赛拉斯叔叔杀了丘比特·邓拉普，而且是蓄意谋杀，他说赛拉斯叔叔就是想要一棍子打死丘比特，目击者还看到他把丘比特藏在灌木丛里，那个时候丘比特已经完全断气了。他声称在晚些时候，赛拉斯叔叔又把他拖进烟草地里，这些都被两个人看到了。他宣称事实证明赛拉斯叔叔在晚上把丘比特埋掉了，而这也有证人。 They swore in the jury, and then the lawyer for the prostitution got up and begun.He made a terrible speech against the old man, that made him moan and groan, and made Benny and Aunt Sally cry.The way HE told about the murder kind of knocked us all stupid it was so different from the old man’s tale.He said he was going to prove that Uncle Silas was SEEN to kill Jubiter Dunlap by two good witnesses, and done it deliberate, and SAID he was going to kill him the very minute he hit him with the club; and they seen him hide Jubiter in the bushes, and they seen that Jubiter was stone-dead.And said Uncle Silas come later and lugged Jubiter down into the tobacker field, and two men seen him do it.And said Uncle Silas turned out, away in the night, and buried Jubiter, and a man seen him at it.
我心想，可怜的老赛拉斯叔叔恐怕对大家撒谎了，他以为不会有人看到，而且他也不想伤萨莉婶婶和本尼的心；他这么做是可以理解的，如果是我的话，我也会撒同样的谎，好让大家都好受一些，不至于让大家都承受痛苦和折磨，因为他们不该对这起谋杀罪负责。这番话让我们的律师很不自在；这也让汤姆吓了好一会儿，但很快他又抖擞精神，装作一点儿都不担心的样子——但即使这样，我也很清楚他还是很担心。　 这在人群中引起了一阵骚动！ I says to myself, poor old Uncle Silas has been lying about it because he reckoned nobody seen him and he couldn’t bear to break Aunt Sally’s heart and Benny’s; and right he was: as for me, I would ’a’ lied the same way, and so would anybody that had any feeling, to save them such misery and sorrow which THEY warn’t no ways responsible for.Well, it made our lawyer look pretty sick; and it knocked Tom silly, too, for a little spell, but then he braced up and let on that he warn’t worried—but I knowed he WAS, all the same.And the people—my, but it made a stir amongst them!

汤姆则继续在沉思，丝毫没有注意其他事情；所以我们的律师便请证人作证，尽其所能地办事，可是结果还是很糟。 Tom he went on thinking, and never took no notice; so our lawyer took the witness and done the best he could, and it was plenty poor enough.

布雷斯·邓拉普在宣誓完后说：“这么长时间以来，我可怜的兄弟总是给我带来不少麻烦，但我从不认为他就如人们声称的那样品性有多坏，我从不会相信会有人去伤害这么一个从无恶意的可怜家伙”——（我确定我当时看到汤姆身上有一个微小的起身动作，但很快他看上去又是很失望）——“我从没想过牧师会伤害他——去想这样一件不可能的事是很不正常的——所以我从没注意过他，而现在我再也没办法原谅自己了；因为如果那时候我采取行动的话，现在我那个可怜的兄弟就会和我在一起了，而不是在那个地方被谋害了，他不是个坏人。”他似乎有点没法控制住自己，一时间说不出话来，此时大家都在等待着他说话，四周的人们都在感叹他太可怜了，大部分妇女都哭了。法庭安静极了，显得极为严肃，而此刻，大家也都听到了可怜的赛拉斯叔叔的叹息声。布雷斯继续说：“在九月二日，周六，他没有回家吃晚餐。不久后我就有点担心，我的其中一个黑奴前去囚犯那，但回来后说他并不在那里。此后我就越来越觉得不安，根本没法休息。我上了床，但根本睡不着；之后便起床外出，那时已经是深夜，我前去囚犯呆的地方，在那呆了好一会儿，四周逛了圈，只是希望能在那碰到我那可怜的兄弟，当时却根本不知道他已经摆脱了当前的麻烦，朝更美好的彼岸驶去了——”　他再度失去了控制，说不出话来，此时，大多数妇女都已经泪流满面了。很快他再次开始讲话：“但那已经起不了任何作用了；所以最后我还是回家了，想试着睡会，可是那根本不可能。过了一两天后，大家都感到了不安，都在谈论着这个囚犯对他的种种威胁，甚至出现了他可能已经被杀的传闻。当时我觉得这个想法根本站不住脚，可是大家已经开始在四处寻找他的尸体，但最终还是没找到，所以大家便放弃了。因此当时我觉得他是离开此地去别的地方让自己安静安静，在麻烦解决后他便会回来。可是在九月九日周六的深夜，莱姆·毕比和吉姆·莱恩赶到我家把所有的事情都告诉了我——告诉我所有的关于这次可怕的暗杀的事情，当时我的心都碎了。这让我想起了一件事，当时并没有让我留意，因为据说这个囚犯会梦游，会在没有意识的情况下去做任何不计后果的事情。我会告诉大家重现我脑中的这件事。在那个可怕的周六深夜，我当时觉得很伤心苦恼，在这个囚犯的农场周围徘徊。当我来到烟草地的角落处，听到一阵像在挖砂质土壤的声音，于是悄悄地靠近然后透过缠绕在篱笆上的葡萄藤偷偷看到囚犯手里拿着一把长柄锹，正在挖土，正用力把土都铲进一个几乎都快被填满的大坑中。他虽然是背对着我，但当时透过皎洁的月光，一看到那件旧绿色台面呢工作服，背部正中央的那块白色补丁，看起来总像是别人往他身上扔雪球一样，我就认出了他。而他正在埋葬他谋杀的那个人！” @BRACE DUNLAP, sworn, said: “I was in considerable trouble a long time about my poor brother, but I reckoned things warn’t near so bad as he made out, and I couldn’t make myself believe anybody would have the heart to hurt a poor harmless cretur like that”—(by jings, I was sure I seen Tom give a kind of a faint little start, and then look disappointed again)—”and you know I COULDN’T think a preacher would hurt him—it warn’t natural to think such an onlikely thing—so I never paid much attention, and now I sha’n’t ever, ever forgive myself; 他呜咽地哭了起来，就此猛跌在椅子上，此刻场内的大部分人都跟着边哭边喊：“噢，这真是太可怕了——真是骇人听闻！”大家的情绪都很激动，你几乎没办法知道自己心里在想些什么；而在此中间，赛拉斯叔叔跳了起来，脸色又如薄纸般苍白，大喊： And he slumped down in his chair crying and sobbing, and ‘most everybody in the house busted out wailing, and crying, and saying, “Oh, it’s awful—awful—horrible!” and there was a most tremendous excitement, and you couldn’t hear yourself think; and right in the midst of it up jumps old Uncle Silas, white as a sheet, and sings out:
“他们讲的每句话都是真的——确实是我心狠手辣地杀了他！”天啊，这让他们都呆掉了！此时满屋子里的人都不自禁地站了起来，都伸长脖子以便能更清楚地看到他，法官见状立即敲了他的锤子，而治安官则大喊：“肃静——法庭内要保持肃静——肃静！” “IT’S TRUE, EVERY WORD—I MURDERED HIM IN COLD BLOOD!”By Jackson, it petrified them!People rose up wild all over the house, straining and staring for a better look at him, and the judge was hammering with his mallet and the sheriff yelling “Order—order in the court—order!”
老头子一直颤抖地站着，眼里似乎燃烧着怒火，对身边紧紧抓住他并恳求他保持冷静的妻子和女儿看都没看一眼，并用手把他们都推开了，高声说他会把罪恶从他黑暗的心灵中洗去，他会把这个难以忍受的重负全都卸掉，而这个重负他一个小时都不能再忍受了！　　然后他便怒不可遏地宣泄着他那可怕的故事，不管是法官，陪审团，律师还是其他任何人，无一不是震惊不已，喘着气，而本尼和萨莉婶婶已经哭得撕心裂肺了。天啊，汤姆·索耶居然连看都没看他们一眼！　他根本就没有往他们那边看过——坐在那边眼睛始终在久久凝视着其他东西，而我并不知道他到底在看什么。老头子仍然在那边气得直哆嗦，他的话如同团团火焰般大肆地涌出： And all the while the old man stood there a-quaking and his eyes a-burning, and not looking at his wife and daughter, which was clinging to him and begging him to keep still, but pawing them off with his hands and saying he WOULD clear his black soul from crime, he WOULD heave off this load that was more than he could bear, and he WOULDN’T bear it another hour!And then he raged right along with his awful tale, everybody a-staring and gasping, judge, jury, lawyers, and everybody, and Benny and Aunt Sally crying their hearts out.And by George, Tom Sawyer never looked at him once!Never once—just set there gazing with all his eyes at something else, I couldn’t tell what.And so the old man raged right along, pouring his words out like a stream of fire:
“是我杀了他！我有罪！但在我此生中，我从没想过要伤害他，即使人们都在传言着我威胁过他。只是在我举起棍子的那一刻——我的心才冷血过——后来我就开始后悔了，我竟然真的杀了他。　　　　在那一霎那，我所受过的不公正的待遇一下子全都涌进脑子；那个人和他的无赖兄弟对我的侮辱，以及他们串通在一起利用人们来诋毁我的名声，这些东西都逼我做出了足以使我及家庭破碎的行为，而我的这些行为给家里人造成了无尽的伤害，却丝毫无法打击到他们。上帝啊，救救我吧！　　　　而他们这么做就是为了报复我——为什么这么说呢？因为我身边这位天真而又单纯的女孩子不愿意嫁给那个富有，傲慢，无知的懦夫，布雷斯·邓拉普，就是那个刚刚在这边为他的兄弟伤心啼哭而平时却从没关心过他的这个混蛋——”（这个时候我看到汤姆跳了起来，看起来万分欣喜）“——而在那个时候，正如我告诉过你们的，我完全忘记了上帝，只记得心中的苦涩，请上帝原谅我吧，是我杀了他。　　　在那一刻，我觉得很难受，心里对他万分抱歉——噢，我觉得非常后悔；但是一想到我可怜的一家，为了他们我一定要掩盖住这件事；我确实是把尸体藏在灌木丛中了；而不久之后，我就把他背到烟草地里；然后在深夜里，我拿着铲子把他埋了起来——” “I killed him!I am guilty!But I never had the notion in my life to hurt him or harm him, spite of all them lies about my threatening him, till the very minute I raised the club—then my heart went cold!—then the pity all went out of it, and I struck to kill!In that one moment all my wrongs come into my mind; all the insults that that man and the scoundrel his brother, there, had put upon me, and how they laid in together to ruin me with the people, and take away my good name, and DRIVE me to some deed that would destroy me and my family that hadn’t ever done THEM no harm, so help me God!And they done it in a mean revenge—for why?Because my innocent pure girl here at my side wouldn’t marry that rich, insolent, ignorant coward, Brace Dunlap, who’s been sniveling here over a brother he never cared a brass farthing for—” (I see Tom give a jump and look glad THIS time, to a dead certainty) “—and in that moment I’ve told you about, I forgot my God and remembered only my heart’s bitterness, God forgive me, and I struck to kill.In one second I was miserably sorry—oh, filled with remorse; but I thought of my poor family, and I MUST hide what I’d done for their sakes; and I did hide that corpse in the bushes; and presently I carried it to the tobacker field; and in the deep night I went with my shovel and buried it where—”
汤姆一跃而起，喊道： Up jumps Tom and shouts:
“现在我知道是怎么回事了！”他朝着老头子挥舞着双臂，很欢愉但又略显刻板的一种姿势。他说： “NOW, I’ve got it!” and waves his hand, oh, ever so fine and starchy, towards the old man, and says:
“坐下！确实有人行凶，但是你和那毫无关联！” “Set down!A murder WAS done, but you never had no hand in it!”
先生，这时你甚至听得到针落地的声音。这时候老头子沮丧地瘫坐在他的座位上，萨莉婶婶和本尼感到很惊讶，正目瞪口呆地望着汤姆而不知所措，也无暇顾及老头子的情况了。整个屋子的人其实都一样。我从来没见过人们看起来如此混乱和无助，我从没见过大家眼睛张得如此之大，而且久久凝视，眨都不眨一下。汤姆很镇静地说： Well, sir, you could a heard a pin drop.And the old man he sunk down kind of bewildered in his seat and Aunt Sally and Benny didn’t know it, because they was so astonished and staring at Tom with their mouths open and not knowing what they was about.And the whole house the same.I never seen people look so helpless and tangled up, and I hain’t ever seen eyes bug out and gaze without a blink the way theirn did.Tom says, perfectly ca’m:
“法官大人，我可以说话吗？” “Your honor, may I speak?”

“从两周前到现在，这幢法院大楼前就一直贴着一张小告示，告示上表示对追回在圣路易斯失窃的几颗大钻石悬赏两千美元。那些钻石价值一万两千美元。但先别管这个，呆会儿我会再说这个。现在关于这起谋杀案。我将会告诉你们所有事情——它是如何发生的——它是谁做的——以及任何一个细节。” “For about two weeks now there’s been a little bill sticking on the front of this courthouse offering two thousand dollars reward for a couple of big di’monds—stole at St. Louis.Them di’monds is worth twelve thousand dollars.But never mind about that till I get to it.Now about this murder.I will tell you all about it—how it happened—who done it—every detail.”
现在你可以看到每个人都相对平静了下来，开始聆听，期待听到所有有价值的东西。 You could see everybody nestle now, and begin to listen for all they was worth.
“这里的这位先生，布雷斯·邓拉普，刚在为他那位死去的兄弟而抽泣，而事实上你们都知道，他从不关心他那位兄弟。他所想要做的就是娶那位年轻貌美的女士，可是她不想嫁给他。所以他曾警告过赛拉斯叔叔总有一天他会让他难堪。赛拉斯叔叔明白布雷斯很有权势，而他自己若要和他对着干，他根本没有赢的机会。他既害怕又担心，所以他尽其所能地做任何事以平息他的怒气，期望布雷斯可以不要为难他；为此赛拉斯叔叔甚至雇佣了他那个一无是处的兄弟丘比特在农场上干活，不惜让整个家庭省吃俭用来赔偿他们；可是丘比特却做了他兄弟安排的每件事去羞辱赛拉斯叔叔，以让他心神不安，想以此逼迫赛拉斯叔叔去伤害他，最后让人们反过来唾弃赛拉斯叔叔。　　结果正如他们所愿。每个人都反过来针对他，对他说刻薄的话，渐渐得他的心都碎了——的确，他因为过于担忧和悲伤，所以他常有些神志不清。 “This man here, Brace Dunlap, that’s been sniveling so about his dead brother that YOU know he never cared a straw for, wanted to marry that young girl there, and she wouldn’t have him.So he told Uncle Silas he would make him sorry.Uncle Silas knowed how powerful he was, and how little chance he had against such a man, and he was scared and worried, and done everything he could think of to smooth him over and get him to be good to him: he even took his no-account brother Jubiter on the farm and give him wages and stinted his own family to pay them; and Jubiter done everything his brother could contrive to insult Uncle Silas, and fret and worry him, and try to drive Uncle Silas into doing him a hurt, so as to injure Uncle Silas with the people.And it done it.Everybody turned against him and said the meanest kind of things about him, and it graduly broke his heart—yes, and he was so worried and distressed that often he warn’t hardly in his right mind.
“好吧，在那个周六，我们遇到了些麻烦，现在这两位证人，莱姆·毕比和吉姆·莱恩当时正好经过赛拉斯叔叔和丘比特·邓拉普在工作的地方——他们刚所说的这部分是真的，可剩下的就是假的了。他们根本没有听到赛拉斯叔叔说他要杀丘比特；他们根本没有听到猛打声；他们根本没有看到尸体，他们甚至也没有看到赛拉斯叔叔往灌木丛中藏任何东西。现在看看他们吧——他们是如此不安得坐在那，真希望自己不曾如此巧舌如簧地说谎。不论怎样，在我揭开真相之前他们真该这样希望着。 “Well, on that Saturday that we’ve had so much trouble about, two of these witnesses here, Lem Beebe and Jim Lane, come along by where Uncle Silas and Jubiter Dunlap was at work—and that much of what they’ve said is true, the rest is lies.They didn’t hear Uncle Silas say he would kill Jubiter; they didn’t hear no blow struck; they didn’t see no dead man, and they didn’t see Uncle Silas hide anything in the bushes.Look at them now—how they set there, wishing they hadn’t been so handy with their tongues; anyway, they’ll wish it before I get done.
“而就在同一个周六晚上比尔和杰克·威瑟斯确实看到了一个人在拉着另外一个人。他们说的就这一部分是真的，而剩下的就都是假的了。刚开始他们以为是一个黑奴在偷赛拉斯叔叔的玉米——现在，你们可以看看，他们是显得如此愚蠢不堪，尤其在发现了那晚他们的话都被另外一个人听到了。这是因为不久后他们便知道到底是谁在拖东西，其实他们很清楚为何他们要在这里宣誓说通过走路的姿态可以辨认出那个人就是赛拉斯叔叔，虽然事实上并不是这样。其实当他们宣誓时，他们就知道自己即将说的是谎言。　 “That same Saturday evening Bill and Jack Withers DID see one man lugging off another one.That much of what they said is true, and the rest is lies.First off they thought it was a nigger stealing Uncle Silas’s corn—you notice it makes them look silly, now, to find out somebody overheard them say that.That’s because they found out by and by who it was that was doing the lugging, and THEY know best why they swore here that they took it for Uncle Silas by the gait—which it WASN’T, and they knowed it when they swore to that lie.
“那个在月光下的人确实看到凶手在烟草地里掩埋尸体——但那并不是赛拉斯叔叔在掩埋尸体。在那个时候他正在他的床上睡觉。 “A man out in the moonlight DID see a murdered person put under ground in the tobacker field—but it wasn’t Uncle Silas that done the burying.He was in his bed at that very time.
“现在，在我继续讲之前，我想问问你们是否注意过他们：他们在沉思或担心时，总是用手势表示某些事，可大家并不能理解，所以也没去注意他们的手到底在干什么，有时会摸摸下巴；有时会轻抚鼻子；有时会用手抚摸着下巴以下的地方；有时缠绕链子，有时笨手笨脚地摸索着纽扣，有时会用手指在脸上描绘着一个数字或者一个字母，或者画在下巴下，或者在下唇上。”我就是这个样子。当我因烦躁而静不下心来，或者担忧，抑或是艰难地思考时，我就会在我的脸颊上，或是嘴唇下，也可能是下巴下面画上一个字母“Ｖ”，仅仅是字母“Ｖ”，不会有别的东西——有一半的时候我并没有注意它，也没有意识到我正在那么做。”这很古怪。这和我所做的一模一样；只有我会画一个字母“Ｏ”。我可以看到大家相互点着头，在他们都表达‘的确是这样’时也是这么做的。” “Now, then, before I go on, I want to ask you if you’ve ever noticed this: that people, when they’re thinking deep, or when they’re worried, are most always doing something with their hands, and they don’t know it, and don’t notice what it is their hands are doing, some stroke their chins; some stroke their noses; some stroke up UNDER their chin with their hand; some twirl a chain, some fumble a button, then there’s some that draws a figure or a letter with their finger on their cheek, or under their chin or on their under lip.That’s MY way.When I’m restless, or worried, or thinking hard, I draw capital V’s on my cheek or on my under lip or under my chin, and never anything BUT capital V’s—and half the time I don’t notice it and don’t know I’m doing it.”That was odd.That is just what I do; only I make an O.And I could see people nodding to one another, same as they do when they mean “THAT’s so.”
“好，现在我继续讲。还是那个同样的周六——不，应该是在之前一天的夜里——有一艘汽船停泊在弗拉格勒的登陆处，就在离这四十英里的地方，那时候风雨交加。在那艘船上有一个贼，他身上携带着法院大楼外悬赏着的那两颗大钻石；他拿着手提袋偷偷溜上岸，冲进夜色之中，冒着暴风雨，他希望能到这个镇上以保生命安全。但与此同时，在船上还藏有他的其他两个同伙，他知道只要他们一有机会，就会杀了他抢走钻石；因为钻石是他们三个一起偷的，而那个家伙得到了那些钻石后就逃跑了。 “Now, then, I’ll go on.That same Saturday—no, it was the night before—there was a steamboat laying at Flagler’s Landing, forty miles above here, and it was raining and storming like the nation.And there was a thief aboard, and he had them two big di’monds that’s advertised out here on this courthouse door; and he slipped ashore with his hand-bag and struck out into the dark and the storm, and he was a-hoping he could get to this town all right and be safe.But he had two pals aboard the boat, hiding, and he knowed they was going to kill him the first chance they got and take the di’monds; because all three stole them, and then this fellow he got hold of them and skipped.
“好吧，他离开还没到十分钟，他的同伙就发现他跑了，然后他们都跳上岸去抓他。他们可能是点了火柴才看到他留下的足迹。无论如何，他们周六一整天一直在跟踪着他，而且还没让他看见；在快要日落时，他去赛拉斯叔叔田地旁的悬铃木丛，随后他在草丛里从手提袋里取出伪装用具，他先换上了这套伪装，然后才到镇子上现身——提醒诸位一下，就在赛拉斯叔叔用棍子打了丘比特·邓拉普之后不久，他就做了这一系列事情——他确实是打了他。 “Well, he hadn’t been gone more’n ten minutes before his pals found it out, and they jumped ashore and lit out after him.Prob’ly they burnt matches and found his tracks.Anyway, they dogged along after him all day Saturday and kept out of his sight; and towards sundown he come to the bunch of sycamores down by Uncle Silas’s field, and he went in there to get a disguise out of his hand-bag and put it on before he showed himself here in the town—and mind you he done that just a little after the time that Uncle Silas was hitting Jubiter Dunlap over the head with a club—for he DID hit him.
“可是那两个家伙一看到那个贼溜进悬铃木丛时，就从灌木丛中跳了出来，跟着他悄悄进去。 “But the minute the pals see that thief slide into the bunch of sycamores, they jumped out of the bushes and slid in after him.
“他们把他打倒了，还用棍子打他想置他于死地。 “They fell on him and clubbed him to death.
“没错，即使他高声惨叫，哀号，可他们对他没有一点儿心慈手软，就是要用棍棒打死他。那个时候两个在路上奔跑的人听到他的呼喊，便突然冲进悬铃木丛里——即使如此，这就是他们去的地方——那两个家伙一看到他们，便立马匆匆忙忙地逃走，而后面出现的那两个人也马上全力追赶他们。但也就过了一两分钟——后面出现的那两个人又悄悄地溜回悬铃木。 “Yes, for all he screamed and howled so, they never had no mercy on him, but clubbed him to death.And two men that was running along the road heard him yelling that way, and they made a rush into the sycamore bunch—which was where they was bound for, anyway—and when the pals saw them they lit out and the two new men after them a-chasing them as tight as they could go.But only a minute or two—then these two new men slipped back very quiet into the sycamores.
“后来他们做了什么呢？我会告诉你们他们做了些什么。他们发现那个贼当时已从他的地毯织料大袋子里拿出伪装用具准备穿上；所以其中一个人便脱掉了衣服，穿上了那套伪装用具。” “THEN what did they do?I will tell you what they done.They found where the thief had got his disguise out of his carpet-sack to put on; so one of them strips and puts on that disguise.”
讲到此处，汤姆停了一下，他想再制造些“效果”——然后他便不慌不忙地说： Tom waited a little here, for some more “effect”—then he says, very deliberate:
“那位穿上死者伪装品的人就是——丘比特·邓拉普！” “The man that put on that dead man’s disguise was—JUBITER DUNLAP!”
“天啊！”屋子里的所有人都喊道，而赛拉斯叔叔则完全被惊呆了。 “Great Scott!” everybody shouted, all over the house, and old Uncle Silas he looked perfectly astonished.
“是的，他就是丘比特·邓拉普。你看，他并没有死。后来他们把死者的靴子脱了，给尸体穿上丘比特·邓拉普破旧的靴子，而丘比特·邓拉普则穿上了尸体的靴子。此后丘比特·邓拉普继续呆在那里，另外一个人则在暮色下背着那具尸体离开；午夜过后，他又去赛拉斯叔叔家，在一条位于屋子和厨房间的走廊内，拿走了一件挂在挂衣钩上的旧绿色工作服，并穿上了它，还偷走了一把长柄铲子，随后便去烟草地里，把遇害人埋了起来。” “Yes, it was Jubiter Dunlap.Not dead, you see.Then they pulled off the dead man’s boots and put Jubiter Dunlap’s old ragged shoes on the corpse and put the corpse’s boots on Jubiter Dunlap.Then Jubiter Dunlap stayed where he was, and the other man lugged the dead body off in the twilight; and after midnight he went to Uncle Silas’s house, and took his old green work-robe off of the peg where it always hangs in the passage betwixt the house and the kitchen and put it on, and stole the long-handled shovel and went off down into the tobacker field and buried the murdered man.”
他停了下来，在那站了半分钟。其后——“那么你们认为被害者会是谁呢？他就是——杰克·邓拉普，就是那个失踪很久的窃贼！” He stopped, and stood half a minute.Then—“And who do you reckon the murdered man WAS?It was—JAKE Dunlap, the long-lost burglar!”
“天啊！” “Great Scott!”
“那个埋葬他的人就是——布雷斯·邓拉普，他的兄弟！” “And the man that buried him was—BRACE Dunlap, his brother!”
“天啊！” “Great Scott!”
“你们觉得最近这几周那个装聋作哑的陌生人，也就是现在在座的这个长相怪异的傻瓜是谁呢？他就是——丘比特·邓拉普！” “And who do you reckon is this mowing idiot here that’s letting on all these weeks to be a deef and dumb stranger?It’s—JUBITER Dunlap!”
我的天啊，他们激动地叫嚷着，从你出生那天起，你肯定没见过现在这样这么激动人心的场面。汤姆跳向丘比特，一把拉下他的护目镜和假的络腮胡，而这个人就是所谓的被害者，他和任何人一样都活得好好的。这时萨莉婶婶和贝尼走上前去抱着老赛拉斯叔叔哭泣，她们亲吻着他，几乎让他感到窒息。此时的赛拉斯叔叔脑袋一片茫然混乱，甚至比之前还手足无措。经历这次大劫难后一时还回不了神，这也是情理之中的。接着，大家开始呼喊： My land, they all busted out in a howl, and you never see the like of that excitement since the day you was born.And Tom he made a jump for Jubiter and snaked off his goggles and his false whiskers, and there was the murdered man, sure enough, just as alive as anybody!And Aunt Sally and Benny they went to hugging and crying and kissing and smothering old Uncle Silas to that degree he was more muddled and confused and mushed up in his mind than he ever was before, and that is saying considerable.And next, people begun to yell:
“汤姆·索耶！汤姆·索耶！大家都闭嘴，让他继续说！继续吧，汤姆·索耶！” “Tom Sawyer!Tom Sawyer!Shut up everybody, and let him go on!Go on, Tom Sawyer!”
这让他觉得非同寻常地爽，对于汤姆·索耶来说，成为一个公众人物是一大乐事，正如他所说的，那叫做英雄。因此当全场静下来后，他说： Which made him feel uncommon bully, for it was nuts for Tom Sawyer to be a public character that-away, and a hero, as he calls it.So when it was all quiet, he says:
“现在基本上没什么东西还没讲，只剩下这个。那个人，就是布雷斯·邓拉普，过去一直在为纠缠着赛拉斯叔叔的生命和心智而绞尽脑汁，直到他看到赛拉斯叔叔失去理智而用棍子打了他那个废话连篇的兄弟时，我猜他看到了自己的机会。丘比特冲进树林里躲起来，我想按照他们的计谋，他是计划躲到晚上再出来，离开这个国家。接着布雷斯会让每个人都相信是赛拉斯叔叔杀了他并把他埋在某个地方；而这会彻底毁了赛拉斯叔叔的名声，会将他驱逐出乡村——可能会把他绞死，我并不知道具体会怎样。但是当他们在悬铃木发现死去的兄弟时，可能因为他当时被打得很惨，所以他们并没有认出他来，所以他们看到了这个更好的方案；那就是他们都伪装起来并把杰克埋掉，他们立刻为他挖了个墓地，并同时穿上丘比特的衣服，还唆使雇佣了吉姆·莱恩，比尔·威瑟斯以及其他人起誓一些拙劣的谎言——他们现在就坐在那里，我刚说过在我结束讲话前他们就会让我觉得恶心，那么现在我对他们就是这个感觉。 “There ain’t much left, only this.When that man there, Bruce Dunlap, had most worried the life and sense out of Uncle Silas till at last he plumb lost his mind and hit this other blatherskite, his brother, with a club, I reckon he seen his chance.Jubiter broke for the woods to hide, and I reckon the game was for him to slide out, in the night, and leave the country.Then Brace would make everybody believe Uncle Silas killed him and hid his body somers; and that would ruin Uncle Silas and drive HIM out of the country—hang him, maybe; I dunno.But when they found their dead brother in the sycamores without knowing him, because he was so battered up, they see they had a better thing; disguise BOTH and bury Jake and dig him up presently all dressed up in Jubiter’s clothes, and hire Jim Lane and Bill Withers and the others to swear to some handy lies—which they done.And there they set, now, and I told them they would be looking sick before I got done, and that is the way they’re looking now.
“事实上，我和哈克·费恩是在乘坐到这里的船时碰上盗贼的，死者当时把所有和钻石有关的事都告诉了我们，还说其他那几个人如果找到机会的话，就会把他杀了；所以我们打算尽我们所能去帮助他。在我们前往悬铃木时，我们听到了他们在谋杀他时从那里发出的叫喊声；可是在第二天早晨暴风雨后我们到那里时，以为没有人被谋杀。在我们看到丘比特·邓拉普身穿和杰克几乎一样的伪装用具在到处乱晃时，便想起杰克当时有告诉过我们会这么打扮，他当时还与我们约定说会装成聋哑人，所以我们就以为他就是杰克本人。 “Well, me and Huck Finn here, we come down on the boat with the thieves, and the dead one told us all about the di’monds, and said the others would murder him if they got the chance; and we was going to help him all we could.We was bound for the sycamores when we heard them killing him in there; but we was in there in the early morning after the storm and allowed nobody hadn’t been killed, after all.And when we see Jubiter Dunlap here spreading around in the very same disguise Jake told us HE was going to wear, we thought it was Jake his own self—and he was goo-gooing deef and dumb, and THAT was according to agreement.
“接着，在其他人都停止寻找尸体后，我和哈克继续去找，并最终找到了他。我们也觉得很自豪；但是当赛拉斯叔叔告诉我们是他杀了那个人时，我们差点发疯了。　所以我们为发现尸体而感到很愧疚，如果我们可以的话，一定要保住赛拉斯叔叔；这也会是个很艰巨的任务，因为他不同意让我们按当时救老黑奴吉姆那样的方法把他救出来。 “Well, me and Huck went on hunting for the corpse after the others quit, and we found it.And was proud, too; but Uncle Silas he knocked us crazy by telling us HE killed the man.So we was mighty sorry we found the body, and was bound to save Uncle Silas’s neck if we could; and it was going to be tough work, too, because he wouldn’t let us break him out of prison the way we done with our old nigger Jim.
“整个月来，为了能救出赛拉斯叔叔，我做了任何事，可还是一点儿都不奏效。所以我们今天就毫无准备地来到法庭上，而且没有看到任何地方有一丝机会。但是过了没多久，我有一瞥让我有所思考——仅仅是很细微的一瞥——只是那么一点点，完全不足以肯定，但这让我使劲地想——当我装作在发愣时，其实我还在继续观察；然后又过了一会，当赛拉斯叔叔一下子说出很多关于是他杀了丘比特·邓拉普的话时，我再次注意到了那一瞥，这回我已经很确定，所以这一次我跳了起来，中止了庭审程序，因为我知道丘比特·邓拉普就坐在我面前。我能认出他，是因为我看见他做过一件事——我一直记得它。一年前我在这里时，我曾经见过他做了那件事。” “I done everything I could the whole month to think up some way to save Uncle Silas, but I couldn’t strike a thing.So when we come into court to-day I come empty, and couldn’t see no chance anywheres.But by and by I had a glimpse of something that set me thinking—just a little wee glimpse—only that, and not enough to make sure; but it set me thinking hard—and WATCHING, when I was only letting on to think; and by and by, sure enough, when Uncle Silas was piling out that stuff about HIM killing Jubiter Dunlap, I catched that glimpse again, and this time I jumped up and shut down the proceedings, because I KNOWED Jubiter Dunlap was a-setting here before me.I knowed him by a thing which I seen him do—and I remembered it.I’d seen him do it when I was here a year ago.”
然后他停了一下，沉思了一分钟——为了制造“效果”——这点我很清楚。接着他就不说话了，看起来他即将离开讲台，带着一副满不在乎而又懒洋洋的样子说： He stopped then, and studied a minute—laying for an “effect”—I knowed it perfectly well.Then he turned off like he was going to leave the platform, and says, kind of lazy and indifferent:
“好了，我相信这样就够了。” “Well, I believe that is all.”
天啊，你从来都不会听到如此尖声的嚎叫！——而这声音居然来自这件屋子： Why, you never heard such a howl!—and it come from the whole house:
“你认为他这么做的目的是什么？别停在那儿呀，小鬼！你这分明是在吊我们的胃口呀，你怎么能让嘴里流出的口水就停止在那儿呢？他到底做了什么？” “What WAS it you seen him do?Stay where you are, you little devil!You think you are going to work a body up till his mouth’s a-watering and stop there?What WAS it he done?”
没错，你看到了吧——他这么做就是想要得到某种“效果”；你即使用套着轭的牛也不可能把他从台上拉下来。 That was it, you see—he just done it to get an “effect”; you couldn’t ’a’ pulled him off of that platform with a yoke of oxen.
“噢，这还不够，”他说，“当他发现赛拉斯叔叔快被他从未所犯的谋杀判绞刑时，我看到他似乎有点激动；随后，他变得越来越胆怯，越来越担忧，我表面看起来没在注意他，其实我正在敏锐地观察他——突然间他的手开始动了，开始烦躁不安，很快他的左手悄悄往上移动，他的手指在他的脸颊上画了一个十字形，这回我敢确定就是他了！” “Oh, it wasn’t anything much,” he says.“I seen him looking a little excited when he found Uncle Silas was actually fixing to hang himself for a murder that warn’t ever done; and he got more and more nervous and worried, I a-watching him sharp but not seeming to look at him—and all of a sudden his hands begun to work and fidget, and pretty soon his left crept up and HIS FINGER DRAWED A CROSS ON HIS CHEEK, and then I HAD him!”
好了，随后他们高声叫喊，用力跺脚，使劲鼓掌直到汤姆·索耶得意得忘乎所以。　　　　　　　　　　　 　　 Well, then they ripped and howled and stomped and clapped their hands till Tom Sawyer was that proud and happy he didn’t know what to do with himself.
然后法官低头望了望台下的情况，说： And then the judge he looked down over his pulpit and says:
“我的孩子，你有亲眼看到你刚刚描述的这起非同寻常的阴谋及悲剧的所有细节吗？” “My boy, did you SEE all the various details of this strange conspiracy and tragedy that you’ve been describing?”
“没有，法官大人，我没有看到任何一件事。”　　 “No, your honor, I didn’t see any of them.”
“你没看到任何一件事！为什么你可以把整个事情的来龙去脉准确地描述得就像你亲眼所见一样。　你是如何做到的？” “Didn’t see any of them!Why, you’ve told the whole history straight through, just the same as if you’d seen it with your eyes.How did you manage that?”
汤姆从容不迫地说， Tom says, kind of easy and comfortable:
“噢，法官大人，我只不过是留心到了证据，然后把各种各样的证据拼凑起来；这只不过是很小很普通的侦探工作；任何人都做得到。” “Oh, just noticing the evidence and piecing this and that together, your honor; just an ordinary little bit of detective work; anybody could ’a’ done it.”
“这事情根本不是这样简单的！这种事一百万人中也没有两个人能够做到。你真是个了不起的孩子。” “Nothing of the kind!Not two in a million could ’a’ done it.You are a very remarkable boy.”
此后他们不再追问下去了，又一次给予了汤姆雷鸣般的掌声，而他——好了，他不会为了金钱而被收买。接着法官说： Then they let go and give Tom another smashing round, and he—well, he wouldn’t ’a’ sold out for a silver mine.Then the judge says:
“但你敢确定对于这次离奇事件的来龙去脉你描述得准确无误吗？” “But are you certain you’ve got this curious history straight?”
“我十分确定，法官大人。布雷斯·邓拉普——如果他想抓住机会否认自己的罪行的话，随便好了，我会努力让他为自己编造的谎言而忏悔。好吧，你看，他现在十分安静。他的兄弟也是十分安静，而那四位作伪证并将为此付出代价的证人也是非常安静。至于赛拉斯叔叔，发誓对他来说没有任何用处，根本不要相信他在宣誓下说出的话！” “Perfectly, your honor.Here is Brace Dunlap—let him deny his share of it if he wants to take the chance; I’ll engage to make him wish he hadn’t said anything…Well, you see HE’S pretty quiet.And his brother’s pretty quiet, and them four witnesses that lied so and got paid for it, they’re pretty quiet.And as for Uncle Silas, it ain’t any use for him to put in his oar, I wouldn’t believe him under oath!”
看吧，先生们，这让他们都欢呼起来，甚至连法官也不再拘谨，开怀大笑起来。汤姆觉得一切就像做梦一样。当他们都在放声大笑时，他仰起头看着法官说： Well, sir, that fairly made them shout; and even the judge he let go and laughed.Tom he was just feeling like a rainbow.When they was done laughing he looks up at the judge and says:
“法官大人，在这间屋子里有一个贼。” “Your honor, there’s a thief in this house.”
“屋子里有贼？” “A thief?”
“是的，大人。他身上带着价值一万两千美元的钻石。” “Yes, sir.And he’s got them twelve-thousand-dollar di’monds on him.”
天啊，但这引起了一阵骚动！每个人都喊了起来。 By gracious, but it made a stir!Everybody went shouting:
“那个人是谁？那个人是谁？快把他揪出来！” “Which is him? which is him? p’int him out!”
而法官说： And the judge says:
“我的孩子，把他指出来吧。治安官，你要准备逮捕他。是哪个人？” “Point him out, my lad.Sheriff, you will arrest him.Which one is it?”
汤姆说： Tom says:
“在这里的是这位已故的人——丘比特·邓拉普。” “This late dead man here—Jubiter Dunlap.”
其后屋子里又发出了一阵带有惊讶和兴奋的雷鸣般的声音；然而，之前已经受够惊吓的丘比特，这次着实又被吓了一跳。随后他开口说话了，带着哭腔： Then there was another thundering let-go of astonishment and excitement; but Jubiter, which was astonished enough before, was just fairly putrified with astonishment this time.And he spoke up, about half crying, and says:
“现在这个真的是谎言。法官大人，这不公平；我虽说是很坏，可我并没有做这件事。我有做其他事——是布雷斯唆使我做这些坏事的，是他说服我并保证终有一天他会让我富起来，所以我才这么做，我为我所做的事感到抱歉，我真的希望从没做过这些坏事；但我从来都没有偷钻石，我根本没有偷什么钻石；如果我有说假话，天打雷劈。治安官可以来搜搜我，看看到底有没有。” “Now THAT’S a lie.Your honor, it ain’t fair; I’m plenty bad enough without that.I done the other things—Brace he put me up to it, and persuaded me, and promised he’d make me rich, some day, and I done it, and I’m sorry I done it, and I wisht I hadn’t; but I hain’t stole no di’monds, and I hain’t GOT no di’monds; I wisht I may never stir if it ain’t so.The sheriff can search me and see.”
汤姆说： Tom says:
“法官大人，称呼他为盗贼可能不大准确，对于这点我会对他宽容一点儿的。他确实偷了钻石，可他自己并不知道。他的兄弟杰克从其他贼那里偷了钻石，在杰克死的时候丘比特从他身上偷了钻石；但是他并不知道他偷了钻石；这一个月里他一直带着钻石四处乱晃；是的，先生们，他一直带着那些价值一万两千美元的钻石，每天就像个穷人一样到处闲逛。是的，法官大人，他现在身上就带着。” “Your honor, it wasn’t right to call him a thief, and I’ll let up on that a little.He did steal the di’monds, but he didn’t know it.He stole them from his brother Jake when he was laying dead, after Jake had stole them from the other thieves; but Jubiter didn’t know he was stealing them; and he’s been swelling around here with them a month; yes, sir, twelve thousand dollars’ worth of di’monds on him—all that riches, and going around here every day just like a poor man.Yes, your honor, he’s got them on him now.”
法官大声说： The judge spoke up and says:
“治安官，搜他全身。” “Search him, sheriff.”
好了，先生，治安官把他全身从上到下每个地方都彻底搜了个遍；搜了他的帽子，短袜，边缝，靴子，以及其他任何东西——而汤姆安静地站在那，他在等待着下一个效果的到来。最后治安官放弃了，每个人看上去都很失望，而此时丘比特说： Well, sir, the sheriff he ransacked him high and low, and everywhere: searched his hat, socks, seams, boots, everything—and Tom he stood there quiet, laying for another of them effects of hisn.Finally the sheriff he give it up, and everybody looked disappointed, and Jubiter says:
“现在你看看！我刚怎么跟你说的？” “There, now! what’d I tell you?”
法官说： And the judge says:
“我的孩子，看起来这回你犯错了。” “It appears you were mistaken this time, my boy.”
这时汤姆故作姿态，挠着头，装作很用心在沉思。接着他又假装突然间想到啥，兴高采烈地抬起头，说道： Then Tom took an attitude and let on to be studying with all his might, and scratching his head.Then all of a sudden he glanced up chipper, and says:
“噢，现在我知道了！我差点儿把它给忘了。” “Oh, now I’ve got it!I’d forgot.”
哪个说的是谎话，我心里自然清楚。他接着说： Which was a lie, and I knowed it.Then he says:
“是否有人愿意借我一把小螺丝刀呢？丘比特，当时在你兄弟的手提袋里有一把，但我想你并没有把他取来带在身上。” “Will somebody be good enough to lend me a little small screwdriver?There was one in your brother’s hand-bag that you smouched, Jubiter, but I reckon you didn’t fetch it with you.”
“是的，我没有。我不想要它，所以就把它给仍了。” “No, I didn’t.I didn’t want it, and I give it away.”
“那是因为你不知道那是用来做什么用的。” “That’s because you didn’t know what it was for.”
丘比特已经又穿上他的靴子了， 那么现在汤姆所想要做的就是从每个人面前经过，走到丘比特那边，他对丘比特说： Jubiter had his boots on again, by now, and when the thing Tom wanted was passed over the people’s heads till it got to him, he says to Jubiter:
“抬起你的脚放在这张椅子上。”于是他蹲了下来，开始从鞋跟铁片上旋出螺丝钉，此时每个人都在注视着他；当他从鞋底取出那几颗大钻石，并握在手中时，它们闪烁着发出耀眼的光，阳光将钻石的光芒折射到每一个角落，大家一下子都屏住了呼吸；你从没见过丘比特像现在这么恼火和后悔。当汤姆再拿起其他几颗钻石出来时，丘比特看起来比任何时候都懊恼。天哪！他在想，如果他当时碰巧猜到大包里螺丝刀的用处的话，他现在早已离开了这里，在国外某块大陆上发大财，逍遥快乐地活着。 “Put up your foot on this chair.”And he kneeled down and begun to unscrew the heel-plate, everybody watching; and when he got that big di’mond out of that boot-heel and held it up and let it flash and blaze and squirt sunlight everwhichaway, it just took everybody’s breath; and Jubiter he looked so sick and sorry you never see the like of it.And when Tom held up the other di’mond he looked sorrier than ever.Land!he was thinking how he would ’a’ skipped out and been rich and independent in a foreign land if he’d only had the luck to guess what the screwdriver was in the carpet-bag for.
好了，这是最激动人心的时刻，四周都沸腾了，汤姆就像一个打了胜仗的将军一样拥有无比的荣耀。法官取走了钻石，站在台上，清了清他的嗓子，把眼镜推到脑袋后头，说： Well, it was a most exciting time, take it all around, and Tom got cords of glory.The judge took the di’monds, and stood up in his pulpit, and cleared his throat, and shoved his spectacles back on his head, and says:
“我会暂先保存钻石，并告知主人；物归原主时，我将很高兴能亲手将两千美元交给你们，因为这是你们该得的钱——是的，我们社区将给予你们最诚挚最深切的感谢，因为你们把一个清白无辜的家庭从破败和羞耻的困境中解救了出来，把一个善良而又真诚的人从死囚的悲剧中挽救了回来,并且揭露了那个残忍，可恨的恶棍和他那卑鄙兄弟的恶行，让他们得到法律应有的制裁！” “I’ll keep them and notify the owners; and when they send for them it will be a real pleasure to me to hand you the two thousand dollars, for you’ve earned the money—yes, and you’ve earned the deepest and most sincerest thanks of this community besides, for lifting a wronged and innocent family out of ruin and shame, and saving a good and honorable man from a felon’s death, and for exposing to infamy and the punishment of the law a cruel and odious scoundrel and his miserable creatures!”
好了，先生，如果这里有支管乐队可以演奏些音乐的话，这将是我见过的最完美的事情，汤姆·索耶也这么说。 Well, sir, if there’d been a brass band to bust out some music, then, it would ’a’ been just the perfectest thing I ever see, and Tom Sawyer he said the same.
后来治安官就把布雷斯·邓拉普和他的同伙都逮捕了，下个月法官便会对他们进行一一审判，把他们几个人都监禁起来。　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　于是每个人都蜂拥回到赛拉斯叔叔那个旧的小教堂里，大家都非常热情友善地对待他和他的家人，而大家觉得这样还远远不够。赛拉斯叔叔给他们讲道，而这也许是你所听到过的最该死，最混乱，最愚蠢的布道了，会让你精神混乱，甚至在大白天都找不到回家的路。但人们并没泄露出这个事实，因为在他们看来，这个是最纯洁，最美好，最高尚的布道，会让他们甘愿坐在那儿，祈求博爱和怜悯；但天啊，这样的场景会让我神经过敏，精神错乱，崩溃，僵做一团。但没过一会儿，他们再次崇拜起老头子的智慧，我认为，这不是恭维，他的确是一如往昔的睿智。此后，一家人像鸟儿一样幸福，没有什么能比这样的结局更让汤姆·索耶开心了；虽然我什么事情都没做，我和汤姆一样都很愉快。　　　　　　　　　　　　　　　当两千美元分发下来时，汤姆把一半分给了我，而且没把这事告诉任何人，这也在我的意料之中，因为我了解他。 Then the sheriff he nabbed Brace Dunlap and his crowd, and by and by next month the judge had them up for trial and jailed the whole lot.And everybody crowded back to Uncle Silas’s little old church, and was ever so loving and kind to him and the family and couldn’t do enough for them; and Uncle Silas he preached them the blamedest jumbledest idiotic sermons you ever struck, and would tangle you up so you couldn’t find your way home in daylight; but the people never let on but what they thought it was the clearest and brightest and elegantest sermons that ever was; and they would set there and cry, for love and pity; but, by George, they give me the jim-jams and the fan-tods and caked up what brains I had, and turned them solid; but by and by they loved the old man’s intellects back into him again, and he was as sound in his skull as ever he was, which ain’t no flattery, I reckon.And so the whole family was as happy as birds, and nobody could be gratefuler and lovinger than what they was to Tom Sawyer; and the same to me, though I hadn’t done nothing.And when the two thousand dollars come, Tom give half of it to me, and never told anybody so, which didn’t surprise me, because I knowed him.
