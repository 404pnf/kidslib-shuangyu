Rumpelstiltzkin 侏儒怪
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
There was once upon a time a poor miller who had a very beautiful daughter. 从前，有一个穷磨坊主，他有一个很漂亮的女儿。
Now it happened one day that he had an audience with the King, and in order to appear a person of some importance he told him that he had a daughter who could spin straw into gold. 有一天，他碰巧去觐见国王。为了表现出他是一个有点儿重要性的人，他告诉国王自己有一个能把麦秆纺成金子的女儿。
“Now that’s a talent worth having,” said the King to the miller; “if your daughter is as clever as you say, bring her to my palace to-morrow, and I’ll put her to the test.” “这是一个值得拥有的技能，”国王对磨坊主说，“如果你的女儿如你说得这么聪明，明天把她带到我的宫殿，我要考验一下她。”
When the girl was brought to him he led her into a room full of straw, gave her a spinning-wheel and spindle, and said: “Now set to work and spin all night till early dawn, and if by that time you haven’t spun the straw into gold you shall die.” 当女孩被带到国王面前时，国王把她带到了一间堆满麦秆的屋子，给了她纺轮和纺锤，然后说：“现在开始干活，今晚纺到明天一早，要是到那时你还没能把这些麦秆纺成金子，你就得死。”
Then he closed the door behind him and left her alone inside. 然后国王关上了身后的门，把她单独留在了里面。
So the poor miller’s daughter sat down, and didn’t know what in the world she was to do. 就这样，那个可怜的磨坊主的女儿坐了下来，不知道接下来要做什么。
She hadn’t the least idea of how to spin straw into gold, and became at last so miserable that she began to cry. 她根本就不知道怎么把麦秆纺成金子，最后她变得很伤心以至于哭了起来。
Suddenly the door opened, and in stepped a tiny little man and said: “Good-evening, Miss Miller-maid; why are you crying so bitterly?” 突然门被打开了，走进一个小矮人，他说：“晚上好，磨坊主家的小姐；你为什么哭得这么伤心？”
“Oh!” answered the girl, “I have to spin straw into gold, and haven’t a notion how it’s done.” “哎！”女孩回答道，“我必须把麦秆纺成金子，我根本都不知道该怎么纺。”
“What will you give me if I spin it for you?” asked the manikin. “如果我为你纺，你能给我什么呢？”小矮人问。
“My necklace,” replied the girl. “我的项链。”女孩答道。
The little man took the necklace, sat himself down at the wheel, and whir, whir, whir, the wheel went round three times, and the bobbin was full. 小矮人拿了项链，坐在织轮前，转呀，转呀，转呀，纺轮转了三次，卷线筒就满了。
Then he put on another, and whir, whir, whir, the wheel went round three times, and the second too was full; and so it went on till the morning, when all the straw was spun away, and all the bobbins were full of gold. 接着他又放上一个，转呀，转呀，转呀，纺轮转了三次，第二个卷线筒也满了；就这样一直转到第二天早上，所有的麦秆都纺好了，并且所有的卷线筒都绕满了金子。
As soon as the sun rose the King came, and when he perceived the gold he was astonished and delighted, but his heart only lusted more than ever after the precious metal. 太阳一升起来，国王就赶来了。当他看到那些金子时，又惊讶又高兴，但是他的心比之前更加贪恋那些珍贵的金子了。
He had the miller’s daughter put into another room full of straw, much bigger than the first, and bade her, if she valued her life, spin it all into gold before the following morning. 国王把磨坊主的女儿带到了另一个装满麦秆的屋子，比第一个屋子更大，然后命令她，如果她还想活命，就在第二天早上之前把所有的麦秆纺成金子。
The girl didn’t know what to do, and began to cry; then the door opened as before, and the tiny little man appeared and said: “What’ll you give me if I spin the straw into gold for you?” 女孩不知道该做什么，开始哭了起来；后来，门和前一次一样被打开了，小矮人出现了，说：“要是我帮你把麦秆纺成金子，你会给我什么？”
“The ring from my finger,” answered the girl. “我手指上的戒指。”女孩答道。
The manikin took the ring, and whir! round went the spinning-wheel again, and when morning broke he had spun all the straw into glittering gold. 那个小矮人拿了戒指，开始纺起来！纺轮又开始运转起来了。到了第二天早上，他把所有的麦秆都纺成了闪闪发光的金子。
The King was pleased beyond measure at the sights but his greed for gold was still not satisfied, and he had the miller’s daughter brought into a yet bigger room full of straw, and said: “You must spin all this away in the night; but if you succeed this time you shall become my wife.” 国王看到那么多金子，高兴得无法形容，但他对金子的贪婪还没有得到满足。接着他把磨坊主的女儿带到了一个更大的装满麦秆的屋子，说：“你今晚必须把所有的麦秆纺成金子；如果这次你成功了，你就将成为我的妻子。”
“She’s only a miller’s daughter, it’s true,” he thought; “but I couldn’t find a richer wife if I were to search the whole world over.” “她只是一个磨坊主的女儿，这是事实，”他想，“但是就算我找遍整个世界我也找不到一个比她更富有的妻子了。”
When the girl was alone the little man appeared for the third time, and said: “What’ll you give me if I spin the straw for you once again?” 当只剩小女孩一个人的时候，小矮人第三次出现了，说：“如果我再次帮你把麦秆纺成金子，你会给我什么？”
“I’ve nothing more to give,” answered the girl. “我再没有什么能给的了。”女孩答道。
“Then promise me when you are Queen to give me your first child.” “那你答应我，在你成了王后以后，把你的第一个孩子给我。”
“Who knows what may not happen before that?” “谁知道在那之前会发生什么呢？”
thought the miller’s daughter; and besides, she saw no other way out of it, so she promised the manikin what he demanded, and he set to work once more and spun the straw into gold. 磨坊主的女儿心想；而且，除此之外，她也没有别的办法，因此她答应了小矮人的要求。接着他再一次开始工作，把麦秆纺成了金子。
When the King came in the morning, and found everything as he had desired, he straightway made her his wife, and the miller’s daughter became a queen. 第二天早上国王进来时，得到了他想要的所有东西，他立即娶她为妻，磨坊主的女儿就成了王后。
When a year had passed a beautiful son was born to her, and she thought no more of the little man, till all of a sudden one day he stepped into her room and said: “Now give me what you promised.” 一年过去后，王后生了一个可爱的儿子，她完全忘记了小矮人。直到有一天，他突然走进她的房间，说：“现在把你承诺的东西给我。”
The Queen was in a great state, and offered the little man all the riches in her kingdom if he would only leave her the child. 王后非常焦躁不安，愿意把她王国所有的财富都给小矮人，只要把孩子留给她。
But the manikin said: “No, a living creature is dearer to me than all the treasures in the world.” 但是小矮人说：“不行，对我来说，活生生的东西比世界上所有的财宝都珍贵。”
Then the Queen began to cry and sob so bitterly that the little man was sorry for her, and said: “I’ll give you three days to guess my name, and if you find it out in that time you may keep your child.” 然后，王后就开始哭了起来，呜咽得如此伤心以至于小矮人有点儿同情她，说道：“我给你三天的时间来猜我的名字，要是你猜出来了，到那时你就可以留下你的孩子。”
Then the Queen pondered the whole night over all the names she had ever heard, and sent a messenger to scour the land, and to pick up far and near any names he could come across. 整个晚上王后都在回想她听过的每一个名字，并且派出一个使者到全国四处打听，到处收集他能发现的名字。
When the little man arrived on the following day she began with Kasper, Melchior, Belshazzar, and all the other names she knew, in a string, but at each one the manikin called out: “That’s not my name.” 在第一天小矮人到了时，她从卡斯帕、梅尔希奥、伯沙撒开始说，一个一个地说她知道的所有名字，但是对每个名字小矮人都叫着：“那不是我的名字。”
The next day she sent to inquire the names of all the people in the neighborhood, and had a long list of the most uncommon and extraordinary for the little man when he made his appearance. 第二天，她派人到邻国去询问所有人的名字。在小矮人出现时，她得到了一份最不常见、最奇怪的长长的名单。
“Is your name, perhaps, Sheepshanks Cruickshanks, Spindleshanks?” but he always replied: “That’s not my name.” “或许，你的名字是希普尚克斯、克鲁克尚克斯、斯宾德尚克斯？”但是他一直回答：“那不是我的名字。”
On the third day the messenger returned and announced: “I have not been able to find any new names, but as I came upon a high hill round the corner of the wood, where the foxes and hares bid each other good-night, I saw a little house, and in front of the house burned a fire, and round the fire sprang the most grotesque little man, hopping on one leg and crying: 在第三天，使者回来报告道：“我没能找到任何新的名字，但是在我路过森林角落附近的一座高山时，那里狐狸和兔子在互道晚安。我看见了一座小房子，在房子前面烧着一堆火。有一个最奇异的小矮人围着火堆，用一只腿跳着，还大喊道：
“To-morrow I brew, to-day I bake, “明天我酿酒，今天我烘焙，
And then the child away I’ll take; 然后我将把那孩子带走；
For little deems my royal dame 我高贵的夫人想不到
That Rumpelstiltzkin is my name!” 我的名字是侏儒怪！”
You can imagine the Queen’s delight at hearing the name, and when the little man stepped in shortly afterward and asked: “Now, my lady Queen, what’s my name?” she asked first: “Is your name Conrad?” 你可以想象王后在听到名字时有多高兴，很快小矮人走进来了，问道：“现在，我的王后陛下，我叫什么名字？”她先问：“你的名字是康纳德吗？”
“No.” “不是。”
“Is your name Harry?” “你的名字是哈里吗？”
“No.” “不是。”
“Is your name perhaps, Rumpelstiltzkin?” “或许，你的名字是侏儒怪吗？”
“Some demon has told you that! some demon has told you that!” “一定是魔鬼告诉你的！一定是魔鬼告诉你的！”
screamed the little man, and in his rage drove his right foot so far into the ground that it sank in up to his waist; then in a passion he seized the left foot with both hands and tore himself in two. 小矮人尖叫着，生气地把自己的右脚重重地踩在地上，结果腰陷入了地里；然后他发狂地用双手抓住左脚，把自己撕成了两半。
