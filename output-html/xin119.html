She did what any other woman would have done—sought relief in a wholesome tide of unreasonable tears, and her last words, murmured to herself before slumber, capitulating, came softly to woo her, were “He has forgotten.” 她做了其他女人都会做的事——毫无理由地痛哭一场，在泪水中寻求解脱。陷入沉睡前她喃喃自语的最后一句话就是：“他忘了。”她只能无奈地靠这句话来安慰自己了。
The manager of the Rancho de las Sombras was no dilettante. 树阴牧场的经理绝不是个外行。
He was a “hustler.” 他非常勤劳肯干。
He was generally up, mounted, and away of mornings before the rest of the household were awake, making the rounds of the flocks and camps. 在其他人都还在休息的时候，他多半已经起床，骑上马去巡视羊群和营房了。
This was the duty of the major-domo, a stately old Mexican with a princely air and manner, but Teddy seemed to have a great deal of confidence in his own eyesight. 这本是那个一派威严的墨西哥老管家的分内之事，但特迪好像对自己亲眼所见的更为放心。
Except in the busy seasons, he nearly always returned to the ranch to breakfast at eight o’clock, with Octavia and Mrs. Maclntyre, at the little table set in the central hallway, bringing with him a tonic and breezy cheerfulness full of the health and flavour of the prairies. 除了在忙季的时候，他几乎总是在八点的时候回到牧场，与奥克塔维亚和麦金太尔太太一起坐在走廊中央的那张小桌子旁吃早饭，带来令人心旷神怡的健康活力和草原的芬芳。
A few days after Octavia’s arrival he made her get out one of her riding skirts, and curtail it to a shortness demanded by the chaparral brakes. 奥克塔维亚到达几天之后，特迪让她拿出一件骑装裙，按照灌丛的情况改短一些。
With some misgivings she donned this and the pair of buckskin leggings he prescribed in addition, and, mounted upon a dancing pony, rode with him to view her possessions. 带着些许疑虑，奥克塔维亚穿上了改制后的裙子，又按照特迪额外的吩咐穿上了鹿皮裹腿，然后就骑上一匹活泼的小马，跟他一起去巡视自己的产业。
He showed her everything—the flocks of ewes, muttons and grazing lambs, the dipping vats, the shearing pens, the uncouth merino rams in their little pasture, the water-tanks prepared against the summer drought—giving account of his stewardship with a boyish enthusiasm that never flagged. 他带着她看了所有的一切——有一群群母羊、绵羊、正在吃草的小羔羊、浸洗缸、剪毛栏和牧场上粗野的美利奴羊，还有为了预防夏天干旱准备的水槽——特迪一一汇报着工作，始终充满了从未消逝的孩子般的热情。
Where was the old Teddy that she knew so well? 那个她熟知的特迪去哪里了？
This side of him was the same, and it was a side that pleased her; but this was all she ever saw of him now. 他的这一面仍旧维持着旧时的模样，也正是她喜欢的那一面。但是她现在看到的这一切就是全部。
Where was his sentimentality—those old, varying moods of impetuous love-making, of fanciful, quixotic devotion, of heart-breaking gloom, of alternating, absurd tenderness and haughty dignity? 他的感情用事都去哪里了——那些旧时求爱的鲁莽、幻想般堂吉诃德式的专一、让人心碎的忧伤以及时不时可笑的温柔和傲慢的尊严，这些多变的情绪都去哪里了？
His nature had been a sensitive one, his temperament bordering closely on the artistic. 他天性敏感，气质非常具有艺术性。
She knew that, besides being a follower of fashion and its fads and sports, he had cultivated tastes of a finer nature. 她知道，除了紧跟时尚潮流，喜欢运动之外，特迪还有着很好的品位。
He had written things, he had tampered with colours, he was something of a student in certain branches of art, and once she had been admitted to all his aspirations and thoughts. 他写过文章，摆弄过画，对一些艺术领域颇有见地，她也曾一度了解过他所有的愿望和想法。
But now—and she could not avoid the conclusion—Teddy had barricaded against her every side of himself except one—the side that showed the manager of the Rancho de las Sombras and a jolly chum who had forgiven and forgotten. 但是现在——她不得不得出一个结论——特迪不再让她了解他自己人格的各个方面，除了一面——作为树阴牧场的经理、已经宽恕和遗忘的好朋友这一面。
Queerly enough the words of Mr. Bannister’s description of her property came into her mind—“all inclosed within a strong barbed-wire fence.” 奇怪的是，这个时候她脑海中浮现出班尼斯特先生用来描述她产业的那句话——“在整个牧场外围还有一圈牢固的带刺铁丝网。”
“Teddy’s fenced, too,” said Octavia to herself. 奥克塔维亚自言自语道：“特迪也给自己筑了一道网。”
It was not difficult for her to reason out the cause of his fortifications. 要追究特迪为什么对自己如此防备的原因对她来说并不难。
It had originated one night at the Hammersmiths’ ball. 一切都始于哈默史密斯家舞会的那一晚。
It occurred at a time soon after she had decided to accept Colonel Beaupree and his million, which was no more than her looks and the entrée she held to the inner circles were worth. 事情发生在她决定接受博普雷上校和他百万家产之后不久，但这一切跟她的容貌，以及上层社会的地位相比，或许根本算不了什么。
Teddy had proposed with all his impetuosity and fire, and she looked him straight in the eyes, an said, coldly and finally: “Never let me hear any such silly nonsense from you again.” 特迪带着所有的热情、不顾一切地跟她求婚，而她却直直地盯着他的眼睛，冷酷且决绝地说道：“别再让我听到你说这样的蠢话。”
“You won’t,” said Teddy, with an expression around his mouth, and—now Teddy was inclosed within a strong barbed-wire fence. “你不会再听到了。”特迪说，嘴角的表情显得有些奇特。而现在——特迪给自己筑了一道特别坚固的铁丝网。
It was on this first ride of inspection that Teddy was seized by the inspiration that suggested the name of Mother Goose’s heroine, and he at once bestowed it upon Octavia. 在首次巡视时，特迪突发灵感，想起了鹅妈妈故事里女主人公的名字，他立即将这个名字“授予”了奥克塔维亚。
The idea, supported by both a similarity of names and identity of occupations, seemed to strike him as a peculiarly happy one, and he never tired of using it. 这个想法来源于奥克塔维亚和那个女主人公相似的名字，她们的职业也相同。对于特迪来说，这个名字特别有趣，简直是“屡试不爽”。
The Mexicans on the ranch also took up the name, adding another syllable to accommodate their lingual incapacity for the final “p,” gravely referring to her as “La Madama Bo-Peepy.” 墨西哥工人们也用这个外号称呼她。由于发不出最后的“普”这个音，他们就在后面加了个音节，认真地称呼她为“博皮贝夫人”。
Eventually it spread, and “Madame Bo-Peep’s ranch” was as often mentioned as the “Rancho de las Sombras.” 后来这个外号竟然流传开来，而“博皮普夫人的牧场”则经常用来代指“树阴牧场”。
Came the long, hot season from May to September, when work is scarce on the ranches. 随着五月到九月这一漫长而又炎热的季节的到来，牧场里的活也闲下来了。
Octavia passed the days in a kind of lotus-eater’s dream. 奥克塔维亚整天过着浑浑噩噩的日子。
Books, hammocks, correspondence with a few intimate friends, a renewed interest in her old water-colour box and easel—these disposed of the sultry hours of daylight. 书、吊床、与闺蜜通信、对水彩盒和画架重新燃起的兴趣——用这些东西可以打发掉白天闷热的时光。
The evenings were always sure to bring enjoyment. 晚上总是享受的时光。
Best of all were the rapturous horseback rides with Teddy, when the moon gave light over the wind-swept leagues, chaperoned by the wheeling night-hawk and the startled owl. 最开心的就是能够在盘旋的夜莺和受惊的猫头鹰的陪伴下，跟特迪在满是月光、还有微风拂过的草原上开心地骑马。
Often the Mexicans would come up from their shacks with their guitars and sing the weirdest of heart-breaking songs. 这时候，墨西哥工人往往也会拿着吉他，走出棚屋，唱起最怪异的伤心情歌。
There were long, cosy chats on the breezy gallery, and an interminable warfare of wits between Teddy and Mrs. MacIntyre, whose abundant Scotch shrewdness often more than overmatched the lighter humour in which she was lacking. 阵阵微风袭来的长廊上，经常会有人在那里舒适地聊上半天。特迪和麦金太尔太太也常在那里无休无尽地斗智，而麦金太尔太太满腹的苏格兰式精明弥补了她缺乏的轻幽默，反而吃不了什么亏。
And the nights came, one after another, and were filed away by weeks and months—nights soft and languorous and fragrant, that should have driven Strephon to Chloe over wires however barbed, that might have drawn Cupid himself to hunt, lasso in hand, among those amorous pastures—but Teddy kept his fences up. 一个又一个轻柔、令人倦怠而充满芬芳的夜晚到来了，又随着一个星期或者一个月的逝去而消逝了。这样的夜晚本应使得斯特雷方翻过任何铁丝网，去到克洛伊身边，或者使得丘比特自个儿拿起套索，在这个多情的草原上搜寻猎物。但是特迪仍然还筑着那道铁丝网。
One July night Madame Bo-Peep and her ranch manager were sitting on the east gallery. 七月的一个晚上，博皮普夫人和他的牧场经理一起坐在长廊的东头。
Teddy had been exhausting the science of prognostication as to the probabilities of a price of twenty-four cents for the autumn clip, and had then subsided into an anesthetic cloud of Havana smoke. 特迪用上了所有的理论来推算秋天剪下的羊毛能够获得二十四分盈利的概率，最后再也没话可说，只能静静坐在哈瓦那雪茄令人麻木的烟雾中。
Only as incompetent a judge as a woman would have failed to note long ago that at least a third of his salary must have gone up in the fumes of those imported Regalias. 只有女人这样失格的判断者才会在这么久之后都没有发现，他工资中至少有三分之一的钱都随着那些进口雪茄的烟雾消散了。
“Teddy,” said Octavia, suddenly, and rather sharply, “what are you working down here on a ranch for?” “特迪，”奥克塔维亚突然说，显得相当突兀，“你来这个牧场工作到底是为了什么？”
“One hundred per,” said Teddy, glibly, “and found.” 特地不假思索地回答道：“一百美元一月的工资和免费膳宿。”
“I’ve a good mind to discharge you.” “我真想解雇你。”
“Can’t do it,” said Teddy, with a grin. “你做不到。”特迪咧嘴笑道。
“Why not?” demanded Octavia, with argumentative heat. “为什么？”奥克塔维亚问道，似乎想要跟他争论一番。
“Under contract. “合同规定。
Terms of sale respect all unexpired contracts. 买卖要遵循所有还未到期的合同。
Mine runs until 12 P. M., December thirty-first. 我的合同要到十二月三十一日晚上十二点才终止。
You might get up at midnight on that date and fire me. 你可以在那天半夜爬起来解雇我。
If you try it sooner I’ll be in a position to bring legal proceedings.” 如果你想提前解雇我的话，我可以提起法律诉讼。”
Octavia seemed to be considering the prospects of litigation. 奥克塔维亚似乎在考虑采取法律途径的前景。
“But,” continued Teddy cheerfully, “I’ve been thinking of resigning anyway.” “但是，”特迪继续高兴地说道，“不管怎样，我已经在考虑辞职了。”
Octavia’s rocking-chair ceased its motion. 奥克塔维亚不再摇动椅子。
There were centipedes in this country, she felt sure; and Indians, and vast, lonely, desolate, empty wastes; all within strong barbed-wire fence. 在这个地方，她确信有蜈蚣、印第安人，有浩瀚、孤独、荒凉、空旷的旷野，这所有的一切都围在牢固的铁丝网内。
There was a Van Dresser pride, but there was also a Van Dresser heart. 她有范德雷瑟家的骄傲，但也有范德雷瑟家的内心。
She must know for certain whether or not he had forgotten. 她必须弄清楚他是否忘记了。
“Ah, well, Teddy,” she said, with a fine assumption of polite interest, “it’s lonely down here; you’re longing to get back to the old life—to polo and lobsters and theatres and balls.” “哦，好吧，特迪，”她说，装作很有礼貌地询问道，“这里确实很孤寂，你想要回到过去的生活吧——想要回去打马球，吃龙虾，看电影，去舞会吧。”
“Never cared much for balls,” said Teddy virtuously. “我对舞会从来没有什么兴趣。”特迪正经地说。
“You’re getting old, Teddy. “你老了，特迪。
Your memory is failing. 你都没什么记性了。
Nobody ever knew you to miss a dance, unless it occurred on the same night with another one which you attended. 谁都知道你是有舞会必到，除非它跟另一场你参加的舞会冲突了。

“No; Adéle. “不，她叫阿黛尔。
Mabel was the one with the bony elbows. 梅布尔是胳膊肘没什么肉的那个。
That wasn’t wall in Adéle’s eyes. 阿黛尔也不是白星眼。
It was soul. 那里有她的灵魂。
We used to talk sonnets together, and Verlaine. 我们过去经常在一起聊十四行诗，还有魏尔兰。
Just then I was trying to run a pipe from the Pierian spring.” 那个时候，我还试图自己写诗呢。”
“You were on the floor with her,” said Octavia, undeflected, “five times at the Hammersmiths’.” 奥克塔维亚并不让他岔开话题，说：“你还跟她一起跳舞，在哈默史密斯家的舞会上就跳了五支。”
“Hammersmiths’ what?” questioned Teddy, vacuously. “哈默史密斯家的什么？”特迪装傻充愣地问道。
“Ball—ball,” said Octavia, viciously. “舞会——舞会！”奥克塔维亚狠狠地回答道，
“What were we talking of?” “我们刚刚一直在谈论什么？”
“Eyes, I thought,” said Teddy, after some reflection; “and elbows.” “我想是眼睛，”特迪想了一下后说道，“还有胳膊肘。”
“Those Hammersmiths,” went on Octavia, in her sweetest society prattle, after subduing an intense desire to yank a handful of sunburnt, sandy hair from the head lying back contentedly against the canvas of the steamer chair, “had too much money. 奥克塔维亚特别想猛地从那颗脑袋上扯下一把久经日晒的黄头发，此刻它正舒适地躺在帆布躺椅上。她好不容易才压制下这种强烈的冲动，用最甜美的社交口吻继续说道：“哈默史密斯家的那些人太有钱了。
Mines, wasn’t it? 开矿的，不是吗？
It was something that paid something to the ton. 那可能赚很多钱啊。
You couldn’t get a glass of plain water in their house. 你在他们家甚至都喝不到白水。
Everything at that ball was dreadfully overdone.” 那个舞会的所有一切都太夸张了。”
“It was,” said Teddy. “确实是的。”特迪说，
“Such a crowd there was!” “那儿特别挤！”
Octavia continued, conscious that she was talking the rapid drivel of a school-girl describing her first dance. 奥克塔维亚意识到自己就像是一个在描述生平第一次舞会的女学生一样,完全是在说傻话。
“The balconies were as warm as the rooms. “阳台上就跟房间里一样温暖。
I—lost—something at that ball.” 我——我在那次舞会上丢了——丢了一个东西。”
The last sentence was uttered in a tone calculated to remove the barbs from miles of wire. 最后一句话的语气就是专门用来清除特迪那道铁丝网的。
“So did I,” confessed Teddy, in a lower voice. “我也一样。”特迪低声坦白道。
“A glove,” said Octavia, falling back as the enemy approached her ditches. 当敌人靠近她的壕沟之时，奥克塔维亚却后退了。她说：“我丢了一只手套。”
“Caste,” said Teddy, halting his firing line without loss. “我丢了社会地位。”特迪毫无损失地停止了攻击。
“I hobnobbed, half the evening with one of Hammersmith’s miners, a fellow who kept his hands in his pockets, and talked like an archangel about reduction plants and drifts and levels and sluice-boxes.” “我跟一个哈默史密斯家的矿工聊了半宿，他总是把手放在口袋里，说起话来就像个天使，老是聊些关于矿石粉碎厂、小平巷、主平巷和洗矿槽的话题。”
“A pearl-gray glove, nearly new,” sighed Octavia, mournfully. 奥克塔维亚悲伤地叹息道：“一只珍珠灰的手套，几乎是全新的。”
“A bang-up chap, that McArdle,” maintained Teddy approvingly. 特迪赞许地接着说道：“那个麦卡德尔，真是个不错的小伙子。
“A man who hated olives and elevators; a man who handled mountains as croquettes, and built tunnels in the air; a man who never uttered a word of silly nonsense in his life. 他讨厌橄榄和电梯，把大山看作肉丸子，还凌空架隧道。他生平没有说过一句蠢话。
Did you sign those lease-renewal applications yet, madama? 夫人，你有签那些续约申请吗？
They’ve got to be on file in the land office by the thirty-first.” 三十一号之前必须交给国土局存档。”
Teddy turned his head lazily. 特迪懒懒地扭过头来。
Octavia’s chair was vacant. 奥克塔维亚的椅子上已经空无一人了。
A certain centipede, crawling along the lines marked out by fate, expounded the situation. 一条沿着命数的轨迹爬行的蜈蚣很好地理清了这一切。
It was early one morning while Octavia and Mrs. Maclntyre were trimming the honeysuckle on the west gallery. 那是一个清晨，奥克塔维亚和麦金太尔太太正在走廊西头修剪忍冬植物。
Teddy had risen and departed hastily before daylight in response to word that a flock of ewes had been scattered from their bedding ground during the night by a thunder-storm. 特迪早在天亮前就匆忙起身离开了，据说基地有群母羊被前一晚的那场雷雨吓得走散了。
The centipede, driven by destiny, showed himself on the floor of the gallery, and then, the screeches of the two women giving him his cue, he scuttled with all his yellow legs through the open door into the furthermost west room, which was Teddy’s. 受命运驱使的蜈蚣出现在了长廊的地板上。两个女人的尖叫声给了它暗示，它便撒开所有黄腿，没命地从敞开的房门那里溜进了最西头特迪的房间。
Arming themselves with domestic utensils selected with regard to their length, Octavia and Mrs. Maclntyre, with much clutching of skirts and skirmishing for the position of rear guard in the attacking force, followed. 奥克塔维亚和麦金太尔太太挑了两件长家具当武器，提起裙摆，就谁当后卫、谁当前锋这一问题争论了一番后，跟了上去。
Once outside, the centipede seemed to have disappeared, and his prospective murderers began a thorough but cautious search for their victim. 蜈蚣一进房间仿佛就消失了，而那两个要害它性命的女人则开始了彻底而小心的搜索。
Even in the midst of such a dangerous and absorbing adventure Octavia was conscious of an awed curiosity on finding herself in Teddy’s sanctum. 即使是在最危险和最专注的冒险中，奥克塔维亚都还能感到自己身处特迪的房间时那种敬畏的好奇心。
In that room he sat alone, silently communing with those secret thoughts that he now shared with no one, dreamed there whatever dreams he now called on no one to interpret. 他独自坐在那个房间，默默地与自己的小秘密谈着心。他现在不再与别人分享这些念头，也希望不再有人知道他现在的想法。
It was the room of a Spartan or a soldier. 这就是个斯巴达人或者军人的房间。
In one corner stood a wide, canvas-covered cot; in another, a small bookcase; in another, a grim stand of Winchesters and shotguns. 一个角落里摆着一张宽大的帆布床，一个角落里放着一个小书架，而另一个角落里则放着几把可怕的温切斯特枪和猎枪。
An immense table, strewn with letters, papers and documents and surmounted by a set of pigeon-holes, occupied one side. 房间里还有一张巨大的桌子，上面摆满了信、纸张和文件以及一个支起的分类箱。
The centipede showed genius in concealing himself in such bare quarters. 蜈蚣能在这么空旷的住所找到藏匿之处，确实很有才。
Mrs. Maclntyre was poking a broom-handle behind the bookcase. 麦金太尔太太用扫帚柄捅了捅书架的后面。
Octavia approached Teddy’s cot. 奥克塔维亚走近特迪的床。
The room was just as the manager had left it in his hurry. 房间的样子跟他匆忙离开时一模一样。
The Mexican maid had not yet given it her attention. 墨西哥女佣还没有过来收拾。
There was his big pillow with the imprint of his head still in the centre. 他的枕头大大的，中央部分仍然留有他睡过的痕迹。
She thought the horrid beast might have climbed the cot and hidden itself to bite Teddy. 她想，这个可恶的小畜生可能已经爬上床藏了起来，以伺机咬特迪一口。
Centipedes were thus cruel and vindictive toward managers. 蜈蚣对经理的态度总是那样残忍恶毒。
She cautiously overturned the pillow, and then parted her lips to give the signal for reinforcements at sight of a long, slender, dark object lying there. 她小心地翻过枕头，看见一条长长的柔软的黑色物体躺在那里，张开嘴正准备呼叫援军。
But, repressing it in time, she caught up a glove, a pearl-gray glove, flattened—it might be conceived—by many, many months of nightly pressure beneath the pillow of the man who had forgotten the Hammersmiths’ ball. 但是，她及时压下了求救信号。她抓起一只手套，一只珍珠灰的手套，它被压在这个忘记哈默史密斯家舞会的男人的枕头底下应该已经好几个月了，看上去有些扁扁的。
Teddy must have left so hurriedly that morning that he had, for once, forgotten to transfer it to its resting-place by day. 特迪那天早上肯定是走得太匆忙了，以至于这一次忘记把它放回白天的位置。
Even managers, who are notoriously wily and cunning, are sometimes caught up with. 即使是众所周知狡猾又诡计多端的经理们，有时候也会被抓住尾巴。
Octavia slid the gray glove into the bosom of her summery morning gown. 奥克塔维亚把手套揣进她穿着夏季晨袍的怀里。
It was hers. 它是她的了。
Men who put themselves within a strong barbed-wire fence, and remember Hammersmith balls only by the talk of miners about sluice-boxes, should not be allowed to possess such articles. 躲在坚固的铁丝网之中，只记得在哈默史密斯家舞会上跟矿工聊过洗矿槽的男人，不配拥有这样的东西。
After all, what a paradise this prairie country was! 说到底，这个草原国度多么像天堂啊！
How it blossomed like the rose when you found things that were thought to be lost! 当你在这里找到以为丢失的东西时，这里就更美了，就像是盛开的玫瑰！
How delicious was that morning breeze coming in the windows, fresh and sweet with the breath of the yellow ratama blooms! 那夹杂着黄金雀花芬芳，从窗口吹来的清新而香甜的晨风是多么美妙啊！
Might one not stand, for a minute, with shining, far-gazing eyes, and dream that mistakes might be corrected? 你能不多站一会儿，用那双闪亮的眼睛凝视远方，幻想误会都消除吗？
Why was Mrs. Maclntyre poking about so absurdly with a broom? 为什么麦金太尔太太要可笑地用个扫帚去捅呢？
“I’ve found it,” said Mrs. MacIntyre, banging the door. “我找到了，”麦金太尔太太砰地关上了门，
“Here it is.” “就在这儿。”
“Did you lose something? asked Octavia, with sweetly polite non-interest. “你有丢什么东西吗？”奥克塔维亚礼貌地用甜甜的声音问道，但实际却没有什么兴趣。
“The little devil!” said Mrs. Maclntyre, driven to violence. “那个小恶魔！”麦金太尔太太愤愤地说道，
“Ye’ve no forgotten him alretty?” “你难道已经忘记它的存在了吗？”
Between them they slew the centipede. 她们俩一起将蜈蚣弄死了。
Thus was he rewarded for his agency toward the recovery of things lost at the Hammersmiths’ ball. 正是由于它搭的桥，才让奥克塔维亚在哈默史密斯家舞会上丢的东西失而复得，但它得到的下场却是这样。
It seems that Teddy, in due course, remembered the glove, and when he returned to the house at sunset made a secret but exhaustive search for it. 特迪似乎也在随后不久想起了这只手套。傍晚回房间之后，他悄悄地彻底搜寻了一番。
Not until evening, upon the moonlit eastern gallery, did he find it. 直到晚上的月光洒向东头长廊，他才找到了手套。
It was upon the hand that he had thought lost to him forever, and so he was moved to repeat certain nonsense that he had been commanded never, never to utter again. 它出现在了他原以为永远失去的那只手上。特迪感动得又再次说出了那些曾被禁止的胡话。
Teddy’s fences were down. 特迪的铁丝网彻底垮塌了。
This time there was no ambition to stand in the way, and the wooing was as natural and successful as should be between ardent shepherd and gentle shepherdess. 这次再也没有横亘在他们之间的野心，求爱也相当自然，相当成功，就像热情的牧羊人和温柔的牧羊女之间该有的情形一样。
The prairies changed to a garden. 整个草原变成了一个花园。
The Rancho de las Sombras became the Ranch of Light. 树阴牧场也充满了光明。
A few days later Octavia received a letter from Mr. Bannister, in reply to one she had written to him asking some questions about her business. 几天之后，奥克塔维亚从班尼斯特先生那儿收到了一封回信，之前她就一些生意上的问题写信问过他。
A portion of the letter ran as follows: 信上的一部分这样写道：
“I am at a loss to account for your references to the sheep ranch. “关于牧场的问题，我真的不知道该怎么跟你说。
Two months after your departure to take up your residence upon it, it was discovered that Colonel Beaupree’s title was worthless. 你出发去那儿定居的两个月后，我们才发现博普雷上校的产权根本无效。
A deed came to light showing that he disposed of the property before his death. 一份契据表明他在死之前就已经卖掉了这份财产。
The matter was reported to your manager, Mr. Westlake, who at once repurchased the property. 我们跟你的经理，也就是韦斯特莱克先生已经说过了，他立马就把这份产业赎了回去。
It is entirely beyond my powers of conjecture to imagine how you have remained in ignorance of this fact. 我完全没有预料到，你到现在都还蒙在鼓里。
I beg that you that will at once confer with that gentleman, who will, at least, corroborate my statement.” 我希望你立马跟那位先生协商一下，至少他能证实我所言不假。
Octavia sought Teddy, with battle in her eye. 奥克塔维亚找到特迪，眼泛怒意。
“What are you working on this ranch for?” she asked once more. 她再次问道：“你在这个牧场工作到底是为了什么？”
“One hundred—” he began to repeat, but saw in her face that she knew. “一百——”他本来又要再重复一遍，但却看到了奥克塔维亚一脸了然的样子。
She held Mr. Bannister’s letter in her hand. 她手里握着班尼斯特先生的信。
He knew that the game was up. 他知道他瞒不住了。
“It’s my ranch,” said Teddy, like a schoolboy detected in evil. “这是我的牧场。”特迪说道，就好像是学生做坏事被当场抓住的样子，
“It’s a mighty poor manager that isn’t able to absorb the boss’s business if you give him time.” “如果你给一个经理足够多的时间，他还不能将老板的生意收为己有的话，这个经理就太无能了。”
“Why were you working down here?” pursued Octavia still struggling after the key to the riddle of Teddy. 奥克塔维亚仍然想要打破沙锅问到底：“你为什么要在这儿工作？”
“To tell the truth, ’Tave,” said Teddy, with quiet candour, “it wasn’t for the salary. “实话说，塔维亚，”特迪相当正经地回答道，“我并不是为了工资。
That about kept me in cigars and sunburn lotions. 这点儿工资只够我抽雪茄，买防晒霜。
I was sent south by my doctor. 我来南部是因为我的医生。
’Twas that right lung that was going to the bad on account of over-exercise and strain at polo and gymnastics. 我因为打马球和体操而运动过度，导致我的右肺情况变得很糟。
I needed climate and ozone and rest and things of that sort.” 我需要好的气候、清新的空气、足够的休息以及类似的条件。”
In an instant Octavia was close against the vicinity of the affected organ. 奥克塔维亚立马靠向了那个情况不好的部位。
Mr. Bannister’s letter fluttered to the floor. 班尼斯特先生的信飘到了地上。
“It’s—it’s well now, isn’t it, Teddy?” “现在——现在好了吗，特迪？”
“Sound as a mesquite chunk. “就跟牧豆树干一样健康。
I deceived you in one thing. 我有件事骗了你。
I paid fifty thousand for your ranch as soon as I found you had no title. 当我知道你并没有牧场的所有权之后，就立即花了五万买了下来。
I had just about that much income accumulated at my banker’s while I’ve been herding sheep down here, so it was almost like picking the thing up on a bargain-counter for a penny. 在这儿牧羊期间，我银行户头里累积起来的工资差不多有这个数，所以我几乎就是捡了个便宜。
There’s another little surplus of unearned increment piling up there, ‘Tave. 除了这些，我还另外有一小笔‘不劳而获’的盈余，塔维亚。
I’ve been thinking of a wedding trip in a yacht with white ribbons tied to the mast, through the Mediterranean, and then up among the Hebrides and down Norway to the Zuyder Zee.” 我一直在想，新婚旅行的时候，我要乘着游艇穿越地中海，登上赫布里底群岛，途径挪威到达须德海，游艇的桅杆上还要系着白色绸带。”
“And I was thinking,” said Octavia, softly, “of a wedding gallop with my manager among the flocks of sheep and back to a wedding breakfast with Mrs. MacIntyre on the gallery, with, maybe, a sprig of orange blossom fastened to the red jar above the table.” “我一直在想，”奥克塔维亚轻轻说道，“我结婚的时候要跟我的经理一起在羊群中骑马，然后回去长廊跟麦金太尔太太一起共进婚礼早餐，或许，还能有一簇橘子花系在桌子上方悬挂着的红陶罐上。”
Teddy laughed, and began to chant: 特迪笑了，唱起了歌儿：
“Little Bo-Peep has lost her sheep, “小小博普丢了羊，
And doesn’t know where to find ’em. 却不知道上哪儿找。
Let ’em alone, and they’ll come home, 随它们去吧，它们自己会回家的，
And—” 然后——”
Octavia drew his head down, and whispered in his ear, But that is one of the tales they brought behind them. 奥克塔维亚勾住他的脖子，在他耳边低语了些什么，不过这就是后话了。
