The Black Bull of Norroway 诺罗威的黑公牛
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
And many a hunting song they sung, 他们唱了很多关于狩猎的歌，
And song of game and glee; 还有很多关于赌博和欢乐的歌，
Then tuned to plaintive strains their tongue, 然后变成了悲伤的曲调，拉紧了他们的舌头，
“Of Scotland’s luve and lee.” “苏格兰的爱情和守护”。
To wilder measures next they turn 接着他们转向了更激烈的旋律。
“The Black, Black Bull of Norroway!” “黑色的，黑色的诺罗威公牛！”
Sudden the tapers cease to burn, 突然烛芯熄灭了，
The minstrels cease to play. 吟游诗人也停止了弹奏。
“The Cout of Keeldar,” by J. Leyden. 《季尔达的外套》，J. 莱登著。
In Norroway, langsyne, there lived a certain lady, and she had three dochters. 很久很久以前，在诺罗威住着一位太太，她有三个女儿。
The auldest o’ them said to her mither: “Mither, bake me a bannock, and roast me a collop, for I’m gaun awa’ to seek my fortune.” 大女儿跟她母亲说：“妈妈，给我煎一张燕麦饼，烤一块薄肉片，我要离开家去寻找我的幸福。”
Her mither did sae; and the dochter gaed awa’ to an auld witch washerwife and telled her purpose. 她母亲照做了，这个女儿离开去找一个做洗衣活的巫婆，跟她说明了来意。
The auld wife bade her stay that day, and gang and look out o’ her back door, and see what she could see. 当晚，老妇人嘱咐她留下来，让她从后门往外看，看她能看见什么。
She saw nocht the first day. 第一天她什么都没看到。
The second day she did the same, and saw nocht. 第二天她照做了，还是什么都没看到。
On the third day she looked again, and saw a coach-and-six coming along the road. 第三天她又去看，看见了一辆六匹马拉的马车沿着道路驶来。
She ran in and telled the auld wife what she saw. 她跑回屋告诉老妇人她看到的景象。
“Aweel,” quo’ the auld wife, “yon’s for you.” “好吧，”老妇人说，“那是来接你的。”
Sae they took her into the coach, and galloped aff. 他们把女孩接到马车上，马车奔驰而去。
The second dochter next says to her mither: “Mither, bake me a bannock, and roast me a collop, fur I’m gaun awa’ to seek my fortune.” 第二个女儿也对他的母亲说：“妈妈，给我煎一个燕麦饼，烤一片薄肉片，我要离家去寻找我的幸福。”
Her mither did sae; and awa’ she gaed to the auld wife, as her sister had dune. 她母亲照做了，二女儿像她姐姐一样，离家去这找这个老妇人。
On the third day she looked out o’ the back door, and saw a coach-and-four coming along the road. 第三天她从后门望去，看见了一辆四匹马拉的马车沿着道路驶来。
“Aweel,” quo’ the auld wife, “yon’s for you.” “好吧，”老妇人说，“那是来接你的。”
Sae they took her in, and aff they set. 他们把女孩接上马车，动身离开。
The third dochter says to her mither: “Mither, bake me a bannock, and roast me a collop, for I’m gaun awa’ to seek my fortune.” 小女儿对母亲说：“妈妈，给我煎一个燕麦饼，给我烤一块薄肉片，我要离开家寻找我的幸福。”
Her mither did sae; and awa’ she gaed to the auld witch-wife. 她母亲照做了，她离开家去找那个老女巫。
She bade her look out o’ her back door, and see what she could see. 老女巫叫她从后门向外看，看她能看到什么。
She did sae; and when she came back said she saw nocht. 她照做了，回来时说她什么都没看到。
The second day she did the same, and saw nocht. 第二天她又去看，还是没看到任何东西。
The third day she looked again, and on coming back said to the auld wife she saw nocht but a muckle Black Bull coming roaring alang the road. 第三天她再去看的时候，回来告诉老妇人她只看到了一只巨大的黑公牛沿着道路咆哮着跑来。
“Aweel,” quo’ the auld wife, “yon’s for you.” “好吧，”老妇人说，“那是来接你的。”
On hearing this she was next to distracted wi’ grief and terror; but she was lifted up and set on his back, and awa’ they went. 听到这里，小女儿心里满是悲伤和恐惧，但她被举上了牛背，牛把她带走了。
Aye they traveled, and on they traveled, till the lady grew faint wi’ hunger. 他们一路走啊，走啊，一直走到女孩饿得头晕目眩。
“Eat out o’ my right lug,” says the Black Bull, “and drink out o’ my left lug, and set by your leavings.” “吃掉我的右耳，”黑牛说，“喝掉我的左耳，吃剩的你留下。”
Sae she did as he said, and was wonderfully refreshed. 她照他说的做了，奇迹般地恢复了精神。
And lang they gaed, and sair they rade, till they came in sight o’ a very big and bonny castle. 经过漫长而艰辛的旅程，他们眼前出现了一座雄伟美丽的城堡。
“Yonder we maun be this night,” quo’ the bull; “for my auld brither lives yonder”; and presently they were at the place. “我们今天必须在那里过夜，”黑牛说，“我的一个哥哥住在那里。”他们不久就到达了城堡。
They lifted her aff his back, and took her in, and sent him away to a park for the night. 他们把她从牛背上接下来，带她进入城堡，把黑牛送到一个花园里过夜。
In the morning, when they brought the bull hame, they took the lady into a fine shining parlor, and gave her a beautiful apple, telling her no to break it till she was in the greatest strait ever mortal was in in the world, and that wad bring her o’t. 早上，他们给公牛安上颈轭，把女孩带进一个富丽堂皇的会客厅，给了她一个漂亮的苹果，告诉她，一定是要遇到了致命的困境，才能切开这个苹果，这个苹果会帮她脱离困境。
Again she was lifted on the bull’s back, and after she had ridden far, and farer than I can tell, they came in sight o’ a far bonnier castle, and far farther awa’ than the last. 她再一次被举上了牛背，之后不知道走了多远，远处出现了一座漂亮的城堡，比第一座更漂亮，也比第一座更远。
Says the bull till her: “Yonder we maun be the night, for my second brither lives yonder”; and they were at the place directly. 公牛对她说：“我们今晚必须在那里过夜，因为我的第二个兄弟住在那里。”很快他们就到了城堡。
They lifted her down and took her in, and sent the bull to the field for the night. 他们把她从牛背上接下来，把她带入城堡，让公牛在田野里过夜。
In the morning they took the lady into a fine and rich room, and gave her the finest pear she had ever seen, bidding her no to break it till she was in the greatest strait ever mortal could be in, and that wad get her out o’t. 早上，他们把女孩带进了一个富丽堂皇的房间，给了她一个她从未见过的漂亮的梨子，嘱咐她一定要在遇到最艰难的困境时再切开这个梨子，它可以帮助她摆脱困境。
Again she was lifted and set on his back, and awa’ they went. 她又一次被举上牛背，离开了城堡。
And lang they gaed, and sair they rade, till they came in sight o’ the far biggest castle, and far farthest aff, they had yet seen. 经过漫长而艰辛的旅程，远远的，他们眼前出现了所见过的最大的城堡，也是路程最远的。
“We maun be yonder the night,” says the bull, “for my young brither lives yonder”; and they were there directly. “我们今天必须在那里过夜，”公牛说，“因为我的弟弟住在那里。”然后他们就直接去了城堡。
They lifted her down, took her in, and sent the bull to the field for the night. 他们把她从牛背上接下来，带进城堡，让公牛在田野里过夜。
In the morning they took her into a room, the finest of a’, and gied her a plum, telling her no to break it till she was in the greatest strait mortal could be in, and that wad get her out o’t. 到了早上，他们把她带进一个她见过的最华丽的房间，给了她一个李子，告诉她除非是她遇到了世上最大的困境，才能切开这个李子，这个李子会帮助她摆脱困境。
Presently they brought hame the bull, set the lady on his back, and awa’ they went. 他们马上给公牛上颈轭，让女孩在公牛背上坐好，他们就出发了。
And aye they gaed, and on they rade, till they came to a dark and ugsome glen, where they stopped, and the lady lighted down. 旅途漫长而艰辛，他们最终到了一个幽暗可怕的峡谷，他们停了下来，女孩点灯照亮了峡谷。
Says the bull to her: “Here ye maun stay till I gang and fight the deil. 公牛说：“你必须留在这里，等我打败恶魔。
Ye maun seat yoursel’ on that stane, and move neither hand nor fit till I come back, else I’ll never find ye again. 你必须保持手脚不动坐在石头上，直到我回来，否则我永远都找不到你了。
And if everything round about ye turns blue I hae beated the deil; but should a’ things turn red he’ll hae conquered me.” 如果你周围的东西都变成蓝色，那么意味着我已经打败了恶魔；如果变成红色，则意味着他征服了我。”
She set hersel’ down on the stane, and by-and-by a’ round her turned blue. 她爬下来，坐在石头上，周围的东西一点点都变成了蓝色。
O’ercome wi’ joy, she lifted the ae fit and crossed it owre the ither, sae glad was she that her companion was victorious. 她非常高兴，抬起一只脚跨过另一只，她真开心，她的伙伴胜利了。
The bull returned and sought for but never could find her. 公牛回来找她，却一直都找不到。
Lang she sat, and aye she grat, till she wearied. 她一直坐在那里，从欣喜不已等到筋疲力尽。
At last she rase and gaed awa’, she kedna whaur till. 最终，她起身离开，四处游荡。
On she wandered till she came to a great hill o’ glass, that she tried a’ she could to climb, bat wasna able. 她一直游荡，直到走到了一座巨大的玻璃山前，她尝试着想爬上去，可是却失败了。
Round the bottom o’ the hill she gaed, sabbing and seeking a passage owre, till at last she came to a smith’s house; and the smith promised, if she wad serve him seven years, he wad make her iron shoon, wherewi’ she could climb owre the glassy hill. 她沿着山脚走，一边哭一边寻找上山的路，直到最后她来到一座铁匠的房子，铁匠承诺，如果她能够为他干七年活，他就为她做一双铁鞋，让她能够爬过那座玻璃山。
At seven years’ end she got her iron shoon, clamb the glassy hill, and chanced to come to the auld washerwife’s habitation. 七年后她得到了那双铁鞋，她爬上玻璃山，偶然到了一个洗衣妇的住所。
There she was telled of a gallant young knight that had given in some bluidy sarks to wash, and whaever washed thae sarks was to be his wife. 在那里，她听说有一个勇敢的骑士拿了一件带血渍的衬衣来洗，说谁能洗掉这些血渍就可以成为他的妻子。
The auld wife had washed till she was tired, and then she set to her dochter, and baith washed, and they washed, and they better washed, in hopes of getting the young knight; but a’ they could do they couldna bring out a stain. 老妇人直洗到自己疲惫不堪，她叫来她的女儿跟她一起洗，她们洗啊洗啊，越洗越卖力，希望可以得到那个年轻的骑士，但她们根本洗不掉一个污点。
At length they set the stranger damosel to wark; and whenever she began the stains came out pure and clean, but the auld wife made the knight believe it was her dochter had washed the sarks. 最后她们让这个陌生少女去洗那件衬衣，她一开始洗，所有的污点就都消失了，衬衫变得干净洁白，但老妇人告诉骑士是她的女儿把那件衬衫洗干净了。
So the knight and the eldest dochter were to be married, and the stranger damosel was distracted at the thought of it, for she was deeply in love wi’ him. 于是骑士和妇人的大女儿准备结婚，一想到这个消息，少女就心烦意乱，因为她已经深深爱上了骑士。
So she bethought her of her apple, and breaking it, found it filled with gold and precious jewelry, the richest she had ever seen. 她想起了她的苹果，便把它切开，发现里面装满了她从未见过的金子和珍稀的珠宝，价值连城。
“All these,” she said to the eldest dochter, “I will give you, on condition that you put off your marriage for ae day, and allow me to go into his room alone at night.” “所有这些，”她对老妇人的大女儿说，“我都给你，只要你能够把婚期推迟一天，然后允许我晚上独自一人进到骑士的房间。”
So the lady consented; but meanwhile the auld wife had prepared a sleeping-drink, and given it to the knight, wha drank it, and never wakened till next morning. 大女儿同意了这个交易，但是老妇人事先准备了安眠药给骑士，一旦喝下这种安眠药，就会一直昏睡到第二天早上。
The lee-lang night ther damosel sabbed and sang: 在那个漫长的夜晚，少女哭着唱道：
“Seven lang years I served for thee, “为了你，七年来我拼命把活干，
The glassy hill I clamb for thee, 为了你，我爬过玻璃山，
The bluidy shirt I wrang for thee; 为了你，我洗净沾满血污的衬衫，
And wilt thou no wauken and turn to me?” 你为何不醒来把我看？”
Next day she kentna what to do for grief. 第二天，她非常悲伤，不知道该怎么办。
She then brak the pear, and found it filled wi’ jewelry far richer than the contents o’ the apple. 于是她切开了那个梨子，发现里面满是比苹果里更昂贵的珠宝。
Wi’ thae jewels she bargained for permission to be a second night in the young knight’s chamber; but the auld wife gied him anither sleeping-drink, and he again sleepit till morning. 她拿着这些珠宝，讨价还价希望在年轻骑士的房间里再呆上一晚。但老妇人又给骑士喝了安眠药，他又一次睡到了第二天早上。
A’ night she kept sighing and singing as before: 那天夜里，她像前一次一样，叹息着唱：
“Seven lang years I served for thee,” &c. “为了你，七年来我拼命把活干，为了你，我翻过玻璃山，为了你，我洗净沾满血渍的衬衫，你为何不醒来把我看？”
Still he sleepit, and she nearly lost hope a’thegither. 他仍然睡着，她几乎绝望了。
But that day when he was out at the hunting, somebody asked him what noise and moaning was yon they heard all last night in his bedchamber. 第二天骑士出去打猎的时候，有人告诉他，昨天夜里从他房间一直传出呜咽声。
He said he heardna ony noise. 他说他什么都没听到。
But they assured him there was sae; and he resolved to keep waking that night to try what he could hear. 但他们向骑士保证确有其事，骑士决心在夜里保持清醒，看看他能听到什么。
That being the third night, and the damosel being between hope and despair, she brak her plum, and it held far the richest jewelry of the three. 那是第三晚，少女又憧憬又绝望，她切开了那只李子，里面的珠宝是三个里最昂贵的。
She bargained as before; and the auld wife, as before, took in the sleeping-drink to the young knight’s chamber; but he telled her he couldna drink it that night without sweetening. 她像之前一样请求，老妇人也像之前一样，拿着安眠药去了骑士的房间，但骑士告诉老妇人，他需要一些甜的东西才能把它喝下去。
And when she gaed awa’ for some honey to sweeten it wi’, he poured out the drink, and sae made the auld wife think he had drunk it. 趁老妇人去拿蜂蜜的时候，骑士倒掉了药，让老妇人以为他已经喝下去了。
They a’ went to bed again, and the damosel began, as before, singing: 大家都睡觉了，像之前一样，少女又开始唱歌：
“Seven lang years I served for thee, “为了你，七年来我拼命把活干，
The glassy hill I clamb for thee, 为了你，我爬过玻璃山，
The bluidy shirt I wrang for thee; 为了你，我洗净满是血渍的衬衫，
And wilt thou no wauken and turn to me?” 你为何不醒来把我看？”
He heard, and turned to her. 骑士听了便转过身来。
And she telled him a’ that had befa’en her, and he telled her a’ that had happened to him. 她告诉了她之前经历的一切，他也告诉了她发生在他身上的一切。
And he caused the auld washerwife and her dochter to be burned. 他烧死了老妇人和她的女儿。
And they were married, and he and she are living happy till this day, for aught I ken. 他们结婚了，据我所知，一直幸福快乐地生活在一起。
