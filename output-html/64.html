托宾的手相 Tobin’s Palm
作者：欧·亨利 by O.Henry
一天，托宾和我，我们两人一起去了科尼岛；我俩身上有四美元，而托宾需要散散心。因为托宾的心上人——来自斯莱戈郡的凯蒂·马霍纳，自从三个月前动身来美国便失踪了。她出发时，身上揣着自己存的二百美元，还有卖掉托宾继承的财产得来的一百美元，那是位于香诺沼泽的一座漂亮农舍和一头猪。自从托宾收到凯蒂写来的那封信，说她已经动身来和他相聚之后，他就再也没有她的消息了。托宾在报纸上登了寻人启事，可这个爱尔兰姑娘仍是音讯全无。 Tobin and me, the two of us, went down to Coney one day, for there was four dollars between us, and Tobin had need of distractions.For there was Katie Mahorner, his sweetheart, of County Sligo, lost since she started for America three months before with two hundred dollars, her own savings, and one hundred dollars from the sale of Tobin’s inherited estate, a fine cottage and pig on the Bog Shannaugh.And since the letter that Tobin got saying that she had started to come to him not a bit of news had he heard or seen of Katie Mahorner.Tobin advertised in the papers, but nothing could be found of the colleen.
于是，我和托宾去了科尼岛，以为玩玩“惊险滑梯”、闻闻爆米花的香味或许能让他振作起来。但托宾是个固执的人，况且悲伤已经渗入了他的每一寸肌肤。看着漫天飞舞的气球，托宾咬牙切齿；看到放电影，他又骂骂咧咧；虽然他总是有酒就喝，却仍然对木偶戏中的庞奇和朱迪嗤之以鼻。当那些铁版照相师走过来时，他居然想揍他们一顿。 So, to Coney me and Tobin went, thinking that a turn at the chutes and the smell of the popcorn might raise the heart in his bosom.But Tobin was a hardheaded man, and the sadness stuck in his skin.He ground his teeth at the crying balloons; he cursed the moving pictures; and, though he would drink whenever asked, he scorned Punch and Judy, and was for licking the tintype men as they came.
于是我把他拉到一条木板小道上，那里的娱乐项目没有那么刺激。在一个六英尺宽八英尺长的小摊前，托宾停了下来，眼神多多少少恢复了正常。 So I gets him down a side way on a board walk where the attractions were some less violent.At a little six by eight stall Tobin halts, with a more human look in his eye.
“就是这儿了，”他说，“我要在这儿散散心。我打算让尼罗河边来的那位神奇的手相师给我看看手相，算算我的未来会怎么样。” “’Tis here,” says he, “I will be diverted.I’ll have the palm of me hand investigated by the wonderful palmist of the Nile, and see if what is to be will be.”
托宾很相信征兆这类不合自然规律的东西。他深信那些不合常理的东西，比如黑猫、幸运数字、报纸上的天气预报等等。 Tobin was a believer in signs and the unnatural in nature.He possessed illegal convictions in his mind along the subjects of black cats, lucky numbers, and the weather predictions in the papers.
我们走进那个施了魔法的鸡笼，里面挂着红布和一张张手相图，显得十分神秘。图上的线条纵横交错，宛如一座铁路枢纽站。门上方的标牌告诉人们，这里面坐的是埃及手相师佐佐女士。里面有位胖女人，穿着红色无袖套衫，那上面绣着涂鸦和小动物图案。托宾给了她十美分，然后伸出一只手。她抬起托宾的手，这手活像运货马的马蹄。她仔细端详着，仿佛在检查是有石头卡在了蹄楔中，还是掉了只马蹄铁。 We went into the enchanted chicken coop, which was fixed mysterious with red cloth and pictures of hands with lines crossing ’em like a railroad centre.The sign over the door says it is Madame Zozo the Egyptian Palmist.There was a fat woman inside in a red jumper with pothooks and beasties embroidered upon it.Tobin gives her ten cents and extends one of his hands.She lifts Tohin’s hand, which is own brother to the hoof of a drayhorse, and examines it to see whether ’tis a stone in the frog or a cast shoe he has come for.
“先生，”佐佐女士说，“你的命运线显示——” “Man,” says this Madame Zozo, “the line of your fate shows—”
“这可不是我的脚啊。”托宾打断她说，“当然啦，它不怎么好看，但你手里握的可是我的手掌啊。” “’Tis not me foot at all,” says Tobin, interrupting.“Sure, ’tis no beauty, but ye hold the palm of me hand.”
“这条线显示，”女士说，“你仍处在不幸之中。并且不幸的事情还会更多。维纳斯丘——或者这是石头砸的瘀伤？——说明你已坠入爱河。因为你的心上人，你的生活出现了一些波折。” “The line shows,” says the Madame, “that ye’ve not arrived at your time of life without bad luck.And there’s more to come.The mound of Venus—or is that a stone bruise?—shows that ye’ve been in love.There’s been trouble in your life on account of your sweetheart.”
“她指的是凯蒂·马霍纳。”托宾在我的一侧大声耳语。 “’Tis Katie Mahorner she has references with,” whispers Tobin to me in a loud voice to one side.
“我看见，”手相师说，“你难以忘怀的那个人现在伤心欲绝，苦难缠身。从这些指示线里，我能看出她的名字带有字母‘K’和‘M’。” “I see,” says the palmist, “a great deal of sorrow and tribulation with one whom ye cannot forget.I see the lines of designation point to the letter K and the letter M in her name.”
“嘘！”托宾对我说，“你听到了吗？” “Whist!” says Tobin to me, “do ye hear that?”
“当心一个深色皮肤的男人和一个浅色皮肤的女人，”手相师接着说，“因为他们将给你带来麻烦。你很快要进行一次水上旅行，还会破点财。我发现一条能给你带来好运的掌纹。有一个男人将走进你的生活，给你带来好运。他长了个歪鼻子，凭这一点，当你看到他的时候就能认出他来。” “Look out,” goes on the palmist, “for a dark man and a light woman; for they’ll both bring ye trouble.Ye’ll make a voyage upon the water very soon, and have a financial loss.I see one line that brings good luck.There’s a man coming into your life who will fetch ye good fortune.Ye’ll know him when ye see him by his crooked nose.”
“能确切知道他的名字吗？”托宾问道，“当他在背后支援我，给我带来好运的时候，我也好和他打个招呼啊。” “Is his name set down?” asks Tobin.“’Twill be convenient in the way of greeting when he backs up to dump off the good luck.”
“他的名字，”手相师若有所思地说，“手纹并没有显示，但可以看出他的名字很长，里面应该有字母‘O’。没有别的可说了。晚安。别堵着门口啦。” “His name,” says the palmist, thoughtful looking, “is not spelled out by the lines, but they indicate ’tis a long one, and the letter ‘o’ should be in it.There’s no more to tell. Good-evening. Don't block up the door.”Good-evening.Don’t block up the door.”
“太奇妙了，她居然都知道。”当我们走向码头的时候，托宾说。 “’Tis wonderful how she knows,” says Tobin as we walk to the pier.
我们挤过大门时，一个黑鬼用点着的雪茄戳了一下托宾的耳朵，这下可麻烦了。托宾捶打着这个黑鬼的脖子，周围的女人们尖叫起来；我还算镇定，赶在警察来之前把这个小个子男人拽走了。托宾只顾自己快活的时候，脾气总是很坏。 As we squeezed through the gates a nigger man sticks his lighted segar against Tobin’s ear, and there is trouble.Tobin hammers his neck, and the women squeal, and by presence of mind I drag the little man out of the way before the police comes.Tobin is always in an ugly mood when enjoying himself.
在回去的船上，有个男人高喊：“谁要英俊的服务生？”托宾正想喝一大罐满是泡沫的啤酒，便准备“认罪”；但当他把手伸进口袋摸钱，才发现自己因“证据不足”，已经被“无罪释放”了。 On the boat going back, when the man calls “Who wants the good-looking waiter?” Tobin tried to plead guilty, feeling the desire to blow the foam off a crock of suds, but when he felt in his pocket he found himself discharged for lack of evidence.
看来在刚才的混乱中，有人动了他的钱。于是，我们口干舌燥地坐在凳子上，听那些拉丁佬在甲板上拉小提琴。如果说现在跟我们刚来时相比有什么不同，那就是托宾的情绪更低落了，更加难以忍受自己的不幸了。 Somebody had disturbed his change during the commotion.So we sat, dry, upon the stools, listening to the Dagoes fiddling on deck.If anything, Tobin was lower in spirits and less congenial with his misfortunes than when we started.
栏杆旁的一个座位上坐着一个年轻女人，看穿着，她应该属于坐在红色小轿车里的那类女人，而她头发的颜色很像崭新的海泡石烟斗。托宾从她面前经过时，不小心踢到了她的脚。托宾喝醉的时候，对女士总是彬彬有礼的，于是他一边道歉，一边设法转动他的帽子。可是他把帽子弄掉了，风直接把它吹到了船下。 On a seat against the railing was a young woman dressed suitable for red automobiles, with hair the colour of an unsmoked meerschaum.In passing by, Tobin kicks her foot without intentions, and, being polite to ladies when in drink, he tries to give his hat a twist while apologising.But he knocks it off, and the wind carries it overboard.
托宾回到我身边坐下，我开始留神他，因为这个男人身上的不幸越来越频繁了。噩运缠身时，他完全可能冲他看到的穿戴最讲究的男人踢上一脚，或者试图指挥这条船。 Tobin came back and sat down, and I began to look out for him, for the man’s adversities were becoming frequent.He was apt, when pushed so close by hard luck, to kick the best dressed man he could see, and try to take command of the boat.
不久，托宾抓住我的胳膊，兴奋地说：“贾恩，你知道我们在做什么吗？我们正在水上旅行呢。”“好啦，”我说，“安分一点吧。再有十分钟船就靠岸啦。”“看，”他说，“长椅上那个浅色皮肤的女人。你还记得那个烫我耳朵的黑鬼吧？而且，我不是的确丢了钱吗——一美元六十五美分，是吧？” Presently Tobin grabs my arm and says, excited: “Jawn,” says he, “do ye know what we’re doing?We’re taking a voyage upon the water.”“There now,” says I; “subdue yeself.The boat’ll land in ten minutes more.”“Look,” says he, “at the light lady upon the bench.And have ye forgotten the nigger man that burned me ear?And isn’t the money I had gone—a dollar sixty-five it was?”
我以为他只不过是在总结自己的种种不幸，好替自己的粗暴找个说辞，男人都是这样的。于是我努力想让他明白，这些都是鸡毛蒜皮的小事而已。 I thought he was no more than summing up his catastrophes so as to get violent with good excuse, as men will do, and I tried to make him understand such things was trifles.
“听着，”托宾说，“对于预言家的天赋，还有受神灵启示的人们创造的奇迹，你总是充耳不闻、视而不见。手相师从我的手相中看到了什么呀？这一切都已在你眼前成为事实了啊。‘当心一个深色皮肤的男人和一个浅色皮肤的女人，’她说，‘他们会给你带来麻烦。’难道你忘了那个黑鬼给我带来的麻烦吗，虽然我也让他尝了尝我拳头的厉害？你能找出哪个女人比那个金发碧眼的女人皮肤更白吗？就是因为她，我的帽子才掉进水里的。当我们从打靶场离开的时候，我马甲里那一美元六十五美分哪儿去了？” “Listen,” says Tobin.“Ye’ve no ear for the gift of prophecy or the miracles of the inspired.What did the palmist lady tell ye out of me hand?’Tis coming true before your eyes.‘Look out,’ says she, ‘for a dark man and a light woman; they’ll bring ye trouble.’Have ye forgot the nigger man, though he got some of it back from me fist?Can ye show me a lighter woman than the blonde lady that was the cause of me hat falling in the water?And where’s the dollar sixty-five I had in me vest when we left the shooting gallery?”
经托宾这样一说，那些预言好像真的得到了印证；尽管在我看来，就算没有手相师的暗示，这些事情也可能会发生在科尼岛上的任何一个人身上。 The way Tobin put it,it did seem to corroborate the art of prediction, though it looked to me that these accidents could happen to any one at Coney without the implication of palmistry.
托宾站起身，在甲板上来回走动，用他那双布满血丝的小眼睛仔细地观察着船上的旅客。我问他这样做是为什么。你永远不知道托宾脑子里想什么，直到他开始付诸行动你才能明白。 Tobin got up and walked around on deck, looking close at the passengers out of his little red eyes.I asked him the interpretation of his movements.Ye never know what Tobin has in his mind until he begins to carry it out.
“你应该知道，”他说，“我的手纹显示有人能够化解我的不幸，所以我正在想办法解救自己。我正在找那个可以给我带来好运的歪鼻子男人。只有他才能救我们。贾恩，你这辈子见过鼻子不歪的捣蛋鬼吗？” “Ye should know,” says he, “I’m working out the salvation promised by the lines in me palm.I’m looking for the crooked-nose man that’s to bring the good luck.’Tis all that will save us.Jawn, did ye ever see a straighter-nosed gang of hellions in the days of your life?”
我们坐的是九点三十分的船；上岸后，我们沿着第二十二大道向市区走去。托宾没了帽子。 ’Twas the nine-thirty boat, and we landed and walked up-town through Twenty-second Street, Tobin being without his hat.
在一个街角，有个男人站在煤气灯下，目光掠过高架路，望着天上的月亮。这个人个头高高的，衣着体面，嘴里叼着一支雪茄。我看见他的鼻子从鼻梁到鼻尖拐了两道弯，宛如一条蜿蜒前行的蛇。与此同时，托宾也看到了；我听见他呼吸加重，就像一匹刚卸下马鞍的马。他径直走向那个男人，我跟在他旁边。 On a street corner, standing under a gas-light and looking over the elevated road at the moon, was a man.A long man he was, dressed decent, with a segar between his teeth, and I saw that his nose made two twists from bridge to end, like the wriggle of a snake.Tobin saw it at the same time, and I heard him breathe hard like a horse when you take the saddle off.He went straight up to the man, and I went with him.
“晚上好。”托宾对那个男人说。这个人把雪茄从嘴里拿出来，友善地同托宾打招呼。 “Good-night to ye,” Tobin says to the man.The man takes out his segar and passes the compliments, sociable.
“请问您尊姓大名啊？”托宾问道，“让我们看看您的名字有多长，可以吗？也许我们应该认识您。” “Would ye hand us your name,” asks Tobin, “and let us look at the size of it?It may be our duty to become acquainted with ye.”
这个人礼貌地回答：“我姓弗里登豪斯曼，全名马克西莫斯·G.弗里登豪斯曼。” “My name,” says the man, polite, “is Friedenhausman—Maximus G. Friedenhausman.”
“这个名字够长了。”托宾说，“要是把名字拼写出来的话，里面有字母‘O’吗？” “’Tis the right length,” says Tobin.“Do you spell it with an ‘o’ anywhere down the stretch of it?”
“没有。”这个人说。 “I do not,” says the man.
“要是加上一个字母‘O’，能拼吗？”托宾有点着急了。 “Can ye spell it with an ‘o’?” inquires Tobin, turning anxious.
“要是你打心眼里不喜欢外国语，”歪鼻子男人说，“为了让自己高兴，你可以把这个字母偷偷塞进倒数第二个音节中。” “If your conscience,” says the man with the nose, “is indisposed toward foreign idioms ye might, to please yourself, smuggle the letter into the penultimate syllable.”
“太好了。”托宾说，“站在您面前的是贾恩·马隆和丹尼尔·托宾。” “’Tis well,” says Tobin.“Ye’re in the presence of Jawn Malone and Daniel Tobin.”
“很荣幸认识你们。”这个人边鞠躬边说，“我猜你们不是打算在这个街角举办一场拼字比赛会吧。那么，能否给我一个合理的解释，为什么你们如此无所事事，在外面闲逛？” “’Tis highly appreciated,” says the man, with a bow.“And now since I cannot conceive that ye would hold a spelling bee upon the street corner, will ye name some reasonable excuse for being at large?”
“根据您的两个特征，”托宾试着解释道，“再对照埃及手相师对我手相的解说，您就是那个能够用好运气化解我手纹上的倒霉线的人。我手上的霉运线使我遭受了很多不幸，除了丢了一美元六十五美分之外，还让我碰上了一个黑鬼，还有船上那个两脚交叉坐着的金发碧眼的女人。到目前为止，一切不幸都如预言所说应验了。只有您带给我的好运才能抵消这些不幸。” “By the two signs,” answers Tobin, trying to explain, “which ye display according to the reading of the Egyptian palmist from the sole of me hand, ye’ve been nominated to offset with good luck the lines of trouble leading to the nigger man and the blonde lady with her feet crossed in the boat, besides the financial loss of a dollar sixty-five, all so far fulfilled according to Hoyle.”
这个人停止抽烟，看着我。 The man stopped smoking and looked at me.
“对于刚才他所说的，”他问，“你还有什么要修正的吗？或者你也跟他一样脑子有问题？看你的表情，我想你可能是负责照顾他的吧。” “Have ye any amendments,” he asks, “to offer to that statement, or are ye one too?I thought by the looks of ye ye might have him in charge.”
“没有什么了，”我对他说，“不过我想告诉您，因为马掌和马掌总是很相像，所以，根据我朋友的手相，您的种种特征正好表明您能给他带来好运。如果您不是那个人，那也许是丹尼的手纹交叉的缘故吧，我想。” “None,” says I to him, “except that as one horseshoe resembles another so are ye the picture of good luck as predicted by the hand of me friend.If not, then the lines of Danny’s hand may have been crossed, I don’t know.”
“你们两个，”这个歪鼻子男人一边说着一边四下张望，看看有没有警察，“同你们聊天很愉快。晚安。” “There’s two of ye,” says the man with the nose, looking up and down for the sight of a policeman.“I’ve enjoyed your company immense.Good-night.”
说完，他把雪茄猛地往嘴里一塞，快步穿过街道。但是托宾紧紧地跟在他的一侧，我则跟在另一侧。 With that he shoves his segar in his mouth and moves across the street, stepping fast.But Tobin sticks close to one side of him and me at the other.
“干什么！”他叫道。他在对面的人行道停下来，往后推了一下帽子。“你们跟踪我吗？我告诉你们，”他大声说道，“我很荣幸见到你们俩。但我现在想离你们远点儿。我要回家了。” “What!” says he, stopping on the opposite sidewalk and pushing back his hat; “do ye follow me?I tell ye,” he says, very loud, “I’m proud to have met ye.But it is my desire to be rid of ye.I am off to me home.”
“您走吧。”托宾紧贴着他的衣袖，“您只管回家好了。我会坐在您家门口，直到您第二天早上出来。因为只有靠您，我才能摆脱那些灾祸——那个黑鬼，金发碧眼的女人，还有损失掉的那一美元六十五美分。” “Do,” says Tobin, leaning against his sleeve.“Do be off to your home.And I will sit at the door of it till ye come out in the morning.For the dependence is upon ye to obviate the curse of the nigger man and the blonde lady and the financial loss of the one-sixty-five.”
“这真是个奇怪的幻觉，”这个人转头对我说，好像我是一个比较通情达理些的疯子，“你最好带他回家，好吗？” “’Tis a strange hallucination,” says the man, turning to me as a more reasonable lunatic.“Hadn’t ye better get him home?”
“听着，兄弟。”我对他说，“丹尼尔·托宾从前很理智，现在也很理智。也许他有点精神错乱，因为他喝的酒多得足以让他给人添乱，却还不够让他彻底变聪明。只是出于迷信和不幸，他才走上了这条道路，这是可以理解的。让我来为你解释解释这一切。”之后我给他讲了那个看手相的女人，以及托宾如何满怀期待地用手指着他，认为他会带来好运。“现在，”我总结道，“你明白我在这场混乱中的处境了吧。按照我的解释，我是我朋友托宾的朋友。和发达的人做朋友是很容易的，因为你有利可图；和穷人做朋友也不困难，因为别人的感激会让你得意洋洋，而且他们会把你的照片洗出来，放在廉价公寓的前面——照片中你一只手拿着一个煤斗，另一只手抱着一个孤儿。但要跟一个天生的傻瓜成为真正的朋友，则要将交友的技艺发挥到极致。而我正在这样做。”我说，“因为，我认为，我的手纹是看不出什么命运来的，拿镐把的手上并没有印下此类东西。尽管你长了全纽约市最歪的鼻子，我仍然怀疑是不是所有的算命者都认为能从你身上挤出好运。不过丹尼的手纹清楚地指向你，因此我会帮他在你这儿试一试，直到让他相信你已经被挤干了。” “Listen, man,” says I to him.“Daniel Tobin is as sensible as he ever was.Maybe he is a bit deranged on account of having drink enough to disturb but not enough to settle his wits, but he is no more than following out the legitimate path of his superstitions and predicaments, which I will explain to you.”With that I relates the facts about the palmist lady and how the finger of suspicion points to him as an instrument of good fortune.“Now, understand,” I concludes, “my position in this riot.I am the friend of me friend Tobin, according to me interpretations.’Tis easy to be a friend to the prosperous, for it pays; ’tis not hard to be a friend to the poor, for ye get puffed up by gratitude and have your picture printed standing in front of a tenement with a scuttle of coal and an orphan in each hand.But it strains the art of friendship to be true friend to a born fool.And that’s what I’m doing,” says I, “for, in my opinion, there’s no fortune to be read from the palm of me hand that wasn’t printed there with the handle of a pick.And, though ye’ve got the crookedest nose in New York City, I misdoubt that all the fortune-tellers doing business could milk good luck from ye.But the lines of Danny’s hand pointed to ye fair, and I’ll assist him to experiment with ye until he’s convinced ye’re dry.”
我说完之后，这个人突然大笑起来。他倚着墙角，哈哈大笑。然后他轻轻拍了拍我和托宾的背，抓住我们一人一只胳膊。 After that the man turns, sudden, to laughing.He leans against a corner and laughs considerable.Then he claps me and Tobin on the backs of us and takes us by an arm apiece.
“这是我的错。”他说，“我能让人时来运转，还有什么事比这更美好、更奇妙呢？从前我在别人眼里几乎是一文不值的。附近有一家咖啡馆，”他说，“那里温暖舒适，适合发表些奇谈怪论。我们去那儿喝一杯吧，边喝边探讨绝对事物的无用性。” “’Tis my mistake,” says he.“How could I be expecting anything so fine and wonderful to be turning the corner upon me?I came near being found unworthy.Hard by,” says he, “is a cafe, snug and suitable for the entertainment of idiosyncrasies.Let us go there and have drink while we discuss the unavailability of the categorical.”
说着，他把我和托宾带到了一家酒吧的里间，叫了几杯酒，并把钱放在了桌上。他看着我和托宾，仿佛我们是他的兄弟，还让我们抽他的雪茄。 So saying, he marched me and Tobin to the back room of a saloon, and ordered the drinks, and laid the money on the table.He looks at me and Tobin like brothers of his, and we have the segars.
“要知道，”这位幸运星说，“我从事的行业就是所谓的文学。我晚上到处游荡，在人群中搜寻各种奇谈怪论，探求天上的真理。你们碰见我的时候，我正在沉思，思索高架路和夜晚的主要发光体——月亮放在一起有什么意义。高速交通是诗和艺术，而月亮只不过是一个乏味的、毫无生气的天体，机械地运转着。不过这些纯属个人观点，因为在文学这个行业里，情况是恰好相反的。我的心愿是能写一本书，解释我在生活中发现的那些奇异的事情。” “Ye must know,” says the man of destiny, “that me walk in life is one that is called the literary.I wander abroad be night seeking idiosyncrasies in the masses and truth in the heavens above.When ye came upon me I was in contemplation of the elevated road in conjunction with the chief luminary of night.The rapid transit is poetry and art: the moon but a tedious, dry body, moving by rote.But these are private opinions, for, in the business of literature, the conditions are reversed.’Tis me hope to be writing a book to explain the strange things I have discovered in life.”
“你会把我写进书里吧。”托宾厌烦地说，“你会把我写书里吗？” “Ye will put me in a book,” says Tobin, disgusted; “will ye put me in a book?”
“我不会，”这个人说，“因为书里的内容太多，已经容不下你了。目前容不下你。我能做的，最多就是自己欣赏你的故事，因为时机还不成熟，印刷品的种种限制还未被取消。你的故事写成书一定很棒。可惜我必须独饮这杯欢乐酒啦。但我还是要谢谢你们，兄弟们；我真的很感激。” “I will not,” says the man, “for the covers will not hold ye.Not yet.The best I can do is to enjoy ye meself, for the time is not ripe for destroying the limitations of print.Ye would look fantastic in type.All alone by meself must I drink this cup of joy.But, I thank ye, boys; I am truly grateful.”
“你讲的那些，”托宾喘着粗气，小胡子直颤悠，一边用拳头捶着桌子，“我实在听得不耐烦了。你的歪鼻子会带来好运，但你说起话来却像一只聒噪的大鼓，废话连篇。你，加上你那些关于书的论调，就像大风吹过裂缝，发出空洞的呜呜声。当然啦，现在，要不是那个关于黑鬼和金发碧眼的女人的预言已经应验，我很可能会怀疑我的手纹是不是在撒谎，而且——” “The talk of ye,” says Tobin, blowing through his moustache and pounding the table with his fist, “is an eyesore to me patience.There was good luck promised out of the crook of your nose, but ye bear fruit like the bang of a drum.Ye resemble, with your noise of books, the wind blowing through a crack.Sure, now, I would be thinking the palm of me hand lied but for the coming true of the nigger man and the blonde lady and—”
“嘘！”高个子说，“难道你想让观相术把你引入歧途吗？我的鼻子会做它力所能及的事情。来，我们把杯子斟满。用酒给奇谈怪论保保鲜也不失为一件好事呀，在这种干枯的道德氛围中，它们很容易变质的。” “Whist!” says the long man; “would ye be led astray by physiognomy?Me nose will do what it can within bounds.Let us have these glasses filled again, for ’tis good to keep idiosyncrasies well moistened, they being subject to deterioration in a dry moral atmosphere.”
如此看来，我觉得这个舞文弄墨的人已经补偿了我们俩的损失，因为他很痛快地把酒钱都付了。因为那手相师的预言，我跟托宾的老本都已经光了。但是托宾很恼火，他默默地喝着酒，眼睛红红的。 So, the man of literature makes good, to my notion, for he pays, cheerful, for everything, the capital of me and Tobin being exhausted by prediction.But Tobin is sore, and drinks quiet, with the red showing in his eye.
不久，我们从酒吧出来了。已经十一点了，我们在人行道上站了一会儿。之后这个人说他必须回家了，并且邀请我和托宾一起走。走过两个街区，我们来到了一条小街上，两排砖房沿着街道向前延伸，每座房子都有着高高的门廊和铁栅栏。这个人停在一座房子前面，抬头看了看最上面的窗户；灯是熄着的。 By and by we moved out, for ’twas eleven o’clock, and stands a bit upon the sidewalk.And then the man says he must be going home, and invites me and Tobin to walk that way.We arrives on a side street two blocks away where there is a stretch of brick houses with high stoops and iron fences.The man stops at one of them and looks up at the top windows which he finds dark.
“这就是寒舍。”他说，“看样子，我妻子已经入睡了。因此，我想冒昧地略尽地主之谊。我能否邀你们到地下室去，我们可以在那儿适当吃点儿点心，喝点儿东西。那儿有美味的凉拌鸡，奶酪，还有一两瓶麦芽酒。欢迎你们进来品尝一下，我要感谢你们陪我解闷儿。” “’Tis me humble dwelling,” says he, “and I begin to perceive by the signs that me wife has retired to slumber.Therefore I will venture a bit in the way of hospitality.’Tis me wish that ye enter the basement room, where we dine, and partake of a reasonable refreshment.There will be some fine cold fowl and cheese and a bottle or two of ale.Ye will be welcome to enter and eat, for I am indebted to ye for diversions.”
我和托宾此时也很想吃点东西，他的提议正合我们的心意，虽然迷信的丹尼一想到手纹上预示的好运居然只是几杯酒和一顿冷餐，心里就特别不是滋味。 The appetite and conscience of me and Tobin was congenial to the proposition, though ’twas sticking hard in Danny’s superstitions to think that a few drinks and a cold lunch should represent the good fortune promised by the palm of his hand.
“你们沿着台阶下去，”这个歪鼻子男人说，“我从上面的门进去，再给你们开门。在你们走之前，”他说，“我会叫厨房里新来的女佣给你们煮一壶咖啡喝。别看凯蒂·马霍纳是个新手，三个月前才来的，但这姑娘煮的咖啡味道很不错呢。请进，”那个人说，“我去叫她下来服侍你们。” “Step down the steps,” says the man with the crooked nose, “and I will enter by the door above and let ye in.I will ask the new girl we have in the kitchen,” says he, “to make ye a pot of coffee to drink before ye go.’Tis fine coffee Katie Mahorner makes for a green girl just landed three months.Step in,” says the man, “and I’ll send her down to ye.”
