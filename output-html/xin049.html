Tale of a Tortoise and of a Mischievous Monkey 一只乌龟的故事和一只淘气猴子的故事
by Andrew Lang 作者：安德鲁·朗格
Once upon a time there was a country where the rivers were larger, and the forests deeper, than anywhere else. 从前有一个国家，那里比其他任何地方的河流更宽广，森林更浓密。
Hardly any men came there, and the wild creatures had it all to themselves, and used to play all sorts of strange games with each other. 那里人迹罕至，是野生动物们的天地。动物们常常在一起玩着千奇百怪的游戏。
The great trees, chained one to the other by thick flowering plants with bright scarlet or yellow blossoms, were famous hiding-places for the monkeys, who could wait unseen, till a puma or an elephant passed by, and then jump on their backs and go for a ride, swinging themselves up by the creepers when they had had enough. 繁茂的开花植物开满红色和黄色的花，将参天大树彼此链接起来，为猴子们提供了一个极好的隐藏处。猴子们一直躲在里面，等到一头美洲狮或是一头大象经过，就跳到它们背上去兜风，玩够了就攀上匍匐植物，摇荡开去。
Near the rivers huge tortoises were to be found, and though to our eyes a tortoise seems a dull, slow thing, it is wonderful to think how clever they were, and how often they outwitted many of their livelier friends. 在河流附近可以看到体型庞大的乌龟。如今乌龟在我们眼中似乎是一种反应迟钝、行动缓慢的生物，但是让人感到奇妙的是，乌龟过去非常聪明，并且靠智慧战胜过很多更加活泼的动物。
There was one tortoise in particular that always managed to get the better of everybody, and many were the tales told in the forest of his great deeds. 特别是有这么一只乌龟，它总是比所有的动物都要优秀，很多关于它伟大行为的故事在森林里流传着。
They began when he was quite young, and tired of staying at home with his father and mother. 故事最初，乌龟还相当年轻，厌倦了和父母一起一直呆在家里。
He left them one day, and walked off in search of adventures. 有一天，它离开父母，出去探险。
In a wide open space surrounded by trees he met with an elephant, who was having his supper before taking his evening bath in the river which ran close by. 在一片树木环绕的广袤空地上，它看到一头大象。那头大象在吃晚餐，之后便会去附近的河里沐浴。
‘Let us see which of us two is strongest,’ said the young tortoise, marching up to the elephant. “让我们来看看我们俩当中谁最强。”年轻的乌龟边说边朝着大象走过去。
‘Very well,’ replied the elephant, much amused at the impertinence of the little creature; ‘when would you like the trial to be?’ “好啊，”大象被这个鲁莽的小东西逗乐了，答道，“那你准备什么时候开始较量呢？”
‘In an hour’s time; I have some business to do first,’ answered the tortoise. “一个小时之内，不过这之前我有些事要先去处理。”乌龟回答说。
And he hastened away as fast as his short legs would carry him. 说完，乌龟就用那几条短腿能跑的最快速度急匆匆地离开了。
In a pool of the river a whale was resting, blowing water into the air and making a lovely fountain. 在河流一隅，一头鲸鱼正在休憩，它把水吹到空中，形成一股可爱的喷泉。
The tortoise, however, was too young and too busy to admire such things, and he called to the whale to stop, as he wanted to speak to him. 但是这只乌龟年轻气盛，又过于匆忙，无暇欣赏这种美景。它叫鲸鱼停下来，想和鲸鱼谈一谈。
‘Would you like to try which of us is the stronger?’ said he. “你想试试咱俩谁更强吗？”乌龟说道。
The whale looked at him, sent up another fountain, and answered: ‘Oh, yes; certainly. 鲸鱼看着乌龟，又射出一股喷泉，然后说道：“哦，想啊，当然想。
When do you wish to begin? 你希望什么时候开始呢？
I am quite ready.’ 我已经准备好了。”
‘Then give me one of your longest bones, and I will fasten it to my leg. “那把你最长的骨头给我一根，我会把它固定在我的腿上。
When I give the signal, you must pull, and we will see which can pull the hardest.’ 等我发出信号，你就拉，看看谁的力气大。”
‘Very good,’ replied the whale; and he took out one of his bones and passed it to the tortoise. “很好。”鲸鱼回答道，然后取出自己的一根骨头递给乌龟。
The tortoise picked up the end of the bone in his mouth and went back to the elephant. 乌龟衔起骨头末端，回到大象身边。
‘I will fasten this to your leg,’ said he, ‘in the same way as it is fastened to mine, and we must both pull as hard as we can. “我把这根骨头固定在你的腿上，”乌龟说，“并以同样的方式固定在我的腿上，然后我们俩使尽全力拉。
We shall soon see which is the stronger.’ 要不了多久，我们就会知道谁更强。”
So he wound it carefully round the elephant’s leg, and tied it in a firm knot. 于是乌龟将骨头仔细地缠绕在大象的腿上，打了一个牢固的结。
‘Now!’ cried he, plunging into a thick bush behind him. “现在开始！”乌龟叫道，纵身投入身后浓密的灌木丛中。
The whale tugged at one end, and the elephant tugged at the other, and neither had any idea that he had not the tortoise for his foe. 鲸鱼拉着骨头的一端，大象拉着另一端，俩人谁也不知道自己的对手其实并不是乌龟。
When the whale pulled hardest the elephant was dragged into the water; and when the elephant pulled the hardest the whale was hauled on to the land. 鲸鱼力气大的时候，大象就被拖入水中，而当大象力气大的时候，鲸鱼就被拖到岸上来。
They were very evenly matched, and the battle was a hard one. 双方势均力敌，战役十分艰难。
At last they were quite tired, and the tortoise, who was watching, saw that they could play no more. 最后两方都筋疲力尽。乌龟一直观察着，看到两方都无法再比下去了。
So he crept from his hiding-place, and dipping himself in the river, he went to the elephant and said: ‘I see that you really are stronger than I thought. 于是乌龟从灌木丛中慢悠悠地爬出来，在河里泡了泡，来到大象身边说：“我发现你真的比我想象中的要强。
Suppose we give it up for to-day?’ 要不今天咱们就到此为止？”
Then he dried himself on some moss and went to the whale and said: ‘I see that you really are stronger than I thought. 随后乌龟又在苔藓上将身体擦干，来到鲸鱼身边说道：“我发现你真的比我想象中的要强。
Suppose we give it up for to-day?’ 要不今天咱们就到此为止？”
The two adversaries were only too glad to be allowed to rest, and believed to the end of their days that, after all, the tortoise was stronger than either of them. 两个对手获准可以休息，喜出望外，并且终其一生都坚信乌龟比它们任何一方都要强。
A day or two later the young tortoise was taking a stroll, when he met a fox, and stopped to speak to him. 一两天后，年轻的乌龟正在散步，看到一只狐狸，就停下来和它说话。
‘Let us try,’ said he in a careless manner, ‘which of us can lie buried in the ground during seven years.’ “让我们来比一比，”乌龟漫不经心地说道，“看谁能在地里埋上七年。”
‘I shall be delighted,’ answered the fox, ‘only I would rather that you began.’ “我会很乐意的，”狐狸回答说，“只不过我希望你先开始。”
‘It is all the same to me,’ replied the tortoise; ‘if you come round this way to-morrow you will see that I have fulfilled my part of the bargain.’ “对我来说都一样，”乌龟回道，“明天你来的时候，就会看到我已经完成我的任务了。”
So he looked about for a suitable place, and found a convenient hole at the foot of an orange tree. 于是乌龟四下看了看，想找个合适的地方，然后在一棵橘子树下发现了一个十分方便的洞。
He crept into it, and the next morning the fox heaped up the earth round him, and promised to feed him every day with fresh fruit. 乌龟爬进了洞里。第二天早晨，狐狸将土堆在乌龟身上，并答应每天给乌龟送新鲜水果吃。
The fox so far kept his word that each morning when the sun rose he appeared to ask how the tortoise was getting on. 狐狸一直遵守着诺言，每天早上太阳升起的时候，他就会露面问一问乌龟过得怎样。
‘Oh, very well; but I wish you would give me some fruit,’ replied he. “哦，很好，但是我希望你会给我一些水果。”乌龟答道。
‘Alas! the fruit is not ripe enough yet for you to eat,’ answered the fox, who hoped that the tortoise would die of hunger long before the seven years were over. “唉！现在水果不太成熟，还不能吃。”狐狸回答说。它希望乌龟在这七年结束之前早一点儿饿死。
‘Oh dear, oh dear! “哎呀，哎呀！
I am so hungry!’ cried the tortoise. 我好饿呀！”乌龟叫喊道。
‘I am sure you must be; but it will be all right to-morrow,’ said the fox, trotting off, not knowing that the oranges dropped down the hollow trunk, straight into the tortoise’s hole, and that he had as many as he could possibly eat. “我知道你肯定很饿，但是明天就会好了。”狐狸说着，慢慢跑开了。它不知道那根中空树干上的橘子落了下来，正好掉进了乌龟所在的洞里，并且是要多少就有多少。
So the seven years went by; and when the tortoise came out of his hole he was as fat as ever. 就这样，七年过去了，乌龟从洞里出来的时候还跟先前一样胖。
Now it was the fox’s turn, and he chose his hole, and the tortoise heaped the earth round, promising to return every day or two with a nice young bird for his dinner. 现在轮到狐狸了，它选好了自己的洞，然后乌龟把土堆在狐狸身上，并答应每隔一两天就会给狐狸带一只美味的小鸟来当晚餐。
‘Well, how are you getting on?’ he would ask cheerfully when he paid his visits. “你过得怎样？”每次来访的时候，乌龟都会高兴地问道。
‘Oh, all right; only I wish you had brought a bird with you,’ answered the fox. “哦，还行，只是我希望你带了一只鸟儿来。”狐狸回答说。
‘I have been so unlucky, I have never been able to catch one,’ replied the tortoise. “我真是太不走运了，一只鸟儿也没捉到。”乌龟答道，
‘However, I shall be more fortunate to-morrow, I am sure.’ “但是，明天我会幸运一点儿的，我敢肯定。”
But not many to-morrows after, when the tortoise arrived with his usual question: ‘Well, how are you getting on?’ he received no answer, for the fox was lying in his hole quite still, dead of hunger. 但是没过几天，乌龟过来像往常一样问道：“你过得怎样？”他却没有听到回答，因为狐狸静静地躺在洞里，饿死了。
By this time the tortoise was grown up, and was looked up to throughout the forest as a person to be feared for his strength and wisdom. 到这时，乌龟已经长大了，森林里的每个动物都对它的力量与智慧深感敬畏。
But he was not considered a very swift runner, until an adventure with a deer added to his fame. 只不过没有人认为它是一个跑步健将，直到与鹿的一次比拼才让它名声大振。
One day, when he was basking in the sun, a stag passed by, and stopped for a little conversation. 一天，乌龟正在晒太阳，一只牡鹿经过，停下来和它搭讪。
‘Would you care to see which of us can run fastest?’ asked the tortoise, after some talk. “你愿不愿意跟我比比，看谁跑得快？”谈了一会儿之后，乌龟问道。
The stag thought the question so silly that he only shrugged his shoulders. 牡鹿认为这个问题太愚蠢，所以只是耸了耸肩。
‘Of course, the victor would have the right to kill the other,’ went on the tortoise. “当然，胜出的一方将有权杀死另一方。”乌龟继续说道。
‘Oh, on that condition I agree,’ answered the deer; ‘but I am afraid you are a dead man.’ “哦，如果是那样，我同意，”牡鹿回答说，“但是我恐怕死的是你。”
‘It is no use trying to frighten me,’ replied the tortoise. “想要吓唬我，一点儿用都没有，”乌龟回答说，
‘But I should like three days for training; then I shall be ready to start when the sun strikes on the big tree at the edge of the great clearing.’ “但是我想要三天的时间来训练一下。等到太阳照到这大片空地边上那棵大树上的时候，我就会准备好开始了。”
The first thing the tortoise did was to call his brothers and his cousins together, and he posted them carefully under ferns all along the line of the great clearing, making a sort of ladder which stretched for many miles. 乌龟做的第一件事就是将它的兄弟及表兄弟们叫到一起，然后小心地将它们沿途安置在空地周围的篱笆下，排成类似梯子的样式，队伍绵延几英里。
This done to his satisfaction, he went back to the starting place. 心满意足之后，乌龟回到起跑的地方。
The stag was quite punctual, and as soon as the sun’s rays struck the trunk of the tree the stag started off, and was soon far out of the sight of the tortoise. 牡鹿相当准时，阳光一照到树干上，就开始跑了，并且很快就跑出了乌龟的视线。
Every now and then he would turn his head as he ran, and call out: ‘How are you getting on?’ and the tortoise who happened to be nearest at that moment would answer: ‘All right, I am close up to you.’ 牡鹿跑的时候，总是时不时地转过头大喊：“你怎么样了？”而那时，正巧相隔最近的乌龟就会回答说：“很好，我离你很近。”
Full of astonishment, the stag would redouble his efforts, but it was no use. 牡鹿大吃一惊，于是加倍努力，可是一点儿用也没有。
Each time he asked: ‘Are you there?’ the answer would come: ‘Yes, of course, where else should I be?’ 每次它问：“你在那儿吗？”回答就会是：“是的，当然在，不然我该在哪儿？”
And the stag ran, and ran, and ran, till he could run no more, and dropped down dead on the grass. 牡鹿跑啊，跑啊，跑啊，一直跑到再也跑不动，然后倒在草地上死掉了。
And the tortoise, when he thinks about it, laughs still. 那只乌龟每念及此，仍会大笑不已。
But the tortoise was not the only creature of whose tricks stories were told in the forest. 但是森林里流传着的那些关于玩把戏的故事里，主角并不全是这只乌龟。
There was a famous monkey who was just as clever and more mischievous, because he was so much quicker on his feet and with his hands. 还有一只有名的猴子，它和乌龟一样聪明，因为手脚相当敏捷，所以也更加淘气。
It was quite impossible to catch him and give him the thrashing he so often deserved, for he just swung himself up into a tree and laughed at the angry victim who was sitting below. 要抓住它，并给它应有的鞭打，实在不太可能，因为它会恰巧荡进树里，嘲笑坐在树下怒气冲冲的受害者。
Sometimes, however, the inhabitants of the forest were so foolish as to provoke him, and then they got the worst of it. 但是，森林里的居住者们有时总是蠢到要去惹它，结果也总是败得一塌糊涂。
This was what happened to the barber, whom the monkey visited one morning, saying that he wished to be shaved. 以下就是理发师的遭遇。有一天早晨，猴子来访，说想要刮一下脸。
The barber bowed politely to his customer, and begging him to be seated, tied a large cloth round his neck, and rubbed his chin with soap; but instead of cutting off his beard, the barber made a snip at the end of his tail. 理发师向客人礼貌地鞠了一躬，请它坐下，在它脖子上系了一块很大的布，并在它下巴上涂上肥皂。但是理发师并没有给客人刮胡子，而是把客人尾巴末端给剪掉了。
It was only a very little bit and the monkey started up more in rage than in pain. 其实只剪了很少一点儿，猴子突然站起来，与其说是痛苦，倒不如说是盛怒。
‘Give me back the end of my tail,’ he roared, ‘or I will take one of your razors.’ “把我的尾巴末端还给我，”它咆哮道，“不然我就把你的剃刀拿走。”
The barber refused to give back the missing piece, so the monkey caught up a razor from the table and ran away with it, and no one in the forest could be shaved for days, as there was not another to be got for miles and miles. 理发师拒绝把剪掉的那一截还给猴子，于是猴子从桌子上抓起一把剃刀跑掉了。那之后，森林里的动物好几天都不能刮脸了，因为方圆好几英里内再找不到另一把剃刀。
As he was making his way to his own particular palm-tree, where the cocoanuts grew, which were so useful for pelting passers-by, he met a woman who was scaling a fish with a bit of wood, for in this side of the forest a few people lived in huts near the river. 猴子有一棵独有的棕榈树，那里生长着可可豆，可以用来攻击路人。在去棕榈树的路上，猴子看到一个女人正在用一小块木头给鱼刮鳞。在森林的这一边，有一小群人居住在河流附近的小屋里。
‘That must be hard work,’ said the monkey, stopping to look; ‘try my knife—you will get on quicker.’ “那肯定是件苦差事，”猴子不再看了，说道，“试一下我的刀子——你会弄得快一点儿。”
And he handed her the razor as he spoke. 猴子说着将剃刀递给她。
A few days later he came back and rapped at the door of the hut. 几天后，猴子回来了，轻轻敲了敲小屋的门。
‘I have called for my razor,’ he said, when the woman appeared. “我来拿回我的剃刀。”女人出现的时候，猴子说道。
‘I have lost it,’ answered she. “我弄丢了。”女人回答说。
‘If you don’t give it to me at once I will take your sardine,’ replied the monkey, who did not believe her. “如果你不马上把它还给我，我就拿走你的沙丁鱼。”猴子不相信女人，回应道。
The woman protested she had not got the knife, so he took the sardine and ran off. 女人抗议说没拿那把刀子，于是猴子拿了沙丁鱼跑开了。
A little further along he saw a baker who was standing at the door, eating one of his loaves. 没走多远，猴子看到一个面包师正站在门口，嘴里吃着一大块烤过的食物。
‘That must be rather dry,’ said the monkey, ‘try my fish’; and the man did not need twice telling. “一定很干吧，”猴子说道，“尝尝我的鱼吧。”面包师没等猴子再次开口邀请，就把沙丁鱼吃了。
A few days later the monkey stopped again at the baker’s hut. 几天后，猴子再次在面包师的小屋前停下来。
‘I’ve called for that fish,’ he said. “我来要回那条鱼。”猴子说。
‘That fish? “那条鱼？
But I have eaten it!’ exclaimed the baker in dismay. 可是我已经吃了！”面包师沮丧地解释道。
‘If you have eaten it I shall take this barrel of meal in exchange,’ replied the monkey; and he walked off with the barrel under his arm. “如果你吃了鱼，作为交换，我就该拿走这桶食物。”猴子回答说，然后将桶夹在胳膊下走掉了。
As he went he saw a woman with a group of little girls round her, teaching them how to dress hair. 猴子走着，看到一个女人，她身边围着一群小女孩。女人正在教孩子们梳头。
‘Here is something to make cakes for the children,’ he said, putting down his barrel, which by this time he found rather heavy. “这儿有一些东西，可以给孩子们做些蛋糕。”猴子说着放下桶，因为当时它觉得那只桶相当重。
The children were delighted, and ran directly to find some flat stones to bake their cakes on, and when they had made and eaten them, they thought they had never tasted anything so nice. 孩子们很高兴，马上跑去找来一些平坦的石头，在上面烤起蛋糕来。她们做好蛋糕，吃完后觉得这是她们吃过的最美味的东西。
Indeed, when they saw the monkey approaching not long after, they rushed to meet him, hoping that he was bringing them some more presents. 实际上，没过多久，孩子们看到猴子来的时候，匆忙跑去见它，希望它给她们带来了更多的礼物。
But he took no notice of their questions, he only said to their mother: ‘I’ve called for my barrel of meal.’ 但是猴子并没有理会孩子们的问题，只是对她们的母亲说道：“我来要回我的那桶食物。”
‘Why, you gave it to me to make cakes of!’ cried the mother. “什么，那是你送给我做蛋糕用的！”母亲喊道。
‘If I can’t get my barrel of meal, I shall take one of your children,’ answered the monkey. “如果我拿不回我的那桶食物，我就带走你的一个孩子，”猴子回答说，
‘I am in want of somebody who can bake my bread when I am tired of fruit, and who knows how to make cocoanut cakes.’ “我正需要有人在我吃腻了水果的时候能给我烤面包，并且会烤可可豆面包。”
‘Oh, leave me my child, and I will find you another barrel of meal,’ wept the mother. “哦，把孩子留在我身边，我给你另外找一桶食物。”母亲哭泣道。
‘I don’t WANT another barrel, I want THAT one,’ answered the monkey sternly. “我不要另外一桶，我要我的那一桶。”猴子坚定地回答道。
And as the woman stood wringing her hands, he caught up the little girl that he thought the prettiest and took her to his home in the palm tree. 母亲紧握着双手，这时，猴子抓住它认为最可爱的那个小女孩，然后把她带到了棕榈树上的家里。

‘Why did you not tell me it would bite?’ he asked. “你为什么不告诉我它会咬人？”牡鹿质问道。
‘Is it my fault if you are an idiot?’ replied the puma. “你是个傻子，这难道也是我的错？”美洲狮回答说。
At last they reached their journey’s end, but by this time it was late, and the puma’s comrade was ready for bed, so they slung their hammocks in convenient places, and went to sleep. 旅程终于结束了，但是天已经晚了，美洲狮的伙伴也该睡觉了。于是，它们把吊床吊在舒适的地方，就睡了。
But in the middle of the night the puma rose softly and stole out of the door to the sheep-fold, where he killed and ate the fattest sheep he could find, and taking a bowl full of its blood, he sprinkled the sleeping stag with it. 但是美洲狮半夜悄悄爬起来，偷偷溜出门，来到羊栏，找了一只最肥的羊杀掉吃了，并且弄了一满碗羊血撒在熟睡的牡鹿身上。
This done, he returned to bed. 做完这些，美洲狮就回到床上去了。
In the morning the shepherd went as usual to let the sheep out of the fold, and found one of them missing. 早上，牧羊人像往常一样来放羊出栏，发现少了一只。
He thought directly of the puma, and ran to accuse him of having eaten the sheep. 他马上想到是美洲狮吃了羊，跑去找美洲狮理论。
‘I, my good man? “我的好伙计，你说是我？
What had put it into your head to think of such a thing? 你脑子里都装了些什么，竟然会这么想？
Have I got any blood about me? 我身上有血吗？
If anyone has eaten a sheep it must be my friend the stag.’ 如果说有人吃了羊，那一定是我的朋友牡鹿。”
Then the shepherd went to examine the sleeping stag, and of course he saw the blood. 牧羊人于是去检查熟睡着的牡鹿，当然就看到了血。
‘Ah! “啊！
I will teach you how to steal!’ cried he, and he hit the stag such a blow on his skull that he died in a moment. 我要教训你这个小偷！”牧羊人叫道，一拳打在牡鹿的头盖骨上，牡鹿一下子就死了。
The noise awakened the comrade above, and he came downstairs. 响声弄醒了楼上美洲狮的伙伴，他走下楼来。
The puma greeted him with joy, and begged he might have some of the famous milk as soon as possible, for he was very thirsty. 美洲狮高兴地迎接了他，并且恳求他是否可以尽快把那出了名的牛奶给自己喝，因为美洲狮实在太渴了。
A large bucket was set before the puma directly. 很快，一只大桶就放在了美洲狮面前。
He drank it to the last drop, and then took leave. 美洲狮喝到一滴不剩，然后离开了。
On his way home he met the monkey. 回去的路上，美洲狮碰到了猴子。
‘Are you fond of milk?’ asked he. “你喜欢喝牛奶吗？”美洲狮问道，
‘I know a place where you get it very nice. “我知道有个地方可以让你喝到美味的牛奶。
I will show you it if you like.’ 如果你喜欢的话，我就带你去。”
The monkey knew that the puma was not so good-natured for nothing, but he felt quite able to take care of himself, so he said he should have much pleasure in accompanying his friend. 猴子知道美洲狮不会无缘无故这么好心，但又觉得自己有能力照顾好自己，所以说它很乐意陪朋友一起去。
They soon reached the same river, and, as before, the puma remarked: ‘Friend monkey, you will find it very shallow; there is no cause for fear. 很快，它们就来到先前的那条河边。美洲狮说：“猴子朋友，河很浅，你不用害怕。
Jump in and I will follow.’ 跳进去吧，我会跟着你的。”
‘Do you think you have the stag to deal with?’ asked the monkey, laughing. “你觉得你是在和牡鹿打交道吗？”猴子笑着问道，
‘I should prefer to follow; if not I shall go no further. “我更喜欢跟着你，不这样的话我就不走了。”
The puma understood that it was useless trying to make the monkey do as he wished, so he chose a shallow place and began to swim across. 美洲狮明白，再怎么试图让猴子按照自己的意愿行事，也是无用的，所以选了一个浅水处开始游过去。
The monkey waited till the puma had got to the middle, then he gave a great spring and jumped on his back, knowing quite well that the puma would be afraid to shake him off, lest he should be swept away into deep water. 猴子等美洲狮游到了河中央，一跃而起，跳到美洲狮背上。猴子非常清楚，美洲狮一定怕把它晃下来，以免自己被卷到深水区去。
So in this manner they reached the bank. 就这样，它们游到了对岸。
The banana grove was not far distant, and here the puma thought he would pay the monkey out for forcing him to carry him over the river. 香蕉园就在不远处。猴子迫使美洲狮驮它过河，美洲狮觉得是时候要让猴子一报还一报了。
‘Friend monkey, look what fine bananas,’ cried he. “猴子朋友，瞧，多好的香蕉啊。”美洲狮大声说，
‘You are fond of climbing; suppose you run up and throw me down a few. “你喜欢爬树，不如你爬上去，然后扔一些给我。
You can eat the green ones, which are the nicest, and I will be content with the yellow.’ 你可以吃那些青的，那是最好的，而我能吃些黄的就心满意足了。”
‘Very well,’ answered the monkey, swinging himself up; but he ate all the yellow ones himself, and only threw down the green ones that were left. “很好。”猴子说着一跃而起。然而，猴子自己吃了所有的黄香蕉，只把剩下的青香蕉扔了下来。
The puma was furious and cried out: ‘I will punch your head for that.’ 美洲狮生气地大叫道：“我要为此敲破你的头。”
But the monkey only answered: ‘If you are gong to talk such nonsense I won’t walk with you.’ 可是猴子仅仅回了一句：“你要再说这些没用的，我就不跟你走了。”
And the puma was silent. 于是美洲狮不作声了。
In a few minutes more they arrived at the field where the men were reaping the maize, and the puma remarked as he had done before: ‘Friend monkey, if you wish to please these men, just say as you go by: “Bad luck to all workers.” 又过了几分钟，它们来到那片玉米地，男人们正在地里收割玉米。美洲狮像先前那样说道：“猴子朋友，如果你想让这些人高兴，走过去的时候，你就说：‘祝所有劳动者霉运连连。’”
‘Very well,’ replied the monkey; but, instead, he nodded and smiled, and said: ‘I hope your industry may be rewarded as it deserves.’ “行。”猴子回答说。但是猴子并没这样做，而是点点头，笑了笑，说道：“希望你们的勤劳能得到应有的回报。”
The men thanked him heartily, let him pass on, and the puma followed behind him. 男人们衷心向它表示感谢，让它过去了，而美洲狮就跟在它后面。
Further along the path they saw the shining snake lying on the moss. 它们沿着小路又走了一阵子，看到那条光亮美丽的蛇躺在苔藓上。
‘What a lovely necklace for your daughter,’ exclaimed the puma. “多么漂亮的一条项链啊，以后可以送给你女儿。”美洲狮说，
‘Pick it up and take it with you.’ “捡起来带在身边吧。”
‘You are very kind, but I will leave it for you,’ answered the monkey, and nothing more was said about the snake. “你心地真好，但是我想把它留给你。”猴子回答道。于是，有关蛇的话题就此告一段落。
Not long after this they reached the comrade’s house, and found him just ready to go to bed. 不久，它们来到了美洲狮伙伴的家，却发现他刚准备好睡觉。
So, without stopping to talk, the guests slung their hammocks, the monkey taking care to place his so high that no one could get at him. 因此，客人们没有停下来交谈，而是吊起吊床。猴子很小心，把床吊在别人都够不到的地方。
Besides, he thought it would be more prudent not to fall asleep, so he only lay still and snored loudly. 此外，猴子觉得应该更谨慎一点儿，不能睡着，所以它只是静静地躺着，大声地打着呼噜。
When it was quite dark and no sound was to be heard, the puma crept out to the sheep-fold, killed the sheep, and carried back a bowl full of its blood with which to sprinkle the monkey. 天很黑了，听不到任何声音。美洲狮偷偷溜出去，来到羊栏，杀了羊，然后带回一满碗羊血，打算洒在猴子身上。
But the monkey, who had been watching out of the corner of his eye, waited until the puma drew near, and with a violent kick upset the bowl all over the puma himself. 但是猴子一直用眼睛的余光观察着。等到美洲狮靠近的时候，它猛地一踢，把碗打翻在美洲狮身上。
When the puma saw what had happened, he turned in a great hurry to leave the house, but before he could do so, he saw the shepherd coming, and hastily lay down again. 美洲狮见状，急忙转身想要离开，但它还没来得及走，就看到牧羊人来了，于是又慌忙躺下来。
‘This is the second time I have lost a sheep,’ the man said to the monkey; ‘it will be the worse for the thief when I catch him, I can tell you.’ “我又少了一只羊，这是第二次了。”男人对猴子说道，“我可以告诉你，要是抓到那个贼，他的下场会更惨。”
The monkey did not answer, but silently pointed to the puma who was pretending to be asleep. 猴子没有回答，而是默默地指了指正在假装睡觉的美洲狮。
The shepherd stooped and saw the blood, and cried out: ‘Ah! so it is you, is it? then take that!’ and with his stick he gave the puma such a blow on the head that he died then and there. 牧羊人弯下腰，看到血，然后大叫道：“啊！所以是你干的，对吗？那就受罚吧！”牧羊人抡起棍子，朝美洲狮头上猛击一下，美洲狮当时就死了。
Then the monkey got up and went to the dairy, and drank all the milk he could find. 然后，猴子下了床，到了制酪厂，喝了所有能够找到的牛奶。
Afterwards he returned home and married, and that is the last we heard of him. 之后，猴子就回家成亲了，那也是我们最后一次听到有关它的消息。
